================================================================================
STEALING SKILL TREE — LEVEL 4 PERK CONCEPTS
================================================================================
Document type : brainstorm / design proposals
Skill context : Stealing (XP: 50% of picked-up item value)
                L1 Pickpocket — activated, ADJACENT; STS/2 dmg + steal 1-10+STS cash; +2 STS
                L2 Sticky Fingers — passive; chance (item_value/1000, max 50%) to +1 STS on pickup
                L3 Shakedown — passive; on kill, (10 + STS/3)% chance to drop bonus zone consumable
Tree identity : Street Smarts economy. Rewards picking up loot, killing enemies, and using
                the Pickpocket ability. STS is the primary scaling stat.
XP thresholds : L4 requires 800 potential XP (threshold index 3 in DEFAULT_EXP_CURVE).
                At 50% of item value per pickup: a $50 item = 25 potential XP.
                Reaching L4 means the player has picked up a significant haul.
================================================================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 1: "BOOSTER BAG"
Perk Type: activated (ability)
Design philosophy: ACTIVE / COMBAT + ECONOMY / MODERATE COMPLEXITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
You yank everything off an enemy mid-fight: their gear, their cash, whatever they
are carrying. Works on living enemies. Does no damage but instantly strips one
equipped item or cash drop from the target and adds it to your inventory. On enemies
with no equipment it just pays out their full cash_drop immediately. Streetwear
and chain drops become viable mid-combat loot instead of corpse pickings.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
Ability ID    : booster_bag
TargetType    : ADJACENT
ChargeType    : INFINITE (cooldown-gated)
Cooldown      : 20 turns

Execute logic (pseudo-code):
  target = adjacent living monster
  stolen_item = None

  Step 1 — try to strip equipped item:
    if target has a "carried_item" field (list of item_id strings):
      stolen_item = random.choice(target.carried_item)
      target.carried_item.remove(stolen_item)
    elif target has death_drop_table and len > 0:
      -- Roll the drop table as if the enemy just died, but don't require a death
      if random.random() < target.death_drop_chance:
        stolen_item = random.choice(target.death_drop_table)
        -- Prevent the item from dropping again on actual death this run:
        target.death_drop_chance = 0.0   (zero out; steal used up the drop)

  Step 2 — steal cash (always happens as a secondary reward):
    bonus_cash = 5 + engine.player_stats.effective_street_smarts
    engine.cash += bonus_cash

  Message if item stolen:
    "Booster Bag! You yanked [item] off [enemy] and pocketed $[bonus_cash]."
  Message if only cash:
    "Booster Bag! Nothing worth taking — but you pocketed $[bonus_cash] anyway."

  -- Award stealing XP for the stolen item as if it were picked up:
  if stolen_item:
    gain_potential_exp("Stealing", get_item_value(stolen_item) * 0.5, bksmt, ...)
    spawn the item in player inventory (via create_item_entity)

  engine.ability_cooldowns["booster_bag"] = 20

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- New field needed on Entity: carried_item (list[str], optional). Enemy definitions
  that should have lootable gear would list item IDs here. This is additive — it
  doesn't require reworking death_drop_table, it simply gives another mechanism.
- Simplest version skips carried_item entirely and just uses death_drop_chance/table.
  This is backward-compatible with all existing MonsterTemplates immediately.
- In abilities.py: add "booster_bag" AbilityDef with TargetType.ADJACENT and the
  execute_at callable. Pattern matches pickpocket exactly.
- Pickpocket and Booster Bag stack: Pickpocket steals cash + deals damage;
  Booster Bag strips a potential item drop + smaller cash. Different cooldowns.
- The death_drop_chance zeroing is important: prevents double-dipping (strip item
  alive, then get it again on corpse). Store original chance on entity if the system
  needs to restore it across floors (it does not — monsters don't persist).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- At STS 18 (achievable by L4 with L1 +2 and several Sticky Fingers procs):
  secondary cash = 23 per use.
- Cooldown of 20 means roughly 1 use per encounter, sometimes 2 in long fights.
- The item strip is high-variance and exciting: rare enemies (Chemist, Scryer mages)
  have interesting drop tables, making targeting them a real choice.
- Enemy death_drop_chance on typical fodder is 15-30% — Booster Bag guarantees the
  roll at the cost of cooldown charges. Player trades "maybe nothing" for "definitely
  rolls now." This is strictly better against high-value targets, making enemy threat
  assessment feel strategic.
- Does NOT reduce enemy combat effectiveness (no damage component) — using it is an
  economy-positive action that costs a turn. Risky in tight corridors.

SYNERGIES
---------
- L3 Shakedown: Shakedown fires on death for a second bonus drop. Booster Bag procs
  the death_drop early but Shakedown still fires on the actual kill separately
  (Shakedown draws from pick_random_consumable, not death_drop_table). Two sources
  of bonus loot from one enemy.
- L2 Sticky Fingers: If Booster Bag delivers an item into inventory, Sticky Fingers'
  pickup XP check fires on that item. Delivering high-value items mid-combat can
  trigger the STS bonus.
- Pickpocket: Open with Pickpocket (cash + damage), then Booster Bag once weakened
  (strip item drop before finishing). Stylistically coherent combo.
- High STS builds: secondary cash reward scales with STS, creating a feedback loop
  where investing in this tree keeps paying dividends.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 2: "FIVE-FINGER DISCOUNT"
Perk Type: passive
Design philosophy: PASSIVE / ECONOMY / SIMPLE AND CLEAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Everything costs less when you take it without asking. Whenever Pickpocket or
Booster Bag (if unlocked) deals the killing blow to an enemy, or when any enemy
dies within 2 tiles of the player, loot that spawns is worth more — the base
value of items landed this way is treated as 20% higher for purposes of Sticky
Fingers' STS chance and for Stealing XP gain. Additionally, any floor-spawned item
the player picks up grants a flat +STS/4 bonus cash (rounded down, minimum 1).

More simply: every item pickup is a micro-payday.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
This perk has two components:

COMPONENT A — Pickup Cash Bonus (passive, fires every pickup):
  On any item entity pickup:
    bonus_cash = max(1, engine.player_stats.effective_street_smarts // 4)
    engine.cash += bonus_cash
    -- No message for small amounts; show message if bonus_cash >= 5:
    if bonus_cash >= 5:
      engine.messages.append(f"Five-Finger: +${bonus_cash}")

  Hook location: same place _sticky_fingers_check fires in xp_progression.py
  (or engine._on_item_pickup). Add a new _five_finger_discount_check function.
  Level gate: Stealing level >= 4.

COMPONENT B — Value Inflation for Sticky Fingers and XP:
  When calculating item_value for Sticky Fingers (L2) and Stealing XP:
    effective_value = item_value * 1.20   (20% bonus)
  This makes Sticky Fingers proc more often on mid-tier items and speeds XP gain.
  Implementation: pass an optional multiplier into _sticky_fingers_check and the
  XP gain call, or maintain a player_stats.item_value_bonus float field (0.0 default,
  set to 0.20 by this perk on unlock). The latter is cleaner.

New field on PlayerStats: item_value_bonus (float, default 0.0).
  Read by: _sticky_fingers_check, xp_progression gain_potential_exp for Stealing.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- No new effects or abilities needed. Pure passive data-layer change.
- The pickup cash bonus needs a level gate check in the pickup handler, same pattern
  as _sticky_fingers_check. Can be combined into the same function call.
- The 20% value inflation is a multiplier on computed value, not a field change.
  Implement as: effective_value = get_item_value(item_id) * (1 + ps.item_value_bonus)
- This is the simplest possible L4: rewards continued looting, synergizes tightly
  with existing L2/L3, no new systems needed.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- At STS 18: +4 cash per pickup. Ten pickups per floor = +40 cash per floor.
  By floor 4 (end of Crack Den) with 30+ pickups: around 120-160 bonus cash total.
  This is meaningful but not game-breaking; cash economy in NIGRL is not a win
  condition by itself.
- The 20% value inflation on Sticky Fingers: baseline STS proc chance on a $50
  item goes from 5% to 6%. Not huge, but over 30+ pickups is 1-2 extra STS on average.
  This compounds nicely with an already-invested STS build.
- Scaling concern: at STS 30 (late game with many Sticky Fingers procs), bonus cash
  per pickup = 7. Still modest. Cap at STS/4 keeps it in check.
- Does not affect combat at all. Pure economy perk. Good choice for players who want
  the passive fantasy without managing another ability cooldown.

SYNERGIES
---------
- L2 Sticky Fingers: 20% value inflation on both components = tighter feedback loop.
  More pickups, more STS, more cash per pickup, faster XP gain to L5.
- L3 Shakedown: Shakedown drops a consumable; player picks it up; Five-Finger fires
  again. Kill enemies, get loot, get cash, get more STS. The whole tree sings together.
- Cash economy: In zones with vendors or special cash interactions (future content),
  the accumulated bonus becomes more valuable. Future-proof design.
- Jaywalking: Jaywalking rewards exploring rooms. More rooms = more floor loot =
  more Five-Finger procs. Cross-tree synergy with two loot-hunting skills.

IDEA 3: "FENCE IT"
Perk Type: passive (on-use trigger)
Design philosophy: PASSIVE / ECONOMY + COMBAT / MODERATE COMPLEXITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
You've got a guy. Whenever you consume a consumable item (drink, smoke, eat, pill),
you've mentally appraised it and sold the recipe knowledge to your contact. You gain
a cash bonus equal to the item's value * STS/100, capped at item_value * 0.40. In
other words, you get 1% of item value per STS point, up to 40% of item value back
as cash whenever you USE something. High-value consumables reward you just for
using them normally.

At STS 20 (realistic late L4): using a $50 item returns $10. Using a $100 item
returns $20. Using cheap items still returns a small amount.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
Trigger: any item use (consumable, food eaten, joint smoked, pill popped).
         NOT on pickup — only on the actual use/consume action.

Hook location: item_effects.py in the item use dispatch function, after effect
  application and before awarding skill XP. Or a new event "item_used" that
  engine.py emits and a handler checks level gate.

Cash calculation:
  stsmt = engine.player_stats.effective_street_smarts
  rate = min(stsmt / 100.0, 0.40)          -- 1% per STS, cap 40%
  fence_cash = int(get_item_value(item_id) * rate)
  if fence_cash > 0:
    engine.cash += fence_cash
    engine.messages.append([
      ("Fence It: +$", (200, 230, 100)),
      (str(fence_cash), (255, 255, 100)),
    ])

Level gate: Stealing level >= 4.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- Requires an "item_used" event emission or a direct call in item_effects.py.
  The latter is simpler and consistent with how _sticky_fingers_check is called.
- New function: _fence_it_check(engine, item_id) in xp_progression.py.
  Called from item_effects.py right after effect application.
- Foods also count (eat = use). The check should fire in the food completion
  callback, not the "start eating" action. Eating 4 turns of ramen and then
  abandoning it mid-floor should not reward cash.
- Joints/weed: Smoking uses the item. Fire on successful smoke completion.
  The existing SMOKING_XP pattern in xp_progression.py is the hook point.
- Does NOT fire on crafting or dropping items. Only actual consumption.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- At STS 15 (attainable L4): rate = 15%. A Tropical Drank ($55) returns $8.
  If player uses 5 items per floor: +40 cash/floor across 4 floors = ~160 bonus cash.
- At STS 30 (late game): rate = 30%. A $55 drink returns $16. Very strong but the
  player has committed 4+ skill levels and multiple Sticky Fingers procs to get here.
- The 40% cap prevents absurd returns on very high-value items at extreme STS.
  A hypothetical $200 item at STS 60 would cap at $80 (40%), not $120 (60%).
- Tension with Munching/Smoking: using consumables faster is now economically
  incentivized. Cross-tree synergy: deep-invested Stealing players become enthusiastic
  consumers, which benefits food/smoking tree XP rates too.
- Potential feel-bad moment: player saves a valuable item for the right moment,
  uses it, sees a cash return. This is GOOD feel: it rewards using items rather
  than hoarding them. Anti-hoarding design is healthy in permadeath games.

SYNERGIES
---------
- L1 Pickpocket: Pickpocket yields cash + forces engagement with enemies. Fence It
  turns item consumption into an additional cash stream. Two complementary cash sources.
- L2 Sticky Fingers: Sticky Fingers rewards pickup; Fence It rewards USE. The perk
  pair creates a full economic cycle: find items (STS gain) → use items (cash gain).
- Munching tree: eating food triggers Fence It. Players investing in both trees
  get compounding returns.
- Smoking tree: smoking joints triggers Fence It. Tolerance + Smoking + Stealing
  is a natural multi-tree consumer build.

IDEA 4: "SLEIGHT OF HAND"
Perk Type: passive (modifies existing ability)
Design philosophy: PASSIVE / COMBAT UTILITY / ELEGANT UPGRADE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Pickpocket gets an upgrade. At L4, Pickpocket no longer just steals cash — it also
has a chance to apply the "distracted" status to the target, causing them to MISS
their next attack. The chance is street_smarts * 2%, capped at 60%. You are not
just stealing, you are misdirecting. The enemy grabs for their wallet and finds
it's gone right as your knife finds them.

This is a passive upgrade to an existing L1 ability rather than a new ability.
It makes investing all the way to L4 feel like a meaningful evolution of the
thief fantasy, not a bolt-on.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
New status effect: "distracted"
  Category: debuff
  Duration: 1 turn (single next attack)
  Effect: the affected entity has a 100% chance to miss their next melee attack
          (attack resolves with 0 damage or a "miss" message, enemy still consumes
          the attack action)
  Implementation: modify_incoming_damage hook on the PLAYER is not quite right here.
    Better: add a flag to the monster entity — "distracted" effect applies
    has_distracted_debuff = True. In ai.py, when a monster attempts a melee attack,
    check: if entity has "distracted" in status effects, skip damage and clear effect.
  Technically: DistractedEffect subclass with before_turn or a per-attack check.
    The cleanest hook is on_melee_attack_attempt(attacker, target) in combat.py,
    which already handles defender defence. Add: if attacker has "distracted",
    deal 0 damage, display "MISS", remove effect. One-turn total immunity.

Pickpocket modification:
  After the existing cash steal logic (success path only, not on "no enemy" fail):
    stealing_skill = engine.skills.get("Stealing")
    if stealing_skill and stealing_skill.level >= 4:
      stsmt = engine.player_stats.effective_street_smarts
      distract_chance = min(0.60, stsmt * 0.02)
      if random.random() < distract_chance:
        effects.apply_effect(target, engine, "distracted", duration=1)
        engine.messages.append([
          ("Sleight of Hand! ", (255, 200, 50)),
          (f"{target.name} is distracted!", (200, 200, 100)),
        ])

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- DistractedEffect: thin subclass. Override apply() to do nothing special; the
  check happens in combat.py when the monster attacks.
  In combat.py resolve_melee() or equivalent: before damage calculation, check
  if attacker has effect id "distracted" in status_effects. If so, display "MISS",
  call effect.expire(), return 0 damage.
- Duration=1 means "one proc then gone" — not 1 turn in the energy sense, but
  1 attack. This can be implemented as duration in ticks (1-2 ticks) or as a
  consumed-on-proc flag. Consumed-on-proc is cleaner.
- No new ability ID needed. Sleight of Hand is purely a _execute_at_pickpocket
  level-gate check appended to existing code.
- The effect itself is lightweight: no tick logic, no movement modification, just
  a combat intercept.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- At STS 20 (realistic L4 build): 40% chance to cause a miss.
  Against floor 3-4 enemies doing 6-10 damage, one avoided hit = 6-10 HP saved.
  With a 15-turn Pickpocket cooldown, this fires once per ~2 encounters.
  Expected damage mitigation: roughly 2.4-4 HP per encounter. Modest but real.
- The 60% cap prevents it from being a pseudo-guarantee even at very high STS.
  At STS 30: 60% (capped). STS investment past 30 gives no further benefit here.
- Most importantly: the perk changes HOW Pickpocket is used. Before L4, Pickpocket
  is "open the fight with a cash grab." After L4, it's also "insurance against the
  next hit." Using it reactively (enemy about to attack) vs. offensively is now a
  genuine positioning decision.
- Distracted does NOT stack: applying it twice just refreshes the single proc.
  No duration inflation cheese.

SYNERGIES
---------
- L1 Pickpocket: Direct upgrade. Always-on synergy, no other requirements.
- Stabbing tree / Windfury: Windfury (Stabbing L3) gives extra hit chance.
  Open with Pickpocket (distracted, enemy misses next attack), then Stab combo.
  The distracted miss window creates a free extra-hit moment.
- Gouge (Stabbing L1): Gouge stuns adjacent enemy. Pickpocket + Sleight of Hand
  combo: steal cash → enemy is distracted → follow up with Gouge for stun.
  Multi-status layering from two different skill trees.
- Swagger builds: avoiding a hit protects Swagger-based defense indirectly (fewer
  damage events means fewer status effects that might debuff SWG).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 5: "HUSTLE ECONOMY"
Perk Type: stat bonus (with passive rider)
Design philosophy: PASSIVE / STAT INVESTMENT / SIMPLE CAPSTONE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
The grind pays off. Reaching L4 in Stealing is itself a testament to picking up
everything in sight. Hustle Economy grants a permanent +3 Street Smarts immediately
on unlock — the largest single STS grant in the game at this point — plus a
passive rider: the Stealing XP cap (the 50% of item_value conversion) is raised
to 75% of item value for all future pickups. Getting to L5 becomes meaningfully
faster for committed Stealing players.

This is a "commit and be rewarded" design. It doesn't introduce new mechanics —
it makes your existing investment snowball harder.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
Perk type: "stat" with rider

Stat component (perk_type="stat"):
  effect = {"street_smarts": 3}
  Applies immediately on perk unlock, same as all stat perks in the system.

Passive rider — XP multiplier:
  New field on PlayerStats: stealing_xp_bonus (float, default 0.0).
  Set to 0.25 on Hustle Economy unlock (additive to the base 0.50 multiplier → 0.75).

  In xp_progression.py, where Stealing XP is calculated on item pickup:
    base_xp = get_item_value(item_id) * (0.50 + engine.player_stats.stealing_xp_bonus)
  At L4+: base_xp = item_value * 0.75.

  This is not communicated as "75%" to the player — the message just shows the
  higher XP number naturally. Players who investigate will figure it out.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- Stat component: zero new code. Already handled by the perk unlock stat application
  in skills.py. Just set the effect dict.
- Rider: requires one new float field on PlayerStats (stealing_xp_bonus, 0.0).
  Set in the perk application path in skills.py when Stealing hits L4.
  Read in xp_progression.py's Stealing XP gain calculation.
- Alternatively: instead of a new PlayerStats field, check stealing level directly
  in the XP calculation: multiplier = 0.75 if skill.level >= 4 else 0.50.
  This is simpler and avoids a new field, at the cost of a small coupling.
- The +3 STS is the largest single stat grant designed for this tree (L1 gave +2
  as a rider). Combined L1+L4 stat riders = +5 STS from the Stealing tree alone,
  plus any Sticky Fingers procs. Strong STS identity.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- +3 STS at L4: Crit chance goes up 9 percentage points (STS*3%). At STS 20,
  that's 60% crit. This is noticeable and meaningful. Compare: Stabbing L2 gives +2
  STS; this gives +3 for a higher investment level. Justified.
- XP multiplier to 75%: reaching L5 from L4 requires 2000 potential XP. At $50
  items, that's 27 pickups at 75% vs. 40 pickups at 50%. Saves ~13 pickups worth
  of grinding, which in a 4-floor run is roughly one full floor of looting. Meaningful
  but not obscene.
- This is the "boring but good" option. No new systems. Reliable power spike.
  Some players will prefer this over the mechanical complexity of Sleight of Hand
  or Booster Bag. Both are valid fantasy fulfillments.
- Potential concern: if Sticky Fingers has been procs-ing all run, STS may already
  be very high (20+) by L4. Adding +3 more could push crit chance high enough
  to feel mandatory. Monitor STS totals: if the tree routinely gets STS to 22-25
  by floor 3, reduce to +2 STS.

SYNERGIES
---------
- L2 Sticky Fingers: Higher STS from Hustle Economy increases Sticky Fingers proc
  rate (no direct mechanical link, but more STS = more crit = better combat =
  more kills = more Shakedown procs). Indirect amplification of the whole tree.
- Crit build: STS*3% crit. Stabbing Windfury also scales off STS. A character
  investing in both Stealing and Stabbing trees arrives at L4 with very high
  STS and meaningful crit + extra-hit frequency. This perk is the keystone.
- L5 acceleration: The faster L5 arrival is the real meta-reward. Design L5 to be
  the true capstone and let Hustle Economy serve as the ramp to get there.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IDEA 6: "SMASH AND GRAB"
Perk Type: activated (ability)
Design philosophy: ACTIVE / ZONE CONTROL + ECONOMY / HIGH COMPLEXITY / HIGH RISK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
You declare the whole room yours. Smash and Grab is a PER_FLOOR ability that,
when activated, causes every entity in the current room to react: enemies become
ALERTING (if not already chasing) and the player instantly "loots" all items
currently on the ground in the room, pulling them into inventory without walking
to each tile. You get everything — but now every enemy in the room knows exactly
where you are.

High-risk, high-reward. You can vacuum up an entire room's loot in one action,
but you will be fighting for your life immediately after. Perfect for a confident
player with strong STS and enough health to weather the aggression.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
Ability ID    : smash_and_grab
TargetType    : SELF
ChargeType    : PER_FLOOR, 1 charge
No cooldown (already limited to 1/floor)

Execute logic:
  Step 1 — identify current room:
    Get player's current room from dungeon.get_room_at(px, py).
    If player is in a corridor (no room), display "Smash and Grab: nowhere to rob."
    and return False.

  Step 2 — vacuum all items in room:
    items_collected = []
    for entity in dungeon.entities:
      if entity.entity_type == "item" and entity.alive:
        if dungeon.get_room_at(entity.x, entity.y) == current_room:
          -- Add to player inventory
          engine._pickup_item(entity)   -- or inline the pickup logic
          items_collected.append(entity.name)
    -- Also collect cash piles in the room:
    for entity in dungeon.entities:
      if entity.entity_type == "cash" and entity.alive:
        if dungeon.get_room_at(entity.x, entity.y) == current_room:
          engine.cash += entity.amount
          entity.alive = False
          items_collected.append(f"${entity.amount}")

  Step 3 — alert all enemies in room:
    for entity in dungeon.entities:
      if entity.entity_type == "monster" and entity.alive:
        if dungeon.get_room_at(entity.x, entity.y) == current_room:
          entity.ai_state = AIState.CHASING
          entity.target = engine.player  -- if target field exists

  Step 4 — feedback:
    if items_collected:
      engine.messages.append([
        ("Smash and Grab! ", (255, 150, 50)),
        (f"Snatched: {', '.join(items_collected[:3])}", (200, 200, 200)),
        (f"{'...' if len(items_collected) > 3 else ''}. Every enemy is on you!", (255, 100, 100)),
      ])
    else:
      engine.messages.append("Smash and Grab! Room was empty — enemies aggro anyway.")

  -- Fire Sticky Fingers check for each item collected (same as normal pickup):
  for item_id in [grabbed items]:
    _sticky_fingers_check(engine, item_id)
    _five_finger_discount_check(engine, item_id)   -- if Idea 2 is also implemented

  -- NOTE: Shakedown does NOT fire; enemies don't die. Items just appear in inventory.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- dungeon.get_room_at(x, y): this method may need to be added if not present.
  Implementation: iterate over dungeon.rooms (list of Rect/room objects), check
  if (x, y) falls within any room's bounds. Return room index or None for corridors.
- The _pickup_item path needs to handle Sticky Fingers without double-firing.
  Best approach: refactor pickup into a helper that accepts a flag "trigger_hooks=True"
  and call it from normal floor pickup AND Smash and Grab.
- Cash piles: Entity with entity_type="cash" have an "amount" field. The vacuum
  logic should handle these separately from item entities.
- One concern: if the room has 15 items, the message truncates to first 3 + "...".
  Alternatively show just the count: "Snatched 7 items and $40!"
- The ALERTING transition is the most important balance lever. All enemies in room
  should immediately path toward player. This uses the existing AIState.CHASING
  transition — same as if they spotted the player.
- If the room is already cleared of enemies (empty), Smash and Grab is a free
  loot magnet with no downside. That's fine — the player is only PER_FLOOR, so the
  strategic question is: use it when enemies are present for burst loot but risk
  the fight, or save it for a cleared room for guaranteed safety?

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
- This is the highest-risk, highest-reward L4 option. Not suitable for all builds.
  Low-HP or low-defense characters may find the enemy aggression immediately lethal.
- PER_FLOOR 1 charge is the hard cap. Cannot be spammed. One dramatic moment per floor.
- The loot vacuum itself is not inherently overpowered — the player would have gotten
  those items anyway by walking around. The value is time compression: getting
  everything at once without spending turns walking, at the cost of guaranteed combat.
- In the Meth Lab zone, Smash and Grab in a room full of toxic enemies is particularly
  dangerous (multiple enemies attacking under tox multiplier). High risk but the
  Meth Lab's better loot makes it more tempting.
- Counter-play: players who scout a room via FOV before entering and see many
  enemies will likely save Smash and Grab for a safer room. Encourages careful play.
- Potential abuse: using it in a 0-enemy room to safely collect all loot. This is
  fine — it's still spending a resource for convenience, and the player already
  knows the room is safe by that point. The ability's power ceiling in this case
  is "saved a few turns walking."

SYNERGIES
---------
- L2 Sticky Fingers: Every item vacuumed fires Sticky Fingers independently.
  In a loot-heavy room (6-8 items), one Smash and Grab could proc Sticky Fingers
  multiple times. This is the highest-value Sticky Fingers interaction in the tree.
- L3 Shakedown: Does NOT synergize directly (enemies don't die). But using Smash
  and Grab in a room where enemies then chase you — if you kill them in the fight —
  Shakedown fires on each kill. The aggro is a double setup: all the items, all
  the Shakedown procs.
- Graffiti skill (if present): Home Ground effect in tagged rooms could reduce the
  danger from the aggro burst, making Smash and Grab safer in rooms you've tagged.
- Jabbing/Beating builds: Characters with high burst damage output can accept the
  aggro more readily and immediately fight back. Smash and Grab is less attractive
  for pure kiting/ranged builds.
- Multiple rings (accessory stacking): Collecting 6 items in one action and having
  several be ring/chain equips means a Sticky Fingers proc could fire multiple
  times in one turn. Very satisfying in the right room.

RECOMMENDATION FOR IMPLEMENTATION
----------------------------------
If picking ONE:
  SLEIGHT OF HAND (Idea 4) — best demonstrates the thief fantasy as a lived skill.
  It changes how you USE an existing ability, creates interesting timing decisions,
  and requires no major new systems. The "distracted miss" is readable, satisfying,
  and fits the tone perfectly.

If picking TWO (main + rider):
  SLEIGHT OF HAND as primary mechanic + HUSTLE ECONOMY as alternative.
  Players who want active play take Sleight of Hand; players who want passive power
  take Hustle Economy. Both are thematically coherent and mechanically clean.

For maximum design density (branching tree approach per mechanic-branching-skill-trees.txt):
  Split L4 into two paths at the branch point:
    Combat branch: Sleight of Hand (upgrade Pickpocket's combat utility)
    Economy branch: Five-Finger Discount or Hustle Economy (double down on loot)
  L5 would then have two separate capstones following each branch.

NOTES FOR DEVELOPER
-------------------
- All ideas use existing TargetType.ADJACENT, ChargeType.PER_FLOOR, and INFINITE
  patterns. No new targeting modes required.
- The "distracted" effect in Sleight of Hand is the only genuinely new effect needed.
  It is thin: one flag, consumed on the attacker's next melee attempt.
- Smash and Grab requires dungeon.get_room_at() — check if this exists in dungeon.py
  before committing to this option. If rooms are stored as Rect objects with x/y/w/h,
  it is a 5-line helper.
- Cash pickup in Smash and Grab: verify Entity field name for cash piles ("amount"
  vs. another field). Cross-reference entity.py line ~128.
- Fence It: fire on completed food consumption (not start), completed joint smoke,
  and instant-use consumables. Do NOT fire on crafting.
