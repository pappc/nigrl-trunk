================================================================================
TYRONE'S PENTHOUSE — FINAL 4 NPC DESIGNS (COMPLETING THE POOL OF 10)
================================================================================

Context: These 4 NPCs complete the Penthouse NPC roster alongside the 6 already
implemented (Gun Dealer, Chef, Plug, La'Quisha, Precious, Rico). Total after this
batch: 10 one-time free service NPCs. Same interaction model throughout: bump NPC
to open menu overlay, make one choice, NPC flags used for the rest of the run.

The 4 NPCs designed here:
  1. THE ENCHANTER  — weapon/item permanent enchantment with equip-lock tradeoff
  2. THE MIXOLOGIST — drinks as transformation fuel, not just consumable pick
  3. THE GENE DOCTOR — mutation system manipulation (unique to Meth Lab prep)
  4. THE FIXER      — permanent debuff / negative status cleanse

================================================================================
NPC 1 — THE ENCHANTER: SOLOMAN
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Soloman is a former jeweler who moved into slightly less legitimate enhancements.
He takes a piece of your equipment and permanently supercharges it — but the process
fuses the item to your body. You cannot unequip it for the rest of the run. The
enchantment is powerful enough that giving up the ability to swap is a genuine
tradeoff, not an obvious choice. Best items to enchant are already slot-defining
investments (a unique weapon, a ring you plan to keep forever). Weakest lock is a
ring you were going to keep anyway. Strongest lock is your weapon — 1.5x damage on
your primary weapon is enormous, but you lose the flexibility to swap to a situational
alternative for the entire Meth Lab.

Works on unique items. One-time per run.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'E'  (Enchanter)
color = (255, 215, 80)   — deep gold, craftsmanship

POSITION
--------
Northwest alcove, small room with a workbench hazard (char='_') in front of him.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "SOLOMAN'S ENCHANTMENT — ONE ITEM (PERMANENT LOCK)"

  Soloman: "I can make something of yours untouchable. Permanently. Pick your piece."

  STEP 1 — SELECT ITEM TO ENCHANT
  Menu lists all currently equipped items across all slots:
    [Weapon]     Serrated Shiv        (ATK +6, STR scaling)
    [Sidearm]    .38 Special          (12/12 ammo, 8 dmg)
    [Hat]        Fitted Cap           (+1 STS)
    [Neck]       Gold Chain           (+1 SWG)
    [Feet]       Air Forces           (+1 STS, +5% dodge)
    [Ring 1]     Scholar's Band       (+2 BKS)
    ... etc.

  Player selects one slot. Only equipped items are shown — empty slots omitted.

  STEP 2 — CONFIRMATION WITH ENCHANTMENT PREVIEW
  After selection, a confirmation overlay appears that shows EXACTLY what the
  enchantment will do AND that the item will be permanently locked:

    "ENCHANT [Serrated Shiv]?"
    "  Effect: Damage x 1.5 (permanent)"
    "  Cost:   Item becomes LOCKED — cannot be unequipped this run"
    "  [Y] Confirm    [N] Cancel"

  Soloman: "Once I do this, it stays on you."

  On confirm: apply enchantment (see Mechanical Specification), set lock flag on
  the item entity. Message: "Soloman: Now that's a weapon."

  On cancel: no effect. Player can re-bump to try again (step 1 repeats).
  Once confirmed, engine flag sets and Soloman is done.

  On second bump after confirmation: "Soloman: My work's already on you. Go use it."

ENCHANTMENT EFFECTS BY SLOT
----------------------------

  WEAPON (melee or ranged medium/large):
    Power bonus: item.power_bonus = round(item.power_bonus * 1.5)
    If item has no power_bonus (power comes from base formula), add a flat
    +power_bonus equal to half the item's calculated base power (floored).
    STR scaling items: the scaling is unchanged; the bonus is on top.
    Special rule for weapons with break_hits (fragile weapons): break_hits is
    doubled (enchantment reinforces the weapon as well as sharpening it).

  SIDEARM (gun):
    Base damage: item.gun_damage = round(item.gun_damage * 1.5)
    Mag size is unchanged. Fire mode is unchanged.
    If item has gun_damage defined (all guns do): multiply it directly.
    Wind-up guns (wind_up_gun=True): the wind-up damage is also multiplied by 1.5.

  HAT:
    All stat bonuses on the item increase by +2 each.
    If the hat has no stat bonuses (cosmetic only), it instead grants +2 SWG and
    +2 STS as new bonuses (Soloman improvises a street crown).

  NECK (chain):
    defense_bonus += 40.
    If the chain already has a defense_bonus, add 40 to it.
    If the chain has no defense_bonus (rare — purely stat-bonusing chains), the
    +40 defense is added as a new field.

  FEET (boots):
    speed += 20 on the item entity.
    Entity's actual speed is recalculated: engine.player.speed = BASE_SPEED +
    all equipment speed bonuses. The +20 applies at that layer.
    If boots already grant speed, this stacks additively.

  RING:
    Every stat bonus on the ring is doubled.
    A +2 BKS ring becomes +4 BKS. A +1 STS / +1 CON ring becomes +2 STS / +2 CON.
    Rings with no stat bonuses (purely ability-granting rings): instead gain
    +1 to all stats (a flat power boost when doubling zero yields nothing).
    Ability-granting rings: ability is unaffected — only stat bonuses double.

LOCK MECHANIC
-------------
  - The enchanted item gains a new field: item_entity.enchanted_locked = True.
  - In inventory_mgr.py, the unequip path checks: if item_entity.enchanted_locked
    and not engine.game_over: block unequip, display message "This item is
    permanently fused to you." (same red color as other block messages).
  - Death (game_over = True): the lock is irrelevant — permadeath ends the run.
  - Dropping an enchanted locked item from inventory is also blocked.
  - The item still shows in the equipment menu normally; it simply cannot be
    unequipped by any means (NPC trades, item destruction, etc.).
  - Render the item with a special suffix in the equipment screen: "(Enchanted)"
    in gold color to communicate the lock visually at a glance.

UNIQUE ITEMS
------------
  Works identically. Unique items with elaborate on-hit effects or resource systems
  benefit from the slot-appropriate enchantment in the same way. A unique weapon
  gets x1.5 damage on top of all its special properties. A unique ring gets doubled
  stat bonuses. The enchantment does not interact with or cancel ability grants.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.soloman_used: bool = False; set True on first confirmation.
  - enchanted_locked field: add to entity.py as entity.enchanted_locked: bool = False.
    Serialized in save_system.py entity serialization block.
  - Weapon enchantment: read current power_bonus from entity fields. If power_bonus
    is 0 or None, derive base_power from the item's item_def (items.py lookup) and
    set power_bonus = base_item_power // 2. Then multiply: power_bonus = round(
    power_bonus * 1.5). Minimum result: 1.
  - Gun enchantment: entity.gun_damage = round(entity.gun_damage * 1.5). Also
    handle wind_up_per_turn: entity.wind_up_per_turn = round(wind_up_per_turn * 1.5)
    if that field is present and nonzero.
  - Hat +2 all stats: iterate over stat fields on the item's equip bonuses. Look up
    the item_def's stat_bonus dict and add +2 to each key present. If empty, write
    stat_bonus = {"swagger": 2, "street_smarts": 2} directly.
  - Neck +40 defense: item_entity.defense_bonus = (item_entity.defense_bonus or 0) + 40.
  - Feet +20 speed: item_entity.speed_bonus = (item_entity.speed_bonus or 0) + 20.
    Then call engine._recalculate_player_speed() if such a function exists, or
    inline: engine.player.speed = 100 + sum(speed bonuses from all equipped items).
  - Ring doubling: iterate equipped ring's stat_bonus dict (as stored on entity or
    item_def), double each value. For rings that store bonuses on player_stats directly
    (ring_bonuses list), recalculate after doubling.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  The most powerful enchantments by scenario:

  Best case (high ceiling):
    - Weapon x1.5 on a unique weapon with STR scaling = multiplicative synergy with
      STR investment. Potentially +4 to +6 extra damage per hit at late Crack Den
      stats. This is strong but the Meth Lab has high-HP enemies — it is justified.
    - Ring doubling on a +3 BKS ring = +6 BKS total, approaching the stat cap
      contribution. Very powerful for spell builds. The lock is trivial since you
      planned to keep the ring.

  Worst case (acceptable floor):
    - Neck +40 defense on an already good chain is meaningful (+40 DR in an
      environment where enemies deal 8-15 damage is approximately 3-5 fewer damage
      per hit). Worth locking.
    - Feet +20 speed on boots with no other strong bonuses — less exciting than
      other slots, but +20 speed is a significant turn-order advantage.

  The lock is the equalizer. Players who are deeply committed to their kit will
  find Soloman enormously valuable. Players who are still exploring their options
  mid-run should hesitate. This creates legitimate decision tension.

  One edge case to watch: locking the weapon slot before finding a better weapon
  in the Meth Lab. Players should be warned this is a run-long commitment.
  The confirmation screen's wording handles this ("cannot be unequipped this run").

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Rico (Pawn Man): Rico trades your equipped item for a random Meth Lab item.
    Since Rico removes the equipped item from the player, and the lock prevents
    unequipping, the lock SHOULD also block Rico's trade for that specific slot.
    Rico should check enchanted_locked before accepting the trade.
    This interaction is intentional — Soloman and Rico are mutually exclusive for
    the same slot.
  - STR scaling weapons: Enchanting a weapon with STR scaling means the flat
    power_bonus added by the enchantment stacks on top of the scaling. Players
    with high STR who enchant a scaling weapon get the largest absolute damage gains.
  - Unique items: Enchanting a unique weapon with an on_kill effect or on_hit effect
    makes every proc of that effect hit harder (damage-based procs scale with the
    underlying damage increase). Ability-granting uniques are unaffected since the
    ability charge system is separate from damage.

================================================================================
NPC 2 — THE MIXOLOGIST: DEJA
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Deja is the Penthouse's resident cocktail artist. She doesn't just hand out drinks —
she takes two drinks the player already has in inventory and FUSES them into a custom
cocktail that carries both drinks' effects simultaneously AND adds a bonus effect
based on the specific combination. This parallels the Chef (food fusion) but is
non-trivially different: the Chef always makes a Sandwich with both foods' effects,
flat. Deja's cocktails have emergent third effects based on what you mix — a
discovery mechanic. Players who know the combination matrix get more value; players
who experiment learn the system.

The emphasis is on the PLAYER bringing ingredients, not receiving a handout. Deja's
cocktail is more powerful than either drink alone, but the player must have two
drinks in inventory to use her. This creates meaningful inventory decisions:
do you drink your reserves before the Penthouse, or save them for Deja?

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'D'  (Deja, mixologist)
color = (200, 80, 200)   — vibrant purple, cocktail bar aesthetic

POSITION
--------
North wall, east quadrant, behind a counter hazard (char='='). Adjacent to the
Chef (whose counter is in the NE corner) but visually distinct by color.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "DEJA'S MIX — COMBINE TWO DRINKS (ONE-TIME)"

  Deja: "Bring me two and I'll make something better than both."

  If player has fewer than 2 drink items in inventory:
    Deja: "You ain't got enough to work with. Come back with two drinks."
    No menu opens. Engine flag is NOT set — player can return after acquiring drinks.
    (This is the only Penthouse NPC that can be declined and re-approached without
    penalty, specifically because the requirement is inventory-state dependent.)

  If player has 2 or more drinks:

    STEP 1 — SELECT FIRST DRINK
    Title: "DEJA'S MIX — STEP 1: FIRST DRINK"
    Lists all drink items in inventory (by name and count):
      a) Malt Liquor         x2
      b) Homemade Hennessy   x1
      c) Steel Reserve       x1
    Player selects one. That drink is flagged as "ingredient A."

    STEP 2 — SELECT SECOND DRINK
    Title: "DEJA'S MIX — STEP 2: SECOND DRINK"
    Same list but ingredient A is now grayed out (not re-selectable).
    If only 1 drink item with count > 1 is available, that same item can be used
    twice (mixing two of the same drink is valid and produces a "double" cocktail).
    Player selects ingredient B.

    PREVIEW AND CONFIRM
    After both selections, display the result:
      "DEJA WILL MIX:"
      "  [Malt Liquor] + [Steel Reserve]"
      "  Result: IRON MALT"
      "  Effects: STR buff + tox | +30 max HP for floor | Toughness buff (1 floor)"
      "[Y] Mix it    [N] Reconsider (re-pick ingredients)"

    Player confirms or goes back to re-pick.

    On confirm: both source drinks are consumed from inventory (count -= 1 each;
    remove entity if count reaches 0). A new item entity is created for the cocktail
    and added to inventory. One cocktail per use. engine.deja_used = True.

    Message: "Deja: *shakes* That's a special one. Don't waste it."

  On second bump: "Deja: I made you one. You're done."

THE COCKTAIL SYSTEM — COMBINATION MATRIX
-----------------------------------------
Cocktails are resolved by matching the two input drink item_ids against a
COCKTAIL_MATRIX: dict[frozenset[str, str], str] where each value is a new
item_id for a unique cocktail item. The matrix is order-independent (frozenset).

The cocktail item is a new item defined in items.py with:
  - category = "consumable"
  - use_verb = "Drink"
  - use_effect that applies both source drinks' effects + a bonus third effect
  - zones: [] (only obtainable via Deja — never spawns naturally)
  - weight: 0

DEFINED COMBINATIONS AND THEIR COCKTAILS
-----------------------------------------
The matrix covers common/likely drink pairings. Uncovered combinations fall back
to a DEFAULT_COCKTAIL that simply delivers both drinks' effects without a bonus.

Named cocktail items (new item_ids to add to ITEM_DEFS):

  1. "lean_special"  (Purple Drank + Malt Liquor)
     Name: "Lean Special"
     Effects: Purple Drank effects + STR buff + bonus: SlowedEffect on ALL enemies
     in sight for 3 turns (Slow aura burst on drink — crowd control cocktail)
     Flavor: "Two textures, one vibe."

  2. "iron_malt"  (Malt Liquor + Steel Reserve)
     Name: "Iron Malt"
     Effects: STR buff + tox | CON buff + bleed slow + bonus: +30 flat max HP for
     the current floor (floor_duration temp HP increase added to max)
     Flavor: "Built different."

  3. "black_out"  (Homemade Hennessy + any spirit with STR buff)
     Name: "Black Out"
     If second drink is Homemade Hennessy OR Malt Liquor OR Platinum Reserve:
     item_id "black_out"
     Effects: combined STR buffs (both apply sequentially) + bonus: all enemies
     currently in the player's FOV have their AI state forced to WANDERING for 2
     turns (they "lose" the player — a brief disengage tool)
     Flavor: "They didn't see what you did last night. Neither did you."

  4. "venom_sling"  (Virulent Vodka + Purple Drank)
     Name: "Venom Sling"
     Effects: on-hit tox transfer (Virulent Vodka) + Purple Drank slow + bonus:
     each on-hit tox transfer now deals an additional 8 flat tox damage on top of
     the transferred amount for the next 15 turns. Applied via a new
     VenomSlingEffect (dur=15, floor_duration=False).
     Flavor: "Sweet poison."

  5. "rad_punch"  (any radiation-flavored drink + any STR drink)
     Name: "Rad Punch"
     Requires item_ids tagged with rad_on_drink=True (see below) combined with
     a STR-buff drink. item_id "rad_punch"
     Effects: STR buff + radiation gain + bonus: adds 50 radiation to player
     immediately BUT also triggers the player's next mutation attempt as if
     rad_level were 75 higher than it is (forced_weak_mutation_next = True, a
     new engine flag). Flavor: "You'll feel it later."

  6. "double_down"  (same drink + same drink — any two identical item_ids)
     Name: "Double Down [drink name]"  (dynamic name based on source)
     item_id "double_down" (generic)
     Effects: source drink's effects applied TWICE in sequence + bonus: if the
     drink had a duration buff, that duration is tripled.
     Useful for players who stocked up on one drink type.
     Flavor: "Nothing in moderation."

  7. "midas_pour"  (Rainbow Rotgut + any drink)
     Name: "Midas Pour"
     Effects: Rainbow Rotgut effects (random stat reroll) + source drink effects
     + bonus: the stat rerolled by Rainbow Rotgut is GUARANTEED to be a stat boost
     (no negative outcome on that roll for this drink only).
     Flavor: "Even the chaos cooperates tonight."

  DEFAULT COCKTAIL (no matrix match):
     Name: "House Mix"
     item_id "house_mix" (generic fallback item with two dynamic effects)
     Effects: both source drinks applied sequentially, no bonus third effect.
     A guaranteed "at least as good as drinking both separately" floor.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR COCKTAIL SYSTEM
-----------------------------------------
  - COCKTAIL_MATRIX lives as a module-level constant in item_effects.py or a new
    cocktail_defs.py. Keys are frozenset({id_a, id_b}); values are result item_ids.
  - "Same drink" check: frozenset({id, id}) == frozenset({id}) — Python frozenset
    deduplicates. Handle by checking id_a == id_b explicitly before frozenset lookup,
    and mapping to "double_down" result.
  - The cocktail item entities are created via create_item_entity(cocktail_id) and
    then potentially augmented with dynamic display (e.g., "Double Down Hennessy").
  - VenomSlingEffect: new Effect subclass. Override on_player_melee_hit() to check
    if the hit triggers tox transfer; if so, add_toxicity(target, engine, 8,
    pierce_resistance=True) in addition to the normal transfer amount.
  - forced_weak_mutation_next: new engine bool flag. In mutations.py tick loop,
    before the normal threshold check, if this flag is True, add 75 to the
    effective rad level for mutation roll only (do not permanently add rad), then
    set flag False.
  - rad-tagged drinks: drinks with radiation gain effects need a tag for the matrix
    lookup. Add "rad_on_drink": True to relevant ITEM_DEFS (any drink that calls
    add_radiation in its use_effect).

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.deja_used: bool = False
  - DEJA_CAN_RETRY: unlike all other NPCs, if Deja says "not enough drinks" she
    does NOT set deja_used. Only confirmation of a mix sets it.
  - Inventory check on bump: count all entities in player inventory with
    category == "consumable" and use_verb == "Drink" (or a "is_drink" tag in
    ITEM_DEFS). Minimum 2 to open the menu.
  - Consuming ingredients: inventory_mgr.consume_item(ingredient_a); consume_item
    (ingredient_b). If ingredient_a == ingredient_b (same entity, count >= 2):
    call consume_item twice on the same entity.
  - Cocktail creation: create_item_entity(result_id), add to player inventory.
  - "Double Down" result: create a "double_down" entity but mutate its display_name
    to "Double Down [source_name]" before adding to inventory (engine.add_message
    for flavor; display_name is ephemeral/not serialized separately).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - Deja's power ceiling is high because the player invests two items. Getting a
    Lean Special (mass slow on sight enemies) or Black Out (FOV disengage) is
    genuinely powerful. But the player sacrificed two drinks that they could have
    used independently.
  - The requirement to HAVE two drinks means Deja rewards preparation. Players who
    hoard drinks specifically for Deja are playing a deeper game. Players who
    burned their drinks earlier simply can't use her.
  - The default fallback (House Mix = both effects, no bonus) ensures Deja is never
    a trap — you always get at least what you put in.
  - Rad Punch is the most dangerous cocktail (forces a mutation), balanced by the
    fact that it is opt-in (the player chose to mix a rad drink with a STR drink)
    and mutations at this point in the run can be good or bad.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Plug (smoking NPC): Plug gives 5 joints. Joints are not drinks. No overlap.
    The thematic parallel is: Plug gives a type-5 choice; Deja requires you bring
    YOUR OWN two drinks. Skill expression rather than a handout.
  - Chemical Warfare Spillover Aura (Swamp Gas strain): Venom Sling cocktail
    amplifies on-hit tox transfer, which then interacts with Spillover Aura
    for chain-tox kills. Strong Meth Lab combo for tox builds.
  - Mutation system: Rad Punch gives players who are close to a mutation threshold
    a push — useful if you want a specific mutation tier and are close.
  - Chef (food NPC): Chef fuses two FOODS into a Sandwich (both food effects).
    Deja fuses two DRINKS into a cocktail (both drink effects + bonus). Neither
    overlaps the other. Together, a player can optimize both food and drink
    consumption via Penthouse NPCs.

================================================================================
NPC 3 — THE GENE DOCTOR: PROFESSOR KLINE
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Professor Kline is a disgraced academic who fled to the Penthouse after his
unauthorized experiments were shut down. He offers a single free mutation
consultation — a targeted intervention in the player's genetic profile. Specifically:
he can either REROLL one of the player's existing mutations (bad or good), or
TRIGGER a free guaranteed mutation at a tier of the player's choosing (if the
player has enough radiation accumulated). This is the only in-game mechanism that
gives the player agency over the mutation system, which is otherwise purely random.

The design fills the mutation system gap in the Penthouse (no other NPC touches
mutations). It is particularly relevant for Meth Lab prep — players entering with
bad mutations from Crack Den radiation events can fix one, and players who want to
turbocharge a mutation build can get a free guaranteed trigger.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'K'  (Kline)
color = (100, 220, 180)   — radioactive teal, science aesthetic

POSITION
--------
East alcove, north side. A shelf hazard (char=']') beside him stacked with vials.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "PROFESSOR KLINE — GENETIC CONSULTATION (ONE-TIME)"

  Kline: "Your genome is a mess. I can tidy one thing. Pick your problem."

  MENU: TWO SERVICES
  If the player HAS existing mutations (engine.mutations list is nonempty):
    a) REROLL A MUTATION
       "Remove one of your current mutations and replace it with a new random one
        of the same tier."
    b) INDUCE A MUTATION
       "Spend radiation to trigger a guaranteed mutation at your chosen tier."
       (Grayed out if player has 0 radiation)

  If the player has NO mutations:
    Only option b is shown (reroll requires something to reroll).

  SERVICE A — REROLL A MUTATION:
    Sub-menu lists all current mutations by name and tier:
      a) Brittle Bones       [WEAK — BAD]
      b) Accelerated Healing [STRONG — GOOD]
      c) Skin Hardening      [WEAK — GOOD]

    Player selects one mutation to reroll.
    Confirmation: "Replace [Brittle Bones] with a random WEAK mutation? [Y/N]"

    On confirm:
      1. REMOVE the selected mutation entirely (reverse all stat effects it applied).
      2. Run the normal mutation roll at the SAME TIER as the removed mutation,
         but REROLL until the result is NOT the mutation just removed (no immediate
         repeat). Result is applied immediately.
      3. Display the new mutation name and description in a popup.
      4. Message: "Kline: The sequence is cleaner now."

    NOTE: The new mutation is random — it can be bad again. Kline cannot guarantee
    a good outcome. He can only guarantee CHANGE. Players with a bad mutation can
    get a different bad mutation. The expected value is neutral (same bad/good ratio
    as the normal mutation table), but the player is taking agency over WHICH
    mutation they remove.

  SERVICE B — INDUCE A MUTATION:
    Sub-menu asks which tier to trigger:
      a) WEAK mutation      (costs 50 radiation to trigger — same as normal threshold)
         "Available if player.radiation >= 50"
      b) STRONG mutation    (costs 125 radiation)
         "Available if player.radiation >= 125"
      c) HUGE mutation      (costs 200 radiation)
         "Available if player.radiation >= 200"

    Locked options (insufficient radiation) are grayed out and show current rad:
      c) HUGE mutation  — LOCKED (need 200 rad, you have 140)

    On selection:
      1. Deduct the radiation cost: remove_radiation(player, engine, cost).
      2. Call the normal mutation trigger function at the specified tier directly
         (bypassing the per-tick probability check).
      3. The mutation is random — normal polarity table applies (67% bad / 33% good).
      4. Display mutation popup immediately.
      5. Message: "Kline: You'll feel that."

    The radiation cost is IDENTICAL to the normal mutation cost. Kline is not
    offering free mutations — he is offering GUARANTEED mutations NOW, removing
    the RNG of "will this tick trigger?" Players who are close to a threshold
    but want certainty use this option.

  engine flag: engine.kline_used = True on either service completion.
  On second bump: "Kline: I've done what I can. The rest is up to your cells."

MUTATION REMOVAL LOGIC
-----------------------
  Mutations in NIGRL apply permanent stat/skill/equipment changes at the moment
  of trigger. Removing a mutation must reverse those changes. This requires:

  A. Each mutation entry in engine.mutations stores not just the mutation name but
     the CONCRETE STAT DELTA it applied: {"name": "brittle_bones", "tier": "weak",
     "polarity": "bad", "stat_deltas": {"constitution": -2}, "skill_deltas": {},
     "equipment_changes": []}
     If mutations are not currently stored with their deltas, Kline's reroll requires
     a retroactive delta table: MUTATION_REVERSAL_TABLE: dict[str, dict] in
     mutations.py that maps mutation_name -> reversal instructions.

  B. Reversal process for reroll:
     - Apply the negated stat_deltas (if mutation gave -2 CON, add +2 CON back).
     - Reverse skill_deltas (if mutation gave +1 level to a skill, remove that level).
     - Reverse equipment_changes if applicable.
     - Recalculate HP if CON changed: player.max_hp = 30 + constitution * 10 +
       iron_chin_bonus; clamp player.hp to new max.
     - Remove the mutation from engine.mutations list.

  C. Implementation path: The cleanest approach for implementation is to add
     delta storage to mutations.py when a mutation fires (store what was applied
     alongside the mutation entry). For Kline to work correctly, this storage
     needs to be in place. The retroactive MUTATION_REVERSAL_TABLE is the fallback
     if delta storage isn't added.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.kline_used: bool = False
  - engine.mutations: list[dict] — existing structure; add "stat_deltas" and
    "skill_deltas" fields when mutations are applied (mutations.py apply step).
  - Reroll: select mutation from list, call _reverse_mutation(engine, mutation_entry),
    then call _trigger_mutation_at_tier(engine, tier=mutation_entry["tier"]) but
    exclude the just-removed mutation from the result table for 1 roll.
  - Induce: call remove_radiation(engine.player, engine, cost), then call
    _trigger_mutation_at_tier(engine, tier=selected_tier) directly.
  - _trigger_mutation_at_tier(engine, tier): this function should already exist
    (or be easily extracted from) the normal mutations.py tick loop. If not, extract
    the mutation selection + application block into a callable function.
  - If no mutations exist and player has 0 radiation: Kline says "You're clean.
    Nothing to work with." engine.kline_used is NOT set (like Deja's retry).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  The reroll has neutral expected value but high player-perceived value: eliminating
  a specifically bad mutation (e.g., Brittle Bones, which reduces CON and effectively
  loses 20 max HP) feels like a huge gain even if the replacement is mediocre. The
  psychological benefit is real even when the mechanical EV is 0.

  The induce option is the more mathematically significant offer: triggering a
  guaranteed STRONG mutation (125 rad cost) early in the run compresses the ramp-up
  timeline for mutation builds. Players running Mutation skill tree or Double Helix
  strain get disproportionate value here since those builds already manage rad
  intentionally.

  The 67% bad / 33% good ratio is preserved in both services. Kline gives control
  over timing and targeting, not over outcome quality. This keeps the mutation system
  fundamentally chaotic while giving players one meaningful intervention.

  Hard cap: one use per run. This prevents Kline from being used to fish for good
  mutations repeatedly.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Double Helix strain (SWG/Mutation): the strain triggers mutations directly.
    Kline's Induce on top of a Double Helix smoke is a double mutation window in one
    Penthouse visit — a dedicated Mutation build can force 2 mutations here.
  - Mutation skill tree: if the player has Mutation perks that improve mutation
    polarity or give bonuses to good mutations, Kline's Induce is worth more
    because the odds of a good outcome are higher (perks shift the baseline).
  - Decontamination skill tree: if the player is trying to REMOVE radiation
    strategically (managing rad levels), Kline's Induce lets them dump rad at a
    known cost with a known mutation instead of waiting for a random tick trigger.
  - Precious (Tattoo Artist): Precious might penalize a stat Kline just restored
    via reroll reversal. Sequencing matters — visit Kline before Precious if you
    are fixing a CON mutation, then visit Precious to boost something else.

================================================================================
NPC 4 — THE FIXER: CARLTON
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Carlton is a smooth-talking man in a pressed shirt who has connections everywhere.
He offers one free "fix" — the removal of a permanent or persistent negative
condition on the player. Unlike standard cleansing (which handles turn-based status
effects like poison or bleed), Carlton addresses STRUCTURAL problems: negative
permanent stat debuffs, run-locked negative conditions, and most powerfully, the
permanent lock on an enchanted item (with a steep cost). He is the last resort for
players who made a decision they regret or who accumulated structural damage to
their character.

Carlton's service is intentionally rare-feeling: most runs, players won't need him.
But in runs where something went badly wrong — a bad Precious outcome, an accidental
Soloman enchant on the wrong item, or a stack of permanent debuffs from Meth Lab
mutation bad luck — Carlton is invaluable.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'C'  (Carlton)
color = (220, 220, 180)   — off-white, clean shirt aesthetic

POSITION
--------
South wall, center-west. Accessible from the main floor without going into an alcove.
He is intentionally easy to find — players who need him are usually in trouble.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "CARLTON'S FIX — ONE JOB (FREE)"

  Carlton: "I can make one problem go away. What's the issue?"

  Carlton's menu dynamically lists available fixes based on what is wrong with the
  player's current run state. Categories:

    CATEGORY A — ENCHANT UNLOCK (only shown if player has an enchanted locked item)
      "Unlock [item name] — remove the permanent equipment lock."
      Cost: player loses 30% of their current cash (rounded down).
      Carlton: "My guy needs paying. That's the deal."

      If confirmed: item_entity.enchanted_locked = False. Cash deducted immediately.
      The item can now be unequipped normally. The enchantment effects REMAIN
      (x1.5 damage, doubled ring stats, etc.) — Carlton frees the lock only,
      not the power. This is intentional and important: Carlton is not a scam.
      You keep the enchantment. You just regain the option to swap.

    CATEGORY B — PERMANENT STAT RESTORE (only shown if player has negative permanent
    stat deltas from sources other than character creation)
      Sources that create negative permanent stats:
        - Precious (Tattoo Artist) random penalty
        - Mutation bad outcomes (CON/STR/etc. reductions)
        - Any future system that applies permanent stat penalties
      Carlton identifies ALL negative stat deltas currently applied from these
      sources and presents them as a combined fix:
      "Restore all permanently penalized stats to their Penthouse entry values."
      Cost: none — this is his free service when no enchant unlock is needed.
      Carlton: "Whatever they did to you, I can put it right."

      On confirm: for each permanent stat penalty tracked in player_stats:
        - Reverse the penalty (add back the absolute value of each negative delta).
        - Recalculate HP if CON was involved.
        - Recalculate all derived stats.
      engine flag: engine.carlton_used = True.
      Message: "Carlton: Back to where you should be."

      IMPORTANT: Carlton cannot restore stat LOSSES from character creation or
      normal leveling. He only reverses externally-applied permanent penalties.
      This requires a new tracking structure: player_stats.permanent_penalties:
      list[dict] — a ledger of externally-applied permanent stat changes, written
      to whenever Precious, Kline, or any other NPC/system applies a permanent
      negative stat. Carlton reads this ledger and reverses every entry.

    CATEGORY C — GENERIC STRUCTURAL FIX (fallback if no A or B applies)
      If the player has no enchanted locked items AND no tracked permanent penalties:
      Carlton offers a minor but useful consolation:
      "Clear all negative temporary status effects currently active."
      Cost: none.
      Carlton: "You're in better shape than you think. Let me clean you up."
      Effect: removes all Effect entries on the player where category == "debuff"
      and duration is not floor_duration=True (i.e., temporary debuffs only —
      the kind that would clear anyway in a few turns but Carlton removes them now).
      This includes: poison, bleed, chill, burn, shock, fear, stun (if active).
      Does NOT remove floor_duration debuffs (those are structural to the floor).

  PRIORITY ORDERING
  -----------------
  If multiple categories apply simultaneously:
    Carlton shows ALL applicable options in the menu. Player picks one.
    After one pick, Carlton is used (engine.carlton_used = True) and he is done.
    This means: if the player has both an enchanted item AND permanent stat penalties,
    they must choose which to fix. Carlton does not fix everything — he fixes ONE thing.

  On second bump: "Carlton: I squared it away. That's all I do."

PERMANENT PENALTY TRACKING
----------------------------
The player_stats.permanent_penalties ledger is the key new structure for Carlton
to function. Schema for each entry:

  {
    "source": "precious_tattoo",   # which system applied it
    "stat": "constitution",         # which stat was penalized
    "amount": -1,                  # delta (negative value = penalty)
    "applied_floor": 4             # diagnostic: which floor it was applied on
  }

This ledger must be:
  - Written to by Precious when her random penalty fires.
  - Written to by any mutation outcome that reduces a player stat permanently.
  - Written to by any other future NPC (e.g., a bad trade deal) that applies a
    permanent negative stat.
  - Serialized in save_system.py alongside player_stats.
  - NOT written to by character creation base stats (those are not "external" penalties).
  - Read and reversed by Carlton's service B.

If the permanent_penalties ledger is not yet implemented when Carlton is coded,
Carlton defaults to showing only Category A (enchant unlock) and Category C
(temp debuff clear). Category B requires the ledger. The ledger can be added
incrementally — Carlton gracefully degrades without it.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.carlton_used: bool = False
  - Category A (enchant unlock):
    Check: any equipped item where entity.enchanted_locked == True.
    Cash cost: int(engine.player.cash * 0.30).
    Apply: engine.player.cash -= cost; item_entity.enchanted_locked = False.
    The enchantment stat effects on the item are NOT reversed.
  - Category B (stat restore):
    Check: player_stats.permanent_penalties is nonempty.
    Apply: for entry in player_stats.permanent_penalties:
      setattr(player_stats, "_base_" + entry["stat"],
              getattr(player_stats, "_base_" + entry["stat"]) - entry["amount"])
    Then clear: player_stats.permanent_penalties = []
    Recalculate HP: player.max_hp = 30 + effective_constitution * 10 + iron_chin_bonus;
    player.hp = min(player.hp, player.max_hp).
  - Category C (temp debuff clear):
    For effect in list(entity.effects):
      if effect.category == "debuff" and not effect.floor_duration:
        effect.expire(entity, engine); entity.effects.remove(effect)
  - Carlton can see MULTIPLE applicable categories at once. Show all in the menu.
    Setting engine.carlton_used blocks future visits regardless of which was picked.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  Carlton's power level scales inversely with how well the player has been playing:
  players who avoided Precious's stat penalty, never got a bad mutation, and didn't
  over-commit with Soloman need Carlton only for the minor debuff clear (Category C),
  which is almost trivially useful. Players who stacked bad decisions may find Carlton
  is the single most powerful NPC in the Penthouse.

  The 30% cash cost for Enchant Unlock is calibrated to sting without being fatal.
  At the Penthouse transition, players typically have 100-500 cash. 30% is 30-150 cash.
  This represents a real economy hit but not a run-ending loss. The value of freeing
  a weapon slot is often worth it (access to Meth Lab weapons instead of being stuck
  with a Crack Den locked weapon).

  The stat restore (Category B) has no cost — because it only undoes externally
  forced harm. If the player chose Precious's boost knowing the risk, Carlton undoes
  the specific element they couldn't control (the random penalty). This is philosophically
  consistent: Carlton doesn't undo the player's choices, he undoes forced outcomes.

  Category C (temp debuff clear) is a nearly trivial service. Walking into the Meth
  Lab without any debuffs active is worth approximately nothing — debuffs expire
  naturally. Carlton's Category C is the consolation prize for players who have a
  clean run and simply don't need his deeper services.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Soloman (Enchanter): The most direct synergy in the Penthouse. Lock an item with
    Soloman for the Meth Lab power boost, then if you find a better item mid-Meth Lab
    or want to adjust, Carlton's Category A unlocks it for 30% cash. The loop is:
    Soloman buffs the item; Carlton can release the lock later if needed. Together
    they form a two-phase power-management system.
  - Precious (Tattoo Artist): Precious applies a random stat penalty. Carlton's
    Category B reverses it. This makes Precious lower-risk if you know Carlton will
    catch the worst outcomes. However, Carlton only fixes one thing per run — if you
    use Carlton for Precious's penalty, you can't use him for Soloman's lock later.
    The player must choose which problem is bigger.
  - Mutation system (Professor Kline): Kline can reroll a bad mutation, which
    reverses the mutation's stat damage directly. Carlton's stat restore would catch
    the SAME damage. Using both on the same problem wastes one NPC. Players should
    use Kline for mutation reversals and Carlton for Precious/Soloman fallout.
  - Economy (cash management): Carlton's Enchant Unlock costs 30% cash. Players
    using Tyrone's shop or buying faction rep from Smooth Carl should budget for
    Carlton if they plan to use Soloman. Running all three (Soloman + Carlton + Shop)
    is an expensive but powerful Penthouse visit.
