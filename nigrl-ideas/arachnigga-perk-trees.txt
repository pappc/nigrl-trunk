NIGRL — Arachnigga Skill: 5 Perk Tree Proposals
=================================================
Date: 2026-03-22
Skill XP Source: Applying web or venom/poison effects to enemies (webbing, poisoning, venom procs)
Core mechanic: Cobweb tiles — enemies entering are FROZEN (stunned, can't act).
              Enemies trying to leave: 50% chance they fail and lose their action.
              Slow debuff applies to any enemy that has been in a web tile.
Tone: Street/dark humor. Think bootleg pest control, crackhead entomologist, gutter druid.

New effects referenced throughout this doc (to be added to effects.py):
  webbed        — 50% chance to lose turn-action on any attempt to move, 6t duration
  venom_dot     — DOT 3 dmg/turn, 4t (like BleedingEffect but flagged as poison)
  venom_pool    — on-death: spawns a 1-tile venom puddle hazard (like fire, poisons on contact)
  molt          — flat +3 armor, 10t; player becomes temporarily chitinous
  silk_rush     — +20 energy/tick, 6t; gained after successfully webbing an enemy
  spiderling    — allied entity tag: hp=1, MEANDER AI vs nearest enemy, speed=120
  acid_web      — floor tile variant that deals 3 dmg/turn to any entity standing on it

ASCII chars:
  Cobweb tile: '#' (rendered over floor glyph in a distinct color, e.g. dark yellow)
  Venom puddle tile: '~' (green-tinted)
  Spiderling entity: 's' (small spider ally, color lime green)

===========================================================================================
TREE A — "The Trapper" (Web Control Specialist)
===========================================================================================
Playstyle: Methodical crowd-controller. You own the floor. Enemies never move freely.
Focus: Cobweb quantity, web permanence, multi-tile control.
XP emphasis: Webbing enemies. More webs placed = more XP.

--- L1: "Pest Control" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: lay_web)
Ability: lay_web — ADJACENT_TILE, PER_FLOOR 3 charges.
  Place a cobweb tile on any adjacent empty floor tile. The web persists until an enemy
  is webbed in it (standard web breakage rules apply). Webbed enemies gain the "webbed"
  effect (50% chance to lose action on any move attempt, 6 turns).
  Starting point — gets the player into the web-laying game without committing full builds.
  Tactical decision: where do you pre-place webs? Choke points vs retreating corridors?

--- L2: "Sticky Situation" ---
Type: passive
Effect: Webbed enemies are also Slowed (SlowEffect, -30 energy/tick, 8t).
  Whenever an enemy gains the "webbed" effect, they simultaneously receive the slow debuff.
  Web + slow = enemies approach 40-50% slower even after escaping the web.
  Tactical decision: web a fast enemy early vs saving charge for clustered enemies.
  Synergy: web slows the enemy; slow means they stay in the web longer before their escape
  attempt, which increases the effective chance the 50% miss triggers again.

--- L3: "Web of Lies" ---
Type: passive + activated (grants ability: web_snare, INFINITE charges, CD 8 turns)
  Passive component: Webs no longer break when an enemy escapes them. The cobweb tile
  persists on the floor for the rest of the floor — enemies re-entering it get webbed again.
  Ability component: web_snare — SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, hurl a web strand at a visible enemy.
  If it hits (always hits), applies "webbed" to the target immediately without a tile placed.
  Range: 6 tiles. Does 0 damage. Useful when you can't pre-place webs.
  Big power spike — permanent web tiles turns the map into a spider's parlor.

--- L4: "Thousand Legs" ---
Type: passive + stat (+2 Street-Smarts)
Stat bonus: +2 Street-Smarts.
Passive: Gain the silk_rush effect (+20 energy/tick, 6t) whenever you successfully
  web an enemy (either via lay_web tile contact or web_snare hit).
  silk_rush does not stack duration — reapply refreshes to 6t.
  The speed bonus represents the adrenaline of a trap snapping shut. Now you can
  capitalize on the webbed window: rush in, strike, and retreat before it wears off.
  Tactical decision: do you use silk_rush to re-position more webs, or to attack?

--- L5: "Mine Field" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: web_burst, PER_FLOOR 1 charge)
Ability: web_burst — SELF, targets all cobweb tiles currently on the floor.
  Every existing web tile simultaneously detonates: any entity (enemy or neutral) on
  or adjacent (Chebyshev 1) to a web tile takes STR + Street-Smarts damage (no defense).
  Web tiles are NOT destroyed — they remain as permanent traps post-burst.
  This is a nuke button for when you've built up a web grid. Late-floor multiple-enemy
  encounters become free kills if you've been laying webs throughout.
  Balance note: PER_FLOOR 1 and requires pre-placed webs — purely reactive/prep tool.

TREE B — "Venom Dealer" (Poison DOT Specialist)
===========================================================================================
Playstyle: Patient assassin. Stack venom, watch them melt. The floor is your territory.
Focus: venom_dot application, stacking, and on-death exploitation.
XP emphasis: Poisoning enemies. More poison ticks applied = more XP.

--- L1: "Street Venom" ---
Type: passive
Effect: All melee attacks with any weapon have a 20% chance to apply venom_dot
  (3 dmg/turn, 4 turns, poison flag — bypasses standard damage reduction, not armor).
  Venom_dot re-apply refreshes duration (does not stack damage per application).
  This hooks into on_player_melee_hit lifecycle. Sets up a passive damage stream
  that rewards staying in melee. Gives the tree identity immediately at L1.
  Tactical decision: you want to hit enemies multiple times to fish for procs.

--- L2: "Nasty Bite" ---
Type: stat + passive (+1 Strength, +2 Street-Smarts)
Stat: +1 Strength, +2 Street-Smarts.
Passive: venom_dot now deals 5 dmg/turn (upgraded from 3). Duration unchanged at 4t.
  The stat bonus synergizes directly — more Street Smarts increases crit chance,
  which means more hits, which means more venom procs. More Strength means direct
  damage also scales. A double-dip improvement on the L1 setup.

--- L3: "Necrotic" ---
Type: passive
Effect: When an enemy dies while afflicted with venom_dot, their corpse creates a
  venom_pool tile on their death location. Venom pools behave like fire tiles:
  any entity stepping on them applies venom_dot. Pools last 15 turns then dissipate.
  Tactical: kill a poisoned enemy in a doorway = natural venom trap. Groups of
  enemies chasing through the same tile chain-poison each other.
  Edge case: player can step on their own venom pools (venom_dot is applied to player
  too — this is intentional, it's a risk). CON builds can afford it; SWG builds cannot.

--- L4: "Concentrated" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: venom_strike, INFINITE charges, CD 10 turns)
Ability: venom_strike — ADJACENT (quick-select), INFINITE charges, 10t cooldown.
  A focused bite/stab that deals Street-Smarts damage directly and applies a
  supercharged venom_dot variant: heavy_venom (8 dmg/turn, 6 turns, poison flag).
  If target already has venom_dot or heavy_venom active, deal an additional +Street-Smarts
  bonus damage on the initial hit (the venom reacts with existing poison).
  Tactical: fish for a normal venom proc with L1 passive, then venom_strike for a
  bonus damage proc. Or use venom_strike first, then rely on passive refreshes.

--- L5: "The Last Spider" ---
Type: passive + stat (+2 Constitution)
Stat: +2 Constitution.
Passive: While any enemy on the floor has venom_dot or heavy_venom active, the
  player gains a passive +2 armor (does not appear as a buff icon — it's structural).
  Additionally, when the player deals the killing blow to a poisoned enemy, heal for
  the venom's remaining tick count * 2 HP (e.g., 3 ticks left on 5 dmg/t = 6 HP heal).
  "The spider always wins." Gives late-game sustain that rewards keeping enemies
  poisoned rather than killing them too fast. Pairs with Munching for fat builds.

TREE C — "Eight Legs McGee" (Mobility + Harassment)
===========================================================================================
Playstyle: Skitter around the map, web-and-retreat, never let enemies pin you down.
Focus: Speed, web placement while moving, making webs from movement.
XP emphasis: Webbing enemies AND dodging/moving while enemies are webbed.

--- L1: "Chitinous" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: molt, PER_FLOOR 2 charges)
Ability: molt — SELF, 2/floor.
  Shed your outer layer. Instantly gain molt effect: +3 armor for 10 turns.
  Additionally, molt leaves a cobweb tile on the player's CURRENT position when
  activated (the shed skin acts as a web). If an enemy pursues the player and steps
  on that tile, they are immediately webbed.
  Tactical: use molt while fleeing down a corridor — the tile you were standing on
  becomes a web trap. Incentivizes the "back up into the enemy" combat style.

--- L2: "Silk Spitter" ---
Type: passive
Effect: Whenever the player moves 3+ consecutive tiles in a straight line without
  attacking (using the dash-style movement from Jaywalking or just normal movement),
  a cobweb tile is deposited on the tile they left. This "web trail" means actively
  kiting enemies creates traps behind you.
  Synergy: Stack with Jaywalking (Dash ability) — one dash deposits a cobweb on the
  origin tile. Stack with molt: each molt deposit + trail deposit = two webs from
  one movement burst. Rewards the skirmisher who keeps moving.
  Implementation note: track player's consecutive-move streak; reset on attack/ability use.

--- L3: "Aight Imma Head Out" ---
Type: stat + passive (+2 Street-Smarts)
Stat: +2 Street-Smarts.
Passive: While any enemy on the current floor has the "webbed" effect active, the
  player gains +15 energy/tick (equivalent to a mild SpeedBoostEffect, but it's
  structural/passive — not an effect object, just a bonus in modify_energy_gain).
  Flavor: you FEEL faster when your prey is stuck. The bonus vanishes the instant
  all webs are cleared. Encourages actively refreshing webs.
  Tactical decision: do you burn an extra web charge to keep the speed bonus, or save it?

--- L4: "Crawl Space" ---
Type: passive
Effect: The player can move through their own cobweb tiles without triggering them
  and without slowing (webs of your own silk recognize you). Enemies stepping on
  player-origin webs have their escape-chance halved: 75% chance to lose action
  instead of 50%. Web tiles the player passes through gain a "reinforced" flag.
  This removes the main downside of filling the floor with webs (you can still
  move freely) and makes your traps deadlier. Incentivizes web-dense maps.

--- L5: "Spider Run" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: spider_run, PER_FLOOR 2 charges)
Ability: spider_run — SELF, 2/floor.
  For 8 turns: player movement costs 0 energy (each step is free). During this time,
  every tile the player steps OFF deposits a cobweb tile (not ON — behind them).
  When spider_run expires, if the player webbed 3+ enemies during the run, they gain
  silk_rush (+20 energy/tick, 6t).
  This is a panic-escape + trap-layer hybrid. Use when surrounded: sprint out, leave
  a web grid in your wake, enemies all get stuck, silk_rush fires, you're now faster.
  Balance: 2/floor, 8 turns max, requires actually running through or near enemies.

TREE D — "Spiderling Wrangler" (Summon + Swarm)
===========================================================================================
Playstyle: Horde commander. Grow a spider gang. Be the spider mom.
Focus: Spawning and buffing spiderling allies, turn-based swarm tactics.
XP emphasis: When spiderlings kill enemies (their kills also grant you web XP).

--- L1: "Lil Homie" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: call_spider, PER_FLOOR 2 charges)
Ability: call_spider — ADJACENT_TILE, 2/floor.
  Summon a Spiderling on an adjacent tile. Spiderling: hp=6, dmg=2-3, speed=120 (faster
  than base), MEANDER AI targeting nearest enemy, char='s' (lime green).
  Spiderlings are friendly — no friendly fire. They don't give the player XP on kill but
  THEIR kills do feed Arachnigga XP as if the player poisoned/webbed.
  Starts the summon identity. Two spiderlings per floor is modest — just scouts.

--- L2: "Spider Squad" ---
Type: passive + stat (+1 Constitution, +1 Strength)
Stat: +1 Constitution, +1 Strength.
Passive: Spiderling max hp increased to 12. call_spider gains +1 charge per floor
  (now 3/floor). Additionally, when a spiderling dies, it leaves a cobweb tile on
  its death location (the little guy's last act of love).
  The passive death-web incentivizes letting spiderlings engage enemies in chokepoints
  — they web the area when they fall. Raises the charge count to allow a true squad.

--- L3: "Auntie Venom" ---
Type: passive
Effect: Each spiderling the player has alive on the floor grants the player a passive
  +1 flat damage on ALL attacks (melee, gun, ability). Max bonus: +5 (capped at 5
  living spiderlings). This is a squad-size damage buff — more spiders = more you.
  Tactical: do you sacrifice spiderlings to web choke points (L2 passive), or keep
  them alive for the damage bonus? Classic summon vs control split decision.

--- L4: "Big Momma" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: egg_sac, PER_FLOOR 1 charge)
Ability: egg_sac — ADJACENT_TILE, 1/floor.
  Place an Egg Sac entity on an adjacent tile (char: 'O', brown, hp=1, blocks_movement=False).
  Egg Sacs are inert — enemies may walk past them. When an enemy ends their turn adjacent
  to an Egg Sac, it hatches: 2 spiderlings spawn on adjacent open tiles (beyond the call_spider
  limit — egg hatches are free). Egg Sac is destroyed on hatch.
  If the enemy attacks the Egg Sac first (it has hp=1), it also hatches (protecting itself).
  Tactical: place an Egg Sac at a T-junction. First enemy to step near it triggers a
  free 2-spider spawn. Enemies don't know to avoid it.

--- L5: "Colony" ---
Type: passive + stat (+2 Constitution)
Stat: +2 Constitution.
Passive: All spiderlings are upgraded permanently: hp=20, dmg=4-6, apply venom_dot
  on hit (20% chance, 2 dmg/turn 3t — baby venom). Spiderlings now pathfind to webbed
  enemies preferentially (not just nearest). When a spiderling attacks a webbed enemy,
  they deal double damage (the web holds them — easier to bite).
  L5 transforms the early scouts into actual threats. The synergy with Tree A (web grid)
  becomes lethal: web the floor, call spiders, spiders rush webbed targets, double damage.
  With Auntie Venom active: 5 spiders = +5 damage AND +20-24 dmg of spider attacks/turn.

TREE E — "Acid Freak" (Chemical Web + Venom Corruption)
===========================================================================================
Playstyle: Reckless corrosive mess. You don't care if you get hurt — everything dissolves.
Focus: acid_web tiles, armor-stripping, venom synergizing with chemical effects.
Risk profile: This tree has deliberate self-harm risks. High floor — high ceiling.

--- L1: "Caustic Threads" ---
Type: passive
Effect: All cobweb tiles placed by the player (via lay_web from Tree A or any ability)
  become acid_web tiles instead. Acid_web tiles deal 3 dmg/turn to any entity standing
  on them (including the player — risk!). They ALSO apply the webbed effect on contact.
  If the player doesn't have lay_web from Tree A, this perk grants a stripped-down
  version: acid_drip (ADJACENT_TILE, PER_FLOOR 2, places acid_web tile only).
  The acid_web tile is rendered with '~' in a green-acid color.
  Immediately raises stakes: your webs now hurt enemies AND you. Position carefully.

--- L2: "Corrosive Bite" ---
Type: passive
Effect: Venom_dot (from any source — Tree B passive, items, abilities) now also strips
  1 point of armor per stack application (not per tick — just on apply/reapply).
  This is a soft armor-strip. Against an enemy with 5 armor, you need 5 venom procs
  to full strip. Against 0-armor enemies (most floor 1-3 enemies), irrelevant — so the
  perk scales better in later floors where armored enemies exist.
  Tactical: open with venom procs to strip armor, then switch to heavy attacks.

--- L3: "Boiling Point" ---
Type: passive
Effect: Enemies standing on an acid_web tile when they die explode in a 3x3 acid splash:
  all entities (enemies AND player) in the splash take 5 + Street-Smarts acid damage
  (no defense). Each entity splashed also receives venom_dot if they don't already
  have it. Acid_web tiles created in the blast area persist for 5 turns.
  Classic risk/reward: kill a webbed enemy on an acid tile = big aoe, but you might be
  in the blast too. Encourages ranged kills on acid-webbed enemies, or at least not
  standing in the blast zone.

--- L4: "Exoskeleton" ---
Type: stat + passive (+2 Constitution, +1 Tolerance)
Stat: +2 Constitution, +1 Tolerance.
Passive: The player takes 50% reduced damage from acid_web tiles and venom_dot
  self-applications. Acid and venom you created recognizes you as kin.
  Also: whenever the player takes acid_web or venom_dot damage (even the reduced amount),
  they gain molt (+3 armor, 10t, refreshes on each instance of acid/venom self-damage).
  This perk completely flips the risk of L1 and L3 — your own acid/venom actually
  generates free armor for you. Now standing on your own acid webs is desirable.

--- L5: "Full Dissolution" ---
Type: activated (grants ability: acid_flood, PER_FLOOR 1 charge)
Ability: acid_flood — SELF, 1/floor.
  For 5 turns, every tile the player steps ON (not off — opposite of spider_run) becomes
  an acid_web tile. During acid_flood, the player is immune to acid_web damage entirely.
  When acid_flood expires, all acid_web tiles the player created this activation
  simultaneously fire: each acid_web tile explodes as per Boiling Point (3x3 splash,
  5 + STS dmg, venom_dot, new acid tiles persist 5t), whether or not an enemy died on them.
  The player takes 50% reduced damage from the post-flood explosion (Exoskeleton still applies).
  Nuke level: this can dissolve an entire room if you walk through it during the 5-turn window.
  Balance: 1/floor, 5 turns, requires setup of L1 (acid_web) and ideally L3 for max payoff.
  The 5-turn walk-through-acid fantasy is deeply street: you just walked through a room
  dripping acid like nothing happened and then the whole place melted.

BALANCE NOTES
===========================================================================================

Tree A vs Tree B comparison:
  Tree A tops out at a floor-nuke (web_burst). High ceiling but requires setup time.
  Tree B tops out at sustained healing + constant poison. Lower ceiling, more consistent.
  A player mixing both L1-L2 from each tree gets venom on webbed enemies (excellent).

Spiderling DPS sanity check (Tree D):
  Base spiderling: 2-3 dmg/turn at speed 120 vs enemy speed 100 = fires ~17% more than player.
  L5 upgrade to 4-6 dmg with double vs webbed = 8-12 effective.
  With 3 spiderlings vs a webbed enemy: 24-36 dmg/turn, plus player melee ~10+.
  This is strong but requires 3+ active spiderlings and a webbed target — clear conditions.

Tree E self-damage exposure:
  acid_web: 3 dmg/turn. At 130 HP (CON 10), this is ~2.3% max HP per turn.
  Acid explosion (L3): 5 + STS = roughly 10-15 dmg in splash. One-time, avoidable.
  acid_flood + post-flood explosion: without Exoskeleton you'd take ~10-20 from your own webs.
  With Exoskeleton (50% reduction): ~5-10. At 130+ HP that's survivable for a payoff nuke.
  Recommend: Tree E as a high-risk optional path, not default. Synergizes best with high CON.

Cobweb tile interaction with other systems:
  Fire tiles + cobweb tile on same space: cobweb burns away (destroyed) after 2 turns.
    (Prevents Pyromania + Trapper from becoming trivially OP — webs burn.)
  Acid_web tiles + fire: acid_web burns away after 1 turn (acid is organic in this universe).
  Venom_pool tiles + fire: burn away immediately (venom is flammable, too bad).
  Cobweb tiles are NOT affected by the player's own weight (Tree C L4 "Crawl Space").

===========================================================================================
CROSS-TREE SYNERGIES
===========================================================================================

Best two-tree combos:
  A + B: Web traps the enemy, venom_dot ticks while it's stuck. Most natural pairing.
  A + D: Spiderlings attack webbed enemies (Tree D L5 double-damage). Grid + swarm.
  B + E: Necrotic venom pools + acid corrosion — enemies die leaving acid AND venom zones.
  C + A: Silk Spitter + permanent webs (Tree A L3) = movement creates lasting traps.
  C + D: Spider Run deposits webs while spiderlings rush from behind. True spider fantasy.

Three-tree builds (deep investment):
  A + B + E: "Chemist Arachnigga" — webs, acid webs, venom. Maximum hazard density.
  A + C + D: "Tactical Arachnigga" — web grid, mobility, summons. Requires strong CON to last.

Synergy with other NIGRL skills:
  Stabbing (Gouge stun): Stun + webbed on same target = near-permanent CC on one enemy.
  Catchin' Fades (Unfazed): extra swagger while enemies are stuck in webs = safe swag grind.
  Pyromania (Wildfire L5): fire spreading to adjacent ignites cobwebs, destroying them.
    This is a NEGATIVE interaction — warn players that fire destroys webs.
  Deep-Frying (Fry Shot): Fry Shot AoE + venom pool tiles = burn + poison zone combo.
  Jaywalking (Dash): pairs with Tree C L2 Silk Spitter (dash deposits cobweb on origin tile).
