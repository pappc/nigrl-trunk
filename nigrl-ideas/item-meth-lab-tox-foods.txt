METH LAB TOX-REMOVAL FOOD CONCEPTS
====================================
Design document for 5-6 new Meth Lab food items that remove toxicity, heal HP,
and grant a mechanically novel buff. Intended zone: "meth_lab" exclusively (or
"meth_lab" primary). All concepts designed to fit the declarative FOOD_DEFS
structure in foods.py with a new named effect type dispatched in item_effects.py
and implemented as an Effect subclass in effects.py.

Balance reference:
  - Standard food heal: 50 HP
  - Standard eat_length: 5-10 turns
  - Altoid: removes 50 tox (no heal, no buff)
  - Iron Lung strain best roll: removes ALL tox, heals tox*2+CON*2, temp DEF
  - Hot Cheetos: 5 turns to eat, +2 all stats / 50% ignite for 30 turns
  - Phase Walk (Jolly Rancher): 3 turns to eat, walk through walls 12 turns
  - Hard Boiled Egg: 2 turns to eat, revive at 50% HP once
  - Tox damage multiplier: 1.0 + (tox/100)^0.6 — at 100 tox: x1.58, at 200 tox: x2.0

Design philosophy:
  - The buff must interact with the Meth Lab's toxicity/radiation threat loop
  - Prefer buffs that create interesting decision points or emergent interactions
  - Avoid simple stat buffs (+X STR etc.) — the effect should DO something
  - The 10-turn eat window is the key risk; eating requires standing still
  - Tox-removal + heal means these are crisis items; buff should reward the risk

CONCEPT 1: MYSTERY MEAT SURPRISE
==============================================================================

Name:       Mystery Meat Surprise
Food ID:    mystery_meat_surprise
Char:       f
Color:      (160, 80, 60)    # dark reddish-brown, unappetizing
Zones:      ["meth_lab"]
Eat length: 8 turns
Skill:      Munching
Weight:     6

Flavor:
  A styrofoam container of unidentified meat swimming in brackish liquid.
  Nobody in the Meth Lab knows what's in it. Nobody asks. The man who sells it
  goes by "Glands." It smells like chemicals and regret and somehow tastes fine.

Effects on completion:
  1. heal: 40 HP
  2. remove_toxicity: 80
  3. mystery_meat_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Tox Immunity Window" (effect id: "tox_immunity_window")

  Mechanical description:
    For 15 turns, ALL incoming toxicity gain is blocked entirely
    (100% tox resistance, not just resistance — a hard immunity, piercing nothing).
    Additionally, when a hit would DEAL toxicity to the player during this window,
    the tox is reflected back: the attacker gains that tox instead (or the hazard
    source is ignored). This is a one-way mirror for tox only.

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass ToxImmunityWindowEffect (id="tox_immunity_window")
    - Override modify_incoming_damage: no — tox gain is not combat damage
    - Need a hook in add_toxicity() in combat.py: check if player has
      "tox_immunity_window" active; if so, skip gain and instead call
      add_toxicity() on the attacker entity (if source entity provided)
    - add_toxicity() already exists as a central function — add optional
      `source_entity` param; if effect active, redirect tox to source
    - Duration: 15 turns (short enough to feel precious, long enough to matter)
    - Message on apply: "You feel chemically armored!"
    - Message on expire: "Your tox immunity fades."

  Why it's interesting:
    The Meth Lab constantly drips toxicity from environmental sources, enemy
    attacks, and AoE. A 15-turn hard window lets you wade through a toxic room
    without kiting. The reflect mechanic punishes enemies that rely on on-hit
    toxicity (Covid-26, Toxic Slug, Purger) by turning their tox attacks back
    on themselves. Creates a "fight back with their own weapon" power fantasy.

  Balance:
    15 turns is roughly 1 room's worth of aggressive play. Not renewable
    mid-fight (8-turn eat). Tox reflect on mobs is situational (only tox-dealing
    enemies). Comparable upside to Alco-Seltzer tox resist, but trades the
    debuff immunity for the reflect component.

  Edge cases:
    - Environmental grease tiles add NO tox — reflect doesn't apply there
    - Fire hazards don't deal tox — unaffected
    - If player has normal tox resistance AND this effect, immunity takes
      precedence (effect check runs first in add_toxicity)
    - On expire, do NOT remove existing tox that accumulated before eating

CONCEPT 2: GUTTER NACHOS
==============================================================================

Name:       Gutter Nachos
Food ID:    gutter_nachos
Char:       f
Color:      (220, 180, 60)   # tortilla yellow
Zones:      ["meth_lab"]
Eat length: 6 turns
Skill:      Deep-Frying
Weight:     7

Flavor:
  A tin tray of chips pulled from a dumpster behind a gas station, drenched in
  liquid cheese product of unknown vintage and sprinkled with something green.
  The Meth Lab's best chef, a man named Boodrow, made these. He did not survive
  the experience.

Effects on completion:
  1. heal: 50 HP
  2. remove_toxicity: 60
  3. gutter_nachos_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Tox Recycling" (effect id: "tox_recycling")

  Mechanical description:
    For 20 turns: whenever the player would gain toxicity, instead of adding
    it to their tox pool, 50% of it is converted to temporary HP (max temp HP
    pool: 40). The other 50% goes to tox as normal.
    Formula: converted = incoming_tox * 0.5; temp_hp += converted (cap 40).
    The temp HP decays at 2 per turn (normal temp HP decay rate if one exists,
    or a fixed 2/turn drain on the temp HP pool from this effect only).

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass ToxRecyclingEffect (id="tox_recycling")
    - Hook in add_toxicity(): if "tox_recycling" active, halve the incoming
      amount before applying, then add halved_amount to entity.temp_hp
      capped at 40 via min(entity.temp_hp + halved, 40)
    - Temp HP decay: add a tick() method that does entity.temp_hp -= 2 each
      turn IF entity.temp_hp > 0 (only tox_recycling-sourced temp HP; or
      just drain shared temp HP pool by 2/turn — simpler and acceptable)
    - Duration: 20 turns
    - Message on apply: "Your body starts converting toxins to vitality!"
    - Temp HP gain message: "Tox converted to temp HP! (+X)"

  Why it's interesting:
    Completely flips the risk model of the Meth Lab. Instead of toxicity being
    pure punishment, it becomes a resource that fills a temp HP buffer.
    Enemies that deal heavy tox (Purger, Covid-26) actually help you build a
    buffer. Incentivizes staying near toxic sources rather than retreating.
    Creates a gameplay loop where high-tox environments become SAFER with this
    buff active. Pairs beautifully with Swamp Gas strain and Chemical Warfare.
    The decay means you burn through the buffer if you stop taking tox hits.

  Balance:
    20 turns, 40 temp HP max — that's roughly 2 big tox hits converted.
    The 50/50 split means tox still accumulates, just slower. Not infinite:
    temp HP decays at 2/turn so you burn through 40 in 20 turns naturally
    (roughly matches the effect duration). Eat window of 6 turns is short
    enough that this might be eaten pre-fight in a corridor.

  Edge cases:
    - If player has tox resistance: resistance applies first, then recycling
      applies to the post-resistance amount
    - No interaction with pierce_resistance — same as normal tox gain path
    - Temp HP from this effect uses the same entity.temp_hp field as all
      other temp HP sources (consistent with existing system)

CONCEPT 3: PRISON PRUNO
==============================================================================

Name:       Prison Pruno

Char:       f
Color:      (120, 60, 100)   # murky purple
Zones:      ["meth_lab"]
Eat length: 4 turns
Skill:      Alcoholism
Weight:     5

Flavor:
  A zip-lock bag of fermented fruit juice, bread, and suffering, carefully aged
  in a toilet for 14 days by someone who owed you a favor. Technically food.
  Technically drink. Technically illegal. Tastes like raisins had a funeral.

Effects on completion:
  1. heal: 30 HP
  2. remove_toxicity: 50
  3. prison_pruno_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Scavenger's Eye" (effect id: "scavengers_eye")

  Mechanical description:
    For 25 turns: enemies killed while this buff is active have a 40% chance
    to drop an extra consumable item from a weighted table of:
      - Altoid (remove 50 tox)          — weight 4
      - Light ammo stack (5)             — weight 3
      - Random strain (meth_lab table)   — weight 2
      - Instant Ramen                    — weight 1
    Additionally, the player can detect all items on the floor even through
    unexplored tiles (item omniscience: items appear as their char on the map
    regardless of FOV or exploration state, for the buff's duration).

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass ScavengersEyeEffect (id="scavengers_eye")
    - On-kill hook: override on_kill() or check in engine's entity death handler
      for "scavengers_eye" effect on player; roll 40% chance, then spawn
      a random item from the weighted table at the corpse's position
    - Item omniscience: in render.py, when drawing map tiles, if player has
      "scavengers_eye" effect, render ALL items (not just FOV/explored) using
      their entity char at dimmed color (e.g., multiply color channels by 0.5)
    - Weighted drop table: implement as a local list of (weight, item_id) pairs
      in the effect's on_kill method or a helper dict in effects.py
    - Duration: 25 turns
    - Message on apply: "Your eyes sharpen. You can sense every scrap on this floor."
    - Message on expire: "Your hunter's instinct fades."

  Why it's interesting:
    The two components synergize: you're incentivized to actively hunt enemies
    during the buff (for drops) while the item radar lets you plan a safe route
    to grab resources. In a resource-scarce Meth Lab, finding a hidden Altoid or
    ammo pile mid-combat is massively impactful. Rewards aggressive play and
    exploration literacy. The 40% drop rate on a 25-turn window against typical
    enemy density means you'll probably net 3-6 free items if you push hard.

  Balance:
    4-turn eat makes this a fast tactical pivot. 30 HP heal and 50 tox removal
    are below average for the eat time (trade-off for the powerful buff). The
    drop table is deliberately low-value items: this isn't a loot explosion,
    it's a survivability sustain system. Item radar is limited to the current
    floor — no floor-transition persistence.

  Edge cases:
    - Summon kills (spider hatchlings, mirror_entity copies) trigger the on_kill
      hook — should they count? Safest: only trigger for player-kill events,
      not player-adjacent ally kills. Filter: entity_type == "monster" and
      killed_by == "player" or killed_by_entity == engine.player
    - Item spawned by drop must not stack-spawn in a wall tile: use dungeon
      helper to find nearest open floor tile if corpse is on a wall (shouldn't
      happen, but defensive)
    - Item omniscience rendering: only draw items (entity_type == "item") in
      dimmed color; do not reveal enemies or hazards

CONCEPT 4: CHALUPA SUPREME (METH LAB EDITION)
==============================================================================

Name:       Chalupa Supreme
Food ID:    chalupa_supreme
Char:       f
Color:      (200, 140, 60)   # golden brown
Zones:      ["meth_lab"]
Eat length: 10 turns
Skill:      Munching
Weight:     4

Flavor:
  A deep-fried tortilla shell that's somehow still crispy despite having been
  in a trash bag for an indeterminate amount of time. Stuffed with seasoned
  meat, sour cream, and what appears to be a crystalline substance that is
  definitely not cheese. Tastes incredible. You feel wrong for enjoying it.

Effects on completion:
  1. heal: 60 HP
  2. remove_toxicity: 100
  3. chalupa_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Tox Threshold Spike" (effect id: "tox_threshold")

  Mechanical description:
    For 30 turns: the player's effective tox for the damage formula is treated
    as 0, regardless of actual tox level. This means the tox damage multiplier
    is locked at 1.0 — the player takes no extra damage from their toxicity.
    Additionally, if the player's actual tox is above 150 when this buff
    expires, they immediately receive 20 HP of restoration (the body "purging"
    the stored tox in a final flush).

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass ToxThresholdEffect (id="tox_threshold")
    - Hook in combat.py's tox damage multiplier calculation:
        if player has "tox_threshold" effect, skip the multiplier (return 1.0)
    - Check is in the tox_damage_multiplier function/section in combat.py
    - On expire: check engine.player.toxicity >= 150; if so, heal player 20 HP
    - The buff does NOT remove tox — tox continues to accumulate normally.
      It just masks its damage penalty.
    - Message on apply: "You feel chemically insulated. Tox can't slow you down."
    - Message on expire (low tox): "Your chemical insulation fades."
    - Message on expire (high tox): "Your system purges! (+20 HP)"

  Why it's interesting:
    This is the "glass cannon tox" fantasy: you deliberately let toxicity
    build to dangerous levels knowing the damage penalty is suspended, then
    the buff expiring forces a hard decision — do you keep fighting at full
    tox efficiency, or burn more resources to reduce tox before the shield
    drops? The expire heal at 150+ tox creates a counterintuitive incentive
    to NOT reduce your toxicity while the buff is active (so you hit the 150
    threshold for the heal bonus). Pairs explosively with Swamp Gas strain
    (which gains tox intentionally) and Chemical Warfare abilities.

  Balance:
    10-turn eat makes this the highest-commitment food. 60 HP and 100 tox
    removal are above average — you need the buff to compensate for standing
    still 10 turns in a dangerous zone. 30-turn duration is long but it ONLY
    masks tox penalty; the actual tox still accumulates and becomes lethal
    when buff ends if unchecked. The expire heal is a safety valve, not a
    power spike. Net effect: this buys you 30 turns to play at high tox
    without penalty, but tox management is still required.

  Edge cases:
    - If player is already at tox=0 when buff expires: no expire heal (below 150)
    - Tox still kills via radiation interactions or other tox-check systems —
      only the combat damage multiplier is bypassed, not actual tox number
    - If buff is dispelled early (debuff removal): no expire heal
    - Stacking with alco_seltzer_tox_resist: both can be active; this bypasses
      the multiplier entirely; tox_resist reduces incoming gain; different layers

CONCEPT 5: SLOPPY JOE DELUXE
==============================================================================

Name:       Sloppy Joe Deluxe
Food ID:    sloppy_joe
Char:       f
Color:      (180, 70, 30)    # saucy red-brown
Zones:      ["meth_lab"]
Eat length: 7 turns
Skill:      Munching
Weight:     6

Flavor:
  A bun barely containing a meat sauce that has been simmering on a hot plate
  in an abandoned office for three days. Someone left a sticky note on it that
  says "DO NOT EAT" but the note is three weeks old and you are very hungry.
  Served with a side of resolution.

Effects on completion:
  1. heal: 45 HP
  2. remove_toxicity: 70
  3. sloppy_joe_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Danger Sense" (effect id: "danger_sense")

  Mechanical description:
    For 20 turns: the player gains awareness of enemy toxicity levels and
    is alerted to "tox aura" threats before stepping into them.
    Mechanically:
    a) ALL enemies within FOV have their current toxicity displayed as a
       small indicator next to their character on the map (e.g., a "~" glyph
       rendered adjacent to any enemy with tox > 30).
    b) The player receives a message warning ("A Purger is primed!") when any
       visible enemy with a tox-based special attack enters attack range and has
       tox > 50. This warning fires once per enemy per buff activation.
    c) The player gains +2 to FOV radius (effectively gains sight range boost).

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass DangerSenseEffect (id="danger_sense")
    - Part (a): in render.py, when drawing enemy characters in FOV, check if
      player has "danger_sense" active; if so, draw a "~" in tox-amber color
      (200, 180, 60) one tile to the right of any enemy with toxicity > 30.
      If that tile is occupied, skip the indicator (don't overwrite other chars).
    - Part (b): in ai.py or engine.py, during the enemy AI turn, when an enemy
      with a tox special attack (check enemy_type in a whitelist: "purger",
      "toxic_slug", "covid_26") moves within attack range of the player, check
      player "danger_sense" and if entity.toxicity > 50, add a warning message.
      Track warned enemies via a set on the effect object to avoid spam.
    - Part (c): Apply +2 to FOV radius in apply(), remove in expire(), using
      engine's FOV radius modifier (same approach as read_the_room_active or
      eagle_eye). Recompute FOV on apply.
    - Duration: 20 turns
    - Color for "~" indicator: (200, 140, 40) — amber, visible but not alarming

  Why it's interesting:
    The Meth Lab's tox enemies are terrifying because their attack telegraphing
    is weak. Danger Sense makes enemy toxicity visible as information — suddenly
    you know the Purger next to you is maxed out and about to unload. It shifts
    the gameplay from reactive (take the hit, then manage tox) to proactive
    (see the threat, plan a response). The FOV bonus synergizes by letting you
    see more enemies to read. This is a scouting/information buff in a game
    where information is usually very limited, making it feel qualitatively
    different from any combat stat buff.

  Balance:
    Mostly informational — it does not reduce damage, only helps you avoid it.
    The FOV bonus (+2) is the only direct mechanical boost. Its power is
    entirely skill-dependent: a player who can act on the information gets
    enormous value; a player who can't still gets 45 HP and 70 tox removal.
    7-turn eat is a moderate commitment. No interaction with permadeath to worry
    about — it expires cleanly with just the FOV reversal.

  Edge cases:
    - The "~" indicator should NOT be drawn if the tile to the right is a wall,
      another entity, or the player. Fallback: skip drawing (no crash).
    - FOV radius: apply +2 on apply(), subtract 2 on expire(). If multiple FOV
      modifiers exist, they stack additively (same pattern as read_the_room).
    - Warning message spam: use a set on the effect instance tracking
      warned_entity_ids; clear it only if the effect is re-applied.
    - On floor change (floor_duration=False on this one): the effect expires
      naturally when its turn counter runs out, not floor-based.

Flavor:

Effects on completion:

  2. remove_toxicity: 150
  3. grandmas_casserole_buff  (new effect type)

BUFF — "Tox Insurance" (effect id: "tox_insurance")

  Mechanical description:
    A passive floor-duration buff. While active, if the player's toxicity
    ever hits 200 or above (a threshold that normally means extreme danger),
    this effect automatically triggers a one-time emergency purge:
      - Immediately remove 100 tox
      - Heal player for 25 HP
      - Display message: "Grandma's cooking saves you!"
    After the emergency purge triggers, the insurance is consumed (effect ends).
    If the player never hits 200 tox, the buff persists until end of floor.
    The buff is not otherwise visible except as a status indicator.

  Implementation notes:
    - New Effect subclass ToxInsuranceEffect (id="tox_insurance")
    - floor_duration=True (persists until floor change if not consumed)
    - No tick() passive behavior; the trigger logic lives in add_toxicity()
      in combat.py: after tox is added, check if player has "tox_insurance"
      effect AND player.toxicity >= 200; if so, call the emergency purge,
      then remove the effect from player.status_effects
    - Emergency purge: remove_toxicity(100) + heal(25) + message + effect.expire()
    - The effect should display as a small icon/indicator in status — name:
      "Tox Insurance" with description "Auto-purge 100 tox if you hit 200"
    - On apply: "Grandma's love protects you. (Tox Insurance active)"
    - On expire without trigger: "Grandma's blessing fades." (floor-end expire)
    - On trigger: "Grandma's cooking saves you! (100 tox purged, +25 HP)"
    - eat_length is 12 turns — longest of all concepts. This is intentional:
      it is a premium safety item. 80 HP + 150 tox are the best flat numbers
      here, and the insurance is stackable in value with careful play.

  Why it's interesting:
    This is a "safety net" mechanic that changes how risk is calculated.
    Knowing you have Tox Insurance lets you push into a tox-heavy room with
    confidence — if things go wrong and you spike to 200 tox, the safety net
    fires. This shifts decision-making from conservative to aggressive because
    the downside is bounded. It also creates a mini-game around the trigger:
    experienced players might DELIBERATELY let tox creep to 190 (burning
    resources carefully) then use the insurance as a free 100-tox purge exactly
    when they need it most. The floor-duration means you carry the peace of mind
    for the whole floor, not just 20 turns.

  Balance:
    12-turn eat is the biggest time investment — you're committed. 80 HP and
    150 tox removal on completion are generous; this is clearly a "premium"
    food. The insurance being one-time prevents it from being an infinite
    safety blanket. Low weight (3) = rare spawn. Not worth crafting shortcuts
    around since you can't guarantee the trigger fires at the right moment.

  Edge cases:
    - If player is ALREADY at 200+ tox when they finish eating, the insurance
      does NOT retroactively fire — the check only runs inside add_toxicity()
      on new tox gain. This is intentional: eating it when already critical
      still gives the 80 HP + 150 tox removal, just no insurance bonus.
    - On floor transition while active: effect expires cleanly via floor_duration
      without triggering the emergency purge.
    - Multiple casseroles: eating a second one while insurance is still active
      would normally re-apply the effect. Simplest handling: re-applying just
      resets the effect (it's already there); don't double-stack.
    - save_system: floor_duration effects serialize correctly already.

RANKING BY DESIGN INTEREST (subjective)
==============================================================================

  1. Gutter Nachos (Tox Recycling) — most novel mechanic, flips risk model
  2. Grandma's Casserole (Tox Insurance) — best strategic depth, floor-duration
  3. Chalupa Supreme (Tox Threshold) — best build synergy with Swamp Gas/ChemWar
  4. Prison Pruno (Scavenger's Eye) — most fun in play, dual utility
  5. Mystery Meat Surprise (Tox Immunity Window) — cleanest implementation
  6. Sloppy Joe (Danger Sense) — most informational, unique information buff

  If only implementing 2-3: recommend Gutter Nachos + Grandma's Casserole
  as they cover different strategic roles (active vs. passive protection).
