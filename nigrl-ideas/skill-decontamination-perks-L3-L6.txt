DECONTAMINATION SKILL — PERKS L3 THROUGH L6
NIGRL Design Document
======================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document designs six perk options for Decontamination levels 3-6,
replacing the placeholder L3 perk ("Fallout") and filling L4-L6. The
existing L1-L2 perks are kept as-is:

  L1 — Radiant: +30% rad resist, +2 TOL, +2 STR
  L2 — Emission: set all visible enemies' rad to yours (1/floor); irradiated
       enemies take rad//50 damage/tick and decay 5 rad/tick

SKILL IDENTITY RECAP
--------------------
Decontamination is the "rad removal" skill. Its XP sources are: radiation
resisted (1 XP/point via add_radiation) and radiation removed (2 XP/point via
remove_radiation). The Skywalker OG strain fuels the fantasy: smoke it, purge
rad from yourself, and each 25 rad removed awards +2 temp STR via Force
Sensitive. The L2 Emission dumps your rad onto enemies so they irradiate each
other to death.

The design problem is that L1-L2 are both "pass rad around" mechanics. The
tree needs L3-L6 to answer: what does a high-level Decontamination player DO
with their radiation beyond sharing it?

The answer this doc proposes: you consecrate the ground. You leave irradiated
wake. You cycle rad up and purge it for burst damage or burst heal. You become
a moving hazard zone.

The tree's verb set by endgame:
  - Emit (share rad with enemies, L2)
  - Consecrate (place irradiated ground tiles, L3 option A or L4)
  - Purge-surge (convert your own rad removal into a burst, L3 option B)
  - Absorb (drink rad from the floor or enemies into yourself, L5)
  - Radiant Wake (leave rad tiles passively as you walk, L6)

This is NOT a tank build (White Power owns that). It is a mobile skirmisher
that treats radiation as a resource to cycle in and out, leaving hazardous
terrain behind. It pairs naturally with Emission (dump rad onto enemies) and
Force Sensitive (profit off purging it back off yourself).

LEVEL 3 OPTIONS
======================================================================

L3 OPTION A — "CONSECRATE"
Type: activated ability (ChargeType.PER_FLOOR, 2 charges)
New ability id: "consecrate"
Target type: SELF (no targeting UI — fires on player position)
Cost: 50 radiation (paid with pierce_resistance=False so resistance applies)

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Spend 50 rad to irradiate the floor beneath you and all tiles within radius 1
  (a 3x3 patch, 9 tiles max, clipped to walkable tiles). Each affected tile
  becomes a "rad_tile" for 20 turns.

  Rad tile behavior (mirrors grease_tiles pattern in engine.py):
    - Enemies that end their turn on a rad tile gain +20 radiation.
    - The player standing on a rad tile gains +5 radiation per turn (not blocked
      by rad resistance, but generates Decontamination XP as if resisted: XP =
      5 * resistance_pct / 100 rounded, since it technically bypasses). This
      creates a minor cost/benefit: you laid it, you can still be in it.
    - Tiles render as "+" character in yellow-green (fg=(160, 255, 80),
      bg=(25, 40, 10)) when visible; dimmed when explored-not-visible.
    - Tile data structure: dungeon.rad_tiles: dict[tuple, int] — (x,y) -> turns_remaining
      (same pattern as dungeon.grease_tiles).

  Passive bonus at L3: +15% rad resistance added on top of Radiant's 30%, so
  cumulative 45%.

VISUAL / RENDERING:
  Add dungeon.rad_tiles check in render.py's tile loop, priority between
  grease_tiles and spray_paint (grease > rad_tile > spray_paint). Render as
  "+" in yellow-green when visible, muted yellow-green when explored.

IMPLEMENTATION HOOKS:
  - Add dungeon.rad_tiles: dict = {} in dungeon.py alongside grease_tiles.
  - In engine.py's _run_energy_loop, after grease tile processing (step 4c),
    add step 4d: rad tile tick — for each entity on a rad tile: if enemy, add
    +20 rad via add_radiation(engine, enemy, 20, from_player=True); if player,
    add_radiation(engine, player, 5, pierce_resistance=True). Decrement
    rad_tiles timers. Remove expired entries. Grant Nuclear Research XP for
    enemy rad gained (handled inside add_radiation already).
  - Add rad_tiles to save_system.py dungeon serialization.
  - _execute_consecrate: compute 3x3 walkable tiles around player, set each in
    dungeon.rad_tiles to 20 (or refresh if already present). Deduct 50 rad via
    remove_radiation (this fires Force Sensitive on_rad_lost, giving STR if
    active — a natural synergy). Cost remove means you want to have 50+ rad
    before consecrating.

BALANCE:
  2 charges/floor. Each charge dumps 20 rad per enemy on the tile per turn,
  for 20 turns — potentially 400 rad total per enemy if they stand there, which
  via Emission's irradiated DOT (rad//50 = 8 damage/tick at 400 rad) is
  meaningful zonal damage. The 50 rad cost is the loop: you use Skywalker OG
  to build rad, then consecrate (which itself removes the 50 via remove_radiation
  and thus fires Force Sensitive), leaving a live zone.

SYNERGIES:
  - With Force Sensitive: consecrating removes 50 rad, which crosses the 25
    threshold twice for +4 temp STR — you get offensive upside from placing
    tiles.
  - With Emission (L2): irradiated enemies will walk into your consecrated
    zones and receive even more rad stacks, accelerating their DOT.
  - With Nuclear Research Rad Bomb: two different zone-control abilities with
    distinct characters — Rad Bomb is a placed timed explosive, Consecrate is a
    persistent aura patch.

WHY THIS IS UNIQUE:
  No other skill places persistent irradiating ground tiles. Grease tiles do
  fire damage to enemies; rad tiles do radiation buildup — a different class of
  hazard that synergizes with the existing irradiated DOT system.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate — 2/floor. Spend 50 rad: irradiate the 3x3 area beneath you
   (20t). Enemies on rad tiles gain +20 rad/turn. +15% rad resistance."

L3 OPTION B — "PURGE SURGE"
Type: passive
New engine field: engine.purge_surge_accumulated (int, 0; cleared on floor)
New PlayerStats field: player_stats.purge_surge_active (bool, False)

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Whenever you lose radiation by any means (remove_radiation is called on the
  player), track the cumulative amount lost this floor. At every 50-rad
  milestone, your NEXT melee attack deals bonus damage equal to (rad_lost_this_
  milestone * 0.4) rounded, capped at 30 bonus damage per surge. The bonus
  damage applies only once and then resets the "pending" counter for that
  milestone.

  Separately: while you are above 100 rad, each melee hit applies 10 rad to
  the struck enemy (this is new — Fallout's 20% irradiate at 100 rad replaced
  by a softer, always-on version tied to carrying charge).

  The "next attack deals bonus" is signalled by a status icon / message: "Purge
  Surge ready! (+N bonus dmg)".

  Passive bonus: +1 STR (smaller than Fallout's +2 since the damage upside is
  passive and potentially larger over a full floor of cycling).

IMPLEMENTATION HOOKS:
  - Add engine.purge_surge_accumulated = 0 (reset on floor start, not on surge
    proc — tracks cumulative for milestone calculation).
  - Add engine.purge_surge_pending_dmg = 0 (cleared after next melee hit).
  - In remove_radiation (combat.py), after the XP line: if entity is
    engine.player, increment engine.purge_surge_accumulated by removed; check
    if new total // 50 > old total // 50; if so, compute bonus =
    min(30, int(removed * 0.4)) and set engine.purge_surge_pending_dmg += bonus,
    emit a message.
  - In combat.py's player melee attack resolution, after base damage computed:
    if engine.purge_surge_pending_dmg > 0 and Decontamination level >= 3,
    add engine.purge_surge_pending_dmg to hit; reset to 0; display surge proc.
  - Above-100-rad irradiate on melee: in player melee hit resolution, if player
    rad >= 100 and Decontamination >= 3, call add_radiation(engine, defender,
    10, from_player=True).
  - Save/load: purge_surge_accumulated and purge_surge_pending_dmg in engine
    state.

BALANCE:
  Over a full floor using Skywalker OG twice (removing ~60 rad each time),
  you'd cross 50-rad milestones twice and 100-rad twice: roughly 4 surge procs
  for ~15-25 bonus damage each, total ~60-100 bonus melee damage across a floor.
  That's equivalent to ~5-8 bonus weapon hits — meaningful but not dominant.
  The persistent on-hit irradiation at 100+ rad stacks with Emission's DOT.

SYNERGIES:
  - With Force Sensitive: both proc off remove_radiation — Purge Surge turns
    the same act (purging rad) into both an STR bonus AND a queued damage bonus.
    High-cycle play gets both benefits simultaneously.
  - With Skywalker OG: the strain's primary use (removing rad) now has two
    offensive payoffs instead of one.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Purge Surge — Passive. Every 50 rad lost this floor: next melee hit deals
   +N bonus damage (N = rad_lost * 0.4, max 30). While above 100 rad: melee
   hits irradiate target for 10 rad. +1 STR."

RECOMMENDATION NOTE:
  Consecrate (Option A) introduces a wholly new mechanic verb (placing terrain).
  Purge Surge (Option B) is more combat-fluent and easier to implement. They
  have different feelings: Consecrate is deliberate and positional; Purge Surge
  is reactive and automatic. Option A is the stronger design pick for build
  identity; Option B is the safer gameplay pick for players who don't want to
  think about terrain.

LEVEL 4 — "HALF-LIFE AURA"
======================================================================

Type: activated ability (ChargeType.PER_FLOOR, 3 charges)
New ability id: "half_life_aura"
Target type: SELF

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Activate to gain the Half-Life Aura buff for 15 turns. While active:
    - Any enemy that moves within 2 tiles of you gains +15 rad/turn they remain
      in range (checked during the enemy's turn, not on every engine tick).
    - You passively drain 3 rad/turn from yourself while the aura is up. This
      rad loss fires Force Sensitive (on_rad_lost) if the buff is active —
      meaning the aura literally charges your Force Sensitive stacks over time.
    - Visual: player's character pulses with a rad-green tint (SDL overlay glow;
      or simpler: status effect icon shows in the effects bar as "AURA").

  The aura does NOT deal direct damage — it irradiates, which the existing
  irradiated DOT (from Emission or any irradiated state) then handles. The
  design question answered: "what if I'm an irradiation source just by
  existing in a room?"

  Upgrade interaction at L4 if Consecrate (L3A) is taken: rad tiles now also
  deal +5 rad/turn to enemies instead of +20 (net: same rate, but slightly
  extended since the aura keeps enemies in range — NOTE: the two values are
  independent systems; consider instead that Consecrate + Half-Life Aura
  together mean enemies in rad zones near you are gaining up to +35 rad/turn,
  which is significant zone denial).

  If Purge Surge (L3B) taken: Half-Life Aura's 3 rad/turn self-drain counts
  toward purge_surge_accumulated, generating surge procs passively over the
  15-turn window (~3-4 surge procs just from the aura's drain over 15 turns if
  they cluster at 50-rad milestones — this is a passive bonus loop).

NEW ENGINE STATE:
  engine.half_life_aura_active = False (track for aura passive logic)
  engine.half_life_aura_turns_remaining = 0

  Alternatively: implement as an Effect subclass "half_life_aura_effect" with:
    - tick(): self-drain 3 rad (remove_radiation), check enemy proximity
      (iterate dungeon.entities within 2 Chebyshev distance of player, add_radiation
      +15 to each alive enemy, from_player=True), decrement duration.
    - expire(): clear aura, message.
  This is cleaner than engine flags and follows the existing effect pattern.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - _execute_half_life_aura(engine): apply_effect(engine.player, engine,
    "half_life_aura", duration=15). Message: "Half-Life Aura activated!"
  - HalfLifeAuraEffect(Effect): id="half_life_aura", category="buff".
    tick(): remove 3 rad from player (calls on_rad_lost on Force Sensitive if
    active), find enemies within Chebyshev 2 of player, add 15 rad each
    from_player=True.

BALANCE:
  3 charges/floor, 15 turns each = 45 aura-turns per floor. At average 2
  enemies in range for 8 turns per activation: +240 rad to those enemies per
  use. With Emission already having set them to high rad, this pushes them
  further past the DOT threshold. The self-drain of 45 rad across 15 turns is
  meaningful — you need to have been building rad via Skywalker OG or zone
  exposure. This rewards staying at medium-high radiation rather than always
  purging.

SYNERGIES:
  - Force Sensitive: 45 rad drained passively over 15 turns = almost 2 Force
    Sensitive threshold crossings (+4 temp STR) without smoking anything.
  - Emission (L2): set enemies to 100+ rad, then activate aura — aura pushes
    them past the mutation-dangerous 250+ threshold faster, and their irradiated
    DOT escalates.
  - Consecrate (L3A): position a rad zone, stand in it with the aura. Enemies
    now get +20 rad/turn from the tile AND +15 rad/turn from the aura AND the
    irradiated DOT from Emission. True zone denial.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Half-Life Aura — 3/floor. For 15 turns: enemies within 2 tiles gain
   +15 rad/turn. You drain 3 rad/turn (fuels Force Sensitive). +1 TOL."

LEVEL 5 — "SPONGE"
======================================================================

Type: activated ability (ChargeType.PER_FLOOR, 2 charges)
New ability id: "rad_sponge"
Target type: AOE_CIRCLE (radius 3, centered on player)

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Activate: absorb radiation from all irradiated enemies within radius 3. For
  each enemy in range:
    - Remove 50% of that enemy's current radiation.
    - Add half of the removed amount to your own radiation (rounded down).
      Example: enemy at 200 rad — you remove 100, you gain 50.
    - Award Decontamination XP for the rad removed from enemies (2 XP per point
      removed, same rate as self-remove, since you're doing the decontamination).

  After absorbing: if you now exceed 150 rad, you emit a Rad Pulse — deal
  (current_rad // 10) damage to all enemies within radius 2. This pulse fires
  once per Sponge activation, not per enemy absorbed. It is NOT a guaranteed
  bonus — it only triggers if the absorption pushed you over 150.

  Passive at L5: rad tiles (if Consecrate was taken) now also remove 5 rad/turn
  from enemies (in addition to adding 20 rad — net is +15 per turn). This means
  the tiles are siphoning rather than purely adding, which is thematically
  the "decontamination" fantasy: the zone is actively processing radiation.

  If Consecrate was NOT taken: passive instead grants +10% rad resistance
  (cumulative total: 55% if Radiant taken; 45% if only Radiant with Purge Surge
  path).

NEW ENGINE STATE:
  None — the absorb is a single fire operation. The Rad Pulse is an inline
  damage call in the execute function.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  _execute_rad_sponge(engine):
    player = engine.player
    visible = engine.dungeon.visible  # only visible enemies can be sponged
    monsters = [e for e in engine.dungeon.entities if e.entity_type == "monster"
                and e.alive and visible[e.y][e.x]]
    from engine import chebyshev_distance  # or however distance is computed
    in_range = [m for m in monsters if distance(player, m) <= 3 and m.radiation > 0]
    if not in_range:
        engine.messages.append("No irradiated enemies in range!")
        return False
    total_absorbed = 0
    for m in in_range:
        removed = m.radiation // 2
        m.radiation -= removed
        gained = removed // 2
        add_radiation(engine, player, gained, pierce_resistance=True)
        bksmt = engine.player_stats.effective_book_smarts
        engine.skills.gain_potential_exp("Decontamination", removed * 2, bksmt)
        total_absorbed += removed
    engine.messages.append(f"Sponge: absorbed {total_absorbed} rad from {len(in_range)} enemies!")
    # Rad Pulse check
    if player.radiation >= 150:
        pulse_dmg = player.radiation // 10
        for m in [e for e in engine.dungeon.entities if e.entity_type == "monster"
                  and e.alive and distance(player, e) <= 2]:
            deal_damage(engine, m, pulse_dmg)
        engine.messages.append(f"Rad Pulse! ({pulse_dmg} damage in radius 2)")
    return True

BALANCE:
  2 charges/floor. Requires enemies to already have radiation (set by Emission
  or Half-Life Aura or Consecrate). At its best — 3 enemies at 200 rad each:
  removes 300 total rad across enemies, gains 150 rad yourself, triggers pulse
  for 15 damage each in radius 2. That's a big swing. At its worst — no
  irradiated enemies — it literally does nothing (returns False).

  The dependency on setup (enemies must be irradiated) is the cost. Sponge
  rewards investing in the full skill tree: Emission sets them up, Consecrate
  or Half-Life Aura builds their rad further, then Sponge harvests it. Used
  alone on floor 3 with low-rad enemies it's a wasted action.

SYNERGIES:
  - Emission (L2) -> Consecrate or Half-Life Aura (L3/L4) -> Sponge (L5) is
    the core combo loop: set rad, grow rad, harvest rad. One Sponge use after
    Emission can pull 50% of your emitted rad back plus trigger a pulse.
  - Force Sensitive: adding radiation to yourself via Sponge does NOT trigger
    Force Sensitive (which only cares about rad removal). But the subsequent
    Skywalker OG smoke (to purge that absorbed rad) fires it. So Sponge loads
    up your rad resource for a future Force Sensitive activation.
  - Purge Surge (L3B): absorbing rad that you then purge via Skywalker crosses
    more 50-rad milestones. Sponge -> Skywalker OG is a burst cycle.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Sponge — 2/floor. Absorb 50% of each irradiated enemy's rad within 3 tiles
   (you gain half). If this pushes you above 150 rad: Rad Pulse (rad//10 dmg,
   radius 2). Rad tiles sponge 5 rad/turn from enemies (if Consecrate known)."

LEVEL 6 — "RADIANT WAKE"
======================================================================

Type: passive

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Every tile you walk away from leaves a Radiant footprint (rad tile with 6
  turns duration) IF your current radiation is 75 or above.

  Footprint behavior:
    - Same rad_tile system as Consecrate uses (dungeon.rad_tiles).
    - Enemies walking onto a footprint tile gain +10 rad (instead of Consecrate's
      +20/turn — these are smaller, shorter-lived).
    - Duration: 6 turns. At your base speed that's roughly 4-5 moves before
      the oldest tile decays.
    - If Consecrate is known: Radiant Wake tiles additionally generate 2
      Decontamination XP per enemy that steps on them (XP for "decontaminating"
      via passive irradiation — flavor stretch but mechanically motivates leveling).

  Condition: only generates tiles when player rad >= 75. This is a meaningful
  threshold — you need to be "charged up" to leave a wake. Below 75, nothing.
  Above 75, every move leaves a trail.

  Passive stat line: +2 STR, +10% rad resistance (cumulative total now 55%
  from Radiant alone; with Pure's eventual scaling, higher still).

  Interaction with dungeon.rad_tiles: tiles placed by Wake use shorter duration
  (6t) vs Consecrate's (20t). Distinguish in storage: they're the same dict,
  same logic, just created with shorter values. No separate system needed.

NEW ENGINE STATE:
  engine.player_stats.radiant_wake_active (bool, derived from Decontamination
  level >= 6 — no explicit flag needed; check in handle_move).

  OR: In engine.handle_move, after successful player move, check:
    if engine.skills.get("Decontamination").level >= 6 and engine.player.radiation >= 75:
        old_pos = (old_x, old_y)
        if engine.dungeon.tiles[old_y][old_x] == TILE_FLOOR:
            engine.dungeon.rad_tiles[old_pos] = max(
                engine.dungeon.rad_tiles.get(old_pos, 0), 6
            )
  This is 4 lines in handle_move — extremely low implementation cost.

BALANCE:
  Active whenever rad >= 75, which Skywalker OG builds to easily. The tiles are
  short-lived and individually weaker than Consecrate's (6t, +10 rad vs 20t,
  +20 rad/turn). But they're automatic and create interesting tactical play:
    - Kiting enemies through your own wake irradiates them incrementally.
    - Retreat paths through old rooms leave lingering radiation that affects
      any enemy that follows.
    - At 75+ rad, walking in circles before a fight "primes" a room.

  The +2 STR is the mechanical reward for the stat-hungry player who hasn't
  been getting much STR from L3-L5 (which were ability/passive focused). The
  cumulative rad resist at 55% means incoming rad damage (from nuclear enemies
  and Meth Lab hazards) is well-mitigated.

SYNERGIES:
  - All of them: Radiant Wake is the capstone that makes every prior investment
    pay off more. More rad-tiles on the board means Emission's AOE irradiation
    stacks further, Half-Life Aura enemies encounter more tiles, Sponge has more
    irradiated targets to harvest from.
  - Particularly strong with Consecrate: you lay a 3x3 zone, kite through it,
    and your Wake extends the radiation perimeter around that zone.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Radiant Wake — Passive. While above 75 rad: every tile you leave behind
   becomes a rad tile (6t, +10 rad/turn to enemies). +2 STR, +10% rad resist."

RECOMMENDED PERK SELECTION SUMMARY
======================================================================

  L3: Consecrate (Option A) — introduces terrain mechanic, core to build identity
      [Alt: Purge Surge (Option B) — simpler, purely melee-fluent version]
  L4: Half-Life Aura — personal irradiation field, rewards staying at med-high rad
  L5: Sponge — harvest enemies' rad; loop closer with Emission + Consecrate
  L6: Radiant Wake — capstone passive that makes all prior tiles more relevant

This arc creates a build that:
  - Floors 1-2: Resist rad and share it (Radiant + Emission)
  - Floor 3 onwards: Lay terrain, cycle rad through Force Sensitive + Skywalker OG
  - Mid-game: Add a personal aura and start harvesting rad from enemies
  - Late-game: Walking itself becomes a weapon when charged

The cycle loop in full build:
  1. Build rad via Skywalker OG or Half-Life Aura self-drain or Meth Lab exposure
  2. Emission -> sets enemy rad to yours
  3. Half-Life Aura -> pushes enemy rad higher while you drain yours (double purpose)
  4. Consecrate -> leave rad zones (costs 50 rad, fires Force Sensitive)
  5. Sponge -> harvest enemy rad, trigger Rad Pulse, reload your rad pool
  6. Skywalker OG -> purge remaining rad for Force Sensitive STR, Purge Surge procs
  7. Walk away -> Radiant Wake trails irradiate pursuers

======================================================================
BALANCE BENCHMARKS — COMPARISON AGAINST PEER SKILLS
======================================================================

White Power L3-L6 offensive output:
  - L5 Absolution: 3/floor, 15-turn toxic discharge -> deals tox// to 2 random
    enemies. High damage but requires high tox resource.
  - L6 Immaculate: Purity cap doubled. More defensive scaling.

Chemical Warfare L3-L6 offensive output:
  - L3 Acid Meltdown: 25 tox cost, halves move, kills create acid pools.
  - L5 Toxic Shell: consume tox as temp HP, nova on break. Very high damage ceiling.

Nuclear Research L3-L6:
  - L4 Half-Life Mark: placed detonation, 2 charges. High but conditional.
  - L5 Enrichment: stacks for boosted detonations. Deliberate setup.

Decontamination vs these:
  - Consecrate + Sponge + Radiant Wake is lower burst damage ceiling than
    Toxic Shell or Enrichment detonations, which is appropriate. Decontamination's
    strength is area denial, DoT stacking via Emission, and resource cycling
    (Force Sensitive STR gains). It wins on sustained zonal control, not single
    burst kills.
  - Half-Life Aura's passive rad feed to enemies in melee range is comparable
    to Toxic Frenzy's always-on melee bonus — both scale with the player being
    in dangerous proximity. The risk/reward is similar.

The skill is intentionally mid-tier on raw damage, high-tier on area disruption
and resource self-sustainability.


======================================================================
POTENTIAL EDGE CASES
======================================================================

1. Rad tiles persist after floor transition: solve by clearing dungeon.rad_tiles
   in zone_generators.py spawn() or in engine floor transition logic (already
   done for grease_tiles since a new dungeon is generated).

2. Consecrate while at exactly 50 rad: costs all your rad, leaves you at 0.
   This is intentional — you can't consecrate on empty. If player rad < 50,
   _execute_consecrate returns False with message "Not enough radiation!"

3. Sponge with no irradiated enemies visible: returns False, does NOT consume
   the charge. Message: "No irradiated enemies in sight!"

4. Radiant Wake + Consecrate overlap: player walks through their own Consecrate
   zone. The Wake tiles don't override Consecrate tiles — use max(existing, 6)
   so Consecrate's 20-turn tiles aren't degraded by the 6-turn Wake tiles. Done
   in: dungeon.rad_tiles[pos] = max(dungeon.rad_tiles.get(pos, 0), 6) for Wake,
   and dungeon.rad_tiles[pos] = max(dungeon.rad_tiles.get(pos, 0), 20) for
   Consecrate.

5. Half-Life Aura + Force Sensitive interaction: draining 45 rad over 15 turns
   is almost 2 full threshold crossings. This could award +4 STR just from the
   aura. That's a significant passive bonus layered on top of the aura's
   offensive irradiation. Monitor in playtesting — if too strong, reduce self-
   drain to 2 rad/turn (30 rad total for 15 turns = 1 threshold crossing = +2 STR).

6. Save/load of HalfLifeAuraEffect: since it's a timed Effect, it serializes
   via the existing effects save system. No special handling needed.
