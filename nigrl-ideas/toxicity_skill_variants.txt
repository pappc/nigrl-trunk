TOXICITY SKILL TREE VARIANTS — ALTERNATE TAKES
================================================================================

Document Status: Brainstorm / Variant Design
Related Doc:     nigrl-ideas/toxicity_mechanic.txt (first-pass Chemical Warfare
                 and Clean trees; confirmed mechanical foundation)
Purpose:         5 completely fresh takes on each toxicity skill tree. Not
                 tweaks to the first pass — new names, new identities, new
                 playstyle fantasies. For use when selecting the final L1-L3
                 design for both trees.


================================================================================
FIRST PASS RECAP (for avoidance reference)
================================================================================

CHEMICAL WARFARE (offensive):
  L1 Synthesize     — SELF cooldown ability; +100 tox on demand
  L2 Chemical Fury  — passive; spend tox → flat dmg bonus for 6t
  L3 Acid Bath      — LOS nuke; costs 150 tox, dmg = tox_before // 10

CLEAN (defensive):
  L1 Constitution of a Cockroach — +25 tox_resistance; +2 CON while tox < 50
  L2 Flush                       — PER_FLOOR 2 SELF; remove 120 tox, heal = amt
  L3 Purity Offensive            — passive; +25% dmg while tox < 100

Nothing in the five takes below should replicate these mechanics.


================================================================================
PART 1: OFFENSIVE TREE — 5 ALTERNATE TAKES
================================================================================

The offensive tree's core question: how does the player BUILD toxicity, and
how do they SPEND it? Each take gives a different answer to both halves.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OFFENSIVE TAKE 1: RUNOFF
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: DOT Spreader — you are a walking contamination event. You do not spend
toxicity in big bursts; you leak it continuously into enemies through lingering
chemical stains that kill rooms long after you've moved on.

HOW TOXICITY IS GENERATED: Absorbing damage from enemies. Every hit you take
in the Meth Lab zone adds base tox from the environment, but Runoff makes that
loop work harder — you turn your own poisoning into a weapon.

  L1 — SEEP (Passive)
    Whenever you take damage from a melee hit, you automatically apply
    "corroded" to the attacker (a new DOT effect, 3 turns, damage = your
    current tox // 150, minimum 1). No action or resource cost — this fires
    automatically on every melee hit you receive.
    Flavor: They hit you and their hands dissolve a little.
    Implementation note: New "corroded" effect (DOT, tox-scaled damage at
    apply time — value locked in at application, not recalculated each tick).
    Engine on-hit hook: after player receives melee damage, if Seep perk active,
    call apply_effect(attacker, "corroded", damage=player.toxicity // 150).
    Balance: at tox=300 this deals 2/turn for 3 turns = 6 total bonus damage
    per hit absorbed. Rewards taking fights in fume-heavy rooms. Perversely
    encourages letting yourself get hit.

  L2 — STAIN (Passive)
    When any enemy dies in your presence (within sight radius), they leave a
    "slick" on the tile they occupied for 10 turns. Any entity stepping on
    a slick tile takes 1 + (your toxicity // 200) damage and gains the
    "slowed" status for 3 turns (-15 energy/tick). Slicks do not stack —
    only one slick per tile at a time.
    Flavor: They melt a little when they go down. You made them toxic too.
    Implementation note: engine.on_entity_death() hook spawns a temporary
    hazard entity (entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="slick") at death
    coords. Slick tick handled in _run_energy_loop same as fire. Damage
    formula reads engine.player.toxicity at time of step (not at death time).
    Balance: encourages body-kiting through rooms (funnel enemies through the
    graveyard you made). Not broken because slick damage is modest unless
    tox is very high.

  L3 — BLEED THROUGH (Passive + Activated hybrid)
    Passive component: your corroded and poisoned effects on enemies now refresh
    their duration (not stack) when you deal ANY damage to the affected target.
    Activated component: once per floor, PURGE — spend any amount of tox
    (minimum 50, you choose) to immediately deal all remaining DOT ticks of
    every active corroded/poisoned effect on ALL visible enemies at once,
    collapsing the full duration into a single instant damage hit.
    Flavor: You decide everyone's time is up simultaneously.
    Implementation note: ability_id "purge". Execute callable: iterate all
    visible enemies, for each check for corroded/poisoned effects, sum remaining
    ticks * tick_damage, deal as instant damage, remove the effect, subtract
    chosen tox cost from player.toxicity.
    Balance: PURGE is the payoff for planting DOTs across a room. In a dense
    room with 5 enemies all corroded at 2dmg/tick * 2 turns remaining: 20
    instant damage split across 5 targets = minor. At high tox with multiple
    stacked application cycles it can hit hard. Requires setup, rewards patience.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OFFENSIVE TAKE 2: COOKER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Burst Alchemist — you pre-load tox through deliberate self-dosing
rituals between fights, then detonate single targets with enormous single-hit
spikes. The fantasy is: a chef who poisons himself to make the meal deadlier.

HOW TOXICITY IS GENERATED: Active self-dosing ability that locks you in place
for 1 turn (risky to use mid-fight, designed for prep between rooms).

  L1 — HIT THE BATCH (Activated)
    ability_id: "hit_the_batch"
    Target: SELF. ChargeType: INFINITE with 8-turn cooldown.
    Effect: Skip your next energy action (you stand still for 1 full turn),
    then gain +150 toxicity and +3 STR as a temporary bonus for 8 turns.
    Flavor: You sample the product. The product is outstanding.
    Implementation note: the skip-turn is implemented as a stun with duration=1
    applied immediately; the tox and STR bonus apply at the END of the stun
    turn (engine resolves pending bonuses after stun tick). Store pending bonus
    in engine._pending_batch_bonus: bool flag; after stun expires apply_effect
    for strength bonus and call _apply_toxicity(player, 150, "consumable").
    Balance: +150 tox and +3 STR for 8 turns is strong, but the 1-turn stun
    is genuinely dangerous in the Meth Lab. Use it in corridors between rooms.
    The 8-turn cooldown means you can use it about once per room clear.

  L2 — CUTTING AGENT (Passive)
    Your melee attacks have a 25% chance to apply "contaminated" to the target
    for 5 turns. Contaminated enemies take +20% more damage from all sources.
    If you use Hit the Batch THEN attack the contaminated enemy, the
    contaminated damage bonus increases to +40% for the remainder of that
    enemy's contaminated duration (a one-time upgrade on the existing effect).
    Flavor: Everything you touch has a little extra in it now.
    Implementation note: contaminated is a new debuff effect; "modify_incoming_damage"
    multiplier = 1.20 (or 1.40 if upgraded flag is set). Upgrade fires in the
    Hit the Batch execute callable: find all contaminated enemies in sight, set
    upgraded=True on their contaminated effect instance.
    Balance: base 20% damage amp on 25% proc is modest; the 40% upgrade requires
    deliberately sequencing Hit the Batch → attack, which rewards prep.

  L3 — OVERCOOK (Activated)
    ability_id: "overcook"
    Target: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS. ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=2.
    Toxicity cost: 200.
    Effect: Deal damage equal to (tox_spent // 8) to the target. If the target
    was contaminated (from Cutting Agent), the contaminated effect is immediately
    consumed (removed) and the target's current tox value (if any) is also added
    to the damage. Apply "melting" to the target for 4 turns (new effect:
    defense halved, rounded down, cannot be healed by allies).
    Flavor: You cook them properly. They stop being a person.
    Implementation note: ability_id "overcook". Execute: check tox >= 200,
    remove 200 tox, calculate damage = 200//8 = 25 base. If target has
    contaminated effect: remove it, add target.toxicity to damage. Apply melting.
    New "melting" effect: modify_defense = 0.5x multiplier, blocks heal flags.
    Balance: at minimum (200 tox spent): 25 damage + melting. Against a
    contaminated, toxicity-exposed Meth Lab enemy: can reach 50-70 damage.
    Strong but requires 2 perk investments and deliberate contaminate setup.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OFFENSIVE TAKE 3: PRODUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Supply Chain Murderer — you treat toxicity like inventory. You
accumulate it via throwing hazardous consumables at enemies, and your skill
tree makes that activity progressively more devastating. The fantasy is a
dealer who weaponizes his own supply.

HOW TOXICITY IS GENERATED: Throwing consumable items (Crystal Shards, Batch
Samples, Fume Canisters) at enemies applies toxicity to THEM and also grants
a portion back to the player via "contact exposure." The Product tree makes
this bidirectional exchange your primary combat loop.

  L1 — CONTACT EXPOSURE (Passive)
    Whenever you hit an enemy with a thrown consumable item that applies
    toxicity to them (via TOXICITY_DOSE on impact), you also gain 30% of the
    toxicity you applied to the target (rounded up). Additionally, each throw
    grants 20 XP toward the Product skill tree regardless of outcome.
    Flavor: You're not wearing gloves. You never were.
    Implementation note: in engine's thrown-item resolution, after applying
    TOXICITY_DOSE to target, if player has Contact Exposure perk, call
    _apply_toxicity(player, ceil(applied_amount * 0.30), "consumable").
    The 30% is not modified by player's Tolerance (it's an intentional
    source, like Synthesize) — treat as source="enemy_hit" to bypass resistance.
    Balance: a Fume Canister applies 25 tox per target hit. In a radius-2 AOE
    with 3 enemies: 75 total applied, player gets back 23 tox. Meaningful but
    not dominant — you're still spending items.

  L2 — MARKED BATCH (Passive)
    Enemies who have toxicity > 0 (acquired by any means) are "marked" in your
    eyes. Your melee attacks against marked enemies deal +1 bonus damage per
    25 toxicity they carry (rounded down, capped at +12 bonus damage).
    Additionally, killing a marked enemy causes their toxicity to splash to
    adjacent enemies: each adjacent enemy gains half the dead enemy's toxicity.
    Flavor: You know which ones are cooked. You exploit it.
    Implementation note: in combat damage calculation, after base damage: if
    target.toxicity > 0 and player has Marked Batch, add min(12, target.toxicity // 25).
    On entity death hook: if dead.toxicity > 0 and player has Marked Batch,
    iterate Chebyshev(1) neighbors, call _apply_toxicity(neighbor, dead.toxicity // 2).
    Balance: enemy toxicity in Zone 2 stays low (they gain via fumes, same rate
    as player). Marked Batch is a small but consistent damage buff against
    any tox-touched enemy, plus a chain-reaction splash mechanic for rooms where
    multiple enemies stand in fumes together.

  L3 — FINAL PRODUCT (Activated)
    ability_id: "final_product"
    Target: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS. ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=1.
    Toxicity cost: none — instead costs 2 consumable items from inventory
    (player selects which; any item with a use_effect counts). The two items
    are consumed without their normal effects firing.
    Effect: combine the two items into a concentrated toxicity dose thrown at
    the target. Target takes damage equal to the combined "weight" of the two
    items (use item.value as proxy: combined_value // 3), gains 200 toxicity,
    is stunned for 2 turns, and gains "saturated" for 8 turns (new effect:
    saturated enemies take double toxicity from all future applications).
    Flavor: You make something custom. One of a kind. For them specifically.
    Implementation note: ability_id "final_product" uses a new targeting flow:
    after confirming ability, enter an item-select sub-menu (similar to combine
    menu). Selected items are removed from inventory. Execute callable applies
    effects based on sum of item.value fields.
    New "saturated" effect: stored multiplier=2.0 on TOXICITY_DOSE applications
    to this entity. Engine _apply_toxicity checks for saturated and doubles.
    Balance: costs 2 items (opportunity cost) plus 1 PER_FLOOR charge. The
    stun + saturated combo sets up enormous follow-on toxicity applications.
    Intentionally the most setup-heavy L3 in any tree.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OFFENSIVE TAKE 4: VENOM HOUSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Aura/Zone Projector — you radiate toxicity outward passively. Your
mere presence corrodes enemies standing near you, and your skill tree rewards
getting in close and staying there. The fantasy is a living hazard zone.

HOW TOXICITY IS GENERATED: Natural accumulation accelerated by proximity to
enemies (they breathe you in while you breathe them in). The tree makes your
own toxicity a proximity weapon.

  L1 — EXUDE (Passive)
    At the start of each player turn, any enemy within Chebyshev distance 1
    (adjacent) gains 5 + (your toxicity // 100) toxicity. This fires before
    your action resolves. You do not gain toxicity from this — the direction
    is outward only.
    Flavor: You walk in and the room gets worse. You stay and it gets much worse.
    Implementation note: engine.before_player_turn() hook iterates entities
    within Chebyshev(1), calls _apply_toxicity(entity, amount, "enemy_hit")
    for each non-player entity. The "enemy_hit" source bypasses player's own
    tox_resistance logic (it's going out, not in).
    Balance: at tox=0 this deals 5 tox/turn to adjacent enemies. At tox=300
    it's 8/turn. Monster toxicity multiplier means 300 monster tox = 1 + (300/50)^0.6
    = 1 + ~3.7 = 4.7x incoming damage. So sustained adjacency in a fume-heavy
    room with high player tox becomes a meaningful damage ramp on enemies.
    Rewards melee-range combat, punishes kiting.

  L2 — HOTBOX (Passive)
    Your toxicity aura (from Exude) now extends to Chebyshev distance 2 with
    halved effect (floor division). Additionally, enemies who leave your aura
    range (move from within 2 tiles to 3+ tiles) immediately take burst damage
    equal to your tox // 80 as their system is suddenly deprived of the
    contamination they were adapting to. This is a one-time burst per departure
    event, not a continuous effect.
    Flavor: They get used to it. Then they leave and it kills them.
    Implementation note: track per-entity "was_in_aura" bool flag (set each
    turn); on the following turn if was_in_aura=True and now out of range,
    trigger departure damage. Engine handles this in the pre-turn enemy check.
    Clear "was_in_aura" at end of each player turn.
    Note: "hot_box" ability_id is already reserved for Smoking L5 (see MEMORY.md).
    This perk is passive and does not use an ability_id — name it "Hotbox" as
    a perk label only.
    Balance: departure damage at tox=300 is ~3. At tox=1000 it's ~12. It
    discourages enemies from fleeing (or punishes AI that flees naturally).
    Room_guard AI that never retreats is unaffected; hit_and_run AI that pulls
    back will eat this repeatedly.

  L3 — DEAD AIR (Activated)
    ability_id: "dead_air"
    Target: SELF (area implicit). ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=2.
    Toxicity cost: 100.
    Effect: For 6 turns, your Exude aura range extends to Chebyshev 3 and the
    toxicity applied per turn is tripled. Additionally, enemies in the aura
    during Dead Air cannot use special attacks (their SpecialAttack chance
    is overridden to 0% for the duration — the toxicity clouds their judgment).
    Flavor: The room becomes uninhabitable. They know it. They can't do anything
    about it.
    Implementation note: engine tracks dead_air_active: bool and dead_air_turns:
    int. During active turns: modify Exude's range and multiplier, and in
    do_ai_turn() special attack resolution check engine.dead_air_active and
    skip special attack roll if target is within radius 3 of player.
    Balance: costs 100 tox + 1 floor charge. 6-turn window of tripled aura
    at range 3 is a strong crowd-control tool but requires being in melee range
    of multiple enemies — exactly where the aura build wants to be.

OFFENSIVE TAKE 5: HOLLOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Self-Destruction as Power — you are not building a resource to spend
strategically; you are accepting that the toxicity will kill you and extracting
maximum violence from the process. The fantasy is: the faster you die, the
harder you hit. An escalating kamikaze tree.

HOW TOXICITY IS GENERATED: This tree does not have a dedicated generator ability.
Instead, the player takes toxicity from every source available (enemies, fumes,
items) and this tree makes the resulting high-toxicity DEATH SPIRAL into a
combat advantage. The intended player: someone who has given up on survival
management and commits to the damage ceiling.

  L1 — NOTHING TO LOSE (Passive)
    Your melee damage dealt is increased by 1 for every 50 toxicity you carry
    (no cap). This replaces the base toxicity crit-damage bonus for your
    melee attacks — instead of scaling crit multiplier, you scale base flat
    damage on every hit, crit or not.
    Note: the toxicity crit-damage bonus still applies to all ability damage and
    ranged damage; this perk only replaces it for MELEE hits specifically.
    Flavor: You've stopped worrying. Your fists hurt more.
    Implementation note: in combat damage resolution (player melee), check for
    Nothing to Lose perk. If active: flat_bonus += player.toxicity // 50.
    Skip the crit_mult += toxicity/1000 addition on this hit (use base 2.0x).
    Balance: at tox=500 this is +10 flat to every melee hit. Compare to
    Chemical Warfare's Chemical Fury (L2) which requires a spend event for +10.
    Hollow gets it passively and permanently, but sacrifices the crit spike
    ceiling. Encourages standing in fumes and swinging relentlessly.

  L2 — BLEED OUT (Passive)
    When your toxicity exceeds 500 for the first time each floor, you enter
    "Bleed Out" state for 8 turns. During Bleed Out: you deal +30% all damage,
    you cannot heal (HP cannot increase by any means), and the toxicity
    damage multiplier on incoming attacks is halved for you (but still active).
    After Bleed Out ends: you take 20 damage as crash (non-lethal: cannot
    reduce below 1 HP) and the 500-tox threshold resets so it can trigger again
    next floor.
    Flavor: Your body is betraying you in the most useful possible direction.
    Implementation note: engine tracks per-floor bleed_out_triggered: bool.
    On any toxicity application: if player.toxicity >= 500 and not triggered,
    set flag, apply_effect("bleed_out", duration=8). BleedOutEffect: no-heal
    flag (blocks all HP restoration), modify_incoming_damage *= 0.5 (for the
    tox component only — requires splitting tox multiplier from base multiplier
    in engine damage resolution), modify_outgoing_damage *= 1.30. On expire:
    deal 20 non-lethal damage, log message.
    Balance: the window for Bleed Out is 8 turns — short. You need to be
    already in combat when it triggers. At 500 tox the normal incoming mult
    is already 3.48x; halving the tox portion brings it to roughly 2.24x.
    Still dangerous, but not instantly fatal. The +30% damage output during
    those 8 turns is the highest single-perk damage multiplier in the game.

  L3 — SPITE (Passive)
    Whenever you take damage that would reduce you below 30% of your maximum HP
    (a "threshold cross" event), you immediately retaliate: deal damage equal
    to your current toxicity // 20 to the attacker, ignoring defense. This can
    only fire once per threshold cross per combat engagement (tracked per enemy
    entity — each enemy that crosses your threshold once gets the Spite hit once,
    but if a second enemy later crosses the same threshold in the same fight,
    they also eat it).
    Additionally: while below 30% max HP, your toxicity drain from the natural
    decay system is disabled. Low health means high concentration. You are no
    longer metabolizing.
    Flavor: Hitting you hard just made things worse for everyone.
    Implementation note: engine.on_player_threshold_cross(attacker) — fires when
    player HP drops below 30% of max in a single hit. Track per-enemy flag
    "spite_triggered": bool. Damage formula ignores defense, uses plain subtraction.
    Natural decay gate: in _natural_tox_decay(), check if player HP < player.max_hp * 0.30.
    Balance: at tox=300 Spite deals 15 instant damage. At tox=1000 it's 50.
    This is a reactive defense layer, not proactive damage. Pairs extremely
    well with Bleed Out since staying near the threshold keeps both perks active
    simultaneously for extended periods.


================================================================================
PART 2: DEFENSIVE TREE — 5 ALTERNATE TAKES
================================================================================

The defensive tree's core question: what is the player's relationship with
toxicity? Each take gives a different answer — from eliminating it entirely
to converting it into something else to embracing a threshold and camping there.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFENSIVE TAKE 1: QUARANTINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Hard Tank — you do not resist toxicity through chemistry; you resist
it through sheer physical refusal. You are built like a bunker. The payoff is
not offensive — it is the ability to absorb what would kill anyone else and
keep fighting. The fantasy: the man who should be dead three times over.

RELATIONSHIP TO TOXICITY: Caps incoming tox per-hit and grants substantial
CON-scaling resistance. The tree does not remove accumulated toxicity; it just
slows how fast it gets there and limits peak exposure per event.

  L1 — SEALED OFF (Passive)
    All toxicity applications to you are capped at 20 per event, regardless
    of source (enemy on-hit, fume tiles, ambient zone, consumables — everything).
    This is an absolute hard cap, not a percentage reduction. A Fume Warden
    hitting you for 30 on-hit tox deals 20 instead. A fume tile doing 8/tick
    is unchanged (under cap). A Crystal Shard you use yourself still does its
    full +60 (no — wait: this tree intentionally ALSO caps self-dosing, because
    Quarantine players aren't interested in the tox metagame at all).
    Additionally: your maximum HP is increased by 20 (permanent, like a CON bonus
    but separate from CON — tracked as engine.player_bonus_maxhp: int).
    Flavor: You sealed everything. You wear it like armor.
    Implementation note: in _apply_toxicity(entity, amount, source), if player
    has Sealed Off, apply cap: amount = min(amount, 20). Bonus HP: added to
    max_hp calculation in stats.py effective_max_hp property.
    Balance: Fume Warden's always-on 30 tox/hit becomes 20. Crystal Shard
    self-dose drops from 60 to 20 — this tree actively discourages Crystal Shard
    use, which is thematically correct. The +20 HP is the second-largest HP grant
    in any single perk (after CON bonuses from Munching tree).

  L2 — PRESSURE SUIT (Passive)
    Gain defense equal to your current toxicity // 75 (recalculated each combat
    round). At low tox this is nothing. At tox=300 it's 4 defense. At tox=600
    it's 8. This converts accumulated toxicity into passive armor instead of a
    pure liability. Does not reduce the incoming damage multiplier — it reduces
    the pre-multiplier base damage, which then gets scaled by whatever the tox
    mult is. The interaction is intentional: even at high tox, Quarantine builds
    have a base to absorb from before the multiplier kicks in.
    Flavor: Whatever got into you, it's hardening you.
    Implementation note: in PlayerStats.effective_defense property (or wherever
    armor is computed), add min(10, player.toxicity // 75) if Pressure Suit perk
    active. Cap at 10 to prevent the armor from totally negating hits at extreme
    toxicity values.
    Balance: caps at tox=750 for maximum 10 defense bonus. Combined with equipped
    armor, Quarantine builds at moderate tox have the highest total defense of
    any archetype. But they still take 3-4x damage from the mult at that tox
    level — the armor is mitigation, not immunity.

  L3 — LAST MAN STANDING (Passive)
    If you take a hit that would kill you (reduce HP to 0 or below), the hit
    is instead reduced to deal exactly (current_hp - 1) damage, leaving you at
    1 HP. This is a death-prevention passive. Cooldown: once per floor — the
    first time per floor it fires, it works. Subsequent lethal hits in the same
    floor kill you normally.
    Additionally: when Last Man Standing fires, you immediately lose 100 toxicity
    (not a resource spend — a purge representing your body burning through reserve)
    and gain a 3-turn status "Rallied" (new effect: +15% all damage, cannot be
    stunned, +10 temporary defense for 3 turns).
    Flavor: Your body knows it's not done yet. It argues the point.
    Implementation note: engine tracks last_man_standing_used_this_floor: bool.
    In player damage application: check if final_damage >= player.hp and perk
    active and not used this floor. If so: reduce damage to player.hp - 1, set
    flag, apply_toxicity(player, -100, "consumable"), apply_effect("rallied", 3).
    New "rallied" effect: modify_outgoing_damage *=1.15, blocks stun applications,
    modify_defense += 10.
    Balance: this is the "oh no" button for the tank build. Once per floor. The
    Rallied window encourages immediate aggressive counterplay after surviving
    the hit rather than retreating to heal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFENSIVE TAKE 2: BOILED DOWN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Counter-Attacker — you deliberately sit at a moderate toxicity
threshold (not low, not high) and use the tension of that position as a
weapon. When enemies hit you and push your tox up, that moment of elevation
becomes an attack. The fantasy: a deliberate fighter who turns every poisoning
into a jab back.

RELATIONSHIP TO TOXICITY: You want to stay in a "sweet spot" band — roughly
150-400 tox. Below that, your counter-attack damage is weak. Above it, incoming
damage starts hurting too much. The tree rewards meticulous toxicity management
through combat, not through avoidance.

  L1 — COUNTER DOSE (Passive)
    Whenever an enemy applies toxicity to you via a melee on-hit effect, you
    immediately counter: deal damage to that attacker equal to (the amount of
    toxicity they just applied // 5), ignoring defense. This fires after the
    original damage and tox application resolve.
    Flavor: They gave you some. You gave some back. Right in the face.
    Implementation note: in engine._apply_toxicity(), if source == "enemy_hit"
    and entity is player and player has Counter Dose, call instant damage on
    the attacker (bypassing defense). Requires passing attacker reference into
    _apply_toxicity or storing it as engine._current_tox_attacker in the
    on-hit resolution chain.
    Balance: Fume Warden on-hit (30 tox, always) → 6 counter damage. Rat on-hit
    (5 tox, 30% chance) → 1 counter damage when it fires. Modest but consistent
    — adds up across a fight where multiple enemies spam on-hit tox.

  L2 — SIMMER (Passive)
    While your toxicity is between 100 and 500 (inclusive), all your melee
    attacks gain +10% crit chance and +2 flat damage.
    When your toxicity is outside this range (below 100 or above 500), these
    bonuses are removed entirely.
    Flavor: There is a precise amount of poison that makes you dangerous. You
    know what it is. You stay there.
    Implementation note: simple threshold check in combat resolution. Engine
    checks player.toxicity in [100, 500] range each attack for the bonuses.
    Display: character sheet note "(SIMMERING)" if in range — a status indicator.
    Balance: +10% crit chance is substantial (doubles a low-Street-Smarts player's
    crit rate). Combined with the flat +2 this is a meaningful DPS boost but
    locked to a narrow tox band. The player must actively use cleansers to stay
    under 500 and must absorb enough tox to stay over 100.

  L3 — FLASH POINT (Activated)
    ability_id: "flash_point"
    Target: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS. ChargeType: INFINITE with 10-turn cooldown.
    Effect: Deal damage to the target equal to (your current toxicity // 15),
    then your toxicity is set to exactly 100 (not reduced by 100 — SET to 100).
    If your tox was above 100, this removes the excess. If below 100, it
    increases to 100 (unusual case: you chose to use it at low tox to reset).
    The reset-to-100 always places you in Simmer range for the following turns.
    Flavor: You convert everything you're carrying into one moment.
    Implementation note: ability execute: dmg = player.toxicity // 15, deal
    damage ignoring defense (toxicity-fueled burst), then set player.toxicity = 100
    (hard set, not delta — bypass all resistance/tolerance calculations).
    Balance: at tox=400 (mid Simmer): 26 damage + reset to 100 (Simmer maintained).
    At tox=800 (above Simmer ceiling): 53 damage + reset to 100 (re-enters Simmer).
    At tox=100: 6 damage, no net tox change. This makes Flash Point a dual-purpose
    tool: damage spike AND tox management. Fits Boiled Down's deliberate identity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFENSIVE TAKE 3: SOBER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Healer / Sustainer — you treat toxicity as a disease to be
aggressively cured. Unlike the first-pass Clean tree (which removed tox for
damage bonuses), Sober converts tox REDUCTION into HP and survivability. The
fantasy: a filthy medic who keeps patching himself up mid-combat.

RELATIONSHIP TO TOXICITY: You want to be perpetually reducing it. The more
you cleanse, the more HP you recover. Low toxicity is not the goal — the ACT
of cleansing is the mechanic. You intentionally let it climb so you can turn
the cleanse into a big heal.

  L1 — STOMACH OF STEEL (Passive)
    Consumable items that reduce toxicity (Activated Charcoal, Hazmat Shot,
    Clean Water, Ipecac Syrup, Flush ability) grant bonus HP healing equal to
    40% of the toxicity removed (in addition to any healing those items already
    provide). Additionally, the multi-turn eat time for Activated Charcoal is
    reduced from 2 turns to 1.
    Flavor: Your body treats the antidote as food. It's not, but the result is
    the same.
    Implementation note: engine._apply_toxicity() with negative amount (reduction):
    if source in ("consumable", "ability") and player has Stomach of Steel,
    after clamping: heal = int(abs_reduction * 0.40); player.hp = min(player.max_hp,
    player.hp + heal). Store abs_reduction as local before clamping to 0.
    The 1-turn eat time for charcoal is a foods.py change (eat_time field).
    Balance: Flush ability removes 120 tox → 48 bonus HP. Clean Water removes
    30 tox → 12 bonus HP. Ipecac removes 200 tox → 80 bonus HP (on top of
    its existing -15 HP cost, netting +65 effective HP). Ipecac goes from
    nuclear option to a real sustain tool for Sober builds.

  L2 — METABOLIZE (Passive)
    The natural toxicity decay (max(1, tox//50) per 20 player turns, outside
    Meth Lab) now also triggers inside the Meth Lab zone for you specifically.
    Additionally, the decay rate is doubled: max(2, tox//25) per 20 turns.
    In the Meth Lab, the ambient gain rate will still outpace your natural decay,
    but it narrows the gap significantly. Each unit of tox you naturally metabolize
    also heals 1 HP.
    Flavor: You detox in real time. Slowly, but it's happening.
    Implementation note: gate check in _natural_tox_decay(): normally skips if
    current_zone == "meth_lab"; if player has Metabolize, skip this zone gate and
    use the doubled formula instead. HP healing: after tox reduction, player.hp
    += tox_reduced (capped at max_hp).
    Balance: inside Meth Lab, ambient gain is +1/10 turns = 0.1/turn; enemy on-hit
    adds more. Natural decay at tox=300 is max(2, 300//25) = 12 per 20 turns =
    0.6/turn. In the Meth Lab this partially counteracts ambient; enemy hits still
    push it up. The 1 HP per tox metabolized at that rate = 0.6 HP/turn passive
    sustain. Modest but stacks with Stomach of Steel for a cohesive sustain identity.

  L3 — SECOND WIND (Activated)
    ability_id: "second_wind"
    Target: SELF. ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=1.
    Effect: Over the next 4 turns, your toxicity is reduced by 25% per turn
    (applied at the start of each of your turns, 4 reductions total — roughly
    equivalent to eliminating ~68% of current tox over the window). Each turn
    of the reduction also heals HP equal to the tox removed that turn.
    Additionally, during the 4-turn window, you cannot gain toxicity from any
    source (all TOXICITY_DOSE and passive applications are blocked).
    Flavor: You lean on something and breathe until you feel like a person again.
    Implementation note: apply_effect("second_wind_regen", duration=4). Effect
    subclass: each before_turn tick: calculate 25% of player.toxicity, reduce it,
    heal that amount. Set engine.player_tox_immune: bool = True on apply, False
    on expire. In _apply_toxicity: if player_tox_immune, return without applying.
    Balance: at 300 tox, over 4 turns: 75 + 56 + 42 + 32 = 205 tox removed,
    205 HP healed. That is an enormous heal — effectively full HP for most builds.
    But it is 1 PER_FLOOR, costs a turn to activate, and gives enemies 4 turns
    of attacking you while you regenerate. The tox immunity window is the real prize.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFENSIVE TAKE 4: GHOST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Speed / Evasion — you solve the toxicity problem by not being there
when it tries to affect you. Movement-based resistance. Fume tiles can't hurt
you if you never stop moving. The fantasy: slippery, fast, untouchable in the
short term. High risk if cornered.

RELATIONSHIP TO TOXICITY: You maintain low toxicity through positioning and
speed, not cleansing. The tree gives you tools to never be on a fume tile long
enough for it to matter, and punishes enemies who try to land on-hit tox.

  L1 — LIGHT FEET (Passive)
    Moving through or off of a fume tile does not apply toxicity to you if you
    move in the same turn you entered it (i.e., if you step onto a fume tile
    and immediately step off — no net toxicity applied). Standing still on a
    fume tile for a full turn still applies the normal toxicity gain.
    Additionally, gain +10 energy/tick permanently (you move slightly faster,
    baseline speed raised).
    Flavor: You don't stop moving. You've never stopped moving.
    Implementation note: engine._run_energy_loop step 4 (fume tile check): before
    applying fume tox, check if entity.just_moved == True (a flag set when the
    player's movement action was their last action). If just_moved and player has
    Light Feet, skip fume tox application this tick.
    Balance: +10 energy/tick brings base to 110/tick — about 10% faster. Light
    Feet effectively neutralizes fume tiles in corridors (you walk through, never
    linger). Room centers with fume tiles force standing still to fight — that's
    where tox still accumulates.

  L2 — MISS ME (Passive)
    Enemies' on-hit toxicity effects (TOXICITY_DOSE from melee attacks) have a
    chance to fail: equal to your dodge chance percentage. If a tox application
    would be dodged, no toxicity is applied (the hit still deals its normal HP
    damage — you took the hit, but something about the angle or timing prevented
    full chemical transfer).
    Flavor: It gets on them. Somehow it misses you.
    Implementation note: in engine._apply_toxicity() when source == "enemy_hit",
    if player has Miss Me: roll against player.dodge_chance (existing stat, used
    for actual hit dodging). If roll succeeds, return early (no tox applied),
    add to log: "The chemicals miss you somehow."
    Balance: dodge_chance at moderate Street Smarts is 15-25%. Applied to tox
    grants, this dramatically reduces sustained on-hit pressure from enemies
    like the Fume Warden (30 tox/hit, 100% proc rate → effectively 22-25 average
    after misses). Does NOT affect fume tiles or ambient (only on-hit source).

  L3 — CUT AND RUN (Activated)
    ability_id: "cut_and_run"
    Target: SELF. ChargeType: INFINITE with 8-turn cooldown.
    Effect: Immediately teleport to any explored tile within 6 Chebyshev distance
    that does not contain a blocking entity or wall. Then remove all "on-hit tox"
    debuffs from yourself (any enemy effect applied in the last 2 turns that
    added toxicity — the move purges freshly acquired exposure before it sets in).
    Your toxicity is reduced by a flat 30 after teleport.
    Flavor: You ghost. The whole situation stops being your problem.
    Implementation note: ability_id "cut_and_run". Execute: select target tile
    (enter tile-targeting mode, valid tiles = explored + unblocked within range 6).
    On confirm: teleport player (set position), then reduce player.toxicity by 30.
    "Purge recent on-hit tox" mechanic: engine tracks engine._recent_tox_hits: list
    of (turn, amount) pairs; on cut_and_run, clear all entries from the last 2 turns
    and subtract their amounts from player.toxicity (clamped to 0). This effectively
    cancels up to 2 turns of enemy on-hit tox applications retroactively.
    Balance: cooldown 8 turns means usable roughly once per significant fight. The
    retroactive tox purge is the unique element — you saw it coming, you left before
    it mattered. Strong repositioning tool for avoiding Fume Warden adjacency.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFENSIVE TAKE 5: TRANSMUTE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTITY: Converter — you do not resist toxicity and you do not remove it. You
transmute it into something else entirely: Street Smarts, swagger, temporary
power, or brief moments of clarity. The toxicity is still there; you've just
redirected what it does to you. The fantasy: an alchemist who reinterprets
what the chemicals are telling his body.

RELATIONSHIP TO TOXICITY: High toxicity is not scary because you have converted
its effects into something useful. You do not reduce it or avoid it — you
metabolize it into stats and let natural decay handle the rest. Tox sits high
but its sting is dulled by the conversion.

  L1 — BAD TRIP TO GOOD TRIP (Passive)
    The incoming damage multiplier from toxicity (1.0 + (tox/100)^0.6) is
    reduced: you use (tox/150)^0.6 instead of (tox/100)^0.6 for your multiplier.
    This effectively shifts your danger curve 50% further out — what would
    normally hit at 100 tox now hits with that intensity at 150 tox.
    Additionally: while toxicity >= 200, gain +2 Street Smarts (temporary,
    removed if tox drops below 200).
    Flavor: The chemicals teach you something. You start reading situations better.
    Implementation note: in engine damage calculation, if player has Bad Trip to
    Good Trip, substitute: tox_mult = 1.0 + (player.toxicity / 150) ** 0.6
    instead of the standard formula. Temporary Street Smarts: check each tox
    application/reduction; maintain engine._transmute_ss_bonus: int.
    Balance: at tox=300 standard mult is 2.66x; with this perk it becomes 2.18x.
    A significant reduction in danger curve. The +2 Street Smarts at 200+ tox
    translates to +6% crit chance — not enormous, but relevant for Street Smarts
    builds already investing in crit.

  L2 — JUNK SCIENCE (Passive)
    Once per turn, when you would gain toxicity from any source, if your toxicity
    is already >= 100, you instead gain a "converted" proc: 50% of the toxicity
    that would have been applied is instead converted into temporary Swagger bonus
    (1 Swagger per 15 converted tox, rounded down, duration 10 turns, stacks
    but max effective Swagger bonus = 6). The remaining 50% still applies as
    normal toxicity.
    Flavor: Your brain is making it make sense. The swagger is not entirely fake.
    Implementation note: in _apply_toxicity(), if player has Junk Science and
    player.toxicity >= 100: split amount. Apply half normally. Convert half:
    swagger_bonus = half_amount // 15, capped so total engine._junk_swagger does
    not exceed 6. Update a "converted" effect with the swagger bonus.
    Remember: Swagger defense formula is (effective_swagger - 5) // 2. At base
    swagger 7 + 6 bonus = 13: defense = 4. Against an enemy dealing 6 base
    damage, that's a meaningful reduction.
    Balance: at tox=300 and Fume Warden hitting for 30 tox: 15 applies normally,
    15 converts to 1 Swagger for 10 turns. At maximum conversion (6 Swagger
    bonus): defense = 4 from swagger alone. Junk Science builds sit at medium
    tox permanently, converting the overflow into a defensive wall.

  L3 — CLARITY BURST (Activated)
    ability_id: "clarity_burst"
    Target: SELF. ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=2.
    Effect: Your toxicity is NOT reduced. Instead, for the next 6 turns, all
    of your toxicity's NEGATIVE effects are suspended (the incoming damage
    multiplier is set to 1.0, as if toxicity were 0) while all POSITIVE effects
    remain (crit multiplier still scales with tox, on-crit procs still fire,
    Bad Trip to Good Trip Street Smarts bonus still applies). Flavor: a window
    of artificial clarity where the chemicals work FOR you perfectly.
    After 6 turns: the suspended incoming damage multiplier snaps back to its
    correct current value. Your toxicity is unchanged.
    Flavor: For six turns, your body figures it out. All of it, at once.
    Implementation note: apply_effect("clarity_burst", duration=6). ClarityBurst
    effect: modify_incoming_damage overrides tox multiplier to 1.0 (bypasses
    the formula entirely for player incoming damage while active). Does not
    interfere with outgoing crit calc or on-crit procs.
    Balance: 2 PER_FLOOR. A 6-turn window where a tox=800 build is effectively
    immune to their own multiplier while retaining 3.0x crit mult and Chemical
    Cascade procs. This is the equivalent of a "go mode" button for high-tox
    builds who chose a defensive tree. Intentionally strong — it requires 3 perk
    investments in a tree that provides no offensive tools until this point.


================================================================================
CROSS-TAKE NOTES AND DESIGN OBSERVATIONS
================================================================================

GENERATION DIVERSITY — Offensive takes:
  Runoff    — absorb damage to generate (reactive)
  Cooker    — deliberate self-stun ritual (high-commitment active)
  Product   — throwable consumables loop (resource-intensive)
  Venom House — proximity/aura (positional)
  Hollow    — no generator; just accept all sources (passive sponge)

TOXICITY RELATIONSHIP DIVERSITY — Defensive takes:
  Quarantine  — cap per-event intake; tank the multiplier with armor
  Boiled Down — sit in a band; use tox as a counter-attack meter
  Sober       — let it climb then convert cleansing into big heals
  Ghost       — avoid fume tiles and dodge on-hit tox; stay low
  Transmute   — accept high tox; convert negative multiplier into stats

NONE of the above replicate:
  - Synthesize (SELF cooldown generator)
  - Chemical Fury (spend-event damage bonus)
  - Acid Bath (LOS nuke, tox cost = damage)
  - Constitution of a Cockroach (flat tox_resistance %)
  - Flush (remove tox, heal = amount removed)
  - Purity Offensive (damage mult below threshold)

POTENTIAL HYBRIDIZATIONS (notes for future design):
  - Hollow L1 + Boiled Down L2: both care about melee combat, both want
    moderate-to-high tox. Could be a merged "street brawler" tree if needed.
  - Sober + Munching: Stomach of Steel heals from cleanses; Munching's CON
    bonuses raise max HP, making those heals more valuable. Strong synergy
    candidate for a tanky scavenger build.
  - Ghost + Jaywalking: if Jaywalking develops movement-speed bonuses, Light
    Feet (+10 energy) stacks with any Jaywalking speed grants. Ghost builds
    become the fastest archetype in the game.
  - Transmute + Chemical Warfare: deliberately strange pairing. Bad Trip to
    Good Trip lowers tox danger; Clarity Burst suspends it entirely for 6 turns.
    A player who takes both trees gets insane windows of safety at high tox,
    then spends it all on Acid Bath. Probably too strong — note for balance pass.

IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITY NOTES:
  The simplest L1-L3 sets to implement (fewest new systems needed):
    Offensive: Hollow (passive scalars only, no new effect types)
    Offensive: Venom House (Exude is a simple per-turn loop like fume tiles)
    Defensive: Quarantine (per-event cap + HP bonus + passive defense scalar)
    Defensive: Transmute (damage formula substitution + stat checks)
  Most complex:
    Offensive: Product (new item-targeting sub-menu for Final Product)
    Defensive: Sober (multi-phase regen effect, tox immunity window)
