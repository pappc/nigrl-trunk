Research Notes (from codebase)
-------------------------------
- grants_ability (str): supported on weapon, sidearm, feet slots — revoked on unequip
- grants_abilities (list[str]): supported on neck slot — all revoked on unequip
- soul_count: attribute set directly on the entity (neck item instance), tracked in engine.py
  kill handler at "Amulet of Equivalent Exchange"
- Resource items (like Amulet of EE) store their counter as an attribute on the item entity,
  check it in execute functions via engine.neck / engine.equipment["weapon"] etc.
- ability_cooldowns dict: {ability_id: turns_remaining}, ticked each energy loop
- ChargeType.INFINITE + ability_cooldowns = standard cooldown pattern
- ChargeType.PER_FLOOR + max_charges = per-floor limit pattern
- For hat/ring/sidearm grants_ability: inventory_mgr.py already handles weapon/sidearm/feet.
  Hat and ring slots do NOT currently process grants_ability on equip/unequip — new code needed
  for those slots (see Implementation Notes per item)
- Entity fields relevant: toxicity, radiation, infection, meth, temp_hp, armor, hp
- Status effect hooks available: modify_incoming_damage, on_player_melee_hit, modify_energy_gain,
  before_turn, modify_movement, tick, apply, expire
- Player stats: constitution, strength, book_smarts, street_smarts, tolerance, swagger
- New stat fields proposed in previous designs: melee_damage_bonus (PlayerStats), surge_charges

ITEM 1: Crown of the Corner Boy
Slot: Hat | Char: 0xE00D (hat graphical tile) | Color: (255, 200, 60) gold
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A battered gold-painted plastic crown — the kind you'd wear at a fast food birthday party —
repurposed as street royalty headwear. Every enemy you put in the dirt fills your "rep" meter.
Spend rep on three abilities: intimidate enemies into fleeing, call a temporary henchman to your
side, or blow the whole stack on a fearsome battle cry that locks every visible enemy in place.
It's the urban warlord build: accumulate clout, spend clout, dominate.

ITEM DEFINITION (items.py ITEM_DEFS entry)
  "crown_of_the_corner_boy": {
      "name": "Crown of the Corner Boy",
      "char": chr(0xE00D),
      "color": (255, 200, 60),
      "category": "equipment",
      "subcategory": "hat",
      "equip_slot": "hat",
      "power_bonus": 0,
      "defense_bonus": 0,
      "armor_bonus": 0,
      "stat_bonus": {"swagger": 2},
      "tags": ["crown_corner_boy", "unique"],
      "name_gradient": [(255, 200, 60), (220, 120, 20), (255, 200, 60)],
      "grants_abilities": ["corner_intimidate", "corner_henchman", "corner_war_cry"],
      "zones": [],
      "use_verb": None,
      "use_effect": None,
  }

RESOURCE: REP (Respect)
- Stored as: engine.player (hat item instance not used — attribute on engine: engine.rep_count)
  Or, mirroring the Amulet of EE pattern: store as engine.hat.rep_count, checked/set
  via engine.hat reference.
- Accumulation: +1 rep per kill while hat is equipped (checked in engine.py kill handler)
- Display: New effect "rep_count" (visual-only, like soul_count) applied on equip; stores
  display value. Updated each tick to match engine.hat.rep_count.
- Rep persists across floors while equipped; resets to 0 on unequip.

ABILITY 1: Corner Intimidate
  ability_id: "corner_intimidate"
  name: "Corner Intimidate"
  description: "Spend 3 rep to frighten the nearest visible enemy, sending it fleeing for 6 turns."
  TargetType: SELF (auto-picks nearest visible enemy)
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 3 rep
  Execute logic:
    - Check engine.hat.rep_count >= 3
    - Find nearest visible enemy in engine.dungeon.entities
    - Apply effect "fear" (duration=6) to that enemy — uses existing FearEffect
    - Deduct 3 rep from engine.hat.rep_count
    - Message: "Corner Intimidate! [name] backs the fuck up! (-3 rep)"

ABILITY 2: Corner Henchman
  ability_id: "corner_henchman"
  name: "Corner Henchman"
  description: "Spend 8 rep to summon a Henchman ally for 20 turns. He attacks any enemy adjacent to him."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 8 rep
  Execute logic:
    - Check engine.hat.rep_count >= 8
    - Spawn a new "henchman" entity adjacent to player (first free adjacent tile)
    - Henchman: char='h', color=(200,160,60), entity_type="monster", is_summon=True,
      summon_lifetime=20, ai_type="escort", hp=25, power=6, defense=1
    - Deduct 8 rep
    - Message: "Corner Henchman! Your boy rolls up! (-8 rep)"
  Note: uses existing is_summon=True system — henchman will not be auto-attacked by player,
  and will use escort AI to follow player and attack enemies.
  IMPLEMENTATION NOTE: "henchman" needs a MonsterTemplate in enemies.py (or created inline)

ABILITY 3: Corner War Cry
  ability_id: "corner_war_cry"
  name: "Corner War Cry"
  description: "Spend ALL rep (min 12) to stun every visible enemy for 1 turn per 4 rep spent."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL rep (minimum 12 to activate)
  Execute logic:
    - Check engine.hat.rep_count >= 12
    - total_rep = engine.hat.rep_count
    - stun_duration = total_rep // 4  (e.g., 12 rep = 3 turns, 20 rep = 5 turns)
    - Apply "stun" effect to every visible enemy (engine.dungeon.visible[e.y, e.x] == True)
    - Set engine.hat.rep_count = 0
    - Message: "WAR CRY! All visible enemies stunned for [stun_duration] turns! (-[total_rep] rep)"

BALANCE:
- Rep at 1/kill is slow early but picks up. 12 rep for War Cry = 12 kills, roughly 1 floor.
- Corner Intimidate at 3 rep is the cheap "get out of jail" option — you can fire it every few kills.
- Henchman at 8 rep is the big investment — a summon companion that fights for you.
- War Cry incentivizes hoarding rep for big moments. Dumping all rep = max stun duration.
- +2 Swagger makes it worthwhile as headgear even if you never use the abilities.
- Synergizes with high-kill playthroughs (Stabbing, Beating skill trees).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- engine.py kill handler: add "if self.hat and 'crown_corner_boy' in (get_item_def(self.hat.item_id) or {}).get('tags', []): self.hat.rep_count = getattr(self.hat, 'rep_count', 0) + 1"
- inventory_mgr.py equip_item(): hat slot currently does NOT call grant_ability on equip.
  Add grants_abilities processing to the hat equip block (mirror neck slot logic).
- Add "rep_count" display effect similar to "soul_count" (visual-only, no gameplay effect).
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries. FearEffect already exists in effects.py.
  StunEffect already exists. henchman entity can be spawned inline without a full template.
- engine.py unequip: add grants_abilities revoke for hat slot.

ITEM 2: The Knuckle Diary
Slot: Weapon (beating) | Char: / | Color: (180, 140, 100) worn leather
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A dog-eared spiral notebook wrapped in athletic tape, stiff with dried blood, used as an improvised
blunt weapon. Every time you land a crit with this relic you "write an entry" — scrawl a tally of
your destruction in its pages. Spend those entries on three abilities: snap a quick combo hit,
shatter an enemy's defense temporarily, or unleash a full retrospective — a flurry of strikes
equal to your entry count.

ITEM DEFINITION
  "knuckle_diary": {
      "name": "The Knuckle Diary",
      "char": "/",
      "color": (180, 140, 100),
      "category": "equipment",
      "subcategory": "weapon",
      "equip_slot": "weapon",
      "weapon_type": "beating",
      "power_bonus": 4,
      "defense_bonus": 0,
      "armor_bonus": 0,
      "stat_bonus": {"street_smarts": 1},
      "str_scaling": {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2},
      "tags": ["knuckle_diary", "unique"],
      "name_alternating": [(180, 140, 100), (220, 80, 80)],
      "grants_abilities": ["diary_quick_hit", "diary_break_defense", "diary_retrospective"],
      "zones": [],
      "use_verb": None,
      "use_effect": None,
  }

  NOTE: grants_abilities is currently only wired for neck slot. For weapon slot,
  we either use the existing grants_ability (single) or extend inventory_mgr.py to support
  grants_abilities list on weapon/sidearm. Simplest path: add grants_abilities support
  to weapon equip block in inventory_mgr.py (mirror neck logic).

RESOURCE: ENTRIES (diary tally marks)
- Stored as: engine.equipment["weapon"].entry_count (attribute set on item entity)
- Accumulation: +1 entry per critical hit dealt while weapon is equipped
- Tracked in: engine.py's melee crit code (combat.py handle_attack triggers a callback, or
  check in engine.py after handle_attack returns — check if it was a crit)
- Entries persist across floors; reset to 0 on unequip.
- Max entries: 10 (cap at 10, display shows "10 entries" at cap)

CRIT DETECTION: In engine.py, after handle_attack: if the attack was a crit (currently,
  combat.py returns (damage, is_crit) — check is_crit, or detect via engine.last_attack_was_crit
  flag set in combat.py). The simplest approach: add engine.knuckle_diary_entries tracking in
  the same kill-handler block, triggered on any crit with the weapon equipped.

ABILITY 1: Diary Quick Hit
  ability_id: "diary_quick_hit"
  name: "Diary Quick Hit"
  description: "Spend 2 entries to instantly deal a bonus attack against an adjacent enemy. Free action."
  TargetType: ADJACENT
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 2 entries
  Execute logic (_execute_at style):
    - Check weapon is equipped and is "knuckle_diary"
    - Check entry_count >= 2
    - Deal STR + power_bonus damage to target (no defense, no crits) — pure bonus hit
    - No action cost (free action: do NOT set engine.player_attacked_this_turn flag)
    - Deduct 2 entries
    - Message: "Quick Hit! [name] takes [dmg] dmg! (-2 entries)"
  IMPLEMENTATION: free action requires careful handling — set engine._free_action = True
  before returning, which bypasses the normal turn cost. Or check engine.player_free_action
  (already proposed in previous designs).

ABILITY 2: Diary Break Defense
  ability_id: "diary_break_defense"
  name: "Diary Break Defense"
  description: "Spend 3 entries to shatter an adjacent enemy's defense to 0 for 8 turns."
  TargetType: ADJACENT
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 3 entries
  Execute logic:
    - Check entry_count >= 3
    - Apply new effect "broken_defense" (duration=8) to target — sets target.defense = 0
      while active; expire restores original defense
    - Deduct 3 entries
    - Message: "Break Defense! [name]'s defense crumbles! (-3 entries)"
  NEW EFFECT NEEDED: "broken_defense" — stores original defense, zeroes it on apply,
  restores on expire.

ABILITY 3: Diary Retrospective
  ability_id: "diary_retrospective"
  name: "Diary Retrospective"
  description: "Spend ALL entries to strike an adjacent enemy once per entry. Spends entire tally."
  TargetType: ADJACENT
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL entries (minimum 3 to activate)
  Execute logic:
    - Check entry_count >= 3
    - For each entry (up to entry_count), deal one full melee attack via handle_attack
    - Each attack can crit independently (do NOT accumulate new entries during this burst)
    - Set entry_count = 0
    - Message: "RETROSPECTIVE! [count] strikes! (-[count] entries)"
  BALANCE: With 10 entries this is 10 attacks — devastating but you just dumped all your ammo.

BALANCE:
- Power bonus 4 + STR tiered makes it competitive as a beating weapon.
- +1 Street Smarts raises crit chance, synergizes with the entry-accumulation loop.
- Entries cap at 10 — hoarding doesn't get out of control.
- Retrospective with 10 entries is similar to Whirlwind but single-target; different risk profile.
- Quick Hit free action is the bread-and-butter spend for mid-combat bursts.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- inventory_mgr.py: extend grants_abilities support to weapon equip/unequip blocks.
- combat.py or engine.py: detect crits and increment entry_count on knuckle_diary.
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries.
- effects.py: new BrokenDefenseEffect (stores/restores defense).
- New engine state: knuckle_diary_entry_adding_active (bool, set during Retrospective
  burst to block new entry accumulation mid-burst).

ITEM 3: The Hustle Ring
Slot: Ring | Char: 0xE00A | Color: (80, 200, 120) green
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A chunky green plastic ring with a dollar sign cutout — the kind sold at a corner store for 50
cents. Every square you move earns you "hustle" — keep moving, keep stacking. Pop the hustle on
mobility abilities: rush through walls of enemies, leave a trail of speed boost tiles, or cash
it all in for a short window where every step also heals you.

ITEM DEFINITION
  "hustle_ring": {
      "name": "The Hustle Ring",
      "char": chr(0xE00A),
      "color": (80, 200, 120),
      "category": "equipment",
      "subcategory": "ring",
      "equip_slot": "ring",
      "power_bonus": 0,
      "defense_bonus": 0,
      "stat_bonus": {"street_smarts": 1, "tolerance": 1},
      "tags": ["hustle_ring", "unique"],
      "name_gradient": [(80, 200, 120), (30, 100, 60)],
      "grants_abilities": ["hustle_rush", "hustle_trail", "hustle_recovery"],
      "zones": [],
      "use_verb": None,
      "use_effect": None,
  }

  NOTE: Ring slot does NOT currently support grants_ability/grants_abilities.
  Implementation requires adding this support to inventory_mgr.py ring equip block.

RESOURCE: HUSTLE (move tally)
- Stored as: engine.hustle_ring_steps (int, set to 0 on ring unequip)
- Accumulation: +1 per tile the player moves while ring is equipped
  (in engine.handle_move, after successful movement, check for hustle_ring tag on any ring)
- Max hustle: 20. Display via a new "hustle_count" status effect (visual only).

ABILITY 1: Hustle Rush
  ability_id: "hustle_rush"
  name: "Hustle Rush"
  description: "Spend 6 hustle to instantly move 3 tiles in a chosen direction, passing through enemies (they take STR damage)."
  TargetType: ADJACENT_TILE (player picks a direction)
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 6 hustle
  Execute logic (_execute_at with adjacent tile direction):
    - Check hustle >= 6
    - Derive direction (dx, dy) from chosen adjacent tile
    - For each of 3 steps in that direction:
        - If terrain blocked: stop
        - If enemy on tile: deal STR damage to enemy, continue moving through
        - Move player to tile
    - Compute FOV after move
    - Deduct 6 hustle
    - Message: "Hustle Rush! You blow through for [tiles] tiles! (-6 hustle)"

ABILITY 2: Hustle Trail
  ability_id: "hustle_trail"
  name: "Hustle Trail"
  description: "Spend 10 hustle to paint the last 5 tiles you walked with speed-boost spray. Allies and player gain +10 energy/tick on those tiles for 8 turns."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 10 hustle
  Execute logic:
    - Check hustle >= 10
    - Retrieve engine._player_move_history (list of last 5 (x,y) positions)
      If that list doesn't exist, create it in handle_move (keep a deque(maxlen=5))
    - For each position in move history:
        - Mark tile as "hustle_trail" in dungeon.spray_paint (new spray type)
        - Set a timer of 8 turns
    - During energy loop: if player or any monster is on a hustle_trail tile, +10 energy/tick
    - After 8 turns: clear hustle_trail spray from those tiles
    - Deduct 10 hustle
    - Message: "Hustle Trail! Your path glows with momentum! (-10 hustle)"
  IMPLEMENTATION: simplest path — add "hustle_trail" spray type to dungeon.spray_paint dict
  with a timeout tracked in engine. Energy bonus via engine._run_energy_loop.

ABILITY 3: Hustle Recovery
  ability_id: "hustle_recovery"
  name: "Hustle Recovery"
  description: "Spend ALL hustle (min 8) to enter a flow state: for 1 turn per hustle spent, every step heals 2 HP."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL hustle (minimum 8)
  Execute logic:
    - Check hustle >= 8
    - duration = engine.hustle_ring_steps
    - Apply new effect "hustle_recovery_flow" (duration = duration)
      During flow state: modify_movement hook heals player by 2 HP each time they move
    - Set hustle_ring_steps = 0
    - Message: "Flow State! Heal 2 HP per step for [duration] turns! (-[hustle] hustle)"
  NEW EFFECT NEEDED: "hustle_recovery_flow" — overrides modify_movement to call player.heal(2).

BALANCE:
- Hustle accumulates at 1/step so 6-hustle cost is ~6 steps from last floor entry or use.
- Trail is a 10-hustle investment that rewards movement-centric play.
- Recovery at 8+ hustle gives ~8-20 steps of healing — meaningful in tough fights where you
  need to maintain distance and heal simultaneously.
- +1 STS, +1 TOL are minor but thematic (street agility + drug endurance).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- inventory_mgr.py: add grants_abilities support to ring equip/unequip blocks.
- engine.py handle_move: check hustle_ring equipped, increment engine.hustle_ring_steps.
  Also track engine._player_move_history = deque(maxlen=5).
- effects.py: new HustleRecoveryFlowEffect with modify_movement hook calling entity.heal(2).
- New engine state: hustle_ring_steps (reset on ring unequip).
- dungeon.spray_paint: add "hustle_trail" type with expiry timer.

ITEM 4: Tap-In Talisman
Slot: Neck | Char: * | Color: (255, 100, 40) orange
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A beaten-up pager dangling from a lanyard — the kind your dealer used in the 90s. Every time you
take damage you "earn a page" — the system logs each hit. Spend those pages on three abilities:
call a dodge window that makes the next hit miss, dump all pages for a massive temp-HP shield
proportional to damage you've eaten, or trigger a trauma response that briefly multiplies all
your outgoing damage.

ITEM DEFINITION
  "tap_in_talisman": {
      "name": "Tap-In Talisman",
      "char": "*",
      "color": (255, 100, 40),
      "category": "equipment",
      "subcategory": "neck",
      "equip_slot": "neck",
      "power_bonus": 0,
      "defense_bonus": 0,
      "armor_bonus": 15,
      "stat_bonus": {"constitution": 1},
      "tags": ["tap_in_talisman", "unique"],
      "name_gradient": [(255, 100, 40), (200, 50, 20), (255, 100, 40)],
      "grants_abilities": ["tap_dodge_window", "tap_shield", "tap_trauma_response"],
      "zones": [],
      "use_verb": None,
      "use_effect": None,
  }

RESOURCE: PAGES (hit counter)
- Stored as: engine.neck.page_count (mirroring soul_count pattern)
- Accumulation: +1 page each time the player takes HP damage while talisman is equipped.
  Tracked in engine.py's damage-taken pathway (entity._on_damage_callback fires; engine
  should check if the player took damage and the talisman is equipped).
  Alternatively: hook in the energy loop where damage is dealt to player.
- Max pages: 15. Display via new "page_count" visual effect.
- Pages persist across floors; reset to 0 on unequip.

ABILITY 1: Tap Dodge Window
  ability_id: "tap_dodge_window"
  name: "Tap Dodge Window"
  description: "Spend 2 pages to gain a 100% dodge chance for 2 turns."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 2 pages
  Execute logic:
    - Check page_count >= 2
    - Apply new effect "dodge_window" (duration=2)
      dodge_window effect: sets player_stats.dodge_chance += 90 on apply, removes on expire.
      (100% clamped by system's 90% cap — effectively guaranteed dodge for 2 turns)
    - Deduct 2 pages
    - Message: "Tap Dodge! Next hits will miss for 2 turns. (-2 pages)"

ABILITY 2: Tap Shield
  ability_id: "tap_shield"
  name: "Tap Shield"
  description: "Spend ALL pages (min 5) to generate temp HP equal to 3x pages spent."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL pages (minimum 5)
  Execute logic:
    - Check page_count >= 5
    - temp = page_count * 3
    - engine.player.temp_hp = min(engine.player.temp_hp + temp, 60)  # cap at 60
    - Set page_count = 0
    - Message: "Tap Shield! +[temp] temp HP! (-[pages] pages)"
  BALANCE: 5 pages = 15 temp HP. 15 pages = 45 temp HP. 60 cap prevents runaway.

ABILITY 3: Tap Trauma Response
  ability_id: "tap_trauma_response"
  name: "Tap Trauma Response"
  description: "Spend 6 pages to enter a trauma state: +50% outgoing damage for 5 turns. Costs 3 HP to activate."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 6 pages + 3 HP
  Execute logic:
    - Check page_count >= 6 and player.hp > 3
    - Apply new effect "trauma_response" (duration=5):
        apply: add 1.5 to player_stats.outgoing_damage_mults
        expire: remove 1.5 from player_stats.outgoing_damage_mults
    - Deal 3 HP self-damage (no death — clamp at 1 HP if player would die)
    - Deduct 6 pages
    - Message: "Trauma Response! +50% dmg for 5 turns. (-6 pages, -3 HP)"

BALANCE:
- Takes damage to get pages; spending pages to dodge future damage is a virtuous loop.
- Tap Shield converts defensive suffering into a proactive shield — the more pain you take,
  the bigger the payoff.
- Trauma Response has a HP cost, so it's never free even with full pages.
- +15 armor + +1 CON make this a solid defensive neck item even ignoring abilities.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- engine.py: hook page_count increment on player damage-taken. The _on_damage_callback
  runs on all damage; check if entity is player and talisman is in engine.neck.
- Add page_count visual effect "tap_in_page_count" similar to soul_count.
- effects.py: DodgeWindowEffect (modifies dodge_chance), TraumaResponseEffect (adds/removes
  from outgoing_damage_mults).
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries.
- inventory_mgr.py: on neck unequip, reset page_count = 0 (add to existing neck unequip block).

ITEM 5: Crackhead Rocket Shoes
Slot: Feet | Char: 0xE00B | Color: (255, 50, 50) bright red
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONCEPT SUMMARY
Battered high-tops with what appears to be a half-assed jury-rigged propulsion system made from
WD-40 cans duct-taped to the heels. Grants "Rocket Blast" — you can point yourself at an enemy
and blast across the room like a missile, dealing massive collision damage. But 10% of the time,
the cans misfire and blast YOU backward instead.

      "grants_ability": "rocket_blast",

ABILITY: Rocket Blast
  ability_id: "rocket_blast"
  name: "Rocket Blast"
  description: "Blast to an enemy in LOS (up to 8 tiles). Deal STR*2 + 6 collision damage. 10% misfire: blast backward instead, taking STR damage. 12-turn cooldown."
  TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  max_range: 8.0
  Execute logic (_execute_at with tx, ty):
    - Confirm target is a living monster at (tx, ty) in FOV
    - 10% misfire check:
        If misfire: move player AWAY from target (opposite direction) 3 tiles,
        deal STR damage to player, message "MISFIRE! You blast backward! -STR dmg"
        return True (charge consumed, cooldown starts)
    - On success:
        - Move player to tile adjacent to target (one step away from tx, ty toward player)
        - Deal max(1, STR*2 + 6 - target.defense) collision damage to target
        - Apply "stun" effect (duration=2) to target (impact stun)
        - Message: "ROCKET BLAST! [target.name] takes [dmg] dmg and is stunned! (x2 turns)"
    - Set ability_cooldowns["rocket_blast"] = 12
  NOTE: "move to adjacent tile" means player ends up next to the target, not on them.
  Pathfind: landing tile = player pos + normalized direction * (dist-1), clamped to passable.

BALANCE:
- +30 energy/tick makes this one of the fastest feet items in the game.
- Rocket Blast is devastating (STR*2+6 = 22-30 damage at STR 8-12 before defense) with a stun.
- 10% misfire is a real risk in tight corridors — could kill you.
- 12-turn cooldown means you can't spam it.
- No armor bonus trades defense for pure offense/speed.
- Synergizes with Stabbing/Beating builds that want to close distance fast.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- abilities.py: new AbilityDef "rocket_blast" with execute/execute_at.
- The "move to adjacent tile" logic needs to compute the step toward the target and find
  the last passable tile before the enemy — similar to knockback logic but in reverse.
- Misfire direction: compute vector from player to target, invert it for the blast-back.
- StunEffect already exists in effects.py.
- inventory_mgr.py: grants_ability on feet already supported — no new infrastructure.
