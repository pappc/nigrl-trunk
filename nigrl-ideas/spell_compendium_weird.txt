NIGRL SPELL / ABILITY COMPENDIUM — SUPPLEMENT II
WEIRD, BUSTED, AND DANGEROUS ABILITIES
================================================================================

This supplement designs 34 spells and abilities that break conventions,
weaponize existing systems in unexpected ways, and come with genuine risk.
These are NOT polished utility spells. They are chaotic, powerful, and
potentially self-destructive — the kind of ability that makes a run memorable.

Design philosophy for this supplement:
  - Every ability has a COST or RISK, not just a cooldown
  - Many interact with NIGRL's background systems in non-obvious ways
    (the energy loop, toxicity, mutations, food buffs, AI state machine)
  - Some permanently change the rules of the run
  - Some scale inversely — more powerful when YOU are in trouble
  - All fit the gritty urban tone: no fantasy BS, all street logic

------------------------------------------------------------------------
W-A-01  STOP TIME
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Time Stop (D&D), Time Freeze (various roguelikes)
  NIGRL Name:  "Stop Time" — everything freezes. You keep moving.
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L5 activated perk (thematic capstone)
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      ONCE (once per run, cannot be recovered)
  Stat:        Book-Smarts
  Cost:        None (used in moment of desperation — the cost is burning your
               only ONCE ability on a non-ideal moment)

  Mechanic:
    When activated, set a flag engine._time_stopped = True and
    engine._time_stop_turns_remaining = 3 + BKS // 3 (minimum 3, max 7).
    While this flag is active, the _run_energy_loop() skips energy accumulation
    for all monsters (their energy does not tick upward). The player acts
    normally — they still gain energy and can take full actions each turn.
    From the player's perspective they get (3-7) free consecutive turns where
    no enemy moves or acts. Effects still tick on enemies (DoTs, burning, etc.)
    since those are triggered by the player's own turn resolution, not the
    monster energy loop. The flag auto-clears when turns_remaining reaches 0.
    Message on use: "The world goes still. Only you move."
    Message on expiry: "Time resumes. The street comes back to life."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    In a corridored roguelike, 3-7 free turns is DEVASTATING. You can reposition
    completely, eat food (eating turns ARE turns you spend), unload your gun into
    a frozen target, or simply run. On the wrong floor with 5 HP this is a
    guaranteed get-out-of-death-free card. The ONCE ChargeType means you have
    exactly one of these per run — every run you carry it around asking "is THIS
    the moment?" That tension is the fun.

  Implementation:
    Add engine._time_stopped: bool = False and
    engine._time_stop_turns_remaining: int = 0 to __init__.
    In _run_energy_loop, wrap the monster energy accumulation block with:
      if not self._time_stopped:
          [accumulate monster energy]
    else:
          self._time_stop_turns_remaining -= 1
          if self._time_stop_turns_remaining <= 0:
              self._time_stopped = False
              self.messages.append("Time resumes...")
    Edge cases: cannot activate while player is eating (eating is multi-turn
    action). If player is in the middle of eating, refuse activation.
    Gun targeting and other modes function normally since those are player-side.

  Balance:
    ONCE means you get exactly one. BKS-scaling on duration rewards investment
    (BKS 6 = 5 turns, BKS 12 = 7 turns). Enemies with ongoing effects STILL
    get ticked (the fire under them keeps burning) — this distinguishes it from
    a true game-pause and ensures it cannot trivially delete a stunned boss.

W-A-02  GEAR GRIND
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Haste + Exhaustion (D&D 5e), Swift Strider (DCSS)
  NIGRL Name:  "Gear Grind" — overdrive your system, then crash hard
  Source:      Meth-Head L3 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 25 turns
  Stat:        Constitution, Tolerance
  Cost:        On expiry: Crash debuff (−40 energy/tick for 8 turns)

  Mechanic:
    Apply GearGrindEffect to the player for 10 turns:
      - +80 energy/tick (nearly doubles speed at 180 energy/tick)
      - Player has 2 ACTION_POINTS — on each turn where player energy crosses
        threshold, they get a second free action (another move, attack, or item
        use). Implement by tracking engine._gear_grind_bonus_action_pending = True
        immediately after the first player action resolves; the engine processes
        a second input-free action that duplicates the previous action type
        (move repeats in same direction, attack repeats on same target if still
        alive, item use repeats on same item). This is complex — simpler MVP:
        just the +80 energy/tick with no second action.
      - On expire: apply CrashEffect: −40 energy/tick for 8 turns. Cannot use
        Gear Grind again until Crash expires (checked at ability use time).
    Duration scales with CON: 8 + CON // 3 turns.
    Crash duration scales with TOL inversely: 12 − TOL // 4 (min 6).

  Why it's busted/fun:
    At +180 effective energy/tick the player acts almost twice per monster turn.
    In practice, against a pack of enemies you will land 4-6 hits in the time
    they swing once. The crash afterward is genuinely scary — slow as a snail
    for 8 turns after the high wears off. This is the meth fantasy encoded into
    game mechanics.

  Implementation:
    New effects: GearGrindEffect (buff, modify_energy_gain returns amount+80),
    CrashEffect (debuff, modify_energy_gain returns amount-40). GearGrindEffect
    expire() calls apply_effect(player, engine, "crash", duration=...).
    CrashEffect needs on_reapply to block Gear Grind activation.

W-A-03  BORROW TIME
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Energy Drain (D&D), time theft
  NIGRL Name:  "Borrow Time" — steal turns directly from an enemy
  Source:      Negromancy L4 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 12 turns
  Stat:        Street-Smarts
  Cost:        None, but see mechanic

  Mechanic:
    Target a living monster. That monster's energy is set to 0 (hard reset —
    they just acted so they have to wait a full cycle). The player's energy is
    boosted by ENERGY_THRESHOLD (100) immediately, granting a free action.
    Message: "You reach out and take their moment. It's yours now."
    Essentially: steal the next action from one monster and give it to yourself.
    Scales with STS: if STS >= 10, also apply slow (ratio 0.5) for 3 turns to
    the target, making the stolen time worth more.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Not flashy — but mechanically dirty. Against a boss or high-threat enemy
    with 2 HP left, you don't need to outdamage them; you just need one more
    turn. Borrow Time gives you that turn while delaying theirs. This is
    'technically correct' time manipulation that interacts with the exact energy
    number the game tracks. Cooldown 12 turns means you can chain it every other
    fight if enemies go fast enough.

  Implementation:
    In execute_at: target.energy = 0
    engine.player.energy += ENERGY_THRESHOLD (from config)
    If player energy exceeds threshold * 2, cap at threshold * 1.5 to prevent
    stacking into double-turn cheese. Apply slow if STS >= 10.
    The player's bonus energy means the engine will immediately give them
    another turn next time through the energy loop — this is a free action.

W-B-01  JUST A LITTLE BIT MORE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Exposure (Brogue radiation mechanic idea), Gamma World
  NIGRL Name:  "Just a Little Bit More" — deliberately dose yourself
  Source:      Nuclear Research L3 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      TOTAL 3 uses
  Stat:        Constitution, Tolerance
  Cost:        +75 rad applied to self immediately

  Mechanic:
    Immediately grant +75 rad to the player. This crosses the weak mutation
    threshold (50 rad) and may trigger a mutation immediately (the normal
    per-tick check runs at end of turn after applying the rad). The player
    gets: +1 to a random stat (or any other mutation outcome), a flash message
    about the dose, and spends a TOTAL charge. Three uses per run because you
    probably only need 3 before you're either mutated usefully or dead.
    The REAL cost: 75 rad is not joke. If you're already at 40 rad, you're
    now at 115 rad — in the strong mutation tier. Two consecutive uses at 0 rad
    puts you at 150 rad — you WILL mutate, and it's 67% bad.
    The gamble: you're deliberately trying to trigger a specific tier of
    mutation, with full knowledge of the polarity odds.
    At CON 12 and TOL 12: your max HP survives most bad mutations. At CON 6
    and TOL 6: a bad strong mutation (-3 to a key stat) could end you.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    The mutation system already exists and is probabilistic. This turns a
    passive background hazard into an active decision. High-BKS players can
    do the math: "I have 30 rad, I want a strong mutation, I need 95 more rad,
    I'll use this twice." It's a knowledge-checks-as-power-lever design.
    Three charges means exactly 225 rad total from this ability — enough to
    hit huge mutation tier twice (250+ rad each) IF you time it with nat rad
    gain. Incredibly powerful if mutations go well; run-ending if they don't.

  Implementation:
    execute(): player.toxicity += 75 (err: this is rad, use rad field)
    Actually: engine.player.radiation += 75 (entity.radiation field from Meth Lab)
    Then trigger the normal per-tick mutation check immediately rather than
    waiting for next tick. Message: "You crack open another canister. Bad idea.
    Great idea. You feel it already."
    After rad application, call mutations.try_mutate(engine) directly.
    Edge case: if player has rad_resistance >= 100, the ability still applies
    rad (it's self-inflicted and intentional — bypasses resistance checks).

W-B-02  MUTATION GRENADE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Mutagenic bomb (various games), D&D 5e Chaos Bolt
  NIGRL Name:  "Mutation Grenade" — weaponize radiation as targeted chaos
  Source:      Nuclear Research L5 activated perk (or drop from Chemist enemy)
  Target:      AOE_CIRCLE radius 2, SINGLE_LOS to pick center
  Charge:      TOTAL 2
  Stat:        Book-Smarts
  Cost:        The player ALSO receives 30 rad from proximity splash

  Mechanic:
    Throw a mutation grenade at a tile in FOV (range 10). All monsters within
    radius 2 take 30 rad immediately. Each affected monster rolls the mutation
    check independently using the existing mutations.try_mutate() logic adapted
    for monsters. Monster mutations are simplified: a random stat is reduced or
    increased by 1, immediately affecting their combat math. The player receives
    30 rad splash from proximity (there's no clean radiation — you're standing
    on the same floor). If BKS >= 10, the player receives only 15 rad.
    Message for each monster hit: "[Name] is hit by radiation! It mutates..."
    Monster mutation roll: 33% good (power+2 or defense+2), 67% bad (power-2
    or defense-2 or hp-10). These are simple stat mutations on entity fields.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    AOE control that's totally chaotic. A room full of enemies might come out
    STRONGER from bad targeting. The player takes radiation too. Two total uses
    means you're burning a scarce resource on a gamble. In optimal play vs a
    boss: throw at feet of a strong enemy hoping for a bad mutation that nerfs
    their offense. Can also be used to finish a cluster of weakened enemies if
    you pray for the bad mutation outcome (hp-10 mutant dies).

  Implementation:
    New execute_at function. Iterate entities within Chebyshev(2) of target.
    For each living monster: entity.radiation += 30, then call simplified
    _monster_mutation(entity, engine) which does: rand < 0.33 → good, else bad.
    Good: random.choice(["power", "defense"]), entity[field] += 2.
    Bad: random.choice(["power", "defense", "hp"]).
    If "hp": entity.take_damage(10), else entity[field] = max(0, entity[field] - 2).
    Player: engine.player.radiation += 30 (or 15 with BKS >= 10).

W-B-03  IRRADIATE SELF
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Arcane Surge (Path of Exile), Rage mechanic (various)
  NIGRL Name:  "Irradiate Self" — bath in radiation for a temporary power surge
  Source:      Mutation skill L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 20 turns
  Stat:        Tolerance (resistance to self-harm)
  Cost:        +50 rad, applied to self

  Mechanic:
    Apply a 10-turn buff: RadSurgeEffect.
      - +4 power (raw melee damage boost)
      - +10% dodge chance (you're moving erratically, glowing, hard to hit)
      - On every melee hit deal an extra 1d6 radiation damage to the target
        (their radiation field increases by (3-8) per hit, pushing them toward
        radiation-triggered death on Meth Lab floors — or just extra DoT
        elsewhere)
    Cost: +50 rad to player on activation.
    With rad_resistance, the actual rad gain is reduced proportionally.
    Without resistance: you're aggressively shortening your mutation window.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    +4 power is significant (equivalent to a strong weapon upgrade). The
    melee radiation transfer means you're spreading hazard to enemies. On Meth
    Lab floors where enemies also have toxicity mechanics, the secondary
    radiation build-up on enemies has compounding consequences. The cost
    builds your own radiation — eventually you will mutate, and each use of
    this moves the clock forward.

W-C-01  PURGE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Osmosis (Caves of Qud), toxin expulsion
  NIGRL Name:  "Purge" — expel your toxins as a weapon
  Source:      Chemical Warfare L3 activated perk
  Target:      AOE_CIRCLE radius 1 centered on player (immediate, no targeting)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 8 turns
  Stat:        Constitution, Tolerance
  Cost:        Player loses ALL accumulated toxicity (set to 0)

  Mechanic:
    The player vomits out their entire accumulated toxicity as a cloud hitting
    all adjacent monsters. Damage dealt to each monster = player.toxicity // 5
    (minimum 0). Each affected monster gains toxicity equal to the damage dealt
    (in their toxicity field, if they have one — Meth Lab enemies — or just
    take the damage directly as a tox-flavored hit with no tox field change).
    Player toxicity is then set to 0. Message: "You heave your guts out. What
    comes up is green and wrong. The enemies near you recoil."
    Scale with CON: if CON >= 10, damage multiplier increases from //5 to //4.
    Scale with TOL: if TOL >= 10, the player retains 20% of toxicity (instead
    of full purge) — they're TOO tolerant to fully expel it, but also take
    less long-term harm.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    This is AMAZING if you're already poisoned. High toxicity is normally
    terrible for the player (damage mult formula). Now it's fuel. A player who
    has been running through toxic hazards and is sitting at 200 toxicity can
    Purge for 40 damage to everything adjacent — while simultaneously resetting
    their damage multiplier from ~3.5x back to 1.0x. This is a double win:
    offense AND defense. The TOL interaction is wonderfully counterintuitive:
    being TOL-built means you can't fully purge (drug tolerance = hard to
    vomit properly). CON-built players get more damage per unit of toxicity.

  Implementation:
    execute(): dmg = engine.player.toxicity // (4 if CON >= 10 else 5)
    For each entity in Chebyshev(1) that is a living monster:
      entity.take_damage(dmg)
      if hasattr(entity, "toxicity"): entity.toxicity += dmg
    Retain_ratio = 0.2 if TOL >= 10 else 0.0
    engine.player.toxicity = int(engine.player.toxicity * retain_ratio)
    engine._recalculate_tox_damage_mult() (update the combat multiplier)

W-C-02  SEPTIC AURA
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Poison Cloud (NetHack), Radiation Aura (DCSS Transmuter)
  NIGRL Name:  "Septic Aura" — you leak toxins passively for a while
  Source:      Chemical Warfare L5 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      /FL 1 (once per floor)
  Stat:        Tolerance
  Cost:        +30 toxicity to self when activated; during duration, player
               gains +5 toxicity per turn (aura is leaking into them too)

  Mechanic:
    Apply SepticAuraEffect for (6 + TOL // 3) turns.
    Each turn, all monsters within Chebyshev(2) of the player gain 15 toxicity
    and take _monster_toxicity_multiplier(15) bonus damage from the aura leakage
    (damage = 15 * _monster_toxicity_multiplier(entity.toxicity) but at minimum 1).
    Simpler approach: each adjacent monster takes 5 raw damage per turn from
    proximity, and gains 10 toxicity if they have a toxicity field.
    During the aura, player gains +5 tox/turn (they're surrounded by their own
    cloud and inhaling it). This is tracked in SepticAuraEffect.tick().
    On expire: nothing except the aura dissipates. Player's accumulated tox
    from the side effect remains.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Passive AoE damage. In a corridor, every enemy that enters your space takes
    5 damage/turn from proximity. At TOL 12: duration 10 turns — 10 turns of
    the enemy just sitting next to you and taking 5/turn. Combined with your
    melee attacks, this is 5 free damage per monster per turn in melee range.
    The player also accumulates +50 toxicity over a 10-turn duration — this
    HURTS their damage intake via the tox mult. The tradeoff: you're spending
    your own health (indirect) to deal passive damage. An Iron Lung / high-CON
    player can sustain this comfortably; a glass-cannon player suffers.

W-C-03  VENOM EXCHANGE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Swap curse (DCSS), damage link
  NIGRL Name:  "Venom Exchange" — link your toxicity to an enemy
  Source:      Chemical Warfare L4 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 6)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 15 turns
  Stat:        Street-Smarts
  Cost:        None, but requires player to have > 20 toxicity to use

  Mechanic:
    Target a monster. Validation: player must have toxicity >= 20. On fire:
    - Player and target swap toxicity values
    - If target does not have a toxicity field (non-Meth Lab enemy): player
      simply transfers HALF their toxicity to the target as damage
      (damage = player.toxicity // 2; player.toxicity = player.toxicity // 2)
    - If target DOES have toxicity: straight swap — player.tox becomes
      target.tox; target.tox becomes player.tox
    Message: "You push your poison across the gap. It's their problem now."
    Scales with STS: if STS >= 10, transfer 75% instead of 50% to non-tox
    enemies; straight swap for tox-enabled enemies remains the same.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Swap is an incredible mechanic against high-toxicity enemies (Toxic Slug,
    Sludge Amalgam, Purger) who have enormous toxicity values. Swapping with
    a Toxic Slug that has 500 toxicity is WILD — you briefly become nearly
    unkillable from their melee while they take (1 + (500/100)^0.6 * x)
    incoming damage from everything. And your own toxicity is now 0 or near 0.
    Against non-tox enemies: a dirty half-transfer that deals respectable damage
    while cleaning your own slate. Requires 20 tox minimum so it can't be used
    from zero — you actually have to have GOTTEN poisoned for this to activate.

W-D-01  FORCE FEED
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Forceful Feeding (original), Quaff of Feeding (Nethack variant)
  NIGRL Name:  "Force Feed" — shove food down an enemy's throat
  Source:      Munching L4 activated perk
  Target:      ADJACENT
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 8 turns
  Stat:        Strength
  Cost:        Consumes one food item from inventory (chosen before activation;
               fails if no food present)

  Mechanic:
    Player selects an adjacent enemy. The ability opens a sub-menu (or auto-
    picks the first food in inventory if only one food present). Consume one
    food item from inventory. The food's effects are applied to the ENEMY as
    their own buff/debuff (inverted for harmful context where possible). Logic:
      - "heal" type foods: deal that amount as damage to the enemy instead
        (you choke them on it — the caloric energy hits wrong)
      - "hot" (HoT) foods: apply as a DOT to the enemy (same amounts/duration
        as the HoT but dealing damage each tick instead of healing)
      - "speed_boost" foods: apply SLOW instead (ratio 0.5, duration matching
        the speed boost duration — inverse effect on foreign system)
      - "hot_cheetos" foods: apply 2 ignite stacks to the enemy and a 5-turn
        DOT of 2/turn (from the spice)
      - Food with stat effects: apply RabiesEffect equivalent (−1 each key stat
        for the food's normal duration) — forced overconsumption ruins stats
    The enemy does not "eat" (no multi-turn process) — you SHOVE it in.
    Damage bonus from STR: heal foods deal +STR//2 extra on inversion.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Chicken deals 30-50 damage + STR bonus as a single-use ability use. On
    a weakened enemy that's already near death, consuming your Chicken to
    Force Feed it does MORE damage than a normal attack. It also uses your
    food as a resource in a new way: do you eat the Instant Ramen for a speed
    boost, or Force Feed it to the fast enemy to slow them down? Hot Cheetos
    becomes an AoE ignition tool against one adjacent target. The mechanic
    rewards food collection beyond mere self-buffing.

  Implementation:
    execute(): check food items in inventory. If none, fail.
    If one food: auto-select. If multiple: open a minimal selection sub-menu
    (simplification: just use the first food item in inventory, documented that
    players should sort food to front).
    Retrieve food_def. Apply inverted effects. Remove food from inventory.
    Message: "You CRAM the [food] down their throat. They hate it."

W-D-02  SYMPATHY EAT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Original (eat your enemy's pain)
  NIGRL Name:  "Sympathy Eat" — when they hurt, you eat
  Source:      Munching L5 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      /FL 2
  Stat:        Constitution
  Cost:        None; buff expires on floor change

  Mechanic:
    Apply SympathyEatEffect for (8 + CON // 2) turns.
    While the effect is active, every time the player deals melee damage to an
    enemy, they heal for (damage_dealt // 4) HP. This is applied in the

    Message on activation: "You channel your hunger. Every blow feeds you."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Sustained lifesteal on every melee hit that doesn't require anything other

    existing Lifesteal effect from Jungle Boyz strains, this stacks additively.
    Two charges per floor means you can sustain for two separate fight clusters.
    The "eat" framing (healing through violence while buffed by your hunger) is
    thematically consistent with the Munching skill's survivalist angle.

W-D-03  BOTTOMLESS PIT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Gluttony mechanic, Eating Contest challenge
  NIGRL Name:  "Bottomless Pit" — eat everything, regardless of context
  Source:      Munching L3 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      ONCE (once per run — you can only be this hungry once)
  Stat:        Constitution
  Cost:        After the effect ends, apply Hungover (1 stack) — the gorging
               catches up with you

  Mechanic:
    Apply BottomlessPitEffect for 5 turns. During this time:
      - Eating time is set to 1 turn regardless of food (all foods complete
        in 1 turn — the eat_length is overridden to 1 during this buff)
      - All food effects are doubled (heal amounts x2, HoT durations x2,
        speed boost amounts x2)
      - The player can eat while moving (normally eating prevents all actions —
        during this buff, eating is a free action that happens alongside movement)
    Implemented by: BottomlessPitEffect sets engine._bottomless_pit_active = True.
    In the eating-resolution code, check this flag:
      - if flag: eat_length = 1 (complete immediately), double all effect values
    On expire: apply HangoverEffect(stacks=1) — you ate too much.
    Duration 5 turns: that's 5 food items you can emergency-inhale.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Five turns to eat anything in your inventory instantly and with doubled
    effects. Stack Chicken (60-100 instant heal) + Instant Ramen (+100
    energy/tick speed for 40 turns) + Hot Cheetos (4/2 all stats for 60 turns)
    in rapid succession. This is a legitimate mid-combat healing rotation. The
    Hangover cost is annoying but manageable. The ONCE constraint means you
    save it for the right moment — it's a panic button that just happens to
    work by eating.

SECTION W-E — SCALING WITH YOUR OWN SUFFERING

W-E-01  FROM THE GRAVE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Last Gasp (Path of Exile), Second Chance (DCSS)
  NIGRL Name:  "From the Grave" — the less HP you have, the more you hit
  Source:      Negromancy L2 activated perk (passive once unlocked)
  Target:      SELF (passive, no activation needed)
  Charge:      N/A (passive ability — unlocked, always active)
  Stat:        Street-Smarts (crit scaling at low HP)
  Cost:        Designed around being at death's door — the "cost" is the
               risk of staying at low HP

  Mechanic:
    Passive. Track player HP percentage. The following bonuses apply based
    on current HP / max HP:
      - Below 75% HP: +2 damage on all attacks
      - Below 50% HP: +5 damage on all attacks; +10% crit chance
      - Below 25% HP: +10 damage on all attacks; +20% crit chance; all
        incoming damage reduced by 2 (minimum 1 still applies)
    These bonuses are computed in the attack-resolve and defense-resolve code,
    checked at the time of each action. They are NOT applied as status effects
    (so they can't be removed). They're conditional bonuses calculated
    on-the-fly each time.
    At STS >= 10: additional +5% crit at each threshold.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Forces a completely different playstyle. Normally: stay healthy, don't take
    risks. With From the Grave: you WANT to be at 24% HP. A low-HP player with
    this passive is critting on 35-50% of swings for double damage, with +10
    bonus on top. This is genuinely terrifying because it rewards the most
    dangerous state in a permadeath game. The 25% DR at sub-25% HP means you
    also live slightly longer there, extending the window. The player becomes
    most dangerous at the edge of death — very on-theme for the street setting.

  Implementation:
    In combat.py _compute_player_attack: check hp_ratio.
    Add conditional:
      bonus_dmg = 0; bonus_crit = 0; dr = 0
      if ratio < 0.75: bonus_dmg += 2
      if ratio < 0.50: bonus_dmg += 5; bonus_crit += 10
      if ratio < 0.25: bonus_dmg += 10; bonus_crit += 20; dr = 2
    Add bonus_dmg to final damage (after min(1,x-defense)).
    Bonus_crit adds to player's crit_chance.
    dr subtracts from incoming damage (after armor, before min(1)).
    Flag to check: check if player has "from_the_grave" passive unlocked.
    Store as engine._from_the_grave_active: bool = False, set True when perk
    unlocked.

W-E-02  DEBT COLLECTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Payback mechanic, inverse scaling
  NIGRL Name:  "Debt Collection" — spend your HP like currency
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L3 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 6 turns
  Stat:        Constitution (survival), Book-Smarts (damage calc)
  Cost:        Player spends 10 HP to activate. If player HP <= 10, fail.

  Mechanic:
    Player spends 10 HP (take_damage(10), bypassing armor but NOT death — if
    HP would drop to 0 or below, the ability is refused). Damage dealt to
    target = 15 + (engine.player.max_hp - engine.player.hp) * 0.4.
    This means: the more total HP the player has LOST from max, the more damage
    this spell does. At full HP, with 10 HP spent to cast: 15 + (10 * 0.4) = 19
    damage (spending HP for only 19 damage is bad). At 50 HP missing + cast
    cost: 15 + (60 * 0.4) = 39 damage. At 100 HP missing: 15 + (110 * 0.4) = 59
    damage. Scale with BKS: multiply base by (1 + BKS * 0.05).
    Message: "You reach into yourself and pull something ugly out."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    The more desperate you are, the better this spell is. A player with 150 max
    HP who is at 30 HP has "lost" 120 HP — Debt Collection fires for
    15 + 130*0.4 = 67 damage at BKS 6, ~87 damage at BKS 12. That's one of the
    highest single-target damage values in the game, available from a desperate
    state. The self-HP cost means you're gambling: spending 10 more HP when
    you're already at 30 to fish for a kill-shot.

W-E-03  DESPERATION STRIKE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Berserk (Norse mythology), Fury builds (PoE, D3)
  NIGRL Name:  "Desperation Strike" — all in, one shot, nothing left to lose
  Source:      Beating L4 activated perk (requires beating weapon equipped)
  Target:      ADJACENT
  Charge:      FLOOR_ONLY 1 (one use per floor, not carried over)
  Stat:        Strength, Constitution
  Cost:        After use: apply BurntOutEffect: player cannot attack for 3 turns
               (their arms give out from the exertion)

  Mechanic:
    Single adjacent attack. Damage = (STR * 4) + (player.max_hp - player.hp)
    (raw, ignoring defense and armor). This always crits (x2 multiplier). No
    defense roll at all — the target takes the full value.
    At STR 10, 80 HP missing: (10*4) + 80 = 120 damage, doubled = 240.
    This is an instant-kill button against almost any non-boss enemy.
    Requires a beating weapon. Requires at least 2 living monsters in LOS
    (you're pushing yourself past the limit because there's no choice — a
    single enemy doesn't justify it). Actually: remove this requirement —
    too complex. Just: requires beating weapon, FLOOR_ONLY.
    After use: BurntOutEffect: "before_turn" returns True for 3 turns.
    Message: "You put EVERYTHING behind it. Every ounce. Now you've got nothing."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Guaranteed one-shot most enemies if used at low HP. The FLOOR_ONLY charge
    means you get exactly one per floor, and it's gone whether you use it or
    not. "I save this for the boss room" is a legitimate strategy — but then
    you descend without it. The 3-turn lockout afterward is terrifying in a
    dungeon — if there's another enemy nearby, you cannot attack for 3 full
    turns. You have to run or tank it. Extremely high risk, highest melee
    damage in the game when it works.

W-F-01  SPOOK
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Scare (NetHack), Fear (D&D)
  NIGRL Name:  "Spook" — flip an enemy to FLEEING state directly
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L1 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 10 turns
  Stat:        Swagger, Street-Smarts
  Cost:        None

  Mechanic:
    Target a monster. Set monster.ai_state = AIState.FLEEING and apply a
    new SpookedEffect (duration: 3 + SWG // 3). While SpookedEffect is active,
    the AI state machine's normal transition logic is suppressed for this enemy
    (they cannot re-enter CHASING even if they would normally). The effect
    hook before_turn does NOT return True (the enemy still acts — they just
    act scared). Their movement uses the existing FLEEING action, which moves
    them away from the player.
    On the turn after SpookedEffect expires, the enemy re-evaluates AI state
    normally (likely snapping back to CHASING if player is in sight radius).
    Scales with STS: if STS >= 9, also apply a 2-turn stun at the START of
    the Spook (the jump-scare moment freezes them briefly).
    Fails against enemies with no FLEEING behavior in their ai_type (room_guard
    types ignore spook — they don't run). Message on failure: "[Name] stares
    back, unimpressed."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Direct AI state manipulation. In a corridor situation with 3 chasing enemies,
    Spook the front one and it runs BACK into the cluster, blocking the others'
    path. It creates useful terrain by using the enemy as a blocker. The
    cooldown (10 turns) means you can't spam it, but in the right corridor it
    rearranges the entire combat geometry.

  Implementation:
    In execute_at: find monster at (tx,ty). Check ai_type not in ["room_guard"].
    Set monster.ai_state = AIState.FLEEING.
    Apply SpookedEffect(duration = 3 + SWG // 3) to monster.
    SpookedEffect: before_turn returns False (doesn't skip turn — runs their
    AI but overrides transitions). Need to block the transition back to CHASING
    while effect is active. Easiest: store monster.ai_state = AIState.FLEEING
    at start of each before_turn call and return False (keep overwriting state).
    Actually the cleaner approach: SpookedEffect.before_turn sets
    monster.ai_state = AIState.FLEEING (without returning True) each turn.
    This forces FLEEING regardless of transition logic — aggressive but clean.

W-F-02  MAKE FRIENDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Charm Person (D&D), Dominate (DCSS), Taming (ADOM)
  NIGRL Name:  "Make Friends" — briefly convince an enemy you're cool
  Source:      Stealing L4 activated perk (social engineering)
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 5)
  Charge:      /FL 1
  Stat:        Swagger
  Cost:        None, but see restriction

  Mechanic:
    Target a living non-boss monster. Apply CharmedEffect (duration: SWG // 2,
    minimum 3, maximum 8 turns). While charmed:
      - Monster's AI state is locked to WANDERING (they meander randomly, not
        toward player)
      - If player attacks the charmed monster, effect immediately expires and
        they snap to CHASING with +2 speed for 5 turns (anger rebound)
      - The charmed monster does NOT attack the player if adjacent (their
        attack action is skipped while charmed — check flag before combat)
    Fails if monster is already in FLEEING state (can't charm a fleeing enemy).
    Message: "You give them that look. For a moment, they think you're alright."
    With SWG >= 12: extends duration to (SWG // 2 + 3) and the charmed monster
    will attack other monsters if they wander adjacent to one (temporary ally).

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Turn a danger into a neutral or even an ally for 3-6 turns. Against a
    high-HP enemy you can't kill: charm them, run past them, get what you need.
    The SWG 12 extended version where they attack other enemies is EXTREMELY
    powerful — you're using a tough enemy as a temporary shield or aggressor.
    One charge per floor keeps it from being infinite repositioning.

W-F-03  SNITCH
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Alarm (various roguelikes), Alert mechanic
  NIGRL Name:  "Snitch" — deliberately trigger all enemies to alert
  Source:      Jaywalking L4 (you know how the street works — now you exploit it)
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      TOTAL 3
  Stat:        Street-Smarts (determines what you do with the chaos)
  Cost:        All dormant enemies on the floor become ALERTED (ai_state = CHASING)

  Mechanic:
    This ability makes every dormant (IDLE or WANDERING) monster on the floor
    enter CHASING state simultaneously, as if the floor alarm triggered.
    Additionally, set dungeon.first_kill_happened = True (the game already has
    this mechanism for triggering alarm_chaser enemies).
    WHY would you want this? Because "Snitch" is designed to be OFFENSIVE: you
    want them all to come to you because you have a trap set, an AoE ready,
    or because you want to run them into a corner. The player who uses Snitch
    is saying "I know where they all are, I want them all here, now."
    STS scaling: if STS >= 10, the player also gets +20 energy immediately
    (you anticipated this moment and tensed up first).
    3 TOTAL charges because this is a tactical commitment, not a panic button.
    Message: "You shout. Every head on the floor turns."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Total chaos tool that rewards preparation. Use it after placing fire traps
    (place_fire ability), setting a cloudkill trap, or just because you've
    cleared enough of the floor that you want the last 5 enemies to walk into
    your kill corridor. It's "wrong" from a survival standpoint and "right"
    from a calculated roguelike brain standpoint. The TOTAL limit prevents
    it from becoming a routine mob-calling trick.

W-G-01  BURN THE MAP
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Scroll of Amnesia (NetHack), Nuclear Bomb (various)
  NIGRL Name:  "Burn the Map" — permanently alter the floor
  Source:      Pyromania L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      ONCE (one use ever)
  Stat:        N/A
  Cost:        The current floor is set ablaze: EVERY non-player entity on the
               floor instantly gains the "ignite" effect (2 stacks each). ALL
               items on the floor are destroyed. Crates explode (contents lost).
               The player ALSO gains ignite (1 stack, duration 4).

  Mechanic:
    On activation: iterate ALL entities on current dungeon floor.
    For every living monster: apply_effect(entity, engine, "ignite", duration=8,
    stacks=2, silent=True). For every item entity: dungeon.remove_entity(entity).
    For every crate hazard: dungeon.remove_entity(entity).
    Apply ignite x1 to player: apply_effect(player, engine, "ignite", duration=4).
    Message: "You pull out your lighter and just... start. Floor by floor.
    Everything burns. Even things that shouldn't."
    The burning DoT from ignite will kill most enemies within 8-12 turns.
    Heavily armored enemies survive longer but are still damaged each turn.
    The loss of all floor items is the real cost — no loot from this floor.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Instant floor clear at the cost of all items. One use ever. This is the
    nuclear option — you press it when surrounded, when cornered, when the floor
    has given you nothing but enemies. It's also potentially the worst decision
    you can make if you desperately needed the loot. The player ignite is a
    gentle "you dumb idiot" reminder that fire doesn't care who started it.
    The pyromania theming is perfect: level 4 of "I love fire" and you literally
    set the whole floor on fire.

  Implementation:
    ONCE charge type. execute(): iterate engine.dungeon.entities (copy the list
    first since we're modifying it). Apply ignite to all monsters. Remove all
    items and crates. Apply ignite to player. Set flag engine._burn_the_map_used.
    One subtle implementation note: some enemies may already be dead (from
    combat this turn) — check entity.alive before applying ignite.

W-G-02  SIGN THE LEASE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Bless Weapon (D&D), permanent enchantment
  NIGRL Name:  "Sign the Lease" — bind your weapon to you permanently
  Source:      Rolling L5 activated perk (ritual blunt smoking as a contract)
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      ONCE
  Stat:        Tolerance
  Cost:        You can NEVER unequip or replace the bound weapon again

  Mechanic:
    Consumes a joint (one cannabis item from inventory — fails if none present).
    The currently equipped weapon is permanently bound:
    - Set engine._lease_weapon_id = engine.equipment["weapon"].instance_id
    - The equipment screen cannot remove or replace this weapon; attempting to
      do so shows: "You signed the lease. That's your weapon now."
    - The weapon gains permanent bonuses: power_bonus += 3, and the weapon's
      char is rendered in gold color.
    - Additionally: when attacking with the bound weapon, 15% chance to proc
      "Second Wind" — gain 5 HP.
    TOL scaling: if TOL >= 10, the weapon also gains a 20% chance to return
    1 ammo to a currently equipped gun each time you attack (the joint ritual
    blessed your whole fighting style).

  Why it's busted/fun:
    +3 damage permanently on your chosen weapon is significant. The 15% 5HP
    proc across a whole run adds up to meaningful healing. But you can NEVER
    change weapons. If you find a stronger weapon, it rots in your inventory.
    If your bound weapon is mediocre, you've signed your combat potential away.
    Deciding WHEN to use Sign the Lease is a meta-game: early for more fights
    with the bonus but higher chance of finding something better; late for a
    weapon you KNOW is good but fewer fights remaining.

W-G-03  TAKE THE DEAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Devil's Bargain, Faustian pact (original)
  NIGRL Name:  "Take the Deal" — sacrifice a stat permanently for raw power
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF
  Charge:      TOTAL 1 per stat (can use up to 6 times total, once per stat)
  Stat:        Book-Smarts (higher BKS = you understand the contract better)
  Cost:        PERMANENTLY reduce one chosen stat by 3. Cannot be reversed.

  Mechanic:
    On activation: open a stat selection sub-menu (or cycle with arrow keys).
    Choose a stat to sacrifice. That stat's base value is reduced by 3
    (permanent — uses modify_base_stat, not a temporary bonus).
    In return: +50 skill points, +15 to a random other stat temporarily (10
    turns), and the player gains a permanent +2 bonus to power (raw damage).
    The BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, the temporary +15 stat bonus becomes
    permanent +5 instead. At BKS < 8, the power bonus is +1 instead of +2.
    Message: "The deal is done. You feel the loss immediately. But something
    else fills the gap."
    Limit: once per stat — you can sacrifice CON, STR, etc. each once.
    Stack up to 6 total sacrifices for potentially enormous power if you
    can sustain the stat losses.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Sacrificing Constitution (-3 CON = -30 max HP permanently) for +2 power
    and 50 skill points is a brutally calculated tradeoff. At high BKS, the
    bonus becomes permanent +5 to another stat — effectively a free +5 to
    your best stat at the cost of -3 in your worst. Stacking 6 uses: -3 to
    all stats, but +12 damage, +300 skill points, and +30 to various stats.
    The math can go positive if you're clever. High risk of stat collapse.

W-H-01  FOLD
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Spatial Fold (original), D&D Astral Projection
  NIGRL Name:  "Fold" — fold space so two tiles are adjacent
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L3 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 12 — unusually large)
  Charge:      /FL 2
  Stat:        Book-Smarts
  Cost:        Player cannot move for 2 turns after use (spatial disorientation)

  Mechanic:
    Target any tile in FOV (including one with a monster on it — can target
    them). The player teleports to that tile's adjacent tile (chosen as the
    open cardinal adjacent to the target that is closest to the player's
    original position — avoids teleporting INTO a wall). This is NOT full
    teleport to the target tile — you appear beside it.
    Use case: there's an enemy across the room with a wall between you. Fold
    closes the distance in one action. Or: an item in a room you haven't
    entered yet — Fold to its doorway without crossing the room.
    After use: FoldDisorientEffect (2 turns, prevents movement — player can
    attack, use items, wait, but cannot move). This simulates spatial dissonance.
    BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, the disorientation is reduced to 1 turn.
    At BKS >= 12: no disorientation at all.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Gap closer AND room traversal. Against a ranged enemy who is standing far
    from you: Fold closes the distance instantly. Before they can use their
    ranged attack next turn, you're adjacent and in melee range. The 2-turn
    movement lockout means you're committed to fighting whoever you folded next
    to — can't fold and run. BKS 12 with no lockout is genuinely powerful but
    requires massive stat investment.

W-H-02  POCKET DIMENSION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Bag of Holding (D&D), DCSS scroll of mapping
  NIGRL Name:  "Pocket Dimension" — phase an item out of existence temporarily
  Source:      Dismantling L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF (opens inventory — select item to phase out)
  Charge:      TOTAL 5
  Stat:        Book-Smarts
  Cost:        Item is gone for 3 floors; retrieved automatically on 3rd descent

  Mechanic:
    Player selects one item from inventory. The item is removed from inventory
    and stored in engine._pocket_dimension: list of {"item": entity, "floor":
    current_floor + 3}. The item disappears completely.
    On each floor descent, engine checks _pocket_dimension entries and returns
    any with target_floor == current_floor, adding them back to inventory with
    a message: "Something falls out of the air into your hands. Right, you
    forgot about that."
    Why useful? Weight reduction (if game had weight), but mainly: stash an
    item you can't use now but need later (ammo for a gun you don't have yet;
    a food item you want for a specific floor; a key weapon you found too early).
    BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, the return delay is 2 floors instead of 3.
    TOTAL 5 uses because this is a logistics ability, not an infinite save slot.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Temporal inventory management. "I found a High-Caliber Round but no gun.
    Pocket Dimension it, find a gun in 3 floors, get the ammo back." Or: "I'm
    overloaded with items (if inventory is limited), I'll phase out the less
    urgent one for later." Five uses is a decent number for a 4-floor zone.
    Mostly utility, but "utterly broken utility" in a game where the right item
    at the right moment can win a run.

W-I-01  SPITE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Reflect damage (various), "Hurt me? I dare you." archetype
  NIGRL Name:  "Spite" — when you're debuffed, charge into them harder
  Source:      Smacking L3 activated perk
  Target:      SELF (passive)
  Charge:      N/A (passive)
  Stat:        Tolerance
  Cost:        None — the debuffs are already a cost

  Mechanic:
    Passive. For each unique debuff effect currently on the player, all
    melee attacks deal +3 damage. This scales with debuff count, not stacks:
    having 2 stacks of Chill counts as 1 debuff. Check by counting unique
    effect IDs where category == "debuff".
    At TOL >= 10: bonus increases to +4 per unique debuff.
    Message when a debuff is applied while Spite is active: "+X damage (Spite)".
    Maximum 8 unique debuffs tracked (cap bonus at +24 damage at TOL >= 10).
    This explicitly rewards getting debuffed: being Chilled, Shocked, Feared,
    and Bleeding while fighting gives you +12 base damage (+16 at TOL 10).

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Emergent interaction with the debuff ecosystem. Enemies that stack debuffs
    (like the chain-lightning shocked effect, the Jungle Boyz strain, or rabies)
    are actively BUFFING you. A player who understands Spite will sometimes
    choose NOT to cure debuffs because the power bonus is worth more than the
    removal. At 6 stacks (Spite plus Agent Orange plus Rabies plus Chill plus
    Shocked plus Bleeding): +18 damage on every swing. That's insane at any HP
    level.

  Implementation:
    In engine.py, _compute_player_attack_power(): if engine._spite_active:
      debuff_count = len(set(e.id for e in player.status_effects
                             if e.category == "debuff"))
      bonus_per = 4 if TOL >= 10 else 3
      return base + min(8, debuff_count) * bonus_per
    engine._spite_active = False by default, set True when perk unlocked.

W-I-02  TRANSMUTE PAIN
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Alchemy of suffering (original), Pain as Power
  NIGRL Name:  "Transmute Pain" — convert your active debuffs into a burst
  Source:      Smacking L5 activated perk
  Target:      AOE_CIRCLE radius 2, centered on player (immediate)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 15 turns
  Stat:        Street-Smarts, Tolerance
  Cost:        ALL debuffs on the player are cleared when used (the fuel is spent)

  Mechanic:
    Collect all unique debuffs currently on the player. Calculate damage:
    base = 8 per unique debuff. All enemies within Chebyshev(2) take this
    damage. Additionally, each enemy gains the "shocked" effect (1 stack, 5t)
    for each of the player's debuffs that had an energy effect (chill, slow,
    stun, gouge = these are "energy debuffs"). Then: all debuffs are cleared
    from the player simultaneously.
    STS scaling: +STS//3 bonus damage per debuff.
    TOL scaling: if TOL >= 10, you retain ONE debuff of your choice (you've
    internalized it — you WANT to keep it, probably for Spite).
    Message: "You alchemize the pain. Every curse becomes a hammer. The
    enemies around you take the hit."
    At 4 unique debuffs: 32 base damage (+ STS bonus) to everything in radius 2.
    That clears most enemies on a mid-floor.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    The "I've been beaten to hell, now I'm going to use that" moment. You've
    been Chilled, Shocked, Bleeding, and Feared — terrible debuffs accumulating
    for several turns. Transmute Pain: 32 AoE damage, shocked applied to all,
    and you're suddenly clean. Combined with Spite: you were at +12 bonus while
    debuffed, now you do the AoE nova, and you're clean but weaker. Unless you
    have TOL 10 and kept the best one. The decision of WHEN to nova is the fun.

W-I-03  HANGOVER FUEL
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Fighting through the hangover, "hair of the dog" extreme
  NIGRL Name:  "Hangover Fuel" — your hangover is your pre-workout
  Source:      Alcoholism L4 activated perk (item: can of Red Bull with hangover)
  Target:      SELF (passive)
  Charge:      N/A (passive, always-on once unlocked)
  Stat:        Tolerance
  Cost:        None — the hangover stacks are the already-existing cost of drinking

  Mechanic:
    Passive. While the player has a HangoverEffect active, for each stack of
    Hangover, the player gains:
      - +10 energy/tick (more speed per hangover stack)
      - +1 power (raw damage per hangover stack)
    These are computed dynamically: check the HangoverEffect on the player,
    read its .stacks field, add (stacks * 10) to speed and (stacks * 1) to
    power. At TOL >= 10: bonus becomes +15 energy/tick and +2 power per stack.
    The Hangover effect already halves all 6 stats. The Hangover Fuel passive
    makes those penalty stacks into combat resources.
    At hangover 4 stacks: -4 to all stats BUT +40 energy/tick and +4 power.
    The speed gain alone (now at 140 energy/tick) means you act 40% more often.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Drinking becomes a speed and power investment when this passive is active.
    Stack hangovers deliberately across floors (drink heavily on one floor,
    enter the next with 3-4 hangover stacks, fight at massive speed advantage).
    The stat penalties from hangover are REAL (losing 4 from each stat is harsh)
    but the power/speed compensation is good enough to offset them if you're
    STR-built. Synergizes insanely well with Malt Liquor (which already boosts
    STR) — you drink for Malt Liquor's buff, accumulate the hangover, and then
    Hangover Fuel makes the hangover an asset.

  Implementation:
    engine._hangover_fuel_active: bool = False. Set True on perk unlock.
    In _run_energy_loop and in combat power calculation: check flag, find
    HangoverEffect on player, read stacks, apply bonuses. The hangover effect
    already exists and has a .stacks field — this is trivial to wire up.

W-J-01  DRY FIRE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Desperate attack, press further
  NIGRL Name:  "Dry Fire" — fire with an empty gun, use it as a club
  Source:      Gatting L4 activated perk
  Target:      ADJACENT
  Charge:      INF (no cooldown — this is always available once unlocked)
  Stat:        Strength
  Cost:        None (you already have no ammo — this IS the cost)

  Mechanic:
    Passive: When the player attempts to fire a gun with 0 ammo (normally shows
    "Gun is empty!"), instead of failing, the player can press the attack key
    to execute a Dry Fire: an adjacent melee attack using the gun as an
    improvised weapon. Damage = (STR * 2) + (gun base damage average) // 2.
    Example: Ruger with avg damage 7, STR 8: (8*2) + 7//2 = 19 damage.
    This applies standard defense calculation. Always hits (no miss chance).
    Message: "*click* *THWACK*. Empty gun makes a solid club."
    This does NOT require ammo and does NOT consume a reload action.
    The gun's current_ammo stays at 0. This is just hitting someone with a gun.
    With Gatting L5 (hypothetical higher unlock): Dry Fire crits on 50% chance.
    At Gatting L4, Dry Fire simply functions as the above.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Turns the "out of ammo" state from helplessness into a choice. A Ruger
    Dry Fire (19 damage typical) beats most base melee attacks. It rewards
    bringing a gun even when you can't reload — you always have this fallback.
    The "no cooldown" is critical: you can Dry Fire every turn as long as you
    have no ammo. This creates a funny playstyle where you never reload because
    Dry Fire is good enough, intentionally staying at 0 ammo.

W-J-02  FULL AUTO PRAYER
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Spray and Pray (already exists but as separate), Hail Mary fire
  NIGRL Name:  "Full Auto Prayer" — dump the entire magazine at once
  Source:      Drive-By L4 activated perk
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (pick a target tile — all shots from the dump go
               toward that general direction with cone spread)
  Charge:      FLOOR_ONLY 1 (once per floor, not carried to next)
  Stat:        Street-Smarts (spread determines miss chance), Strength (damage)
  Cost:        All ammo in current gun is consumed. Gun is empty after use.

  Mechanic:
    Activating Full Auto Prayer: dump ALL remaining ammo in the current gun
    in one action. For each round fired:
      - 60% hit chance on the primary target
      - 20% hit chance on each adjacent monster (splash from the spray)
      - 20% miss (wild shot, no effect)
    Each hit deals the gun's normal damage roll.
    Follows the existing AOE_CIRCLE rule: no single target takes more than
    ceil(num_shots / 2) hits.
    At STS >= 10: hit chance on primary becomes 75%, splash stays 20%.
    At STS >= 8: 30-round UZI fires 30 shots. That's up to 15 hits on primary
    (ceil(30/2) = 15). At 9 damage/hit: 135 damage to the primary from one
    action. The FLOOR_ONLY means this is truly a boss-fight button.
    After use: gun_jammed = True for 5 turns (overheated from the dump) — must
    wait 5 turns before reload is allowed.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    This is what happens when you dump a full magazine. Against a boss or a
    tightly clustered group: devastating. Against a single enemy in a corridor:
    still strong (primary target is hit majority of times). The FLOOR_ONLY
    constraint and gun_jammed afterward means you've spent your ranged resource
    for the floor AND locked yourself out of gun use temporarily. This is
    deliberate, maximum-commitment offense.

W-J-03  RICOCHET SHOT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Trick Shot (billiards), Ricochet (action films)
  NIGRL Name:  "Ricochet Shot" — bounce your shot off a wall
  Source:      Sniping L4 activated perk
  Target:      ADJ_TILE (target an ADJACENT WALL tile — fire through it)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 8 turns
  Stat:        Street-Smarts (accuracy), Book-Smarts (trajectory calculation)
  Cost:        One round of ammo. Requires at least 1 ammo.

  Mechanic:
    Player targets an adjacent wall tile. The shot fires into the wall and
    bounces, hitting the first enemy in the direction perpendicular to the
    approach angle. Implementation: determine the player's facing direction to
    the wall (dx, dy). The bounce direction is 90-degree rotated: if the wall
    is to the east (+x), bounce propagates north or south (the direction that
    hits the nearest enemy in those tiles — check both, pick the one with a
    target in FOV). If no enemy in bounce path: shot embeds in the wall, ammo
    wasted, message: "The round buries itself in the concrete."
    Damage = gun normal damage, no miss chance (you calculated this — it hits).
    One enemy hit: the first living monster in the bounce path within range 6.
    BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, the shot ALSO damages the second enemy in the
    bounce path (chain bounce) with 50% of the original damage.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Hit enemies around corners. This is a HUGE tactical tool in a corridor-heavy
    dungeon: the enemy is one tile around a corner, you can't see them (not in
    FOV), but you can target the wall beside you, bounce the shot, and hit them
    blind. The validate function needs to check: if no enemy in bounce path,
    fail with message. Cooldown 8 keeps it from being used for every single
    corner engagement.

W-K-01  SHARE THE WEALTH
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Contagion (D&D), spreading effects
  NIGRL Name:  "Share the Wealth" — exhale your strain effect onto an enemy
  Source:      Smoking L4 activated perk (phat cloud upgrade path)
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 3 — close enough to blow smoke in their face)
  Charge:      INF, cooldown 5 turns
  Stat:        Tolerance
  Cost:        Consumes the active strain effect on the player (if none: fails)

  Mechanic:
    Validation: player must have an active strain effect (columbian_gold,
    agent_orange, jungle_boyz buff, fiery_fists, etc.).
    On activation: copy the current strain effect to the target enemy (same
    duration remaining). Remove it from the player.
    Effect inversion for buffs applied to enemies:
      - FieryFistsEffect on enemy: each of their attacks has 1 stack ignite
        applied to the PLAYER instead of them (reversed origin — they ignite
        US on hit)
      - LifestealEffect on enemy: each enemy attack heals the enemy (not useful
        if they're at full HP, very bad if they're low)
      - ColumnianGoldEffect on enemy: DoT still applies (they burn too) AND
        their power increases by 5 (briefly stronger enemy — risky)
      - AgentOrangeEffect on enemy: they cannot attack for the duration and
        take 20 damage on expiry (this is EXCELLENT to force on an enemy)
      - GloryFistsEffect on enemy: 5% per hit chance to gain a permanent stat
        boost (scary — do not transfer this one)
    TOL scaling: at TOL >= 10, player RETAINS a weakened copy (5t duration)
    of the effect instead of fully losing it.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Weaponize your own buffs. The meta-game: get Agent Orange (normally applied
    to enemies on bad strain roll) on yourself from some source, then immediately
    Share the Wealth to put it on the most dangerous enemy — they can't attack
    for its duration. Get Jungle Boyz minor_self_reflection, share it to an
    enemy, now THEY hurt themselves when they hit you. The Transfer is the
    puzzle; knowing which effects are worth giving away is the skill test.

W-K-02  JOINT ROLLING FACTORY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Crafting alchemy, production line
  NIGRL Name:  "Joint Rolling Factory" — roll joints from other strains into one
  Source:      Rolling L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF (opens inventory)
  Charge:      /FL 1
  Stat:        Book-Smarts, Tolerance
  Cost:        Consumes 2 joints from inventory (of any strains). One new joint
               is produced.

  Mechanic:
    Player selects 2 joints from inventory. A combined joint is produced that:
      - Has both strains simultaneously — when smoked, applies the effect of
        BOTH strains sequentially
      - The combined joint has a slightly reduced tolerance check (combined
        tolerance threshold = average of the two strains' thresholds)
      - The combined joint grants double Smoking XP when smoked
    BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, the combined joint applies both strain effects at
    FULL strength instead of sequential application (they overlap).
    TOL scaling: at TOL >= 10, the combined joint has chance to auto-proc
    bonus roll (treat as TOL 2 higher for Tolerance Threshold check).
    Message: "You roll a masterwork. This one's special."
    The combined joint displays as "Mixed Joint [Strain1 + Strain2]" in
    inventory.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Combine Jungle Boyz (lifesteal, proc attacks) with Columbian Gold (+5 power,
    DoT risk) for a monster-fight that heals you AND gives you +5 damage. Or
    combine OG Kush (heal) with Iron Lung (tox tankiness) for a defensive
    smokable. Or combine Agent Orange (cannot melee) with Columbian Gold for an
    enemy-targeting smoke that both disables them AND burns them. The BKS 10
    simultaneous application is a build goal that completely changes the
    smoking meta.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
W-L-01  LAST HIT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Coup de Grace (D&D), Killing Blow (various)
  NIGRL Name:  "Last Hit" — guaranteed instant-kill any enemy below 20% HP
  Source:      Stabbing L5 activated perk (precise death-blow)
  Target:      SINGLE_LOS (range 4)
  Charge:      /FL 3
  Stat:        Street-Smarts
  Cost:        None, but FAILS if target is above 20% HP (the blow lands wrong)

  Mechanic:
    Target an enemy. Validation: target.hp <= target.max_hp * 0.2.
    If valid: the enemy instantly dies (target.hp = 0, entity_died emitted).
    No damage roll. No defense. No armor. Just: dead.
    If invalid (above 20%): the ability fires, but instead of a kill, deal
    STS damage (no bonus, no crit, just flat STS value). The charge IS still
    consumed on invalid use (you tried to time it, you missed the window, you
    wasted it). Message on success: "You find the gap. One precise cut.
    It's done." Message on failure: "The moment wasn't right. You hit for
    [x] instead."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Three uses per floor of "everything below 20% HP dies instantly." This
    is particularly powerful against enemies that are dangerous at low HP
    (anything that splits on death, anything that Fled and is weakened, bosses
    at their dangerous final phase). The penalty for mis-timing (consuming
    the charge AND dealing paltry damage instead of a kill) rewards patience
    and observation. Three per floor means you're still doing real combat — this
    is an execution finisher, not a combat replacement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
W-L-02  BLESS THIS MESS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Holy Word (D&D), Chaos Magic (Warhammer)
  NIGRL Name:  "Bless This Mess" — invoke chaos, good and bad in equal measure
  Source:      Blackkk Magic L5 activated perk (capstone)
  Target:      AOE_CIRCLE radius 4, centered on player
  Charge:      ONCE
  Stat:        Book-Smarts, Swagger (contested roll)
  Cost:        The player ALSO receives 50% of the effects (see mechanic)

  Mechanic:
    Roll D100 for each monster within radius 4. Each monster independently
    gets a random outcome from this table (roll 1d8):
      1: Instant death (monster's HP set to 0 — entity_died emitted)
      2: Full heal (monster.hp = monster.max_hp — dangerous)
      3: Stun for 8 turns (very good for player)
      4: Apply berserk: monster gains +6 power for 6 turns but attacks other
         monsters first (treat player as last priority if another monster is
         adjacent)
      5: Monster explodes for STR*2 damage in Chebyshev(1) AoE, hitting
         other monsters and player (dangerous)
      6: Teleport monster to random floor location (removes from combat)
      7: Apply agent_orange effect to monster (can't attack, takes 20 on expiry)
      8: Monster gains 2x speed for 5 turns (scary — they act twice per cycle)
    Player: for EACH monster affected, the player has a 30% chance to also
    get one random effect (same table, re-rolled for each trigger).
    Message: "You speak the words. The street trembles. You're not sure
    what's going to happen next. Neither is anything else."

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Pure chaos in a radius-4 AoE. In a room with 6 enemies, BLESS THIS MESS
    produces six independent random outcomes, some of which blow up in your
    face. The 30% chance the player also gets an effect means you might be
    stunned for 8 turns (terrible) or instantly killed by a monster explosion
    (ironic). This is the ability you use when you have nothing left to lose.
    The ONCE constraint makes it a true "nuclear option" like Stop Time — you
    carry it all run waiting for the right terrible situation.

W-L-03  SKIN SHED
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Revivify (D&D), Doppelganger (various), Clone
  NIGRL Name:  "Skin Shed" — one-time death prevention, at a cost
  Source:      Negromancy L5 activated perk
  Target:      SELF (passive — auto-triggers on what would be a lethal hit)
  Charge:      ONCE (auto-triggers on death, cannot be manually used)
  Stat:        Constitution, Street-Smarts
  Cost:        Player respawns with 10% max HP AND all status effects transferred
               to random enemies on the floor. Cannot trigger twice.

  Mechanic:
    Passive ONCE ability. When the player would die (hp reduced to 0 or below):
    - Check if this ability has not been used (engine._skin_shed_available = True)
    - If available: set player.hp to max(1, player.max_hp // 10). Set flag = False.
    - Teleport player to a random open tile on the current floor (emergency escape)
    - Transfer ALL player status effects to the nearest 3 living enemies
      (random assignment — each effect goes to one of those 3 enemies)
    - Message: "You shed it like a skin. Everything that was killing you is their
      problem now. You're something new. Barely."
    CON scaling: respawn HP = max(1, player.max_hp // 10 + CON * 2). At CON 12:
    10% + 24 HP. At CON 8: 10% + 16 HP. Survivable amounts, not comfortable.
    STS scaling: at STS >= 10, also grant the player a 5-turn "invulnerable" buff
    to get their bearings after the skin shed.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    This is the second-chance button but it costs everything — you respawn with
    nearly no HP, your debuffs are gone (because they transferred) but so are
    your buffs. You're in an unknown location on the floor with 10% HP and
    no status effects. This is "a second chance at surviving" not "a second
    chance at winning the fight." The transferred effects might actively help
    you: if enemies get your speed boost and lifesteal, they're still your
    enemies; if they get your Bleeding and Shocked, that's hilarious symmetry.
    ONCE per run — this is the one time death doesn't catch you.

W-L-04  DEBT SPIRAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Exponential scaling (math horror), compound interest
  NIGRL Name:  "Debt Spiral" — every kill makes you stronger AND more dangerous
  Source:      Negromancy L4 activated perk
  Target:      SELF (passive)
  Charge:      N/A (passive, always-on once unlocked)
  Stat:        Book-Smarts (tracks the count), Tolerance (caps the downside)
  Cost:        Each kill adds to your "debt" — on death or floor descent,
               the debt comes due

  Mechanic:
    Passive. Track engine._debt_spiral_count: int = 0.
    Each kill via melee: _debt_spiral_count += 1.
    While count > 0: player gains _debt_spiral_count * 1 bonus damage (raw).
    At count 10: +10 bonus damage on every attack. At count 20: +20 damage.
    Maximum count: 30 (cap at +30 bonus damage).
    On floor descent: debt comes due. Player takes damage equal to
    _debt_spiral_count * 3 (bypassing armor). At count 10: -30 HP on stairs.
    At count 20: -60 HP. At count 30: -90 HP. Very possible to kill yourself
    on a staircase.
    TOL scaling: at TOL >= 10, the debt damage on descent is reduced to
    count * 2 instead of count * 3.
    Reset: count resets to 0 after debt is paid.
    BKS scaling: at BKS >= 10, each kill only adds 0.5 to count (requires 2
    kills per bonus point — slower accumulation, slower debt).

  Why it's busted/fun:
    A kill-streak ability that punishes safe play. Stop killing and you lose the
    bonus (well, you don't lose it until you descend, but you're not gaining).
    Go on a 15-kill rampage: +15 bonus damage for the rest of the floor, then
    pay 45 HP on the stairs. A high-HP build can absorb that toll; a glass
    cannon cannot. The endgame math: 30 kills, +30 bonus damage (near-doubling
    your combat power), and -90 HP on stairs. If your max HP is 130 and you
    have 130 HP: you survive with 40 HP. If you have 50 HP: you die on the
    stairs. This is the most dangerous reward in the compendium.

W-L-05  UNFINISHED BUSINESS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Inspiration: Zombie walk, Bloodied condition
  NIGRL Name:  "Unfinished Business" — you can finish a fight, then die
  Source:      Smacking L4 activated perk (sheer stubborn toughness)
  Target:      SELF (passive trigger)
  Charge:      ONCE
  Stat:        Strength, Constitution
  Cost:        You WILL die at the end of the effect. No exceptions.

  Mechanic:
    Passive auto-trigger: when the player would die (hp hits 0 or below):
    - If UnfinishedBusiness has not been used (engine._unfinished_business = True):
    - Set player.hp = 1. Set player status_effect: UnfinishedBusinessEffect.
    - UnfinishedBusinessEffect duration = 3 + STR // 3 turns (minimum 3, max 7).
    - During these turns: player CANNOT die from damage (all damage reduces HP
      but cannot go below 1 — they're already dead, just don't know it yet).
    - Player deals 25% bonus damage from pure adrenaline.
    - Message: "Not yet. You've got something left to do."
    - On EXPIRY of UnfinishedBusinessEffect: player.hp = 0, entity_died emitted.
      The player then actually dies. Message: "Okay. Now."
    CON scaling: CON >= 10 extends duration by 2 additional turns.
    This is purely a "finish the fight" mechanic — it doesn't save you,
    it just lets you fight for 3-7 more turns before dying.

  Why it's busted/fun:
    Permadeath is the scariest thing in roguelikes. Unfinished Business tells
    you: you died, you just don't know it yet. For 3-7 turns you're a ghost
    in the machine finishing the job. This can swing the entire outcome of a
    fight: you died, but you had 5 more turns, and you killed the enemy that
    was about to kill you, meaning the floor is now clear for your... ghost
    to wander. You will die no matter what. But you get to fight back. That
    matters.
