================================================================================
NIGRL — SKILL PERK DESIGNS: L4 & L5 (AND FULL L1-L5 FOR NEW TREES)
================================================================================
Document scope:
  - L4 and L5 perks for every skill tree that currently has L1-L3 defined
  - Full L1-L5 designs for Negromancy and Meth-Head (fully placeholder)
  - Pyromania L5 only (L4 "Neva Burn Out" is already designed)

Design philosophy:
  - L4 is a meaningful power spike. The player feels the investment pay off.
  - L5 is BUILD-DEFINING. Getting here reshapes how you play the whole game.
  - Stat bonuses inside perks are allowed and encouraged where thematically apt.
  - Names should be gritty, street-level, and thematic.

Balance reference:
  - Player base stats: range 5-12 at start, +6 total points from L1-3 perks
  - Player max HP at mid-game (CON ~10): 130 HP
  - Common enemy damage: 4-8 per hit, typical floor defense 0-3
  - Crit multiplier: ~1.5x-2x base
  - Energy system: 100 energy = 1 action; player has 100 base energy/tick

Implementation vocabulary:
  - perk_type: "stat", "passive", "activated"
  - "activated" grants an AbilityDef from ABILITY_REGISTRY via effect dict:
      {"ability": "ability_id"}
  - "passive" hooks live in engine.py; effect key = None in skills.py
  - Stat bonuses use the six stat names:
      constitution, strength, street_smarts, book_smarts, tolerance, swagger

================================================================================
1. SMOKING  (L1-3 done; design L4 and L5)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: The smoke build. High Tolerance, tanky CON investments,
and passive battlefield smoke control. By L5 the player is a walking chimney
who weaponizes every puff.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L4: "Contact High"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "The cloud ain't just for you anymore."

  Mechanic:
    When the player smokes (uses any smokeable item), every enemy within
    Chebyshev range 3 that is in LOS receives a 10-turn "Confused" debuff.
    Confused enemies have a 40% chance each turn to move in a random direction
    instead of executing their normal AI action (they skip attacks while
    randomly wandering, but do NOT skip turns entirely — they just flail).

    - The confused state does NOT stack with itself; re-smoking refreshes
      the duration to 10 turns.
    - Enemies that are already chasing will resume chasing after confusion
      expires (it doesn't permanently disengage them).
    - This does NOT require the player to have Phat Cloud (L1), but synergizes
      well: Phat Cloud fires the damage first, then Contact High applies
      confusion on all nearby survivors simultaneously.

  Implementation notes:
    - Add a new "confused" Effect subclass in effects.py with a before_turn
      hook: roll random.random() < 0.40; if True, move in a random open
      direction instead of calling do_ai_turn().
    - In engine.py on_smoke handler (near the Phat Cloud check), after the
      Phat Cloud damage, iterate visible enemies within Chebyshev(3) and call
      apply_effect(enemy, engine, "confused", duration=10).
    - Confused should be category="debuff", priority=10.

  Balance notes:
    - Smoking already has strong Tolerance stacking (L2/L3 give +4 TOL total).
      This perk pays off that investment by converting smoking into a crowd
      control tool, not just a healing/buff source.
    - 40% wander chance means ~1 in 2.5 turns they do nothing useful. Against
      3 enemies simultaneously that is significant — but smoking is still a
      consumable action, so the cost is real.
    - Synergy with Rolling L2 (Seeing Double) and L3 (Spectral Paper):
      more joints smoked = more confusion procs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L5: "Hot Box"
  Type:     ACTIVATED
  Effect:   {"ability": "hot_box"}
  Flavor:   "Seal the room. Nobody leaves til the smoke clears."
  Stat bonus included: +3 Tolerance

  Mechanic:
    ACTIVATED (SELF, PER_FLOOR, max_charges=1):
    The player seals the current room in a thick smoke cloud for 12 turns.
    All enemies currently in the same room as the player are affected each turn:
      - 30% chance per enemy per turn to receive 1 stack of "confused" (10t).
      - 15% chance per enemy per turn to receive a "cough" debuff (4t stun).
      - Player is immune to their own Hot Box effect.

    The "room" is determined by which dungeon room contains the player tile.
    Enemies outside the room are unaffected. If the player moves between rooms
    mid-effect, the effect does NOT follow — it stays in the original room.
    The cloud persists for 12 turns after activation regardless of player
    position.

    "Cough" debuff: same as stun mechanically (skip turn), but named differently
    for flavor. Duration: 4 turns. Cannot stack (refresh only). Chance to proc
    per turn is low enough that enemies aren't permanently locked down — they
    have chaotic windows of incapacity.

    The +3 Tolerance stat bonus is permanent (applied once on unlock).

  Implementation notes:
    - New AbilityDef "hot_box" in abilities.py:
        target_type=TargetType.SELF, charge_type=ChargeType.PER_FLOOR,
        max_charges=1
    - execute(engine) stores a "hot_box_timer" on the engine (or as a floor
      state variable) set to 12. Each turn during _run_energy_loop step 4
      (after tick_all_effects), if hot_box_timer > 0, iterate all enemies in
      player's current room and roll both procs.
    - Add "cough" as a stun-aliased Effect subclass with id="cough" in
      effects.py. Mechanically identical to stun except display_name = "Cough".
    - Determine player's room via dungeon.get_room_at(player.x, player.y) —
      this method may need to be added to Dungeon.

  Balance notes:
    - 1/floor is a hard limit. This is a room-clear tool, not something you
      spam every fight.
    - Against Big Nigga Jerome or boss encounters: 15% cough chance per turn
      means ~1-2 turns of boss incapacity per use — meaningful but not
      trivial to rely on.
    - The +3 Tolerance bonus rewards going 5 deep into Smoking. Combined
      with L2/L3 bonuses, the full Smoking tree grants +7 TOL and +2 CON.
    - Synergy: Contact High (L4) + Hot Box creates a turn-1 confusion burst
      followed by a sustained 12-turn probability field of more confusion and
      coughs. Genuine room-control build identity.

2. ROLLING  (L1-3 done; design L4 and L5)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: The crafter. Rolling is the support/crafting tree —
the player who makes the tools. By L5, they're manufacturing resources so fast
that consumable scarcity almost disappears.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L4: "Quality Leaf"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "You know the difference between dirt weed and the real thing."

  Mechanic:
    Joints and blunts rolled by the player have their Phat Cloud (Smoking L1)
    damage increased by +Tolerance//2 (additive). The existing formula is
    10 + tolerance//2; with Quality Leaf active it becomes:
    10 + tolerance//2 + tolerance//2 = 10 + tolerance (i.e., doubles the
    tolerance component).

    Additionally, all rolled items now apply their smoking effects as if the
    player's Tolerance is 2 points lower (duration multiplier increase):
    this affects how long drug buffs last — lower effective Tolerance when
    computing duration means effects last longer per the existing Tolerance
    mechanic.

    Note: This only applies to items the player ROLLED themselves (items with
    a crafted=True flag or originating from a rolling recipe). Pre-placed loot
    joints do not benefit.

  Implementation notes:
    - Add a "quality_leaf_active" flag checked in engine.py on smoke handler.
    - For Phat Cloud damage: check if Quality Leaf unlocked, then use
      tolerance instead of tolerance//2 for the bonus component.
    - For effect duration: when applying drug effects from rolled items,
      temporarily offset effective_tolerance by -2 for the duration calculation
      only (not for the actual stat).
    - Needs a "crafted" flag on items produced by the rolling system, or
      check the item_id against a known set of rollable items.

  Balance notes:
    - Rolling is already strong (Seeing Double + Spectral Paper effectively
      means infinite joints). Quality Leaf makes each joint more potent,
      converting quantity into quality.
    - Does not make pre-placed items overpowered since it only affects
      player-crafted items.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L5: "The Plug"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "Everyone knows your name. Every floor has a package waiting."
  Stat bonus included: +2 Strength, +2 Tolerance

  Mechanic:
    On every floor descend, the player automatically receives 2 random
    craftable materials as if they were found in the stash (added directly to
    inventory). The items are drawn from the same pool as loot generation's
    "material" category for the current zone.

    Additionally, all crafting recipes the player uses consume 1 fewer material
    of any type (minimum 1 total — recipes that already cost 1 item are
    unaffected). This means a 2-ingredient recipe becomes a 1-ingredient recipe.

    The +2 STR and +2 TOL bonuses are permanent.

  Implementation notes:
    - On floor descent in engine.py (handle_descend or equivalent), if
      "The Plug" is unlocked, call loot.pick_random_materials(zone, count=2)
      and add each item to player inventory.
    - For the recipe cost reduction: in the crafting execution code, check for
      "the_plug" perk and subtract 1 from the ingredient count consumed
      (floor to 1 minimum per ingredient type, OR reduce total count by 1).
      The simpler implementation is: skip consuming the first ingredient
      of a recipe (the cheapest one), keeping all others.
    - This is a QoL/economic build-defining perk: it makes the Rolling player
      self-sustaining through the dungeon.

  Balance notes:
    - 2 materials per floor is not enormous; the build-defining part is the
      recipe cost reduction. Going from 2-ingredient recipes to 1-ingredient
      opens up faster crafting rotations.
    - Synergy: Seeing Double (L2) procs a free extra roll result, Spectral
      Paper (L3) never consumes papers, Quality Leaf (L4) makes each roll
      more powerful, The Plug (L5) guarantees you always have materials and
      reduces recipe costs. The full tree creates a genuinely self-contained
      crafting loop.

3. PYROMANIA  (L4 already done; design L5 only)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: Fire control, escalating AoE destruction, attrition warfare
through ignite stacking.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L5: "Cremation Service"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "They don't need a casket when you're done with 'em."
  Stat bonus included: +3 Constitution, +2 Tolerance

  Mechanic:
    Enemies killed while they have ANY ignite stacks active explode in a fire
    burst on death. The burst:
      - Spawns a fire hazard on the dead enemy's tile (1 turn duration, then
        disappears — unlike placed fires which persist longer).
      - Deals ignite_stacks * 3 damage (no defense reduction) to all living
        enemies in Chebyshev range 2 of the death tile.
      - Does NOT chain: enemies damaged by the explosion but not killed do
        not re-trigger another explosion.

    This transforms the Pyromania build into an avalanche: ignite a cluster
    of enemies, kill the weakest one, the explosion chain-damages the rest,
    enabling faster kills which may trigger more explosions.

    The +3 CON and +2 TOL bonuses are permanent.

  Implementation notes:
    - Hook into entity_died event in engine.py event bus handler.
    - Check if dead entity had any ignite stacks (search status_effects for
      id="ignite" and stacks > 0).
    - If yes, and if player has Pyromania L5: spawn a temporary fire entity
      at death tile, deal burst damage to Chebyshev(2) enemies.
    - The temporary fire entity needs a 1-turn lifespan — either use a
      short-duration flag on the fire entity or handle it inline without
      actually spawning a persistent hazard.
    - Burst damage formula: ignite_stacks * 3, bypasses defense (direct HP
      reduction).

  Balance notes:
    - Requires killing an ignited enemy — there's still player action required.
    - Maximum ignite stacks from Ignite (L3) ability is 2/cast, 5 casts/floor.
      At 2 stacks, explosion deals 6 no-defense damage to a Chebyshev-2 radius.
      Against tweakers (10-20 HP typically), that can finish weakened enemies.
    - Neva Burn Out (L4) extends ignite duration, making it easier to keep
      enemies ignited long enough to hit the kill threshold.
    - Big rooms with clustered enemies (e.g., tweaker packs from drug_dealer
      spawn_with) become high-value Pyromania targets.

4. NEGROMANCY  (FULL L1-L5 — no existing perks)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: Death magic. The Negromancy player turns corpses and death
into a resource. They don't fight enemies — they FARM them and use their
spent bodies as fuel. High-risk, high-reward: spending your HP for power,
raising fallen enemies, and draining life.

XP source: Killing enemies. Every death feeds the Negromancer.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L1: "Soul Harvest"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "Every body leaves something behind."

  Mechanic:
    When the player kills an enemy, they gain a Soul Shard (tracked as a
    player stat/counter: engine.soul_shards). Soul Shards cap at 5.
    Each Soul Shard provides +1 to all damage dealt (flat bonus, applies to
    melee, ranged, and spells).

    Shards are not a consumable by themselves — they are consumed by higher-tier
    Negromancy perks. At L1 only, they simply sit as a passive damage multiplier.

  Implementation notes:
    - Add engine.soul_shards counter (int, 0-5).
    - Hook into entity_died event: if killed by player, soul_shards = min(5,
      soul_shards + 1).
    - In _compute_player_attack_power() and spell damage formulas, add
      engine.soul_shards as a flat bonus.
    - Display soul shards in the stats panel UI (e.g., "Souls: 3/5").

  Balance notes:
    - +5 max bonus when capped is modest but meaningful. It encourages staying
      in combat rather than fleeing. The Negromancer rewards aggressive play.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L2: "Blood Price"
  Type:     STAT
  Effect:   {"constitution": -1, "street_smarts": 3, "book_smarts": 2}
  Flavor:   "You paid in flesh. You got sharper in return."

  Mechanic:
    Permanent stat change: -1 CON (loses 10 max HP), +3 Street Smarts,
    +2 Book Smarts.

    The CON loss is intentional — Negromancy is a build that trades survival
    headroom for power. The Street Smarts bonus increases crit chance (SS*3%),
    directly rewarding the kill-focused playstyle. Book Smarts speeds up skill
    point conversion.

    Note: "stat" perk_type with negative constitution is unusual but valid —
    the implementation applies the delta directly to PlayerStats.

  Implementation notes:
    - In skills.py, perk_type="stat" with effect dict including "constitution":-1
      is fine as long as the stat application code handles negative deltas
      (PlayerStats.constitution += delta works with negative values).
    - Warn in the UI when this perk is about to be unlocked that HP will
      decrease (optional QoL).

  Balance notes:
    - Net: -10 HP, +9% crit, faster SP gain. This is the "commit to the path"
      moment. A player who takes this is all-in on Negromancy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L3: "Reap"
  Type:     ACTIVATED
  Effect:   {"ability": "reap"}
  Flavor:   "Take what's left."

  Mechanic:
    ACTIVATED ability — "reap":
      TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, range 6
      ChargeType: INFINITE (cooldown-gated, 15-turn cooldown)
      Consumes 2 Soul Shards on use (requires 2+ shards).

    Effect: Deal damage equal to 8 + (current soul_shards * 4) to a target
    enemy in LOS. After firing, soul_shards is reduced by 2.
    Also heals the player for 50% of damage dealt (lifesteal mechanic).

    Formula example: at 4 shards before cast: damage = 8 + (4*4) = 24,
    then shards drop to 2, heal = 12 HP.

    The ability does NOT restore shards on the kill — but if the target dies,
    Soul Harvest (L1) would immediately grant 1 new shard.

  Implementation notes:
    - New AbilityDef "reap" in abilities.py:
        target_type=TargetType.SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE
        execute_at=_execute_at_reap
    - _execute_at_reap: check engine.soul_shards >= 2; compute damage; call
      target.take_damage(damage); engine.soul_shards -= 2; heal player by
      damage // 2 (capped at max_hp); set cooldown 15.
    - Flavor message: "Reap! You drain {damage} from {target.name} and absorb
      {healed} HP. ({shard_count} souls remain.)"

  Balance notes:
    - 2-shard cost is significant at low levels. Players must kill 2 enemies
      before they can cast again after spending down to 0.
    - The lifesteal makes it a sustain tool, not just a nuke.
    - At max shards (5): damage = 28, heal = 14. Respectable for an INFINITE
      cooldown-only ability.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L4: "Grave Robber"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "Dead men got pockets too."

  Mechanic:
    Each time an enemy dies anywhere on the floor (not just kills by the player),
    the player has a chance to receive an item drop equal to:
      10% + (soul_shards * 5)% chance

    At 0 shards: 10% base. At 5 shards: 35%.

    The item dropped is a random consumable from the zone loot table (same as
    what the dead enemy might have carried). It appears on the enemy's death
    tile as a normal item the player must walk over to pick up.

    This applies to ALL deaths (enemy-on-enemy, fire, fall damage, etc.) —
    not just direct player kills. This creates synergy with hot box, confusion-
    related infighting, and Pyromania explosion chains.

  Implementation notes:
    - Hook into entity_died event. Check if Negromancy L4 unlocked.
    - Roll the proc chance: 0.10 + engine.soul_shards * 0.05.
    - If proc: call loot.pick_random_consumable(zone) and create the item
      entity at the dead entity's (x, y) position.
    - Do NOT trigger for player death.

  Balance notes:
    - Against a floor with 12 enemies and 4 average shards: proc rate ~30%.
      Expected 3-4 extra consumable drops per floor. Meaningful but not
      gamebreaking.
    - This makes the Negromancer a "longer fights = more rewards" build.
      Killing fast and spending shards is a trade-off: more kills = more shards
      = higher drop rate, but spending shards on Reap trades drop rate for
      immediate sustain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L5: "Raise Dead"
  Type:     ACTIVATED
  Effect:   {"ability": "raise_dead"}
  Flavor:   "Get up. We ain't done."
  Stat bonus included: +2 Constitution, +3 Street Smarts

  Mechanic:
    ACTIVATED — "raise_dead":
      TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS (cursor aimed at a dead enemy's tile)
      ChargeType: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=2
      Consumes ALL current soul_shards on activation.

    Effect:
    The target dead enemy (must have died this floor — tracked by a "fresh
    corpse" flag lasting 20 turns on the entity) is temporarily reanimated
    as a Thrall for a number of turns equal to 5 + (soul_shards consumed * 3).

    While a Thrall:
      - The entity is re-marked alive=True, added back to dungeon entities.
      - The Thrall uses MEANDER AI toward the nearest non-player enemy.
      - It has 1 HP (cannot survive being hit) and deals its original power
        in damage.
      - It is NOT controlled by the player beyond the initial raise —
        it just aggressively attacks enemies.
      - If there are no living enemies, the Thrall stands still.
      - When the Thrall timer expires, it collapses again (alive=False).
        No further soul shard is gained from its second death.

    At 5 shards consumed: Thrall lasts 20 turns.
    At 0 shards consumed: Thrall lasts 5 turns (minimum useful duration).

    The +2 CON and +3 Street Smarts bonuses are permanent.

  Implementation notes:
    - New AbilityDef "raise_dead" in ABILITY_REGISTRY.
    - Need "thrall" flag on entity: entity.is_thrall = True.
      Mark entity with a thrall_turns_remaining counter.
    - Each turn in _run_energy_loop, decrement thrall_turns_remaining for all
      thrall entities. At 0, mark alive=False.
    - execute_at: find a dead entity at (tx, ty) that has is_thrall=False and
      a "fresh_corpse" flag. Set alive=True, is_thrall=True, hp=1,
      thrall_turns_remaining = 5 + soul_shards * 3. Then soul_shards = 0.
    - The "fresh_corpse" flag: when an entity dies, set entity.fresh_corpse=True
      and entity.fresh_corpse_timer=20 (decremented in the same loop).
    - AI dispatch for thralls: in do_ai_turn(), check is_thrall flag before
      the main AI decision. Thralls use MEANDER logic toward the closest living
      non-player enemy.
    - Entity char and color remain the same — a dead body suddenly standing
      up with its original appearance is the intended ASCII horror flavor.

  Balance notes:
    - 2/floor is generous for a perk this strong. The catch is consuming ALL
      shards (the damage bonus from Soul Harvest drops to 0), the corpse
      freshness requirement (can't raise a body from 2 floors ago), and the
      1 HP fragility of Thralls.
    - A Thrall of Big Nigga Jerome (power ~6-9) standing between the player
      and enemies for 20 turns is enormously powerful in tight corridors.
      The 2/floor limit is the primary balancing lever.
    - Synergy: Blood Price (L2) increases kill speed → more fresh corpses
      available. Reap (L3) keeps player alive long enough to set up raises.
      Grave Robber (L4) converts the raised enemy's death (when Thrall timer
      expires) into a potential extra loot drop.

5. BLACKKK MAGIC  (L1-3 done; design L4 and L5)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: Arcane intelligence build. Spells scale with Book Smarts.
By L5, the player's entire spellcasting identity is amplified — a glass cannon
who ends fights before they can be hit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L4: "Forbidden Knowledge"
  Type:     PASSIVE + STAT
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py) — PLUS stat: {"book_smarts": 3}
  Flavor:   "You read things you weren't supposed to."

  Mechanic:
    Permanent: +3 Book Smarts.

    Passive bonus: The player's spell damage bonus (player_stats.spell_damage)
    is now also increased by Book Smarts // 3 (integer division).
    Example: BkSmt = 12: +4 spell damage from this perk alone.

    Also: all abilities with is_spell=True have their cooldowns reduced by 2
    turns (minimum 0). This applies globally to any spell-tagged ability.

  Implementation notes:
    - perk_type="stat" covers the +3 BkSmt. The passive spell damage bonus
      requires an engine check — on each level-up of this perk, recalculate
      player_stats.spell_damage += player_stats.book_smarts // 3.
    - For cooldown reduction: store a "spell_cooldown_reduction" int on the
      engine (default 0). When Blackkk Magic L4 is unlocked, set it to 2.
      In ability cooldown set operations, apply max(0, cd - spell_cooldown_reduction).
    - Note: if both the stat and passive must be encoded, consider a hybrid
      where perk_type="stat" handles the BkSmt grant, and the passive hook
      triggers separately as part of the level-up logic.

  Balance notes:
    - +3 BkSmt at L4 stacks with L1's +2 BkSmt for +5 total from Blackkk Magic
      alone. The spell damage scaling compounds: BkSmt 5+3=8 base + 5 = 13
      at mid-game minimum. Spell damage bonus at BkSmt 13: +4.
    - The cooldown reduction is subtle but meaningful — Force Push (20t CD)
      drops to 18t, etc. Not dramatic, but rewards the pure spellcaster.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L5: "Dark Epiphany"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "You figured it out. Everything clicks. Reality bends to your will."
  Stat bonus included: +4 Book Smarts

  Mechanic:
    Permanent: +4 Book Smarts.

    On every floor, the player's Arcane Intelligence stacks (from L3) are
    permanently carried to the next floor instead of resetting. Arcane
    Intelligence is normally a per-combat stacking buff — at L5, this becomes
    a permanent compounding advantage across the entire run.

    Additionally: when the player kills an enemy with a spell (is_spell=True
    ability), there is a 25% chance the ability does NOT consume its charge.
    This applies to PER_FLOOR, TOTAL, and ONCE abilities — effectively 25%
    refund on kill. INFINITE charge abilities are unaffected (they're already
    infinite).

  Implementation notes:
    - Arcane Intelligence stack carry: when transitioning floors in engine.py
      (handle_descend), normally the buff would expire. If Dark Epiphany is
      unlocked, do NOT clear the Arcane Intelligence effect — just transfer
      its current stack count to the new floor state.
    - 25% charge refund on spell kill: in entity_died event handler, check
      if the killer is the player AND the kill was from a spell (engine needs
      to track "last ability used" with its is_spell flag). If conditions met,
      roll 25% and call engine.current_ability_instance.uncharged() (reverse
      the consume() call from this turn).
    - "last_ability_used" tracking: store in engine during _fire_ability_at
      before charge is consumed, or as an event payload on entity_died.

  Balance notes:
    - The Arcane Intelligence carry is the real build-definer. By floor 3-4,
      if the player has been casting every combat, their stacks could be 10+,
      providing significant spell damage. Carrying that across floors means
      the Blackkk Magic mage only gets more powerful as the run progresses.
    - 25% charge refund on kill rewards targeting the correct kill shot
      rather than overkilling damaged enemies with expensive abilities.
    - Combined with Forbidden Knowledge (L4) cooldown reduction, the L4+L5
      combo makes the full Blackkk Magic mage feel like a continuously casting
      powerhouse with near-infinite sustain.

6. STABBING  (L1-3 done; design L4 and L5)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: The assassin. High single-target kill speed, stun
exploitation, and surgical precision. The Stabbing build rewards patience:
Gouge first, kill while stunned, Windfury procs send you into murder sprees.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L4: "Jugular"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "You know where to stick it."

  Mechanic:
    Attacks against enemies that are currently stunned (gouge debuff, black_eye
    stun, cough, corn_dog stun, or any stun-category effect) deal double damage.
    The doubling applies to the final damage value after defense reduction
    (i.e., a clean multiplication, not a pre-defense buff).

    This interacts with Windfury (L3): if the first hit triggers Windfury
    against a stunned target, the extra hit ALSO gets the 2x multiplier.
    This is intentional — maximum payoff for the stun setup.

  Implementation notes:
    - In engine.py player attack resolution (around the final damage calculation),
      check if target has any effect with category="debuff" and id in
      {"gouge", "black_eye", "stun", "cough", "corn_dog_stun"} (or check for
      a shared "stun" tag on the effect class).
    - Better long-term approach: give stun-category effects a stun=True class
      attribute and check for that.
    - Windfury follow-up hit should recheck the stun state (the first hit may

17. METH-HEAD  (FULL L1-L5 — no existing perks)
================================================================================
Thematic identity: Speed, violence, and volatility. The Meth-Head is the
fastest build in the game — highest energy/tick, most aggressive AI, and
extreme highs followed by brutal crashes. High risk, enormous peak performance,
dangerous downside.

XP source: Taking damage and dealing damage in rapid succession. The tweak
feeds on chaos.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
L1: "Tweakin"
  Type:     PASSIVE
  Effect:   None (logic in engine.py)
  Flavor:   "You're moving before you even thought about it."

  Mechanic:
    After killing any enemy, the player gains a "Wire" buff for 10 turns:
      +30 energy/tick (faster movement and attack rate)
    Wire stacks with itself: each kill while Wire is active adds 10 more turns
    to the duration. Maximum cumulative duration: 40 turns.
    Wire does NOT stack in magnitude — always +30 energy/tick regardless of
    how many kills have refreshed it.

    When Wire expires naturally (not from taking damage), there is no crash.
    If Wire is up when the player takes damage from any source, Wire
    immediately expires and the player receives a "Crash" debuff for 5 turns:
      -20 energy/tick (moves and attacks noticeably slower)
    Crash is the balancing force: the Meth-Head must avoid getting hit while
    wired or pay the speed penalty.

  Implementation notes:
    - On entity_died (killed by player): apply_effect(player, engine, "wire",
      duration=10). WireEffect's on_reapply: add 10 to duration, cap at 40.
    - WireEffect.modify_energy_gain: return +30.
    - On player taking damage: in damage resolution, check for Wire. If active,
      expire it and apply_effect(player, engine, "crash", duration=5).
    - CrashEffect.modify_energy_gain: return -20.

  Balance notes:
    - +30 energy/tick means from 100 base the player acts at 130 energy/tick.
      Enemies at 100 energy/tick act 23% less often relative to the player.
      This is significant but not overwhelming at +30.
    - Crash at -20 drops the player to 80 energy/tick — slightly below enemy
      base speed. Punishing but not crippling.

    - Synergy with Rage Quit (L4): during Rage, damage taken is increased but
      crashes don't trigger. When Rage ends and Burned Out starts (no Wire gain),
      the player is briefly vulnerable. If they then get hit on a subsequent
      Wire (after Burned Out expires): shockwave. This is complex timing but
      creates genuine depth for a skilled Meth-Head player.
