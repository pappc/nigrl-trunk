DECONTAMINATION SKILL — LEVEL 4 AURA OPTIONS
NIGRL Design Document
Generated: 2026-04-02
======================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
Design 5-6 bold, creative perk options for Decontamination Level 4. Every even-numbered
Decontamination perk is a toggleable aura (L2 = Gamma Aura, L4 = this). The chosen perk
replaces the current _PLACEHOLDER at index 3 in SKILL_PERKS["Decontamination"].

TREE STATE AS OF THIS DOCUMENT
-------------------------------
  L1 — Radiant: +30% rad resist, +2 TOL, +2 STR
  L2 — Gamma Aura: +3 STR. Toggle aura: enemies within 2 tiles gain +5 rad/turn. 2x Decon XP
       from resisted rad. Nearby enemy dying with 50+ rad removes 25 of your rad.
  L3 — Consecrate: 2/floor, 30t CD. Lay a 5x3 rad zone (20t). Enemies: +10 rad/turn, true dmg.
       Standing in zone: +1 Swagger/turn (cap 5). Low rad = chance to preserve charge. +15% rad
       resist.
  L4 — THIS DOCUMENT
  L5 — Consecrate upgrade (already planned; not designed here)

AURA RULES (established pattern from Gamma Aura and L2 design)
--------------------------------------------------------------
  - Toggle is free (no charge cost). One Decon aura active at a time. Toggling on a new aura
    while a different one is active swaps them out.
  - Implemented as a floor_duration Effect with a tick() method. Toggle logic in the ability's
    execute function checks for existing aura effect and either expires it (OFF) or applies
    the new one (ON).
  - The toggle ability should be ChargeType.INFINITE, TargetType.SELF.
  - Swap detection: in the execute function, before applying the new aura, check for any active
    Decon aura effect (gamma_aura or whatever the L4 id is) and expire it first.
  - Always-on passive component: exists regardless of toggle state (small bonus that comes with
    unlocking the perk, distinct from the aura itself).

DESIGN BRIEF
-----------
L4 must feel like a DIFFERENT choice from Gamma Aura. Gamma Aura is OFFENSIVE — it builds
radiation on nearby enemies, which the irradiated DOT (rad//50 dmg/tick) then kills. The L4
aura should open a different design space: defensive, utility, vision-based, terrain-warping,
or something entirely novel. The key design question: why would a player SWAP to this aura
instead of Gamma? The answer defines the option's build identity.


======================================================================
OPTION 1 — "SACRED GROUND AURA" (terrain conversion)
High concept: your FOV itself is the consecration. The ground you walk on becomes irradiated
simply by you being near it.
======================================================================

NAME: Sacred Ground Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — every rad tile in your FOV gains +2 turns of duration per turn you
stand near it. You consecrate by presence, not by ability use.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Each tick the aura is active: iterate dungeon.rad_tiles. For every tile that is currently
    visible (dungeon.visible[y][x] == True) AND within 5 tiles of the player (Chebyshev):
    increment its remaining duration by 2 (capped at 30 turns max per tile).
  - Additionally: any NEW floor tile the player steps onto while this aura is active has a 15%
    chance to spontaneously become a rad tile with 8 turns duration (rad_tiles[(x,y)] = 8).
    This is checked in engine.handle_move after the player moves. If the destination is already
    a rad tile, no check occurs.
  - The effect: Consecrate's limited zones become PERMANENT while this aura is on. You stop
    worrying about the 20-turn clock on your rad tiles. You can kite enemies back into old
    Consecrate zones that are still alive because you sustained them. And you scatter faint
    radiation across the floor just by exploring it.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free. No per-tick cost to the player.
  - Toggle OFF: free.
  - The "cost" is opportunity cost: while this aura is ON, Gamma Aura is OFF, so you stop
    irradiating enemies within 2 tiles. You get zone persistence instead of direct enemy rad.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Holy Footprint": After unlocking L4, when the player walks off a Consecrate rad tile, it
    gains +4 turns duration instead of just decrementing normally. You literally leave blessed
    ground behind — your departure refreshes the tile rather than just aging it.
  - Implementation: in engine.handle_move, after player moves, check old position in
    dungeon.rad_tiles. If present and player has Decon L4+: dungeon.rad_tiles[old_pos] =
    min(30, dungeon.rad_tiles[old_pos] + 4).

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: you want to irradiate enemies RIGHT NOW. Fast rooms with clustered enemies.
  - Sacred Ground: you have already placed Consecrate zones, or you're in a long dungeon run
    where kiting is your strategy. You want the tiles to last. You want the room to become
    a permanent hazard rather than generating burst rad.
  - Ideal swap moment: after placing Consecrate early in a floor, toggle off Gamma and activate
    Sacred Ground so you get the full run of that zone while you kite.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Consecrate (L3): This is THE synergy perk. Sacred Ground makes Consecrate's zones
    persistent instead of expiring. A 20-turn zone that gets +2 turns/tick while you're in
    range is effectively indefinite during combat.
  - Gamma Aura (L2): Swap back after you've "primed" a room with Sacred Ground tiles. Use
    Sacred Ground to build your zone network, then swap to Gamma to accelerate enemy irradiation
    into those tiles.
  - Radiant Wake (proposed L6): if Radiant Wake is also taken, Sacred Ground sustains those
    6-turn wake tiles into 10+ turn tiles. The entire floor becomes irradiated over the course
    of a run.
  - Force Sensitive: standing on your own rad tile while the aura is active lets you choose
    when to absorb and purge, since the tile keeps running. More controlled cycle timing.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect: SacredGroundAuraEffect (id="sacred_ground_aura", floor_duration=True)
    tick(): for (x,y), remaining in dungeon.rad_tiles.items(): if visible and dist <= 5:
    dungeon.rad_tiles[(x,y)] = min(30, remaining + 2).
  - handle_move hook: if Decon L4+ and sacred_ground_aura active: check step-onto tile for
    15% spontaneous rad tile creation. Check old tile for +4 duration holy footprint.
  - Toggle execute: standard pattern (check for active Decon aura effect, expire it, apply new).
  - Save/load: no new engine state fields needed (dungeon.rad_tiles already serialized).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - The aura makes Consecrate's zones free to sustain. Without the zone ability, the aura
    creates only scattered 8-turn tiles at 15% chance — not enough to be dominant on its own.
    The perk is intentionally synergy-dependent on L3. A player who took a different L3 gets
    much less from it.
  - The 15% spontaneous tile chance generates roughly 1 free tile every 7 tiles walked. In a
    typical floor that's maybe 5-8 free rad tiles. Modest without Consecrate, strong with it.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (one Decon aura at a time): rad tiles in your FOV within 5 tiles gain +2 turns
  per tick (cap 30t). Each tile you walk onto has a 15% chance to become a rad tile (8t).
  Passive: when you step off a rad tile, it gains +4 turns duration."

OPTION 2 — "DIVINE SIGHT AURA" (the FOV angle, taken to the extreme)
High concept: your FOV becomes a weapon. Enemies that are visible to you — that your righteous
gaze falls upon — accumulate a slowly building Condemned debuff. The moment they leave your
sight, it converts.
======================================================================

NAME: Divine Sight Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — every enemy you can SEE accumulates "Condemned" stacks each tick. When
a Condemned enemy leaves your FOV, the stacks burst as radiation damage on that enemy.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Each tick the aura is active: every living enemy in dungeon.visible (visible[e.y][e.x] ==
    True) gains 1 Condemned stack (stored on the entity as entity.condemned_stacks: int = 0,
    or as a lightweight CondemnedEffect with a stack counter).
  - Stacks cap at 20 per enemy. No damage while they are visible — you are building a charge.
  - When an enemy leaves FOV (visible[e.y][e.x] becomes False) AND they have >= 1 Condemned
    stack: immediately trigger the burst. Deal condemned_stacks * 3 as true radiation damage
    to that enemy (bypasses defense). Reset condemned_stacks to 0.
  - "Leaving FOV" is detected in the engine's FOV recompute step: before recomputing, record
    which enemies were visible. After recomputing, compare. Enemies that dropped out of
    visibility fire their burst if condemned_stacks > 0.
  - Important: the burst is true radiation DAMAGE (not radiation gain). It is not add_radiation.
    It deals direct HP damage. Flavor: your gaze was the only thing keeping them alive; the
    moment they try to flee your light, your judgment descends.
  - Enemy dies before leaving FOV? Condemned stacks are lost. This is intentional — killing
    them in sight is Gamma Aura's job. Divine Sight punishes enemies that flee or break line
    of sight.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free. No per-tick cost.
  - Toggle OFF: free. All accumulated Condemned stacks on enemies are LOST (not triggered —
    toggling off is mercy). This is a meaningful toggle penalty.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Righteous Gaze": While the player has Decontamination L4+, any enemy that has 5+ Condemned
    stacks is visually marked in the message log: "[Enemy] is marked by your gaze." Also: the
    player gains +1 rad resistance for each distinct enemy in FOV with 5+ stacks (cap +3 extra
    resist). This turns your accumulated pressure into a defensive payoff.
  - Implementation: check in the FOV-visible monster list. If enemy.condemned_stacks >= 5 and
    Decon L4+ unlocked: player_stats temporary rad_resistance += 1 (up to 3 stacks total;
    recalculate each turn as (count of visible enemies with 5+ stacks) capped at 3, set as
    a temporary_stat_bonus or a dedicated engine field reset each tick).

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: melee fighters who stay close to enemies and want continuous irradiation within
    2 tiles. Front-line, aggressive play.
  - Divine Sight: ranged-to-mid players who use FOV angles. You position to maximize the number
    of enemies you can see. You want enemies to feel forced to flee — and punish them when they
    do. Great against enemies with hit-and-run AI (they'll constantly pop in and out of sight,
    each time eating a burst). Great against fast-moving enemies with room_guard AI that patrol.
  - Swap moment: when you've kited an enemy down a corridor and know they're about to turn a
    corner. Toggle to Divine Sight as they flee — the burst fires the moment they round the
    corner and drop out of FOV.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Consecrate (L3): place a Consecrate zone at a chokepoint. Enemies must cross it to reach
    you, gaining rad stacks while in sight. When they retreat through the zone, they're still
    in your FOV and still building Condemned. When they finally escape, they get hit with both
    the rad tile's ongoing effect AND a Condemned burst. Double punishment for fleeing.
  - Gamma Aura (L2): use Gamma in open-room engagements, swap to Divine Sight in corridor fights
    where enemies break LoS. This is the cleanest "when to swap" decision in any option here.
  - Radiant (L1): your +30% rad resist plus the +3 bonus from Righteous Gaze means your resist
    can reach +33% with 3 marked enemies — capping you near the top of the resist range while
    actively fighting.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New entity field: entity.condemned_stacks: int = 0. Must be serialized in save_system.py.
  - New effect or per-tick engine logic: in _run_energy_loop (or a dedicated step before FOV),
    check visible enemies and add 1 condemned_stack each tick when aura is active.
  - FOV comparison: in engine.handle_move or the FOV recompute step, before calling
    compute_fov(): store prev_visible = {(e.x,e.y) for e in monsters if visible[e.y][e.x]}.
    After compute_fov(): for each monster not in the new visible set — if condemned_stacks > 0:
    deal condemned_stacks * 3 true damage, message, reset stacks to 0.
  - Alternatively: track per-enemy "was_visible_last_tick" as a simpler flag. More robust
    against edge cases (teleport, door, etc.).
  - Toggle off cleanup: in DivineSightAuraEffect.expire(): for each monster in entities, reset
    entity.condemned_stacks = 0. Message: "Your gaze releases them."
  - The Righteous Gaze passive bonus to rad resist: recalculate in a before_turn hook on the
    effect, or as an inline check in the stat calculation path.
  - New effect class: DivineSightAuraEffect (id="divine_sight_aura", floor_duration=True)
    tick(): add 1 condemned_stack to all visible enemies (cap 20).
    expire(): clear all condemned_stacks.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - Maximum burst: 20 stacks * 3 = 60 true damage on one enemy. That takes 20 ticks to build,
    which is roughly 7-8 player turns. Not achievable every fight, but meaningful vs tough
    enemies you can kite.
  - Average burst: enemy spends 6-8 ticks in sight = 18-24 stacks = 54-72 damage. Against
    floor 3-4 enemies with 50-100 HP, this is a significant delayed nuke that fires for "free"
    when they retreat.
  - The "no damage while visible" constraint is intentional: it prevents the aura from just
    being a damage-over-time that rivals Gamma Aura. It rewards tactical positioning and
    punishes AI behaviors that break sight.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (one Decon aura at a time): enemies in your FOV gain 1 Condemned stack/tick
  (cap 20). When an enemy leaves your sight: burst for stacks * 3 true damage and reset.
  Passive: each visible enemy with 5+ stacks grants +1 rad resist (cap +3)."

OPTION 3 — "CORONA OF JUDGMENT" (the most BOLD option — FOV as a reverse shield)
High concept: your presence is blinding. Enemies looking at you are dazzled by your radiance.
While the aura is ON, every enemy in your FOV that ATTACKS you takes radiant backlash. The
paladin fantasy of being untouchable, righteous, wreathed in light that burns those who dare
strike you.
======================================================================

NAME: Corona of Judgment
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — enemies that successfully hit you while in your FOV receive a radiant
backlash: they take true damage AND you gain radiation fuel for Force Sensitive.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Reactive rather than proactive. No per-tick effect.
  - Whenever the player is hit by a melee or gun attack: if the attacking entity is in the
    player's FOV (dungeon.visible[attacker.y][attacker.x] == True):
      1. Deal (10 + player.radiation // 20) true damage to the attacker. No defense bypass
         needed — this is player's raw power, not combat system damage. Direct entity.hp
         reduction with death check.
      2. The player gains 8 radiation (NOT blocked by rad resistance — this is the aura
         feeding your rad resource). Flavor: their blow glances off your radiance and you
         ABSORB the force as pure radiation energy.
      3. Generate Decontamination XP: 8 * bksmt_modifier (same formula as add_radiation's
         XP grant). You're earning Decon XP FOR BEING HIT.
  - The backlash does NOT trigger on out-of-FOV attacks (enemies behind doors, grenades from
    unseen enemies, etc.). You must see the enemy coming.
  - No per-tick energy drain. The cost is that you MUST take hits to get value. Switching to
    this aura means leaning into taking damage, not avoiding it.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: costs 15 radiation (paid immediately from your current rad pool). You must have
    at least 15 rad to activate — flavor: you must already be "charged" to radiate judgment.
  - Toggle OFF: free. Refunds nothing.
  - Decision logic: worth toggling on mid-combat when you know you're about to eat several hits.
    Worth toggling off in hit-and-run situations where enemies won't be in your FOV.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Radiant Constitution": unlocking L4 adds a new persistent mechanic: while the player's
    radiation is between 50-200, all incoming damage is reduced by 1 (flat reduction, not %).
    Below 50 or above 200: no reduction (high rad is dangerous even for the paladin).
    This sweet spot "mid-rad" DR creates a strong reason to actively maintain radiation levels
    rather than always purging immediately. It rewards the Decontamination playstyle identity
    of "I want to carry medium rad as a resource."
  - Implementation: in combat.py's player_take_damage or the existing damage reduction path,
    check player.radiation >= 50 and <= 200 and Decon L4+. If so, damage -= 1 (min 1 total).

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: you're the aggressor. You want to irradiate enemies faster than they can react.
    Best when you have high movement/damage and can close quickly.
  - Corona of Judgment: you're the immovable object. You stand in the corridor, let enemies
    come to you, and punish every hit. Best against enemies with high attack frequency (multiple
    hits per turn = multiple backlash procs). Best when you're low on damage but expecting a
    prolonged fight.
  - Swap moment: when you're about to tank a boss room or a cluster of aggressive enemies.
    Toggle to Corona before they reach you, then soak hits while generating rad and dealing
    backlash. When they're softened up, swap back to Gamma to finish them with the irradiation.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Force Sensitive: EVERY HIT you take while this aura is on gives you 8 rad. If you take
    5 hits, that's 40 rad gained. After a tough fight you emerge with a full Force Sensitive
    cycle ready to fire — smoke Skywalker OG, purge the absorbed rad, convert it to temp STR.
    The aura LOADS your resource cycle from combat itself.
  - Radiant Constitution (passive): keeping rad between 50-200 for the -1 DR means you don't
    want to purge all your rad immediately. This creates exactly the right tension with Force
    Sensitive: purge or hold? Corona rewards holding; Force Sensitive rewards purging.
  - Consecrate (L3): place a zone, let enemies walk into it, stand in it yourself. While they
    attack you in your own zone, Corona fires backlash AND the zone is irradiating them. You
    take hits but deal true damage, absorb rad, get Decon XP, AND the zone stacks their rad.
  - Gamma Aura (L2): Corona generates rad (from being hit) that you can then spend activating
    Gamma Aura (Gamma costs nothing to toggle, but you want rad for Force Sensitive). The two
    auras are a natural sequence: Corona loads you up, Gamma spends your advantage.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: CoronaOfJudgmentEffect (id="corona_of_judgment", floor_duration=True)
    No tick() needed.
    Hook needed: on_player_take_damage(attacker, damage, engine) — this lifecycle hook does
    NOT currently exist on the Effect base class. It would need to be added.
    ALTERNATIVE (avoid new lifecycle hook): add an inline check in combat.py player_take_damage:
    check if player has "corona_of_judgment" effect active and attacker is in FOV. If so:
      deal backlash damage to attacker, add 8 rad to player, generate XP.
    The inline approach is simpler and doesn't require adding a new lifecycle hook.
  - Radiant Constitution passive: inline check in combat.py player_take_damage. No effect object
    needed. Check: Decon level >= 4 and 50 <= player.radiation <= 200: damage = max(1, damage-1).
  - New AbilityDef "corona_of_judgment": ChargeType.INFINITE, TargetType.SELF. Execute function:
    standard toggle pattern. Check for existing Decon aura, expire it. Then check player.radiation
    >= 15 (cost check). If insufficient: message + return False. Else: remove 15 rad (via
    remove_radiation, which fires Force Sensitive if appropriate), apply effect.
  - Save/load: no new engine state. Effect serializes via existing system.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - Backlash damage formula: 10 + rad//20. At 0 rad: 10 flat. At 100 rad: 15. At 200 rad: 20.
    At MAX 500 rad: 35. This scales with maintaining medium rad, which Radiant Constitution also
    incentivizes. The curve is modest — never exceeds 35 at maximum rad. Backlash is not the
    kill vector; it's chip damage that compounds over many hits.
  - The real payoff is the 8 rad gain per hit. In a fight with 10 incoming hits: 80 rad gained
    = 3 Force Sensitive threshold crossings = +6 temp STR after smoking. That is the true value.
  - 15 rad toggle cost is cheap — roughly 2 ticks of Gamma Aura's self-drain in the L2 options.
    It's a signal cost, not a real gate.
  - Risk: against enemies with very low attack frequency (1 hit total), Corona gives minimal
    value. It's best against swarms or boss-type enemies with multi-attack patterns.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (costs 15 rad to activate): when a visible enemy hits you, deal (10+rad//20)
  true damage to them and gain 8 rad. Passive: while your radiation is 50-200, take 1 less
  damage from all sources."

OPTION 4 — "PENITENT AURA" (the risk/reward gambler option)
High concept: the holier you are, the more you've suffered. This aura INVERTS your current HP
— the lower your HP, the stronger your radiation field. The paladin walking with one foot in
death, blazing brightest at the edge.
======================================================================

NAME: Penitent Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — your radiation field strength scales inversely with your HP. The more
wounded you are, the more radiation you emit to nearby enemies per tick.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Per-tick: calculate your "penitent ratio" = (max_hp - current_hp) / max_hp (range 0.0-1.0).
    Apply radiation to all enemies within 3 tiles equal to:
    base_rad = 3 (minimum emission even at full HP)
    bonus_rad = int(penitent_ratio * 17)  # up to +17 at 1 HP
    total_per_tick = base_rad + bonus_rad  # range 3 to 20 per tick
  - At full HP: emits 3 rad/tick (less than Gamma Aura's 5/tick) — weaker than Gamma while
    you're healthy. The player gets NOTHING extra for being healthy. This is intentional.
  - At 50% HP: emits 3 + 8 = 11 rad/tick — significantly more than Gamma Aura.
  - At 25% HP: emits 3 + 12 = 15 rad/tick.
  - At 10% HP: emits 3 + 16 = 19 rad/tick, nearly 4x Gamma Aura's rate.
  - Radius: 3 tiles (slightly larger than Gamma Aura's 2 — at max risk you're also a wider beacon).
  - No self-drain cost. The cost is being wounded.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free.
  - Toggle OFF: free.
  - Decision logic: swap from Gamma to Penitent when you're already hurt and can't avoid taking
    more damage. Turn your near-death state into an offensive amplifier instead of a panic state.
    Swap BACK to Gamma after healing to get the reliable 5/tick instead of the floor-3 minimum.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Martyrdom": the first time you would die on each floor (hit points would go to 0), instead
    survive at 1 HP and become briefly Irradiated yourself (gain 50 rad immediately). This does
    NOT prevent death from subsequent hits — it is a single-use death-prevention per floor.
  - The 50 rad gain from Martyrdom: if Penitent Aura is active, you're now at 1 HP = maximum
    penitent output = 20 rad/tick to nearby enemies while also carrying 50 rad for Force
    Sensitive. The perk turns a near-death moment into a final blaze-of-glory combat window.
  - Martyrdom uses a floor-scoped flag: engine.martyrdom_used_this_floor (bool, reset on floor
    transition). Check in the player death path before applying death state.
  - This passive is GENUINELY EXCITING in a roguelike context: it turns the scariest moments
    into opportunities.

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: steady, reliable 5/tick irradiation. Best when you're healthy and want
    consistent enemy rad buildup.
  - Penitent Aura: situational peak power. When wounded, dramatically outperforms Gamma.
    When healthy, underperforms Gamma. The skill expression is knowing when to swap.
  - Natural swap rhythm: start floor with Gamma Aura. Take significant damage mid-fight.
    Swap to Penitent. At low HP, you're now irradiating enemies at 3-4x normal rate. Finish
    the fight. Heal up (Skywalker OG / food). Swap back to Gamma.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Force Sensitive + Martyrdom: survive to 1 HP → gain 50 rad via Martyrdom → Penitent Aura
    at full power (19/tick) → enemies in range take 19 rad/tick → smoke Skywalker OG → purge
    rad for Force Sensitive STR stacks. The worst possible moment becomes a power spike.
  - Consecrate (L3): standing on your own rad zone at low HP. You're taking +5 rad/turn from
    the tile (minor), but Penitent is blasting enemies for 19/tick. The zone keeps enemies in
    range for the full duration.
  - Constitution stat: CON affects max HP (30 + CON*10). High CON means a bigger HP gap to
    fall into, which means slightly more Penitent scaling headroom. Low CON builds get smaller
    penitent bonus at the same absolute HP count, but also have less HP to lose.
  - STR from Radiant (L1) and Consecrate: the combined STR bonuses help you deal more damage
    on the aggressive swing after Martyrdom fires, letting you convert the near-death window
    into kills while Penitent is at max power.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: PenitentAuraEffect (id="penitent_aura", floor_duration=True)
    tick(): compute max_hp = engine.player_stats.max_hp (or engine.player.max_hp — whichever
    field tracks current max HP in the system). current_hp = engine.player.hp. If max_hp <= 0:
    return. ratio = (max_hp - current_hp) / max_hp (clamp 0.0-1.0). per_tick = 3 + int(ratio*17).
    For each monster within Chebyshev 3 of player: add_radiation(engine, monster, per_tick,
    from_player=True). XP per monster: gain_potential_exp("Decontamination", per_tick, bksmt).
  - Martyrdom passive: add to combat.py's player_take_damage, in the death check section:
    if player.hp <= 0 and not engine.martyrdom_used_this_floor and skills.get("Decontamination").
    level >= 4: player.hp = 1; engine.martyrdom_used_this_floor = True; add_radiation(engine,
    player, 50, pierce_resistance=True); engine.messages.append("Martyrdom! You survive on 1 HP!
    +50 rad"); return (don't apply death).
  - engine.martyrdom_used_this_floor: bool = False. Reset in floor transition logic.
  - Save/load: martyrdom_used_this_floor to engine state serialization.
  - max_hp: verify this field exists on player entity or player_stats. If it lives in
    player_stats as 30 + CON*10, use that calculation inline.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - Martyrdom is extremely powerful — death prevention is rare. Balanced by:
    1. One use per floor only.
    2. You survive at 1 HP (next hit kills you unless you heal first).
    3. The 50 rad gained is both a resource and a liability in other build contexts.
  - Penitent Aura at full power (19/tick, radius 3) exceeds Gamma Aura (5/tick, radius 2) by
    roughly 14 rad/tick per enemy. Over a 15-tick fight at low HP with 2 enemies in range:
    +420 rad total vs Gamma's +150 rad total. That is a large gap. But you must be near-dead
    to achieve it, which means Martyrdom has already fired (one use), and the 1 HP vulnerability
    is live. High ceiling, high risk.
  - At full HP, Penitent (3/tick) is strictly worse than Gamma (5/tick). A healthy player gets
    more from Gamma. Penitent is only worth having if you expect to take significant damage.
    In easier content, Gamma wins. In desperate fights, Penitent is the better choice.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (one Decon aura at a time): emit (3 + (1-HP%)*17) rad/tick to enemies within
  3 tiles — the more wounded you are, the more powerful the field. Passive: once per floor,
  surviving a lethal hit leaves you at 1 HP and grants 50 rad (Martyrdom)."


======================================================================
OPTION 5 — "ABSOLUTION AURA" (the healer-paladin option, wildly different feel)
High concept: your radiation field can CLEANSE. While this aura is on, enemy radiation that
exceeds a threshold burns away, but instead of killing them slowly, it explodes off them all
at once as direct damage — AND heals you. You're not the source of radiation. You're the
purifier. You pull their contamination out of them.
======================================================================

NAME: Absolution Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — enemies within 3 tiles that exceed 100 rad are "cleansed": they lose
half their radiation instantly, which deals that radiation as true damage AND heals you.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Per-tick check: for each living enemy within 3 tiles of the player, if enemy.radiation >= 100:
      - stripped = enemy.radiation // 2
      - enemy.radiation -= stripped
      - Deal stripped // 4 (rounded) true damage to that enemy (NOT radiation damage — this is
        instant direct damage from the purge). Example: enemy at 200 rad = strip 100 = 25 damage.
      - Heal player for stripped // 8 HP (cap: cannot exceed normal max HP).
        Example: 100 stripped = 12 HP. Not enormous — this is healing earned by purging.
      - Generate Decontamination XP: stripped * 2 (same rate as removing radiation from self,
        since you are doing the decontaminating).
      - Message: "[Enemy] is cleansed! (25 dmg, +12 HP)"
  - This check fires ONCE per enemy per tick, not continuously. After cleansing, the enemy drops
    below 100 rad, so it won't cleanse again until they re-accumulate.
  - No per-tick cost to the player.
  - The flavor: you are not irradiating them further. You are burning their contamination OUT.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free. No per-tick cost.
  - Toggle OFF: free.
  - Decision logic: ONLY useful against already-irradiated enemies. Against non-irradiated
    targets, this aura does nothing at all. It is the ultimate "all-in on the irradiation build"
    choice: you need Emission/Gamma Aura to irradiate enemies first, then Absolution to harvest.
    Swap TO Absolution when you've maxed enemy rad via Gamma Aura or Consecrate.
    Swap BACK to Gamma if enemies enter the room fresh (not yet irradiated).

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Decontamination Mastery": Decontamination XP gains are increased by 25% permanently.
    Specifically: in combat.py's remove_radiation function, the XP grant line currently reads
    gain_potential_exp("Decontamination", removed * 2, bksmt). At L4+ with this passive: change
    to removed * 2.5 (multiply by 1.25 factor). This accelerates skill progression to L5/L6/L7
    significantly, rewarding deep investment.
  - Additionally: the Decontamination skill's XP-to-level thresholds are static. The 25% bonus
    means players investing in the full tree can reach L6/L7 faster — making L5's Consecrate
    upgrade feel more accessible.

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: you're building radiation pressure. Good for sustained fights. Enemies are your
    radiation storage tanks; you want them full.
  - Absolution Aura: you're cashing in radiation pressure. Enemies as your battery that you
    drain for HP and burst damage. Classic "swing the pendulum" design — fill with Gamma, drain
    with Absolution, fill with Gamma again.
  - Optimal macro pattern: Enter room. Toggle Gamma. Build enemy rad to 100+. Swap to Absolution.
    Cleanse enemies for burst damage + healing. Swap back to Gamma to refill. This gives the
    player a satisfying toggle loop with meaningful timing decisions each fight.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Emission (from old L2 design context): Emission set all enemy rad to yours in one shot. If
    you entered a room at 200 rad and Emission'd, every enemy immediately hits 200 — immediately
    triggering Absolution cleanse on the same turn if active. One-shot combo potential.
  - Gamma Aura (L2): the active setup for Absolution. Fill enemies with rad using Gamma, then
    swap to Absolution and harvest. The toggle swap IS the combo.
  - Consecrate (L3): rad tiles fill enemies to 100+ even if you've swapped off Gamma Aura.
    Having a Consecrate zone running means Absolution can fire even mid-swap, since the zone
    is doing the irradiation work independently. Strong tri-part combo.
  - Force Sensitive: Absolution's healing component doesn't trigger Force Sensitive (which
    tracks rad REMOVED from the player, not healed HP). But the Decontamination XP bonus (25%)
    means you level this skill faster and reach Sponge (L5) quicker, which DOES have rad-cycling
    synergy with Force Sensitive.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: AbsolutionAuraEffect (id="absolution_aura", floor_duration=True)
    tick(): for each monster within Chebyshev 3 of player, if monster.radiation >= 100:
      stripped = monster.radiation // 2; monster.radiation -= stripped;
      direct_dmg = stripped // 4; deal_damage(engine, monster, direct_dmg, bypass_armor=True);
      heal_amount = stripped // 8; engine.player.hp = min(engine.player.max_hp, engine.player.hp
      + heal_amount); gain_potential_exp("Decontamination", stripped*2, bksmt); message.
  - The HP healing is direct player.hp modification — NOT through a heal ability (to avoid
    triggering weird heal-blocking effects). Simple min(max_hp, hp + amount) clamp.
  - Decontamination Mastery XP bonus: add a multiplier check in the XP gain calls within
    add_radiation and remove_radiation in combat.py. If Decon >= L4 and passive is this one:
    multiply by 1.25. Cleanest if stored as engine.decon_xp_mult float (default 1.0; set to
    1.25 on unlock). This field would persist through floor transitions.
  - NOTE: engine.decon_xp_mult should be initialized in engine.__init__ and serialized.
  - The heal check "cannot exceed max_hp" uses player.max_hp. Verify this field is set correctly
    (should be 30 + CON*10 based on config.py and stats notes).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - Healing from Absolution: stripped // 8 means 200-rad enemy = 100 stripped = 12 HP healed.
    Against 3 enemies all at 200 rad: 36 HP healed in one tick. That is significant but requires
    extreme setup (Gamma Aura running for many turns first). In practice, enemies rarely sustain
    200 rad for long before dying from the irradiated DOT (rad//50/tick).
  - Direct damage from Absolution: 200 rad enemy = 100 stripped = 25 damage. This is meaningful
    burst damage but not instant kill. Combined with the irradiated DOT already ticking, the
    cleanse accelerates kills without replacing the core DOT mechanic.
  - The "nothing against non-irradiated enemies" limitation is a real constraint. Players must
    build the irradiation pipeline first. This keeps Absolution from being the go-to starting
    aura on every floor.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (one Decon aura at a time): enemies within 3 tiles at 100+ rad are cleansed:
  lose half their rad, taking that//4 as true damage; you heal for that//8 HP. Passive: +25%
  Decontamination XP from all sources."


======================================================================
OPTION 6 — "IRONSOUL AURA" (the tank-paladin option, pure defensive novelty)
High concept: your FOV is your armor. Enemies you can see cannot surprise you. More precisely:
this aura channels your FOV into a damage reduction field — the more enemies in your sight,
the stronger your protection. Overwhelm you with numbers and you grow HARDER.
======================================================================

NAME: Ironsoul Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura — for each enemy in your FOV, gain 1 flat damage reduction (cap 5).
When you take a hit from a VISIBLE enemy, gain 10 rad as feedback.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Per-tick (or recalculated before each player turn via before_turn hook):
    count = number of living enemies currently in dungeon.visible (player's FOV).
    dr_bonus = min(5, count)  # 1 DR per enemy, hard cap at 5
    Store this as a temporary effect field: self.current_dr_bonus.
    Apply it as a modification to player_stats.DR or as a modify_incoming_damage hook:
    reduce incoming damage by self.current_dr_bonus (additive with other DR, not multiplicative).
  - Additionally: whenever the player is hit by an attack from a VISIBLE enemy while the aura
    is ON: gain 10 rad (add_radiation, pierce_resistance=False so Radiant's 30% resist applies).
    This creates the feedback loop: you're being hit, you absorb radiation, you fuel Force
    Sensitive and the Consecrate/Penitent loops.
  - At 5+ enemies visible: 5 flat DR. At 1 enemy: 1 DR. At 0 enemies visible: 0 DR (aura
    does nothing in empty rooms but costs nothing either).
  - The DR is checked in combat.py's damage calculation. Modify the defense calculation or add
    to engine.player_stats.DR (which already exists as a field).

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free.
  - Toggle OFF: free. DR bonus immediately drops to 0.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Line of Sight Awareness": while Decontamination L4 is unlocked, the player's FOV radius
    is increased by 1 (from 8 to 9). This is implemented as a permanent +1 to
    player_stats.sight_radius (or the dungeon's FOV computation parameter). It is not large, but
    it is UNIQUE — no other perk in the game extends FOV radius. It rewards the Decontamination
    player's interest in vision mechanics. A larger FOV means more enemies visible = more DR
    from the aura.
  - Additionally: the player receives a brief message whenever a new enemy enters their FOV for
    the first time each floor: "[enemy type] spotted." This is a QoL/information perk that no
    other skill provides. Implemented by tracking first_seen_entity_ids: set, reset each floor.
    When engine FOV updates, check for new entities entering visibility not in the set.

WHY THIS AURA vs GAMMA AURA?
  - Gamma Aura: offensive, wants enemies close (2 tile radius) to irradiate them.
  - Ironsoul: defensive, wants enemies visible but possibly at range. Best in open rooms with
    multiple enemies where DR-5 meaningfully reduces cumulative incoming damage.
  - Swap moment: when entering a large room with many enemies. Toggle to Ironsoul to get DR-5,
    absorb the incoming volley with DR and rad feedback, then swap to Gamma when you've closed
    in and want to melt them with irradiation.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist means the 10 rad feedback from Ironsoul is actually 7 rad
    after resist. Over 15 hits from visible enemies: 105 rad gained (at 30% resist). Nearly
    enough for 4 Force Sensitive threshold crossings.
  - Swagger (base stat): the existing Swagger defence = int((SWG-8)/2) stacks additively with
    Ironsoul's DR. At SWG 12: base +2 defence. With Ironsoul at 5 enemies: +7 effective DR
    total. This approaches tanking territory without being a tank build — it's still dependent
    on having enemies visible and the aura active.
  - Consecrate (L3): place a zone, enemies step into it. They're all visible (in your FOV) and
    nearby. Ironsoul gives you DR-5, Consecrate irradiates them, and each hit gives 10 rad back.
    The zone becomes a brawl arena where you get tougher the more enemies are in it with you.
  - Penitent Aura (Option 4) cross-reference: these two auras serve similar "I'm taking hits,
    reward me" niches but from different angles. Ironsoul is the DR-defensive version;
    Penitent is the offense-amplified version. They would be meaningful choices against each
    other.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: IronsoulAuraEffect (id="ironsoul_aura", floor_duration=True)
    Fields: current_dr_bonus: int = 0
    before_turn() hook OR tick(): count visible enemies in dungeon.entities. Set self.
    current_dr_bonus = min(5, count). Store in a way that combat.py can read it.
  - Combat integration: in combat.py player_take_damage, after swagger_defence calculation:
    if any effect on player has id == "ironsoul_aura": extra_dr = effect.current_dr_bonus;
    damage = max(1, damage - extra_dr). Also: if attacker is visible, add_radiation(engine,
    player, 10, pierce_resistance=False).
  - FOV radius passive: in stats.py or dungeon.py's compute_fov call, add a check:
    if engine.skills.get("Decontamination").level >= 4: fov_radius += 1. Or add a
    player_stats.sight_radius_bonus field (already proposed in previous design batches).
  - "Enemy spotted" notification: in engine's FOV update step, compare new visible set against
    engine.first_seen_entity_ids (set, reset per floor). New entries fire a quick message.
    Add engine.first_seen_entity_ids: set = set() to engine state. Reset on floor transition.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS:
  - DR-5 is meaningful but not game-breaking. Against a 10-damage attack: DR-5 reduces to 5.
    Against a 4-damage attack (floor 1-2 range): DR-5 reduces to max(1, 4-5) = 1 (min 1
    enforced). So against weak enemies the cap is min 1. Against strong Meth Lab enemies
    dealing 15-20 damage: DR-5 reduces each hit by 5, which is a 25-33% reduction. Very solid.
  - Requires 5 visible enemies for max DR. In many floors that's achievable in large rooms but
    not in narrow corridors. The aura has natural ebb/flow: strong in rooms, weak in hallways.
  - The 10 rad per visible-attacker feedback generates moderate rad over a full fight. It does
    not snowball out of control since each hit only gives 10 rad (and 30% is resisted by Radiant,
    so net 7 rad per hit). Less rad gen than Corona of Judgment (Option 3's 8 rad per hit with
    pierce_resistance=True).
  - FOV +1 is tiny but has cascading effects: more room covered, more enemies visible for DR,
    more tiles for Consecrate zones to be detected. Low impact individually, thematically clean.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (one Decon aura at a time): gain 1 flat DR per enemy in your FOV (max 5 DR).
  Each hit from a visible enemy grants 10 rad. Passive: FOV radius +1; new enemies entering
  sight are announced."

COMPARISON TABLE — WHEN TO PICK WHICH
======================================================================

  OPTION 1 — Sacred Ground: "I want my Consecrate zones to last forever."
    Best for: zone-control players, kiting builds, slow deliberate exploration.
    Requires: L3 Consecrate for full value. Nearly useless without it.
    Vibe: Patient paladin who blesses the entire dungeon.

  OPTION 2 — Divine Sight: "I want to punish enemies that try to flee."
    Best for: corridor fighters, players who kite around corners intentionally.
    Requires: enemies that break LoS (hit-and-run AI, room_guard patrollers).
    Vibe: Righteous judge whose gaze condemns the guilty.

  OPTION 3 — Corona of Judgment: "I want to get hit and come out stronger."
    Best for: high-CON tanks who want to convert enemy aggression into Force Sensitive fuel.
    Requires: willingness to take hits, medium rad maintenance.
    Vibe: Immovable paladin who absorbs punishment and returns judgment.

  OPTION 4 — Penitent Aura: "I like high-risk high-reward. I want death to mean something."
    Best for: aggressive players who live dangerously. Martyrdom is the most exciting perk.
    Requires: accepting that near-death windows are power spikes, not emergencies.
    Vibe: The wounded warrior who blazes brightest at the brink of death.

  OPTION 5 — Absolution Aura: "I want to cash in my enemy irradiation as burst + healing."
    Best for: builds that already run Gamma Aura heavily. The "completion" of the irradiation
    loop — fill enemies with Gamma, drain them with Absolution.
    Requires: enemy rad to already be built up (needs Gamma or Consecrate active first).
    Vibe: The healer-purifier who cleanses contamination at great force.

  OPTION 6 — Ironsoul Aura: "I want to tank hits and have vision be my defense."
    Best for: players in large rooms fighting multiple enemies simultaneously. FOV synergy.
    Requires: open rooms; weak in corridors.
    Vibe: The unshakeable sentinel whose awareness of danger is itself a shield.


======================================================================
RECOMMENDED PICK — AUTHOR'S CHOICE
======================================================================

CORONA OF JUDGMENT (Option 3) is the boldest and freshest. Reasons:
  1. It introduces a completely new lifecycle hook (or combat.py path): player-is-hit triggers
     a counter-effect on the attacker. Nothing in the game does this in the "aura" paradigm.
  2. The Radiant Constitution passive (DR at 50-200 rad) creates a brilliant tension with Force
     Sensitive: purge rad = lose the DR. Hold rad = keep the DR. This is the first mechanic
     that creates a COST to purging, which is the core Decontamination loop.
  3. Martyrdom (Option 4 passive) is actually more exciting — but Corona's overall synergy with
     Force Sensitive is tighter and more universally useful.

DIVINE SIGHT (Option 2) is the most UNIQUE to NIGRL specifically. FOV is a system that no
other skill interacts with. Condemned stacks that burst on leaving sight creates tactical play
around corridor angles, doorways, and hit-and-run AI that is completely emergent and specific
to how NIGRL's dungeon geometry works.

IF THE USER IS MOST EXCITED BY: the paladin fantasy of "my presence changes how the fight
works" → Ironsoul (Option 6) or Sacred Ground (Option 1).

IF THE USER IS MOST EXCITED BY: punishment mechanics, risk/reward, near-death power spikes →
Penitent (Option 4), specifically Martyrdom.

IF THE USER IS MOST EXCITED BY: completing a satisfying gameplay loop → Absolution (Option 5),
as the closer of the Gamma → Absolution → Gamma cycle.

IF THE USER IS MOST EXCITED BY: novel mechanics not seen anywhere else in the game → Divine
Sight (Option 2), which uses the FOV system in a completely unprecedented way.
