OVERVIEW
--------
The Graffiti skill tree is built around TERRITORIAL CONTROL — painting the
dungeon floor with spray-painted tiles that alter the rules of combat in those
zones. Unlike existing hazard mechanics (fire tiles that ignite, acid pools that
deal damage, trap tiles from Numbers Game that trigger once), Graffiti tiles are
PERSISTENT FLOOR-DURATION ZONES that apply ongoing effects to enemies standing
in them AND buff the player when they reclaim their own painted ground.

XP SOURCE:
  Graffiti XP is earned whenever an enemy takes damage while standing on a
  player-painted tile. Each hit while an enemy is on graffiti = 10 XP.
  Killing an enemy on graffiti = 25 XP bonus.

STAT DEPENDENCY:
  Street Smarts  — spray range, number of tiles painted per cast
  Swagger        — potency of damage amplification on painted tiles

VOCABULARY / AESTHETIC:
  "Tag"    — a quick single-tile mark (the simplest graffiti)
  "Throw-up" — a quick bubble-letter piece, covers more ground than a tag
  "Burner" — a complex full-color piece, takes more effort, more impact
  "Chrome" — pure silver paint, the most prestigious style
  "Bombing" — hitting as many surfaces as fast as possible (quantity over quality)
  "Buff"   — to REMOVE graffiti (used ironically here as a passive mechanic name)
  "Cap"    — to spray over someone else's work (here used as an enemy debuff)
  "Crew"   — your people; standing with your work on the ground

================================================================================
  LEVEL 1 — "Tag"
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "tag"}
================================================================================

NAME: Tag

FLAVOR:
  A quick hit — mark a tile with a red tag and make your enemies suffer for
  walking on your art. Every crew starts here.

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Grants the "tag" ability. On activation, press a directional key to paint
  an adjacent floor tile with a red graffiti mark (ADJACENT_TILE targeting).
  The painted tile becomes a "graffiti" hazard entity that persists until the
  floor ends (floor_duration hazard).

  While any living enemy stands on a graffiti tile, they take +20% increased
  damage from all sources (player melee, player ranged, player abilities,
  DoTs, everything). This is a damage MULTIPLIER applied to the defending
  entity — not a flat bonus to attacker power.

  The +20% damage amp is implemented as a debuff effect ("tagged") on the
  enemy that is applied each tick they remain on the tile, similar to how
  ignite is applied from fire tiles. The "tagged" effect lasts 2 turns after
  the enemy leaves the tile (so kiting an enemy off the tag then immediately
  attacking still gets the bonus — rewards clever play).

  The player can have up to STS/2 (min 3) active graffiti tiles at once.
  Painting a new tile over the limit destroys the oldest tag (FIFO).

  Charge type: INFINITE (no charge limit — spray as much as you want).
  Energy cost: 50 (half-turn action, like a quick spray).

  Visual: Graffiti tile displays as '%' in red (RGB 220, 60, 60).
  The hazard entity uses char='%', color=(220, 60, 60), hazard_type="graffiti".

ABILITY DEFINITION:
  ability_id: "tag"
  name: "Tag"
  description: "Paint an adjacent tile red. Enemies on it take +20% damage."
  char: "%"
  color: (220, 60, 60)
  target_type: TargetType.ADJACENT_TILE
  charge_type: ChargeType.INFINITE
  max_range: 1

EXECUTE LOGIC (execute_at):
  1. Validate target tile is a floor tile and not already a wall or blocked
     by movement-blocking entity.
  2. Check graffiti tile count; remove oldest if at cap.
  3. Spawn a graffiti hazard entity at (tx, ty) with floor_duration=True.
  4. Message: "You tag the floor."
  5. Award potential Graffiti XP: 5 XP for placing the tag.

NEW EFFECT — "tagged" (debuff):
  id: "tagged"
  category: "debuff"
  duration: 2 (refreshed each tick the entity remains on the tile)
  floor_duration: False
  hook: modify_incoming_damage(damage, entity) -> return int(damage * 1.20)
  display_name: "Tagged"
  on_reapply: refresh duration to 2 (simple refresh; does not stack amp)

NEW HAZARD PROCESSING in engine._run_energy_loop step 4:
  Add case for hazard_type == "graffiti":
    For each entity (player + monsters) at the tile's position:
      If entity is a monster: apply_effect(entity, engine, "tagged", silent=True)
      (Player standing on own graffiti is handled separately — see L2)

ENGINE TRACKING:
  engine.graffiti_tiles: list[Entity] — ordered list of placed graffiti hazards
  (FIFO cap enforcement uses this list)

IMPLEMENTATION FILES:
  - abilities.py: add AbilityDef for "tag"
  - effects.py: add TaggedEffect class
  - hazards.py: add create_graffiti(x, y) factory function
  - engine.py: add graffiti processing in step 4, add graffiti_tiles list,
               add graffiti XP grant in xp_progression.py
  - xp_progression.py: add GRAFFITI_HIT_XP = 10, GRAFFITI_KILL_XP = 25

BALANCE NOTES:
  +20% damage amp is meaningful — roughly equivalent to the player having
  +2 effective power on a 10-damage hit. Not overwhelming at L1 since it
  requires positioning (you have to get the enemy onto your tile).
  The 2-turn linger after leaving rewards players who kite through their art.
  INFINITE charges means the skill is always active-feeling — you're always
  painting. The tile cap (STS/2, min 3) keeps the map from becoming
  entirely red by endgame without punishing the early player.

SYNERGIES:
  - Stabbing/Windfury: extra hit while enemy is tagged = double-dipping the amp
  - Sniping/Dead Eye: snipe from range after positioning enemy on tag
  - Blackkk Magic/Numbers Game: trap tiles + graffiti amp = layered zone control
  - Jaywalking/Air Jordans: free movement lets you kite enemies onto tags cheaply

================================================================================
  LEVEL 2 — "Throw-Up"
  perk_type: "passive"
================================================================================

NAME: Throw-Up

FLAVOR:
  A throw-up covers more ground — bubble letters, five tiles fast. And when
  you're standing on your own work, something clicks. This is YOUR block.

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION (two effects):

PART A — EXPANDED SPRAY PATTERN:
  The Tag ability now paints in a PLUS pattern (cross): the target tile plus
  the 4 cardinal neighbors, for 5 tiles per cast. Each of the 5 tiles becomes
  a separate graffiti hazard entity, all sharing the same floor_duration.
  The tile cap is now STS tiles (was STS/2). Because you paint 5 at a time,
  effective "casts" before cap = STS/5 (so at STS 10 you can have 2 full
  throw-ups active before the oldest begins expiring).

  If any of the 5 target tiles are walls or out of bounds, those tiles are
  simply skipped (partial throw-ups are allowed).

PART B — HOME GROUND BUFF:
  When the player stands on any graffiti tile they own, they gain the passive
  buff "home_ground":
    - +15% crit chance (additive with existing crit chance)
    - +10 energy per tick (equivalent to a minor speed boost)
  "home_ground" is a floor_duration buff re-applied each tick the player stands
  on a graffiti tile. It expires 3 turns after the player leaves graffiti.
  The crit bonus stacks with Street Smarts crit scaling.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  Throw-Up modifies the "tag" ability's execute_at function behavior. Cleanest
  approach: check engine.player.skills.get("Graffiti").level >= 2 inside the
  execute_at callable and paint the plus pattern instead of the single tile.

  home_ground effect processing: in engine step 4 graffiti handling, after
  applying "tagged" to enemies, check if entity == engine.player and entity is
  on a graffiti tile — if so, apply_effect(player, engine, "home_ground").

NEW EFFECT — "home_ground" (buff):
  id: "home_ground"
  category: "buff"
  duration: 3 (refreshed each tick player stands on graffiti)
  floor_duration: False
  stat bonus while active: +15% crit_chance (additive), +10 energy/tick
  hook: modify_energy_gain(amount, entity) -> return amount + 10
  Additional: PlayerStats needs to expose a temporary_crit_bonus field, or
  home_ground can set a flag engine reads during crit resolution. Simplest:
  add a temporary_crit_bonus to PlayerStats summed during crit calculation,
  analogous to temporary_stat_bonuses. The effect's apply() sets it; expire()
  clears it.
  display_name: "Home Ground"

IMPLEMENTATION FILES:
  - abilities.py: modify "tag" execute_at to check Graffiti L2 for pattern
  - effects.py: add HomeGroundEffect class
  - stats.py: add temporary_crit_bonus: int field to PlayerStats (defaulting 0)
  - engine.py or combat.py: sum temporary_crit_bonus into crit chance calculation
  - engine.py: add home_ground application in step 4 graffiti processing

BALANCE NOTES:
  The plus pattern dramatically increases coverage per cast. It's now efficient
  enough that you can genuinely control corridors — 5 tiles covers most hallway
  widths. The tile cap increase keeps it from flooding every room.
  Home Ground is the first instance of a player-tile-interaction buff in the
  game. +15% crit and +10 energy/tick is roughly equivalent to a good drink
  buff — justified because it requires positioning and investment of painted
  tiles. This perk has no "activate and forget" value — you must actively stay
  on your art, creating interesting risk/reward tension (standing on your tags
  means enemies can reach you).

SYNERGIES:
  - Stabbing/Windfury: Home Ground crit bonus + Windfury proc chance = fishing
    for multi-hit crits on tagged enemies
  - Jaywalking/Dash: Quickly reposition onto graffiti after placing it

================================================================================
  LEVEL 3 — "Burner"
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "burner"}
================================================================================

NAME: Burner

FLAVOR:
  A burner is the real deal — hours of work, full color, the kind of piece
  that makes people stop. It also makes enemies stop. Dead in their tracks,
  staring at it, confused.

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Grants the "burner" ability. On activation, target a visible floor tile
  (SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS or open-tile targeting) within STS/2 range (min 4 tiles).
  From that tile, a line of 3 graffiti tiles is painted in the direction from
  the player to the target tile (so it extends away from the player).
  These are "burner" graffiti tiles — visually and mechanically distinct from
  red tags.

  Burner tiles use char='%', color=(0, 160, 255) (vivid blue).
  They are stored with a custom field: graffiti_type="burner".

  BURNER EFFECT ON ENEMIES:
  When an enemy FIRST ENTERS a burner tile (walks onto it for the first time
  from an adjacent non-burner tile), they are hit with the "captivated" debuff:
    - Enemy skips their next turn entirely (one turn stun, but flavor is
      "stopped to stare at the art" — mechanically it's a before_turn skip)
    - After skipping, the enemy continues normally; re-entering the same burner
      tile does NOT retrigger captivated (immune for 10 turns after first trigger)
  Enemies already standing on the tile when it's painted are NOT affected —
  you have to lure them through it.

  Burner tiles also apply the "tagged" damage amp (+20%) the same as red tags.
  They count toward the tile cap (STS tiles total across all graffiti types).

  Charge type: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=2
  Energy cost: 100 (full turn — painting a burner takes time)

ABILITY DEFINITION:
  ability_id: "burner"
  name: "Burner"
  description: "Paint a 3-tile line of vivid blue. Enemies who walk through it
                are captivated for 1 turn. 2 uses/floor."
  char: "%"
  color: (0, 160, 255)
  target_type: TargetType.SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS  (retargeted as open tile)
  charge_type: ChargeType.PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 2

NEW EFFECT — "captivated" (debuff):
  id: "captivated"
  category: "debuff"
  duration: 1
  hook: before_turn(entity, player, dungeon) -> if duration > 0: return True (skip)
  on_reapply: do NOT refresh (immune window handled below)
  display_name: "Captivated"

ENGINE TRACKING:
  Each graffiti hazard entity with graffiti_type="burner" stores a set:
    hazard_captivated_ids: set[uuid] — entity UUIDs that have already triggered
    the captivated effect on this tile. Prevents re-triggering.
  Immune window: when captivated expires, add the entity's UUID to a
    burner_immune set on the engine (or on the tile entity) with a 10-turn timer.

ENTRY DETECTION:
  In engine step 4 (hazard processing), burner tiles need entry detection, not
  just "standing on" detection. Add a new substep:
    engine.entity_prev_positions: dict[uuid, (x, y)] — track where each entity
    was last turn. At the start of each tick, compare current to previous.
    If entity moved onto a burner tile (current pos = burner tile, prev pos != burner tile):
      check hazard_captivated_ids; if entity.id not in it, apply captivated.
      add entity.id to hazard_captivated_ids.

ALTERNATIVE (simpler): A "captivation_immune" effect (10-turn debuff immunity)
  applied alongside captivated expiry. apply_effect with on_reapply that does
  nothing. Easier than per-tile tracking.

IMPLEMENTATION FILES:
  - abilities.py: add AbilityDef for "burner"; add execute_at that paints 3-tile line
  - effects.py: add CaptivatedEffect, BurnerImmuneEffect
  - hazards.py: add create_graffiti supports graffiti_type parameter
  - engine.py: add entry detection in step 4 for burner tiles;
               add entity_prev_positions dict (keyed by entity id)

BALANCE NOTES:
  "Captivated" is mechanically a stun but flavorfully distinct — it's triggered
  by MOVEMENT through the tile, not by being hit. This creates a dungeon design
  challenge: place burner tiles in enemy patrol paths, doorways, or chokepoints.
  The 2/floor limit is key — this is a strategic resource, not spam.
  3-tile line placement covers corridors. The damage amp continues to apply
  even after the captivation immune window — the tile keeps working.
  Entry-detection makes it strictly better to place in paths enemies travel,
  not just where they stand — fundamentally different from fire tiles.

SYNERGIES:
  - Blackkk Magic/Numbers Game: hex trap triggers after captivated = double lock
  - Jaywalking: Burner in a doorway, Dash behind it = perfect funnel
  - Beating/Bash: stun + captivated = extended lockdown window

================================================================================
  LEVEL 4 — "Chrome"
  perk_type: "passive"
================================================================================

NAME: Chrome

FLAVOR:
  Silver paint. The hardest to get, the most prestigious. When you make your
  first kill on your turf, the whole piece transforms. Pure chrome.

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Once per floor, the FIRST time you kill an enemy while standing on one of
  your own graffiti tiles, ALL graffiti tiles you currently own on this floor
  are upgraded to "chrome" state simultaneously.

  Chrome tiles:
    - Damage amp increases from +20% to +40% (the "tagged" effect on enemies
      now uses 1.40x multiplier instead of 1.20x)
    - Player gains "chrome_aura" buff while standing on chrome (not plain graffiti):
        +20 energy/tick (stacks WITH home_ground from L2, total +30 energy/tick)
        +1 to all attack rolls (flat power bonus, same layer as ring bonuses)
    - Visual: chrome tiles display as '%' in silver (RGB 200, 200, 200) instead
      of red — a clear signal that the whole map has shifted

  Only one chrome upgrade is possible per floor (tracked by engine flag:
  engine.graffiti_chrome_triggered: bool, reset on floor change).
  Any graffiti tiles placed AFTER the chrome upgrade are already chrome by default
  (because you're now in "chrome mode" for the rest of the floor).

  This perk has no ability — it's entirely passive. The chrome upgrade fires
  automatically on kill, with a message: "Your tags blaze into chrome."

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  Chrome upgrade trigger: in engine._handle_monster_death() (or the entity_died
  event handler), check:
    1. engine.player is alive
    2. player is standing on a graffiti tile
    3. not engine.graffiti_chrome_triggered
    4. player has Graffiti L4
  If all true: set engine.graffiti_chrome_triggered = True, iterate
  engine.graffiti_tiles and set each entity's graffiti_chrome = True,
  update color to (200, 200, 200), update hazard_type data.

  NEW EFFECT — "chrome_aura" (buff):
    id: "chrome_aura"
    category: "buff"
    duration: 3 (refreshed each tick player stands on chrome tile)
    hook: modify_energy_gain(amount, entity) -> return amount + 20
    Also: while chrome_aura is active, player.temporary_power_bonus += 1
          (set in apply(), cleared in expire())
    display_name: "Chrome"

  Tagged effect needs to check graffiti_chrome flag on the tile entity:
    In the hazard processing loop: if hazard.graffiti_chrome == True,
    apply_effect(enemy, engine, "tagged_chrome") instead of "tagged".

  NEW EFFECT — "tagged_chrome" (debuff):
    id: "tagged_chrome"
    Same as "tagged" but modify_incoming_damage returns int(damage * 1.40)
    display_name: "Chrome'd"

IMPLEMENTATION FILES:
  - engine.py: add chrome upgrade trigger in death handler; reset flag on floor change
  - effects.py: add ChromeAuraEffect, TaggedChromeEffect
  - hazards.py: graffiti entity supports graffiti_chrome: bool field
  - stats.py: add temporary_power_bonus: int (summed into player effective power)

BALANCE NOTES:
  Chrome is a high-risk/high-reward power spike. The trigger condition (kill
  on your own tile) requires that you commit: stand on your graffiti, take the
  hit if the enemy reaches you, land the kill. The reward — +40% amp and
  +30 energy/tick on chrome tiles — is the strongest position in the game for
  a graffiti player. But you have to be on the tile to get it.
  The once-per-floor limit prevents chrome from trivializing early floors.
  At L4, with STS tiles active, a player can have significant coverage and
  the chrome upgrade affects ALL of them at once — rewarding investment in
  tile placement throughout the floor (not just right before a boss).

SYNERGIES:
  - Jaywalking: +30 energy/tick chrome + Jaywalking speed effects = very fast
  - Stabbing/Windfury: +40% amp + extra hit = extreme burst on chrome tiles
  - Sniping: +1 power bonus + chrome amp + sniper mark = stacking multipliers
  - Chemical Warfare: toxicity multi + chrome amp multiply independently

  LEVEL 5 — "Bombing Run"
  perk_type: "activated"

================================================================================

NAME: Bombing Run

FLAVOR:
  One night. Every surface in the spot. Hit it all before sunrise. A bombing
  run means you OWN this room — and everybody in it knows it.

MECHANICAL DESCRIPTION:
  Grants the "bombing_run" ability. On activation (SELF targeting, instant):

  STEP 1 — ROOM SATURATION:
  All floor tiles in the current room (the room the player is standing in, as
  identified by the dungeon's room list) become graffiti tiles simultaneously.
  If chrome mode is already active (engine.graffiti_chrome_triggered), all
  spawned tiles are chrome graffiti. Otherwise, they are standard red graffiti.
  These count toward the tile cap but the cap is suspended for this cast —
  no existing tiles are evicted when the room is painted.

  STEP 2 — "SPOTTED" DEBUFF (NEW):
  Every enemy currently INSIDE the room when Bombing Run activates receives
  the "spotted" debuff for 20 turns:
    - Enemy cannot use fleeing AI behavior (AIState.FLEEING is locked out).
      If currently fleeing, forced back to CHASING.
    - Enemy cannot use wandering AI behavior (AIState.WANDERING, IDLE, ALERTING
      with retreat patterns). If in those states, forced to CHASING.
    - The "spotted" effect forces enemies to commit to the player — they CANNOT
      disengage. The tags mark them and they know they've been seen.
    - Spotted does NOT affect AI speed, attack, or defense — purely removes
      the flight/disengagement option.

  STEP 3 — GRAFFITI XP BURST:
  Immediately award 50 Graffiti potential XP for executing a bombing run.

  Charge type: PER_FLOOR, max_charges=1
  Energy cost: 100 (full turn)

ABILITY DEFINITION:
  ability_id: "bombing_run"
  name: "Bombing Run"
  description: "Paint every tile in this room. Enemies inside are Spotted
                (cannot flee) for 20 turns. 1 use/floor."
  char: "%"
  color: (220, 60, 60)
  target_type: TargetType.SELF
  charge_type: ChargeType.PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 1

EXECUTE LOGIC:
  1. Determine current room: find which Room object the player occupies by
     iterating dungeon.rooms and checking if player.(x,y) is within bounds.
     If player is in a corridor (no room match), paint only a 3x3 area around
     the player (fallback behavior).
  2. Collect all floor tiles in the room: use room.floor_tiles(dungeon).
  3. For each tile (tx, ty): spawn a graffiti hazard entity (chrome if active).
  4. For each monster in dungeon.entities that is alive, is a monster, and is
     within the room bounds: apply_effect(monster, engine, "spotted").
  5. Message: "You bomb the whole room. Everyone's spotted."
  6. Award 50 Graffiti potential XP.

NEW EFFECT — "spotted" (debuff):
  id: "spotted"
  category: "debuff"
  duration: 20
  display_name: "Spotted"
  hook: before_turn(entity, player, dungeon) -> no skip, but modify AI state:
    From within before_turn, check entity.ai_state. If it is AIState.FLEEING,
    AIState.WANDERING, or AIState.IDLE: set entity.ai_state = AIState.CHASING.
    Return False (don't skip the turn — they're being forced to fight).
  on_reapply: refresh to max(existing.duration, new.duration)

IMPLEMENTATION FILES:
  - abilities.py: add AbilityDef for "bombing_run"
  - effects.py: add SpottedEffect
  - engine.py: no structural changes needed beyond graffiti tile processing
  - dungeon.py: expose room membership check (or iterate dungeon.rooms,
    check if tile is within room bounding box + is floor tile)

BALANCE NOTES:
  Bombing Run is the capstone of territorial control. A full room of graffiti
  (potentially chrome) is a massive zone of power. The Spotted debuff is the
  novel piece: enemies inside the room when you activate this CANNOT flee.
  This creates a high-stakes moment — use it early in the room fight and you
  commit both yourself and the enemies to seeing it through. If you enter a
  room with 5 enemies and bomb it, none of them will run to alert others (no
  ALERTING state either, since that's a form of leaving to find backup).
  Combined with chrome, the room becomes a killing field with +40% damage amp.
  The 1/floor limit keeps this strategic — you're deciding WHICH room to
  completely own. Corridor players will sometimes get the 3x3 fallback, which
  is still solid but less dominant.

SYNERGIES:
  - Chrome (L4): If chrome triggered before Bombing Run, entire room = chrome
    tiles. The ideal sequence: enter room, kill one enemy on your existing tags
    to trigger chrome, then Bombing Run = chrome everything.
  - Stabbing/Lunge: Lunge crits into a room full of chrome graffiti enemies
  - Blackkk Magic/The Mark: multiple marked enemies in spotted room = enormous
    damage amp from stacking modifiers
  - Chemical Warfare/Acid Meltdown: kills in spotted room with acid pools
    spreading into all the graffiti tiles = layered zone denial

================================================================================
  DESIGN NOTES & EDGE CASES
================================================================================

PERMADEATH IMPLICATIONS:
  The "spotted" debuff forcing enemies to chase creates genuine danger —
  Bombing Run in a room with high-level enemies without enough painted ground
  can get the player killed if they can't sustain the fight. This is intentional
  risk/reward: the ability that gives you the most power also commits you hardest.

STACKING RULES:
  - "tagged" and "tagged_chrome" should NOT coexist on the same enemy. Chrome
    replaces tagged. Implementation: in the hazard processing loop, if the tile
    is chrome, apply "tagged_chrome" and remove any existing "tagged" effect.
  - "home_ground" and "chrome_aura" CAN coexist (different IDs). At L4+ on chrome
    tiles, player has both. Total energy bonus = +30/tick. This is intentional;
    it's the L4 reward for the positioning investment.
  - "captivated" and "spotted" can coexist — spotted forces them to fight,
    captivated briefly stops them. A captivated+spotted enemy skips one turn
    then comes right back at you. Good combination for timing attacks.

CORRIDOR EDGE CASE (Bombing Run):
  If player is in a corridor (no room match), fall back to painting a 3x3 area
  centered on the player. Spotted still applies to monsters in that 3x3 zone.
  Message: "You bomb the corridor."

GRAFFITI OVER EXISTING HAZARDS:
  Can graffiti tiles share a position with fire, acid, toxic creep? Yes —
  they are separate entities, both processed in step 4. An enemy on a fire+graffiti
  tile gets both ignite and tagged. A player on their own graffiti+fire gets
  home_ground and ignite. This is interesting, not broken.

ENEMY ON GRAFFITI AT FLOOR START:
  Graffiti only appears through player ability — enemies can never start on it.
  No edge case.

AI STATE OVERRIDE (Spotted):
  The before_turn hook that forces AIState.CHASING must handle the case where
  the entity has no valid path to the player (e.g., separated by walls). In
  that case, set ai_state = AIState.WANDERING (not FLEEING) — the enemy looks
  for a way around, but cannot run away.

GRAFFITI AND LOS:
  Graffiti tiles are floor-level and do not block FOV. They render below entities
  (render order: floor tile first, then graffiti, then entities on top).
  The '%' char is already used in some terminals for percent display — consider
  using a less-common ASCII char if conflicts arise. Alternatives: '#' (taken by
  walls), '~' (taken by creep/acid), '`' (backtick — currently unused), '$'
  (currently used for cash but distinguishable by context). Default '%' is fine;
  NIGRL's renderer is tcod-based so collisions with terminal output are not a concern.

RENDER PRIORITY:
  Graffiti tile (entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="graffiti") should render
  visibly on explored tiles even when no entity is standing on them — this is
  important for territorial gameplay (player needs to see their painted ground).
  Add graffiti hazards to the "always render when explored" list in render.py,
  similar to how fire/crates are rendered. Use tcod's bg color to tint the tile
  subtly: graffiti floor shows as dim red bg with '%' char. Chrome shows as
  silver. This lets the map "glow" with player presence.
