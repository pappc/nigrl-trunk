SMARTSNESS — 10 Skill Tree Variations (L1-L3)
=============================================
Design doc for the NIGRL roguelike.
All variations replace the existing placeholder L1-L3 perks (+2 BKS,
Force Be With You, Arcane Intelligence). Each is a complete, self-contained
tree proposal. The developer picks one variation to implement.

Smartsness theme: General magic / supportive skill. Covers utility, control,
  buffs, debuffs, vision, displacement, shields, and mixed BKS-scaling spells.
  Tone: dark, irreverent street smarts — the kind of power that comes from
  reading the situation, not swinging blindly.

Smartsness XP source: casting abilities from ABILITY_REGISTRY, landing
  status effects on enemies, dealing BKS-scaled spell damage, using items
  with use_effect (applies when consumed).

System references:
  - Stats: constitution (CON), strength (STR), street_smarts (STS),
           book_smarts (BKS), tolerance (TOL), swagger (SWG)
  - Player HP: 30 + CON*10. At CON 10 = 130 HP.
  - Swagger defence: int((eff_swg - 8) / 2). At SWG 8=0, 10=+1, 12=+2.
  - Crit chance: STS * 3%. MEGA_CRIT_MULTIPLIER = 4.
  - Energy: 100/tick base. Speed +30 = ~23% faster.
  - Book Smarts SP rate: min(0.5, 0.1 + 0.3*sqrt(BKS/80)).
  - Temp HP absorbed BEFORE armor, armor before HP.
  - ChargeType: INFINITE+cooldown | PER_FLOOR | TOTAL | ONCE | FLOOR_ONLY
  - TargetType: SELF | SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS | LINE_FROM_PLAYER |
                AOE_CIRCLE | ADJACENT | ADJACENT_TILE
  - AbilityDef fields: target_type, charge_type, charges, cooldown, execute
  - Effects: subclass Effect, @register, lifecycle hooks apply/tick/expire/
    before_turn/modify_movement/modify_energy_gain/modify_incoming_damage/
    on_player_melee_hit
  - Spell damage reference: Fireball does 15+BKS dmg at L6. Ice Lance does
    3*CryoLvl+BKS/2. Blackkk Magic DOT scales 1-5 per stack. Use similar
    formulas for BKS-scaling spells here.
  - Abilities from other trees for cross-reference: Freeze (4/floor),
    Dash (FLOOR_ONLY), Gouge (ADJACENT), Bash (10t cooldown),
    Bitch Slap (25t cooldown), Emission (1/floor), Zombie Stare (15t cooldown).

Cross-tree synergies to leverage:
  - Cryomancy / Electrodynamics: BKS matters for bolt scaling; Smartsness
    BKS boosts all three elemental trees
  - Blackkk Magic: debuff stacking; Smartsness debuffs can set up Curse of DOT
  - Stealing: STS boosts; Smartsness utility (displacement, vision) aids theft
  - Arachnigga: crowd control layering; web synergy
  - Nuclear Research: BKS investment shared; Rad Bomb damage lifted
  - Elementalist: Elemental Staves require 12 BKS; Smartsness can push into that


=======================================================================
VARIATION 1 — "Big Brain"
Fantasy: Raw intellectual horsepower. Stack BKS as high as possible,
let the stat itself do the work — more skill points, better spells,
sharper perception. A support chassis for every caster build in the game.
=======================================================================

L1 — OVERNIGHT STUDY (stat + passive)
  Perk type: stat + passive
  Stat bonus: +3 BKS
  Passive: Each floor you descend to grants +1 BKS (temporary, lasts until
           you descend again). This stacks on top of permanent BKS.
  Desc: "You read the whole floor like a textbook. By the time you go deeper
        you're dumber again, but it was nice while it lasted."
  Balance: +3 permanent BKS at L1 is generous but appropriate — Elementalist
           requires 12 BKS and benefits enormously. Floor bonus tops at
           +4 combined on deep floors; scales into Cryomancy/Nuclear Research.
  Implementation: engine.floor_bks_bonus (int, resets on each descend,
           applied as temp_stat_bonus["book_smarts"]).

L2 — GALAXY BRAIN (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Your critical hit chance gains a bonus equal to BKS/5 (rounded
          down). At BKS 10 = +2% crit. At BKS 15 = +3% crit. This bonus is
          additive with the base STS*3% formula. In addition, on any critical
          hit, gain 10 Smartsness XP.
  Desc: "You don't land crits by luck. You land them by having already
        figured out where they were gonna be."
  Balance: BKS 15 = +3% crit, modest. Primary value is the XP loop — crits
           feed skill progression, encouraging BKS investment.

L3 — ARCANE INTELLIGENCE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: All abilities and spells cost 30% less energy to use (their
          execute action deducts 30% less from the energy pool). Additionally,
          BKS now grants +1 max HP per 2 points above 10 (so BKS 12 = +1 HP,
          BKS 14 = +2 HP, BKS 16 = +3 HP). This HP bonus is permanent and
          adds to the CON*10 formula.
  Desc: "You've optimized the flow states. Every spell costs half the effort;
        every point of knowledge is worth something in blood too."
  Balance: 30% energy reduction is the old Arcane Intelligence, now locked
           behind L3. BKS-to-HP is weak (BKS 16 = only +3 HP) but meaningful
           for heavy BKS investment builds. Cryomancy + Electrodynamics
           benefit most from the energy cost cut.
  Implementation: ability energy cost multiplied by 0.70 in spells.py
           execute dispatch. HP bonus computed in stats.py max_hp property.

Design notes: Pure stat-and-scaling tree. Rewards players who want BKS as a
  universal multiplier. Three passives with no activated abilities — the
  simplest possible Smartsness for players who don't want a spell-heavy kit.
  Strong synergy with every BKS-scaling spell in the game. Best paired with
  Cryomancy, Electrodynamics, Nuclear Research, or Blackkk Magic.


=======================================================================
VARIATION 2 — "Mind Jacker"
Fantasy: Get inside their heads. Confuse, redirect, and repurpose enemies
as temporary meat shields. Control is the damage. High skill ceiling — the
player who reads the room wins; the one who doesn't wastes every charge.
=======================================================================

L1 — SCRAMBLE (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: scramble
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (4/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Target a visible enemy. For 6 turns that enemy's movement is
          randomized — each turn it moves to a random adjacent open tile
          instead of chasing. It still attacks normally if the player
          walks adjacent to it. Doesn't affect BOSS-tagged enemies.
  Desc: "Short-circuit the lizard brain. They forget what they were doing
        for a little while."
  Balance: 6 turns of disorientation, 4/floor. Comparable to Freeze (5 frozen
           stacks, 4/floor) but non-damaging. Scrambled enemies still dangerous
           if approached; positioning required.
  Implementation: ScrambledEffect (new), before_turn hook overrides movement
           target selection to random open tile.

L2 — BORROWED MUSCLE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Enemies you attack while they are Scrambled grant you a temporary
          melee damage bonus: +BKS/4 melee damage (rounded down, min +1) for
          5 turns after the hit. Multiple hits on Scrambled enemies do not
          stack the bonus but refresh the duration.
  Desc: "Fighting a confused man is easy. You're not even really fighting —
        you're just ending a demonstration."
  Balance: BKS 10 = +2 dmg, BKS 14 = +3 dmg, BKS 16 = +4 dmg. Short window
           (5 turns), requires setup (Scramble first). Encourages using
           Scramble offensively rather than purely defensively.
  Implementation: engine.scramble_melee_bonus (int, timer tracks turns
           remaining via TemporaryMeleeBonus effect or similar flag).

L3 — PUPPETMASTER (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: puppetmaster
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (2/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Take full control of a visible enemy for 3 turns. During control,
          that enemy acts on the player's turn (same energy budget as its
          normal action) and attacks adjacent enemies. At the end of 3 turns
          or if the controlled enemy is killed, control breaks and the enemy
          resumes normal AI (if alive). Cannot control BOSS-tagged enemies.
          Using Scramble on the target first extends control to 5 turns.
  Desc: "Not just confused — working for you. Briefly."
  Balance: 2/floor, 3-turn window. Strong in crowded rooms. Boss immunity
           prevents trivialization. Scramble synergy rewards Variation 2's
           own kit. Compare to Summon Spider (5/floor ally summon) — this is
           shorter but uses existing enemy stats.
  Implementation: PuppetmasterEffect (new), overrides ai_type to "meander"
           targeting nearest non-player entity; restores original ai_type on
           expire/death. engine.puppet_entity_id (str|None).

Design notes: Full control / confusion tree. Interesting risk — controlled
  enemies can be killed by other enemies before doing useful work. Scramble at
  L1 is immediately useful for repositioning threats. L3 is a skill-expressive
  capstone. Pairs well with Stealing (confusion enables Pickpocket setup) and
  Arachnigga (web traps + confused enemies can't escape).

VARIATION 3 — "Warchief"
Fantasy: Buff yourself before the fight, walk in already dangerous.
Temporary stat explosions, brief invincibility windows, and a capstone that
turns every kill into more power. The "go mode" tree.
=======================================================================

L1 — GET RIGHT (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: get_right
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (3/floor)
  Target type: SELF
  Effect: Gain +3 STR and +3 STS for 15 turns. These are temporary stat
          bonuses (added to temporary_stat_bonuses dict). They apply to melee
          damage, crit chance, and skill checks. Cooldown resets to available
          on floor entry (counted against 3/floor cap).
  Desc: "Thirty seconds of clarity. Your hands stop shaking. Everything in
        the room gets smaller."
  Balance: +3 STR +3 STS for 15 turns, 3/floor. Strong opening move. 15 turns
           is short — one or two fights. Comparable to Abandoning L4 which
           doubles ALL stats for 10 turns (1/floor); this is weaker but more
           frequent.

L2 — HARDENED (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +2 CON, +2 SWG permanently. Additionally, the first time you take
          damage on each floor, gain 10 Temp HP. This Temp HP triggers once
          per floor only (first hit per floor).
  Desc: "You've been hit enough times that your body started doing something
        about it. The first one never hurts anymore."
  Balance: +2 CON (+20 HP), +2 SWG (+1 defense). First-hit Temp HP = 10.
           Modest stat package, useful defensive cushion. Rewards aggressive
           play (get hit on purpose to proc Temp HP early).
  Implementation: engine.hardened_proc_used (bool, reset per floor).

L3 — FEED THE BEAST (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Each melee kill grants Frenzy (stacking, up to 5 stacks). Each
          Frenzy stack gives +2 melee damage and +3 speed. Stacks expire 2
          turns after the last kill. If you reach 5 stacks, gain an
          additional free attack against a random adjacent enemy (once per
          5-stack activation).
  Desc: "The more they fall the more dangerous you get. Room three is nothing
        after room one started it."
  Balance: 5 stacks = +10 melee damage, +15 speed. Significant but requires
           sustained combat. Compare to Swashbuckling (+1 slash dmg / +1%
           dodge per stack, 20t, slashing only) — Feed the Beast is stronger
           raw damage and melee-type agnostic but decays faster between fights.
  Implementation: FrenzyEffect (new), stacking count tracked on effect,
           free-attack trigger at exactly 5 stacks (once per 5-stack peak).
           engine.feed_beast_free_atk_used (bool per 5-stack peak).

Design notes: Buff-before-fight identity. Get Right front-loads the combat
  window; Hardened provides sustain; Feed the Beast rewards staying aggressive.
  No control or utility — purely offensive empowerment. Pairs well with
  Stabbing (Windfury + Frenzy stacks multiply hits), Slashing (Execute + Frenzy
  at 5 stacks deletes low-HP enemies), and Pyromania (Ignite sets up the kill
  chain to maintain stacks).

VARIATION 4 — "Blind Eye"
Fantasy: Vision is power. See everything, share nothing. Expand your
awareness to scout ahead, spot ambushes, and read hidden enemies — then
punish enemies who can't see YOU. Dark utility tree with a creepy tone.
=======================================================================

L1 — THIRD EYE (passive + stat)
  Perk type: stat + passive
  Stat bonus: +2 BKS
  Passive: Your FOV radius is permanently increased by 2 tiles (applies to
           dungeon.visible computation). Additionally, you can see 1 tile
           through walls (tile contents, not full FOV) when adjacent to a wall.
  Desc: "+2 BKS. You see further and you see more. The walls got thinner."
  Balance: +2 tiles FOV is meaningful — fewer ambushes, more time to react.
           Wall-piercing (1 tile) is a soft "can hear through walls" scout tool.
           No direct combat value, pure information advantage.
  Implementation: fov_radius + 2 in _compute_fov(). Wall-pierce: check one tile
           past adjacent wall during render, mark as visible but dim.
           PlayerStats.sight_radius_bonus field (already in proposed fields).

L2 — BLIND SPOT (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: blind_spot
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (3/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Afflict a visible enemy with Blind (8 turns). Blinded enemies
          reduce their sight radius to 1 tile (they can only react to
          immediately adjacent targets). Blinded enemies cannot use ranged
          attacks or special attacks with a range component. They wander
          randomly until sight recovers.
  Desc: "Cut their feed. Watch them spin."
  Balance: 8-turn sight disable, 3/floor. Renders ranged enemies (gun enemies,
           throwing enemies) completely neutered for the window. Even melee
           enemies are safer at 2+ tiles. Compare to Scramble (V2 L1) which
           randomizes movement — Blind is cleaner but less total chaos.
  Implementation: BlindedEffect (new), modify sight_radius to 1 during duration,
           ranged special attacks skip if target is player and entity is Blinded.

L3 — UNSEEING EYE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Enemies that cannot currently see you (outside their reduced sight
          radius due to Blind, or naturally beyond their sight range) take 40%
          more damage from all your attacks (melee, ranged, and abilities).
          This bonus does NOT apply if the enemy is simply facing away — they
          must be genuinely unaware (IDLE or WANDERING AI state, not CHASING).
  Desc: "If they don't know you're there, every hit counts double. That's
        not cruelty. That's efficiency."
  Balance: 40% bonus against unaware enemies is very strong. Gated behind
           "genuinely unaware" AI state — CHASING enemies don't count even if
           technically out of sight range. Synergizes with Blind Spot (force
           enemies to lose sight of you), Stealing (Silent approach), and
           Sneaking-adjacent playstyles.
  Implementation: in combat.py damage resolution, check if target.ai_state is
           AIState.IDLE or AIState.WANDERING and target is not in player FOV
           (dungeon.visible check). If so, apply 1.40 multiplier.

Design notes: Information-economy tree. Third Eye gives permanent scouting
  advantage; Blind Spot creates ambush windows; Unseeing Eye capitalizes on
  them. High synergy with Stealing (Pickpocket enemies who can't see you) and
  Arachnigga (web a blinded enemy, they can't escape). Countered by open rooms
  with few walls and enemies already alerted.

VARIATION 5 — "The Grift"
Fantasy: Every interaction is transactional. Spend resources to get power
spikes, convert junk into utility, turn items into weapons. The scavenger-
mage — nothing is wasted, everything is leveraged.
=======================================================================

L1 — QUICK APPRAISAL (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +2 BKS, +2 STS. When you pick up any item, you immediately learn
          its full description (no blind-identification step needed). Cash
          piles you walk over yield +10% bonus value (the entity cash value
          is multiplied by 1.1 before pickup, rounded up).
  Desc: "+2 BKS, +2 STS. One look is enough. You know what it's worth and
        what it does before you've stopped moving."
  Balance: Auto-ID is purely QoL but potent in a game with cursed/unknown
           items. +10% cash is minor per pickup, meaningful over a full run.
  Implementation: on item pickup in inventory_mgr.py, skip any identification
           flags. cash_value * 1.1 rounding up on pickup.

L2 — LIQUIDATE (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: liquidate
  Charge type: INFINITE (15t cooldown)
  Target type: SELF
  Effect: Instantly destroy the lowest-value item in your inventory (auto-
          selected, no confirmation) and gain either: (a) cash equal to 120%
          of that item's value, OR (b) a random consumable worth up to half
          the item's value (player chooses A or B on activation). Cannot
          destroy equipped items. If inventory is empty, the ability fails
          and the charge is not consumed.
  Desc: "Everything converts. You just have to know the rate."
  Balance: 15t cooldown, auto-selects lowest-value item (never surprises you
           with something important). Cash option: always profitable if you
           have junk. Consumable option: gambling for something useful.
           Pairs with Dismantling tree (destroy for different currencies).
  Implementation: on Liquidate execute, sort player inventory by item_value
           ASC, destroy first non-equipped item, apply chosen bonus.
           Sub-menu (choose A or B) uses same pattern as ring-slot replacement.

L3 — SUNK COST (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Each time you use Liquidate, permanently gain +1 to a random stat
          (chosen from the six stats, equal probability). This stacks
          without cap — every Liquidate is a stat roll. Additionally, items
          destroyed via Liquidate have a 20% chance to grant their full
          use_effect before being destroyed (as if you had used them normally).
  Desc: "You burned the last of your supplies. Somehow you came out ahead."
  Balance: +1 random stat per Liquidate is potent over a long run (15t
           cooldown = many uses per floor). But it's truly random — equal
           chance to boost CON or STS, no steering. The 20% use_effect
           proc is the big surprise: destroying a first aid kit might also
           heal you. Risk/reward: you lose the item either way.
  Implementation: on Liquidate destroy, roll random stat, increment base stat.
           Roll 0-4 (0=CON,1=STR,2=BKS,3=STS,4=TOL) or 0-5 including SWG.

Design notes: Economy-manipulation identity. Quick Appraisal turns pickups
  into informed decisions; Liquidate creates a flexible resource loop;
  Sunk Cost makes the loop into a stat-generation engine. High interaction
  with item-heavy builds (Abandoning, Dismantling). The random stat roll at L3
  keeps every run different — sometimes you get five CON bumps, sometimes all
  BKS. Low floor for casual play, high ceiling for optimizers.

VARIATION 6 — "Reactive Shield"
Fantasy: Punishment for hitting you. Layered damage mitigation that
actively fights back — the more they swing, the deadlier you become. Purely
defensive but with aggressive implications. The "don't touch me" tree.
=======================================================================

L1 — SMART ARMOR (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +2 SWG, +2 CON. At the start of each floor, gain 5 armor points.
          This is a flat armor bonus that reduces incoming melee and ranged
          damage before HP (processed in the damage absorption order after
          Temp HP). It does NOT persist between floors — refreshes on descend.
  Desc: "+2 SWG, +2 CON. You walk into every room wearing five invisible
        points of 'not today.'"
  Balance: 5 armor per floor is modest. +2 SWG (+1 defense) + 5 armor totals
           about 6 damage reduction against the first several hits. Worse
           against many small hits; better against a few large ones.
  Implementation: engine.smart_armor_bonus (int, reset per floor, added to
           effective armor in combat.py damage formula).

L2 — REBOUND (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: When you take melee damage that exceeds 10 points in a single hit,
          the attacker takes reflected damage equal to BKS/3 (rounded down,
          min 1). This is not modified by the attacker's defense. Triggers
          once per hit, not per turn. Does not trigger from non-melee sources
          (tox ticks, fire, status effects).
  Desc: "At a certain point the impact just redirects. Not your problem
        anymore."
  Balance: BKS 9 = 3 reflected. BKS 12 = 4. BKS 15 = 5. Low damage on its
           own but forces enemies to pay for every swing. The 10-point
           threshold gates it off weak attacks (rats, low-floor enemies).
  Implementation: in combat.py, after player damage calc, if raw damage > 10
           and source is melee entity, apply rebound damage to attacker
           bypassing defense.

L3 — FEEDBACK LOOP (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Each time Rebound triggers, gain 1 Feedback stack (max 8, persists
          until end of floor). Each Feedback stack gives +1 melee damage and
          +1 spell damage. At 8 stacks, your next melee attack or ability is
          guaranteed to deal maximum possible damage (no roll — use the max
          of the damage range). The 8-stack burst consumes all stacks (resets
          to 0) after firing.
  Desc: "Pain is data. You've been collecting a lot of data."
  Balance: 8 stacks = +8 melee/spell damage before the burst. Burst is
           deterministic max roll, which on high-damage spells (Fireball,
           Ice Lance) can be significant. But stacking requires taking 8+ hits
           above the 10-point threshold — a dangerous path.
  Implementation: FeedbackEffect (new), stacking count in effect params.
           Max-roll logic: in _compute_player_attack_power or spell execute,
           check if engine.feedback_stacks == 8, if so use max dmg value
           and reset.

Design notes: Pure masochistic defense-into-offense. Smart Armor reduces
  early-game spike deaths; Rebound makes trading hits more equitable; Feedback
  Loop rewards the patient player who takes a beating before capitalizing.
  Interesting tension: you WANT to be hit (to build stacks) but each hit is
  also dangerous. Pairs with White Power (SWG stacking for more defense),
  L Farming (Unfazed SWG stacking + Armor Up block), and CON-heavy builds.

VARIATION 7 — "Hex Caster"
Fantasy: Debuff specialist. Stack debilitating curses that shred enemy
stats, slow their actions, and leave them stumbling. Not direct damage —
attrition through compounding penalties. The slow-burn crowd controller.
=======================================================================

L1 — DULLING WORD (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: dulling_word
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (4/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Afflict a visible enemy with Dulled (12 turns). A Dulled enemy
          deals 20% less damage on all attacks. The debuff is visible (a
          '!' overlay on the target, red color). Multiple applications do
          not stack — reapplication refreshes duration.
  Desc: "One word. Their hands get heavy, their aim goes south. They still
        swing. They just stop connecting properly."
  Balance: 20% damage reduction, 12 turns, 4/floor. Comparable to Blackkk
           Magic Curse of Ham (50% less damage, attack speed penalty, 3/floor)
           but narrower effect, more charges, no cone — a softer debuff with
           better uptime.
  Implementation: DulledEffect (new), modify_incoming_damage hook on the
           ENEMY entity (not player) reduces outgoing damage by 20%.

L2 — LEAD FEET (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: lead_feet
  Charge type: INFINITE (20t cooldown)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Afflict a visible enemy with Lead Feet (10 turns). Lead Feet
          reduces the target's speed by 30 (reducing its energy gain per
          tick, making it act roughly 23% less often). Stacks with Dulled
          — an enemy with both debuffs is significantly slowed and weakened.
  Desc: "They're still coming. Just slower. A lot slower."
  Balance: -30 speed, 10 turns, 20t cooldown. At base enemy speed 100 this
           is a 23% action rate reduction. Less dramatic than a stun but
           persistent. Compare to Pyromania Ignite (damage DOT) — Lead Feet
           is pure control, no damage.
  Implementation: LeadFeetEffect (new), modify_energy_gain hook multiplies
           speed contribution by 0.70 (or subtracts 30 from speed).

L3 — HEX AMPLIFIER (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Enemies bearing any debuff you applied (Dulled, Lead Feet, Cursed,
          Blinded, Scrambled, or any negative status from an ability) take
          BKS/4 (rounded down, min 2) bonus damage from all your attacks
          (melee, ranged, and abilities). This is a flat additive bonus
          applied after base damage calculation. The Dulling Word damage
          reduction on the enemy and your attack bonus are both present
          simultaneously.
  Desc: "The hex doesn't just slow them down. It opens them up."
  Balance: BKS 10 = +2 bonus, BKS 12 = +3, BKS 16 = +4. Meaningful but
           not overwhelming. Scales with BKS investment. Requires setup
           (must land a debuff first) — rewards commitment to the Hex Caster
           identity. Works with debuffs from OTHER trees too (Blackkk Magic
           curses, Arachnigga venom, etc.).
  Implementation: in combat.py and ability execute, check target.effects for
           any debuff-category effect applied by player. If present, add
           BKS//4 to raw damage.

Design notes: Debuff-layer identity. Dulling Word is the opener; Lead Feet
  is the follow-up for dangerous targets; Hex Amplifier makes the setup
  directly profitable in damage terms. High cross-tree synergy — any debuff
  from any tree (Blackkk Magic curses, Cryomancy Chill, Electrodynamics
  Shock, Arachnigga Venom) triggers Hex Amplifier. Best in multi-skill builds
  that apply varied debuff types.


=======================================================================
VARIATION 8 — "Displacement Theory"
Fantasy: Move things around — teleport yourself, shove enemies, redirect
attacks, blink out of danger. Pure positional mastery. The parkour mage.
=======================================================================

L1 — FORCE PUSH (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: force_push
  Charge type: INFINITE (15t cooldown)
  Target type: ADJACENT
  Effect: Shove an adjacent enemy directly away from the player by 4 tiles.
          Each tile of movement checks for wall collision — the enemy stops
          on the last valid tile. Each collision tile (wall stop) deals 6
          damage to the enemy. If the enemy passes through or stops on a
          tile occupied by another enemy, both take 4 collision damage.
  Desc: "You don't have to hurt them right now. You just need them to be
        somewhere else."
  Balance: 15t cooldown. Up to 6 damage on wall-stop. Passable corridors =
           full 4-tile displacement, no damage. Against walls = guaranteed 6
           damage. Compare to existing Force Be With You (old L2) — this is
           now the L1 version with a cooldown instead of charges.
  Implementation: ability execute pushes entity via trajectory path check,
           applies collision damage in combat.py.

L2 — BLINK (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: blink
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (3/floor)
  Target type: ADJACENT_TILE (but executed as 5-tile radius blink)
  Effect: Instantly teleport to any open tile within 5 tiles (cursor-
          targeted). Does not provoke opportunity attacks or trigger on-
          enter tile effects in the teleported path. Counts as a full action
          (costs normal action energy). On arrival, gain 5 Temp HP (the
          momentum of the blink).
  Desc: "Three floors of fighting have taught you that being somewhere else
        is the best defense. 5 Temp HP on landing — the energy of arrival."
  Balance: 3/floor blink is powerful escape. 5 Temp HP is minor but rewards
           proactive use over panic use. Compare to Dash (Jaywalking L2,
           FLOOR_ONLY, 3-tile line) — Blink is 5-tile omnidirectional,
           3/floor, better overall but more limited charges.
  Implementation: Blink execute: render targeting overlay within radius 5,
           player moves to selected tile bypassing collision normally.
           engine.blink_temp_hp (5) applied after teleport.

L3 — SPATIAL TRANSFER (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: spatial_transfer
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (1/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Swap positions with a visible enemy up to 8 tiles away.
          The enemy does not get a free attack during the swap (it is
          stunned for 1 turn as the transfer completes). Both entities
          arrive at the new tile as if they walked there. Useful for:
          pulling a distant enemy next to another enemy, throwing yourself
          behind an enemy, using an enemy as a ranged bullet, or getting
          out of a mob. Does not work on BOSS-tagged enemies.
  Desc: "You stop thinking about where you are and start thinking about
        where you should be. Turns out those are often different places."
  Balance: 1/floor, extreme versatility. The 1-turn stun prevents the swap
           from being instantly lethal. Boss immunity. Pulling a dangerous
           enemy INTO a mob of weaker enemies can be a room-wipe. High
           skill ceiling — the player must think about the entire room map.
  Implementation: on execute, swap player and target coords, apply 1-turn
           stun to target (existing stun effect), mark as 1/floor.
           engine.spatial_transfer_used (bool, reset per floor).

Design notes: Pure mobility and displacement. Every perk moves something.
  Force Push is the low-cooldown workhorse; Blink is the escape valve; Spatial
  Transfer is the finisher/room-reshaper. No damage perks at all — relies on
  terrain and enemy interaction for kills. Pairs with Stabbing (Lunge + Blink
  sets up ideal Lunge angles), Slashing (Whirlwind after Force Push clusters),
  and Arachnigga (web an area, Force Push enemies into it).


=======================================================================
VARIATION 9 — "Curse Eater"
Fantasy: Absorb and weaponize the bad stuff that happens to you. Debuffs
heal you, status effects become fuel, and the harder the room hurts you
the harder you hit back. Anti-fragile magic. Masochist's dream.
=======================================================================

L1 — METABOLIZE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Whenever a negative status effect expires on you (stun ends,
          fear ends, Dulled wears off, etc.) you heal for BKS/2 HP
          (rounded down, min 2). This applies to all negative effects
          including those applied by enemies. Duration-shortened effects
          (resisted early) still grant the heal on expiry.
  Desc: "You've gotten good at waiting it out. By the end you always feel
        a little better than before."
  Balance: BKS 10 = 5 HP heal per expiry. Modest per-instance, but
           against enemies that stack many short debuffs (stun/fear combos)
           this becomes meaningful free HP. Stronger in later floors where
           enemies apply more status effects.
  Implementation: in Effect.expire(), check if engine.player_has_metabolize
           and effect.category == "debuff" and entity is player, then heal.

L2 — SPITE REFLEX (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: The first time you are stunned on each floor, you immediately
          consume the stun (it ends instantly on the same turn it was
          applied) and gain Spite (+3 STR, +3 SWG, 15 turns). Cannot
          trigger more than once per floor. Stuns from the same source
          that proc'd Spite on a previous floor reset on floor entry.
  Desc: "They got you. You just absorbed it and came back meaner. That
        tends to discourage follow-up questions."
  Balance: Stun immunity-on-first-trigger is very strong but once-per-floor.
           +3 STR +3 SWG for 15 turns is a solid combat window. Punishes
           stun-heavy enemy compositions but becomes less relevant if the
           player is rarely stunned.
  Implementation: engine.spite_reflex_used_floor (bool, reset per floor).
           In stun application check, if player, flag not used, cancel stun
           duration, apply Spite buff.

L3 — CURSE CONDUIT (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: While you have any negative status effect active (at least one
          debuff on yourself), all your spell and melee attacks deal bonus
          damage equal to the number of negative effects currently on you
          times BKS/3 (rounded down, min 1 per active debuff). Example:
          3 active debuffs at BKS 12 = 3 * 4 = +12 bonus damage. This bonus
          applies to every hit while the condition holds. Expires immediately
          when all debuffs clear.
  Desc: "The curses aren't problems. They're ammunition. You're firing them
        at everything else."
  Balance: Requires being debuffed — inverted risk/reward. 3 debuffs at BKS
           12 = +12 damage per hit, which is massive. But sustaining 3 active
           debuffs means you're hurting. Synergizes with Metabolize (they heal
           on expiry) and Spite Reflex (stun becomes power). Countered by
           items/builds that cleanse debuffs fast.
  Implementation: in _compute_player_attack_power (combat.py) and ability
           execute, count player debuff effects. Multiply count * (BKS//3),
           add as flat bonus.

Design notes: Anti-fragile theme — every bad thing becomes a resource.
  Metabolize makes short debuffs net-positive HP events; Spite Reflex converts
  stuns into burst windows; Curse Conduit scales damage with suffering. Counter-
  intuitive but high-skill: the player might deliberately avoid cleansing
  debuffs to maintain Curse Conduit. Pairs with Chemical Warfare (voluntary
  toxicity = self-debuff), White Power (tox stacking), Mutation (rad ticks =
  periodic debuffs), and any zone with heavy status-effect enemies.


=======================================================================
VARIATION 10 — "Street Smarts Major"
Fantasy: Intelligence that hits the street, not the library. STS-boosted
utility — better reactions, better evasion, sharper situational awareness.
Not a caster tree at all; a BKS-to-STS conversion that makes Smartsness
viable for melee builds who need a support node.
=======================================================================

L1 — READ THE ROOM (stat + passive)
  Perk type: stat + passive
  Stat bonus: +2 STS, +2 BKS
  Passive: You cannot be surprised by enemies entering your FOV. Enemies
           that step into your line of sight for the first time this floor
           are revealed 1 turn before they would normally act (their AI
           tick is delayed by 1 turn on first sight). This gives you one
           action before they can respond.
  Desc: "+2 STS, +2 BKS. You spotted them before they spotted you. Not
        because you're smarter — because you were already watching."
  Balance: +2 STS (+6% crit) + +2 BKS. The first-sight delay is a soft
           ambush-protection passive. In narrow corridors and doorways it's
           extremely strong; in open rooms with many enemies it matters less.
  Implementation: engine.read_the_room_seen_this_floor (set of entity IDs).
           On first sight, skip that entity's AI tick once
           (entity.ai_skip_turn = True flag, cleared after use).

L2 — FAST TALKER (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +3 STS. Your ability activation costs 15% less energy (applies
          to all abilities from any skill tree). This stacks additively with
          other energy cost reductions. In addition, after any successful
          Pickpocket or Shakedown (Stealing tree), gain +5 Smartsness XP.
  Desc: "+3 STS. You move the conversation along. Nobody notices the hand."
  Balance: +3 STS (+9% crit), 15% ability energy reduction. Weaker than
           Arcane Intelligence's 30% (V1 L3) but comes earlier. The Stealing
           XP link rewards multi-tree investment.
  Implementation: ability energy cost * 0.85 in spells.py (stacks with
           V1 L3's 0.70 if both are somehow present — otherwise these are
           alternative trees). XP hook in inventory_mgr.py Pickpocket path.

L3 — SITUATIONAL AWARENESS (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: At the start of each floor, you receive a brief flash of
          "awareness" — the dungeon map shows the location of all enemies
          currently on the floor for 1 turn (as if you had full sight).
          After this turn, normal FOV rules resume. Additionally, you
          gain a permanent dodge chance bonus equal to STS/5% (e.g.,
          STS 15 = +3% dodge). This bonus stacks with other dodge sources.
  Desc: "The moment you step onto the floor your brain has already mapped
        the room. One second of perfect clarity. Then it's back to work."
  Balance: Floor-entry full-sight is 1 turn of intel: not combat advantage,
           pure scouting. STS/5 dodge: at STS 15 = +3%, at STS 18 = +3.6%.
           Modest but permanent. Together these make the tree a scouting +
           evasion hybrid without any activated abilities.
  Implementation: on floor entry, if player has perk, temporarily set all
           enemy entities as visible for 1 render tick, then restore normal
           FOV. engine.situational_awareness_flash (bool, True for 1 tick).
           Dodge bonus: PlayerStats.dodge_chance += effective_sts // 5.

Design notes: Melee-hybrid Smartsness. This tree gives zero spells or
  activated abilities — all passives, all stat-and-perception focused.
  "Smartsness" as tactical intelligence rather than arcane power. Intended for
  Stabbing/Stealing/Slashing builds that want something from Smartsness without
  pivoting to a caster. Read the Room gives early initiative advantage; Fast
  Talker cuts ability overhead; Situational Awareness provides complete scouting.
  Pairs with Stabbing (Lunge benefits from knowing enemy positions), Stealing
  (XP cross-link at L2), and any STS-scaling build.
