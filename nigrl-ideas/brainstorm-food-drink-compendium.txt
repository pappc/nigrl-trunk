A1 — STRAY DOG CHILI
--------------------
Type: Food (multi-turn eat)
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '%', color (150, 80, 40) — muddy brown

Effect:
  Eat time: 3 turns (standard meal).
  On completion: Summons a "Stray Dog" companion entity on an adjacent tile.
  The dog uses meander AI targeting enemies. It has 15 HP, 3 defense, deals
  4-6 damage per hit. It persists until killed or until the player descends
  to the next floor. Only one dog can be active at a time — eating a second
  bowl replaces the first (old dog disappears, no refund).
  Passive while dog is alive: player gains +1 Swagger (street presence of
  having a dog).

Requires:
  - Companion entity system: entity with is_companion=True, allied AI that
    attacks enemies but doesn't block player movement. Must be excluded from
    enemy spawn counts and hostile AI targeting.
  - Floor transition handling for companions (despawn or carry-over decision).

Design Notes:
  First food that produces a lasting battlefield presence. Huge thematic fit —
  eating shady street chili to get a loyal mutt. Weak offensively but absorbs
  hits and provides mild swagger. Synergizes with "dog" item potential later.
  Risk: dog can be killed fast on deep floors. Reward: distraction value.

A2 — HENNESSY PACT
------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Crack Den (floors 3-4)
Appearance: char '!', color (90, 50, 20) — dark brown

Effect:
  On drink: The next enemy you kill within 40 turns is "claimed."
  A ghost/shade version of that enemy (half HP, same damage/AI, but allied)
  rises from the corpse immediately and fights for you for 80 turns.
  The shade's char is the enemy's char but rendered in dark gray (50,50,50).
  Multiple pacts can stack — each kill within the claim window produces one shade.
  Window resets on shade-spawn; you must drink again to claim another.

Requires:
  - Shade entity creation: post-death entity spawn from killer's kill event.
    is_shade=True flag, allied AI, despawn on timeout.
  - Kill event hook that checks active PactEffect and triggers shade spawn.
  - Shade AI: copy of enemy's ai_type but target_faction="enemy" instead of
    target_faction="player".

Design Notes:
  Different from Negromancy Raise Dead — Hennessy Pact is passive/reactive,
  not activated on demand. Rewards aggressive play. Clip window of 40 turns
  means you need to drink right before combat, not stockpile. Strong synergy
  with Negromancy tree's soul shard generation. Dark flavor: you're making a
  deal over a bottle of Henny.

A3 — PIGEON JERKY
-----------------
Type: Food
Zone: Crack Den, future Casino zone
Appearance: char '%', color (180, 170, 140) — grayish tan

Effect:
  Eat time: 2 turns (fast, it's just jerky).
  On completion: Summons 3 Pigeons. Pigeons are tiny (3 HP each, 0 attack)
  but use "swarm patrol" AI — they orbit the player at radius 2, and any
  enemy moving into radius 2 is "startled" (applies a 3-turn Scared debuff,
  -30 energy/tick, cannot move toward player). Pigeons despawn after 60 turns
  or when killed. Killing all 3 ends the effect early.
  Eating again while pigeons active: adds 3 more pigeons (no cap, but
  they share the same expiry timer refresh).

Requires:
  - Swarm AI mode: entities that orbit around a target point (the player's
    position) and trigger on-enter-radius events for enemies.
  - Startled debuff (new effect): similar to intimidated but from animals.

Design Notes:
  Soft crowd control via positioning pressure. Pigeons die easily but buying
  one extra turn per encounter is strong in a roguelike. Thematically perfect —
  everyone knows the pigeons in the hood are everywhere. Synergy with Swagger:
  high-SWG player attracts more pigeons naturally (passive variant idea).

B4 — SPRAY PAINT SODA
---------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (200, 100, 255) — bright purple

Effect:
  Duration: instant (single use).
  On drink: Player enters a "spray" targeting mode (like gun targeting cursor).
  Player selects up to 3 tiles within range 5 that are currently floor tiles.
  Each selected tile becomes a "tagged" tile — rendered with a graffiti char
  (e.g., '#' in bright color) and gives any player on it +15 energy/tick
  (home territory) and any enemy on it -15 energy/tick (slows them down, out
  of their element). Tagged tiles persist for the entire floor.
  Tags can be placed in corridors to create choke-point speed advantages.

Requires:
  - Tile-state system: floor tiles with optional modifier tags that affect
    entities standing on them. Similar to fire hazard logic but positive/negative
    split based on faction.
  - Spray targeting mode: multi-tile selection interface (count-limited).

Design Notes:
  First environmental modification item. Strategic depth: tag a chokepoint
  before pulling enemies. Tags are permanent per-floor so early investment
  pays off all floor. Low floor count (3 tags) keeps it from being trivially
  dominant. Thematically perfect for NIGRL.

B5 — LEAN FLOOD
---------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den (rare)
Appearance: char '!', color (180, 80, 220) — deep purple

Effect:
  On drink: All floor tiles within a 5-tile radius of the player are flooded
  with "lean puddles" for 100 turns. Any entity (player or enemy) entering a
  lean puddle tile loses 20 energy/tick while standing on it (slowed, drowsy).
  Player also gains a passive: while standing IN the flood, +30% crit chance
  (the lean is YOUR element — you know how to walk in it).
  The flood radius cannot be targeted — it always centers on drink position.

Requires:
  - Temporary tile effect system: tiles with timed status that affects entities.
    Lean puddle tile type (distinct from fire) with a drain energy hook.
  - Zone-of-control: player buff only applies inside own flood zone.

Design Notes:
  Defensive zone denial. You drink it, back into your own flood, enemies wade
  toward you at half speed while you crit them. Radius cap prevents map
  flooding abuse. Duration limit means it's a "hold this room" tool, not
  permanent advantage. Does not affect toxicity — purely energy/speed based.

B6 — EMERGENCY CEMENT SMOOTHIE
-------------------------------
Type: Food (but drinks instantly — "meals" can be weird)
Zone: Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (160, 160, 160) — gray

Effect:
  Instant eat (0 turns — it's a smoothie, goes down fast regardless of what
  it's made of).
  On consume: Creates a wall tile at a targeted adjacent tile (range 1,
  cardinal directions only). The wall is temporary — lasts 50 turns then
  crumbles back to floor. Enemies treat it as a real wall (blocked movement,
  LoS blocked). The player cannot pass through it either.
  One use per item. The wall can be smashed by any entity dealing 10+ damage
  to it (it has 20 HP shown as a special entity on the tile).

Requires:
  - Temporary wall entity: entity_type="obstacle", blocks_movement=True,
    blocks_sight=True, has HP, expires on timer OR on death.
  - FOV recalculation trigger when wall is placed/destroyed.
  - Smash interaction: player/enemies can attack the wall tile.

Design Notes:
  Single-use emergency escape/trap tool. Block a doorway, force enemies to
  go around. The 50-turn expiry and 20 HP destroyability keep it from being
  a permanent map-solver. The "eat it instantly" joke is on purpose — your
  character just chugs cement. Meth Lab setting where you'd find this stuff
  lying around makes thematic sense.

B7 — NAPALM HOT SAUCE
---------------------
Type: Food (condiment, instant)
Zone: Crack Den, Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (255, 80, 0) — fiery orange-red

Effect:
  Instant eat.
  On consume: All floor tiles you've walked on in the last 30 turns
  (tracking a "footprint trail") burst into fire simultaneously. Each
  fire tile is a standard fire hazard entity (same as dungeon fire) and
  lasts for 20 turns. If you've walked less than 5 tiles recently, it
  creates fires on all adjacent tiles instead.
  Also applies Heartburn: +4 fire damage on melee hits for 30 turns.

Requires:
  - Player footprint trail: engine tracks last N positions as a deque.
    On napalm eat, create_fire() at each position.
  - This is conceptually simple to add (footprint deque already useful for
    other ideas too).

Design Notes:
  Retrospective AOE — punishes enemies that chased you. Path length variance
  means mobile players get bigger fire fields. Corridor gameplay: running
  down a hall and popping napalm hot sauce behind you is cinematic. Synergy
  with Deep-Frying skill tree (fire damage specialist). Watch for self-damage
  if player backtracks — intentional risk.

CATEGORY C — TIME & TURN MANIPULATION
(NIGRL has no time manipulation currently — huge design space)

C8 — CHRONODRINK (BLACK LABEL SODA)
------------------------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den (rare, floor 3-4 only)
Appearance: char '!', color (20, 20, 20) — near-black with white label

Effect:
  On drink: Records a "snapshot" of the player's current state:
    - HP, armor, position, all currently active effects (names + remaining
      durations), inventory count (not contents), ammo count.
  On death within the next 100 turns: Player is revived at snapshot HP/position
  with snapshot effects restored. The player's inventory is NOT restored —
  only HP/armor/effects/position. A "second chance" that doesn't erase the
  run cost of the death.
  Only one snapshot can be held at a time. Drinking a second replaces the first.
  After activation (i.e., it saves you from death), it cannot be re-activated
  until next floor.

Requires:
  - Snapshot system: engine stores player_snapshot dict on drink.
  - Death handler: check for active snapshot before game_over=True.
  - Partial restore logic (HP/position/effects but not inventory).
  - is_snapshot_active flag on engine.

Design Notes:
  Expensive to find, but THIS is the one that can save a run. Rarity keeps it
  balanced — you won't have one every floor. The inventory-doesn't-restore
  clause is critical: if you had 5 drinks and died, you respawn with 0 drinks.
  The game cost of death is preserved, only instant permadeath is avoided.
  Strategic question: when do you drink it? Now for safety or save it for the
  boss floor? The rare-floor-only restriction prevents stacking snapshots.

C9 — DEJA VU DRANK
------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (180, 180, 255) — pale blue-purple

Effect:
  On drink: For the next 60 turns, a "ghost trail" follows the player — a
  phantom copy of your position 5 turns ago. At any time during this window,
  pressing a keybind (e.g., Shift+Z) teleports you to the ghost position
  instantly (no turn cost). This can escape melee, back out of AOE, or
  reposition after a bad move. Ghost trail is visual only (rendered as
  dim '@' chars).
  One teleport use per drink. After use, the effect ends.

Requires:
  - Ghost trail: position history deque of last N frames (already discussed
    for napalm hot sauce — dual use).
  - Shift+Z keybind: consumes active deja_vu charge, moves player to
    ghost_position, no turn cost.
  - Ghost rendering: dim '@' chars at each position in the trail.

Design Notes:
  Tactical repositioning tool. Unlike a blink/teleport scroll, this is
  deterministic — you know exactly where you'll go. The 5-turn delay means
  planning ahead matters. If you're being pushed into a corner, the ghost
  may be cornered too. Power floor: incredibly strong in tight corridors.
  Weak floor: open rooms where the 5-turn offset doesn't help much.

C10 — DOUBLE-SIPPING (SIMULTANEOUS DRINK MECHANIC)
---------------------------------------------------
Type: Meta-mechanic idea (triggered by consuming two specific items
      in the same turn via a new "chug both" action)
Zone: Both zones
Appearance: N/A — it's a mechanic overlay

Effect:
  New game mechanic: if the player selects "Drink" from inventory while
  holding two drink items of the same type (same item_id), a "Double Sip"
  prompt appears. Choosing Yes: both items consumed in 1 action, effect
  applied twice (as if drinking two separate drinks but simultaneously).
  Alcoholism XP doubled. Hangover stacks: +2 instead of +1 per drink.
  This isn't an item — it's an interaction option unlocked by having 2 of
  the same drink. Some drinks with extreme double-sip effects noted below.

Requires:
  - Input handler check: on drink action, if player has 2+ of same drink,
    offer double sip option.
  - Effect doubling: some effects have explicit double_sip behavior
    (e.g., double-sip Virulent Vodka: +2 CON on kill instead of +1).

Design Notes:
  Gambling mechanic. You get more out of your stack, but the hangover
  compounds. Strong Alcoholism tree perk synergy (future perk: "Iron Liver"
  reduces hangover stacks). The real design interest is in drinks where
  the doubled effect is qualitatively different, not just quantitatively.

C11 — SLOW POKE COLA
--------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (255, 200, 50) — bright yellow

Effect:
  Duration: 80 turns.
  While active: All enemies on the floor move at 25% of their normal speed
  (energy accumulation multiplied by 0.25 for all hostile entities).
  Player speed: unaffected.
  Visual indicator: enemies rendered with a faint "sluggish" color shift
  (tint toward dark blue while the effect is active).
  The player effectively gets 4 turns for every 1 enemy turn.
  Trade-off: drinking it triggers a loud "POP" that alerts every enemy on
  the floor to the player's position (all enemies enter CHASING state).

Requires:
  - Global energy modifier for enemies: engine applies a multiplier to all
    enemy energy accumulation per tick when slow_poke_cola is active.
  - Alert-all mechanic: mass CHASING state trigger (already possible per-enemy;
    needs a "broadcast" wrapper).
  - Faint color-tint override in render for affected enemies.

Design Notes:
  The trade-off is the entire design — you get 4:1 action economy, but every
  enemy knows where you are. Ideal in open rooms where you can see them all
  coming. Disaster in corridors with unknown enemy positions. Duration at 80
  turns means it runs through most of a floor's combat. One of the most
  powerful items in the game if used well.

D12 — POTION OF LOOT (BROWN BAG SPECIAL)
-----------------------------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (140, 100, 50) — brown bag color

Effect:
  On drink: All items currently on the ground anywhere on the floor
  (not yet picked up) are teleported to the player's feet (same tile or
  adjacent tiles). The player doesn't auto-pick them up — they're just
  pulled magnetically to the player's position. Useful for grabbing items
  from rooms you can't safely reach.
  Secondary effect: +2 Book Smarts for 60 turns (you're industrious).

Requires:
  - Floor item query: engine can already get all entities by type; needs
    a "teleport to vicinity" function that places items in open tiles
    near the player.

Design Notes:
  Exploration payoff item. You cleared most of a floor, there's a pile of
  loot in the far corner guarded by a tough enemy. Brown Bag pulls it all
  to you without the risk. The +2 Book Smarts is a minor rider that helps
  with skill XP conversion on the floor you're using it. Simple to implement,
  high fun factor.

D13 — MYSTERY MEAT SMOOTHIE
----------------------------
Type: Food (instant)
Zone: Crack Den, Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (180, 90, 90) — pinkish-red

Effect:
  Instant eat.
  On consume: One random item from the player's inventory is destroyed.
  In exchange, the player receives a random item of the same category
  (consumable → random consumable, equipment → random equipment, etc.)
  from the current zone's loot table at a higher tier (floor_num + 1).
  The destroyed item is chosen randomly — you don't get to pick.
  If inventory is empty: no effect (wasted).

Requires:
  - Random inventory selection: pick random non-equipped item.
  - Loot generation at floor+1: call loot.generate_floor_loot with
    inflated floor number.
  - Category-matching logic.

Design Notes:
  Russian roulette transmutation. Late-game gamble: you're sitting on a
  pile of weak consumables and want to upgrade. The "you don't pick what
  gets destroyed" clause is essential tension. High risk: might destroy
  your best drink. High reward: might produce an amazing item one tier up.
  Dark flavor: your character is grinding up everything in their bag into
  a smoothie and just drinking it.

D14 — YARD SALE JUICE
---------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (255, 160, 0) — orange-yellow

Effect:
  On drink: All equipped items (weapon, rings, armor, hat, neck) are
  instantly unequipped and placed in inventory. Then 3 random items from
  the current floor's loot table are auto-equipped into whatever slots are
  now free. The player cannot decline — whatever spawns goes on immediately.
  Duration of forced equipment: 80 turns, after which the new items are
  unequipped and placed in inventory (originals remain in inventory, must
  be manually re-equipped).

Requires:
  - Batch equip/unequip: existing equipment manager, called in sequence.
  - Timed un-equip: on expiry of YardSaleEffect, batch unequip the
    force-equipped items.
  - is_yard_sale_equipped flag on Entity to distinguish which items to
    remove on expiry.

Design Notes:
  The ultimate chaos item. You might get incredible gear for 80 turns.
  You might get worse gear. Since you can't decline AND can't manually
  re-equip your originals during the 80 turns, this is a genuine commitment.
  Thematically: you stopped at a neighborhood yard sale, bought everything
  they had, and put it all on. Synergy with Abandoning skill tree ("burn
  bridge" — discard past for new future).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D15 — DUPLICATION SODA
-----------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Meth Lab (rare)
Appearance: char '!', color (80, 255, 200) — bright teal

Effect:
  On drink: Select one item in inventory (item-select menu). That item
  is duplicated — you now have 2 of it (or count increases by 1 for
  stackables). The duplicate is an exact copy including any current
  charges on abilities granted by the item. This includes drinks.
  Cooldown: cannot use another Duplication Soda until 3 floors later
  (engine.duplication_floor_lock = current_floor + 3).

Requires:
  - Deep copy of item entity: create_item_entity() called with same item_id.
  - Floor-gated cooldown: engine tracks duplication_floor_lock.
  - Charges carry-over for ability-granting items (store charge count on
    the item entity, copy it to duplicate).

Design Notes:
  Pure power — which is fine because it's Meth Lab rare and floor-locked.
  The interesting decision: duplicate a Chronodrink for two death-saves?
  Duplicate your best gun for two equipped weapons? Duplicate a Duplication
  Soda itself (the recursion is tempting but the floor lock prevents spiraling).
  The "include charges" clause rewards duplicating partially-used items that
  still have charge value.

E16 — ROOFIE COLA
-----------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (200, 200, 200) — clear/white

Effect:
  On drink: Player enters targeting mode. Select one visible enemy within
  range 6. That enemy immediately falls asleep (new "asleep" status: cannot
  move or act, wakes if damaged). Duration: 30 turns or until hit.
  While asleep, any non-damage action against the enemy (i.e., walking
  adjacent) gives player a free "loot" action — taking one item from the
  enemy's guaranteed drop pool immediately without killing it.
  Waking the enemy: it enters CHASING state with +20 energy/tick rage bonus.

Requires:
  - Asleep status effect: variant of stun with wake-on-damage behavior and
    longer duration.
  - Pre-death loot preview: enemies have a "guaranteed_drop" field; asleep
    loot pulls from it.
  - Rage-on-wake: modified CHASING entry that temporarily boosts energy.

Design Notes:
  Stealth/theft fantasy. The Stealing skill tree's spiritual companion item.
  Pre-killing loot is huge in a roguelike — getting your guaranteed drop and
  THEN killing normally is double value. The rage-on-wake makes waking it
  intentionally risky: you wanted that item more than you wanted easy combat.
  Tone is perfectly NIGRL.

E17 — NARC JUICE
----------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Meth Lab
Appearance: char '!', color (100, 200, 100) — suspicious clear-green

Effect:
  On drink: For 80 turns, any enemy that damages the player "narcs" on
  one random nearby ally enemy, causing that ally to become hostile to the
  narced enemy (faction conflict spawned mid-combat). Two enemies from the
  same faction suddenly fighting each other for 40 turns before returning
  to normal hostility toward player.
  The narced enemy has a "NARC" indicator rendered above it while the
  effect is active.

Requires:
  - Enemy-on-enemy targeting: AI already supports faction enemies fighting
    each other (Aldor vs Scryer). Needs a temporary faction-flip mechanic
    for non-faction enemies.
  - Narc event trigger: on_player_damaged hook checks for NarcJuiceEffect,
    fires narc event.
  - Visual NARC indicator: render small label on targeted enemy.

Design Notes:
  Deep NIGRL flavor. Snitching as a game mechanic. Makes you a magnet for
  hits but each hit you take starts internecine fighting. Strong in rooms
  with 4+ enemies — they'll start tearing each other apart. Weak against
  solo enemies. The 40-turn conflict window means fights within fights.
  Synergy: take a hit, watch chaos unfold, sneak away.

E18 — TRANQUILIZER GIN
-----------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Crack Den, Meth Lab
Appearance: char '!', color (160, 200, 160) — pale sage green

Effect:
  Duration: 60 turns.
  While active: Your attacks have a 25% chance to apply "tranquilized" to
  the hit enemy instead of dealing damage. Tranquilized: enemy is set to
  PASSIVE_UNTIL_HIT with a new 20-turn internal timer. If the timer expires
  without being hit again, the enemy wanders away harmlessly (exits map if
  possible, or enters WANDERING state permanently). If hit while tranquilized,
  it wakes normally and enters CHASING.
  Tranquilized enemies count as "fled" for XP purposes (partial XP: 25%).

Requires:
  - Tranquilized status: modified passive state with built-in timer and
    conditional exit behavior.
  - "Flee map" behavior: entity pathfinds to a map edge and is removed.
  - XP-on-flee credit system.

Design Notes:
  Non-lethal gameplay option — actually rare in roguelikes. Lets you
  thin a crowd without killing everything. Partial XP is the balancing
  clause; you can't farm full XP by tranq-fleeing everything. Tone:
  "I'm not trying to kill you, I'm just trying to get through." Future
  synergy with a Diplomatic skills tree (hypothetical).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E19 — BIG HOMIE MALT
--------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Crack Den (floors 2-4 only)
Appearance: char '!', color (200, 160, 60) — golden-amber

Effect:
  On drink: Select one enemy in sight range. That enemy becomes "allied" for
  60 turns — it fights other enemies and does not attack the player. On ally
  expiry, the enemy returns to CHASING state (angry about the alliance ending).
  If the allied enemy is killed by another enemy or the player during the
  alliance period, the player gains +4 Swagger for 50 turns (earned respect).
  This counts as a "crew death" — some penalty system could later track
  how many homies you lost.

Requires:
  - Single-target temporary faction flip: same system as E16/Narc Juice
    but player-controlled targeting.
  - Expiry aggression: on BigHomieMaltEffect expire, set entity back to CHASING.
  - Crew death event hook for future crew tracking system.

Design Notes:
  The "recruit the enemy" fantasy. High risk: you need to be in sight of the
  enemy to drink it (they might kill you before your homie is helping).
  The reward for the homie dying for you is a Swagger boost, not XP — the
  emotional payoff matches NIGRL's tone. Temporal alliance creates interesting
  combat dynamics where you're managing "60 turns until this turns on me."

CATEGORY F — STAT WARPING & BODY HORROR
(Existing drinks are relatively clean stat modifiers; these get weirder)
==============================================================================

F20 — LEAN BONE BROTH
---------------------
Type: Food
Zone: Crack Den, Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (200, 180, 100) — pale yellowish

Effect:
  Eat time: 4 turns (slow soup).
  On completion: Grants "Hollow Bones" status for 80 turns.
    - Speed: +40 energy/tick (you're light as air)
    - Defense: -3 (bones are hollow, you snap)
    - Dodge: +15%
    - Max HP temporarily reduced by 20 (you weigh nothing, less mass to protect)
  This is a high-variance speed/dodge build enabler — trading survivability
  for action economy. Works extremely well with hit-and-run playstyle.

Requires:
  - Max HP modifier during effect: HollowBonesEffect.modify_max_hp()
    (new lifecycle hook). HP clamped to new max on apply, restored on expire.
  - Dodge modifier stacking with existing dodge calculation.

Design Notes:
  First food that has a significant drawback. The max HP reduction is real —
  if you're at 40 HP out of a lowered max of 60, expiry suddenly drops you
  to 60 HP but your current HP stays (positive) or is clamped (if you were
  above new max). On expire: HP stays as-is if under original max. Rewards
  planning around the duration: commit to a floor, then rest.

F21 — CURSED KOOL-AID
---------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank (but should it be?)
Zone: Crack Den (rare, floor 4)
Appearance: char '!', color (120, 0, 0) — deep blood red

Effect:
  On drink: All 6 of the player's stats are randomized. Each stat is
  rerolled to a random value between 6 and 12. The total point sum of all
  stats is preserved (still 46 total + Swagger, but redistributed). This
  is PERMANENT — not a timed effect. The new stat spread is the player's
  new base.
  Also: full HP/armor restore on drink (to compensate for potential
  Constitution loss). And +2 Tolerance (permanent).

Requires:
  - Permanent stat reroll: modify PlayerStats._base values directly.
  - Sum-preserving randomization algorithm: generate valid distribution
    (each stat 6-12) that sums to existing total.
  - HP recalculation after potential CON change.

Design Notes:
  One of the most dramatic single items in any roguelike. The floor-4-only
  placement means you're at the end of Zone 1 with an established build —
  and then this asks you to throw it all away. The sum-preservation means

L45 — TOXIN TONIC SHOT
-----------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Meth Lab
Appearance: char '!', color (150, 255, 100) — bright acidic green

Effect:
  On drink: Current player tox level is converted to offensive buffs:
    Every 10 tox = +1 STR and +1 STS for 50 turns (at 100 tox: +10 STR
    +10 STS — enormous burst).
  After conversion: player tox drops by 50 (the alcohol counteracts some).
  Remaining tox is not cleared.
  For the buff duration: the player cannot gain any tox (tox-gain immunity).
  This is a commitment: you can't stack more tox buffs via Virulent Vodka
  or environmental tox during the Toxin Tonic window.

Requires:
  - Tox-to-stat conversion formula: read current tox, calculate buff magnitude,
    apply temporary StatBuff(str=+n, sts=+n).
  - Tox-gain immunity during window: modify_tox_gain() returns 0.
  - Tox reduction on drink.

Design Notes:
  Tox-as-offensive-resource design (established in strains-v2 paradigm).
  Encourages intentionally building up tox through the floor, then cashing
  it in right before a boss room. The tox-gain immunity prevents double-
  dipping — you either keep building tox (Virulent Vodka path) or convert
  it (Toxin Tonic path). Those are two distinct strategies with this item
  as the pivot point.

L46 — RADIATION ROCKS (CANDY)
-------------------------------
Type: Food
Zone: Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (100, 255, 60) — bright radioactive green

Effect:
  Instant eat (it's candy, quick).
  On eat: +80 radiation immediately.
  For the next 5 turns, mutation chance is 100% (guaranteed mutation per
  tick rather than % chance). You WILL get 1-3 mutations from this — the
  rad level determines tier (80 rad = weak tier).
  After the 5 guaranteed-mutation turns, rad returns to normal decay.
  This is a forced mutation item — you're choosing to mutate right now.

Requires:
  - Guaranteed-mutation override: engine.mutation_guaranteed = True for 5
    turns. Bypasses % chance check in mutation tick logic.
  - Timer on override flag: count down 5 turns, clear flag.

Design Notes:
  Risk/reward mutation item for players who want to force-roll mutations.
  The weak-tier (80 rad) means you're guaranteed weak mutations — not
  dangerous endgame ones. The "I'm choosing this" commitment is the design:
  you KNOW you'll get 1-3 mutations, you just don't know if they're good
  or bad (67% bad baseline). High Tolerance builds reduce mutation risk;
  high Five Loco stacks improve chances. The candy aesthetic against the
  horror of what you're eating is perfect NIGRL tone.

L47 — ESPRESSO SHOT (TRIPLE)
-----------------------------
Type: Food (liquid — instant)
Zone: Crack Den, Meth Lab
Appearance: char '%', color (80, 50, 20) — very dark brown, tiny cup

Effect:
  Instant eat.
  On consume: +100 energy/tick for 10 turns, then +60 energy/tick for
  20 turns, then normal for 20 turns, then -40 energy/tick for 30 turns
  (the crash). Total arc: 80 turns.
  The staged energy decay mimics a real caffeine arc.
  Also: all consumable food/drink actions during the boost window have
  their "eat time" reduced by 1 turn (min 1). So 3-turn foods take 2 turns.
  On crash phase: "Jittery" — any attack has 15% chance to miss (hands shaking).

Requires:
  - Staged energy modifier: EspressoEffect tracks phase by internal timer,
    returns different energy_gain values per phase.
  - Eat-time modifier: modify_eat_time() lifecycle hook that reduces
    food eat turns while active.
  - Jittery: new debuff applied on crash phase, 15% miss chance.

Design Notes:
  First consumable that modifies the eating time of OTHER consumables.
  This creates interesting combos: espresso then immediately start eating
  a 4-turn food — you'll finish it faster. Synergy with Munching tree
  (faster eating is a Munching tree theme). The multi-phase arc makes
  timing matter: don't pop it right before the crash hits.

L48 — STREET PROPHET'S WATER
-----------------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: Crack Den (floor 2-3, rare)
Appearance: char '!', color (200, 230, 255) — pale holy water blue

Effect:
  On drink: The next 5 items the player picks up are revealed before pickup —
  a text display shows the item's name and effect before it enters inventory,
  giving the player the option to leave it on the ground or take it.
  After 5 item-choice reveals, the effect expires.
  Also: +1 Book Smarts for 60 turns.
  Special: if the player REFUSES an item (sees it and leaves it), that item
  is replaced on the ground with a randomly better item (one tier up).
  The refusal reward only triggers once per drink.

Requires:
  - Item-reveal-before-pickup: pickup flow intercepted by ProphetEffect —
    show item name/effect, await Y/N input before adding to inventory.
  - Refusal-upgrade: on player refusing pickup, delete current item entity,
    spawn new item at same position via loot.generate_floor_loot(floor+1).
  - Refusal-once tracking: ProphetEffect.refusal_used = True after first use.

Design Notes:
  Information and decision layering. Roguelikes normally hide item identity
  until you commit. This temporarily removes that uncertainty, AND rewards
  you for having taste (the refusal upgrade). The 5-item count means you
  use it when you're about to loot a room. The "leave it and get something
  better" mechanic models the "patient scavenger" playstyle perfectly.

L49 — BOTTOM-SHELF BOURBON (HANDLE)
-------------------------------------
Type: Alcoholic Drink
Zone: Crack Den
Appearance: char '!', color (160, 100, 40) — mid-brown

Effect:
  On drink: +2 Swagger, +1 STR, minor heal (15% max HP).

Design Notes:
  The first drink that leaves behind a usable weapon. Thematically: drink
  the bourbon, keep the bottle, use it in a fight, smash it over someone's
  head. The shatter bonus rewards committing to all 3 hits. Glass shards
  hazard punishes enemies who step through the shatter zone. Satisfying
  multi-step item arc.

L50 — VENDING MACHINE MYSTERY SODA
-------------------------------------
Type: Nonalcoholic Drank
Zone: All zones (future: vending machine hazard/interactable)
Appearance: char '!', color (160, 160, 160) — gray, unknown brand

Effect:
  On drink: Checks which drinks the player has already consumed this run
  (engine.drinks_consumed_this_run set). The mystery soda mimics the effect
  of a drink NOT yet consumed by the player — a random one from the current
  zone's drink table that the player hasn't tried yet. First time you drink
  it each run, it's a genuinely new effect.
  Second time drinking it (same run): it mimics the effect again, same pick.
  Once all zone drinks have been tried: it generates a small burst of
  random minor buffs (as if drinking 3 random potions half-strength).

Requires:
  - Run-lifetime drink log: engine.drinks_consumed_this_run set, updated on
    each drink action.
  - Drink-table query: loot/items system can list all drink item_ids for
    current zone.
  - Random selection from unconsumed list.
  - Fallback: multi-minor-buff generator for when all drinks exhausted.

Design Notes:
  Exploration reward mechanic. If you're a completionist who samples
  everything, the Mystery Soda gets weaker over the run (fewer new options).
  If you're conservative and skip most drinks, the Mystery Soda could be
  your only way to access certain effects. Pairs beautifully with Blue Drank
  (double next drink) — if you want to gamble on a mystery soda being
  something great. Also: a natural vending machine interactable item for
  future environmental design.
