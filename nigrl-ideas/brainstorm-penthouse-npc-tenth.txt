================================================================================
TYRONE'S PENTHOUSE — 10TH NPC BRAINSTORM
"The Final Slot"
================================================================================

Design context: 9 one-time service NPCs already exist:
  Gun Dealer (free gun), Chef (food fusion), Plug (5 joints),
  La'Quisha (skill level trade), Precious (stat +1 / random -1),
  Rico (equip item for Meth Lab random), Soloman (enchant + lock item),
  Sage/Mixologist (drink on the rocks, 3 charges, clears hangover),
  Dice/Craps Dealer (replayable ghetto craps gambling)

Design spaces not yet owned by any NPC:
  - Abilities / spell charges
  - HP / temp HP / survivability structures
  - Status effect cleanse / debuff removal
  - Armor (defense rating)
  - Movement / speed
  - Senses / FOV / information-at-a-distance

User request: weird. Not a healer. Not a straight buff dispenser.
Think lateral. What strange service maps onto these mechanics unexpectedly?

================================================================================
OPTION 1 — THE MEDIUM: MAMA EZRA
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Mama Ezra is a fat woman sitting in a folding chair with a table full of candles,
cards, and burning incense. She reads your fate. Specifically: she reveals the
exact contents of a single room on the current floor — but the room she reads is
random. She cannot control what the spirits show. After the reading, the player
gets a yes/no offer: she will GUIDE you to that room (placing a trail of revealed
tiles on the map leading to it) or simply tell you what kind of room it is.

The twist: "the current floor" means the Meth Lab floor 1. She is seeing what is
BELOW. The reveal is about the next zone.

But here is the actual weird mechanical hook: Mama Ezra does not show you items
or enemies. She shows you THREATS AND OPPORTUNITIES expressed as raw numeric
information. "There is a room ahead with four creatures and one of them is sleeping"
or "There is a room with a crate. The crate has something sharp inside." She reads
in cryptic language, not game-system language. Players who know the game systems
well can translate; new players must guess.

Design classification: information / scouting hybrid.
Weirdness factor: moderate. The weird part is the intentional abstraction — she
never says "4 enemies," she says "I see four souls restless in a small space."

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'Z'   (no obvious letter; Z for Ezra, uncommon enough to feel distinct)
color = (200, 140, 60)  — amber-orange, candlelight

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Mama Ezra: "Child, sit down. I been waitin' for you."

  A short flavor text paragraph appears (the "reading") describing one room on
  Meth Lab floor 1 in oblique terms. The description is procedurally assembled
  from templates based on actual room contents:

  Template examples:
    - If room has 3+ enemies: "I see a space full of bodies that breathe wrong."
    - If room has a crate: "Something heavy is sitting in a corner. Waiting."
    - If room has a loot item: "The spirits show me something small and useful,
      abandoned by whoever came before."
    - If room is the staircase room: "I see a way down. But something stands
      between you and it."
    - If room has a faction enemy: "There is loyalty in that room. Dangerous loyalty."
    - If room has an elite/special enemy: "One of them is different. Worse."
    - If room is empty: "Quiet. Either nothing was there, or it left."

  After the reading, one follow-up prompt:

    [A] Ask her to mark the direction   (adds a blinking '?' marker at the edge of
        the map in the direction of that room — compass only, not exact location)
    [B] Leave it to chance

  Either way, engine flag sets. One reading per run.

  On second bump: "Mama Ezra: I told you what I saw. Spirits don't repeat themselves."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.mama_ezra_used: bool = False
  - At Penthouse generation time (or on first bump), randomly select one room from
    meth_lab floor 1's room list (dungeon.rooms for that floor, accessed if pre-
    generated, or pre-rolled during Penthouse generation).
  - Build the reading text by checking actual room spawn data: what entity types
    are in that room, whether there is a crate hazard, whether any loot is flagged.
  - Option A: store (direction_angle, room_id) in engine. On Meth Lab floor 1 load,
    render a blinking '?' marker at the closest map edge tile in that compass direction.
    Clear marker when player enters the revealed room.
  - The information is real but encoded. No false readings.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - Pure information. No mechanical stat effect. Value scales with player knowledge
    of the game's systems.
  - The direction marker (option A) is the mechanical "use" — it helps orient the
    player in an unknown floor.
  - Risk: if the pre-revealed room turns out to be unimportant (empty or trivial),
    the information feels worthless. Counterbalanced by template priority: prefer
    rooms with crates, elite enemies, or the staircase for the reading.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Marcus (Fence, from earlier design doc): if Marcus reveals faction distribution
    and Ezra reveals a room with faction enemies, the two pieces of information
    combine into targeted prep.
  - Bestiary: players who have seen faction enemies in the bestiary will decode
    Ezra's "dangerous loyalty" line immediately. New players will not.

VERDICT: Interesting flavor. Not truly weird enough. The "cryptic information" hook
is clever but the mechanical payoff (compass marker) is thin. Better as a flavor
NPC than a service NPC. Rank: C.

OPTION 2 — THE BODY MODIFICATION ARTIST: NEEDLE
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Needle is a gaunt man with stretched piercings, a garage-tattooist aesthetic, and
no license. He offers the player a single permanent body mod that is purely
defensive — but the mod comes from a menu of five options that each protect against
a SPECIFIC DAMAGE TYPE or CONDITION, not raw defense points. This fills the
armor/DR design space without being a straight "+10 defense" handout.

The options are:
  - Subdermal Plates: small pieces of metal sewn under the skin. Gives flat +20
    permanent defense. Simple, reliable, a real upgrade. (The "boring" pick.)
  - Bone Bracing: resin injected around key bones. Reduces all incoming PHYSICAL
    melee damage by a flat 2 per hit, permanently. Stacks with defense. Good for
    the Meth Lab's melee-heavy enemies.
  - Nerve Deadening: localized anesthetic injections. Status effects that deal
    per-turn tick damage (bleed, venom, poison) deal 1 fewer damage per tick,
    permanently. Cannot go below 1.
  - Hollow Knuckles: metal rings driven through the knuckles. Permanently adds
    +1 to melee damage AND +5% crit chance. Offensive body mod in a defensive slot.
  - Scar Tissue: deliberate scarring over vital points. First hit taken each combat
    (i.e., each time the player enters combat from a non-combat state) is reduced
    by 50%, rounded down. One proc per distinct combat encounter.

The player picks one. All are permanent. All are meaningful at different build points.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'N'  (Needle)
color = (180, 80, 80)   — dark red, blood and metal aesthetic

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Needle: "I do permanent work. Nothing decorative. Pick one."

  Menu titled "NEEDLE — BODY MODIFICATION (ONE-TIME, PERMANENT)":
    a) SUBDERMAL PLATES    Defense +20 (permanent)
    b) BONE BRACING        Incoming melee damage -2 per hit (permanent)
    c) NERVE DEADENING     DOT effect tick damage -1 per tick (permanent, min 1)
    d) HOLLOW KNUCKLES     Melee damage +1, Crit chance +5% (permanent)
    e) SCAR TISSUE         First hit each combat encounter -50% (permanent)

  Descriptions are one line each, exactly as shown. Player presses a-e.
  No confirmation prompt — the MENU is the commitment. Needle works fast.

  Immediately after choice:
    Message: "Needle: Done. Try not to get it infected."
    Apply the chosen modification.

  On second bump: "Needle: I don't do two. Go."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.needle_used: bool = False
  - (a) Subdermal Plates: player_stats.permanent_defense_bonus += 20.
    New field on PlayerStats. Applied in combat.py defense calculation alongside
    swagger_defence and equipment defense_bonus.
  - (b) Bone Bracing: player_stats.melee_damage_reduction += 2. New field.
    Applied in combat.py: player takes incoming_damage - melee_damage_reduction
    (min 0 before armor) only when damage source is melee (not gun, not effect tick).
  - (c) Nerve Deadening: player_stats.dot_tick_reduction: int = 1. New field.
    Applied in effects.py in all tick() methods that call add_toxicity / deal damage
    to player: subtract player_stats.dot_tick_reduction from tick_damage (min 1).
    Relevant effects: BleedEffect, VenomEffect, any toxic DOT.
  - (d) Hollow Knuckles: player_stats.melee_damage_bonus += 1 (existing field),
    player_stats.crit_chance_bonus += 5 (new field, added to the crit_chance calc
    in combat.py alongside street_smarts * 3%).
  - (e) Scar Tissue: player_stats.scar_tissue_active: bool = True. New field.
    Tracking: engine.scar_tissue_combat_used: bool flag, reset to False whenever
    the player goes N consecutive turns without taking damage (N = 3, equivalent
    to "left combat"). On next hit while flag is False: multiply incoming damage
    by 0.5 (rounded down, min 1), set flag True.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  Subdermal Plates (+20 def) is the floor. At Meth Lab floor 1, enemies deal
  ~8-15 damage; +20 defense reduces this by roughly 20% on weaker hits. Decent.

  Bone Bracing (-2 melee per hit) is stronger against melee-heavy enemy packs
  (Purger, faction melee enemies) — roughly equivalent to +2-4 effective defense
  depending on how many hits per encounter.

  Nerve Deadening is niche: very powerful if tox DOTs are stacking (Chemical
  Warfare enemies), negligible if the player manages tox well. Highest skill-
  expression option — players who know the Meth Lab enemy roster know this is
  strong.

  Hollow Knuckles is the offensive pick. +1 melee damage and +5% crit are small
  in isolation but permanent. Melee builds that have Stabbing/Beating high already
  may prefer this to a defensive option.

  Scar Tissue is the most situationally swingy: trivially ignored in single-hit
  encounters (works once anyway), extremely strong in rooms where the first hit
  of an ambush is a heavy one. Best in the Meth Lab where some enemies have
  high single-hit damage.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Soloman (Enchanter): if boots are enchanted (+20 speed), Scar Tissue pairs
    well because faster movement lets the player reset combat state more often —
    less time in sustained combat, more first-hit procs.
  - Mutation (Adaptive DR perk): stacks with Bone Bracing for multiple layers of
    flat damage reduction.
  - Stabbing/Crit builds: Hollow Knuckles + Stabbing L3 crit perk + street_smarts
    investment can push crit chance toward 30-40%, which is genuinely impactful.

VERDICT: Solid design. Fills armor/DR cleanly. The five distinct options create
skill expression without randomness. Hollow Knuckles being offensive in a defensive
slot is the "weird" element that makes the menu interesting. Not as strange as the
user requested but mechanically tight. Rank: B.

OPTION 3 — THE DREAM BROKER: SANDMAN
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Sandman is a thin man who barely moves and speaks in a low monotone. He sells
sleep — specifically, he puts the player to sleep right here and now (a voluntary
skip of several turns that heals and cleanses in unusual ways), but charges a price
in permanent ability charges. The player decides how deep to sleep: a light nap
(short, cheap), or full black-out sleep (long, powerful, expensive). The cost is
not cash. The cost is ABILITY CHARGES — Sandman takes a permanent reduction in
charges from one of the player's existing abilities as payment for the restorative
sleep.

This design hits: temp HP (sleep generates temp HP), cleanse (sleep removes
certain status effects), and abilities (costs charges) all at once. The "weird"
angle is that sleeping is the mechanic — it is dangerous in a roguelike (you lose
turns), but here it is a safe zone, so the risk is removed and only the reward
remains... at a price you cannot see until you already chose it.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = '%'   (dreamlike, fractured — not a letter, feels abstract)
color = (100, 100, 200)   — dusky blue, sleep/night aesthetic

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Sandman: "You look tired. I can fix that. How long you got?"

  STEP 1 — CHOOSE SLEEP DEPTH
  Menu titled "SANDMAN — THE SLEEP (CHOOSE DEPTH)":

    a) LIGHT NAP     "10 minutes. Light cleanse and a cushion."
       - Removes one random active negative status effect.
       - Grants 20 temp HP (floor duration).
       - Cost preview: "Will cost: 1 charge from one random ability."

    b) HEAVY SLEEP   "Two hours. Full restoration."
       - Removes ALL current negative status effects.
       - Grants 40 temp HP (floor duration).
       - Heals 30 HP (immediate, non-capped at max HP — capped at max HP).
       - Cost preview: "Will cost: ALL charges from one random ability."

  Note: if the player has no abilities at all, Sandman says "You ain't got nothing
  for me to work with. Come back when you learned something." Flag NOT set.

  STEP 2 — CONFIRM
  After picking light or heavy:
    "Sandman will take charge(s) from one of your abilities. You won't know which
    until after you wake up."
    [Y] Sleep    [N] Stay awake

  On confirm:
    - A short sleep animation text plays ("You close your eyes. The world goes quiet.")
    - The chosen restorative effects are applied immediately.
    - One of the player's abilities is selected at random. For Light Nap: that
      ability loses 1 charge (minimum 0 — cannot go negative). For Heavy Sleep:
      that ability's remaining charges are set to 0 for the rest of the floor
      (floor_duration depletion, resets next floor per normal rules if PER_FLOOR).
      ONCE/TOTAL abilities: permanently lose the charges (they are gone for the run).
    - THEN the message reveals which ability was affected:
      "Sandman: You traded [Smoke Screen] charges for that sleep."
      The player learns the cost AFTER they've accepted.

  engine flag: engine.sandman_used = True.
  On second bump: "Sandman: You've already had your sleep. Move on."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.sandman_used: bool = False
  - Ability pool: all keys in engine.abilities where the ability has at least 1
    remaining charge (for Light Nap: >= 1; for Heavy Sleep: >= 1, since draining
    0 is pointless).
  - If player has abilities but all have 0 charges remaining: Sandman says "You
    got nothing left to give. Not worth my time." Flag sets to True (he tried).
  - Light Nap — cleanse: randomly select one active effect on engine.player where
    effect.category == "debuff". Remove it (call effect.expire() + remove from
    entity.effects). If no debuffs active: cleanse step does nothing (still happens;
    the nap still gives temp HP and costs an ability charge).
  - Heavy Sleep — cleanse all: iterate engine.player.effects, collect all with
    category == "debuff", call expire() + remove for each.
  - Temp HP grant: engine.player.temp_hp += amount (20 or 40). Temp HP decays
    per normal rules (capped at entity.max_hp + temp_hp in the display).
  - Heal (Heavy Sleep only): engine.player.hp = min(engine.player.hp + 30,
    engine.player.max_hp). Does not exceed max.
  - Charge drain — Light Nap: chosen_ability.charges = max(0, chosen_ability.charges - 1).
  - Charge drain — Heavy Sleep:
    - If ability is PER_FLOOR: set engine.player's ability instance charges to 0.
      On next floor, it recharges normally (PER_FLOOR semantics preserved).
    - If ability is TOTAL or ONCE: set charges to 0 permanently (run-wide loss).
    - If ability is INFINITE: no cost is taken. Select a different ability. If all
      abilities are INFINITE: Sandman says "You're not what I thought. Nothing to
      take." No cost, no sleep — flag NOT set.
  - Reveal message fires AFTER effects are applied. The reveal is genuine flavor
    (the player literally wakes up and realizes which one is gone), not a trick.
    The player accepted the deal knowingly; they just didn't know the target.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  The key design tension: the player cannot choose which ability is drained.
  A player with one highly valuable TOTAL-charge ability (like a rare one-use
  ultimate from a unique item) risks losing it entirely on Heavy Sleep.
  A player with several PER_FLOOR abilities and a couple of TOTAL-charge abilities
  gambles on which one Sandman takes.

  Light Nap is safer but thinner: 20 temp HP and one debuff cleanse is modest.
  Heavy Sleep is the interesting decision: all debuffs gone + 30 HP + 40 temp HP
  is genuinely powerful (comparable to a full heal + cleanse), but the cost of
  losing ALL charges from a random ability for a floor (or forever if TOTAL) is
  real.

  Players with only INFINITE abilities are immune to cost — Sandman rejects them.
  This creates an interesting edge case: if a player has stacked only rechargeable
  spells with no cooldown, Sandman can't help them at all (not just won't — cannot).
  This is lore-appropriate (Sandman takes something irreplaceable) and mechanically
  clean.

  Timing matters: taking Heavy Sleep early in the penthouse visit, before deciding
  whether to use an ability-granting item from the Gun Dealer etc., could be
  suboptimal if you get a great ability afterward that you would have preferred
  undrained.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Dr. Pill (if implemented): Pill gives a random ability. If the player takes
    Dr. Pill's ability BEFORE Sandman, Sandman might drain the new ability — a
    sequence dependency.
  - Soloman (Enchanter): Enchanting an ability-granting ring (whose ability is
    INFINITE) before visiting Sandman means Sandman can't touch that ability.
    Clever sequencing.
  - Meth Lab debuffs: Heavy Sleep is strongest if the player somehow entered the
    Penthouse with lingering debuffs (status effects that persist between floors
    via floor_duration=True). A clean slate entering the Meth Lab is valuable.
  - Meth-Head skill (ability charge management): Meth-Head perks that restore
    ability charges would partially recover what Sandman drained — but only on
    the next floor, and only for PER_FLOOR abilities.

VERDICT: This is the strongest weird concept. The blind cost (reveal after commit)
is genuinely unusual for a roguelike NPC. The word "sleep" maps onto the mechanics
in an emotionally resonant way (you feel like you're giving something up to rest).
The split between Light/Heavy sleep creates a real decision. The "no INFINITE
abilities" edge case is elegant. Rank: A.

OPTION 4 — THE SPRINT COACH: LOLA
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Lola is a former track athlete who now runs footwork clinics out of the Penthouse.
She offers a one-time physical conditioning session focused entirely on MOVEMENT.
Instead of giving stat points or items, she teaches the player one of three
permanent movement techniques — each of which changes HOW the player moves, not
just how fast.

The three techniques:
  a) BURST STEP: Once per floor, the player can move 2 tiles instead of 1 on a
     single movement action (no energy extra cost — it is a free double-step).
     Activates automatically on the FIRST move action each floor. Engine clears
     the flag at each floor transition.

  b) SHADOW STEP: The player's movement no longer triggers idle enemies in adjacent
     tiles to enter CHASING state. Enemies in IDLE state that the player walks next
     to remain IDLE for 1 extra tick before transitioning. Essentially: the player
     is 1 tick quieter when sneaking past stationary enemies.

  c) IRON STRIDE: The player is immune to all movement-slowing effects permanently.
     Grease tiles, SlowedEffect from abilities, tox-induced energy reduction on the
     player specifically — none of these affect the player's movement energy cost.
     The player always moves at full speed regardless of terrain or effects.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'O'  (track oval — abstract but distinctive)
color = (255, 180, 60)   — athletic gold

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Lola: "You move like you never had to run from anything. Let me fix that."

  Menu titled "LOLA'S CONDITIONING — ONE TECHNIQUE (PERMANENT)":
    a) BURST STEP    Move 2 tiles for free on your first step each floor
    b) SHADOW STEP   Idle enemies don't notice you one tick longer
    c) IRON STRIDE   Immune to all movement slowing effects permanently

  Player presses a-c.
  Message: "Lola: Run it in. Keep running it in."
  engine flag: engine.lola_used = True.
  On second bump: "Lola: You got what you need. Keep moving."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.lola_used: bool = False
  - (a) Burst Step: player_stats.burst_step_active: bool = False (new field).
    Set True by Lola. Each floor entry: engine.burst_step_available = True.
    In engine.handle_move(): if burst_step_available and this is a movement action,
    move the player 2 tiles in the chosen direction if both are valid (first tile
    then second tile must both be unblocked and in-bounds). Set
    burst_step_available = False. On floor transition: reset to True.
    If the second tile is blocked: only move 1 tile (partial burst is OK).
  - (b) Shadow Step: player_stats.shadow_step_active: bool = False (new field).
    In ai.py, when an IDLE enemy transitions to CHASING due to player adjacency:
    check if player_stats.shadow_step_active. If True, delay transition by 1 tick
    (set a per-enemy counter "noticed_player_tick_delay" = 1; on next tick if
    still adjacent, transition normally). This is a 1-tick grace window.
  - (c) Iron Stride: player_stats.iron_stride_active: bool = False (new field).
    In effects.py modify_movement() for SlowedEffect / any effect that returns a
    movement cost multiplier > 1: check if iron_stride_active; if so, return 1.0
    (no slow). In dungeon.py movement cost for grease tiles: check iron_stride_active;
    if True, treat grease as normal floor. In combat.py tox energy reduction hook:
    if iron_stride_active, skip the tox-based energy penalty for the player.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  Burst Step is the most universally useful: free double-move at the start of each
  floor means the player can immediately close or escape any situation on entering a
  new floor. Particularly strong for melee builds that need to close distance quickly.

  Shadow Step is the most build-specific: its value is essentially zero for players
  who are already in CHASING combat with all enemies, and high for players trying to
  navigate the Meth Lab's faction-heavy floors without triggering every enemy in a
  room. Pairs with stealth/STS builds.

  Iron Stride is the most underrated: the Meth Lab has grease tiles, tox enemies
  with slow effects, and Chemical Warfare terrain hazards. Being immune to ALL of
  these permanently is quietly powerful. Players entering on their second run who
  know what slows down movement in the Meth Lab will pick this immediately.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Jaywalking skill tree: the Jaywalking skill's entire design is about movement
    and diagonal traversal. Iron Stride and Shadow Step both compound with
    Jaywalking perks for elite movement builds.
  - Mutation "Hyperactive" (if such exists): speed bonuses from mutations plus
    Iron Stride means the player is never slowed, period.
  - Sneaky builds (STS heavy): Shadow Step is the only NPC option that rewards
    stealth-adjacent play. Precious can boost STS (+1), and Shadow Step makes
    that STS investment more effective in practice.

VERDICT: Movement tech is an underused design space in roguelikes and a completely
different feel from all 9 existing NPCs. None of the existing 9 touch HOW you move,
only what you can do in combat. Lola is the most genuinely orthogonal design here.
But the "3 passive technique choices" structure feels a little similar to Big Jerome
(if Jerome exists). The specific picks are all strong and distinct. Rank: B+.

================================================================================
OPTION 5 — THE MEMORY MAN: DARNELL
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Darnell is a man who knows things he shouldn't. Specifically: he knows what you
have DONE this run. His service is a mirror — he reflects your run's history back
at you as a mechanical benefit. The more of a specific activity you have done
during the Crack Den (floors 1-4), the more powerful his reward is.

Mechanics: Darnell tallies one of three "run activities" (player's choice which
tally to cash in), then converts the accumulated count into a permanent bonus.
The tallies reset after Darnell cashes them — you trade your run history for
a present benefit.

The three activities:
  A) KILLS: Every enemy killed in the Crack Den. Each 5 kills = +1 max HP
     permanently. Cap: 20 kills = +4 max HP. Above 20, every additional 5 kills
     = +1 extra ability charge on a random ability (once, cumulative, up to 3
     bonus charges total for 35 kills). Formula: floor(kills / 5) max HP, cap at
     4 HP; then floor((kills - 20) / 5) ability charges, cap at 3.
     Flavor: "Darnell: You been putting in work. That kind of work hardens you."

  B) ITEMS USED: Every consumable item used (smoked, eaten, drunk, activated).
     Each 3 items = +1 to one randomly selected stat (permanent, no choice of which
     stat). Cap: 12 items = +4 stat points distributed. The stat is random each
     time (3 items = +1 random stat, 6 items = another +1 random stat, etc.).
     Flavor: "Darnell: You been putting things in your body. It adapted."

  C) CASH SPENT: Every dollar spent in the Crack Den (on Tyrone's shop, at
     vending machines, on any paid service). Each $10 spent = +1 permanent defense.
     Cap: $100 spent = +10 defense. No additional bonus beyond $100.
     Flavor: "Darnell: Money flows through you. Some of it stuck."

The player picks ONE tally to cash in. The other two tallies are gone (Darnell
clears the ledger). You pick which history matters.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'D'  (but note: 'D' may conflict with Deja if both are in the penthouse —
              use '#' instead, implying a ledger/tally mark)
color = (160, 200, 160)   — muted green, accountant aesthetic

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Darnell: "I been watchin'. Let me show you what I got."

  Menu titled "DARNELL'S LEDGER — CASH IN ONE RECORD":

    a) KILL COUNT       [XX kills]   → +Y max HP (+Z ability charges if > 20 kills)
    b) ITEMS CONSUMED   [XX items]   → +Y random stat (across Y separate rolls)
    c) CASH SPENT       [$XXX]       → +Y defense

  The actual counts are shown live — Darnell's menu reveals how well the player
  did in each category before they commit. The player can see exactly what they
  would get from each option and pick the best one.

  Player presses a-c to select. No confirmation — picking is cashing in.
  Both un-chosen tallies are zeroed. The chosen tally's benefit is applied.

  Message: "Darnell: Your record speaks for itself."
  engine flag: engine.darnell_used = True.

  On second bump: "Darnell: Ledger's clean. Nothing left to count."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.darnell_used: bool = False
  - Three tally counters, tracked from run start:
    engine.darnell_kill_count: int = 0   (incremented in combat.py on player kill)
    engine.darnell_item_used_count: int = 0  (incremented in item_effects.py on any
      consumable use — smoke, eat, drink, activate)
    engine.darnell_cash_spent: int = 0   (incremented in shop/vending logic whenever
      engine.player.cash is decremented by a purchase)
  - Kill payout: max_hp_bonus = min(kill_count // 5, 4). Apply as player.max_hp +=
    max_hp_bonus (also heal the same amount so player starts at new max).
    Ability charge bonus: if kill_count > 20, charge_bonus = min((kill_count - 20) // 5, 3).
    Pick random ability from engine.abilities. ability.charges += charge_bonus.
    (For INFINITE abilities: no charge grant — pick another.)
  - Item payout: stat_bonus_count = min(item_used_count // 3, 4). For each bonus:
    random.choice(["constitution", "strength", "street_smarts", "book_smarts",
    "tolerance", "swagger"]) — apply +1 to that stat permanently (player_stats._base
    adjust). If stat is already at 12: reroll until a sub-cap stat is found (max 10
    rerolls, then skip that bonus). Recalculate HP if CON chosen.
  - Cash payout: defense_bonus = min(darnell_cash_spent // 10, 10).
    player_stats.permanent_defense_bonus += defense_bonus (same field proposed in
    Needle's design).
  - Zero all three counters after cashing in (even the unchosen ones).
  - Serialization: all three counters serialize as engine fields in save_system.py.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  Kill count: a typical aggressive player kills 25-40 enemies in floors 1-4.
  25 kills = +5 max HP + 1 ability charge. 40 kills = +8 max HP cap (4 HP) +
  4 ability charges (capped at 3 extra). The HP cap is low enough to not be
  game-breaking; the ability charges on a good ability can be more impactful.

  Item use: casual players use maybe 6-10 items total (some smokes, a few foods).
  6 items = 2 random stats. 12 items = 4 random stats. The randomness of stat
  assignment means this can be great (CON, STR) or mediocre (BKS when you needed
  CON). Players who smoked heavily (Smoking skill build) will have high item counts.

  Cash spent: Tyrone's shop items cost $40-$200. A player who bought two shop items
  spent $80-$200 on those alone plus vending machines. $100+ = +10 defense is a
  real reward. Players who saved all their cash (penny-pinching) will find cash
  spent is their worst option. This retroactively rewards spending.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Smoking skill builds: the Smoking skill earns XP through smoking, meaning
    heavy smokers have high item_used counts. Darnell rewards Smoking build players
    with extra stat boosts for their consumption behavior.
  - Stabbing/melee combat builds: kill counts are highest for aggressive melee
    builds. Darnell rewards them with max HP and ability charges.
  - Shoppers (players who use Tyrone's store extensively): cash spent tally rewards
    heavy buyers with permanent defense. Penny-pinching builds get nothing from C.
  - Precious (Tattoo Artist): the random stat bonus from Darnell's item tally can
    be hedged by Precious — if Darnell gives a bad stat (e.g., SWG when you hate
    SWG), Precious's +1/-1 can help partially rebalance.

VERDICT: Darnell is the most original design in this batch. He is a run-history
mirror: the better you played in the Crack Den at specific activities, the more
powerful the reward. This creates meaningful meta-decisions RETROACTIVELY (players
on their second run will deliberately grind kills or items to maximize Darnell).
The "pick one tally, lose the others" structure is genuinely tense. No cash cost,
no tradeoff in the present — the cost was already paid over the run. Rank: A+.

================================================================================
OPTION 6 — THE CONTRACTOR: BIG PETE
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
---------------
Big Pete is a large man with a clipboard. He is a fixer of a very specific type:
he offers to put a BOUNTY on one enemy type of the player's choosing. For the rest
of the run, every kill of that specific enemy type earns the player a permanent
mechanical reward — but only for the first N kills. Big Pete defines the contract
terms upfront (which rewards, how many kills required), and the player decides
whether to accept the contract.

This design hits multiple spaces simultaneously: it touches HP (one reward option),
temp HP (another option), abilities (another), defense, and movement — because the
REWARD TYPE is chosen by Big Pete, not the player. Pete offers a random reward type
from the list above. The player chooses which ENEMY to hunt. The reward type is
fate; the target is choice.

Example interaction:
  Big Pete: "I got a contract for you. Terms: kill 5 Crackheads, get +15 temp HP
  (floor-start) for the rest of the run. That means every new floor, you begin with
  a 15-point cushion. Do you take the contract?"

Contract template:
  - Enemy type: player picks from a list of Meth Lab enemies (those they know from
    the bestiary or have seen).
  - Kill requirement: 3 to 7 kills (randomly rolled per run by Pete).
  - Reward: randomly selected from:
      (i)   +10 max HP permanently
      (ii)  +15 temp HP at the start of every new floor (run duration, stored as
            player_stats.floor_start_temp_hp: int, applied at each floor transition)
      (iii) +1 charge added to a random ability permanently
      (iv)  +15 permanent defense
      (v)   +20 permanent speed

  The player sees the FULL CONTRACT before accepting (enemy, kill count, reward).
  Either they accept or they don't. If they decline, Pete offers a different contract
  (one re-roll allowed — same random pool, new enemy + new reward). If they decline
  the re-roll too, Pete says "Fine. Don't need the work."

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = '!'  (clipboard / exclamation — urgency of a contract)
color = (220, 200, 120)   — khaki, construction-adjacent

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump:

  Big Pete: "[reading clipboard] Got something for you. Listen up."

  Display contract:
    "TARGET:  [Purger]   REQUIRED: Kill 5    REWARD: +10 max HP"
    "[A] Accept contract    [B] Re-roll (once)    [C] Decline"

  If A: contract is accepted. engine.pete_contract = {enemy_type, kills_required,
  reward_type, kills_done=0}. As the player kills enemies in the Meth Lab,
  engine.pete_contract["kills_done"] increments on matching enemy_type kills.
  When kills_done >= kills_required: contract completes, reward fires, contract
  cleared, success message displayed.

  If B (re-roll, once only): generate new enemy + new reward. Display again.
  If declined again: "Pete: Your loss." engine.pete_used = True (no contract set).

  engine.pete_used: bool = False; set True on first bump regardless of acceptance.
  (Pete only offers once; the re-roll is within that same interaction.)

  On second bump: "Pete: We talked already. You either got a contract or you don't."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.pete_used: bool = False
  - engine.pete_contract: dict | None = None
    Structure: {"enemy_type": str, "kills_required": int, "kills_done": int,
                "reward_type": str, "fulfilled": bool}
  - Enemy pool: all Meth Lab enemy types that exist in MONSTER_REGISTRY and have
    spawn tables in meth_lab zone. At least: purger, covid_26, toxic_slug,
    sludge_amalgam, chemist, and faction enemies. Shown by display_name.
  - Kill tracking: in combat.py / engine death handling, when an enemy is killed,
    check if engine.pete_contract is active and not fulfilled. If killed enemy's
    enemy_type matches, increment kills_done.
  - Reward fulfillment (on kills_done >= kills_required):
    "max_hp"    → player.max_hp += 10; player.hp = min(player.hp + 10, player.max_hp)
    "temp_hp"   → player_stats.floor_start_temp_hp += 15 (new field). On each floor
                  transition, player.temp_hp = max(player.temp_hp,
                  player_stats.floor_start_temp_hp) — floor-start cushion.
    "charge"    → random ability in engine.abilities (non-INFINITE), charges += 1
    "defense"   → player_stats.permanent_defense_bonus += 15 (shared field with Needle)
    "speed"     → player.speed += 20 (permanent speed increase)
  - Serialization: engine.pete_contract serializes as a dict in save_system.py.
  - Re-roll limit: engine.pete_reroll_used: bool = False; set True when B is used.
    If True: option B is grayed out/hidden on the re-roll display.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  The contract is a deferred reward — the player must EARN it in the Meth Lab.
  If they never encounter the contract enemy type (unlucky spawns), the contract
  goes unfulfilled with no benefit. This is a meaningful risk for a penalty-free
  mechanic (acceptance costs nothing directly).

  Kill requirements of 3-7 are calibrated to typical Meth Lab floor density.
  Most enemy types appear 2-4 times per floor on average. So a contract requiring
  5 kills might take 1-2 floors to fulfill, or might require specifically seeking
  out that enemy type.

  The temp_hp floor-start reward is the most interesting long-term: +15 temp HP
  at the start of every floor compounds over a multi-floor Meth Lab run. By floor 3,
  it has contributed 45 total temp HP (3 floors × 15). This is very good value if
  the contract is fulfilled early.

  Speed +20 is the most niche reward — valuable in any build but the weakest of the
  five in isolation.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Bestiary knowledge: players who have already studied the Meth Lab enemy types
    (via bestiary or prior runs) can cherry-pick the easiest enemy to hunt. A player
    who knows toxic_slug is extremely common on floor 1 will target that for a fast
    contract completion.
  - Deja's Cocktails (if implemented): Venom Sling cocktail + a tox-focused enemy
    hunt contract makes both mechanics reinforce each other.
  - Needle's Nerve Deadening: if the player has Nerve Deadening and takes a contract
    on a DOT-heavy enemy like toxic_slug, they are simultaneously more resistant to
    what that enemy does while farming it for the contract reward.

VERDICT: Big Pete is clever but the deferred/conditional reward structure makes
implementation heavier than the others. The "contract" concept is thematically right
for a penthouse fixer, but the mechanical footprint (kill tracking, contract dict,
floor-start temp HP logic) is extensive. The re-roll system adds one more state
variable. Strong design, high complexity. Rank: B+.

================================================================================
FINAL RANKING AND RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

RANKED ORDER (strongest to weakest for "weird + interesting + fillable gap"):

1. DARNELL (The Memory Man)  [OPTION 5] — A+
   Most original. Run-history mirror converts past behavior into present reward.
   Genuinely unusual mechanic with no analog in any existing NPC. The "pick one
   tally, lose the others" is real tension. Three distinct reward paths. Multiple
   retroactive synergies with existing playstyles. Implementation complexity: medium
   (three trackers + payout math). Fills: HP, abilities, defense. Weird factor: high
   (it knows what you did; it never tells you which tally is best until you see the
   numbers).

2. SANDMAN (The Dream Broker)  [OPTION 3] — A
   Weird angle: voluntary blind cost (ability charges drained from unknown ability,
   revealed after sleeping). The "sleep is power but power costs something you can't
   see" is emotionally resonant and mechanically clean. Fills: temp HP, cleanse,
   abilities. Two-tier choice (light/heavy) is well-scoped. Edge case with INFINITE
   abilities is elegant. Implementation complexity: medium.

3. LOLA (The Sprint Coach)  [OPTION 4] — B+
   Three distinct movement tech perks. Genuinely orthogonal to all 9 existing NPCs.
   Iron Stride is the most underrated pick in the set (tox-slow immunity in the Meth
   Lab is quiet but strong). Shadow Step rewards stealth-adjacent builds that no other
   NPC addresses. Fills: movement/speed design space exclusively. Weird factor:
   moderate (movement tech is unusual for an NPC but the "three passive techniques"
   structure echoes Big Jerome's structure). Implementation complexity: medium-low.

4. NEEDLE (The Body Mod Artist)  [OPTION 2] — B
   Cleanest mechanical design. Fills armor/DR and one offensive option cleanly.
   The variety of the five options (one offensive, one DOT-specific, one first-hit
   proc) is good. Hollow Knuckles being an offensive pick in a "body armor" menu is
   the interesting subversion. Weird factor: low (Needle is essentially just a clean
   stat menu). But it fills the armor/DR gap precisely. Implementation complexity: low.

5. BIG PETE (The Contractor)  [OPTION 6] — B+
   Thematically strong (bounty contract in a penthouse kingpin setting fits). The
   deferred reward structure is unique. But: implementation is the heaviest here
   (kill tracking by type, contract state persistence, floor-start temp HP regen).
   Good design held back by complexity cost. Fills: temp HP, speed, defense, abilities.
   Weird factor: moderate.

6. MAMA EZRA (The Medium)  [OPTION 1] — C
   Interesting flavor but thin mechanical payoff. A compass marker toward a pre-
   revealed room is not enough return for a permanent NPC slot. Best as ambient
   flavor text attached to a different service NPC. Fills: information space that
   is already covered by Marcus (if Marcus is implemented). Implementation: moderate.

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
DARNELL is the strongest pick for the 10th slot. He is mechanically novel in a way
that none of the other options — and none of the existing 9 NPCs — approach. He
forces the player to look backwards at their own run rather than forward at their
build, which is a completely different cognitive mode. The three tallies (kills,
items, cash) naturally map onto the three dominant playstyles in the Crack Den
(combat, smoker/consumer, shopper), and the "pick one, lose the others" structure
creates the kind of decision that players will think about for 15 seconds.

SANDMAN is the strongest if the team wants to specifically fill the ability-charges
space and/or temp HP in a single NPC with high weirdness factor.

LOLA is the most practical pick if the priority is low-implementation-complexity
and a clean, memorable ability that touches no other NPC's design space.
