ABILITY-GRANTING UNIQUE ITEMS — BATCH 2 (SINGLE ABILITY)
12 Unique Equippable Items That Each Grant One Powerful Ability
NIGRL Roguelike

Research Notes
--------------
- Builds on Batch 1 infrastructure (ability_cooldowns dict, ChargeType, TargetType).
- Batch 1 covered: Weapon/beating x4, Feet x1, Hat x2, Neck x1, Ring x2.
- Batch 2 targets: Stabbing weapon, Slashing weapon, Gun (sidearm + weapon), Neck,
  Ring x2, Feet, Hat, and one new weapon/beating for different flavor.
- Ability cooldowns: engine.ability_cooldowns[ability_id] = turns_remaining (ticked each
  energy loop). ChargeType.INFINITE + cooldown = the standard pattern for single abilities.
- All single-grant items use grants_ability (str) not grants_abilities (list).
- Existing effects leveraged: stun, fear, slow, confuse, bleeding, ignite, divine_shield,
  cripple_armor, stat_mod, invulnerable, dot.
- New effects proposed here follow the same @register + lifecycle hook pattern.
- Tradeoffs present on every item: self-damage, stat loss, HP cost, break chance,
  cooldown length, ammo dependency, or positional risk.

ITEM 1: The Hoodoo Needle
Slot: Weapon (stabbing) | Char: | | Color: (180, 80, 200) deep violet
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A bone-handled embroidery needle wrapped in electrical tape, scratched with sigils in a
dead language. Nobody knows where it came from — it showed up in a bathroom drain one morning.
It deals modest stab damage but the real power is Hoodoo Pin: fire it into a target and they
become your voodoo proxy. For the next 12 turns, whenever YOU take damage, the pinned enemy
takes the same damage again. A weapon for players who fight on the edge — the more danger you
put yourself in, the more punishment the target soaks.

ABILITY: Hoodoo Pin
  ability_id: "hoodoo_pin"
  name: "Hoodoo Pin"
  description: "Stab a distant enemy with voodoo intent. For 12 turns, every hit you take
    echoes as equal damage on your pinned proxy. Range 6. 20-turn cooldown."
  TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  max_range: 6.0
  Cooldown: 20

  Execute logic (_execute_at_hoodoo_pin):
    - Validate target is visible monster within range 6.
    - Requires The Hoodoo Needle equipped (weapon slot, tag "hoodoo_needle").
    - Apply new effect "hoodoo_proxy" (duration=12) to the target.
    - Store engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id = target.instance_id.
    - Message: "Hoodoo Pin! [name] is your proxy for 12 turns!"
    - Set ability_cooldowns["hoodoo_pin"] = 20.

  NEW EFFECT — HoodooProxyEffect (effects.py):
    id = "hoodoo_proxy"
    category = "debuff"
    On tick: check engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id matches self's entity.
    In engine.py damage-taken path (player takes damage): if engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id
      is set and matches a living entity, call deal_damage(engine, damage, proxy_entity).
    - Mirror damage is NOT reduced by proxy's defense (pure echo damage).
    - Message on echo: "Hoodoo echo! [name] suffers [dmg] mirror damage!"
    expire: clear engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id if it matches this entity.
    on_reapply: refresh duration, keep proxy target.

  TRADEOFF: You must take damage to deal mirror damage — incentivizes risky play.
    Low base damage (5) means Hoodoo Pin is the weapon's primary value, not raw melee.
    If proxy dies or you kill it normally, engine clears hoodoo_proxy_target_id.
    Cooldown 20 means you can't chain-pin multiple targets quickly.

BALANCE:
  - STR 10: 5 + (10-1)//3 = 5 + 3 = 8 base damage. Modest raw power for a stabber.
  - Mirror at full player HP pool: floor 1-2 average hit is 4-8, so proxy receives 4-8 extra
    per hit taken. Over 12 turns at aggressive play: ~6-8 free hits of 4-8 = 24-64 bonus dmg.
  - Risk: player must tank hits, which is dangerous without CON investment.
  - Synergizes with: CON-heavy builds (more HP = more tanking = more mirror), Divine Shield
    (absorbs player hit but the absorbed hit still counts as "damage taken" for mirror?
    — no: Divine Shield zeroes out incoming damage before the hook runs, so no mirror.
    Intentional: divine_shield hard-counters hoodoo stacking which would be oppressive).
  - Countered by: very tanky enemies with high HP (mirror helps a lot) vs. weak enemies
    (mirror is overkill). Best value against bosses and minibosses.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - engine.py: new engine state: engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id (str|None, clears on
    entity death and on floor change).
  - engine.py player_takes_damage path (the hook that ticks the damage-received): add
    "if engine.hoodoo_proxy_target_id" check — find entity by instance_id, deal_damage.
  - effects.py: HoodooProxyEffect (id="hoodoo_proxy"); expire clears engine state.
  - abilities.py: new AbilityDef "hoodoo_pin" with SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, INFINITE, max_range=6.
  - inventory_mgr.py: single grants_ability already supported for weapon slot.
  - No new PlayerStats fields required.

ITEM 2: Last Rites Cleaver
Slot: Weapon (slashing) | Char: / | Color: (200, 30, 30) blood red
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A meat cleaver stamped with a cross — not from a church, from a butcher shop. The blade is
so old the steel is almost black. Last Rites is its ability: sprint across the room and
deliver a devastating leaping slash to one target, dealing amplified damage. But the cleaver
is bound by a strict rule — if you use Last Rites and the target SURVIVES, the blade turns
against you. You take half the damage you dealt as self-harm, the weapon hums with bad luck
for 4 turns (−2 STR debuff), and the cooldown extends to 30 turns instead of 18. Kill your
target, get clean revenge. Fail — bleed for it.

ABILITY: Last Rites
  ability_id: "last_rites"

    target survives: take half damage dealt as self-harm and suffer -2 STR for 4 turns.

  TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS

  Execute logic (_execute_at_last_rites):

        engine.skills.gain_potential_exp("Slashing", damage, bksmt)

    half the attack and suffer a −2 STR stat debuff, turning your best weapon weaker right
    when you need it. The 30-turn cooldown compounds the punishment.

BALANCE:
  STR 10: base_damage=9 + (10-8)//1 = 11 + atk bonuses. Typical floor 3 damage: ~14-18.
  Last Rites on 14: 35 damage. Most floor 3 enemies have 30-60 HP. Near-reliable kill.
  On a 60 HP enemy surviving with 25 HP: self-harm 17, -2 STR for 4t. Player at ~113-17=96
  HP if healthy. Risky but survivable. Cooldown 30 means ~3 floors at moderate play.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - abilities.py: new AbilityDef "last_rites"; no new effect needed — uses existing stat_mod.
  - Handle teleport: scan adjacent tiles to target for first free passable non-monster tile.
  - deal_damage on player: call entity.take_damage directly or via a special self-harm path
    that logs the message without triggering "enemy deals damage" hooks.
    Safest: engine.player.take_damage(self_harm); log message manually.
  - If player dies from self-harm: call engine.event_bus.emit("entity_died",
    entity=engine.player, killer=None) — permadeath fully applies.
  - Slashing XP: follows same pattern as handle_attack Slashing XP in combat.py.

ITEM 3: Parole Officer's Notepad
Slot: Neck | Char: 0xE009 (or plain '"') | Color: (200, 200, 150) manila yellow
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A laminated government notepad on a lanyard — every parole officer in the city carries one.
This one is enchanted. The ability is Mandatory Check-In: mark one visible enemy as "on
parole". For the next floor, that enemy's AI is suppressed — it wanders passively and
ignores the player unless attacked first (passive_until_hit behavior override). Additionally,
if the parolee approaches within 2 tiles, it cannot attack for 1 turn (compliance check).
The real hook: if the parolee dies, the player gets a flat cash bonus (the state pays out
for a successful supervision outcome). One parolee per floor.

ABILITY: Mandatory Check-In
  ability_id: "mandatory_check_in"
  name: "Mandatory Check-In"
  description: "Mark one visible enemy as your parolee. It wanders passively and can't
    attack within 2 tiles (compliance check 1t stun). On its death: +$25 state payout.
    1 parolee per floor."
  TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  ChargeType: PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 1

  Execute logic (_execute_at_mandatory_check_in):
    - Validate target is visible, living monster.
    - If engine.parolee_id is set (already a parolee this floor): message "You already
      have an active parolee this floor!" return False.
    - Apply effect "parolee" (floor_duration=True) to target.
    - engine.parolee_id = target.instance_id.
    - Message: "Mandatory Check-In! [name] is now on parole."
    - Consume charge (charge consumed automatically by system after return True).

  NEW EFFECT — ParoleeEffect (effects.py):
    id = "parolee"
    category = "debuff"
    floor_duration = True
    apply: store entity.ai_type as self._saved_ai, set entity.ai_type = "passive_until_hit"
           set entity.provoked = False  (reset provocation state)
    expire: restore entity.ai_type = self._saved_ai

    before_turn hook (compliance check):
      - If entity's distance to player <= 2 (Chebyshev):
          apply_effect(entity, engine, "stun", duration=1, silent=True)
          engine.messages.append(f"{entity.name} checks in! (stunned 1t)")
          return True  # skip their turn (stun handles it)
      - Return False (normal passive behavior takes over).

    on_reapply: do nothing (one parolee only, PER_FLOOR charge prevents double-apply).

  Cash payout on parolee death (engine.py kill handler):
    if engine.parolee_id and entity.instance_id == engine.parolee_id:
        payout = 25
        engine.player_stats.cash = getattr(engine.player_stats, 'cash', 0) + payout
        engine.messages.append([
            ("State payout! ", (200, 200, 100)),
            (f"+${payout} for successful supervision outcome!", (255, 255, 150)),
        ])
        engine.parolee_id = None

  NEW ENGINE STATE:
    engine.parolee_id: str|None — instance_id of the current parolee; cleared on floor change.

BALANCE:
  - +10 armor and +1 BKS make the neck slot worthwhile defensively even ignoring the ability.
  - One parolee per floor: limited scope, not broken.
  - The parolee AI suppression means you can farm the zone without one enemy aggroing —
    tactical avoidance or clearing other enemies first.
  - Compliance check (stun within 2 tiles) is a proximity "keep away" mechanic; the
    parolee passively won't approach but if pushed toward player (e.g. by Fear) it freezes.
  - Cash payout ($25) is modest — about half a floor's expected cash. Flavour over power.
  - Synergizes with: high-BKS builds (faster SP), Steal skill (mark a target you want
    neutral to steal from later — then kill it for payout).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - engine.py: add engine.parolee_id init (None), clear on floor change.
  - engine.py kill handler: check engine.parolee_id, award payout, clear.
  - effects.py: ParoleeEffect; saves/restores ai_type.
  - abilities.py: "mandatory_check_in" AbilityDef, PER_FLOOR, max_charges=1,
    SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS. No max_range limit (full FOV, like most single-target abilities).
  - neck slot already processes grants_ability (inventory_mgr.py neck equip block already
    handles single grants_ability). Verify with existing neck items (boots_of_striding
    pattern is for feet; neck uses grants_abilities list). Check: inventory_mgr.py for
    neck equip handling of single "grants_ability" field vs "grants_abilities" list.
    If neck only supports grants_abilities, either (a) use grants_abilities: ["mandatory_check_in"]
    or (b) add single grants_ability support to neck equip block.

Slot: Ring | Char: o | Color: (30, 80, 200) police blue

A thick steel signet ring engraved with a thin blue line emblem, looted off a crooked cop or

  - Risk: indiscriminate AI makes it a chaotic use, not a pure win button.
  - Cooldown 22 = roughly 2+ floors of combat to recharge.
  - Synergizes with: Jabbing/melee builds where you want enemies confused first for safe
    approach. Anti-synergy: Stealing (confused enemies may wander, making pickpocket easier
    BUT the phantom cop might attack the enemy you're trying to steal from, triggering
    passive_until_hit and ending the pickpocket window).

Slot: Neck | Char: " | Color: (140, 140, 160) gray-blue

A bundle of undeliverable mail folded into a rough chain — census forms, eviction notices,

is COPIED onto the target. Not moved — copied. Both you and the target suffer, but now they
share your misery. Costs 8 HP to activate (paying it forward).

ABILITY: Return to Sender
  ability_id: "return_to_sender"
  name: "Return to Sender"
  description: "Copy ALL your active debuffs onto a visible enemy. Costs 8 HP.
    Works best when you're suffering — turns your misery into their problem too."
  TargetType: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cooldown: 15

  Execute logic (_execute_at_return_to_sender):
    - Validate target is visible, living.
    - Cost check: if engine.player.hp <= 8: message "Not enough HP!"; return False.
    - engine.player.take_damage(8)  (self-harm, no echo to hoodoo proxy).
    - Collect all debuff effects from engine.player.status_effects where
      effect.category == "debuff".
    - For each debuff effect (by effect id):
        apply_effect(target, engine, effect.id, duration=effect.duration)
        — passes the remaining duration so both tick down simultaneously.
    - Count how many effects were copied.
    - If 0 effects: message "No debuffs to share... and you lost 8 HP. Rough."
    - Else: message f"Return to Sender! {count} debuffs echoed onto {target.name}! (-8 HP)"
    - engine.ability_cooldowns["return_to_sender"] = 15.

  STRATEGIC DEPTH:
    - Strongest when you're heavily debuffed (bleeding, burning, poisoned, feared):
      fire this at a powerful enemy to instantly apply your entire ailment stack to them.
    - Weakest when you're clean — then you spent 8 HP for nothing.
    - Build synergy: intentionally take mild, long-duration debuffs (e.g., walk through
      weak fire, let a Purger touch you) then Return to Sender against a boss.
    - Fear is interesting: if you're feared, Return to Sender copies fear onto the target
      (source_x/y = whatever was stored on your fear effect).

  TRADEOFF: 8 HP cost, 15-turn cooldown, works best when you're hurt/debuffed (risky state).

BALANCE:
  - +5 armor, +1 TOL: modest defensive passives, but TOL is valuable.
  - 8 HP cost: at CON 10 (130 HP), trivial. At CON 6 (90 HP), meaningful.
  - "Worst case" (no debuffs active): you paid 8 HP for nothing — actively punishes
    inefficient use.
  - "Best case" (burning x3, bleeding, slowed): you suddenly make a tough enemy burn
    and bleed simultaneously — a meaningful turn shift in hard encounters.
  - No new effect subclass needed — uses existing effect ids passed to apply_effect.
  - Potential edge case: some effects have special __init__ kwargs (FearEffect stores
    source_x/source_y). Copy logic should use the existing effect instance's attributes.
    Simple approach: for each effect, call apply_effect(target, engine, effect.id,
    duration=effect.duration, **{k: v for k, v in vars(effect).items()
    if k not in ("duration", "floor_duration", "custom_display_name")}).
    Alternatively: only copy effects with standard (duration-only) init — document this
    limitation. Recommended: copy only "safe" effect ids: dot, bleeding, ignite, slow,
    confuse, child_support, mogged, rabies, cripple_armor. Whitelist approach.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - abilities.py: "return_to_sender" AbilityDef, SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, INFINITE,
    execute_at=_execute_at_return_to_sender.
  - Use a WHITELIST of copyable effect ids to avoid fragile kwarg forwarding.
    COPYABLE_EFFECTS = frozenset({"dot","bleeding","ignite","slow","confuse",
                                   "child_support","mogged","rabies","stat_mod"})
    Copy only effects in this set; log skipped effects as "too complex to copy."
  - neck slot grants_ability: see Item 3 implementation notes (same infrastructure fix).
  - self-harm: engine.player.take_damage(8); message before the copy loop.
  - engine.py floor-reset: clear ability_cooldowns["return_to_sender"] on floor change
    (standard pattern — ability_cooldowns already auto-tick, floor reset not needed
    unless chargeType resets — PER_FLOOR would auto-reset; INFINITE only resets cooldown
    via the tick loop, which is already implemented).
