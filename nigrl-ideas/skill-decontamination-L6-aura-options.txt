DECONTAMINATION SKILL — LEVEL 6 CAPSTONE AURA OPTIONS
NIGRL Design Document
Generated: 2026-04-02
======================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
Design 5-6 bold, code-ready perk options for Decontamination Level 6.
This is the CAPSTONE of the tree — the most powerful perk, and the
third toggleable aura alongside Gamma and Ironsoul. It must be
OFFENSIVE (L4 Ironsoul covers defense), DISTINCT from both existing
auras, and strong enough to feel like a capstone.

TREE STATE AT L6
----------------
  L1 — Radiant:             +10 Max Armor, +30% rad resist, +2 TOL, +2 STR
  L2 — Gamma Aura:          +3 STR. Toggle: enemies w/in 2 tiles gain +5 rad/turn.
                             2x Decon XP from resisted rad. Irradiated kills
                             remove 25 player rad.
  L3 — Consecrate:          +20 Max Armor, +15% rad resist. 2/floor, 30t CD.
                             Lay 5x3 rad zone (20t). Enemies: +10 rad/turn +
                             true dmg. Player: +1 SWG/turn in zone (cap 5).
  L4 — Ironsoul Aura:       +2 SWG. Toggle: +1 DR per visible enemy (cap 5),
                             +2 FOV. Hits from visible enemies grant +10 rad.
                             25% on melee hit: rad lost → armor gained.
  L5 — Sanctified Discharge: +30 Max Armor. Consecrate: 4 charges/floor,
                             +20 rad/turn. 30%/turn: +1 Ignite, +1 Shocked.
                             Both capped → double true damage.
  L6 — THIS DOCUMENT

AURA RULES (established engine pattern)
----------------------------------------
  - Toggle is free (ChargeType.INFINITE, TargetType.SELF ability).
  - One Decon aura active at a time. _remove_decon_auras() is called before
    applying the new aura — new id must be added to _DECON_AURA_IDS set.
  - Implemented as a floor_duration Effect (duration=9999, floor_duration=True).
  - Always-on passive bonus: a small stat or armor boost that the player keeps
    regardless of toggle state, separate from the aura body.
  - New aura id must be added to:
      abilities.py: _DECON_AURA_IDS set, _execute_[name] function, ABILITY_REGISTRY
      effects.py: @register class, EFFECT_DESCRIPTIONS dict
      engine.py: skill unlock handler (grant_ability call)
      skills.py: perk entry at index 5

WHAT THE THREE AURAS DO (THE DECISION SPACE)
---------------------------------------------
  Gamma Aura (L2):    Radiation accumulation on nearby enemies. Best vs
                      clustered packs, pairs with irradiated DOT and
                      Consecrate's rad zone. Pure offense via rad buildup.
  Ironsoul Aura (L4): Defensive scaling. DR from visible enemies, FOV bonus,
                      rad-to-armor conversion on melee. Best when taking
                      sustained fire or surrounded. Pure defense.
  L6 Aura:            The player needs a THIRD identity. Should answer:
                      "when is this better than Gamma AND Ironsoul?" The
                      answer must be a situation Gamma/Ironsoul cannot handle.

PLAYER STATE AT L6 (assuming all prior perks)
----------------------------------------------
  - +10+20+30 = +60 max armor, +45% rad resist, +3 STR, +2 SWG, +2 TOL
  - Consecrate: 4 charges/floor, 20 rad/turn, ignite+shocked procs, double
    true damage at cap
  - Gamma Aura and Ironsoul Aura available
  - Likely Decontamination level 6, meaning very high Decon XP investment
  - Player is a melee frontline paladin with high rad levels from Ironsoul

======================================================================
OPTION 1 — "WRATH OF GOD AURA"
High concept: your radiation is no longer a resource — it is a weapon.
Every point of rad you carry translates to direct retributive damage
against anything that dares hit you. The paladin's holy fury made literal.
======================================================================

NAME: Wrath of God Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +2 STS (Street Smarts). Your radiant body reads threats before they arrive.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - When ANY enemy deals melee damage to the player, they immediately take
    retributive true damage equal to player.radiation // 20.
    Example: 200 rad → 10 retribution damage per enemy hit. 400 rad → 20.
  - This retribution hits BEFORE the enemy's damage is resolved (checked in
    modify_incoming_damage or the combat hit handler in combat.py). Retribution
    deals true damage (bypasses enemy defense, not subject to rad resist).
  - Additionally: each retribution proc removes 5 player rad (uncapped —
    this is the DRAIN, the cost of channeling your radiation as fury).
    No rad gain from this aura. Rad is SPENT, not maintained.
  - If the retribution kills the enemy (entity.alive becomes False), the kill
    triggers the normal entity_died event bus event (Gamma kill purge applies,
    normal XP, etc.).
  - Cap: retribution damage capped at 30 per hit (prevents late-game
    degenerate one-shots; cap adjustable).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: WrathOfGodAuraEffect (id="wrath_of_god_aura")
  - Hook: modify_incoming_damage is the cleanest place. It already has access
    to entity (the player). Add engine reference via before_turn cache
    (same pattern as IronsoulAuraEffect._game_engine).
  - The retribution triggers on the incoming hit BEFORE the damage reduction
    is applied by armor/DR. Sequence: attacker hits → wrath_of_god fires
    retribution on attacker → then normal damage to player proceeds.
  - Retribution delivery: call entity.take_damage(retrib) on the attacker.
    Need the attacker reference. The modify_incoming_damage signature only has
    (damage, entity). Propose storing "last_attacker" on engine per hit, OR
    add a new hook: on_player_hit(engine, attacker, damage) that effects can
    override. The latter is cleanest.
  - Simpler alternative: handle in combat.py in the melee resolution block.
    After player takes damage from an attacker, check for wrath_of_god_aura
    effect on player and fire retribution against the attacker inline. This
    avoids any new lifecycle hook and matches how combat.py already handles
    ironsoul_aura.
  - The rad drain (5 rad per proc) uses combat.remove_radiation() with
    pierce_resistance=True so it doesn't hit diminishing returns.
  - New engine state needed: none. Per-hit logic lives in combat.py.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "wrath_of_god_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: irradiates enemies over time. Wrath retaliates instantly.
    Gamma is patient, Wrath is reactive. Against fast, hard-hitting enemies
    (like Meth Lab faction bosses), Wrath punishes aggression. Gamma just
    slowly kills them while they beat you.
  - Ironsoul: reduces incoming damage. Wrath DOESN'T reduce damage; it
    weaponizes being hit. Wrath is the glass-cannon toggle of the three —
    you still take full hits, but every hit hurts them back. Pair with high
    max armor to survive the hits while the retribution kills the attacker.
  - Best situation: tanky frontline, walking into melee with multiple enemies.
    Gamma is better for AOE radiation buildup on clusters. Ironsoul is
    better when you need to survive. Wrath is better when your radiation
    is high AND you want kills to happen fast AND you can tank the incoming.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - L1 Radiant / L3+L5 armor: more max armor = more tolerance for being hit
    without dying. Wrath rewards tanking hits, so armor buys more procs.
  - L4 Ironsoul melee proc: 25% chance to convert rad to armor. Keep
    alternating: swing (Ironsoul fills armor from your rad), then toggle to
    Wrath before an enemy swings at you. The rotation is: hit with Ironsoul
    → build armor → toggle Wrath before the retributive phase → absorb hits
    → toggle back. Real tactical decision.
  - L5 Sanctified Discharge ignite/shocked: enemies standing on Consecrate
    at 10 Ignite and 10 Shocked stacks will also be hammered by Wrath
    retribution — the triple damage vector.
  - Gamma Aura: if you've already stacked rad on enemies with Gamma, they
    take irradiated DOT (rad//50/tick). Toggle to Wrath AFTER they've been
    cooked, so while they swing at you in the last phase of their lives, Wrath
    returns fire. Gamma builds, Wrath finishes.
  - Player radiation drain: since Ironsoul builds rad on player via incoming
    hits, and Wrath SPENDS rad on retribution, these two create a loop if
    alternated: Ironsoul grants rad → toggle Wrath → Wrath spends that rad
    as retribution. Not gamebreaking because they cannot both be active.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  Being surrounded feels different now. With Gamma you slowly cook them.
  With Ironsoul you endure them. With Wrath, every hit they land is a
  mistake. The fantasy is the paladin who INVITES being surrounded because
  each attacker is executing themselves. High radiation = high retribution =
  the player is glowing like a reactor and anything that touches them regrets it.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Wrath of God Aura: +2 STS. Toggle aura: when enemies deal melee damage to
  you, they take true damage equal to your radiation / 20 (cap 30). Each proc
  drains 5 of your radiation. Your suffering is their punishment."

BALANCE NOTES:
  - At 200 rad: 10 retribution per hit. Against a typical enemy with 40 HP,
    you need 4 hits on YOU (they hit you 4 times). That's reasonable.
  - At 400 rad (near max viable): 20 retribution per hit. Two hits kills a
    weak enemy. This is powerful but requires sustained high radiation, which
    conflicts with Ironsoul's rad-to-armor conversion reducing your rad.
  - The 5 rad drain creates a soft timer. At 400 rad and taking 4 hits/combat,
    you spend 20 rad per room. You start each floor with whatever rad you
    carried over — likely 100-200 post-Ironsoul use. This limits Wrath's
    power to maybe 10-40 retribution shots per floor, which is correct for
    a capstone.

OPTION 2 — "CORONA OF JUDGMENT AURA"
High concept: you become a death sentence for any irradiated enemy. The
moment an enemy reaches a radiation threshold near you, they combust in
a nova of radiant force. Enemies flee in terror of what you represent.
======================================================================

NAME: Corona of Judgment Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +15% rad resist (stacks with existing). You are immune to your own judgment.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Each tick the aura is active, scan all living enemies within Chebyshev 5
    of the player. For any enemy whose current radiation >= 100:
    immediately trigger a Judgment Burst on that enemy:
      a) The enemy takes true damage equal to their radiation // 10.
         (100 rad = 10 dmg. 250 rad = 25 dmg. 500 rad = 50 dmg.)
      b) The enemy's radiation is halved (enemy.radiation //= 2). This is
         the "judgment" — their rad is SPENT to deal the burst.
      c) All living enemies within Chebyshev 2 of the BURST enemy take 5
         splash true damage (regardless of their own rad level). The burst
         radiates outward — this is the "corona" effect.
      d) After the burst, the enemy receives the "feared" status effect
         (if not already feared): they flee for 4 turns. At 100+ rad they
         saw the burst happen to a nearby ally. Terror spreads.
  - Each enemy can only be Judgment Burst once per floor
    (track via engine state: judgment_burst_ids: set). This prevents
    the aura from trivially evaporating enemies who sit at high rad.
  - The Judgment Burst proc applies Decontamination XP: 10 XP per burst.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: CoronaOfJudgmentAuraEffect (id="corona_of_judgment_aura")
  - tick() method: iterate entities, check radiation threshold and chebyshev
    distance, fire burst logic. Burst logic: call entity.take_damage(burst_dmg),
    apply feared effect on target, splash on neighbors.
  - New engine state: engine.judgment_burst_ids: set (reset on floor transition).
    Check before firing: if entity.entity_id in engine.judgment_burst_ids: skip.
    After firing: engine.judgment_burst_ids.add(entity.entity_id).
  - The per-enemy "once per floor" rule is critical. Without it, Gamma Aura
    + Corona of Judgment would trivially one-room-clear anything. With it,
    the burst is a single powerful proc that rewards stacking rad first.
  - Feared effect: already implemented in effects.py. Use apply_effect(entity,
    engine, "feared", duration=4).
  - Splash: iterate entities within chebyshev 2 of burst_target, call
    entity.take_damage(5) on each, fire entity_died events for kills.
  - Corona aura and Gamma Aura SYNERGIZE directly: Gamma stacks the rad up,
    Corona detonates it. Toggle Gamma to build rad on enemies, then swap to
    Corona to trigger the burst wave.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "corona_of_judgment_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: irradiates enemies. Corona DETONATES irradiated enemies. They are
    designed to work in sequence: Gamma builds, then you swap to Corona to
    collect the burst. Corona is the "harvest" phase aura.
  - Ironsoul: defensive scaling. Corona is pure offensive, zero defense.
    Corona rewards being offensive (using Gamma, Consecrate) while Ironsoul
    rewards being defensive.
  - Best situation: rooms with multiple irradiated enemies (after using Gamma
    Aura for a floor, then swapping to Corona when they're all at 100+ rad).
    One tick fires a Judgment Burst chain reaction across the whole room.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - Gamma Aura: the ideal loop — use Gamma to irradiate the room, swap to
    Corona when enemies hit 100 rad, watch Judgment Bursts cascade. Then
    swap back to Gamma for the next room.
  - L5 Sanctified Discharge: Consecrate applies 20 rad/turn on enemies.
    Combined with Gamma Aura, enemies in Consecrate zones hit 100 rad in
    5-10 turns. Corona then fires on them as soon as they leave the zone.
  - The splash damage creates incidental fear in clustered groups —
    even enemies that haven't been irradiated yet see their neighbors burst.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  The "burst + fear" fantasy. You're a walking divine bomb trigger. The
  moment enemies get cooked enough by your radiation aura, they explode
  in a nova. The room-clear potential is there but it requires setup: you
  can't just walk in with Corona active. You have to SET UP with Gamma/
  Consecrate first. The capstone rewards mastery of the full tree's toolkit.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Corona of Judgment Aura: +15% rad resist. Toggle aura: each turn, enemies
  within 5 tiles at 100+ radiation trigger a Judgment Burst — true damage
  equal to their radiation / 10, rad halved, nearby enemies splashed, all
  feared for 4 turns. Once per enemy per floor."

OPTION 3 — "MARTYR'S FIRE AURA"
High concept: you choose when to die. While this aura is active, when your
HP would drop to zero, you instead detonate in a massive radiant explosion
that deals damage to everything in sight — then you survive at 1 HP.
Between that detonation and the normal game: you are genuinely dangerous
in a way no other aura can replicate.
======================================================================

NAME: Martyr's Fire Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +2 CON. The paladin body endures so that the soul can sacrifice.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Passive field: while the aura is active, you emit a mild radiant presence.
    All enemies within Chebyshev 3 take 1 true damage per turn. Not
    irradiation — raw radiant true damage. Small but constant. The aura
    "shines" regardless of the detonation mechanic.
  - Detonation trigger (the big mechanic): ONCE PER FLOOR, if an incoming
    attack would reduce your HP to 0 or below while this aura is active:
      a) The killing blow deals 0 damage (blocked entirely this turn only).
      b) You immediately fire a "Radiant Detonation" — deal true damage to
         ALL visible enemies equal to your current radiation // 5 + 20.
         (Example: 200 rad → 60 damage. 400 rad → 100 damage. Base 20 always.)
      c) Your radiation is set to 0 after the detonation.
      d) You survive at 1 HP. You are NOT healed — you survive with 1 HP.
      e) The detonation flag is cleared per floor: "martyr_detonated" on engine.
         Once per floor only. If you trigger it, it's gone until next floor.
  - Message shown to player: "RADIANT DETONATION! [damage] to all visible
    enemies! You survive on faith alone."

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: MartyrFireAuraEffect (id="martyr_fire_aura")
  - The per-turn 1 true damage aura: tick() method iterates visible enemies
    within chebyshev 3, calls entity.take_damage(1), fires entity_died if
    needed.
  - The detonation: this needs a new lifecycle hook OR inline handling in
    combat.py. Propose: in combat.py, when calculating final HP after player
    takes damage, check for martyr_fire_aura effect on player:
      if new_hp <= 0 and has martyr_fire_aura and not engine.martyr_detonated:
          deal aura damage to all visible enemies
          player.hp = 1
          engine.martyr_detonated = True
          return  # skip the actual kill
  - New engine state: engine.martyr_detonated: bool (default False, reset on
    floor transition in engine._on_new_floor or equivalent).
  - New per-turn field: engine state NOT needed for the 1-dmg aura (handled
    purely in tick()).
  - The detonation radiation use: call remove_radiation(engine, player,
    player.radiation, pierce_resistance=True) to zero it.
  - One-shot nature: "once per floor" makes this a genuine permadeath safety
    net — but one you have to equip and hold. The decision to run this aura
    is partly "I'm about to do something risky."
  - The 1-dmg per turn passive hits EVERY enemy in radius 3, always. No
    threshold. No condition. This makes the aura active even when you're
    not at death's door.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "martyr_fire_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: irradiates, slow buildup. Martyr's Fire is immediate —
    small constant damage to everything near you, plus a dramatic trump card.
  - Ironsoul: prevents taking damage. Martyr's Fire LET'S you take the damage
    and then punishes the attacker massively. Philosophically opposite:
    Ironsoul says "you can't hurt me," Martyr says "you CAN hurt me,
    and when you do it wrong, everything dies."
  - Best situation: when you're playing dangerously (low HP on purpose,
    rushing Consecrate zones, baiting enemies into tight corridors). The
    detonation is STRONGEST when your radiation is high — incentivizes
    maintaining high rad, which Ironsoul ALSO builds via incoming hits.
    Load up with Ironsoul first to maximize rad, then swap to Martyr's Fire
    before diving into the boss room.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - L4 Ironsoul builds player radiation via incoming hits. Toggle to Martyr's
    Fire after rad is loaded — the detonation value scales with accumulated
    rad. 400 rad from Ironsoul = 100 detonation damage to ALL visible enemies.
  - L1 Radiant / armor perks: survive longer, more chances to use the
    detonation on a REAL threat rather than wasting it on a small hit.
  - L5 Sanctified Discharge: the 1-dmg per turn aura on enemies who are also
    getting Ignite + Shocked from Consecrate is a nice pressure addition.
    While they burn and get shocked, they also take the small aura tick.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  It's the only permadeath-adjacent safety net in the whole game that isn't
  just "heal." The detonation creates a moment that feels like a legendary
  last stand — you're about to die and instead you NUKE THE ROOM. That story
  moment is the capstone fantasy. The 1-dmg-per-turn passive keeps it useful
  even in uneventful rooms. The opportunity cost is real: other auras are
  more consistently powerful, but Martyr's Fire is dramatic.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Martyr's Fire Aura: +2 CON. Toggle aura: emit radiant heat (1 true damage/
  turn to enemies within 3 tiles). Once per floor: a killing blow instead
  triggers Radiant Detonation — deal radiation / 5 + 20 true damage to all
  visible enemies, zero your radiation, survive at 1 HP."

OPTION 4 — "PENITENT AURA" (Righteous Vulnerability)
High concept: you abandon all defense. While this aura is on, you cannot
gain armor and your DR is 0 — but every point of incoming damage you
WOULD have been protected from by armor/DR is instead returned as bonus
melee damage on your next attack. Suffering becomes power.
======================================================================

NAME: Penitent Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +3 STR. Raw physical power. You have rejected protection and chosen
    to hit harder as compensation.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Your effective armor and effective DR are both treated as 0. You take
    full, unmitigated damage from all incoming attacks.
  - For every point of damage that armor OR DR would have absorbed, add
    that value to a "penitent_charge" counter on engine (int, reset per floor).
  - Your NEXT melee attack deals bonus damage equal to penitent_charge
    (flat addition to damage before defense is subtracted). After the attack
    fires, penitent_charge resets to 0.
  - Cap: penitent_charge capped at 50. You cannot stack indefinitely.
  - Display: show penitent_charge value in the game UI or as a message.
    "Penitent Charge: [N]" so the player knows what they're banking.
  - Taking multiple hits before swinging? Each hit adds to the pot.
    Walking through a room surrounded and NOT attacking builds maximum charge.
    Then one swing dumps it all. Reward for patience and endurance.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: PenitentAuraEffect (id="penitent_aura")
  - The "zero armor/DR": in combat.py player damage resolution, check for
    penitent_aura on player. If present:
    a) Calculate what armor/DR would have absorbed: would_absorb = player.armor + player_stats.dr
    b) Add would_absorb to engine.penitent_charge (capped at 50)
    c) Apply full damage (skip armor/DR subtraction)
  - The bonus damage on next melee: in combat.py or spells.py where player
    melee damage is computed, if engine.penitent_charge > 0:
    bonus_damage += engine.penitent_charge; engine.penitent_charge = 0.
  - New engine state: engine.penitent_charge: int = 0 (reset per floor and
    on aura expire).
  - The aura expire() must also reset penitent_charge to 0 (don't carry
    charge from Penitent mode into a different aura's damage calculations).
  - The effect does NOT zero actual armor values on the entity. It intercepts
    in combat.py: "if has penitent aura, ignore armor/DR and bank it instead."
  - This means if you toggle Ironsoul right before getting hit, your armor
    still saves you. You have to COMMIT to Penitent. That's the tradeoff.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "penitent_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: slow irradiation buildup. Penitent is direct damage amplification
    and only useful if you have armor/DR to bank. Gamma is better against
    groups. Penitent is better for single-target burst where you can
    absorb multiple hits and then unload.
  - Ironsoul: reduces incoming damage. Penitent ELIMINATES damage reduction
    to AMPLIFY outgoing. They are mechanical opposites.
  - Best situation: when you have very high armor (Decon tree gives +60 max)
    and you're fighting a boss or single high-HP enemy. Walk up, eat 3 hits
    (Ironsoul would have absorbed ~15 damage per hit = 45 charge), then
    swing for 45 bonus damage on one punch.
  - Also useful when YOUR armor is already depleted (you're at 0 armor).
    When armor is 0, Penitent doesn't punish you (there's nothing to give up)
    and you still get +3 STR from the passive. Late-fight emergency toggle.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - The Decon tree gives massive max armor. All that armor you've built (60+
    from just the passive bonuses) becomes the FUEL for Penitent's charge.
    At full armor (60+), taking one hit might absorb 10+ damage → 10+ charge.
    Four hits = 40+ charge, near the cap. Then one melee swing hits for 50
    bonus damage. This is the payoff of investing in the full tree.
  - L5 Sanctified Discharge: enemies on Consecrate take 20 rad/turn. While
    they're roasting on your zone, stand in it yourself (swagger bonus) and
    let them hit you to build Penitent charge. Walk into the zone, absorb hits,
    emerge swinging.
  - L2 Gamma Aura: irradiate them, then swap to Penitent. They're slowed by
    the irradiated DOT, you're absorbing their weakened hits and banking
    charge. The irradiated enemy deals less damage per hit BUT you bank
    charge on each one.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  It completely changes how you use your defensive investment. All those armor
  bonuses from L1/L3/L4/L5 become an OFFENSIVE resource when Penitent is
  active. The perk rewards players who deeply understand the tree — you've
  built up 60+ max armor over 5 perk levels, and now the capstone makes that
  armor a sword. The "vulnerability as power" fantasy is different from
  anything else in the game.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Penitent Aura: +3 STR. Toggle aura: your armor and DR are treated as 0.
  Each point that would have been absorbed is instead stored as Penitent
  Charge (cap 50). Your next melee strike spends all charge as bonus damage."

OPTION 5 — "RADIANT PRESENCE AURA" (Aura of Fear + Command)
High concept: your radiation is now visible to enemies as divine terror.
Nearby enemies are suppressed — they cannot gain status buffs, they take
bonus damage while afflicted with any status (ignite, shocked, feared,
rad), and if they would ever flee from you, they instead freeze in place
for 2 turns. You are not a warrior. You are a force of nature they cannot
run from.
======================================================================

NAME: Radiant Presence Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +2 SWG, +2 TOL. Your presence is magnetic. You absorb punishment
    and radiate authority.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - SUPPRESSION FIELD (radius 3, Chebyshev):
    Enemies within 3 tiles who have ANY active status effect (ignite, shocked,
    feared, bleeding, stunned, dazed, hamstrung — any effect with
    category="debuff") take +3 bonus true damage per status effect they carry.
    Check once per player turn in the aura's tick() method.
    Example: an enemy with ignite (1 stack) + shocked + radiation DOT on them
    = 3 active debuffs → takes 9 bonus true damage this turn just from presence.
    The counting uses: len([e for e in enemy.status_effects if e.category == "debuff"]).
  - FREEZE ON FLEE:
    If an enemy within radius 5 enters FLEEING AIState (or would trigger the
    feared effect's flee movement), they instead receive the "stunned" effect
    for 2 turns. Their fear response locks up. They cannot escape you.
    Implementation: in ai.py or the feared before_turn, check if the entity
    fleeing has a player within chebyshev 5 who has radiant_presence_aura
    active. If yes, apply stunned(2) instead of allowing the flee step.
  - BUFF SUPPRESSION:
    Enemy abilities that would apply buffs to themselves (regeneration, temp
    armor gain, etc.) are blocked within radius 3. Check in apply_effect:
    if effect.category == "buff" and target is a monster and radiant_presence
    aura is active on player and chebyshev distance <= 3: skip application
    and log a suppressed message. (This is a rare case; most enemies don't
    self-buff. It's the conceptual pillar of the aura — "divine authority
    cannot be circumvented.")

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: RadiantPresenceAuraEffect (id="radiant_presence_aura")
  - tick() method: iterate entities within chebyshev 3, count debuffs, deal
    true damage equal to count * 3. Call entity.take_damage() and fire
    entity_died events for kills.
  - Freeze on flee: the least invasive approach is in ai.py's FLEEING state
    handler or the feared effect's before_turn. Before executing flee movement,
    check if player has radiant_presence_aura within range 5:
    if yes, apply stunned(2) to entity and return True (skip movement).
  - Buff suppression: check in engine's apply_effect function (or the top of
    Effect.apply()). If the entity receiving the buff is a monster, the
    applicator is themselves (not the player), and player has the aura:
    skip. Practically affects a handful of enemies with self-buff behaviors.
  - The debuff damage scales beautifully with the full tree: Consecrate
    applies irradiated DOT (debuff), Ignite (debuff), Shocked (debuff). An
    enemy in a Consecrate zone with all three = 9 bonus true damage per turn
    from presence PLUS the zone damage itself.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "radiant_presence_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: best for naked enemies (no debuffs yet). Radiant Presence is best
    AFTER you've loaded debuffs onto enemies via Consecrate + Gamma + L5.
    Gamma is setup, Presence is payoff — same sequence as Corona of Judgment
    but continuous rather than burst.
  - Ironsoul: defensive utility. Presence is offensive AND utility (the
    freeze-on-flee closes a real gameplay hole — irradiated enemies at low
    HP often flee, wasting your Decon XP and Consecrate duration).
    With Presence, nothing escapes. That's a qualitatively different play feel.
  - Best situation: mid-combat after multiple debuffs are in play.
    Swap to Presence once Consecrate zone + Gamma have loaded debuffs.
    The freeze-on-flee is also invaluable when irradiated enemies at 1/4 HP
    would normally sprint away and survive.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - L5 Sanctified Discharge applies Ignite + Shocked: that's 2 debuffs per
    enemy per Consecrate zone. Every enemy in the zone takes 6 bonus true
    damage per turn from Presence aura on top of zone damage.
  - The freeze-on-flee synergizes with the irradiated DOT — enemies with 100+
    rad who would flee to escape the DOT are instead frozen and cooked.
  - Gamma Aura's irradiation counts as a debuff (irradiated effect). Toggle
    Gamma → load rad on enemies → swap to Presence → they can't flee and
    take bonus damage per debuff they carry.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  The freeze-on-flee is the moment that makes this perk feel unique.
  Roguelike enemies fleeing is consistently frustrating — chasing a half-dead
  enemy through corridors, wasting turns. Presence says: nothing flees from
  you. Combine that with scaling damage per debuff and you have an aura that
  rewards the full tree's debuff-stacking toolkit.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Radiant Presence Aura: +2 SWG, +2 TOL. Toggle aura: enemies within 3
  tiles take 3 true damage per active debuff on them each turn. Enemies
  within 5 tiles that would flee are instead stunned for 2 turns. They
  cannot run from what you are."


======================================================================
OPTION 6 — "ATOMIC FIST AURA" (Melee Empowerment)
High concept: your melee attacks are nuclear. While this aura is active,
every melee hit detonates a small radiant explosion at the target —
dealing AOE true damage in a 1-tile radius around the hit enemy. You are
not attacking one enemy at a time. Every swing is a small bomb.
======================================================================

NAME: Atomic Fist Aura

ALWAYS-ON PASSIVE (regardless of toggle):
  - +2 STR, +15% rad resist. Your fists are weapons of mass irradiation.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Melee Radiant Explosion: each time the player successfully lands a melee
    attack (including weapon attacks, unarmed, but NOT gun shots or abilities),
    a 1-tile-radius radiant explosion fires at the struck enemy's position:
      a) All living enemies (NOT the main target — they take normal weapon
         damage) within Chebyshev 1 of the struck enemy take true damage
         equal to 5 + player.radiation // 40.
         (At 0 rad: 5. At 200 rad: 10. At 400 rad: 15.)
      b) Each enemy hit by the splash also gains +15 radiation.
      c) The main target (the one you directly hit) only takes normal weapon
         damage, NOT the splash. This prevents double-hit weirdness.
  - Additionally: your melee damage against irradiated enemies (any enemy
    with radiation > 0) gains +3 flat bonus damage. Reward for maintaining
    Gamma Aura pressure or Consecrate rad stacks before swinging.
  - No per-turn tick effect. This is purely a melee trigger, implemented
    in on_player_melee_hit().

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: AtomicFistAuraEffect (id="atomic_fist_aura")
  - Hook: on_player_melee_hit(engine, defender, damage). After the hit lands:
    px, py = defender.x, defender.y
    splash_dmg = 5 + engine.player.radiation // 40
    for entity in engine.dungeon.entities:
        if entity is defender: continue
        if entity.entity_type != "monster" or not entity.alive: continue
        if max(abs(entity.x - px), abs(entity.y - py)) <= 1:
            entity.take_damage(splash_dmg)
            add_radiation(engine, entity, 15, from_player=True)
            if not entity.alive:
                engine.event_bus.emit("entity_died", entity=entity, killer=engine.player)
  - Irradiated bonus: also in on_player_melee_hit, before the normal damage
    is finalized, if defender.radiation > 0: add +3 to damage.
    (This requires that damage be mutable before dealing — check how
    ironsoul_aura on_player_melee_hit works for the pattern.)
  - No new engine state needed. No per-floor tracking.
  - Add to _DECON_AURA_IDS: "atomic_fist_aura"

WHY THIS OVER THE OTHER AURAS?
  - Gamma: irradiates enemies within 2 tiles per TICK (passive spread).
    Atomic Fist irradiates enemies on HIT (active, position-based). Gamma
    is better when you can't melee safely. Fist is better when you're
    aggressively in their faces — each swing propagates radiation AND deals
    AOE damage.
  - Ironsoul: defensive. Atomic Fist is pure offense. No DR, no armor.
    The choice is classic: "do I want to survive longer (Ironsoul) or do I
    want to kill things faster (Atomic Fist)?"
  - Best situation: tight corridors (splash hits EVERYONE in 1 tile, which
    in a 3-wide hallway is both flanking enemies). Also pairs perfectly with
    Consecrate — lay the zone, enemies cluster in it, walk in and swing.
    Every melee hit splashes the adjacent enemies too.

SYNERGY WITH FULL TREE:
  - L2 Gamma Aura: use Gamma to pre-irradiate enemies. Then swap to Atomic
    Fist. Now your melee hits get +3 vs irradiated enemies, AND the splash
    further irradiates anyone nearby (15 rad per splash). The already-
    irradiated enemies also take more DOT from their radiation.
  - L3/L5 Consecrate zone: enemies standing in the zone take rad per turn
    AND true damage. Walk into the zone yourself (swagger bonus from standing
    in it!) and start swinging. Splash from Atomic Fist hits all clustered
    enemies. One swing in a crowd of 4 = one direct hit + 3 splashes.
  - The +15 rad per splash synergizes beautifully with Gamma Aura in sequence:
    Gamma loads rad on multiple enemies, Fist then deals +3 bonus damage to
    all of them AND adds more rad via splash, then swap back to Gamma if you
    need more. Rad stacking loop.
  - Player's own radiation from Ironsoul phase: if you've built up 200 rad
    from an Ironsoul-heavy room, swap to Atomic Fist — splash damage is now
    10 per adjacent enemy, not 5. Radiation investment pays off.

WHY EXCITING AS CAPSTONE:
  It changes the geometry of combat. Every melee encounter becomes a question
  of positioning: "can I angle this swing to splash the enemy behind him?"
  In tight corridors or Consecrate zones with clustered enemies, Atomic Fist
  turns the player into a walking IED. The visual and mechanical fantasy —
  punching one person and making everyone around them explode in radiation —
  is visceral, readable, and uniquely expresses the Decontamination paladin.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Atomic Fist Aura: +2 STR, +15% rad resist. Toggle aura: melee hits trigger
  a radiant explosion — all enemies within 1 tile of the target take 5 +
  radiation / 40 true damage and gain 15 radiation. +3 melee damage vs any
  irradiated enemy."
