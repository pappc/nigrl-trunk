================================================================================
BLACKKK MAGIC — 5 SKILL TREE DESIGNS
================================================================================
Design doc for the Blackkk Magic skill tree (currently fully placeholder).
All 5 designs cover L1-L5. Each takes a meaningfully different mechanical
approach to the curse/hex paradigm.

XP Source (all designs): Blackkk Magic XP is gained each time one of your
curses/hexes LANDS on an enemy. Not from melee hits, not from spell casts
that miss — only on successful application. This rewards stacking curses and
hunting targets with more debuff slots, not just button-mashing.

Tone anchor: street voodoo — evil eye, jinxes, bad juju, voodoo dolls. Not
wizard school. Not D&D. The names should feel like something shouted at
someone who just cut you off in traffic.

================================================================================
DESIGN A — "THE MARK" (Accumulating Hex Stack)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
You put The Mark on an enemy — a spiritual brand that builds in severity with
every additional curse you stack on them. The Mark itself does nothing at first,
but every debuff applied to a Marked target amplifies existing curses and feeds
back into more powerful hexes. Designed for a deliberate, patient player who
picks one target to absolutely ruin before moving on.

PLAYSTYLE: Single-target sustained curse escalation. Reward for curse juggling
on one enemy. Anti-swarm — bad against many weak enemies, excellent against
elites and bosses.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Book Smarts (hex scaling, XP conversion). Street Smarts
(curse application through gouge and sneak hits).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Put It On 'Em" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "put_it_on_em"}
  ability_id: "put_it_on_em"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Brand a visible enemy with The Mark (floor_duration=True). Marked enemies
    glow with a faint purple tint in the render (color override when marked=True
    on entity). The Mark itself has no direct effect. But every debuff applied
    to a Marked enemy by the player refreshes The Mark's debuff-amplification
    tier by +1 (stored as entity.mark_tier, int, max 5). Tier is displayed as
    The Mark (x) in status panel. Each tier adds +1 flat damage to all incoming
    attacks against that target (not just the player's — this is important for
    combos with other enemies).
  impl notes:
    entity.is_marked: bool (set True on application)
    entity.mark_tier: int (0 at application, +1 each time player applies any
      debuff/curse to a marked entity, capped at 5)
    engine tracks: check if target has is_marked when any effect is applied via
      apply_effect(); if so, mark_tier += 1 and recalculate mark bonus.
    damage bonus: flat +mark_tier added to all damage rolls against this entity
      (applied in combat.py after base damage calc, before defense).
    The Mark is removed on floor change. Only one entity can be marked at a time.
    Reapplying to a new target removes mark from the old one (mark_tier resets).
  balance: At tier 5, +5 flat damage to all hits. Requires 5 debuff applications
    to reach. Most enemies die before tier 5 unless boss/elite. Tier 3 (~+3 dmg)
    is a realistic peak on floor bosses.

L2 — "Juju" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When you apply a debuff/curse to a Marked enemy, there is a chance equal to
    (mark_tier * 10)% that the debuff lasts 50% longer on that target (rounded
    up to nearest turn). At tier 1: 10%, tier 3: 30%, tier 5: 50%. Does not
    stack retroactively — only applies to debuffs applied AFTER Juju is unlocked.
  impl notes:
    In apply_effect(), after setting duration: if target.is_marked, roll
    random() < target.mark_tier * 0.10; if pass, duration = ceil(duration * 1.5).
    Works with any debuff (stun, slow, fear, dot, bleeding, etc.) — broad
    synergy that rewards using Put It On 'Em early in a fight.

L3 — "Triple 6 Special" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "triple_6_special"}
  ability_id: "triple_6_special"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 2
  desc:
    Concentrate the Mark's energy into a targeted blast. Requires target to have
    is_marked=True. Deals (6 + mark_tier * 6) damage, ignoring defense entirely
    (spirit damage bypasses armor). Resets mark_tier to 0 on use but does NOT
    remove The Mark itself — the brand stays, tier just collapses back to 0 so
    you can build again. 2 uses per floor.
  impl notes:
    Validate: target.is_marked must be True; if not, message "No mark on target."
    Damage: 6 + entity.mark_tier * 6. Applied via entity.take_damage() with
      defense_bypass=True flag (or direct entity.hp -= damage after clamping).
    After firing: entity.mark_tier = 0.
    Not a spell (is_spell=False) — doesn't interact with Smartsness BKS scaling.
  balance: At tier 5 (max): 36 damage, no defense. At tier 2 (typical build):
    18 no-defense. Most floor 1-2 enemies have 20-40 HP; this one-shots or
    nearly one-shots a marked target even at tier 2. 2/floor limits burst.

L4 — "Voodoo Contagion" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When the Marked target dies while The Mark is active, The Mark's current
    debuffs (all active status effects on that entity at death) are transferred
    at half their remaining duration (rounded down, minimum 1 turn) to a random
    non-marked enemy within 5 tiles. The transferred enemy does NOT receive
    is_marked. This is pure curse propagation — the mark does not follow, but
    the suffering does. If no valid adjacent enemy exists within 5 tiles, the
    effect is wasted.
  impl notes:
    In engine event handler for "entity_died": check if entity.is_marked; if so,
    collect all status_effects on that entity where duration > 0; pick a random
    non-player living entity within Chebyshev(5); for each effect in collected
    list, call apply_effect() with duration = max(1, effect.duration // 2).
    Do not transfer floor_duration effects.
    Mark itself clears after transfer (entity.is_marked = False on old target).
  balance: Great cleanup mechanic. In tight rooms with clustered enemies the
    transferred fear/slow/bleed can chain into solid free damage. Requires
    killing the Marked target quickly enough that debuffs haven't expired, so
    it rewards aggressive play on marked targets.

L5 — "The Reaping Mark" (passive upgrade to Put It On 'Em)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Passive upgrade to Put It On 'Em: whenever mark_tier reaches 5, the Marked
    enemy is immediately inflicted with ALL of the following debuffs simultaneously,
    at their base parameters (no stacking, just base):
      - stun (5 turns)
      - slow (ratio=0.3, 8 turns)
      - cripple_armor (12 turns)
    These are applied automatically and silently (message: "The Mark awakens.")
    without spending any ability charges. This can only trigger once per Mark
    instance — after triggering, mark_tier resets to 0 and the automatic
    buff-trigger is flagged as used (entity.mark_awakened = True, cleared on
    new Mark application).
  impl notes:
    In the mark_tier increment logic: if mark_tier hits 5 and not
    entity.mark_awakened: apply stun(5), slow(0.3, 8), cripple_armor(12);
    set entity.mark_awakened = True; message "The Mark awakens."
    mark_tier stays at 5 after awakening (only resets on Triple 6 Special or
    if enemy dies and Contagion triggers).
  balance: The combo of stun + cripple + slow on a boss target is genuinely
    terrifying. Requires 5 debuff stacks to trigger — on a boss that means
    using Put It On 'Em + 4 other debuff sources (gouge, fear item, dot from
    bleeding, etc.). Payoff is proportional to setup cost.

DESIGN B — "WRIT LARGE" (Hex Writing / Curse Tagging)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
You write curses in the air — visible symbols that float over tagged enemies.
The Writ system uses a queue-based mechanic: you inscribe a WRIT (a floating
hex charge) onto an enemy, then trigger it with a specific action. Different
Writs produce different effects. L4 lets you write TWO Writs on the same
enemy simultaneously. The Writ "detonates" are the payoff — each one is a
different flavored catastrophe.

PLAYSTYLE: Proactive setup into punishing payoff. Requires planning — put the
right Writ on the right enemy before the right action. Skilled play rewards
reading the fight in advance. Good against medium groups.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Street Smarts (Writ accuracy, writ proc chance). Book Smarts
(Writ damage scaling).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Writ of Pain" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "writ_of_pain"}
  ability_id: "writ_of_pain"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Inscribe the Writ of Pain on a visible enemy (entity.writ = "pain", floor
    duration). The Writ is dormant until the enemy is next hit by any melee
    attack (player or enemy-on-enemy). On that melee hit, the Writ detonates:
    the attacker and all enemies within 1 tile of the target take
    (4 + BKS/2) damage with no defense (spiritual burst). The Writ then
    vanishes. Only one Writ can be active per enemy (until L4). Reapplication
    overwrites the existing Writ.
  impl notes:
    entity.writ: str | None — "pain", "slow", "fear", etc.
    Writ detonation check: in combat.py after a melee hit lands on an entity,
      check entity.writ; if "pain", resolve AOE_CIRCLE radius=1 for (4 + BKS//2)
      no-defense damage; then entity.writ = None.
    "No defense" means bypassing the normal defense subtraction in combat calc.
    Writ of Pain is always active (INFINITE charges) — the payoff is the setup.
    Blackkk Magic XP: granted on Writ inscription, NOT on detonation.

L2 — "Writ of Slowing" (passive — expands Writ repertoire)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Adds a second Writ option. When writing a Writ (via Writ of Pain ability),
    you can now choose between "pain" (L1) and "slow". Writ of Slow detonates
    when the enemy MOVES (takes any step). On detonation: enemy and all enemies
    within 2 tiles receive slow (ratio=0.5, 10 turns). You cannot pre-select
    which Writ to apply via the ability menu UI — instead, a secondary key prompt
    appears after targeting: "P=Pain, S=Slow". Both use the same INFINITE charges.
  impl notes:
    Engine needs a minor UI state: after target is confirmed for writ_of_pain
    ability, present secondary prompt (could reuse an input loop or a single-key
    branch in the targeting resolution). Set entity.writ = "pain" or "slow"
    based on key input.
    Writ of Slow detonation: check entity.writ == "slow" at end of any monster
    movement step in ai.py. If entity moved (not just attempted), detonate:
    AOE_CIRCLE r=2 apply slow(0.5, 10) to all living non-player entities in range.
    entity.writ = None after.

L3 — "Writ of Dread" (passive — third Writ type + Writ empowerment)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Adds "dread" to the Writ choices. Writ of Dread detonates when the enemy is
    reduced below 50% HP for the first time. On detonation: applies fear (8 turns,
    source = player position) to the target and all enemies within 3 tiles. Also
    passively empowers all existing Writs: damage-based Writs (pain) deal +BKS/3
    bonus damage. Duration-based Writs (slow, dread) have their effect durations
    increased by 3 turns.
  impl notes:
    Track 50% HP threshold: entity.writ_dread_threshold_crossed: bool (default
    False, reset on floor change or on entity respawn). In combat.py damage
    resolution: if entity.writ == "dread" and not entity.writ_dread_threshold_crossed
    and entity.hp <= entity.max_hp // 2: set flag, detonate — apply fear(8,
    source_x=player.x, source_y=player.y) to target + AOE r=3.
    Passive empowerment: stored as a flag on engine (engine.writ_empowered = True
    once L3 unlocked). In combat.py, when resolving pain writ detonation, add
    floor(BKS/3) if flag is set. In slow/dread, add 3 to duration.

L4 — "Double Writ" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    You can now inscribe TWO Writs on the same enemy simultaneously. The second
    Writ slot is entity.writ_2: str | None. When targeting, the first press
    writes to writ (slot 1); the second press to writ_2. Both detonate
    independently on their trigger conditions. If the same Writ type is applied
    twice, the second overwrites the first in that slot.
    Additionally, when BOTH Writs on the same enemy detonate within the same turn,
    a bonus effect fires: the Double Tap — applies cripple_armor (10 turns) to
    the target on top of the two individual detonations.
  impl notes:
    entity.writ_2: str | None (new field alongside entity.writ).
    Writ application: UI prompts slot choice (1 or 2) after type choice, or
      simpler: writing to an already-written slot overwrites, writing to an
      empty writ writes to writ, if writ is full writes to writ_2.
    Double Tap: engine tracks a per-turn detonation counter per entity
      (entity.writs_detonated_this_turn: int, reset to 0 each turn). If
      count >= 2 after any detonation in the same turn, apply cripple_armor.

L5 — "The Hex Permanent" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Passive capstone. Any Writ that detonates on an enemy that is ALREADY
    afflicted with at least 2 other status effects (at time of detonation) is
    permanently upgraded: its effects are doubled. Pain: damage x2. Slow:
    ratio halved (from 0.5 to 0.25, near-frozen). Dread: fear lasts 16 turns
    and is irresistible (cannot be broken by the normal 50% damage break chance).
    Message on trigger: "[Writ] hexed permanent!"
    Intent: rewards stacking multiple debuffs BEFORE detonating a Writ.
    Pairs with gouge (Stabbing L1), throw bottle (Alcoholism L3), cripple_armor
    from any source, etc.
  impl notes:
    In each Writ detonation branch: count non-expired effects on entity at
    moment of detonation. If len >= 2: apply the upgraded version.
    For irresistible fear: add a flag to FearEffect __init__: irresistible=False.
    modify_incoming_damage override: if self.irresistible, skip the 50% break
    check entirely.
  balance: Requires 2+ pre-existing debuffs at detonation time. In practice
    this means dot + slow, or gouge + bleeding — achievable but requires
    planning. Permanent slow (0.25 ratio) on a boss is nearly as good as a
    multi-turn stun.

DESIGN C — "SOUL TAX" (Punishment / Spiritual Economy)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Soul Tax is about spiritual punishment — you levy a cost on enemies for BEING
alive, and then escalate that cost with hexes. The core loop: curse an enemy
with Soul Tax (a drain-over-time), then cast hex amplifiers that make the drain
compound. Later levels let you redirect that drained spiritual energy back into
yourself as temporary power. It has an economic flavor — you are literally
extracting value from enemies' existence.

PLAYSTYLE: Attrition / snowball. Best in prolonged fights. Weak burst, strong
sustained output. Works well combined with slow/crowd control to keep enemies
alive longer while they bleed out spiritually. Synergizes heavily with DoT
stacking from Smoking, Stabbing (bleeding), and chemical effects.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Tolerance (drain rate scales with TOL). Constitution (the
"reserves" that hold absorbed energy translate to temporary HP).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Levy" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "levy"}
  ability_id: "levy"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Apply the Soul Tax curse to a visible enemy: entity gains soul_tax effect
    (debuff, 15 turns). Soul Tax ticks once per turn: enemy takes 1 damage
    (no defense, no message spam — silent drain). This damage generates a
    "spiritual drain" counter on the player: engine.soul_drain_counter += 1
    each tick. Counter does nothing at L1 but becomes fuel at L2+.
    Only one enemy can be Soul Taxed at a time; reapplication moves the curse
    to the new target (old target's effect expires immediately).
  impl notes:
    New SoulTaxEffect in effects.py:
      id = "soul_tax"
      category = "debuff"
      on_reapply: refresh duration (do not stack — only one per game allowed
        at a time; engine enforces only-one constraint before applying).
      tick: entity.take_damage(1) bypassing defense; engine.soul_drain_counter += 1.
    engine.soul_drain_counter: int (persists per floor, resets on floor change).
    Only-one constraint: before apply_effect("soul_tax", target), engine
      scans all dungeon entities and removes existing soul_tax effect from any
      that have it.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +10 per soul_tax tick (slow steady gain rewards keeping
      the enemy alive and taxed).

L2 — "Compound Interest" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Soul Tax damage scales with how many OTHER debuffs are on the Soul Taxed
    enemy. For each additional debuff/status effect on the target (not counting
    soul_tax itself), Soul Tax deals +1 damage per tick. At 0 other debuffs:
    1 damage/tick. At 3 other debuffs: 4 damage/tick. At 5 other debuffs:
    6 damage/tick. This makes any other debuff source (gouge, slow, bleed,
    fear, dot, burning) directly amplify your curse.
  impl notes:
    In SoulTaxEffect.tick(): count len([e for e in entity.status_effects if
      e.id != "soul_tax" and not e.expired]) as extra_debuffs; deal
      (1 + extra_debuffs) damage instead of flat 1.
    The "no defense" rule still applies — pure HP drain regardless of armor.

L3 — "Cash Out" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "cash_out"}
  ability_id: "cash_out"
  target_type: SELF
  charge_type: PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 3
  desc:
    Convert engine.soul_drain_counter into temporary healing. Consume ALL
    current soul_drain_counter points: heal for ceil(soul_drain_counter / 3) HP
    (capped at 30 HP per use). Counter resets to 0 after cash-out.
    For the next 5 turns after cashing out, player gains +TOL/2 power bonus
    (spiritual energy converted to physical aggression — temporary CashOutEffect
    buff stored on player).
    Message: "Spiritual energy converted. +" [HP] "HP. Power surge (5 turns)."
  impl notes:
    New CashOutEffect(Effect): category="buff", duration=5.
    apply: engine.player.power += engine.player_stats.effective_tolerance // 2
    expire: engine.player.power -= stored_bonus
    Cash Out execute: heal = min(30, ceil(engine.soul_drain_counter / 3));
      engine.player.heal(heal); apply CashOutEffect; engine.soul_drain_counter = 0.
    Validation: if soul_drain_counter == 0, message "Nothing to cash out."
    3 uses/floor keeps burst healing spammable but not infinite.

L4 — "Interest Rate Hike" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When the Soul Taxed enemy dies (from any source while soul_tax is active),
    the Soul Tax JUMPS to a random nearby living enemy within 4 tiles at full
    duration (15 turns) automatically. No charge cost, no action. Chain length
    tracked: engine.soul_tax_chain: int (increments on each auto-transfer).
    For each link in the chain, the auto-transferred Soul Tax deals +1 base
    damage per tick (compounding: chain 1 = 2 dmg/tick base, chain 2 = 3, etc.,
    before Compound Interest multiplier). Chain resets on floor change.
    Message on chain: "Soul Tax transfers! (Chain [n])"
  impl notes:
    In "entity_died" event handler: check if dying entity had soul_tax effect.
    If so, engine.soul_tax_chain += 1; find random living non-player entity
      within Chebyshev(4); apply_effect("soul_tax", target, duration=15,
      chain_bonus=engine.soul_tax_chain).
    SoulTaxEffect needs to accept chain_bonus in __init__ and add it to tick
    damage (before Compound Interest: base = 1 + chain_bonus + extra_debuffs).
  balance: Chain 3+ becomes genuinely scary — 4+ base drain on each tick
    plus Compound Interest with any debuffs. Hard to engineer long chains
    because enemies die faster the longer the chain runs. Natural cap.

L5 — "You Owe Me" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When Soul Tax expires naturally (not from enemy death, not transferred via
    Interest Rate Hike — only natural duration lapse), a final reckoning fires:
    the target instantly takes damage equal to (total_ticks_taxed * 2) where
    total_ticks_taxed is tracked on the SoulTaxEffect instance. This is the
    penalty for outlasting the tax period — the spiritual debt comes due.
    Additionally: the soul_drain_counter refund for that final burst damage
    is tripled (each point of that burst adds 3 to soul_drain_counter instead
    of going to engine normally).
    Message on trigger: "[Enemy] pays the debt."
  impl notes:
    SoulTaxEffect: add self.ticks_taxed: int (increments in tick() alongside
      damage application). Add self.natural_expire: bool = False.
    Override expire(): if self.natural_expire and entity.alive:
      burst = self.ticks_taxed * 2; entity.take_damage(burst) bypass defense;
      engine.soul_drain_counter += burst * 3;
      engine.messages.append(f"{entity.name} pays the debt.")
    In tick(): if self.duration hits 1 (last tick before expiry), set
      self.natural_expire = True.
    If entity dies before expiry, natural_expire stays False so burst doesn't
    double-trigger through the death event chain.
  balance: At 15 ticks taxed with no interruption: 30 bonus damage on expire.
    Against a slow/feared enemy with 5 compound ticks, full 15 turns = massive.
    But the player must NOT kill the enemy early, which creates tension — do
    you cash out the drain now, or wait for the debt to mature?

DESIGN D — "EVIL EYE" (Reactive Hex / Punishing Aggression)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
The Evil Eye is a reactive curse tradition — you curse enemies for their
OWN ACTIONS. Attacking you, moving toward you, being near another enemy —
all become punishable offenses. This design is fundamentally defensive and
AOE-flavored: the curses trigger automatically from enemy behavior, and the
hexes amplify how bad those triggers get. It turns combat from "I attack
enemies" into "enemies attack themselves."

PLAYSTYLE: Defensive positioning, crowd punishment. Best against swarms.
Weak in one-on-one straight fights. Strongest when cornered or surrounded.
Synergizes with fear effects (keeping enemies moving toward you so they
keep triggering curses), and with any AOE damage source.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Swagger (the Eye's "gaze" — higher swagger = wider trigger
radius and stronger fear component). Constitution (HP thresholds for triggers).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    whenever the afflicted enemy takes any damage from any source, they take
    +2 additional spiritual damage (no defense). Essentially the gaze amplifies

    3 uses per floor.
  impl notes:
    New EvilEyeAuraEffect (buff on player):
      id = "evil_eye_aura"
      category = "buff"
      duration = 12

    New EvilGazeEffect (debuff on enemy):
      id = "evil_gaze"
      modify_incoming_damage: return damage + 2 (flat +2 to all incoming)

L3 — "That's On You" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Passive hex amplifier: when an enemy with evil_gaze ATTACKS (deals melee
    damage to ANY entity, not just the player — including friendly fire between
    enemies), the +2 amplifier damage is doubled to +4 for that single hit.
    Additionally, if the attack misses (does 0 damage), the evil_gaze still
    triggers a 1-damage spiritual tick — you never get completely clean even

    Message on missed trigger: "[Enemy] flinches under the gaze."
  impl notes:
    In EvilGazeEffect: add a flag is_attacking_turn: bool (reset each turn).

    In modify_incoming_damage on the DEFENDER entity (not the gazer) — wait,

      if attacker has evil_gaze, add 2 more to the damage they receive (not
      the defender). "Backfire" mechanic — they hurt themselves spiritually by
      attacking.
    Revised impl: when combat.resolve(attacker, defender, damage):
      if attacker.has_effect("evil_gaze"): attacker.take_damage(2) [bypass def];
      if damage == 0 and attacker.has_effect("evil_gaze"): attacker.take_damage(1).
    "That's On You" upgrade: gaze damage when the GAZER attacks = 4 instead of 2.

L4 — "Mal De Ojo" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "mal_de_ojo"}
  ability_id: "mal_de_ojo"
  target_type: SELF
  charge_type: PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 1
  desc:

  impl notes:
    execute(engine): for entity in engine.dungeon.entities:
      if entity.alive and entity.entity_type == "monster"
      and engine.dungeon.visible[entity.y][entity.x]:
        apply_effect("evil_gaze", entity, duration=12);
        apply_effect("stun", entity, duration=2).
    Stun applied after gaze so the first stun tick won't have the gaze
    amplifier — intentional (prevents instant burst from simultaneous triggers).
  balance: 1/floor. All visible enemies = potentially 6-10 targets on a busy
    floor. The stun gives 2 free turns of no incoming damage. Combined with
    the gaze, the moment stun expires, every enemy attacking you or charging
    you takes self-damage. Strong but limited.

L5 — "Generational Curse" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    The most terrifying hex. When an enemy with evil_gaze dies (from any
    source while gazing), the gaze does not simply expire — it TRANSFERS to
    every living enemy within 3 tiles of the dead enemy, at 5 turns duration.
    No cap on transfers from a single death. If all 5 enemies around a gazer
    die, all 5 get cursed. The message: "[Enemy] passes the curse."
    Additionally, any enemy that receives the Generational Curse spread version
    cannot have the gaze removed by death propagation more than once (they can
    still pass it on death, but you don't get XP for the spread kills' XP —
    the generational gaze is spiritually exhausted after one generation of
    passing. Prevents infinite chain abuse.)
  impl notes:
    EvilGazeEffect: add self.is_generational: bool = False.
    In "entity_died" event: if entity has evil_gaze and not is_generational:
      for each living entity within Chebyshev(3): apply_effect("evil_gaze",
        nearby, duration=5, is_generational=True).
    EvilGazeEffect.on_apply(): if self.is_generational: skip Blackkk Magic XP
      (only original gaze applications earn XP).
    Edge case: if generational curse targets are themselves close to each other,
      they can all trigger on death but their propagations will have
      is_generational=True so they don't chain further.
  balance: The radius is small (3 tiles) and duration short (5 turns). In
    practice you get 1-2 spread targets per death in most room layouts. The
    no-double-generation rule prevents exponential spread. But in a packed room
    the first wave of deaths can curse an entire crowd — scary in the right
    situation.

================================================================================
DESIGN E — "JINX JUGGLING" (Multi-Curse / Combo Hex)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
You maintain a small arsenal of specific Jinxes (named curses, each with a
distinct flavor and mechanical effect). The power comes from STACKING different
Jinxes on the same target — each Jinx interacts with every other Jinx on the
same enemy in a specified combo reaction. You're a curse chef: the individual
ingredients are mild, but the combinations are volatile. Design rewards
experimenting with curse combinations and learning the combo chart.

PLAYSTYLE: Multi-tool, knowledge-based. Rewards memorizing which Jinxes combo.
Best against elite/boss enemies where you have time to stack. Usable against
groups by spreading Jinxes across multiple targets. Most complex of the 5
designs but highest ceiling.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Book Smarts (combo detonation damage scales with BKS). Street
Smarts (jinx accuracy — STS% chance a jinx application doesn't consume a charge
but succeeds anyway, representing "reading" the target).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Two-For-One" (activated ability — Jinx: Broke)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "jinx_broke"}
  ability_id: "jinx_broke"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Apply Jinx: Broke to a visible enemy. Jinx: Broke is an active debuff
    (entity.jinxes: set of str, add "broke" to it, 12 turns). Broke effect:
    the enemy's effective defense stat is halved (round down). Just that.
    Mild on its own, but combos with every other Jinx. One target can have
    multiple Jinxes simultaneously (all stored in entity.jinxes set).
    All Jinxes share a single duration tracker per enemy:
      entity.jinx_duration: int — applying any Jinx resets to max(existing, 12).
    When duration expires, ALL Jinxes on that enemy clear at once.
  impl notes:
    entity.jinxes: set[str] = set() (new field, default empty set)
    entity.jinx_duration: int = 0
    entity.jinx_combo_flags: set[str] = set() — tracks which combos already fired
      this floor (prevents re-triggering the same combo over and over)
    JinxEffect (base class, handles shared duration):
      id = "jinxed"  — one effect entry for all jinxes, displays "Jinxed (n)"
      tick: entity.jinx_duration -= 1; if <= 0: entity.jinxes.clear()
    "Broke" effect on entity: entity.defense = max(0, entity.defense // 2)
      applied on jinx application, reversed on jinx clear or Broke removal.
    Actually simpler: compute effective defense in combat.py as
      entity.defense if "broke" not in entity.jinxes else entity.defense // 2.
    Blackkk Magic XP: on each successful Jinx application.

L2 — "Jinx: Jumpy" (passive — second Jinx type)

  effect: None
  desc:

  impl notes:

L3 — "Jinx: Snakebit" (passive — third Jinx + two new combos)
  perk_type: "passive"

================================================================================
BALANCE OVERVIEW — ALL DESIGNS
================================================================================

Design A (The Mark): Single-target focus. Strong vs bosses. Triple 6 Special
  deals up to 36 no-defense damage at max tier — comparable to a full Negromancy
  Reap (which costs soul shards). Balanced by setup requirement.

Design B (Writ Large): Setup-detonation. Each Writ detonation is ~(4 + BKS/2)
  damage no-defense at L1. At BKS 12: 10 damage per melee hit that triggers it.
  Good mid-game payoff. L5 doubling with 2 debuffs turns Writ of Pain into ~20
  no-defense per trigger — strong but requires juggling.

Design C (Soul Tax): Attrition tree. Not burst-oriented. 1-4 damage/tick over
  15 turns = 15-60 total drain, comparable to DoT damage from existing effects.
  Cash Out caps at 30 HP restored per use — roughly equivalent to a mid-tier
  food heal. Tree shines in long fights.

Design D (Evil Eye): Defensive tree. Evil Gaze +2 bonus is modest per hit but
  across a swarm of 6-8 enemies all gaining the gaze in one Mal De Ojo cast,
  the collective backfire damage is significant. Generational spread is the
  "I win a crowded room" button.

Design E (Jinx Juggling): Highest ceiling. "Getting Crossed" at BKS 12 = 24 no-
  defense damage + stun 3t + fear 8t + cripple armor 10t at 30% HP. Against a
  boss this is a death sentence. Hardest to execute — requires patience and
  multi-step setup.
