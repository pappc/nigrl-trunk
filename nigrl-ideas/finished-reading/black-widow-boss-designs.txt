NIGRL — Black Widow Mini-Boss: Three Variations
================================================
Date: 2026-03-23
Zone: Crack Den (zone_floors 1-2, floors 2-3)
Event: spider_infestation
Context: Three distinct mini-boss concepts for the spider event floor.
  These are alternatives/variations to The Landlord concept (spider-enemy-roster.txt).
  One of these would be chosen as the actual implementation, or they could serve
  as a revision/redesign menu. Each occupies the same structural role: one guaranteed
  mini-boss room per spider event floor, the climactic fight of the event.

Reference stats for this design space:
  Zone damage mult: 0.5x (crack_den)
  Player HP range: ~70-130 at these floors
  Wolf Spider effective damage: 3-6/hit — these bosses should hit ~4-8/hit after mult
  Target HP: ~80 (base_hp + con*5; e.g. base_hp=30 + con(8,10) gives 70-80)
  At crack den 0.5x mult, 80 HP is a 160-HP equivalent fight by raw damage — it's meaty
  Existing Sac Spider webbed effect: -25 energy/tick, 10t
  Existing web_stuck: 50% escape per attempt, auto-escape at 5 failures
  EffectKinds available: DOT, STUN, FEAR, CHILD_SUPPORT, MOGGED, WELL_FED,
    RAD_BURST, TOX_BURST, DEPORT, RABIES, BUFF_PURGE, INFECTION
  New effects referenced: each variation spec'd new Effect subclasses needed

Arachnigga note:
  Any player with Arachnigga skill has web immunity (cannot be web_stuck).
  This is a significant power differential on the spider floor. Boss designs
  must be dangerous to BOTH Arachnigga and non-Arachnigga builds.
  The "signature nasty debuff" on each variation is INDEPENDENT of web mechanics
  so it hits Arachnigga players equally hard.

===========================================================================
VARIATION A — "THE RECLUSIVE" (Sustained Pressure)
===========================================================================

Concept:
  The spider that has been in the walls the longest. Not the biggest or the
  fastest — the most venomous. A matriarch black widow the size of a dinner
  plate, abdomen swollen with hemotoxin refined over years of Crack Den
  chemistry exposure. She hits you once, and the poison stays. The fight
  is a race. She has time. You don't.

  Design pillar: SUSTAINED PRESSURE. The fight isn't dangerous in single
  moments — it's dangerous because the damage keeps compounding. The
  player who goes in healthy can survive five minutes if they're careful.
  The player who goes in already hurt from the floor is on a hard timer.

  Distinct from the other variations: no burst, no CC, no repositioning.
  Pure escalating venom attrition. She doesn't need to stun you. She just
  needs to bite you enough times.

Registry key: the_reclusive
char: 'W'   -- uppercase W, suggests widow, large body; distinct from wolf 'w'
color: (180, 20, 20)   -- deep blood red; darker and more saturated than pipe spider's (200,50,50)

  or gets too close while fighting The Reclusive. The tension is: do you
  clear them first (safe but slow, spending time in her room), or do you
  go directly for her (fast but you may accidentally aggro both recluses
  mid-fight)?

--- SIGNATURE NASTY DEBUFF: NECROTIC STACK ---

New effect needed: NeuroVenomEffect (id: "neuro_venom")
  Category: debuff
  Display name: "Neurotoxin (Xd)" where X is stack count

  Mechanism:
  - Each stack is an independent timer that deals 2 dmg/turn for 12 turns
  - On reapply: ADDS a new 12-turn timer alongside existing stacks (true stacking,
    not refresh)
  - Stack count display shows total active stacks
  - Unlike DotEffect, multiple stacks deal cumulative damage per tick:
    e.g. 3 stacks = 6 dmg/turn, 12 turns each
  - Each stack is removed independently when its timer expires
  - Maximum useful stacks: unlimited, but 5+ stacks (10+ dmg/turn) is
    functionally lethal without healing

  Implementation notes:
  - Internally stores list of timers: self.timers: list[int]
  - tick(): decrement all timers, deal len(timers)*2 damage, remove zeros
  - on_reapply(): always appends a new timer=12 (true stacking)
  - This is a NEW pattern in effects.py — most effects either stack by
    refreshing duration OR by incrementing a counter. NeuroVenomEffect uses
    independent timer lists like IgniteEffect does (see effects.py IgniteEffect
    for the reference implementation of the list-of-timers pattern).

Why this is the scary one:
  Every hit has a chance to add a permanent 12-turn DOT stack. These
  don't refresh each other — they pile up. By turn 6 of the fight, if
  the player has taken several hits, they may have 2-4 stacks ticking
  simultaneously (4-8 dmg/turn from venom alone, plus however many other
  venom DOTs accumulated from the floor's other spiders). The player is
  not losing HP from one sudden explosion. They are slowly drowning in
  venom while trying to deal 70-80 effective HP of damage with defense 1-2.

  This is the sustained pressure fantasy. The fight asks: how well
  did you manage your resources on the way here?

SpecialAttack(

    damage_mult=1.4,

  Widow Bite fires 35% of turns. Each fire adds a fresh 12-turn neuro_venom
  stack. The 1.4x damage multiplier means the hit itself also hurts —
  this is not a 0-damage setup move. It hits AND voids.

  Over a 10-attack fight: 97% chance to fire at least 3 times.
  3 stacks of neuro_venom = 6 dmg/turn if all active simultaneously.
  Expect 3-4 stacks in a full fight from Widow Bite alone.

SpecialAttack(
    name="Silk Smear",
    chance=0.15,
    damage_mult=0.5,
    on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
        name="Silk Smear",
        kind=EffectKind.STUN,
        amount=0,
        duration=1,
        chance=1.0,
    ),
)

  Silk Smear: low-damage hit that stuns for exactly 1 turn. This is not
  the heavy stun of the Orb Weaver's 2-3 turn stuns. It's one free attack
  she gets while you're dazed. This fires 15% of the time — one in seven
  attacks. Not reliable, not the focus. Just a reminder that she can.

  Crucially: 1 turn of stun during active NeuroVenomEffect is 1 turn of
  receiving her follow-up normal attack, PLUS 2*stacks venom damage. The
  stun is scarier the longer the fight has gone.

  Base attack also applies neuro_venom at 30% chance. Combined with Widow

--- DEATH BEHAVIOR ---

death_split_type:  "pipe_spider"
death_split_count: 4

  Four Pipe Spiders emerge on death. Classic Brood Mother-style cleanup.
  The player is almost certainly carrying 2-4 active neuro_venom stacks
  at this point. The death-split spiders arrive while the venom is still
  ticking. Resource check: kill four spiders or use healing. The
  neuro_venom stacks naturally expire over 12 turns but that's 12 turns
  of 4-8 dmg/turn during cleanup — 48-96 additional damage.

  Consider: a developer may want to implement the neuro_venom stack so
  that there IS a counter or cure item (anti-venom, benadryl, etc.) that
  clears all stacks. This would provide meaningful resource decision-making
  and make the item pool relevant to the fight outcome.

death_drop_chance: 1.0
death_drop_table: ["mature_spider_egg", "benadryl"]

  Always drops a spider egg (thematic) and benadryl (anti-venom, practical).
  The benadryl drop on death is a consolation prize — if the player
  survives the fight with venom stacks ticking, they immediately receive
  a cure. The timing matters: Benadryl helps the cleanup phase, not the
  fight itself. This is good roguelike loot timing.

cash_drop: (10, 30)

--- ARACHNIGGA INTERACTION ---

  The Reclusive does NOT use web-based attacks. The neuro_venom stacking
  threat is identical for Arachnigga and non-Arachnigga players. However:
  - Arachnigga L3+ Spiderlings can apply steady chip damage while the player
    kites, reducing the number of hits the player takes — directly reducing
    the rate at which neuro_venom stacks accumulate.
  - Arachnigga Tree B "Venom Dealer" creates a philosophical tension: the
    player has their own venom DOT on The Reclusive, which races against
    the boss's venom on the player. Both combatants are slowly poisoning
    each other. Good emergent drama.
  - Web Snare (Tree A L3) does NOT help directly — The Reclusive moves at
    speed 95, so it won't escape. But pinning her while spiderlings attack
    is efficient.

--- FLOOR ROOM SETUP ---

  The Reclusive's room: medium density web cluster (4-6 cobwebs).
  Web placement: along the walls and in two corners — she lives in the
  corners, like a real black widow. The room should NOT be the web-dense
  maze that The Landlord uses. The Reclusive's danger is her venom, not
  her room. Players can pick their path through the room.

  Escort recluses: place 1-2 tiles before the room entrance (in the
  hallway) as ambush. The rest inside. This simulates a natural
  spider distribution across a neglected apartment.

VARIATION B — "MAMA EIGHT" (Burst Danger)
===========================================================================

Concept:
  The one that found a Crack Den chemistry experiment. She's been living
  near a crack pipe for three years and something happened to her venom.
  It's different now. Concentrated. When she bites, you don't get slow
  poison. You get a sudden, catastrophic whole-body event. She's actually
  smaller than you'd expect for something this dangerous. They usually are.

  Design pillar: BURST DANGER. Unlike The Reclusive's grinding attrition,
  Mama Eight creates sudden dramatic spikes. Most of the fight she's
  manageable. Then she lands ONE specific attack and the next 5 turns are
  an emergency. The player is playing around specific attack windows,
  not around sustained pressure.

  Distinct from other variations: erratic behavior, unpredictable speed,
  and a signature debuff that inverts the usual "fight or flight" calculus —
  the debuff makes RUNNING dangerous instead of safe.

Registry key: mama_eight

color: (200, 100, 30)   -- burnt orange; like a black widow that ate something chemical

  not let you pick your targets.

--- SIGNATURE NASTY DEBUFF: VENOM PANIC ---

New effect needed: VenomPanicEffect (id: "venom_panic")
  Category: debuff
  Display name: "Venom Panic (Xt)" where X is remaining turns
  Duration: 8 turns
  Priority: 50 (above most effects, below stun)

  Mechanism:
  - While Venom Panic is active, the player CANNOT WAIT (skip turn).
    Every turn the player must take an action — move or attack.
    Waiting costs 3 HP (active choice to stop moving = the venom surges).
  - Additionally: 2 DOT per turn for 8 turns (16 total)
  - Reapply: refreshes duration, does NOT stack the DOT

  Implementation notes:
  - The "no waiting" mechanic requires a hook in engine.py's handle_wait()
    (or the equivalent rest/wait action handler). Before allowing wait:
    check if player has "venom_panic" in status_effects. If yes: apply
    3 HP, post message "The venom surges — you can't stop moving! (-3 HP)",
    and process the wait action as usual (costs energy). The wait is not
    BLOCKED — the player CAN wait — but it costs HP to do so.
  - This avoids issues with "player gets stuck" (not a stun). They
    always have agency. The cost is just higher.
  - The DOT component is handled in tick() — standard 2 dmg/turn.
  - modify_movement hook: no change. Player moves freely.
  - before_turn hook: returns False (does not skip turn — player chooses).
  - The "wait costs HP" hook is an engine-level check, not an Effect hook.
    Suggest implementing as: in engine.handle_wait(), if any effect has
    attribute punish_wait_cost (new int field on VenomPanicEffect set to 3),
    apply that damage before processing the wait.

Why this is the scary one:
  Roguelikes are about information control and positioning. "Wait" is a
  fundamental tool: heal a turn, let an enemy come to you, time a potion.
  Venom Panic removes that tool. While panicked, the player must always
  be moving — toward the spider, away from it, or attacking. The player
  cannot stand still and drink a potion safely (well, they can, but they
  pay 3 HP per turn while doing it). This forces suboptimal positioning.
  In a cramped room with Wolf Spider escorts present, "you must keep moving"
  transforms the space from manageable to chaos.

  Non-Arachnigga players feel this acutely when web_stuck — trapped and
  panicking, each turn in the web costs 3 HP if they wait, or they risk
  wasted escape attempts from the web. The combo of web_stuck + venom_panic
  is extremely nasty.

--- SPECIAL ATTACKS ---

SpecialAttack(
    name="Crack Bite",
    chance=0.30,
    damage_mult=1.8,
    on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
        name="Venom Panic",
        kind=EffectKind.DOT,   -- DOT is the closest existing kind;
                                -- VenomPanicEffect replaces it at effect layer
        amount=2,
        duration=8,
        chance=1.0,
    ),
)

  Crack Bite: her signature attack. 30% chance to fire. 1.8x damage PLUS
  the Venom Panic debuff. A hit from Crack Bite is a sudden gear-shift in
  the fight: big damage, then 8 turns of forced movement + 2 DOT/turn.
  At speed 130 she will attack approximately every 0.77 player turns.
  Over a 12-turn fight: Crack Bite fires ~3.5 times on average.
  First proc: manageable (resources likely up). Third proc with panic
  still ticking from first: catastrophic.

  The 1.8x multiplier at effective 4-7 = 7-12 effective damage per Crack
  Bite hit. Three Crack Bites over a fight: 21-36 raw damage from the
  special attacks alone, plus 8-24 DOT from the panic ticks.

SpecialAttack(
    name="Scuttle",
    chance=0.20,
    damage_mult=0.0,
    on_hit_effect=None,
)

  Scuttle: a zero-damage repositioning move. When this fires, the spider
  teleports 2-4 tiles in a random valid direction (not into walls, not
  off-screen). No damage, no effect — just movement disruption.

  Implementation: damage_mult=0.0 produces 0 damage. The on_hit_effect
  is None. The ACTUAL behavior (random teleport) must be implemented in
  the special attack dispatch in engine.py: when special_attack.name ==
  "Scuttle", instead of applying damage, teleport the monster to a random
  valid tile within Chebyshev distance 4. Use the existing blink_charges
  teleport logic as a reference (see enemies.py blink_charges field and
  its dispatch in engine.py).

  Why Scuttle exists: Mama Eight is fast but not untouchable. Scuttle
  creates unpredictability. You lined up a ranged shot — she moved. You
  committed to melee — she's on the other side of the room now. Combined
  with Venom Panic (you can't just wait for her to come back), the player
  has to constantly re-chase. This is the "frustrating in the best way"
  variant. Wolf Spider escorts do not move out of the way when she Scuttles —
  she may reappear next to them, creating dangerous new clusters.

--- ARACHNIGGA INTERACTION ---

  Mama Eight and Arachnigga have interesting friction:
  - Arachnigga web immunity: HELPS with the Wolf Spider escorts (who may
    trigger cobweb hazards in the room), but Mama Eight herself doesn't
    use webs. Web immunity is a nice quality-of-life advantage.
  - Venom Panic: NOT affected by web immunity. Arachnigga players are
    just as panicked as anyone else.
  - The web_snare ability (SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, CD 8) can STOP Scuttle
    mid-fight by webbing Mama Eight — web_stuck prevents movement.
    This creates a satisfying counterplay: "I pinned the thing that keeps
    teleporting." However, web_snare is on an 8-turn cooldown. Use it
    wisely.
  - Arachnigga spiderlings chase Mama Eight even through her Scuttles,
    since they use MEANDER AI (approach nearest enemy). She can't escape
    the entire swarm. This makes Arachnigga particularly strong vs Mama
    Eight specifically.

--- FLOOR ROOM SETUP ---

  Mama Eight's room: open-floor priority. Fewer cobwebs than The Reclusive
  variant (2-3), more floor space. She needs room to Scuttle. The danger
  here is not terrain — it's her speed and repositioning.

  Wolf Spider escorts spawn inside the room. Unlike The Reclusive's passive-
  until-hit recluses, these are active as soon as the player enters sight.
  There is no "smart approach" to this room. It is a brawl.

VARIATION C — "THE WIDOW QUEEN" (Control / Denial)
===========================================================================

Concept:
  The original. The one that was here before the Crack Den was a Crack Den.
  She was here when it was an apartment building. She was here when it was
  a boarding house. She is very, very old and her web covers most of this
  floor's infrastructure. You are not fighting a spider. You are trespassing
  in a spider's home.

  Design pillar: CONTROL AND DENIAL. The Widow Queen fights by rearranging
  the battlefield against the player. She doesn't need to hit hard — she
  makes hitting her difficult. She doesn't need to be fast — she controls
  what tiles are safe to stand on. The fight is about finding and holding
  the few tiles that aren't her territory, while dealing enough damage to
  kill something with 80 HP and 3 defense.

  Distinct from other variations: slow, methodical, defensive. The scariest
  thing about her is not what she does to you directly — it's what she
  does to the room. She is the environmental hazard as boss.

--- SIGNATURE NASTY DEBUFF: SILK BURIAL ---

New effect needed: SilkBurialEffect (id: "silk_burial")
  Category: debuff
  Display name: "Silk Burial (Xt)"
  Duration: 12 turns
  Priority: 70 (high)

  Mechanism:
  - Applies -40 energy/tick (severe slow — worse than webbed's -25)
  - Also applies a 25% chance each turn to skip the player's action
    entirely ("You're wrapped in silk! You struggle against the bindings.")
    The skip chance represents silk accumulating around joints. It is NOT
    the same as stun — stun is binary (always skip), Silk Burial is
    probabilistic. The player has agency but an unreliable turn economy.
  - Reapply: EXTENDS duration by 6 turns (to max 18). Does not reset to
    12; it adds 6 more turns on top of current remaining.
  - The extension on reapply means if she hits you with it twice, you
    are under Silk Burial for potentially 18-24 turns total.

  Implementation notes:
  - modify_energy_gain hook: return max(1, amount - 40). Even more severe
    than SlowEffect at ratio=0.6 (which would be 60 energy at speed 100).
    At -40: player acts at 60 energy/tick vs enemy at 80 speed — the Widow
    Queen has effectively a 33% turn advantage despite being slower.
  - before_turn hook (for player): return random.random() < 0.25
    Wait — before_turn is a monster hook. For player, the skip logic
    requires an engine-level check in _run_energy_loop or the player action
    processing phase. Suggest: in engine.py where the player takes their
    turn, check for any effect with attribute chance_to_skip_turn (float).
    If random < that chance, consume the player's energy (treat as wasted
    turn), post message, and return to energy loop. VenomPanicEffect's
    "wait cost" and SilkBurialEffect's "skip chance" are BOTH engine-level
    hooks — add a consistent API for this (e.g. effect.on_player_turn_start()
    returning True to skip the turn).
  - duration extension on reapply (not standard): override on_reapply to
    add 6 to existing.duration (capped at 18) rather than max comparison.

Why this is the scary one:
  -40 energy/tick is debilitating on its own. At 60 energy/tick, the player
  acts only 60% as often as normal — but the 25% skip chance means effective
  action rate is 60% * 75% = 45% of baseline. The Widow Queen at speed 80
  now acts MORE than the player. A 12-18 turn Silk Burial during the fight
  means the player takes many more hits than expected. Her low damage seems
  fine until you realize you're taking it twice as often as you planned.

  This is the control/denial fantasy. The player who respects the debuff
  uses items to cure it (if available). The player who tries to muscle
  through it discovers that "I'll just heal through it" falls apart when
  your turn economy halves.

  Crucially: this is a TIMING debuff, not a damage debuff. The player still
  has health. They can still fight. They just can't keep up with the room.

--- SPECIAL ATTACKS ---

SpecialAttack(
    name="Weave",
    chance=0.35,
    damage_mult=0.0,
    on_hit_effect=None,
)

  Weave is a ROOM MODIFICATION attack, not a damage attack. When Weave
  fires, the Widow Queen places 2-3 cobweb entities on random valid floor
  tiles within a 5-tile radius of herself. No damage. No player effect.
  She is literally re-weaving her room during the fight.

  Implementation: similar to Scuttle, this requires custom dispatch in
  engine.py: when special_attack.name == "Weave", use dungeon.spawn_cobweb()
  (or equivalent from hazards.py) for 2-3 tiles near the monster. If the
  tile is already a cobweb, skip it. The cobwebs persist as normal hazards.

  Why this matters: over a 10-attack fight, Weave fires ~3 times, placing
  6-9 new cobwebs on the battlefield in real time. The room, which started
  with 8-10 cobwebs pre-placed at floor gen, can END the fight with 14-19.
  Any tile that was briefly safe is now a web. Kiting the Widow Queen
  through the room means running into new cobwebs. There is nowhere safe
  to stand by turn 8.

  Arachnigga exception: cobwebs are neutral terrain for the player with
  web immunity. The Widow Queen's Weave attack ACTIVELY BENEFITS Arachnigga
  players who use the cobwebs offensively. This is the intended asymmetry —
  Arachnigga turns her battlefield control into a weapon against her.

SpecialAttack(
    name="Silk Burial",
    chance=0.25,
    damage_mult=0.8,
    on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
        name="Silk Burial",
        kind=EffectKind.STUN,   -- closest existing kind for display purposes;
                                 -- SilkBurialEffect replaces at effect layer
        amount=0,
        duration=12,
        chance=1.0,
    ),
)

  Silk Burial: her signature attack. 25% chance per attack. 0.8x damage
  (slightly reduced — she wraps you more carefully than she hits you) PLUS
  the SilkBurialEffect (12 turns, extendable). If she hits this twice
  (24% cumulative chance in a 10-attack fight for the second proc while
  first is active), duration extends to 18 turns. At 18 turns of Silk
  Burial, the fight is in serious danger of turning into a complete loss.

  Fires less often than Mama Eight's Crack Bite (25% vs 30%). Compensated
  by the duration extension on reapply — the FIRST proc is recoverable;
  the SECOND proc during the first is potentially fight-ending without
  an item response.

--- ON-HIT EFFECTS ---

OnHitEffect(
    name="Binding Silk",
    kind=EffectKind.STUN,   -- duration=1; one-turn micro-stun on every hit
    amount=0,
    duration=1,
    chance=0.20,
)

  20% chance on every basic hit for a 1-turn micro-stun. This is the
  same duration-1 stun used in The Reclusive variation's "Silk Smear."
  But here it serves a different purpose: in combination with the Widow
  Queen's Weave attack (which fires 35% of turns), the player is always
  at risk of a micro-stun followed immediately by Weave, during which two
  new cobwebs appear while they're frozen for one turn. Micro-stun + Weave
  combo creates 2 cobwebs the player had no ability to respond to.

  This is a background threat, not a headline one. It's the room-control
  version of the "chance to miss" effects — a tax on the player's
  positioning decisions.

--- DEATH BEHAVIOR ---

death_split_type:  "sac_spider"
death_split_count: 2

  Two Sac Spiders emerge on death. These are ROOM_GUARD AI — they do not
  chase. They spawn in the remnants of the Widow Queen's room and wait.
  The death-split Sac Spiders bring their web-shooting attack and can
  web the player mid-cleanup. A web during cleanup while Silk Burial is
  still ticking is a very bad situation. The player may need to wait for
  Silk Burial to expire (12+ turns) before safely entering to kill them —
  but that's 12 turns of the DOT from any remaining Silk Burial.

  The cleanup phase is the most deliberate of the three variations. No
  rush. Plan the approach. Use items if needed.

death_drop_chance: 1.0
death_drop_table: ["pipe_venom_antidote", "spider_silk"]

  "spider_silk": new material item suggestion. Could be used in crafting

  The death_drop_table should be implemented with existing item IDs until
  the new items are created. Fallback: ["benadryl", "kush"].

cash_drop: (8, 25)
  Less cash than the other two variations. She doesn't have money. She

--- ARACHNIGGA INTERACTION ---

  The Widow Queen is the most interesting Arachnigga interaction of the
  three variations:

  - Web immunity: the player is COMPLETELY immune to cobweb hazards,
    including all the pre-placed room webs AND the Weave-created webs.
    The densest web room in the game is freely traversable for Arachnigga.
    This is an enormous power advantage — approximately 8-19 cobwebs the
    non-Arachnigga player must navigate around.

  - Silk Burial: NOT blocked by web immunity. The debuff is on the player
    entity (reduced energy/skip chance), not a terrain effect. Arachnigga
    still gets Silk Burial'd.

  - Cobweb Warfare: Arachnigga players who use Tree A's lay_web or web_burst
    have an absurd combo opportunity. Place webs on tiles around the Widow
    Queen; her Sac Spider escorts walk into them (non-immune); the web_burst
    capstone ability detonates ALL pre-placed webs simultaneously. In a
    room with 10+ webs already seeded, a well-timed web_burst while the
    Widow Queen's escorts are positioned in them is a floor-clearing play.
    The Widow Queen is the Arachnigga tree's most thematic encounter. She
    is a spider you out-spider.

  - Spiderlings: they do not interact with webs (immune as allies).
    Against the slow-moving Widow Queen at speed 80, spiderlings are
    steady DPS sources that she can't outrun. Tree D's spiderling army
    is well-suited here.

--- FLOOR ROOM SETUP ---

  The Widow Queen's room is the most web-dense encounter in the game.
  Developer should implement specific spawn logic for this room:
    - 8-10 cobweb hazards pre-seeded throughout the room at floor gen
    - Cobwebs concentrated around the center (where she sits) and
      along the walls, leaving 2-3 clear floor tiles near the entrance
    - Sac Spider escorts placed INSIDE the web cluster, not at the entrance
    - The entrance corridor should have 1-2 cobweb hazards just before
      the doorway — web even before the room

  Message on entering sight: "Something ancient shifts in the silk. The
  walls here are entirely web."

===========================================================================
DESIGN NOTES FOR SELECTION
===========================================================================

  If this is the ONLY mini-boss variation (one per run):
    The three are mechanically distinct enough that the game is meaningfully
    different each run with a random selection. All three satisfy the brief
    (dreadful signature debuff, ~80 HP, spider-floor climactic fight).

  If only one can be chosen (fixed implementation):
    Recommendation: VARIATION A (The Reclusive) for the cleanest mechanics
    and most compatible implementation. NeuroVenomEffect follows an existing
    pattern (IgniteEffect's list-of-timers). No engine hooks needed beyond
    effects.py. However, Variation C (Widow Queen) has the most memorable
    design identity and best Arachnigga synergy if the engine hooks for
    SilkBurialEffect are acceptable to implement.

  Balance note on zone 0.5x damage multiplier:
    At crack_den 0.5x, all effective damages above are post-multiplier.
    The Reclusive's Widow Bite (3-5 base * 1.4 = 4.2-7 * 0.5 = 2.1-3.5)
    seems low but the neuro_venom stacking provides all the pressure.
    Mama Eight's Crack Bite (4-6 base * 1.8 = 7.2-10.8 * 0.5 = 3.6-5.4)
    is the highest single-hit threat. Widow Queen's base hit is negligible
    but her turn advantage from Silk Burial means she gets MORE attacks
    through the 0.5x multiplier than she "should." These are calibrated
    for Crack Den. If these bosses ever appear in a 1.0x zone they would
    be significantly more dangerous.
