================================================================================
BLACKKK MAGIC — 10 NEW SKILL TREE DESIGNS (C through L)
================================================================================
Designs A ("The Mark") and B ("Writ Large") are retained from the original doc.
This file replaces designs C, D, and E with 10 entirely new concepts (C–L).

XP Source (all designs): Blackkk Magic XP is earned when a curse/hex LANDS on
an enemy. Not on cast. Not on detonation unless the detonation also applies a
new curse. XP values specified per design.

Tone anchor: street voodoo. Evil eye, jinxes, bad juju, voodoo dolls, spiritual
hustle, ancestral vengeance. Not wizard school. Not D&D. These names get shouted.

Inspiration lenses baked in:
  WoW: Affliction dot synergies, Haunt travel-and-return, Drain Soul execute
       phase, Seed of Corruption detonation chain, Unstable Affliction dispel
       protection, Soulburn empowerment, Demonic Circle blink, Dark Pact
       self-damage-for-power.
  PoE: Hexblast (consume curse stacks), Doom (curses grow stronger over time),
       Profane Bloom (cursed death explosions), Bane (curse that scales with
       other curses), Impending Doom (curse EXPIRING triggers blast), Temporal
       Chains (time distortion), Punishment (retaliation curse), Malediction
       (damage amp per curse type), self-curse synergy.
  Novel: mechanics that do not exist in either game or NIGRL yet.

================================================================================
DESIGN C — "DOOM CLOCK" (Accumulating Curse Power Over Time)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Lifted from PoE's Doom mechanic and twisted into street voodoo. Every curse you
put on an enemy gains DOOM — a hidden per-curse power counter that increments
every turn the curse sits untriggered on the target. Doom makes curses hit harder
and last longer the longer you WAIT. The skill rewards patience and ANTI-burst
play: applying the curse and then NOT detonating it, letting it fester until the
doom value is maxed. This inverts the normal roguelike instinct to "deal all
damage now." A skilled player learns the doom cap (5 stacks), times a Hexburst
(unlocked at L3) to consume all doom at once, and watches elites simply crumble.

PLAYSTYLE: Marination. You are the cook, the enemy is the meat. Apply curses,
then fight normally while doom accumulates, then cash in. Rewards patience and
pre-planning. Weak against enemies that die in 1-2 turns (no doom time). Dominant
against mini-bosses and named enemies that survive 5+ turns of setup.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Book Smarts (doom scaling base, Hexburst damage multiplier).
Street Smarts (curse application proc rate from passive sources).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Put the Curse On" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "put_the_curse_on"}
  ability_id: "put_the_curse_on"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Afflict a visible enemy with the Doom Curse (entity.doom_curse = True,
    entity.doom_stacks = 0, floor_duration). Each turn the target is alive and
    the Doom Curse is active, doom_stacks increments by +1 (max 5). Doom stacks
    do nothing at L1 except display as "Doomed (x)" in the status panel, rendered
    as a faint number over the enemy. This is a PROMISE — the payoff comes at L3.
    Reapplying to the same target refreshes the floor_duration but does NOT reset
    doom_stacks. Reapplying to a new target removes the Doom Curse from the old
    target (doom is singular — one enemy at a time).
  impl notes:
    entity.doom_curse: bool
    entity.doom_stacks: int (0-5)
    Each turn in the energy loop, after monster actions: for each dungeon entity
      with doom_curse == True and alive == True: if doom_stacks < 5: doom_stacks += 1.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +15 on application.
    Render note: display doom_stacks in enemy status bar parenthetically.
    Only one enemy can be Doomed at a time. Engine enforces on apply.
  balance: At L1 this is pure setup with no payoff — intentional design tension.
    Encourages investment in higher levels before Doom Click feels good.

L2 — "Let It Marinate" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Each doom stack on a Doomed enemy adds a passive debuff penalty to that enemy:
    every doom stack reduces their effective defense by 1 (defense cannot go below
    0). At doom_stacks=5, the enemy has -5 effective defense, making all attacks
    against them deal +5 damage. This persists for as long as the Doom Curse is
    active. Works on all damage sources — melee, guns, other spells, environmental
    fire, other players' faction allies, everything.
  impl notes:
    In combat.py damage resolution: after base defense calc, subtract entity.doom_stacks
    from effective defense before the final max(1, power - defense) calculation.
    Guard: only if entity.doom_curse == True.
    This is a DPS increase for the ENTIRE fight once doom stacks, not just Hexburst.
  balance: At doom_stacks=5: +5 damage to every hit. That's roughly a +40-60%
    damage increase on floor 1-2 enemies with 2-3 base defense. Strong but
    requires 5 turns of setup. Enemies that survive 5 turns (elites, bosses)
    are exactly the ones this needs to be good against.

L3 — "Hexburst" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "hexburst"}
  ability_id: "hexburst"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Consume ALL doom stacks on the target in one concentrated spiritual explosion.
    Deals (BKS * doom_stacks) no-defense damage. At BKS=10, doom_stacks=5:
    50 no-defense damage — enough to one-shot most non-boss enemies. At
    doom_stacks=1: only BKS damage (terrible — discourages panic-firing).
    After firing, doom_stacks resets to 0 but doom_curse remains active on the
    target; stacks begin accumulating again immediately. This is NOT PER_FLOOR —
    it's INFINITE. The restriction is the doom stack requirement.
  impl notes:
    Validate: target.doom_curse must be True. If doom_stacks == 0, refuse:
      "No doom on target yet."
    Damage: entity.hp -= player.stats.effective_book_smarts * entity.doom_stacks.
      Bypass defense entirely (spiritual consume).
    After: entity.doom_stacks = 0. entity.doom_curse remains True.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +25 per doom_stack consumed (scales with patience).
    Not a spell (no spell crit from BKS crit chance system).
  balance: Minimum meaningful use is doom_stacks=3 (BKS*3 = 30 at BKS=10),
    which is only reachable 3 turns after application. Forces real commitment.
    Max burst (BKS=12, stacks=5) = 60 no-defense damage — one-shots almost
    anything on floors 1-4. But requires 5 full turns of patience.

L4 — "Bad Omen" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Doom stacks no longer just reduce defense — they now carry a per-stack 10%
    chance that any hit against the Doomed enemy causes them to waste their next
    action entirely (they "stagger" under the weight of spiritual dread, lose
    their turn). This is checked ONCE per hit, not per stack, but the probability
    is (doom_stacks * 10)%. At doom_stacks=3: 30% skip; at doom_stacks=5: 50%
    skip-turn chance on every hit. The stagger is not a separate effect — it
    simply sets the entity's energy to 0 after the hit (they re-earn it next
    cycle). Separate from stun: stagger cannot be cleared by healing, and
    irresistible entities (bosses with stun immunity) still lose the flat defense
    from doom stacks but are immune to the skip-turn component.
  impl notes:
    In combat.py: after a hit lands on entity with doom_curse == True:
      roll random() < entity.doom_stacks * 0.10.
      If pass: entity.energy = 0. Add message "[name] staggers under the doom."
    Boss/stun-immune flag: if entity has stun_immune field set True in its
      MonsterTemplate, skip the energy=0 step but still apply doom defense reduction.
  balance: 50% skip-turn chance at max doom is enormous but requires 5 turns of
    building AND a hit to proc. In practice enemies rarely stand still for 5 turns
    so this is a late-fight payoff. The stagger also resets to re-earning energy,
    not a true stun (enemy can act if they have leftover energy from speed).

L5 — "Ancestral Doom" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When a Doomed enemy dies, Doom SPREADS. All of the doom_stacks from the dead
    enemy teleport to a random living non-player entity within 8 tiles (doom_curse
    set True on new target, doom_stacks transferred EXACTLY — not halved, not reset).
    Additionally, the Hexburst ability now has a secondary power: if you fire
    Hexburst on a target with doom_stacks >= 4, it also applies a 5-turn fear
    effect to ALL enemies within 3 tiles of the target (the spiritual explosion
    radiates existential horror to witnesses). No new ability — this is a passive
    upgrade to Hexburst's existing execute logic.
  impl notes:
    In entity_died handler: if entity.doom_curse and entity.doom_stacks > 0:
      find random living non-player entity within Chebyshev(8).
      If found: new_target.doom_curse = True, new_target.doom_stacks = entity.doom_stacks.
      Message: "The doom moves on."
    Hexburst execute upgrade: if doom_stacks >= 4 at detonation, after damage:
      AOE_CIRCLE r=3 around target, apply fear(5) to all living non-player entities.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +15 on each doom spread (it's still a curse landing).
  balance: Doom spreading means a single application can chain through an entire
    floor if enemies die quickly. Capped naturally by enemy count. The Hexburst
    fear upgrade turns L3's nuke into a room-clearing panic at high doom stacks.

DESIGN D — "HAUNT" (Spirit Projectile / Return Mechanic)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Named after WoW's Haunt spell — you fling a spirit at an enemy, it arrives on
their next turn (delayed travel), empowers your active dots while it haunts them,
and then RETURNS to you on the enemy's death, healing you. But this is NIGRL, so
the spirit is your dead uncle's ghost and it absolutely hates the person you're
sending it after. The return mechanic is the novel hook: the spirit carries
loot of power back to you when it comes home. The ghost can only be out once.
If the haunted target dies before the ghost returns, the ghost lingers for 3 turns
waiting for a new host before dissipating. Deep investment unlocks a second ghost
(ancestral squad), and the capstone lets you ride the ghost — teleporting to the
dead body's location as the ghost comes home.

PLAYSTYLE: Mobile mid-range. You always want to be near-but-not-on a haunted
target. The delayed arrival rewards positioning. The return heal rewards killing
haunted targets before the ghost's duration expires. High micro-management ceiling.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Street Smarts (ghost travel speed — lower travel turns at high
STS). Swagger (ghost tanking — ghost absorbs one attack while haunting if player
Swagger > 10).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Go Get 'Em, Unc" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "go_get_em_unc"}
  ability_id: "go_get_em_unc"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Fling your uncle's ghost at a visible enemy. The ghost does NOT arrive
    instantly — it travels at 1 tile per turn toward the target (tracked via
    engine.haunt_ghost). After arriving (turn count = Manhattan distance to
    target / 1, min 1 turn), the target gains the "haunted" effect (10 turns).
    While haunted: target takes +2 damage from all sources (spiritual vulnerability).
    When the haunted target dies OR the effect expires naturally, the ghost returns
    to the player: player heals ceil(3 + BKS/4) HP. Ghost-in-transit is not
    "wasted" if the target dies before arrival — ghost auto-re-targets the nearest
    enemy and haunts them instead (one redirect only). Only one ghost active at
    a time.
  impl notes:
    engine.haunt_ghost: dict or None — {
      "state": "travelling" | "haunting" | "returning",
      "x": int, "y": int (current ghost tile, for render),
      "target_id": entity ID,
      "travel_turns_remaining": int,
      "return_target": "player"
    }
    Each energy loop tick: advance ghost state machine by 1 step.
    HauntedEffect in effects.py: duration=10, modify_incoming_damage: +2 flat.
    On haunted target death: remove HauntedEffect, set ghost state = "returning".
    On returning: ghost reaches player (Manhattan 0), heal player, ghost = None.
    Ghost redirect on target-dies-before-arrival: scan nearest living non-player
      within 8 tiles; if found, set new target, recalculate travel_turns.
    Render: ghost as 'u' (for uncle) in a pale purple color on ghost.x, ghost.y.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +15 on haunted application (when ghost arrives and haunts).
  balance: Min 1 turn delay makes it unreliable on weak enemies that die fast.
    Best against tanky enemies (mini-bosses, high-HP regulars). The heal
    on return provides sustained HP regen over a floor = strong in long fights.

L2 — "Old Debts" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    While any enemy is Haunted, all DoT effects (bleeding, soul_tax, ignite, any
    "damage over time" type effects) on that target tick for +1 additional damage
    per tick. This bonus applies to the Haunted target only — it does not spread.
    Flavor: the ghost is in there making everything worse.
  impl notes:
    In effects.py tick() method for any Effect with effect_kind == DOT (or a
    new is_dot: bool = True attribute): if source entity (target) has "haunted"
    effect in status_effects: add 1 to tick damage.
    Alternatively: HauntedEffect.tick() scans the host's status_effects for any
    dot effects and adds 1 to their next tick. Simpler: in each DoT effect's
    tick(), check if entity has HauntedEffect, add +1 if so.
    This is a synergy with Smoking (bleed/dot), Stabbing (gouge bleed), and
    any future DoT sources.
  balance: +1/tick across 3 DoTs = +3 effective damage/turn against a Haunted
    target. Over 10 turns of haunt: +30 bonus damage. Not flashy but reliable.

L3 — "Cold Spot" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    The ghost's travel path leaves a spiritual cold spot: the tile the ghost
    passed through most recently (ghost.x, ghost.y as it moves) becomes a cold
    spot tile for 3 turns. Any non-player entity that walks onto a cold spot
    tile has a 35% chance to receive slow (ratio=0.4, 4 turns). Cold spots
    are tile-based persistent hazards — stored on the dungeon tile layer, not
    as entities. Also: when the ghost RETURNS to the player, it passes back
    through the same tiles (return path is straight line player↔dead_target),
    refreshing cold spots along its return route.
  impl notes:
    dungeon.cold_spots: dict of (x,y) → turns_remaining. Updated each energy loop.
    Ghost movement: each step, add ghost's previous tile to cold_spots with value 3.
    Each energy loop: decrement all cold_spot values; remove at 0.
    In ai.py monster movement: after each step, check if new tile in cold_spots;
      roll 35% for slow application (apply_effect("slow", ratio=0.4, duration=4)).
    Render: cold spot tiles shown as '.' with a slightly different color (pale blue).
    Return path: when state = "returning", ghost retraces using stored path list
      (store tile path at travel time, replay in reverse during return).
  balance: In narrow corridors the cold spots are nearly guaranteed to slow
    pursuing enemies. In open rooms they matter less. Strong floor 1-3 corridor
    design. The return trip refreshing cold spots is the surprise — the ghost
    defends your retreat line when coming home.

L4 — "Second Uncle" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    You can now have TWO ghosts active simultaneously. The second ghost is your
    dead uncle's business partner (entity.haunt_ghost_2). Sending a second ghost
    out while the first is active works identically to the first. Both ghosts'
    healing-on-return stack (two returns = two heals). Additionally: if both
    ghosts are haunting the SAME target simultaneously (both arrive and haunt the
    same entity), that entity is "Double-Haunted" — the +2 damage vulnerability
    from each ghost stacks to +4, AND the target is afflicted with "spirit-sick"
    (a 6-turn slow at ratio=0.5).
  impl notes:
    engine.haunt_ghost_2: same dict structure as engine.haunt_ghost.
    In ability execute: if engine.haunt_ghost is None, use ghost; else use ghost_2;
      else "Both ghosts already active."
    Double-Haunt detection: each time HauntedEffect is applied, check if target
      already has HauntedEffect; if so: set entity.double_haunted = True, add +2
      to the damage vulnerability (so total = +4), apply slow(0.5, 6).
    entity.double_haunted: bool, reset on HauntedEffect expiry.
    Both ghosts return independently on target death — two separate heals fire.
  balance: Double-haunting one target requires two casts on the same target within
    travel time. Practical mainly against slow or stationary enemies. Two ghosts
    on two different targets is the typical L4 usage, doubling floor-wide haunt
    coverage.

L5 — "Spirit Walk" (passive + upgrade)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When a Haunted target dies and the ghost begins its return trip, the player
    may RIDE the ghost — teleporting instantly to the dead target's tile as the
    ghost "snaps" home. This teleport is FREE (costs no energy), triggers
    automatically with a confirmation prompt: "Ride the spirit? [Y/N]". If Y:
    player teleports to the dead entity's last tile; if N: ghost returns normally.
    The teleport delivers the player directly into melee range of the kill, which
    is aggressive positioning — useful for chain-killing clustered enemies.
    Additionally, the ghost now carries LOOT BACK: on return (whether ridden or
    not), the ghost pulls a random consumable item from the dead target's loot
    table (as if the enemy dropped it) and places it on the player's current tile.
    Minimum 1 item per ghost return.
  impl notes:
    When ghost transitions state to "returning": engine sends "haunt_return"
      event. Engine checks if L5 is unlocked; if so, pauses energy loop and
      prompts player Y/N via input_handler's single-key read.
    If Y: player.x = target_last_x, player.y = target_last_y. Ghost dissipates
      immediately (no return trip needed — player rode it). Heal still fires.
    If N: ghost returns normally. Heal fires on arrival.
    Loot carry: on ghost dissipation (either path), call loot.pick_random_consumable
      (zone) and place on player.x, player.y. Message: "The ghost brought somethin' back."
    If player rides ghost into a tile occupied by another enemy (should not happen
      if target is dead), place player on nearest empty adjacent tile.
  balance: Free teleport to kill location is strong mobility. But it puts you in
    the middle of a fight, which is risky. Loot-on-return is a consistent minor
    benefit. The Y/N prompt prevents accidental bad teleports.

================================================================================
DESIGN E — "IMPENDING DOOM" (Curse Expiry Detonation)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Straight from PoE's Impending Doom support, but elevated into NIGRL's design.
The core philosophy: your curses are TIME BOMBS. They deal their damage when
they EXPIRE, not when they're applied. This completely inverts normal status
effect design — instead of wanting curses to last forever, you are engineering
the perfect expiry. The skill rewards knowing exactly when to start the clock.
Higher levels let you control the detonation timing (force-expire a curse early),
protect curses from dispel, and chain detonations. The capstone introduces
"Doomchain" — a sequence of curse expiries that trigger each other in a controlled
cascade. This is the most mechanically complex design in the set.

PLAYSTYLE: Bomb technician. You are always thinking about duration timers.
You apply curses, count turns in your head, position for the detonation. Strong
against rooms you can kite through. Weak against enemies that resist slows (they
reach you before the bomb goes off). High skill ceiling — novices will proc
detonations in bad positions; experts will orchestrate perfect chains.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Book Smarts (detonation damage, curse duration scaling).
Tolerance (blast radius of detonation AOE).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Set the Timer" (activated ability + passive modifier)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "set_the_timer"}
  ability_id: "set_the_timer"
  target_type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Apply a "Hexbomb" curse to a visible enemy (entity.hexbomb = True,
    entity.hexbomb_duration: int, entity.hexbomb_fuse: int).
    The Hexbomb starts with a fuse of 8 turns. Each turn the fuse counts down.
    When the fuse hits 0, it DETONATES: deals (4 + BKS) no-defense damage to the
    target and (2 + BKS/2) no-defense damage to all non-player entities within
    2 tiles of the target. After detonation, entity.hexbomb = False.
    The bomb is VISIBLE to the player in the status panel as a countdown: "[name]:
    Hexbomb (6)" ticking down each turn. This is not just flavor — knowing the
    timer IS the mechanic.
  impl notes:
    entity.hexbomb: bool
    entity.hexbomb_fuse: int (countdown from 8)
    Each energy loop tick: for entities with hexbomb == True: hexbomb_fuse -= 1;
      if hexbomb_fuse <= 0: trigger detonation.
    Detonation: player_bks = engine.player.stats.effective_book_smarts.
      Direct hit: entity.hp -= 4 + player_bks (bypass defense).
      AOE r=2: all living non-player entities within Chebyshev(2):
        entity.hp -= 2 + player_bks // 2 (bypass defense).
    Only one Hexbomb active at a time (enforced by engine before application).
    Blackkk Magic XP: +15 on application. +10 bonus if detonation kills the target.
    Re-application on same target refreshes fuse to 8 (does not stack — one bomb).
  balance: At BKS=10: direct hit = 14 no-defense, AOE = 7 each. On floor 1
    (enemy HP 15-25) this one-shots most enemies on detonation if they haven't
    been damaged. But requires 8 turns of waiting — in practice they'll take
    other damage first. Fuse length 8 is tunable.

L2 — "Dead Man's Switch" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    The Hexbomb is now warded. Any effect that would remove or dispel the Hexbomb
    (including enemy self-heal, negative status removal, or any future "cleanse"
    mechanics) instead INSTANTLY detonates it — triggering the full explosion
    immediately at the point of attempted removal. This is the Unstable Affliction
    principle: you do not want to touch this curse. Additionally, if the Hexbombed
    target dies BEFORE the fuse runs out (killed by other damage), the Hexbomb
    detonates for FULL damage on the death tile as if the fuse expired naturally.
    Flavor: the bomb doesn't care who killed the body.
  impl notes:
    For cleanse-on-death: in entity_died handler, check if entity.hexbomb == True;
      if so, trigger detonation at entity.x, entity.y before clearing the entity.
    For dispel protection: if any future cleanse mechanic would call
      remove_effect("hexbomb_effect") on an entity: intercept and detonate instead.
    This makes Dead Man's Switch a passive upgrade that rewards applying the bomb
    early and NOT worrying about the enemy dying to other damage.
  balance: The "detonates on death" behavior is the main balance value here.
    Previously, if you killed the enemy with melee before the fuse ran out, the
    bomb was wasted. Now it always goes off. Eliminates the anti-synergy between
    Hexbomb and just playing normally.

L3 — "Blow It Early" (passive upgrade to Set the Timer)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    You can now force-detonate the Hexbomb before the fuse expires. When targeting
    a Hexbombed enemy with the "Set the Timer" ability (re-using the same ability
    key), if the target already has a Hexbomb, the ability instead fires a
    force-detonation. Deals REDUCED damage based on remaining fuse:
    Damage multiplier = (8 - hexbomb_fuse) / 8 (so at fuse=4, multiplier=0.5).
    Minimum 1 turn must have elapsed for force-detonate to be available (fuse <= 7).
    This gives players who read the fight correctly an escape hatch: if the enemy
    is about to reach you, pop the bomb early at reduced effect rather than not
    at all.
  impl notes:
    In set_the_timer ability execute: before applying new hexbomb, check if target
      already has hexbomb == True.
    If yes and hexbomb_fuse < 8: calculate multiplier = (8 - hexbomb_fuse) / 8.
      Apply direct hit damage * multiplier (rounded down, min 1).
      Apply AOE damage * multiplier (rounded down, min 1).
      entity.hexbomb = False.
      Message: "Detonated early — partial blast."
    If yes and hexbomb_fuse == 8: message "Bomb just set — wait a turn."
    Note: force-detonate does NOT grant bonus XP from L1's +10 kill bonus.
  balance: The multiplier curve means blowing at fuse=6 (2 turns elapsed): 25%
    damage. At fuse=0 (8 turns elapsed): 100% (same as natural). This is strictly
    worse than waiting — but sometimes 25% is better than 0%.

L4 — "Doomchain" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When a Hexbomb detonates (naturally, on death, or force-detonated), if any
    enemy caught in the detonation's AOE (within 2 tiles) has ANY other curse/debuff
    on them, the detonation CHAINS — those enemies are immediately afflicted with
    a new Hexbomb with a SHORTER fuse of 4 turns (half the normal 8). The chain
    Hexbombs function identically to normal Hexbombs: they detonate at fuse=0,
    deal the same damage, and CAN trigger further chains (each chain reduces fuse
    by 1, minimum fuse = 1, preventing infinite zero-tick loops). Chain Hexbombs
    grant Blackkk Magic XP on application.
  impl notes:
    entity.hexbomb_chain_fuse: int — chain bombs use 4-turn fuse instead of 8.
    In detonation logic: after resolving AOE damage, for each entity hit in AOE
      that has len(status_effects) > 0 (any debuff at all):
        if entity.hexbomb == False (don't overwrite an existing bomb):
          entity.hexbomb = True, entity.hexbomb_fuse = max(1, 5 - chain_depth)
          where chain_depth starts at 1 for the first chain (fuse=4), 2 for
          second chain (fuse=3), etc.
    Track chain_depth by passing it through the detonation call.
    Chain detonations can trigger further chains up to depth=4 (fuse minimum 1).
    AOE splash of chain bombs is radius=2 (same as original).
    XP: +15 for each chain Hexbomb application.
  balance: In a room of 4 enemies all with some debuff (bleed from stabbing, slow
    from writ, etc.), a single original Hexbomb can cascade into 3 additional
    bombs. With 4-turn chain fuses, the cascade plays out over 4 more turns.
    Strong in debuff-heavy builds. In clean rooms with no debuffs, Doomchain does
    nothing extra. Design intentionally rewards multi-system builds.

L5 — "The Final Countdown" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Capstone. Two upgrades simultaneously:
    1. CLOCK ACCELERATION: Each turn the Hexbomb ticks down, the target also
       takes 1 no-defense damage (spiritual dread eating them alive). Flavor:
       they can FEEL it counting down. Over 8 turns: 8 extra no-defense damage
       passively, on top of the detonation burst.
    2. NUCLEAR OPTION: If the Hexbomb detonates while the target is below 25% HP,
       the detonation radius DOUBLES (from 2 to 4 tiles) and damage is multiplied
       by 1.5 (rounded down). This is the execute-phase mechanic — finishing a
       nearly-dead enemy with a Hexbomb turns it into a room-clearing event.
  impl notes:
    Clock Acceleration: in the hexbomb fuse countdown loop, after decrementing:

DESIGN F — "PUNISHMENT" (Retaliation Curse / Reactive Damage)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Borrowed from PoE's Punishment curse but reinvented as street karma. You put a
RETRIBUTION curse on an enemy — every time THEY attack (anyone — player, other
enemies, even inanimate objects), they take spiritual blowback damage proportional
to their own power. This is the "yeah go ahead, try it" school of voodoo. Higher
levels add escalating blowback: the first attack they make after being cursed is
punished lightly; the second is punished harder; by the third attack they've
nearly doubled their own self-damage from karma. The capstone mechanic is uniquely
new: if they deal enough karma-damage to themselves over the fight, they switch
SIDES for one turn (existential crisis from fighting their own spiritual self).

PLAYSTYLE: Defensive aggressor. You want enemies to attack you or each other.
Best against high-power enemies (their blowback scales with their power stat).
Terrible against passive enemies or enemies that only move but don't attack often.
Synergizes with fear effects that make enemies flee (they stop attacking, curse
decays) vs players who want to KEEP enemies attacking (don't fear them).

STAT DEPENDENCY: Swagger (blowback damage scales with player's effective Swagger
— your spiritual presence amplifies the retribution). Street Smarts (curse
application rate, extra hit chance for activating procs).

DESIGN G — "TEMPORAL CHAINS" (Time Distortion Curse)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Adapted from PoE's Temporal Chains but taken much further — this is full
time-distortion voodoo. The core mechanic: enemies you curse exist in a slightly
different temporal frame than you. Their action economy is fractured. Status
effects on them tick SLOWER (last longer effectively from your perspective), their
buffs tick FASTER (expire sooner), and their animations trail by half a beat (in
pure game terms: their energy gain per tick is reduced while yours is boosted by
a proportional amount). The novel mechanic unique to NIGRL: Temporal Snapshot —
you can "save" an enemy's current HP/status-effect state and, if things go badly,
"rewind" them to that state. This is the first rewind mechanic in any traditional
roguelike.

PLAYSTYLE: Time Wizard. You control the pacing of every fight. Slowing enemies
buys setup time for the bomb designs. The rewind is your insurance policy —
a pseudo-undo button that costs a precious charge. High skill expression in
using Snapshot at exactly the right moment (when an enemy is at low HP with
good debuffs applied), then rewinding if you nearly die.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Tolerance (temporal distortion intensity — TOL scales how much
the enemy's energy gain is reduced). Book Smarts (snapshot "fidelity" — higher
BKS means the snapshot captures more status effects).

DESIGN H — "VOODOO DOLL" (Sympathetic Link Between Two Enemies)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
The only design that requires TWO targets simultaneously. You link two enemies
with a sympathetic spiritual connection — what happens to one, happens to the
other. If the linked enemy takes damage, the doll-bearer takes the same damage.
If the linked enemy is healed, the doll-bearer takes damage instead (inverse
healing). If the doll-bearer is stunned, the linked enemy is also stunned. The
novel mechanic: the link is DIRECTIONAL. You choose which enemy is the "Doll"
(suffers) and which is the "Needle" (causes the suffering). Higher levels allow
you to weaponize the link offensively — attacking the Doll to deal double damage
(both take the hit), or using the link to transfer your OWN status effects to
the Needle enemy.

PLAYSTYLE: Setup tactician. You need to identify the right two enemies to link
(high-value Needle with dangerous attacks, tanky Doll to absorb the reflected
damage efficiently). Very weak in single-enemy encounters. Dominant in group
combat and boss+adds scenarios (link the boss to an add, kill the add, boss
takes double damage). High skill ceiling — knowing who to link is everything.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Street Smarts (link range — can link enemies up to STS tiles
apart). Book Smarts (link damage multiplier, determining how much of the Needle's
damage transfers to the Doll).

    Design tension: do you wait for the max-duration burst, or kill now?
  impl notes:
    VoodooLinkEffect.expire(): check if expiry reason is "duration" (not death).
      Burst damage = max(5, player_bks + (12 - duration_remaining) // 2).
      Wait: duration_remaining should be 0 at natural expiry. So burst = BKS + 6.
    To distinguish death-expiry from natural expiry: add an expire_reason field

      removing the effect; default = "natural".
    Apply burst to both entity.voodoo_partner_id-resolved entity and the effect's
      host entity.
  balance: At BKS=10: 16 no-defense burst to BOTH entities on natural expiry.
    Against 2 enemies with 30 HP each after 12 turns of 50% echo damage:
    they've probably taken significant damage already from the echo. The burst
    finishes them. But waiting 12 turns is risky — many roguelike players won't
    survive that long in a fight. High risk, high reward.

L5 — "Me, Myself, and I" (passive — self-link)
  perk_type: "passive"

================================================================================
DESIGN I — "PROFANE BLOOM" (Cursed Death Explosion Chains)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Straight from PoE's Occultist ascendancy — cursed enemies explode on death,
spreading curses to survivors. But in NIGRL's version, the explosion mechanic
has been fundamentally rethought: the explosion's size and damage scales with
HOW MANY different curses the dying enemy had at time of death, not just whether
they were cursed. This rewards stacking multiple different curse types on one
target. The design intentionally synergizes with every other Blackkk Magic build
(more curses = bigger bloom). The capstone introduces Unholy Ground — tiles
stained by the bloom become permanently cursed terrain for the floor, applying
a random curse to any enemy that walks on them.

PLAYSTYLE: Crowd control / chain reaction. You are the arsonist of curses —
you invest in one enemy, blow them up, and watch the room react. Best with
builds that can stack many different debuff types quickly. The Unholy Ground
capstone turns every kill into a persistent environmental hazard.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Tolerance (bloom radius scales with TOL). Street Smarts
(bloom damage scales with STS — the curse spread accuracy).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "You Gon' Blow" (passive — death trigger)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Any enemy that dies while afflicted with at least 1 curse/debuff from the
    player (status effects of category "debuff" where source = player) explodes
    spiritually. The Profane Bloom: deals (4 + STS/2) no-defense damage to
    all non-player living entities within a radius of (1 + len(player-sourced
    debuffs) // 2) tiles of the death location. The bloom damage is fixed and
    undefensible. Enemies hit by the bloom do NOT receive a curse from the
    bloom at L1 — just damage. Flavor: the spirit leaving the body takes all
    its curses with it as shrapnel.
  impl notes:
    In entity_died handler: collect entity.status_effects where effect.category
      == "debuff" and effect.source_entity == player_id.
    num_player_debuffs = len(collected list).
    If num_player_debuffs > 0:
      radius = 1 + num_player_debuffs // 2
      damage = 4 + player.stats.effective_street_smarts // 2
      For each living non-player entity within Chebyshev(radius):
        entity.hp -= damage (bypass defense)
    Message: "[name] blooms."
    Blackkk Magic XP: +10 per enemy hit by the bloom.
  balance: Single-debuff: radius=1, damage=9 at STS=10. Two debuffs: radius=2.
    Four debuffs: radius=3. At max (6 debuffs): radius=4. The scaling is strong
    but requires stacking many debuffs on one target, which takes multiple turns.
    On floor 1-2: radius=1-2 bloom consistently hits clustered enemies.

L2 — "Second-Hand Smoke" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Enemies hit by a Profane Bloom now receive one of the dead enemy's former
    curses (randomly selected from the list of player-sourced debuffs that were
    on the dead enemy at death). The transferred curse is applied at HALF its
    original remaining duration (min 1 turn). This IS the curse-spread mechanic.
    The transferred curse counts for Blackkk Magic XP (+15 per spread application).
    If the dead enemy had 3 debuffs, each bloom-hit enemy receives one random one.
    Multiple enemies hit can receive the same curse type (random roll per enemy,
    not distributed uniquely).
  impl notes:
    In the Profane Bloom loop: before dealing damage to each hit enemy, roll a
      random debuff from the collected player-sourced debuff list.
    Apply that debuff to the hit enemy at half duration:
      apply_effect(hit_enemy, random_debuff.id, duration = max(1, debuff.duration // 2))
    XP grant: +15 per successful application.
    The curse spread itself can trigger further blooms if a spread-cursed enemy
    dies from the bloom damage (L1 damage may kill them, triggering their own bloom
    since they now have 1 debuff). Chain reactions are possible and intentional.
  balance: Chain reactions require enemies dying to bloom damage (9 damage). At
    HP=10 or below: possible. Creates occasional cascade moments where a room
    of 5 enemies collapses because one died and the chain ran. Not reliable but
    spectacular when it happens.

L3 — "Full Bloom" (passive upgrade)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    The Profane Bloom's damage is now boosted: base damage changes from
    (4 + STS/2) to (4 + STS/2 + BKS/3). Additionally, the bloom radius now adds
    1 additional tile for every 3 different CURSE TYPES on the dying enemy
    (not just total debuffs — types like "slow", "fear", "stun", "bleeding" count
    separately). Maximum radius is capped at (2 + TOL/2) tiles regardless of
    curse count (TOL provides the ceiling). At TOL=10: max radius=7. At TOL=6:
    max radius=5. The Bloom can now also hit the player's own tile if enemies are
    adjacent to the player (bloom is indiscriminate at this level — player takes
    50% of the bloom damage if within radius, no defense).
    Flavor: this is CHAOS. You should feel slightly afraid of your own bloom.
  impl notes:
    Count distinct debuff type IDs on the dying entity.
    radius = 1 + num_player_debuffs // 2 + distinct_types // 3
    radius = min(radius, 2 + player.stats.effective_tolerance // 2)
    Damage: 4 + STS//2 + BKS//3
    Player takes bloom damage (50%) if within radius: player.hp -= damage // 2
      (bypass defense; log as "Bloom backsplash."). This is the risk of L3.
  balance: Self-damage makes this dangerous to use in tight corridors. In open
    rooms (player far from the bloom epicenter): pure upside. In corridors:
    risk management. TOL cap on radius prevents runaway scaling.

L4 — "Bloom and Doom" (passive — synergy with Design C)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Profane Bloom explosions now interact with the Doom Clock system (Design C,
    if both trees are somehow in use — but this design is standalone):
    specifically, any enemy hit by a Profane Bloom that has ANY status effect
    on them (including freshly-transferred spread curses) is afflicted with
    entity.bloom_marked = True. While bloom_marked, any further Profane Bloom
    that hits them within 3 turns deals +50% damage. Bloom Marked expires after
    3 turns or when that enemy is hit by a second bloom. "Bloom and Doom" is a
    self-contained synergy within Profane Bloom: the second bloom in any chain
    reaction hits for 1.5x if the first bloom already marked the target.
  impl notes:
    entity.bloom_marked: bool
    entity.bloom_marked_turns: int (countdown from 3)
    In the Profane Bloom loop: after damaging a hit enemy, set bloom_marked = True,
      bloom_marked_turns = 3.
    Each turn: decrement bloom_marked_turns; clear at 0.
    In Profane Bloom damage resolution: if target.bloom_marked: damage *= 1.5 (round up).
    This requires passing bloom-hit context through the loop — the second bloom
    in a chain (triggered by a cursed enemy dying from the first bloom) will see
    bloom_marked = True on survivors who were already hit.
  balance: In chain reactions: the second bloom in the same turn hits for 1.5x.
    Creates a strong incentive to trigger chain reactions (even partially) before
    the marked window expires. The 3-turn window means even staggered blooms
    (enemy A dies, enemy B takes damage, B dies 2 turns later) can qualify.

L5 — "Unholy Ground" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When a Profane Bloom fires, the tile where the DEAD ENEMY stood becomes
    Unholy Ground. Unholy Ground persists for the rest of the floor. Any non-player
    living entity that enters an Unholy Ground tile has a 60% chance to receive
    a random debuff (randomly selected from: slow ratio=0.3 4t, cripple_armor 6t,
    fear 4t, confused 3t). The ground stores a "curse pool" seeded from the dead
    enemy's debuffs — the random roll draws from the specific curses that were on
    the dead enemy at bloom time. If the dead enemy had no specific curse pool
    (edge case), it draws from the global default list above.
  impl notes:
    dungeon.unholy_tiles: dict of (x,y) → curse_pool where curse_pool is a list
      of (effect_id, params) tuples.
    On Profane Bloom: add dead_enemy.x, dead_enemy.y to unholy_tiles with
      curse_pool populated from the dead enemy's player-sourced debuffs at death.
    Each turn in energy loop: for each monster movement step, check if new tile
      is in unholy_tiles. Roll 60%. If pass: pick random entry from curse_pool,
      apply_effect to entity.
    Render: unholy tiles shown as '.' with a dark purple color override.
    Unholy tiles persist until floor change (floor_duration via dungeon cleanup).
    Player walking on unholy tiles: no effect (player is the source of the
      unholy, not subject to it).
    XP: +10 per unholy tile proc on an enemy.
  balance: One dead enemy creates one persistent curse tile. In a dungeon of 8
    rooms with 3-5 enemies each, a full floor could have 20+ unholy tiles by
    the time you reach the stairs. Enemies in that zone get slowed/feared/confused
    constantly. Strong floor-control; weak in isolated 1v1 fights.

DESIGN J — "SPIRITUAL DEBT" (Self-Curse / Power Through Sacrifice)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
Dark Pact from WoW — but the voodoo version involves a literal spiritual debt
to your ancestors. You curse YOURSELF to gain power. Each self-curse has an
immediate downside (you take damage, lose defense, lose speed) but provides a
STRONGER curse to project outward. The debt mechanic: engine tracks your running
debt in "spiritual tally marks." Debt builds from self-curses and discharges when
enemies die to your projected curses. If you die in debt, the penalty on death
screen is higher (for roguelike flavor, though permadeath makes this symbolic).
The novel mechanic unique to NIGRL: Absolution — a rare moment of spiritual
balance where you have exactly 0 debt and your next projected curse is doubled
in power and range.

PLAYSTYLE: Glass cannon spiritualist. You voluntarily weaken yourself to project
devastating curses. The debt discharge on enemy death rewards aggressive play —
keep killing to keep your debt paid. Falling behind on kills = accumulating
spiritual handicap. Unique risk/reward: the player WANTS to take damage (to
discharge through debt mechanics), which synergizes with Retribution (Design F)
and self-Voodoo Doll (Design H L5).

STAT DEPENDENCY: Tolerance (debt capacity — higher TOL allows carrying more
debt without penalties). Constitution (self-curse damage intake mitigated by
CON's HP pool).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Pay the Price" (activated ability — self-targeted)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "pay_the_price"}
  ability_id: "pay_the_price"
  target_type: SELF
  charge_type: INFINITE
  desc:
    Sacrifice 10% of your current HP (rounded up, minimum 1, cannot kill you —
    minimum 1 HP remains) to charge a "Spirit Debt" counter
    (engine.spirit_debt += ceil(player.hp * 0.10)). The sacrifice also immediately
    grants a one-time buff: the player's next projected curse (any Blackkk Magic
    curse applied to an enemy within the next 3 turns) deals double damage and/or
    double duration (depending on the curse type). The buff is consumed on the
    first projected curse or expires after 3 turns. Display: "Debt Charged" in
    status with a turn counter. Spirit Debt counter is displayed in the stats panel.
  impl notes:
    engine.spirit_debt: int (running tally, persists per run unless discharged)
    engine.spirit_debt_charged: bool (the "next curse doubled" flag)

    On activation: hp_cost = max(1, ceil(player.hp * 0.10))
      if player.hp - hp_cost <= 0: hp_cost = player.hp - 1 (cannot kill)
      player.hp -= hp_cost

    In apply_effect() for player-sourced debuffs, if spirit_debt_charged:
      double duration (or damage where applicable); clear the flag.
    Each turn: decrement spirit_debt_charge_turns; if 0: clear spirit_debt_charged.
    Blackkk Magic XP: +10 per activation (you paid).
  balance: 10% HP cost at HP=100: costs 10 HP to empower next curse. Painful but
    fast to recover. The doubled curse can save more than 10 HP in prevented
    damage (a doubled slow lasts twice as long, preventing many hits). Economic
    positive but requires careful HP management.

L2 — "Debt Service" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Enemies that die while afflicted with any player-sourced curse DISCHARGE some
    spiritual debt: engine.spirit_debt -= (3 + enemy.max_hp // 10) per kill.

    At high debt (>= 20): player receives a "spiritual overload" debuff:
    -1 defense and -5% energy gain per 20 points of spirit_debt above the threshold.
    At debt=40: -2 defense, -10% energy. At debt=60: -3 defense, -15% energy.
    The debt is a real penalty that accumulates if you use Pay the Price repeatedly
    without killing cursed enemies. Killing cures the debt.
  impl notes:

    SpiritualOverloadEffect: recalculate on debt change.
    In stats.py or combat.py: apply defense penalty = spirit_debt // 20.
    In energy loop: apply energy gain multiplier = 1.0 - (spirit_debt // 20) * 0.05.
    Display spirit_debt in stats panel. The overload penalty needs visual feedback.
  balance: Kill an enemy with 30 max_hp: discharge = 3+3 = 6 debt. If you paid
    10 HP via Pay the Price twice (debt=20), you need 4 kills to clear. Aggressive
    play clears debt quickly. Passive play accumulates it dangerously. This
    mechanic punishes camping (spiritual standstill = growing debt with no kills).

L3 — "Ancestral Favor" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When spirit_debt == 0 (completely in balance — no debt and no recent sacrifice),
    the player enters Absolution state briefly: the next Blackkk Magic curse cast
    within 2 turns is TRIPLED in power (3x damage or 3x duration). Absolution
    can only be reached after clearing all debt — the engine tracks this via a
    flag (engine.absolution_available = True when spirit_debt transitions to 0
    from any non-zero value). The Absolution flag clears after 2 turns or when
    a powered curse is cast.
    Additional passive: each time debt discharges to exactly 0 (fully paid), the
    player receives a 5-turn +3 Swagger bonus (temporary spiritual confidence).
  impl notes:
    In Debt Service discharge: after calculation, if new spirit_debt == 0:
      engine.absolution_available = True
      engine.absolution_turns = 2
      apply_effect(player, "swagger_boost", magnitude=3, duration=5)
    In apply_effect() for player-sourced curses, if engine.absolution_available:
      triple the duration (or damage). Clear absolution_available.
    Each turn: if absolution_turns > 0: decrement; at 0: clear absolution_available.
    Message: "The ancestors smile." when Absolution triggers.
  balance: Absolution requires reaching exactly 0 debt — which means you must
    have used Pay the Price AND killed enough cursed enemies to clear the debt.
    This is a tight gameplay loop: sacrifice → curse → kill → Absolution → triple
    curse → bigger kill → sacrifice again. The Swagger bonus on each debt clearance
    provides consistent passive Swagger cycling for builds that invest in both.

L4 — "Flesh the Bargain" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None

L5 — "Even Steven" (passive — capstone)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None

DESIGN K — "BANE" (Multi-Curse Amplification)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
PoE's Bane ability — a linked curse that deals more damage per other curse
applied to the target — translated into NIGRL as a curse that acts as an
AMPLIFIER FIELD. You apply Bane to an enemy, and Bane passively reads how many
other debuffs are on the target, using that count to scale its own damage AND
to boost all other debuff effects simultaneously. The novel mechanical twist:
Bane also has a LIMIT BREAK mechanic. Each curse type on the Bane target fills
a spiritual meter. When the meter is full (typically 4-5 different curse types
simultaneously), Bane spontaneously OVERLOADS — dealing a massive burst and
refreshing ALL curses to full duration. This is a combo-meter that rewards
building a diverse debuff loadout, not just spamming one effect type.

PLAYSTYLE: Combo builder. You are assembling a curse cocktail. Every different
debuff type you can put on the Bane target pushes the meter forward. Optimal
play coordinates multiple skill trees (Smoking for bleeds, Stabbing for gouge,
Blackkk Magic for hexes, any food for temp debuffs). The OVERLOAD creates one
massive turn where all your setup pays off simultaneously.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Book Smarts (Bane damage per stack, overload damage). Street
Smarts (curse application success from passive sources, filling the meter faster).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Bane of Their Existence" (activated ability)
  perk_type: "activated"

  ability_id: "bane_of_existence"

  perk_type: "passive"

      burst = player.stats.effective_book_smarts * 5
      entity.hp -= burst
      for each player-sourced debuff on entity: effect.duration = effect.original_duration
        (need to store original_duration on Effect base class)
    entity.bane_overloaded_this_turn: bool (reset each turn in energy loop)
    Effect base class: add original_duration: int, set in apply() to the initial
      duration value.
    XP: +50 on OVERLOAD (significant reward for achieving the combo).
  balance: At BKS=10: OVERLOAD = 50 no-defense damage. Requires 5 distinct debuff
    types simultaneously — a serious build investment. In multi-skill-tree builds
    (Stabbing + Smoking + Blackkk Magic + food debuffs + gun debuffs): achievable
    on bosses in 4-5 turns. Pure Blackkk Magic build: harder; may need to combine
    with gouge, fear, slow, and one other source.

L4 — "Compound Interest" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    After each OVERLOAD event, Bane's tick damage per debuff increases permanently
    for the rest of the floor: base formula changes from BKS/4 per debuff to
    BKS/4 + OVERLOAD_COUNT per debuff. (OVERLOAD_COUNT starts at 0, increments
    +1 per OVERLOAD on this floor.) This means the second OVERLOAD triggers into
    a higher-baseline Bane, making subsequent OVERLOADs both easier to chain (more
    base damage softening the target) and more impactful.
    Also: after an OVERLOAD, the player's next melee or gun hit against the Bane
    target deals +BKS bonus damage (the spiritual resonance of the OVERLOAD echoes
    into physical force). One-time bonus per OVERLOAD, consumed on next hit.
  impl notes:
    engine.bane_overload_count: int (reset on floor change)
    In OVERLOAD trigger: engine.bane_overload_count += 1.
      engine.bane_overload_echo_ready = True (flag for next hit bonus)
    In BaneEffect.tick(): damage formula = floor((player.bks / 4) + engine.bane_overload_count) * bane_meter.
    In combat.py player melee/gun hit: if engine.bane_overload_echo_ready and
      target.bane_active: damage += player.stats.effective_book_smarts.
      Clear echo_ready.
  balance: First OVERLOAD: baseline unchanged. Second: +1/debuff per tick. Third:
    +2/debuff. In a long boss fight, the Bane damage compounds each cycle.
    Getting 3 OVERLOADs in one fight (requiring 15 distinct debuff applications
    total at minimum) is ambitious. The echo hit bonus rewards attacking immediately
    post-OVERLOAD.

L5 — "Everyone Hates You" (passive — area Bane)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When the Bane target dies, Bane SPREADS — simultaneously afflicting all living
    non-player entities within (1 + bane_meter) tiles with a fresh Bane (bane_active
    = True, bane_meter recalculated from their current debuffs). Spread Banes
    start with bane_meter = whatever that entity's current debuff count is (could
    be 0 if no debuffs). Spread Banes count toward the bane_meter fill for OVERLOAD.
    Additionally: when any Bane target (original or spread) triggers an OVERLOAD,
    the blast radius of the OVERLOAD burst is (1 + player.stats.effective_tolerance)
    tiles — hitting all entities in range for the full burst damage. The OVERLOAD
    is now an AOE event.
  impl notes:
    In entity_died handler with bane_active == True:
      spread_radius = 1 + entity.bane_meter
      For each living non-player entity within Chebyshev(spread_radius):
        new_entity.bane_active = True
        new_entity.bane_meter = min(5, count_player_debuffs(new_entity))
        Apply BaneEffect to new_entity with floor_duration=True.
        XP: +15 per spread application.
    OVERLOAD AOE: in OVERLOAD trigger, if engine.bane_overload_count > 0:
      aoe_radius = 1 + player.stats.effective_tolerance
      burst_damage = player.bks * 5
      For each entity within radius: entity.hp -= burst_damage (not just the primary target).
    First OVERLOAD: no AOE (overload_count == 0 when first triggers).
    Second+ OVERLOAD: full AOE. This is a late-fight snowball.
  balance: AOE OVERLOAD is the game-winning play in dense rooms. Requires two
    OVERLOADs (first is single-target). The spread Bane on death creates a room-
    wide curse field that can enable chain OVERLOADs if enemies are already debuffed.
    Power spike is high but requires surviving long enough to reach L5 + stack
    enough debuffs for two OVERLOADs.

================================================================================
DESIGN L — "THE NUMBERS GAME" (Curse Geometry / Hex Trap Tiles)
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY:
An entirely novel mechanic with no direct equivalent in any game: Hex Traps.
You inscribe spiritual sigils onto TILES (not enemies), turning them into curse
landmines. Enemies that walk onto a hex tile trigger the curse on that tile.
The geometry matters: some traps activate in a line, some in a cross pattern,
some only when an enemy is isolated (no adjacent friendlies). The design rewards
reading enemy movement patterns (from AI type knowledge) and pre-placing traps
on the paths you know enemies will walk. The capstone introduces the SPIRIT GRID —
connecting multiple traps in a network so that triggering one POWERS UP all
connected traps simultaneously.

PLAYSTYLE: Tactician / dungeon layer. You are fighting the dungeon, not just the
enemies. You place traps between you and enemies, funnel them through corridors,
and watch the geometry work for you. Weak in open-room fights where enemies can
approach from any direction. Dominant in corridors and chokepoints. Requires
pre-fight setup turns (traps are placed proactively). High knowledge reward —
understanding which AI types will walk predictable paths.

STAT DEPENDENCY: Street Smarts (trap trigger radius — STS-scaling determines
how far from a trap an enemy needs to step for it to activate). Book Smarts
(trap damage scaling).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

L1 — "Drop a Bag" (activated ability — tile targeting)
  perk_type: "activated"
  effect: {"ability": "drop_a_bag"}
  ability_id: "drop_a_bag"
  target_type: ADJACENT_TILE
  charge_type: PER_FLOOR
  max_charges: 3
  desc:
    Place a Hex Trap on an adjacent empty tile (entity_type="hex_trap", rendered
    as '^' in dark purple). The trap is dormant until a non-player living entity
    steps onto the trapped tile. On trigger: the entity receives "hex_snare"
    debuff (2-turn stun equivalent + 4-turn cripple_armor). The trap is consumed
    on trigger (one-time use). Traps last until triggered or floor change.
    Maximum 3 placed simultaneously (3 charges per floor, no refresh — once used
    they're gone). Traps are visible to the player but are they visible to enemies?
    At L1: yes (enemies with sight radius > 0 path around them if doing pathfinding
    via A*). At higher levels: traps become invisible to enemies.
  impl notes:
    HexTrap entity:
      entity_type = "hex_trap"
      char = '^' (ASCII caret, no graphical tile needed — fits tone)
      color = dark_purple (new DARK_PURPLE constant)
      blocks_movement = False (entities walk onto it)
      trap_effect = "hex_snare"
    In energy loop movement step (ai.py): after each entity step, check if new
      tile has a HexTrap entity. If so: apply hex_snare debuff; remove HexTrap
      entity from dungeon.
    hex_snare: 2-turn stun + 4-turn cripple_armor (combined; implement as a
      single SnareEffect with both behaviors, or apply two separate effects).
    PER_FLOOR 3: 3 placements per floor, no regen.
    Enemies path around visible traps: add hex_trap tiles to the pathfinding
      cost map (high cost, not impassable, so enemies avoid if possible).
    Blackkk Magic XP: +15 when a trap triggers.
  balance: 3 traps/floor is enough to set meaningful chokepoints. In a corridor
    of 5 enemies: trap at tile 3 catches the leading enemy, snaring them while
    the player retreats. The 2-turn stun is the hook. Cripple_armor (reduced
    defense) turns the trap into offensive setup.

L2 — "Hidden in Plain Sight" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Hex Traps are now INVISIBLE to enemies — they no longer appear in pathfinding
    cost maps as obstacles. Enemies walk directly over them without attempting
    to path around. They still appear as '^' to the PLAYER (you can see your own
    traps). This is the core tactical upgrade: traps that enemies avoid are
    situational obstacles; traps enemies walk into are guaranteed triggers.
    Additionally: when a trap triggers on an enemy, all other enemies within 3
    tiles of the trigger point are "alerted" and enter CHASING state (they know
    where the snared enemy is). This creates a choice: set traps at chokepoints
    (where alerted enemies will funnel), or set traps in open areas (where the
    alert scatters them in multiple approaches).
  impl notes:
    Remove HexTrap entities from pathfinding cost map (stop adding them to the
    blocked-cell layer that A* uses). The PLAYER render still shows them.
    Alert on trigger: in trap_trigger logic, after applying hex_snare, scan
      all dungeon entities within Chebyshev(3) of trap tile. For each enemy:
        if entity.ai_state not CHASING: set ai_state = CHASING.
        Set entity.target_x, target_y = trap.x, trap.y (move toward the trigger).
    This alert is a double-edged sword: it funnels enemies toward your trap but
    also toward you if you're near the trap.
  balance: Invisible traps mean guaranteed triggers in corridors. The alert means
    triggering one trap will draw nearby enemies — you're creating a fight, not
    avoiding one. Net result: traps are now tactical commitment, not passive avoidance.

L3 — "Pattern Recognition" (passive — unlocks conditional trap types)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    Hex Traps now have 3 variants selectable at placement time (secondary key
    prompt: H=Hex_Snare default, L=Line_Blast, I=Isolation_Trap):
    LINE BLAST: triggers not just at the trap tile but in a 5-tile line emanating
      from the trap toward the PLAYER's current position at trigger time. All
      entities in the line receive (BKS) no-defense damage + slow (ratio=0.4, 5t).
    ISOLATION TRAP: triggers only if the stepping entity has NO adjacent allies
      (no living non-player entities within Chebyshev(1) of the trigger point).
      Effect: full normal hex_snare PLUS an additional 3-turn fear. Designed for
      lone enemies that patrol away from their group.
    Trap variants share the same 3-charge pool — choosing a variant is about
    spending charges wisely on the right tactical setup.
  impl notes:
    HexTrap entity: add trap_type: str ("snare", "line_blast", "isolation").
    Secondary key selection at placement: after adjacenttile targeting, prompt
      "H=Snare L=Line I=Isolation."
    LINE_BLAST trigger: compute direction vector from trap.x/y to player.x/y;
      extend 5 tiles; for each tile in line: apply damage and slow to any entity.
    ISOLATION trigger: before applying effect, count entities within Chebyshev(1)
      of trigger tile. If count == 0 (just the triggering entity): apply snare +
      3-turn fear. If count > 0: apply nothing (trap fizzles — missed the window).
    Isolation trap fizzle: consume the trap entity but apply no effect. Message:
      "Isolation trap fizzled — target wasn't alone."
  balance: Line Blast is strong in corridors (guaranteed to hit everything behind
    the trap in the direction you're standing). Isolation Trap is high-risk,
    high-reward: fizzles if you misread the AI grouping. The three variants mean
    trap placement becomes a tactical decision per encounter.

L4 — "Dead Giveaway" (passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When a Hex Trap triggers, it immediately REFRESHES one random trap charge
    for the player — effectively reducing trap consumption. Each trigger has a
    (BKS * 5)% chance to regenerate one charge of the drop_a_bag ability (up to
    max charges). At BKS=10: 50% refund chance. This means in a trap-heavy
    encounter, roughly half of triggered traps refund themselves — rewarding
    players who commit to heavy trap play.
    Additionally: snared enemies (hex_snare active) take +25% damage from all
    sources while snared (they're spiritually pinned and vulnerable). This turns
    the snare into offensive setup as well as CC.
  impl notes:
    In trap_trigger logic: after applying hex_snare, roll random() < player.bks * 0.05.
    If pass: find the drop_a_bag ability instance in engine.player_abilities;
      if charges < max_charges: charges += 1. Message: "Trap refunds."
    Snare vulnerability: in combat.py damage resolution, if target has hex_snare
      effect: multiply damage by 1.25 (round up). For all sources.
    The +25% vulnerability applies to the snare's 2-turn stun window — a short
    window to exploit. Coordinates with melee attacks and Profane Bloom damage.
  balance: 50% refund at BKS=10 means on a 3-enemy-trigger run, expected trap
    consumption = 1.5 charges instead of 3. Essentially infinite traps in the
    right encounter. Balanced by the trap placement cost (time and positioning).
    The snare vulnerability (+25%) is modest but consistent.

L5 — "The Spirit Grid" (passive — trap networking)
  perk_type: "passive"
  effect: None
  desc:
    When two or more Hex Traps are placed within 4 tiles of each other, they form
    a Spirit Grid — a visible line connects them (rendered as '·' dots between
    trap positions, faintly visible to player only). When ONE grid-connected trap
    triggers, ALL other traps in the same grid POWER UP for 3 turns: their damage
    doubles, their snare duration doubles (4 turns → 8 turns for the stun portion),
    and they emit a dim aura pulse (cosmetic). If the powered-up traps trigger
    during those 3 turns: they fire at doubled strength. After 3 turns, power-up
    fades back to normal.
    Multiple traps can form one large grid (if trap A is within 4 of B, and B
    is within 4 of C: all three are in the same grid even if A and C are 6 apart).
  impl notes:
    dungeon.trap_grid: list of sets, each set = a group of connected HexTrap IDs.
    When placing a new trap: check all existing traps within Chebyshev(4).
      If any found: merge their grid sets with the new trap.
    On trap trigger: find the grid containing this trap; for all OTHER traps in
      same grid: set trap_entity.powered_up = True, trap_entity.power_up_turns = 3.
    In trap_trigger logic: if trap_entity.powered_up: double damage, double stun
      duration.
    Each turn: decrement power_up_turns on powered traps; clear flag at 0.
    Grid visualization: for each pair of grid-connected traps, draw midpoint cells
      between them with char='·' in a faint purple. Update on each trap add/remove.
    Grid update on trap removal: when a trap is triggered or expires, remove it
      from its grid set, re-evaluate connectivity for remaining traps.
  balance: In a corridor with 3 grid-connected traps: triggering the entrance trap
    powers up the two interior traps. The next two enemies to step on powered traps
    get 8-turn stuns with doubled damage. This creates a "corridor from hell"
    scenario that can pin 3 enemies for 8 turns. Corridor-specific but dominant
    in that context. In open rooms: harder to maintain grid connectivity (enemies
    approach from all angles, consume traps quickly).

================================================================================
BALANCE CALIBRATION NOTES
================================================================================

Damage reference:
  Floor 1-2 enemies: 15-30 HP, 0-3 defense
  Floor 3-4 enemies: 30-60 HP, 2-5 defense
  Player HP (CON=10): 130 HP
  Player typical damage per hit: 5-12 + STR scaling

Design C (Doom Clock): Max Hexburst = BKS*5 = 60 at BKS=12. This would one-shot
  nearly any non-boss enemy on floor 4. The 5-turn setup requirement is the
  hard limit. On floor 1-2 enemies that die in 1-3 hits: Doom rarely reaches
  max. Design is boss-focused by construction.

Design D (Haunt): Return heal = 3 + BKS/4 = 6 at BKS=12 per ghost. With two
  ghosts: 12 HP per kill cycle. At 5 kills/floor: 60 HP recovered. Significant
  sustained healing. But each ghost takes 1+ turns to travel first.

Design E (Impending Doom): Max Hexbomb = 4+BKS = 16 direct + 7 AOE at BKS=12.
  With Nuclear Option (below 25%): 24 direct + 10 AOE, radius 4. Strong execute
  but bomb must count down 8 turns — most enemies die faster than that on early
  floors, making Dead Man's Switch (L2) critical for value.

Design F (Punishment): At SWG=12, karma=10: blowback = ceil((2+3)*2.5) = 13
  per attack. Aggressive enemies attacking 2x/turn: 26 self-damage/turn. Dies
  in 2-3 turns from blowback alone if HP < 50. Extremely strong against fast
  attackers; weak against slow ones.

Design G (Temporal Chains): 50% attack skip + 30% energy reduction at max stats
  = ~65% DPS reduction from cursed enemy. This is near-invincibility in 1v1.
  Requires TOL=10 for max values. Time Stop (1/floor) is the strongest individual
  ability in any Blackkk Magic design — deliberately limited to 1/floor.

Design H (Voodoo Doll): Double-hitting both enemies via Needle Drop requires
  adjacency to the Doll. In tight rooms: achievable. Linking boss+add:
  standard damage to add = also full damage to boss. Game-changing against
  "boss + escort" enemy patterns in late floors.

Design I (Profane Bloom): Chain reactions require enemies dying to bloom splash
  (9 damage at STS=10, L1). This kills weak enemies (HP 15-18) reliably on
  floor 1. On floor 3: bloom splash alone may not kill — need DoT pre-damage.
  Unholy Ground (L5) is the most persistent room-control tool in any design.

Design J (Spiritual Debt): 20% lifesteal at zero debt (L5) in extended fights
  (10 hits for 10 avg): 20 HP recovered per 10 hits. vs player HP=130: modest.
  But in a full floor of 8 enemies (80 hits dealt): 160 HP recovered — more than
  max HP. Makes the player nearly unkillable in zero-debt state but fragile while
  in debt. The oscillation between states is the core balance.

Design K (Bane): OVERLOAD at BKS*5 = 60 no-defense at BKS=12. Requires 5 distinct
  debuff types. A dedicated multi-system build can assemble: slow + fear + stun
  + bleeding + soul_tax = 5 types. On floor 4 boss with 80 HP: OVERLOAD + refreshed
  debuffs + L4 echo hit = plausible one-turn death combo. Very powerful if the
  player has invested widely in multiple skill trees.

Design L (Numbers Game): 3-charge traps PER FLOOR with 50% refund at BKS=10 (L4):
  expected 6 effective traps per floor. Spirit Grid power-up doubles snare to
  8-turn stun. An 8-turn stun on a boss-tier enemy is extremely powerful in a
  system where most CC lasts 2-5 turns. Balanced by trap placement investment
  and corridor-specificity.
