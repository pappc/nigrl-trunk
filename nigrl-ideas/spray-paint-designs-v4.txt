SPRAY PAINT DESIGNS V4 — 10 NEW COLORS
=======================================
NIGRL roguelike | Design document
Date: 2026-03-30

Purpose: Add 10 completely new spray paint colors to the roster,
bringing the total to 25. Each design is mechanically distinct from
all prior paints and targets an underserved playstyle niche.

FULL EXISTING ROSTER (must not overlap):
  Red       — enemy on tile: +25% damage taken
  Blue      — player: +5 STS; 25% ability charge preserve
  Green     — player: +4 Defense
  Orange    — all entities: 5+2*Graffiti dmg/turn
  Silver    — entity entry: slip (turn skip + 50% melee vuln)
  Gold      — kill on tile: +$5 cash
  Teal      — player: +20% skill XP
  Lime      — enemy: tox/turn; player: minor tox
  Grey      — enemy: -(1+Graffiti) outgoing damage
  Rad       — enemy: rad/turn; player: rad/turn (Meth Lab)
  Crimson   — player melee: 20% lifesteal
  Copper    — player: +30 energy/tick; enemy: -20 energy/tick
  Cobalt    — player: +4 spell damage; 50% charge refund
  Violet    — player: +(3+Graf) melee dmg; enemies deal +3 back
  Rust      — enemy: accumulate -1 def/turn (max -10, floor-permanent)

DESIGN GOALS FOR V4:
  - Gun-build tile (none exist yet)
  - Dodge/evasion tile (none exist yet)
  - Temp HP generation tile (none exist yet)
  - Fear/flee control tile (none exist yet)
  - CON/HP-threshold scaling tile
  - On-kill effect beyond cash (different from Gold)
  - Swagger/defense scaling interaction
  - Adjacent tile manipulation
  - Tolerance/TOL scaling
  - Meth Lab Meth resource interaction

PAINT 16 — MIDNIGHT (DARK BLUE-BLACK)
============================
Item ID:    midnight_spray_paint
Name:       Midnight Spray Paint
RGB:        (25, 20, 90)
Render bg (visible):   (12, 10, 48)
Render bg (explored):  (6, 5, 24)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Player on tile — gains a flat +15% dodge chance bonus while
  standing here (added directly to player_stats.dodge_chance).
  Works against ALL incoming melee and ranged attacks that go through
  the standard hit-vs-dodge roll.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  No existing paint touches the dodge system. Midnight fills the
  "evasion build" niche: a high-STS or Stealing-tree player who
  already has 15-25% dodge from other sources can push their evasion
  well past 30% while standing on Midnight. For builds without existing
  dodge sources the tile still offers meaningful protection (15% is
  roughly 1-in-7 hits avoided). Unlike Green (+4 flat defense that
  reduces all damage), Midnight is probabilistic — you might dodge
  three in a row or eat five consecutive hits. Variance is the
  tactical tension.

  Midnight is the darkest tile on the map, making it visually
  identifiable as a "shadow" to retreat to under fire. The flavor
  fits the street setting: painting your corner dark helps you
  disappear.

Scale with Graffiti:
  dodge_bonus = 10 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl percent.
  L0: +10%; L1: +15%; L2: +20%; L3: +25%.
  Cap effective total dodge at 60% (if player_stats.dodge_chance
  would exceed 60% after all bonuses, clamp to 60).

Synergies:
  Blue + Midnight: Blue gives +5 STS (boosting whatever STS-based
  dodge sources exist). Midnight adds flat dodge on top. Blue on
  the approach tile, Midnight on the fighting tile maximizes evasion
  during combat.
  Copper + Midnight (sequential tiles): Copper grants energy tempo;
  Midnight grants evasion. A speed-evasion build that moves between
  the two tiles after each action frustrates enemies that rely on
  melee trading.
  Stealing skill tree: passive dodge bonuses from Stealing perks
  stack with Midnight, potentially pushing dodge over 50% total.
  If the Stealing perk "Slippery Fingers" or similar grants dodge,
  standing on Midnight can push it to 45-55%.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile midnight."
  "Player on tile: +(10+5*Graffiti)%"
  "dodge chance."
  "Total dodge capped at 60%."

Implementation notes:
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses (engine.py):
    elif spray == "midnight":
        graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        bonus = 0.10 + 0.05 * graffiti_lvl
        self.player_stats.dodge_chance = min(
            0.60, self.player_stats.dodge_chance + bonus
        )
  IMPORTANT: tile bonus must be reset (same as tile_defense_bonus) when
  the player steps off. On step-off:
    self.player_stats.dodge_chance -= bonus  # then clamp at 0
  Because player_stats.dodge_chance is built from permanent + ring
  + tile bonuses in layers, it is safer to store the tile dodge bonus
  as a new field: tile_dodge_bonus: float = 0.0
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses:
    elif spray == "midnight":
        self.player_stats.tile_dodge_bonus = 0.10 + 0.05 * graffiti_lvl
  Then wherever dodge_chance is read (the hit-vs-dodge check in
  combat.py), use:
    effective_dodge = player_stats.dodge_chance + player_stats.tile_dodge_bonus
  New PlayerStats field: tile_dodge_bonus: float = 0.0
  No new Effect class needed.

Balance notes:
  +15% dodge at L1 is the rough equivalent of +3-4 Defense against a
  uniform damage stream (depends on hit frequency). Green gives a
  guaranteed damage reduction; Midnight gives probabilistic protection
  with higher theoretical ceiling. Against fast enemies that swing
  multiple times per turn sequence, Midnight's value scales with hit
  frequency, while Green's stays flat. Neither strictly dominates.

PAINT 17 — MAGENTA (HOT PINK-RED)
============================
Item ID:    magenta_spray_paint
Name:       Magenta Spray Paint
RGB:        (240, 40, 160)
Render bg (visible):   (115, 12, 70)
Render bg (explored):  (57, 6, 35)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Player on tile — whenever the player kills an enemy (any damage
  source) while standing on a magenta tile, generate +10 Temp HP.
  Temp HP obeys the existing absorption order (Temp HP → Armor → HP)
  and does NOT stack above current max HP.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Gold gives $5 per kill-on-tile. Magenta gives 10 Temp HP per
  kill-on-tile. Both are kill-reward tiles; their payoffs serve
  different build types (economy vs. sustain).

  Temp HP as a kill reward is specifically distinct from Crimson
  (which gives real HP regen as a % of damage dealt during combat).
  Magenta is burst-defensive: you get a surge of ablative protection
  after the kill, protecting you against the NEXT enemy's first hit.
  Against groups, chaining kills on a magenta tile can maintain a
  permanent Temp HP buffer. Against single elites it provides one
  surge of protection after they die.

  Flavor: painting the floor in magenta and making kills feel
  "electric" — each death charges you up. Pairs with the energy
  theme of the Crack Den's neon graffiti aesthetic.

Scale with Graffiti:
  temp_hp_per_kill = 8 + 2*Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: +8; L1: +10; L2: +12; L3: +14.
  Cap total Temp HP at entity.max_hp (can't exceed full HP bar).

Synergies:
  Orange + Magenta: Orange deals DoT to everyone. The player
  can use it as a mass-execution tool and collect Temp HP surges
  as enemies die. However, Orange also damages the player — Temp HP
  from Magenta partially offsets the Orange self-damage if kills are
  fast enough.
  CON-heavy builds: high max HP means each kill restores a larger
  fraction of their buffer. A CON 12 character (max HP ~150) treating
  +10 Temp HP as a "top-up" gets less value per kill than a frail
  BKS caster (max HP 70) treating it as a 14% buffer restoration.
  Violet + Magenta (adjacent tiles): Violet accelerates kills (more
  damage per swing), Magenta rewards kills. Killing fast from Violet,
  then retreating to Magenta if HP is low — two-tile kill loop.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile magenta."
  "Player on tile: +10 Temp HP"
  "per kill (any damage source)."
  "(Scales with Graffiti level.)"

Implementation notes:
  Hook into the entity death event (same location as the gold tile
  cash payout, the Graffiti L2 kill heal, etc.) in engine.py:
    if (self.dungeon.spray_paint.get((self.player.x, self.player.y))
            == "magenta"):
        graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        temp_gain = 8 + 2 * graffiti_lvl
        self.player.temp_hp = min(
            self.player.temp_hp + temp_gain,
            self.player.max_hp
        )
        self.messages.append(
            f"Magenta: +{temp_gain} Temp HP!"
        )
  No new Effect class or PlayerStats field needed.
  This check runs when self.player is alive and is the one who
  caused or triggered the kill — use the same death event handler
  that already runs the gold/graffiti-heal checks.

Balance notes:
  +10 Temp HP per kill is comparable to ~1 turn of survival against
  a floor 2 enemy (who might deal 8-12 damage). Against low-HP
  enemies (grunts, weaklings) that die in 1-2 hits, the Temp HP
  keeps pace with incoming damage. Against tanky enemies that take
  5+ hits before dying, the Temp HP doesn't arrive until after
  significant damage is taken — balancing the benefit.

PAINT 18 — NAVY (GUN-BUFF BLUE)
============================
Item ID:    navy_spray_paint
Name:       Navy Spray Paint
RGB:        (20, 55, 140)
Render bg (visible):   (8, 22, 65)
Render bg (explored):  (4, 11, 32)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 45

Effect:
  Player on tile — two simultaneous gun bonuses:
    1. All gun shots fired from this tile gain +(2+Graffiti_Lvl)
       flat damage bonus per bullet (applied after base gun damage
       is computed, before defense).
    2. Gun ammo has a 25% chance to not be consumed per shot (ammo
       preserved — current_ammo does not decrease on that shot).
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  No existing paint interacts with the gun system. Navy is the
  dedicated gun-build tile. The flat damage bonus compensates for
  the gun system's reliance on ammo types (light/medium/heavy have
  fixed damage tiers); +2-5 bonus bridges the gap between ammo tiers.
  The 25% ammo preservation means a full magazine statistically
  lasts 33% longer (every 4 shots, 1 is free).

  Cobalt serves spell-casters exclusively (spell_damage + charge
  refund). Navy serves gun builds exclusively (gun damage + ammo
  efficiency). These are complementary tiles that don't compete.

  Flavor: a sniper's nest, carefully marked in navy. Once you paint
  it, you own that position — every shot fired from it counts more
  and costs less.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Damage bonus = 2 + Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: +2; L1: +3; L2: +4; L3: +5.
  Ammo preservation chance stays flat at 25%.
  (A higher chance would degenerate into near-infinite ammo for
  high-level Graffiti builds; 25% is sufficient incentive.)

Synergies:
  Red + Navy (two-tile setup): Red tile under the enemy amplifies
  all damage they take (+25%). Navy tile on the player's position
  adds gun damage per shot AND preserves ammo. Result: every shot
  from Navy at a Red-painted enemy is amplified from both ends.
  Sniping skill tree: Sniping already provides crit bonuses and
  accuracy. Navy's flat damage bonus applies to crit hits too,
  scaling up the crit multiplier's base. A megacrit from Navy
  against a Red enemy is multiplicative mayhem.
  Cobalt vs Navy: a hybrid spell+gun build must choose which tile
  to commit to. This is an intentional strategic tension for builds
  using both ability types.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile navy."
  "Player: +(2+Graffiti) gun dmg/shot."
  "25% chance: shot uses no ammo."

Implementation notes:
  GUN DAMAGE BONUS:
  In gun_system.py (the firing function, after base damage is
  computed per shot, before defense subtraction):
    spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get(
        (engine.player.x, engine.player.y)
    )
    if spray == "navy":
        graffiti_lvl = engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        damage += 2 + graffiti_lvl

  AMMO PRESERVATION:
  After the standard current_ammo -= 1 line:
    spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get(
        (engine.player.x, engine.player.y)
    )
    if spray == "navy" and random.random() < 0.25:
        entity.current_ammo += 1  # refund the shot
        engine.messages.append("Navy: shot didn't cost ammo!")
  (Alternatively, gate the ammo decrement: if spray == navy and
  random() < 0.25 → skip the decrement.)

  Both checks run in the gun firing path in gun_system.py.
  No new Effect or PlayerStats fields needed.

Balance notes:
  +3 gun damage at L1 Graffiti on a medium gun dealing ~12-16 base
  is roughly a 20% damage increase. Significant but gun damage is
  already lower-upside than melee burst. The ammo bonus is quality-
  of-life more than power: it extends ammo reserves, making the
  gun viable for longer fights without reloading every 6 shots.
  8 charges: reduced from 10 to force placement decisions — a
  dedicated gun position rather than carpeting corridors.
  Wait — keeping 10 charges; gun builds need consistent tile access.

PAINT 19 — AMBER (WARM YELLOW-ORANGE)
============================
Item ID:    amber_spray_paint
Name:       Amber Spray Paint
RGB:        (255, 175, 0)
Render bg (visible):   (125, 80, 0)
Render bg (explored):  (62, 40, 0)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Enemy entering or standing on tile — each turn, if the enemy's
  current HP is at or below 50% of their max HP (bloodied threshold),
  they must pass a fear check: 50% chance to become "feared" for
  2 turns (flee behavior — enemy moves away from player).
  If their HP is above 50%, no check is made.
  Player on tile — no effect (player does not fear from amber).

Design rationale:
  The game has no existing "fear from environmental source" mechanic.
  Amber provides a fear tile that activates conditionally on enemy HP
  state — it doesn't apply fear blindly like a confusion tile would.
  This rewards players who damage enemies before funneling them onto
  amber. Against full-HP enemies, amber does nothing; against wounded
  enemies, it breaks their nerve.

  Tactically: place amber in a room entrance or corridor midpoint.
  Wound an enemy with ranged shots or spells, then fall back. The
  wounded enemy chasing you steps onto amber and may flee back the
  way it came — buying you positioning time, creating kiting
  opportunities, or splitting a group.

  Unlike Silver (guaranteed CC on entry), amber is conditional and
  repeated-roll: the enemy might resist the fear check multiple turns
  in a row, or it might flee immediately. Variance matters here.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Fear check threshold: enemy HP at or below (45 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl)%
  of max HP.
  L0: below 45%; L1: below 50%; L2: below 55%; L3: below 60%.
  At L3, enemies with up to 60% HP remaining can be feared by amber.
  Fear duration stays flat at 2 turns.
  Fear chance stays flat at 50% per turn on tile.

Synergies:
  Orange + Amber: Orange damages all entities on its tile, including
  enemies. Enemies dragged across orange drop below 50% faster, making
  amber's threshold proc more likely. Place amber just past orange
  in a corridor: enemy walks through orange (takes damage), then
  hits amber (feared if bloodied). A two-tile gauntlet.
  Lime + Amber (Meth Lab): lime stacks tox (inflates incoming damage),
  not HP directly. But subsequent weapon hits deal more damage with
  tox up, so lime accelerates the HP drop that triggers amber's fear.
  Grey + Amber: grey weakens bloodied enemies' attacks while amber
  sends them fleeing. Wounded enemy on grey: they hit softer anyway.
  Chase them to amber: they flee, unable to fight back.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile amber."
  "Enemy on tile: if HP<=50% max,"
  "50% chance to flee (2 turns)."
  "Check repeats each turn on tile."

Implementation notes:
  In the per-tick entity loop (alongside orange DoT block):
    if spray == "amber":
        if monster.alive:
            threshold_pct = 0.45 + 0.05 * graffiti_lvl
            if monster.hp <= monster.max_hp * threshold_pct:
                if not has_effect(monster, "feared"):
                    if random.random() < 0.50:
                        apply_effect(monster, engine,
                                     "feared", duration=2)
                        engine.messages.append(
                            "The enemy flees in terror!"
                        )
  "feared" effect should already exist in effects.py (it is
  referenced as an existing status in CLAUDE.md / the effects
  system). If not, create FearEffect with flee-from-player movement
  behavior in before_turn hook, duration 2. This is the same flee
  behavior as other flee AI states.
  No new PlayerStats field needed.

Balance notes:
  50% chance per turn means an enemy standing still on amber will
  fear roughly every other turn. In practice enemies rarely stand
  still — they move through the tile. The window is typically 1-2
  turns of exposure per encounter. Expected outcome: maybe 1 fear
  proc per bloodied enemy per engagement. This is soft CC, not
  hard CC. The HP threshold gate means healthy enemies are entirely
  immune, preventing amber from trivializing early encounters.

PAINT 20 — BONE (OFF-WHITE)
============================
Item ID:    bone_spray_paint
Name:       Bone Spray Paint
RGB:        (235, 220, 185)
Render bg (visible):   (110, 100, 75)
Render bg (explored):  (55, 50, 37)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Player on tile — incoming damage is reduced by a percentage based
  on the player's current HP vs. max HP. The lower the player's HP,
  the stronger the damage reduction.
  Formula:
    dr_bonus = floor((1.0 - current_hp/max_hp) * 20)
  This means:
    At 100% HP: +0% DR (no bonus)
    At  75% HP: +5% DR
    At  50% HP: +10% DR
    At  25% HP: +15% DR
    At  10% HP: +18% DR
  The reduction is a percentage applied to incoming damage BEFORE
  defense subtraction (or after, as a separate multiplier step):
    effective_damage = raw_damage * (1.0 - bone_dr_bonus/100)
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  This is a CON-scaling "last stand" tile. High-CON players have more
  HP and take longer to reach low thresholds — meaning they gain DR
  slower but from a safer position. Low-CON fragile builds hit low
  thresholds faster, activating stronger DR sooner as a survival
  mechanism. The tile is self-correcting: it's weakest when you don't
  need it and strongest when you're in danger.

  Unlike Green (+4 flat defense, always-on), Bone is a responsive
  tile that reads the player's current health state and adjusts
  accordingly. It rewards standing your ground rather than retreating
  when wounded.

  Flavor: the bone color evokes grim determination — you're down to
  your last dregs, fighting on the painted bones of the tile.

Scale with Graffiti:
  dr_multiplier = 20 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: max 20% DR; L1: max 25% DR; L2: max 30% DR; L3: max 35% DR.
  Formula at Graffiti L2:
    dr_bonus = floor((1.0 - current_hp/max_hp) * 30)
  Cap at 35% DR regardless of Graffiti level (prevents trivialization).

Synergies:
  Crimson + Bone (adjacent tiles): Crimson provides lifesteal to pull
  HP back up; Bone rewards being low HP with DR. At low HP, the player
  stands on Bone (DR active), then swings from Bone to trigger Crimson
  lifesteal (if Crimson is also their tile). Wait — these are
  different tiles; the player can only be on one at a time. Setup:
  paint Bone on the fighting tile, Crimson on the retreat tile.
  Low-HP player fights from Bone (DR mitigates hits), then steps to
  Crimson briefly to leech HP back up.
  Magenta + Bone: Magenta gives Temp HP on kills (buffers incoming
  damage). Bone DR reduces that incoming damage further. Both are
  defensive; Magenta is proactive (kill rewards), Bone is reactive
  (HP-responsive). Overlap nicely without canceling each other.
  CON-heavy builds: a CON 12 player at 50% HP still has 65 HP
  remaining — they're not desperate but Bone gives 10-15% DR anyway.
  A CON 6 player at 50% HP has only 30 HP — same tile gives same
  relative DR. The % formula equalizes across CON values.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile bone."
  "Player on tile: damage reduction"
  "scales with missing HP."
  "Low HP = up to 20% DR bonus."

Implementation notes:
  In combat.py, after computing raw damage to the player (pre-defense
  or post-defense depending on preferred implementation), add a check:
    spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get(
        (engine.player.x, engine.player.y)
    )
    if spray == "bone" and engine.player.max_hp > 0:
        graffiti_lvl = engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        max_dr = 20 + 5 * graffiti_lvl
        missing_hp_ratio = 1.0 - (
            engine.player.hp / engine.player.max_hp
        )
        dr_pct = min(max_dr, int(missing_hp_ratio * max_dr))
        # Apply as damage multiplier:
        damage = max(1, int(damage * (1.0 - dr_pct / 100)))
  Position this check in the incoming-damage-to-player path,
  alongside the tox multiplier, swagger_defence subtraction, etc.
  No new Effect or PlayerStats field needed — inline computation.

Balance notes:
  At full HP (typical tile entry state): 0 bonus. The tile does
  nothing until the player takes damage. This means the tile is
  "wasted" on healthy players who paint it proactively, adding
  planning risk: you might paint bone before a fight but not need
  it, wasting a charge on a tile that gave zero benefit.
  The max 20-35% DR is meaningful but not invulnerability — a 10-
  damage hit at 25% HP becomes 8.5 (round to 9). Against a 20-
  damage hit at 10% HP with L3: 20 * 0.65 = 13. Still hurts.
  Synergy cap: Bone DR does not stack with other % DR effects
  (if any). If a "DR" field already exists on PlayerStats (from
  White Power trees, etc.), Bone's reduction is applied after that.
  Alternatively it can be additive if the formula doesn't exceed
  the global DR cap.

PAINT 21 — SLATE (BLUE-GREY)
============================
Item ID:    slate_spray_paint
Name:       Slate Spray Paint
RGB:        (90, 110, 135)
Render bg (visible):   (38, 48, 60)
Render bg (explored):  (19, 24, 30)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Player on tile — Swagger defense is doubled while standing here.
  The swagger_defence formula is normally: int((eff_swagger - 8) / 2).
  On Slate: effective_defense_from_swagger is doubled:
    swagger_defence_on_slate = int((eff_swagger - 8) / 2) * 2
  This effectively doubles the damage mitigation derived from SWG.
  Swagger itself (the stat value) does NOT change — only the
  defensive calculation is modified.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Green gives a flat +4 defense to everyone equally. Slate rewards
  building into Swagger. A player with 8 Swagger gains 0 extra
  defense (0 * 2 = 0). A player with 10 Swagger gains 2 defense
  (1 * 2 = 2). A player with 14 Swagger (possible with ring bonuses
  and perks) gains 6 defense (3 * 2 = 6) — and that grows further
  with perk stacking.

  This creates a genuine build decision: invest in Swagger (which has
  other benefits across multiple skill trees like Drive-By, White
  Power, etc.) and Slate tiles become significantly more valuable.
  Non-Swagger builds get nothing from Slate (just like non-casters
  get nothing from Cobalt). Build-gated reward tiles are a recurring
  design principle in V3/V4.

  Flavor: Slate is the color of concrete and armor plating. Standing
  on painted slate feels like putting on an invisible vest — but only
  if you've got the swagger to wear it.

Scale with Graffiti:
  swagger_defence_multiplier = 2 + Graffiti_Lvl // 2.
  L0: ×2; L1: ×2; L2: ×2.5; L3: ×2.5; L4: ×3.
  (At low Graffiti levels the multiplier stays at ×2; at L2+ it
  grows. This prevents Graffiti + Slate from scaling too early.)
  At L3 Graffiti, a SWG 14 player gains: int((14-8)/2) * 2.5 = 7.5
  → 7 defense from Swagger alone. Plus weapon defense, other bonuses.

Synergies:
  Drive-By skill: provides Swagger bonuses, directly improving Slate's
  value. A Drive-By build with 14 Swagger standing on Slate has the
  strongest Slate benefit.
  White Power Tox Inversion trees: some perks reference Swagger in
  their damage mitigation calculations. Doubling Swagger defense on
  Slate amplifies these synergies.
  Green + Slate (adjacent tiles): Green flat defense on approach tile,
  Slate doubled-swagger on fighting tile. A layered defense wall.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile slate."
  "Player on tile: Swagger defense"
  "is doubled (Swagger builds only)."
  "No benefit below 8 Swagger."

Implementation notes:
  The swagger_defence calculation in stats.py (or wherever
  effective_defense is computed for combat) needs to be modified when
  the player is on a slate tile. Add a new PlayerStats boolean:
    tile_swagger_double: bool = False
  Set it in _update_tile_stat_bonuses:
    elif spray == "slate":
        graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        mult = 2 + (graffiti_lvl // 2) * 0.5  # 2, 2, 2.5, 2.5, 3
        self.player_stats.tile_swagger_mult = mult
  (Use a float multiplier field instead of bool for Graffiti scaling.)
  New PlayerStats field: tile_swagger_mult: float = 1.0
  In effective_defense computation in stats.py or combat.py:
    swg_def = int((eff_swagger - 8) / 2)
    swg_def = int(swg_def * player_stats.tile_swagger_mult)
    effective_defense += swg_def
  Reset tile_swagger_mult to 1.0 when stepping off.

Balance notes:
  At Swagger 8 (default): completely useless. At Swagger 10: +1 extra
  defense (not impressive, matches green). At Swagger 12: +2 extra
  defense (matching green, but build-gated). At Swagger 14 (ring
  boosts): +3 extra defense (surpassing green, justifying SWG builds).
  This creates a genuine "SWG must be at least 10 to use this" gate
  that gives purpose to investing in Swagger as a primary defensive
  stat. Without Slate, Green is always better than Swagger-defense for
  neutral builds. With Slate, Swagger builds have a tile that rivals
  and then exceeds Green.

PAINT 22 — CHARTREUSE (NEON YELLOW-GREEN)
============================
Item ID:    chartreuse_spray_paint
Name:       Chartreuse Spray Paint
RGB:        (170, 255, 40)
Render bg (visible):   (75, 120, 12)
Render bg (explored):  (37, 60, 6)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Player on tile — each skill XP gain event that occurs while the
  player stands on chartreuse has a 15% chance to award a bonus
  SP (Skill Point) directly — NOT going through the
  potential_exp → real_exp conversion, but instead a direct
  +1 to the currently-active (most recently gained) skill's
  available SP pool.
  Specifically: each time skills.gain_potential_exp(skill_name, xp)
  resolves into at least 1 real XP point, roll 15% for +1 direct SP.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Teal paint gives +20% skill XP (more XP → faster level-up path).
  Chartreuse gives DIRECT SP rewards (bypasses the potential/real
  conversion, the main bottleneck in skill progression).

  These address different parts of the dual XP system:
  - Teal accelerates potential XP gain (you earn faster).
  - Chartreuse occasionally skips the bottleneck entirely (free SP).

  In practice, Teal smooths out the XP curve over time. Chartreuse
  is spiked and unpredictable — you might get 3 free SP in a lucky
  room or none in an unlucky one. The variance creates excitement.

  Flavor: chartreuse is the color of chemical spills and toxic
  electricity — it feels "crackling" with potential energy, like XP
  is jumping out of your skin.

Scale with Graffiti:
  SP chance = 10 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl percent.
  L0: 10%; L1: 15%; L2: 20%; L3: 25%.
  One bonus SP per trigger maximum (not compounding).

Synergies:
  Teal + Chartreuse (adjacent tiles): Use Teal on your primary
  engagement tile (more XP per event), then shift to Chartreuse
  for an active fight if you want SP spikes. Or alternate based
  on whether you're farming XP vs. spending a hot fight on chartreuse.
  Graffiti L3 Living Canvas perk (20% on melee = red tile, etc.):
  the Living Canvas auto-paints tiles, but does not auto-paint
  chartreuse — it must still be placed manually. So Living Canvas
  doesn't trivialize chartreuse's charge usage.
  Deep-Frying / Blackkk Magic builds: these skills earn XP per action
  in combat. More XP-event triggers per fight = more 15% SP rolls on
  chartreuse = potentially 2-3 free SP from a single floor.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile chartreuse."
  "Player on tile: 15% chance per"
  "XP event to gain +1 free SP."
  "(Scales with Graffiti level.)"

Implementation notes:
  In xp_progression.py (or whichever module calls
  skills.gain_potential_exp), at the point where XP is credited:
    # After awarding the XP:
    spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get(
        (engine.player.x, engine.player.y)
    )
    if spray == "chartreuse" and xp_awarded_this_event > 0:
        graffiti_lvl = engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        sp_chance = 0.10 + 0.05 * graffiti_lvl
        if random.random() < sp_chance:
            skill_obj = engine.skills.get(skill_name)
            if skill_obj is not None:
                skill_obj.skill_points += 1
                engine.messages.append(
                    f"Chartreuse: free SP for {skill_name}!"
                )
  Trigger: once per XP event (not once per individual XP point).
  A "block" of XP awarded for a single action = 1 roll.
  No new Effect or PlayerStats field needed.

Balance notes:
  At 15% per XP event and ~5 XP events per floor (conservative),
  expect 0.75 free SP per floor on average. Over 4 floors in Crack
  Den: ~3 free SP total. That's roughly equivalent to 1 extra skill
  level. Significant but not dominant — it's bonus acceleration, not
  free levels. The uncertainty (could be 0 or could be 5) creates
  the gambling tension that makes it exciting.
  Risk: heavily fighting on chartreuse can yield more SP if the build
  generates many XP events per fight (Stabbing, Smoking, etc.). This
  is intended — the tile rewards play styles that generate high XP
  event frequency.

PAINT 23 — UMBER (DARK BROWN-RED)
============================
Item ID:    umber_spray_paint
Name:       Umber Spray Paint
RGB:        (110, 50, 15)
Render bg (visible):   (52, 22, 6)
Render bg (explored):  (26, 11, 3)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Player on tile — grants a stacking "Grit" buff for each enemy
  standing within 3 tiles (Chebyshev distance) of the player when
  the player is on an umber tile. Grit stacks:
    1 nearby enemy: +0 Grit (no bonus — you're not surrounded)
    2 nearby enemies: +1 Grit → +1 Strength for the turn
    3 nearby enemies: +2 Grit → +2 Strength + +1 Defense
    4+ nearby enemies: +3 Grit → +3 Strength + +2 Defense
  Grit stacks recalculate each turn based on current nearby enemy
  count. Grit bonuses are temporary (reset each turn). They do not
  persist if the player steps off the tile.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  This is the "outnumbered hero" tile. Most tiles give their bonus
  unconditionally; Umber scales with the threat level. The more
  enemies surround you while you stand on umber, the stronger you get.
  This creates a compelling question: is it worth staying on the tile
  when 4 enemies close in, knowing you'll deal more damage but take
  more hits?

  The minimum threshold of 2 enemies before any Grit appears prevents
  the tile from giving free bonuses in 1v1 fights. Umber is
  exclusively meaningful in group encounters.

  Flavor: umber is the color of dried blood and road grime — this tile
  is painted by someone who has been jumped and survived. Standing on
  it wakes something up in you.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Grit thresholds shift down by 1 per 2 Graffiti levels:
  Base (L0-L1): 2/3/4+ nearby for Grit 1/2/3.
  L2-L3 Graffiti: 1/2/3+ nearby for Grit 1/2/3.
  (At L2+, even a 1v1 fight grants Grit 1. Tiles become useful
  more frequently, rewarding Graffiti investment.)

Synergies:
  Crimson + Umber (adjacent tiles): fight from Umber to deal more
  damage in group fights (Grit STR bonus), then retreat to Crimson
  to lifesteal from the boosted damage against survivors.
  Violet + Umber: Violet adds flat melee damage (risk/reward).
  Umber adds STR (scales with STR weapon bonuses). Both active on
  the same tile would be powerful, but they're different tiles —
  the player must choose: guaranteed flat damage bonus (Violet) or
  conditional group-scaling bonus (Umber)?
  CON-heavy builds: high HP tolerates being surrounded. Umber
  rewards precisely the tactical position that's most dangerous
  (surrounded), giving a reason to not retreat.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile umber."
  "On tile: +Grit per nearby foe."
  "2 nearby: +1 STR."
  "3 nearby: +2 STR, +1 DEF."
  "4+ nearby: +3 STR, +2 DEF."

Implementation notes:
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses (engine.py), when spray == "umber":
    graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
    threshold_shift = 1 if graffiti_lvl >= 2 else 0
    t1 = 2 - threshold_shift  # enemies for grit 1
    t2 = 3 - threshold_shift  # enemies for grit 2
    t3 = 4 - threshold_shift  # enemies for grit 3
    count = sum(
        1 for m in self.dungeon.entities
        if m.entity_type == "monster" and m.alive
        and max(abs(m.x - self.player.x),
                abs(m.y - self.player.y)) <= 3
    )
    if count >= t3:
        bonuses["strength"] = 3
        bonuses["defense"] = 2
    elif count >= t2:
        bonuses["strength"] = 2
        bonuses["defense"] = 1
    elif count >= t1:
        bonuses["strength"] = 1
    # else: count < t1 → no bonus
  These bonuses go into the tile_stat_bonuses dict used by
  PlayerStats.effective_* properties.
  Because _update_tile_stat_bonuses is called when the player moves,
  the Grit stacks update on player movement turn. For enemy movement
  turns (when enemies approach), the count won't update until the
  player's next action. This slight lag is acceptable and prevents
  micro-gaming the count by looking around.
  No new Effect class needed; use the tile_stat_bonuses mechanism.

Balance notes:
  The STR bonus on Umber applies to weapon damage (STR scaling).
  At STR 10, +3 STR is like having STR 13 — a significant damage
  spike. But it only activates when 4 enemies are nearby, which is
  precisely when the player is at most risk. This is the intended
  tension: you're incentivized to hold the tile when leaving would
  be safer. For group-focused enemies (meanders, faction members),
  Umber creates a dramatic stand: kill fast enough and the tile pays
  off, get overwhelmed and it wasn't enough.

Effect:
  Player on tile — generates +1 Meth resource per action taken
  (any action: move, attack, ability, wait) while standing here.
  This adds to the player's meth resource counter (entity.meth or

  to generate Meth as fast as possible. Tawny turns your fight tile

Synergies:
  White Power skill trees: many perks interact with Meth resource
  as a combat fuel. Tawny directly feeds those builds, making the
  Tawny tile mandatory for committed White Power builds.
  Meth Lab crafting: if Meth is consumed by abilities or passives,
  Tawny helps regenerate it during the fight. The "fight here and
  replenish" loop is a key tactical identity for this tile.
  TOL interaction (Crack Den fallback): Tolerance affects the
  player's resistance to status effects (tox threshold, etc.).
  In Crack Den, Tawny becomes a soft defensive tile for TOL builds.

PAINT 25 — INDIGO (DEEP PURPLE-BLUE)
============================
Item ID:    indigo_spray_paint
Name:       Indigo Spray Paint
RGB:        (65, 25, 180)
Render bg (visible):   (28, 8, 82)
Render bg (explored):  (14, 4, 41)
Tool charges: 8
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 50

Effect:
  Player on tile — activates a passive "Aura" state:
  At the start of each player turn while on indigo, one random
  adjacent enemy (if any exist within 1-tile Chebyshev distance)
  is stunned for 1 turn (cannot act on their next turn).
  This aura is auto-targeting — the player doesn't choose the
  target; a random eligible adjacent enemy is selected each turn.
  Only enemies within direct adjacency (radius 1) qualify.
  The stun is the same "stun" effect already in effects.py.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.
  The aura only fires if at least 1 adjacent enemy exists. If no
  enemies are adjacent, nothing happens (no message, no wasted roll).

Design rationale:
  Indigo is a control tile for close-quarters fighting. Unlike Silver
  (which stuns on tile entry — one-time per enemy), Indigo stuns a
  random adjacent enemy every single player turn while the player
  stands here. In a surrounded situation, one enemy is always stunned,
  effectively reducing the enemy's collective action economy.

  This is distinct from all existing paints because it actively alters
  the combat state (stuns enemies) rather than buffing/debuffing stats.
  It's the only "active on-tile action" paint.

  8 charges (reduced) because the effect is very powerful against
  groups. The player must commit placement decisions carefully.

  Flavor: indigo is the color of occult circles and ritual markings.
  Spray a binding circle on the ground and stand inside it — those who
  step close enough feel their will shut down for a moment.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Base: 1 adjacent enemy stunned per player turn.
  At Graffiti L2+: stun radius extends to 2 tiles (all enemies within
  2 Chebyshev distance are eligible for the random stun pick, not just
  adjacent).
  At Graffiti L3+: the stun lasts 2 turns instead of 1.
  This scaling makes Graffiti investment meaningful specifically for
  Indigo-focused play.

Synergies:
  Violet + Indigo: Violet rewards dealing damage; Indigo randomly
  stuns one nearby enemy per turn. In a multi-enemy fight: stand on
  Indigo first (auto-stun one enemy), then advance to Violet for the
  damage boost once the group is thinned. Two-phase room clearing.
  Umber + Indigo (adjacently placed): Umber powers up when surrounded
  (Grit). Indigo stuns one adjacent enemy per turn, reducing their
  effective counterattack. Together they make being surrounded
  BENEFICIAL: Umber gives stats for the count, Indigo disables one
  attacker per turn, lowering effective DPS against you.
  Blackkk Magic: stuns are synergized by Blackkk Magic perks that
  benefit from incapacitated enemies. Auto-stun from Indigo can
  set up free hits for ability-based builds.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile indigo."
  "While on tile: stun 1 random"
  "adjacent enemy per turn."
  "8 charges. Occult placement."

Implementation notes:
  At the START of each player turn (before input is processed), in
  engine.py's player-turn-start processing:
    spray = self.dungeon.spray_paint.get(
        (self.player.x, self.player.y)
    )
    if spray == "indigo":
        graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        stun_radius = 2 if graffiti_lvl >= 2 else 1
        stun_dur = 2 if graffiti_lvl >= 3 else 1
        candidates = [
            m for m in self.dungeon.entities
            if (m.entity_type == "monster" and m.alive
                and max(abs(m.x - self.player.x),
                        abs(m.y - self.player.y))
                    <= stun_radius
                and not has_effect(m, "stunned"))
        ]
        if candidates:
            target = random.choice(candidates)
            apply_effect(target, self, "stunned",
                         duration=stun_dur)
            # Optional: quiet or visible message
            self.messages.append(
                f"Indigo aura stuns the {target.name}!"
            )
  "stunned" effect already exists in effects.py; reuse as-is.
  The stun fires at player-turn-start (before the player acts),
  meaning the player gets to benefit from the stun on their action.
  Do not stack stuns: if the chosen candidate is already stunned,
  skip (candidates filter handles this).
  Charge consumption: the tile itself is consumed normally (it is
  painted once and lasts until overwritten or floor change). The
  8 charges on the spray paint item control how many tiles can be
  placed per item, not how many stun procs occur. Once the tile
  is placed, the aura fires every turn indefinitely (until the
  player leaves the tile or the floor ends).
  No new Effect class needed — reuses stunned effect.

Balance notes:
  1 stun per player turn against a group of 4 enemies effectively
  removes 25% of their action economy. This is very strong in
  corridors (where adjacency limits the candidate pool to 1-2
  enemies). In open rooms with many adjacent enemies, one of them
  is always stunned but the rest attack freely. The "random" target
  means you can't guarantee the most dangerous enemy is stunned —
  tactical friction that prevents it from being auto-win.
  8 charges: forces the player to decide if Indigo placement is
  worth the commitment. Unlike 10-charge paints, every placement
  of Indigo is a meaningful choice.

FULL V4 COMBO SYNERGIES
============================

Midnight + Umber (two tiles):
  Paint Midnight at your preferred fighting tile (+dodge).
  Paint Umber at a nearby position you retreat to when HP drops.
  Use Umber when surrounded for the Grit STR boost; fall back to
  Midnight if enemies thin out and you want evasion protection.
  Two roles: offense/survival vs. defense/evasion.

Navy + Red (classic gun setup):
  Red under likely enemy patrol/approach position (+25% damage
  taken). Navy at your shooting position (+gun dmg, +25% ammo
  save). Every shot from Navy at a Red-painted enemy gets double
  amplification. Against a boss with 100 HP, this setup cuts
  effective fight length significantly.

Amber + Grey (two-tile wounded-enemy gauntlet):
  Grey in the corridor (wounded enemies approaching have -1 to -4
  outgoing damage). Amber at the room entrance (wounded enemies
  below 50% HP fear on entry). Combined: enemies that manage to
  reach you are weakened AND may flee even at close range.

Bone + Magenta (defensive duo):
  Magenta gives Temp HP on kills (proactive, fills when healthy).
  Bone gives DR when low HP (reactive, protects when hurt). Both
  on adjacent tiles creates a complete survival loop: fight,
  earn Temp HP from kills, use Temp HP as a buffer, Bone's DR
  kicks in if raw HP dips, kill more enemies to replenish Temp HP.

Indigo + Violet (the gladiator's pair):
  Indigo stuns one adjacent enemy every player turn. Violet adds
  melee damage but also makes adjacent enemies hit harder. Sequence:
  fight from Indigo first (reduced enemy action economy, one enemy
  stunned per turn), then shift to Violet for the kill burst once
  enemies are thinned. Never be on both simultaneously — use them
  as phases of the same fight.

Slate + Indigo + existing Drive-By build:
  High-Swagger Drive-By build stands on Slate (doubled swagger
  defense = possibly +6-8 defense). Adjacent enemies get auto-
  stunned from Indigo (if painted on the same tile — one tile can
  only have one paint, so these are different tiles). The Swagger
  defense makes you nearly untouchable; the stunned adjacency from
  Indigo tiles placed around the room creates a kill funnel.

Chartreuse + Stabbing skill + Teal (XP farm trio):
  Teal on primary tile (+20% skill XP). Chartreuse placed nearby
  for a fight that generates many XP events (Stabbing earns XP
  per hit). After enough XP events while on Chartreuse, several
  free SP may be earned. The two tiles serve different XP layers
  (teal = volume; chartreuse = SP spikes). Alternate between them
  per fight phase based on which benefit is more needed.

Tawny + Lime + Orange (Meth Lab three-tile zone):
  Player fights from Tawny (gains Meth resource per action to fuel
  White Power abilities). Lime placed in enemy approach path
  (tox builds up). Orange placed in a corner enemies may retreat
  to (DoT damage). Result: enemy enters toxified, player builds
  Meth resource, and any enemy escaping gets DoT'd on Orange.
  A complete zone management system.
