================================================================================
OSRS-INSPIRED UNIQUE EQUIPPABLE ITEMS
25 Items — NIGRL Roguelike Design Document
================================================================================

Design philosophy lens:
  Old School RuneScape's most celebrated items are defined by their special
  attacks, degradation systems, niche strengths, set effects, and the risk/reward
  of bringing powerful gear into dangerous situations. Each OSRS item has a clear
  "reason to use it" and a clear "cost" — either in opportunity cost, resource
  drain, or mechanical limitation. This batch translates that philosophy to
  NIGRL's urban street setting.

Special Mechanics Vocabulary Used in This Batch:
  - SPEC ENERGY: new engine float engine.spec_energy (0.0–100.0). Drains on
    ability use, restores at +25/turn passively (any spec weapon equipped). Max 100.
    Displayed in status panel when any spec weapon is equipped. Stored on PlayerStats
    as spec_energy: float = 0.0. Restored to full on floor change.
  - DEGRADATION: new field "degrade_max_hits": int on item def; per-hit counter on
    the Entity (entity.degrade_hits_remaining). At 0: item becomes "broken" variant
    (new item_id: "broken_X"). Can be repaired by paying floor_num * 10 cash at a
    vending machine (Repair option). Broken items lose their passive but stay equippable.
  - PRAYER/PROTECTION: new engine bool flags for protect-style mechanics; these are
    per-floor activation toggles with energy costs, not continuous passives.
  - SLAYER EFFECT: bonus damage vs. enemy_type flag; checked in _compute_player_attack
    power if weapon has "slayer_bonus" dict.

Note on "spec weapons": items that grant spec-energy abilities fire from the new
  engine.spec_energy pool. This is mechanically distinct from ability cooldowns —
  you can use multiple spec weapons in the same run; they all draw from the same pool.

ITEM 1 — DRAGON DOUBLE DAGGER
================================================================================
OSRS Inspiration: Dragon Dagger (special attack "Puncture" — 2 rapid hits, doubled
  spec weapon of choice for combo specs into high burst)

Slot:      weapon (stabbing)
Char:      '/'
Color:     (220, 80, 80)  — dark red
Tags:      ["dragon_dagger", "unique"]
zones:     []  (special acquisition only)
name_gradient: [(180, 40, 40), (255, 140, 60)]

Stats:
  base_damage:  8
  str_req:      9
  reach:        1
  str_scaling:  {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}  (+1 per 2 STR above 9)
  weapon_type:  stabbing

Special Mechanic — PUNCTURE (spec ability, 55 spec energy):
  Grants ability "ddd_puncture" on equip, revokes on unequip.
  Target type: ADJACENT
  Effect: Make 2 rapid consecutive attacks on the same target in one action.
    Each attack resolves fully (damage, on-hit effects, crits) independently.
    Second attack has +10% crit chance bonus on top of normal crit chance.
    Total action cost: normal attack cost (not doubled).
    Spec energy cost: 55.
  Implementation notes:
    - ability execute: fire _compute_player_attack_power twice against same target,
      second call passes a crit_bonus=0.10 flag.
    - "ddd_puncture" ability_id; ADJACENT target type; ChargeType.INFINITE with
      spec energy check instead of charge.
    - engine.spec_energy -= 55 before executing; if spec_energy < 55: message "Not
      enough special energy."

Tradeoff:
  Base damage only 8 — competitive only at high STR. Spec costs 55 energy so you
  can do it roughly once per two floors passively. Vulnerable to single-hit weapons
  with higher base that combo out of reach.

Balance:
  Single stab hits ~10-15 at STR 10-12. Puncture double = ~20-30 total per spec use.
  Second-hit crit chance ~42-46% at STS 10-11. Ceiling about 48-60 on mega-crit.
  Comparable to Last Rites Cleaver burst but safer (no punishment for surviving targets).

Synergies:
  - Stabbing skill tree crits amplify both hits independently.
  - Prison Shank bonus_crit_mult would stack — but those are different items.
  - Static Discharge Ring would arc twice on double hit.
  - Hollow Point Hat (3x gun crits) does NOT affect this — melee crit mult stays 2x
    (unless modified by hollow_point_hat's melee crit 1.5x override).

Implementation Notes:
  Files: abilities.py (add "ddd_puncture" AbilityDef), engine.py (add spec_energy
  field to GameEngine init; add spec_energy display in status; add
  spec_energy restore per tick), items.py (add "dragon_double_dagger" entry with
  grants_ability: "ddd_puncture"), combat.py (pass crit_bonus param to
  _player_melee_attack_against, add to crit roll).
  PlayerStats new field: spec_energy: float = 0.0 (or store on engine directly).

ITEM 2 — ARMADILLO GODSWORD
================================================================================
OSRS Inspiration: Armadyl Godsword (spec "Flurry" — massive single hit, +25% max
  hit, high spec cost; best-in-slot spec for boss kills)

Slot:      weapon (slashing)
Char:      chr(0xE00E)  (reuse graphical sword tile)
Color:     (120, 160, 255)  — pale blue
Tags:      ["armadillo_gs", "unique"]
zones:     []
name_gradient: [(80, 120, 255), (180, 210, 255)]

Stats:
  base_damage:  18
  str_req:      14
  reach:        1
  str_scaling:  {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}
  weapon_type:  slashing

Special Mechanic — SKY OPENER (spec ability, 75 spec energy):
  Grants ability "armadillo_sky_opener" on equip.
  Target type: ADJACENT
  Effect: Single strike that deals current_attack_power * 1.5, rounded down.
    The 1.5x multiplier is applied AFTER all additive bonuses (STR bonus, ring
    bonuses, etc.) but BEFORE defense subtraction. This is unlike Whirlwind Axe's
    area sweep — it is pure single-target burst.
    If this kills the target, restore 20 spec energy immediately.
    Spec energy cost: 75.
  Implementation:
    In execute: power = _compute_player_attack_power(engine); boosted = int(power * 1.5);
    damage = max(1, boosted - target.defense); deal damage; if kill: spec_energy += 20.

Tradeoff:
  Highest base damage of any slashing weapon (18), but str_req 14 is very demanding.
  Sky Opener costs 75 energy — only usable once per passive regen cycle unless you
  get a kill refund. Competing with Whirlwind Axe and Yinyang for the slashing slot.

Balance:
  At STR 14: atk_power = 18 + (14-14)//2 = 18. Sky Opener = 27 before defense.
  At STR 12 (ring boosted): 18 + 1 = 19. Sky Opener = 28. Decent boss damage.
  At max STR+ring: ~20-22 power. Sky Opener = 30-33. Best single spike in game.

Synergies:
  - On-kill spec regen turns it into a sustained spec weapon if you're clearing rooms.
  - Slashing skill tree L3+ cleave bonus still applies to base attacks (not the spec).
  - outgoing_damage_mults (trauma response, specter creepers) DO multiply the 1.5x
    base, because the mult is applied in the normal damage path.

ITEM 3 — GRANITE JAWN
================================================================================
OSRS Inspiration: Granite Maul (spec "Quick Smash" — zero delay, hits instantly,
  can chain immediately after any other attack; used for 2-spec combos)

Slot:      weapon (beating)
Char:      '/'
Color:     (160, 150, 140)  — grey granite
Tags:      ["granite_jawn", "unique"]
zones:     []
name_gradient: [(120, 110, 100), (200, 190, 180)]

Stats:
  base_damage:  14
  str_req:      12
  reach:        1
  str_scaling:  {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 1}  (+1 per STR above 12 — steep req, good return)
  weapon_type:  beating

Special Mechanic — QUICK SMASH (spec ability, 30 spec energy):
  Grants ability "granite_quick_smash" on equip.
  Target type: ADJACENT
  ChargeType: INFINITE (spec energy gated instead of charges)
  Effect: Make one immediate attack as a FREE ACTION — it does NOT consume the player's
    turn (engine.player_free_action = True on execute). This allows you to attack twice
    in one turn when combined with the normal attack action.
    The free attack deals HALF damage (atk_power * 0.5, rounded up).
    Spec cost: 30.
    Limitation: can only free-action attack ONCE per turn (engine flag
    engine.granite_free_used_this_turn; resets each turn in energy loop).
  Implementation:
    execute sets engine.player_free_action = True, attacks at 50% power, then
    sets engine.granite_free_used_this_turn = True. Check flag in free action handler
    to prevent double-firing same spec twice in one turn.

Tradeoff:
  Low spec cost (30) but free attack only deals 50% power. Valuable for finishing
  enemies or applying on-hit effects twice in one turn. Base stats are competitive
  (14 damage is solid for beating) but str_req 12 limits early access.
  The "double attack per turn" potential makes this outstanding with on-hit procs.

Balance:
  Quick Smash half-power at STR 12: 14 * 0.5 = 7. Plus on-hit proc chance fires.
  Full turn normal attack: 14 + (12-12)/1 = 14. Total turn damage potential: 21.
  At STR 14 (rings): 14 + 2 = 16 full, 8 quick smash = 24 combined. Excellent with
  stun procs — free action stun, then normal attack against stunned target.

Synergies:
  Outstanding with any on_hit_stun_chance weapon when combined... but this IS the
  weapon. The Blackjack is inferior because Quick Smash lets you apply stun via the
  half-attack and then follow with a full hit. Exception: if Blackjack is in sidearm
  and this is in weapon, the ADJACENT free action hit proc still applies on-hit
  effects from the spec's attack using the equipped weapon.

ITEM 4 — BARROWS BEATER (DHAROK'S FIST)
================================================================================
OSRS Inspiration: Dharok's set effect (lower HP = more damage; full set required)
  Single-item version: damage bonus inversely scales with HP.

Slot:      weapon (beating)
Char:      '/'
Color:     (80, 60, 80)  — dark purple-grey
Tags:      ["dharoks_fist", "unique", "barrows"]
zones:     []
name_gradient: [(40, 30, 40), (140, 100, 140)]

Stats:
  base_damage:  10
  str_req:      8
  reach:        1
  str_scaling:  {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}
  weapon_type:  beating
  "dharoks_passive": true  — engine flag to enable the HP scalar

Passive Mechanic — DHAROK'S FIST:
  No ability granted. Checked in _compute_player_attack_power:
  While equipped, add a bonus equal to:
    max(0, int((1.0 - player.hp / player.max_hp) * 20))
  Examples:
    Full HP: +0 bonus damage
    75% HP: +5 bonus damage
    50% HP: +10 bonus damage
    25% HP: +15 bonus damage
    1 HP: +19 bonus damage (capped at +20 practical maximum)
  The bonus is additive, applied after STR scaling.

Additional field on item: "dharoks_passive": True
In _compute_player_attack_power: if defn and defn.get("dharoks_passive"):
  hp_ratio = engine.player.hp / max(1, engine.player.max_hp)
  atk_power += max(0, int((1.0 - hp_ratio) * 20))

Tradeoff:
  Terrible at full HP (10 base is below average for beating). Becomes exceptional
  at low HP — but low HP means permadeath risk. This is NIGRL's quintessential
  risk/reward weapon. Pairs with death-save mechanics.

Balance:
  At full HP: 10 + STR bonus. Worse than most beating weapons.
  At 25% HP: 25 + STR bonus. Better than almost any melee weapon in the game.
  At ~10 HP on 130 HP character: bonus = int(0.923 * 20) = +18. Total ~28 + STR.
  This is roughly on par with Sky Opener spec but EVERY hit, forever, while alive.

Synergies:
  - Second Wind Ring death save → keep attacking at 1 HP for max Dharok's bonus.
  - Backpedal Boots (below 40% HP: +60 energy/tick) + Dharok's is a full HP-tank build.
  - crackhead_crucifix (dodge per debuff) — get debuffed, survive, destroy enemies.

New Engine State:
  No new state needed. Just add check in _compute_player_attack_power.

  combat.py: check ignore_dodge before dodge roll; check ignore_defense_chance at

ITEM 11 — BERSERKER COIL
================================================================================
OSRS Inspiration: Berserker Ring (+4 str bonus, best-in-slot for melee damage in
  OSRS; imbueable for double bonus)

Slot:      ring
Char:      chr(0xE00A)
Color:     (220, 100, 40)  — orange-red
Tags:      ["berserker_coil", "unique"]
zones:     []
name_gradient: [(160, 60, 20), (255, 140, 60)]

Stats:
  stat_bonus:   {"strength": 3}
  "berserker_passive": True

Passive Mechanic — BERSERKER'S MADNESS:
  After killing an enemy in melee, for the next 3 turns: +4 STR temporarily
  (add_temporary_stat_bonus "strength" +4). The buff stacks with itself up to a
  maximum of +8 STR total bonus from this ring (engine.berserker_coil_bonus: int,
  tracks the current outstanding bonus from this ring; capped at +8).
  On expire per buff instance: if berserker_coil_bonus > 0: subtract 4.
  Implemented via a new BerserkerMadnessEffect (dur=3, amount=4):
    apply: engine.player_stats.add_temporary_stat_bonus("strength", 4);
           engine.berserker_coil_bonus += 4; if berserker_coil_bonus > 8: reject apply.
    expire: engine.player_stats.add_temporary_stat_bonus("strength", -4);
            engine.berserker_coil_bonus -= 4.

Tradeoff:
  Only +3 permanent STR (good but not exceptional for a ring). Kill-triggered bonus
  is powerful in rooms with multiple enemies but inactive for boss fights.
  Competing against rings that grant utility or specific-scenario bonuses.

  Base: +3 STR = +1-2 melee damage depending on weapon scaling.
  With kill stacks: +7-11 STR effective = +3-5 more damage per hit.
  Short duration (3 turns) means the bonus is only relevant for back-to-back kills.

Synergies:
  - Ancestral Beater (kill counter) + this ring: sustained high-damage clear.
  - Double Edged Sword (high base damage): STR matters less so this is relatively weaker.
  - The 9 Ring already gives +5 all stats; this only competes at high STR builds.

New Engine State:
  engine.berserker_coil_bonus: int = 0  (tracks outstanding strength bonus)
  event_bus hook: on melee kill (entity_died with killer == engine.player and
  weapon_type == "melee"), check if berserker_coil_bonus < 8, apply effect.

Passive Mechanic — CALCULATED STRIKE:

Tradeoff:

Passive Mechanic — SKULL AND BONES:
  This ring has two states: ACTIVE and DORMANT.
  ACTIVE: All stats apply. Additionally: +10% to all outgoing damage (append 1.10x
    to outgoing_damage_mults on equip).
  DORMANT: Stat penalties apply but +10% damage does NOT. Ring is dormant until
    you've killed 3 enemies while wearing it.

  The SKULL effect: when the player dies while wearing this ring, the ring is
    PERMANENTLY DESTROYED (removed from game entirely — not dropped on the ground).
    This means all run-carried value of the ring is forfeit on permadeath. Because
    NIGRL has permadeath, this means the ring is only lost when you lose the whole
    run — which is already happening. So the real cost is psychological: wearing this
    ring feels more "committed" because it's gone if you die.
    ADDITIONAL death penalty: while wearing this ring, any CASH held by the player
    is halved on death (stored in death handler, even though permadeath ends the run —
    this is flavor only if the player somehow could survive death, e.g., via Second Wind).

  Activating from dormant: engine.skull_kill_count: int = 0. Each kill while equipped
    increments. At 3: apply 1.10x mult to outgoing_damage_mults, message "Skull activated!".
    On unequip: remove the 1.10x mult, reset skull_kill_count to 0 (ring goes dormant again).

  The -2 CON means max HP is reduced by 20 HP. At max HP of 130 (CON 10): you're at 110.
  This is not a ring for players who want HP cushion.

Tradeoff:
  -2 CON (-20 max HP) is a severe penalty. The +10% damage requires 3 kills to activate.
  In a new floor, you're under-powered until you get your first 3 kills. The ring is
  a deliberate "hot start" design — you earn the bonus each floor by committing.

Balance:
  +2 STR, +3 STS, -2 CON, +10% damage (active):
    +2 STR: +1-2 melee damage
    +3 STS: +9% crit chance
    -2 CON: -20 max HP (significant)
    +10%: roughly +1-3 per hit on average
  Net DPS vs. safety: roughly +3-5 damage per hit at the cost of 20 HP and an
  activation condition. Probably the best offensive ring in the game for sustained
  DPS once activated, but the HP penalty makes early-floor play risky.

New Engine State:
  engine.skull_kill_count: int = 0  (reset on unequip)
  engine.skull_ring_active: bool = False  (set to True at 3 kills, False on unequip)
  engine.skull_ring_equipped: bool = False  (tracks the ring being equipped)
