Research Foundation
-------------------
- Existing resource/currency items (Batch 1): Crown of the Corner Boy (rep/kills, hat),
  Knuckle Diary (entries/crits, weapon), Hustle Ring (hustle/moves, ring),
  Tap-In Talisman (pages/damage-taken, neck). No duplicates from those patterns.
- soul_count pattern: attribute on item entity, synced via tick hook in display effect,
  incremented via engine.event_bus "entity_died" handler.
- grants_abilities currently wired for neck; grants_ability wired for weapon/sidearm/feet.
  Hat, ring, and weapon grants_abilities require inventory_mgr.py extension (mirror neck).
- ChargeType patterns used: INFINITE + ability_cooldowns (cooldown), PER_FLOOR + max_charges,
  TOTAL, ONCE. For resource items: INFINITE + runtime cost check is the standard.
- TargetTypes: SELF, SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, LINE_FROM_PLAYER, AOE_CIRCLE, ADJACENT, ADJACENT_TILE.
- Damage absorption order: temp_hp → armor → hp.
- Gun ammo types: light / medium / heavy. Classes: small=sidearm, medium/large=weapon.
- Status effects with lifecycle hooks: modify_incoming_damage, on_player_melee_hit,
  modify_energy_gain, before_turn, modify_movement, tick, apply, expire.
- outgoing_damage_mults: list of floats, product is the multiplier. Append on apply, pop on expire.
- Entity fields: toxicity, radiation, infection, meth, temp_hp, armor, hp.
- Player stats: constitution, strength, book_smarts, street_smarts, tolerance, swagger.
- ability_cooldowns: {ability_id: turns_remaining}, ticked per energy loop turn.
- Slot coverage (Batch 1 used): weapon/beating x4, feet x1, hat x2, neck x1, ring x2.
  This batch MUST cover: sidearm (gun), weapon (gun), stabbing x2, slashing x1, ring x1+,
  neck x1+, feet x1, hat x1, new beating x1. No plain rings with no resource this batch.

ITEM 1: The Toll Collector
Slot: Neck | Char: $ | Color: (60, 200, 100) dirty green
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A padded money belt worn under the jacket — the kind a street-level operator uses to keep
their float safe. Every dollar you spend (on vending machines, trades, or item use) drops
a toll token in the belt. Spend those tokens on three abilities: bribe a single enemy to
stop fighting, throw your weight around to suppress every visible enemy's energy, or cash
out the entire belt on a burst of temporary max-HP and speed.

The design captures the street economy fantasy: your spending power translates directly into
battlefield leverage. Richer playthroughs that invest in vending machines and crafting
accumulate tokens quickly; cash-poor runs must fight smarter.

RESOURCE: TOKENS (cash spent tally)
- Stored as: engine.neck.token_count (int attribute set on item entity, same as soul_count)
- Accumulation: +1 token per $10 spent (or fraction thereof) on vending machines, crafting,
  or any engine.cash deduction while belt is equipped.
  - Tracked in engine.py: in `_spend_cash()` helper or any site that reduces self.cash
    (vending machine purchase, crafting cost, etc.) — add:
    if self.neck and "toll_collector" in (get_item_def(self.neck.item_id) or {}).get("tags", []):
        self.neck.token_count = getattr(self.neck, "token_count", 0) + spent_amount // 10
- Max tokens: 20. Display via new "token_count" visual effect (same pattern as soul_count).
- Tokens persist across floors; reset to 0 on unequip.

DISPLAY EFFECT (effects.py)
  class TokenCountEffect(Effect):
      id = "token_count"; category = "buff"; priority = 0
      def __init__(self, **kwargs): kwargs.pop("duration", None); super().__init__(duration=9999, **kwargs); self.tokens = 0
      def tick(self, entity, engine):
          neck = engine.neck
          if neck and "toll_collector" in (get_item_def(neck.item_id) or {}).get("tags", []):
              self.tokens = getattr(neck, "token_count", 0)
      @property
      def display_name(self): return f"Tokens: {self.tokens}"

ABILITY 2: Toll Suppress
  ability_id: "toll_suppress"
  name: "Toll Suppress"
  description: "Spend 7 tokens. Every visible enemy loses 40 energy immediately. The collector asserts dominance."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 7 tokens
  Execute logic:
    - Check token_count >= 7
    - For every living monster m where engine.dungeon.visible[m.y, m.x]:
        m.energy = max(0.0, m.energy - 40.0)
    - Deduct 7 tokens
    - Message: [("Toll Suppress! ", (60, 200, 100)), ("Every visible enemy loses 40 energy. ", (200, 255, 150)), ("(-7 tokens)", (180, 180, 180))]

  roughly half a turn. With 7 tokens this is a mid-stack spend during dangerous multi-enemy rooms.

ABILITY 3: Toll Cashout
  ability_id: "toll_cashout"
  name: "Toll Cashout"
  description: "Spend ALL tokens (min 12). Gain 3 temp HP and +8 energy/tick per token spent. Effect lasts until floor change."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL tokens (minimum 12)
  Execute logic:
    - Check token_count >= 12
    - total = engine.neck.token_count
    - engine.player.temp_hp = min(engine.player.temp_hp + total * 3, 80)
    - Apply new effect "toll_cashout_buff" (floor_duration=True):
        apply: engine.player_stats.equipment_energy_per_tick += total * 8
        expire: engine.player_stats.equipment_energy_per_tick -= stored_bonus
    - Set token_count = 0
    - Message: [("CASHOUT! ", (255, 255, 60)), (f"+{total*3} temp HP, +{total*8} energy/tick until floor end. ", (200, 255, 200)), (f"(-{total} tokens)", (180, 180, 180))]
  Balance: 12 tokens = 36 temp HP + 96 energy/tick (nearly double speed). 20 tokens = 60 temp
  HP + 160 energy/tick (nearly triple speed). High payoff for committing a full spending run.
  New effect "toll_cashout_buff" stores the bonus amount and reverses it on expire.

BALANCE OVERVIEW:
- Tokens at $10 each: a $50 vending purchase = 5 tokens, a $120 crafting session = 12 tokens.
- Bribe at 4 is accessible mid-floor after a single vending machine visit.
- Suppress at 7 is a crowd control panic button for multi-enemy rooms.
- Cashout at 12+ is the big power play for deep cash investment runs.
- +10 armor + +1 TOL + +1 SWG make it competitive as a defensive neck piece.
- Tradeoff: you spend money to get tokens; spending money on other things (healing items,
  crafting) is already valuable. This neck competes with cash utility.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- engine.py: all sites that reduce engine.cash must call a helper
  _spend_cash(amount) that increments neck.token_count = getattr(neck, "token_count", 0) + amount // 10
- inventory_mgr.py: on neck equip, set neck.token_count = 0. On unequip, reset to 0.
- effects.py: TokenCountEffect (display), TollCashoutBuffEffect (floor_duration, stores/reverses energy bonus).
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries.
- New engine state: none required beyond token_count on item entity.

SYNERGIES & INTERACTIONS:
- Vending machine runs + Drinking skill: natural synergy for cash-heavy builds.
- Crafting-heavy runs (Deep-Frying tree): crafting gold costs generate tokens.
- Combined with Steal (Stealing skill): stealing cash doesn't generate tokens (only spending does),
  creating an interesting asymmetry — hoarders who spend get more utility.

ITEM 2: The Tox Budget
Slot: Ring | Char: 0xE00A | Color: (100, 220, 80) sickly green
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A chunky plastic ring molded in the shape of a biohazard symbol, filled with some kind of
glowing fluid. You keep a running "tox budget" — every point of toxicity you personally
accumulate charges the ring. Spend that budget to weaponize your own poisoning: vent a tox
cloud, spike your fists with toxin for a few hits, or do the really insane thing and detonate
the whole budget as a massive AoE wave.

The ring rewards the Meth Lab build that actively takes toxicity hits as a resource rather
than a liability. The design is intentionally high-risk: you must be toxified to use it.

RESOURCE: TOX BUDGET (accumulated toxicity tally)
- Stored as: engine.tox_budget_ring_budget (int), separate from actual player toxicity.
  Accumulation: each time engine.player.toxicity increases by any amount while ring is equipped,
  add that increase to engine.tox_budget_ring_budget.
  Tracked in: engine.py or combat.py in the function that calls add_toxicity(engine, player, amount) —
  wrap: if ring equipped, budget += amount.
- Max budget: 200. Display via new "tox_budget_count" effect (visual only, pattern: soul_count).
- Budget does NOT decrease when the player's actual toxicity decreases (toxicity removal does
  NOT reduce budget — budget only moves up).
- Budget resets to 0 on ring unequip.

ABILITY 1: Tox Vent

  Balance: 30 budget requires accumulating 30 tox. The vent both damages enemies and gives small
  tox relief, making it a risk-management tool.

ABILITY 3: Tox Detonation
  ability_id: "tox_detonation"
  name: "Tox Detonation"
  description: "Spend ALL budget (min 80). Detonate the ring — deal damage equal to budget/4 to ALL visible enemies. Your own toxicity jumps by 30. Budget consumed entirely."
  TargetType: SELF

- Tox Spike Fist rewards melee builds that are already taking tox damage.
- Tox Detonation at 200 budget is a massive nuke but requires surviving 200 tox worth of hits.
- +2 TOL and "zones": ["meth_lab"] signal this is zone-specific late-game gear.

- effects.py: ToxSpikeFistEffect (on_player_melee_hit, charge counter, force-expire at 0 charges);
  ToxBudgetCountEffect (display only, syncs from engine.tox_budget_ring_budget).
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries.
- New engine state: tox_budget_ring_budget (int, default 0, reset on unequip).

SYNERGIES & INTERACTIONS:
- Toxic Slug, Sludge Amalgam enemies: deal heavy tox to player, accelerating budget generation.
- White Power tox inversion trees: taking tox is already rewarded by those perks — double synergy.
- Chemical Warfare skill tree: tox-building playstyle.

ITEM 3: The Spellbook Strap
Slot: Neck | Char: + | Color: (130, 80, 220) purple
================================================================================

CONCEPT SUMMARY
A wide leather shoulder strap taken from an old satchel, scrawled with arcane symbols in
ballpoint pen and chalk marker. Every spell or ability you cast adds an "inscription" to the
strap. Spend inscriptions on empowered spell procs: reset a random ability's cooldown, trigger
a free chain cast, or dump the whole strap for a surge of raw spell damage.

This is the magic-user item — it rewards builds that invest in Blackkk Magic, Pyromania,
Cryomancy, Electrodynamics, and any ability-heavy skill. It creates a spell-loop engine.

RESOURCE: INSCRIPTIONS (spells/abilities cast tally)
- Stored as: engine.neck.inscription_count (int attribute on item entity)
- Accumulation: +1 inscription per ability used while strap is equipped.
  Tracked in: abilities.py AbilityInstance.consume() — after consuming a charge, check:
    engine = <passed in> — add check: if engine.neck and "spellbook_strap" in tags: neck.inscription_count += 1
  Alternatively: in engine.py's ability-use dispatch, after an ability fires.
- Max inscriptions: 15. Display via new "inscription_count" effect (visual only).
- Inscriptions persist across floors; reset on unequip.

ABILITY 1: Inscription Recharge
  ability_id: "inscription_recharge"
  name: "Inscription Recharge"
  description: "Spend 3 inscriptions. Wipe one random ability off its cooldown immediately."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 3 inscriptions
  Execute logic:
    - Check inscription_count >= 3
    - Find all ability_ids currently in engine.ability_cooldowns (i.e. on cooldown)
    - If none: message "No abilities on cooldown." return without consuming cost.
    - Pick random one from that list
    - del engine.ability_cooldowns[chosen_id]
    - Deduct 3 inscriptions
    - Message: [("Inscription Recharge! ", (130, 80, 220)), (f"{chosen_id.replace('_',' ').title()} refreshed! ", (200, 180, 255)), ("(-3 inscriptions)", (180, 180, 180))]
  Notes: This is the workhorse — consistent value as long as you have cooldown abilities.
  Edge case: if no cooldown abilities exist, ability should message gracefully and not consume cost.

ABILITY 2: Inscription Chain
  ability_id: "inscription_chain"
  name: "Inscription Chain"
  description: "Spend 6 inscriptions. The last spell you cast fires again automatically at a random visible enemy. Free action — does not use a turn."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: 6 inscriptions
  Execute logic:
    - Check inscription_count >= 6
    - Check engine._last_ability_fired_id (new engine state: str, set when any ability fires)
    - If no last ability: message "No ability to chain." return without cost.
    - Re-execute the last ability's execute function:
        Find a random visible living monster for targeted abilities
        For SELF-type abilities: refire on player tile
        For targeted types: auto-pick random visible monster as (tx, ty)
        Special case: if the re-fired ability would consume charges, those ARE consumed normally.
    - This does NOT cost a turn (engine.player_attacked_this_turn set to False after, or use
      player_free_action pattern)
    - Deduct 6 inscriptions BEFORE the refire
    - Message: [("Inscription Chain! ", (130, 80, 220)), (f"{last_name} chains to [target]! ", (200, 180, 255)), ("(-6 inscriptions)", (180, 180, 180))]
  New engine state: engine._last_ability_fired_id (str|None, updated after every ability use).

ABILITY 3: Inscription Surge
  ability_id: "inscription_surge"
  name: "Inscription Surge"
  description: "Spend ALL inscriptions (min 9). Gain +5 spell damage per inscription spent until floor change. Activates immediately."
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE
  Cost: ALL inscriptions (minimum 9)
  Execute logic:
    - Check inscription_count >= 9
    - total = engine.neck.inscription_count
    - bonus = total * 5
    - engine.player_stats.add_temporary_spell_damage(bonus)
    - Apply new effect "inscription_surge_buff" (floor_duration=True, bonus_stored=bonus):
        expire: engine.player_stats.add_temporary_spell_damage(-bonus_stored)
    - engine.neck.inscription_count = 0
    - Message: [("INSCRIPTION SURGE! ", (200, 150, 255)), (f"+{bonus} spell damage until floor end. ", (180, 255, 200)), (f"(-{total} inscriptions)", (180, 180, 180))]
  Balance: 9 inscriptions = +45 spell damage. 15 inscriptions = +75 spell damage.
  This is a dominant late-game spend for a mage who's been casting all floor.

BALANCE OVERVIEW:
- Inscription accumulates at 1/cast — fast for ability-heavy builds, slow for melee-only.
- Recharge at 3 is a frequent use; great for fireball, gouge, dash builds.
- Chain at 6 is the burst option: doubling an AOE or a big spell hit.
- Surge at 9 is the commitment play: dump the buffer and go nuclear for a whole floor.
- +1 BKS + +1 STS make it viable for any build; not purely locked to casters.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
- abilities.py: in AbilityInstance.consume(), add optional engine parameter. Check for strap.
  OR: in engine.py's ability dispatch, after a successful fire, increment inscription_count.
- engine.py: add engine._last_ability_fired_id (str|None). Set in ability dispatch after fire.
- effects.py: InscriptionSurgeBuff (floor_duration=True, stores bonus, reverses on expire).
  InscriptionCountEffect (display only, syncs from neck.inscription_count each tick).
- abilities.py: three new AbilityDef entries.
- New engine state: engine._last_ability_fired_id.
- inventory_mgr.py neck unequip: reset neck.inscription_count = 0.

SYNERGIES & INTERACTIONS:
- Fireball + Chain: chains fireball to a random second target for free.
- Blackkk Magic + Surge: Blackkk Magic at L5 casts multiple spells per turn; strap fills fast.
- Gouge + Recharge: fight starts, gouge, inscription_recharge resets gouge immediately.
- Deep-Frying: fry_shot is an ability, contributes to inscription count.

Slot: Sidearm (small gun) | Char: ) | Color: (160, 120, 80) worn wood

A cut-down double-barrel shotgun that looks like it survived a fire and two car crashes. Each

SECOND ABILITY MECHANISM:
Since grants_ability only supports one ID, we add a second ability through a different mechanic:
The gun grants ONE ability (sawed_off_mangle). A second ability (sawed_off_ricochet) is unlocked
when breach_count >= 20 (displayed as "Breach unlocks RICOCHET at 20" in gun info).
The execute for sawed_off_mangle checks breach to determine which behavior fires,
OR we extend sidearm to support grants_abilities list (recommended approach for future items).
Simplest approach for this item: one ability (sawed_off_mangle) with a branch inside execute.

================================================================================
BALANCE BENCHMARKS
================================================================================

  Reference HP: 30 + CON*10. At CON 10: 130 HP. Most enemies floor 1-2: 27-47 HP.
  Reference damage: enemy floor 1-2: 4-8 dmg. Player typical: 6-14 dmg.
  Crit chance: STS*3%. At STS 10 = 30%.
  Energy: 100/tick base. +20 energy/tick = ~17% faster. +50 energy/tick = ~33% faster.

  Item-by-item balance assessment:
  - Toll Collector: Cashout at max (20 tokens) = 60 temp HP + 160 energy/tick for 1 floor = strong but requires $200+ spending.
  - Tox Budget: Detonation at 200 budget = 50 dmg AoE. Requires surviving 200 tox accumulation = high risk.
  - Spellbook Strap: Surge at 15 inscriptions = +75 spell damage. Fireball base is ~15-25; +75 is roughly 4-5x. Very strong, but requires 15 casts.
  - Cracked Sawed-Off: Ricochet at 20 breach = 6 x (10/2 = 5 dmg each) = 30 total spread damage. Competitive with other AoE guns.
  - Accumulator: EMP at 18+ charge wipes all energy at cost of player energy. Neutral-to-positive.
  - Scar Ledger: Discharge at 12 debts = 48 unresisted. At floor 2 enemy HP ~40 = one-shot range.
  - Griot's Knife: Finale at 5 effects = 15 bonus damage + 5 free ticks. Each bleed tick = 2 dmg so ~10 extra. Combined ~25 total = significant.
  - Respect Knucks: Haymaker at 10 chain = 10 * 1.5 * (base_atk ~12) = 180 raw. This is extremely high — reduce multiplier or chain cap to 7 for balance.
    REVISED: Haymaker damage = base_atk + (chain * 3) — e.g. 10 chain = 12 + 30 = 42 minus defense = ~35. More reasonable.
  - Dodge Ledger: Inscribe at 15 = +15% permanent dodge. At STS 8 dodge = 0% base; +15 = 15% permanent. Modest but stacks.
  - Block Party: Taunt at 30 = pull all enemies, +5 temp HP per. On floor with 10 enemies = 50 temp HP. High risk warrants.
  - Canteen: Cleanse at 12 = 96 HP heal + debuff clear + tox remove. Near-full heal. Requires 12 foods eaten — possible but investment-heavy.
  - Rad Dial: Burst at 30 = 10 dmg + 30 rad to all in 4 tiles. The mutation acceleration is the real value.
  - Floor Tracker: Surge at 10 rooms = +5 all stats. At CON+5 = +50 max HP, STS+5 = +15% crit. Temporary but powerful.
