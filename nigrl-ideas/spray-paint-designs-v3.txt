SPRAY PAINT DESIGNS V3 — 5 REPLACEMENT COLORS
===============================================
NIGRL roguelike | Design document

Purpose: Replace the 5 rejected V2 paints (Yellow, Purple, Pink, Brown,
White) with new designs. The 5 approved V2 paints (Gold, Teal, Lime, Grey,
Rad) remain unchanged and are not repeated here.

Rejection reasons for the original 5:
  Yellow  — too similar to Blue (+5 STS); dual-stat felt redundant
  Purple  — confusion CC too similar to Silver (which already skips turns)
  Pink    — flat max HP bonus is passive and uninteresting
  Brown   — move-cost slow not impactful enough
  White   — pure information tile with no combat numbers

Design goals for replacements:
  - At least one meaningful risk/reward tradeoff
  - No overlap with existing 10 paints or approved V2 set
  - At least one paint that interacts with spells/abilities
  - At least one paint that interacts with the energy system
  - Each must create genuine placement decisions ("do I paint here or save it?")
  - All exam popup lines max 46 characters

Full roster for reference (what must NOT be overlapped):
  Red     — enemy on tile: +25% damage taken
  Blue    — player on tile: +5 STS; 25% ability charge preserve
  Green   — player on tile: +4 Defense
  Orange  — all entities: 5+2*Graffiti dmg/turn
  Silver  — entry: slipped (turn skip + 50% melee vuln)
  Gold    — kill on tile: +$5 cash
  Teal    — player on tile: +20% skill XP
  Lime    — enemy: tox/turn; player: small tox/turn
  Grey    — enemy on tile: -(1+Graffiti) outgoing damage
  Rad     — enemy: rad/turn; player: rad/turn (Meth Lab)


============================
PAINT 11 — CRIMSON
============================
Item ID:    crimson_spray_paint
Name:       Crimson Spray Paint
RGB:        (210, 35, 65)
Render bg (visible):   (100, 12, 25)
Render bg (explored):  (50, 6, 12)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 45

Effect:
  Player on tile — melee attacks heal the player for 20% of damage dealt
  (rounded up, minimum 1 HP if any damage was dealt).
  Healing applies AFTER damage resolution, once per melee hit.
  This is vampiric healing tied to the tile, not the weapon.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.
  AOE/gun attacks do NOT trigger the heal; melee only.

Design rationale:
  No existing paint provides healing. Crimson fills this gap cleanly.
  It rewards standing your ground in melee, complementing Green (+4 Defense)
  and working against Orange (which punishes staying put). Placement on a
  chokepoint creates a "kill zone" where the player sustains through attrition.
  The 20% rate means killing a 25-HP enemy restores 5 HP — meaningful but not
  broken; the player still needs incoming damage to be manageable.

  Risk: to get the healing, you must remain adjacent to enemies. If you step
  off to retreat, you lose the leech. Encourages greedy positioning decisions.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Suggested: heal_pct = 15 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl percent.
  L0: 15%; L1: 20%; L2: 25%; L3: 30%.
  Cap at 30% to prevent trivializing tanky builds.

Synergies:
  Green + Crimson (adjacent tiles): place Green on the approach tile and
  Crimson on your fighting tile — you gain defense AND lifesteal.
  Red + Crimson: red tiles amplify damage dealt; hitting a red-tile enemy
  from your crimson tile means more damage landed = larger heal (20% of
  the amplified number).
  CON-heavy builds: high HP ceiling absorbs spikes; Crimson fills it back up.
  Strength builds: high weapon damage = large heal per swing.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars):
  "Spray a tile crimson."
  "While standing here, melee hits"
  "heal you for 20% of damage dealt."
  "(Scales with Graffiti level.)"

Implementation notes:
  Hook into combat.py perform_attack or engine._do_player_melee_hit,
  immediately after damage is applied to the target.
  Pattern:
    if engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get((engine.player.x, engine.player.y)) == "crimson":
        heal_pct = 0.15 + 0.05 * engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        heal_amt = max(1, math.ceil(damage_dealt * heal_pct))
        engine.player.hp = min(engine.player.hp + heal_amt, engine.player.max_hp)
        engine.messages.append(f"Crimson paint: healed {heal_amt} HP!")
  Check only player-vs-monster melee path (not gun fire, not AOE spell,
  not entity-on-entity combat). The existing pattern for on_player_melee_hit
  hook in Effect can be studied, but a direct call in the melee branch is simpler.
  No new Effect class needed — inline check in the melee damage path.

Balance notes:
  At L0 (15%): enemy with 30 HP in 4 hits at ~8 dmg each = ~5 HP healed.
  Minor sustain against 1 enemy; weaker than a bandage but free if placed.
  At L3 (30%): same scenario = ~10 HP healed. Covers ~1/10 of a
  typical floor's incoming damage. Not overpowering.
  The tile is consumed (it's spray paint, not a permanent feature), so
  the player cannot paint the whole dungeon crimson.


============================
PAINT 12 — COPPER
============================
Item ID:    copper_spray_paint
Name:       Copper Spray Paint
RGB:        (195, 115, 45)
Render bg (visible):   (95, 55, 15)
Render bg (explored):  (47, 27, 7)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Player on tile — energy gain per tick is increased by +30 (from base 100
  to effectively 130; i.e., ~23% faster action rate while standing here).
  Enemy on tile — energy gain per tick is reduced by -20 (from base 100
  to effectively 80; ~20% slower action rate while standing here).
  Both effects apply simultaneously if both are on the tile (impossible for
  the same tile, but two entities each get their own modifier).

  This is a direct energy-system interaction — the first tile of this kind.

Design rationale:
  Every other CC or speed interaction in the game is delivered via status
  effects (speedball, slowed, chill, etc.). Copper is the only tile that
  mechanically alters the energy accumulation rate in real time, without
  applying a named effect. This makes it feel like a spatial, positional
  advantage rather than a consumable buff.

  For the player: standing on copper means you get roughly one extra action
  every 4 turns. In a 10-turn fight this adds up to 2 extra actions — a
  significant tempo advantage that rewards holding the tile.

  For enemies: an enemy standing on copper (e.g., in a doorway you just
  painted) fires its attacks and moves 20% slower. Weaker than Silver (which
  skips a turn entirely) but applies to ALL time spent there, not just entry.

  Risk/Reward: the player loses the copper bonus the moment they retreat.
  Enemies that move off the tile immediately regain full speed. The tile
  rewards commitment from both sides.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Player energy bonus: +20 + 5*Graffiti_Lvl per tick.
  L0: +20; L1: +25; L2: +30; L3: +35.
  Enemy energy penalty: -15 - 5*Graffiti_Lvl per tick.
  L0: -15; L1: -20; L2: -25; L3: -30.

Synergies:
  Silver + Copper: paint Silver at the approach tile (enemy slips and loses
  a turn on entry), then Copper on your fighting tile (you act faster while
  they're slower). Two-tile speed control system.
  Orange + Copper: if you can tolerate standing on Orange (with high HP),
  Copper lets you deal with enemies faster, reducing the window you take
  orange damage. High risk, high reward combo.
  Speedball / RatRace buffs: already give +100 speed. Copper stacks
  additively — further padding a speed-focused build's advantage.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars):
  "Spray a tile copper."
  "Player on tile: +30 energy/tick"
  "(acts ~23% faster)."
  "Enemy on tile: -20 energy/tick"
  "(acts ~20% slower)."

Implementation notes:
  In the _run_energy_loop energy distribution phase (engine.py), where
  entities accumulate energy each tick:
    graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
    player_bonus = 20 + 5 * graffiti_lvl
    enemy_penalty = 15 + 5 * graffiti_lvl
    for entity in all_entities_including_player:
        spray = self.dungeon.spray_paint.get((entity.x, entity.y))
        if spray == "copper":
            if entity == self.player:
                gain += player_bonus
            else:
                gain = max(0, gain - enemy_penalty)
  This executes in the same block as Meth-Head meth energy bonus and
  other per-tick energy modifiers. Use the same pattern.
  No new Effect or PlayerStats field needed.

Balance notes:
  +30 energy out of a 100-threshold is significant but not game-breaking;
  the player still can't outrun a fast enemy indefinitely. The penalty side
  (-20 on enemies) is softer than Brown's old +50 move cost, but applies to
  ALL actions (attacks and moves) rather than just movement. Functionally
  more interesting while being less punishing in isolation.

PAINT 13 — COBALT
============================
Item ID:    cobalt_spray_paint
Name:       Cobalt Spray Paint
RGB:        (35, 75, 210)
Render bg (visible):   (12, 28, 100)
Render bg (explored):  (6, 14, 50)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 45

Effect:
  Player on tile — two simultaneous bonuses:
    1. +4 to player_stats.spell_damage_bonus while standing here.
       (Applies to all abilities that use spell_damage scaling: Blackkk Magic,
        Pyromancy, Cryomancy, Electrodynamics, Nuclear Research, etc.)
    2. Ability charge refund chance increased from 25% to 50%.
       (Blue paint grants 25% refund; Cobalt supersedes this, granting 50%
        if the player is on Cobalt instead. Only one tile applies at a time.)
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Blue paint rewards STS/ability users with a passive stat bonus and a 25%
  charge refund. Cobalt is the "deep caster" version: it doesn't give raw
  stats but dramatically improves spell output and sustainability. For a
  Blackkk Magic or Nuclear Research build, cobalt is the best possible
  fighting tile. It doesn't help melee or gun users at all — a genuine
  build-dependent tile whose value varies wildly by player archetype.

  The 50% charge refund means a PER_FLOOR ability used from this tile
  statistically lasts twice as long. Correctly placed before a boss floor
  or a dense room, it extends the player's ability window significantly.

  The spell_damage_bonus interacts with book_smarts scaling in spells.py
  (where spell_damage feeds into final damage). At BKS 10 with +4 spell_damage,
  a fireball dealing 20 base now deals 24 — a 20% boost.

Scale with Graffiti:
  spell_damage bonus = 3 + Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: +3; L1: +4; L2: +5; L3: +6.
  Charge refund stays flat at 50% — it replaces Blue's 25%, not stacks.

Synergies:
  Blue + Cobalt: Blue on the approach tile (STS boost while moving up),
  Cobalt on the fighting tile (spell damage + 50% refund while standing).
  Two-tile caster layout: use Blue to enhance targeting/STS-checks, then
  cast from Cobalt for maximum damage.
  Teal + Cobalt (adjacent tiles): Teal grants +20% XP while Cobalt buffs
  spells. If a spell earns Blackkk Magic XP on kill (already implemented),
  standing on Teal while having just painted Cobalt at the kill tile amplifies
  the XP gained from each kill.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars):
  "Spray a tile cobalt."
  "Player on tile: +4 spell damage."
  "Ability charge refund: 50%"
  "(replaces Blue paint's 25%)."

Implementation notes:
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses (engine.py):
    elif spray == "cobalt":
        graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        self.player_stats.tile_spell_damage_bonus = 3 + graffiti_lvl
  New PlayerStats field: tile_spell_damage_bonus: int = 0
  This field should be summed into player_stats.spell_damage wherever
  spell_damage is read in spells.py.
  Reset to 0 when player steps off (same pattern as tile_defense_bonus).

  For the 50% charge refund: the existing Blue-paint charge refund is in
  engine.py wherever ability charges are consumed. It currently reads:
    if spray == "blue" and random.random() < 0.25: refund charge
  Extend this to:
    if spray in ("blue", "cobalt"):
        threshold = 0.50 if spray == "cobalt" else 0.25
        if random.random() < threshold: refund charge
  No new Effect class needed.

Balance notes:
  +4 spell_damage is meaningful for spell builds (comparable to a
  +4-STR weapon giving ~2 extra melee damage after scaling). For non-
  casters, the tile is worthless — which is the intended tradeoff.
  50% charge refund is strong for TOTAL abilities but note that most
  abilities have INFINITE or PER_FLOOR charges; the 50% only matters for
  the latter. At most this doubles a PER_FLOOR ability's effective uses.
  Since few enemies will ever stand on cobalt, it has no enemy interaction
  cost — it is a purely player-facing buff tile, balanced by being build-gated.

PAINT 14 — VIOLET
============================
Item ID:    violet_spray_paint
Name:       Violet Spray Paint
RGB:        (150, 45, 195)
Render bg (visible):   (70, 15, 90)
Render bg (explored):  (35, 7, 45)
Tool charges: 8
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 50

Effect (RISK/REWARD — both edges are sharp):
  Player on tile — melee attacks deal +(3 + Graffiti_Lvl) bonus damage.
  Player on tile — all enemies that successfully hit the player from an
  adjacent tile deal +3 bonus damage on each strike (enemies gain a flat
  +3 to their final damage output while the player stands on violet).
  Enemy on tile — no separate effect (the enemy bonus applies only when
  they are adjacent to the player who is ON violet, not when the enemy
  stands on it themselves).

  Summary: you hit harder, but so does anyone adjacent to you.

Design rationale:
  This is the explicit risk/reward tile. The player voluntarily enters a
  "berserker stance" by standing on violet. The tile punishes hesitation:
  if you don't kill enemies quickly, the accumulated incoming damage will
  punish you for staying. The payoff for skilled play is consistent
  extra melee damage per swing; the cost is every surrounded enemy piles
  on harder.

  Unlike Orange (which damages the player passively), Violet only hurts you
  when an enemy successfully lands a melee hit. If you have high Swagger
  (defense), the enemy's +3 may be partially mitigated. If you have a stun
  weapon or crowd control, you can neutralize the risk by keeping enemies
  stunned. This rewards CC-combo play over straight tanking.

  8 charges (not 10) — the escalated risk and power justify reduced supply.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Player melee damage bonus = 3 + Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: +3; L1: +4; L2: +5; L3: +6.
  Enemy melee damage bonus stays flat at +3 — the risk does not increase
  as the player levels up (reward scales, cost stays constant).

Synergies:
  Green + Violet (adjacent tiles): paint Green at one adjacent tile and use
  it as a "safe retreat" — fall back to Green for defense when low HP,
  advance to Violet for the damage boost when healthy.
  Crimson + Violet on different positions: fight from Crimson to leech
  health back against a single target, move to Violet for burst damage
  on elite enemies.
  Slashing weapons (bleeding): rapid multi-hit attacks compound the bonus
  damage per swing, making bleeding stacks AND violet bonus both apply.
  Stun abilities: stun an adjacent enemy so they can't counterattack while
  on Violet. Reaps the damage bonus with zero risk window.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars):
  "Spray a tile violet."
  "Player on tile: +(3+Graffiti)"
  "melee damage."
  "Risk: adjacent enemies deal"
  "+3 damage back each hit."

Implementation notes:
  PLAYER DAMAGE BONUS:
  In combat.py _compute_player_attack_power or the final damage calc before
  applying to the target:
    if engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get((engine.player.x, engine.player.y)) == "violet":
        graffiti_lvl = engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        attack_power += (3 + graffiti_lvl)

  ENEMY DAMAGE BONUS:
  In the monster-attacks-player branch of perform_attack in combat.py,
  after the base damage is computed, before defense is subtracted:
    spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get((engine.player.x, engine.player.y))
    if spray == "violet":
        # Check that attacker is adjacent (Chebyshev distance == 1)
        dx = abs(attacker.x - engine.player.x)
        dy = abs(attacker.y - engine.player.y)
        if max(dx, dy) <= 1:
            damage += 3
  The adjacency check prevents ranged or long-reach monsters from
  benefiting from the violet bonus (only melee-range attackers get the +3).

  No new Effect class or PlayerStats field needed.
  PlayerStats could optionally carry a tile_melee_damage_bonus: int = 0
  for cleaner access — add to _update_tile_stat_bonuses if preferred.

Balance notes:
  +3-6 player melee damage is significant (floor 1-2 enemies have 15-30 HP;
  this cuts 1-2 extra turns off a fight). The +3 enemy bonus averages
  ~1.5 extra damage per hit after typical defense (swagger_defence ~1-2).
  Net: violet is positive EV against single enemies but risky against packs.
  8 charges limits total floor coverage; the player must decide where
  violet matters most (boss rooms, tight corridors, difficult encounters).

PAINT 15 — RUST
============================
Item ID:    rust_spray_paint
Name:       Rust Spray Paint
RGB:        (170, 75, 30)
Render bg (visible):   (80, 32, 8)
Render bg (explored):  (40, 16, 4)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Enemy on tile — each turn the enemy stands on the rust tile, they receive
  the "rusted" status effect (stacks with itself, max 10 stacks).
  Each stack of "rusted" reduces the enemy's effective defense by 1
  (minimum 0 defense). Stacks are permanent for the floor — they do NOT
  expire when the enemy leaves the tile.
  Player on tile — no effect. The player is immune to rust.

  Summary: parking enemies on rust gradually strips their armor,
  then all future damage to them — from weapons, spells, DoTs, orange paint,
  anything — deals more because defense is zero.

Design rationale:
  This is the "siege" tile — it rewards patient, methodical play. Against
  a 0-defense enemy, the player's base attack power hits for full value,
  and abilities/DoTs deal their complete output. The tile doesn't do any
  damage itself; it just degrades the target.

  Tactically, the player wants to lure the enemy onto rust, do something
  else for several turns while the rust stacks build, then finish with
  high-damage attacks. Pairs brilliantly with silver (slip enemy on rust =
  enemy can't move for a turn, eats 1-2 free rust stacks).

  Unlike Red (+25% damage directly), rust is a PERMANENT debuff that
  compounds over time. Against a boss with 5 defense, it takes 5 turns
  on the tile to strip armor. Against a normal enemy with 2 defense,
  2 turns and they're wide open. The payoff is long-term but reliable.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Stack rate: applies (1 + Graffiti_Lvl // 2) stacks per turn on tile.
  L0: 1 stack/turn; L1: 1; L2: 2 stacks/turn; L3: 2; L4: 3; etc.
  At L2 Graffiti, rust strips a 4-defense enemy in 2 turns instead of 4.

Synergies:
  Silver + Rust: silver slips the enemy (skip a turn). While they're
  slipped and frozen in place, they absorb 1-2 rust stacks for free.
  Orange + Rust: orange deals 5+2*Graffiti per turn. Against a de-armored
  enemy (rust has cleared their defense), orange's DoT hits for full base
  damage with no mitigation — enemies evaporate faster.
  Red + Rust: enemy standing on red tile takes +25% damage from all sources,
  AND has reduced defense from rust. Two-tile setup that creates a kill zone:
  lure enemy from rust (strip defense) onto red (amplify damage), then burst.
  Lime + Rust (Meth Lab): lime stacks tox (amplifies incoming damage
  multiplicatively). Rust removes defense (linear damage increase). Both
  together make enemies hit-squishes even on high-HP Meth Lab enemies.
  High-Strength builds: STR scaling on weapons benefits most from 0-defense
  enemies since the full power value lands.

New Status Effect Required: "rusted"
  id = "rusted"
  category = "debuff"
  display_name = "Rusted"
  duration: floor_duration = True (persists until floor end, not turn-count)
  stacks: int — each stack represents -1 defense.
  max_stacks = 10
  on_reapply: existing.stacks = min(existing.stacks + new.stacks, 10)
  modify_incoming_damage: not applicable (defense reduction is applied
    at the attacker-calculates-damage step, not via incoming damage hook)

  Implementation approach for defense reduction:
  In combat.py _compute_player_attack_power or perform_attack, when
  computing the defender's effective defense:
    rusted_eff = next((e for e in defender.status_effects
                       if getattr(e, 'id', '') == 'rusted'), None)
    rust_penalty = rusted_eff.stacks if rusted_eff else 0
    effective_defense = max(0, defender.defense - rust_penalty)
  Use effective_defense instead of defender.defense in the damage formula.

  Per-tick rust application in the energy loop (alongside orange paint block):
    graffiti_lvl = self.skills.get("Graffiti").level
    rust_stacks_per_turn = 1 + graffiti_lvl // 2
    for monster in monsters:
        if not monster.alive: continue
        if self.dungeon.spray_paint.get((monster.x, monster.y)) == "rust":
            effects.apply_effect(monster, self, "rusted",
                                 stacks=rust_stacks_per_turn,
                                 floor_duration=True, silent=True)

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars):
  "Spray a tile rust."
  "Enemies on tile: -1 defense/turn"
  "(stacks up to -10, permanent"
  "until floor end)."
  "Player is immune."

Files requiring changes for all 5 new paints:

items.py:
  5 new ITEM_DEFS entries: crimson, copper, cobalt, violet, rust spray paint.
  Add to _spray_descs dict for examine popup.

render.py:
  5 new elif branches for visible + explored tile background colors (2 blocks).

engine.py:
  _update_tile_stat_bonuses:
    cobalt — tile_spell_damage_bonus = 3 + graffiti_lvl
    violet — tile_melee_damage_bonus = 3 + graffiti_lvl (optional field)
    crimson — no stat field needed (hook is in melee hit path)
  Per-tick entity loop (alongside orange block):
    rust — apply "rusted" effect stacks to enemies on tile
  Energy distribution phase (alongside Meth-Head meth block):
    copper — player_energy_bonus += (20 + 5*graffiti_lvl) per tick
             enemy_energy -= (15 + 5*graffiti_lvl) per tick
  Ability charge refund: extend blue check to include cobalt at 50%.
  _SPRAY_TABLE: update to include all 15 paints (5 original +
    5 approved V2 + 5 V3 replacements).

    violet — add (3 + graffiti_lvl) to attack_power when player on violet
    crimson — after hit, heal ceil(dmg * heal_pct)
  Enemy damage to player:

============================
COMBO SYNERGIES (V3 PAINTS)
============================

Crimson + Violet (sequential tiles):
  Two tiles, two styles. Stand on Crimson against groups (leech helps
  survive multi-hit trades). Advance to Violet for single-target burst
  (bonus damage kills faster, acceptable risk vs 1 enemy).

Copper + Silver:
  Paint Silver at the corridor entrance (enemy slips on entry, loses a
  turn). Paint Copper on your fighting tile. While slipped enemy can't
  act, you're accumulating energy faster than normal. By the time they
  stand up, you may have already acted twice.

Cobalt + Red:
  Caster build setup. Red tile on the enemy's likely approach position
  (all damage to them increased 25%). Cast spells from Cobalt (+4-6 spell
  damage, 50% charge refund). Every cast from Cobalt hitting an enemy on
  Red is amplified by both sources: spell_damage increase AND 25% amp.

Rust + Orange + Lime (Meth Lab triple):
  Three adjacent tiles forming a gauntlet. Enemy walks through Lime (tox
  stack up, damage multiplier grows), gets lured onto Rust (defense stripped
  over 3-4 turns), then pushed or lured onto Orange (full DoT with no defense
  mitigation and tox-multiplied). Against a boss with 5+ defense and 100+ HP,
  this three-tile system can reduce effective HP by 50%+ before the player
  even draws a weapon.

Violet + Stabbing skill:
  Stabbing grants crit chance (street_smarts * 3%). Violet adds flat damage
  per swing. A crit from Violet multiplies the full (base + violet bonus)
  damage. A MEGA CRIT (Sniping L3) multiplies by 4x. At Graffiti L3: crit
  damage = (base_atk + 6) * 4. Against a dearmored enemy (rust): massive.


============================
BALANCE OVERVIEW
============================

Power ranking (these 5 vs existing 10):

Tier 1 (premium, strategic):
  Rust — long-term payoff; transforms fights against armored enemies.
  Violet — highest risk/reward; rewards skilled play, punishes greed.

Tier 2 (strong, build-relevant):
  Copper — energy system manipulation; consistently valuable for any build.
  Crimson — sustain in melee; powerful for weapon builds, weak for casters.

Tier 3 (build-gated):
  Cobalt — nearly useless for non-casters, strong for spell builds.
