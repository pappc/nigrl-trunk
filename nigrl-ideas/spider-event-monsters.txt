NIGRL — "Infested with Spiders" Floor Event: Enemy Designs
===========================================================
Date: 2026-03-23
Zone: Crack Den (zone_floors 1-2, i.e. floors 2-3 of the Crack Den)
Event ID: spider_infestation (config.py FLOOR_EVENT_REGISTRY)

This document designs 5 hostile spider enemy types for the spider_infestation
floor event. The event triggers on zone_floor 1 or 2 (config.py
ZONE_FLOOR_EVENTS eligible_floors=[1,2]).

When the event fires, the floor should feel distinctly different: more web
hazards scattered across rooms (particularly in corners and doorways), higher
spider density, and a different threat profile than the usual crack den rabble.
The spider enemies replace a significant portion of the normal spawn table for
that floor — not ALL enemies, but enough that the player walks in and
immediately thinks "oh. oh no."

These spiders are urban pests with street-level personality. Brown recluses in
the walls. Roach-adjacent infestation vibes. Not magical or demonic — just big,
fast, and very much not pleased to see you.

Spider event spawn table suggestion (zone_floor 1 or 2 override):
  pipe_spider:     25
  sac_spider:      20
  wolf_spider:     20
  orb_weaver:      15
  brood_mother:     8
  (normal crack den enemies fill remaining ~12% so the floor isn't sterile)

Recommended web density: 8-14 cobweb hazards scattered at the start of the
floor (placed during spawn phase, preferably in room corners, doorways, and
along corridors). This is more than the player would normally encounter from
the Arachnigga skill alone.

Note on the graphical tile: 0xE004 is the lavender Spider Hatchling tile used
for the allied summon. These hostile spiders use ASCII characters to remain
visually distinct from the friendly summon. The WEB_CODEPOINT tile (0xE003)
still renders cobweb hazards normally.

===========================================================================
SPIDER 1 — PIPE SPIDER
===========================================================================

Registry key: pipe_spider
Street concept: The common apartment spider. Every crackhead has seen one in
the pipes — that big brown one with legs. Usually harmless. Usually.
These ones aren't.

--- ASCII & Visual ---
char: 's'   (lowercase, fast and small)
color: (160, 120, 80)   -- muddy brown, pipe-rust colored

--- Stats ---
constitution:  (1, 2)
strength:      (2, 3)
street_smarts: (0, 0)
book_smarts:   (1, 1)
tolerance:     (2, 3)
swagger:       (1, 2)
base_hp:        2
base_damage:   (1, 2)   -- very light hits; damage is from venom accumulation
defense:        0
dodge_chance:  15        -- quick, hard to swat

Computed HP range:    base_hp(2) + con(1-2)*5 = 7-12 HP
Computed power range: base_damage(1-2) + str(2-3) = 3-5 (before zone 0.5x)
Effective damage:     1-2 per hit after zone multiplier (venom does the real work)

--- Spawn ---
spawn_min: 1
spawn_max: 4      -- these come in numbers; a room with 4 is genuinely scary
spawn_with: []    -- pure numbers; no escorts

--- AI ---
ai: WANDER_AMBUSH
sight_radius: 4   -- not great at spotting, but once it sees you it's locked on
speed: 130        -- noticeably faster than the player (100 baseline)

--- Special Attacks ---
None. Simple, predictable biter. What makes it dangerous is the venom.

--- On-Hit Effects ---
  OnHitEffect(
      name="Pipe Venom",
      kind=EffectKind.DOT,
      amount=2,
      duration=6,
      chance=0.60,
  )

Note: Use the existing venom effect ID for Arachnigga XP hooks. The venom
stacks — three Pipe Spiders biting in quick succession means 6 stacks
worth of DOT, which is 6 damage/turn. With 4 in a group and a slow player
in the middle, this gets lethal fast even at low individual power.

--- Behavioral Description ---
The Pipe Spider is the swarm unit. Individually it's a nuisance — 1-2
effective damage per hit with a venom proc. Four of them chewing on you
simultaneously is an emergency. They wander passively until you enter sight,
then sprint. Their higher speed (130) means they catch up even if you try to
kite. Because they have no special attacks, no special AI tricks, and low HP,
a player who is ready for them can clean one up in two hits. A player who
rounds a corner into four of them and gets three venom stacks per turn learns
an important lesson about the Crack Den.

--- Tactical Identity ---
The swarmer / venom accumulator. Forces the player to prioritize targets and
manage DOT before it spirals. If the player has Munching skill (food buffs)
or anti-venom items, this fight is manageable. If not, it's a race against
the clock. Arachnigga players can web one to split the group — fighting 3
instead of 4 is a meaningful difference. The 15% dodge also makes hitting
them slightly frustrating, adding to the "trying to swat a spider" feeling.

--- Interesting to Fight Because ---
The dopamine of swatting them is high — they die in 2-3 hits. But the
pressure of a group applying venom faster than you can heal creates genuine
tension. Players with Stabbing+Windfury can clear them efficiently (extra
hit proc helps), rewarding build investment. Players without burst damage
options have to think about what order to kill them.

--- Arachnigga Interaction ---
Each kill grants spider XP. Killing 4 in a room is a meaningful XP spike
for Arachnigga investment. Web immunity (L1) turns the entire event from
danger into opportunity — you can lay webs to split the swarm, then pick
them off safely. Without L1 immunity, the cobweb hazards on this floor can
trap YOU while they close in, which is the floor event doing its job.

--- cash_drop: (0, 1)   -- spiders don't carry cash, this is flavor ---
--- death_drop_chance: 0.0 ---

===========================================================================
SPIDER 2 — SAC SPIDER
===========================================================================

Registry key: sac_spider
Street concept: Sac spiders make silk sacs instead of webs — they hide in
corners in a little sleeping bag of silk. This one has been here long enough
to silk up half a room before you walked in. It's very territorial about it.

--- ASCII & Visual ---
char: 'S'   (uppercase — medium threat, more substantial than the pipe spider)
color: (200, 200, 160)   -- pale yellowish-white, the color of old silk

--- Stats ---
constitution:  (2, 4)
strength:      (3, 5)
street_smarts: (2, 3)    -- not dumb; will use the webs it's already placed
book_smarts:   (1, 2)
tolerance:     (2, 3)
swagger:       (2, 4)
base_hp:        4
base_damage:   (2, 3)    -- meaningful damage compared to pipe spider
defense:        1        -- silk wrapping around its body, minimal armor

Computed HP range:    base_hp(4) + con(2-4)*5 = 14-24 HP
Computed power range: base_damage(2-3) + str(3-5) = 5-8 (before zone 0.5x)
Effective damage:     2-4 per hit after zone multiplier
Crit chance:          6-9% (from street_smarts 2-3)

--- Spawn ---
spawn_min: 1
spawn_max: 2
spawn_with: []   -- the environment does its own work via pre-placed webs

--- AI ---
ai: ROOM_GUARD    -- territorial; does not chase you into the next room
sight_radius: 5
speed: 110        -- slightly faster than player

--- Special Attacks ---
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Silk Shot",
      chance=0.25,
      damage_mult=0.5,   -- half damage, the point is the web effect
      on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
          name="Webbed",
          kind=EffectKind.STUN,   -- currently STUN; see New Effects section below
          amount=0,
          duration=3,
      ),
  )

Implementation note: Silk Shot should apply the "web_stuck" effect (from
effects.py WebStuckEffect) rather than a standard STUN, so it uses the same
50% escape-attempt logic as a floor cobweb. This makes the Sac Spider the
only enemy that can directly apply the web_stuck status mid-combat, turning
the fight into exactly the situation the Arachnigga player trained for.
EffectKind may need a WEB_SHOT entry, OR the special attack can use a STUN
with duration=2 as a simpler approximation until web_stuck is promotable
to an OnHitEffect kind. Flag this for the developer to decide.

--- On-Hit Effects ---
  OnHitEffect(
      name="Silk Slick",
      kind=EffectKind.DOT,
      amount=1,
      duration=4,
      chance=0.35,
  )

Minimal bleed-style DOT. The Sac Spider is not a venom spider — its danger
is positional and CC-based, not DOT accumulation.

--- Behavioral Description ---
The Sac Spider is the room controller. It guards its space like a Fat Gooner
guards his section of the strip club. It doesn't chase you. It doesn't need
to — it's surrounded by cobweb hazards that were placed at floor gen time,
and if you step into its room you're probably going to walk through a web on
your way in. Once you're stuck, it has time for a slow deliberate attack.
The ROOM_GUARD AI means it won't follow you into a hallway if you run. This
is intentional — the fight should feel like "invading its space" rather than
"being hunted."

The Silk Shot has a 25% chance on any given attack. In a 5-attack fight,
that's ~76% chance to fire at least once. When it hits, the player is web-
stuck for 3 turns of escape attempts, during which the Sac Spider gets free
hits. This is a hard lesson if you blunder in without checking sight lines.

--- Tactical Identity ---
The ambush/punisher. Controls the room. Rewards careful approach: enter from
a side that dodges the pre-placed webs, attack from range (using items or
spells) to bait it out of the web-dense area, or use Arachnigga web_snare to
lock IT down before it locks you down. The room_guard AI actually makes it
more dangerous, not less — you HAVE to deal with it to clear the room, and
you can't lure it somewhere safe.

--- Interesting to Fight Because ---
The asymmetry of CC. This spider can web you; webs are already in the room.
You have to choose: rush it before it can shoot silk, or try to navigate
around webs to get a clean angle. Getting webbed AND taking Silk Slick DOT
while you spend 2-3 turns trying to escape is the intended bad outcome that
careful play avoids. For Arachnigga players, countering a web with YOUR OWN
web on the sac spider feels satisfying.

--- cash_drop: (0, 2) ---
--- death_drop_chance: 0.0 ---

===========================================================================
SPIDER 3 — WOLF SPIDER
===========================================================================

Registry key: wolf_spider
Street concept: The wolf spider doesn't make webs. It HUNTS. Fast, aggressive,
no patience. It has the energy of a crackhead who just did something that
wasn't crack. Named after the wolf — it stalks, it runs, it goes for the
throat.

--- ASCII & Visual ---
char: 'w'
color: (120, 80, 50)   -- dark earthy brown, a hunting animal's camouflage

--- Stats ---
constitution:  (3, 5)
strength:      (5, 8)    -- legitimate damage dealer
street_smarts: (3, 5)    -- crit chance 9-15%; it finds weak spots
book_smarts:   (1, 2)
tolerance:     (3, 5)
swagger:       (3, 5)
base_hp:        5
base_damage:   (2, 4)    -- base hits hard before stat scaling
defense:        0
dodge_chance:  20        -- very hard to hit; dodges like it runs

Computed HP range:    base_hp(5) + con(3-5)*5 = 20-30 HP
Computed power range: base_damage(2-4) + str(5-8) = 7-12 (before zone 0.5x)
Effective damage:     3-6 per hit after zone multiplier — comparable to a Thug
Crit chance:          9-15% from street_smarts

--- Spawn ---
spawn_min: 1
spawn_max: 2
spawn_with: []

--- AI ---
ai: WANDER_AMBUSH
sight_radius: 7     -- wider detection range; it FINDS you
speed: 140          -- fastest enemy on the event floor; 40% faster than player

--- Special Attacks ---
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Pounce",
      chance=0.30,
      damage_mult=1.5,    -- 50% bonus damage
      on_hit_effect=None, -- no status; the damage IS the statement
  )

--- On-Hit Effects ---
None. The Wolf Spider is a pure damage dealer. No venom, no web. It's a mugging.

--- Behavioral Description ---
The Wolf Spider is the monster that makes you turn around and run. Speed 140
means it gains 40 more energy per tick than the player. In practice, it acts
significantly more often. It hunts with a wide sight radius, closes the gap
fast, and then hits hard with a Pounce that deals 1.5x damage about 30% of
the time. No special tricks, no CC. Just pure predator pressure.

The 20% dodge chance means roughly 1 in 5 of your attacks whiffs. Against
an enemy with 20-30 HP, missing attacks extends the fight by a turn or two.
Those are extra turns it gets to hit you. The math is mean. This is designed
to feel like fighting something that's just physically better at this than
you are.

It's a floor 2-3 enemy. Players at this point have meaningful stats and some
items. The Wolf Spider is calibrated to threaten, not instakill. A well-geared
player beats it in 4-5 attacks. A player who walked in wounded, or who has two
more behind it, is in trouble.

--- Tactical Identity ---
The pressure/assassin unit. Forces the player to think about spacing and HP
pools. It can't be kited (too fast). It can't be CC'd reliably (it doesn't
have an on-hit effect to punish, and stunning it just delays). The correct
answers are: fight it when healthy, or use something fast (Niglet's speed
boost items, a speed-boosted weapon hit, Windfury proc) to burst it before
it gets too many actions.

--- Interesting to Fight Because ---
Speed differential creates genuine urgency. This spider is the event's
"oh this is actually threatening" moment. Pipe Spiders are annoying. Sac
Spiders are tricky. Wolf Spiders are actually dangerous. Getting caught by
two of them at the same time is a permadeath scenario for underprepared
players, which is appropriate for a mid-game floor event.

--- Arachnigga Interaction ---
Web snare on a Wolf Spider is a high-value play — stopping it from acting
repeatedly changes the fight from desperate to controlled. This is the enemy
that makes the Arachnigga player feel the investment was worth it. Without
webs, the player needs high damage output or good healing to survive
sustained wolf spider pressure.

--- cash_drop: (1, 4)   -- slightly more; it had something in its mandibles ---
--- death_drop_chance: 0.0 ---

===========================================================================
SPIDER 4 — ORB WEAVER
===========================================================================

Registry key: orb_weaver
Street concept: Big circular web across a whole corner of the room. You've
seen them on Halloween decorations. This one is large, has been here for
months, and is not decorative.

--- ASCII & Visual ---
char: 'O'
color: (180, 140, 200)   -- faint purple-grey; plump abdomen, slightly lavender
                          -- color nod to the spider hatchling tile without
                          -- using the graphical tile (which is player-aligned)

--- Stats ---
constitution:  (2, 4)
strength:      (2, 4)
street_smarts: (1, 2)
book_smarts:   (2, 4)
tolerance:     (3, 5)
swagger:       (1, 3)
base_hp:        6
base_damage:   (1, 2)    -- physically weak; relies on web and venom
defense:        0
dodge_chance:   5

Computed HP range:    base_hp(6) + con(2-4)*5 = 16-26 HP
Computed power range: base_damage(1-2) + str(2-4) = 3-6 (before zone 0.5x)
Effective damage:     1-3 per hit — below average melee

--- Spawn ---
spawn_min: 1
spawn_max: 1   -- always solo; the web is its room
spawn_with: [] -- BUT see note on web hazard seeding below

--- AI ---
ai: STATIONARY_GUARD   -- does not move; sits in its web and waits
sight_radius: 6
speed: 90              -- slower than player; it's built for ambush, not chase

--- Special Attacks ---
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Web Wrap",
      chance=0.40,
      damage_mult=0.3,   -- barely a tap; this is almost entirely the CC
      on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
          name="Wrapped",
          kind=EffectKind.STUN,
          amount=0,
          duration=2,
      ),
  )
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Venom Bite",
      chance=0.30,
      damage_mult=1.2,
      on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
          name="Orb Venom",
          kind=EffectKind.DOT,
          amount=3,
          duration=5,
          chance=1.0,
      ),
  )

Implementation note on special attack ordering: the engine checks special
attacks in list order, firing the first one that succeeds. Web Wrap (40%)
is checked first, then Venom Bite (30%). Combined probability of some
special attack firing: ~58% per turn. This means roughly every other hit,
it's doing something nasty. If Web Wrap fires, the player is stunned 2 turns.
Venom Bite applies 3 DOT per turn for 5 turns (15 total uncapped by zone
mult because venom bypasses the zone damage multiplier — confirm with
developer whether status DOT is zone-multiplied).

--- On-Hit Effects ---
  OnHitEffect(
      name="Silk Tangle",
      kind=EffectKind.DOT,
      amount=1,
      duration=3,
      chance=0.25,
  )

Even the basic attack leaves a small amount of silk irritant. 1 DOT for 3t.

--- Behavioral Description ---
The Orb Weaver sits completely still. It has a territory — a room full of
pre-placed cobwebs — and it waits there. STATIONARY_GUARD means it moves
during the IDLE phase toward its spawn room and then... stops. If you enter
its room, you are in its web. It can't chase you if you run, which is what
separates it from the Wolf Spider.

What makes it threatening is the combination of Web Wrap stun and Venom Bite.
A 2-turn stun while standing in its web cluster means you spend those 2 turns
doing nothing while it attacks you again. If the room was pre-webbed (which
the event guarantees: Orb Weaver rooms should have 3-5 cobwebs scattered
around the room at gen time), the player may have walked through a web to
reach it. Now they're web-stuck, THEN stunned, THEN venomed. This combo is
the event's "oh you thought the room was safe" moment.

Its low speed (90) and stationary behavior mean it's trivial to fight IF you
approach carefully, kill it from range, or burn through it fast enough that
the 58% special-attack rate never fires more than once. But players who walk
into the room without a plan and start button-mashing get the full combo.

--- Tactical Identity ---
The trap spider. Rewards patience and preparation. Punishes rushing.
There is always a clean approach available: use a LOS ability from the
doorway, throw an item, cast a spell. The Orb Weaver is designed to be
solvable before you ever enter melee range. Players who solve it that way
feel clever. Players who walk in without thinking encounter the web/stun/
venom trifecta.

--- Web Hazard Seeding (Implementation Note) ---
At floor generation, when an Orb Weaver is spawned, place 3-5 cobweb
entities (create_web from hazards.py) within a 3-tile radius of its spawn
point. This recreates the "central web" concept. Use the same logic as
existing web spawning in engine.py but executed during dungeon spawn_entities
for the event floor. The Orb Weaver itself is immune to its own webs
(similar to how spider_hatchling is marked immune in engine.py line 1005 —
add a check: if entity has enemy_type == "orb_weaver", skip web_stuck
application).

--- Interesting to Fight Because ---
It's a puzzle, not a brawl. Do you walk in? Or do you find a clever angle?
The correct answer changes based on your build. Magic users have an easy time
(SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS abilities don't require entering the room). Melee builds
need to commit. This split makes the encounter feel different for different
player types without being unfair to either.

--- cash_drop: (0, 3) ---
--- death_drop_chance: 0.15 ---
--- death_drop_table: ["kush"]  -- silky, you know ---

===========================================================================
SPIDER 5 — BROOD MOTHER
===========================================================================

Registry key: brood_mother
Street concept: A HUGE spider. The kind that lives somewhere for a decade and
gets fat. Named for what she does when she's dying — she doesn't go alone.

--- ASCII & Visual ---
char: 'M'   (uppercase — the biggest spider on the floor, rare and dangerous)
color: (220, 80, 60)   -- deep rust red/orange; intimidating coloration that
                        -- stands out from the muddy browns of the other spiders.
                        -- Red = "look at me, I am a problem."

--- Stats ---
constitution:  (6, 9)
strength:      (4, 6)
street_smarts: (2, 4)
book_smarts:   (2, 3)
tolerance:     (5, 8)
swagger:       (4, 6)
base_hp:        10
base_damage:   (2, 4)
defense:        2       -- hard exoskeleton; not trivially deleted

Computed HP range:    base_hp(10) + con(6-9)*5 = 40-55 HP — tankiest spider
Computed power range: base_damage(2-4) + str(4-6) = 6-10 (before zone 0.5x)
Effective damage:     3-5 per hit after zone multiplier — steady, dangerous
Crit chance:          6-12%

--- Spawn ---
spawn_min: 1
spawn_max: 1     -- only ever one Brood Mother per spawn event
spawn_with:
  SpawnEscort(type="pipe_spider", count=(2, 4))

The Brood Mother always arrives with 2-4 Pipe Spiders. She's not a loner.
She has her children with her. This escort creates an immediate triage
decision: kill the small fast ones first to stop venom accumulation, or
go for the Brood Mother to prevent the DEATH_SPLIT from spawning more?

--- AI ---
ai: WANDER_AMBUSH
sight_radius: 8    -- excellent spatial awareness, fills the mother role
speed: 90          -- slow and deliberate. She doesn't run. She doesn't need to.

--- Special Attacks ---
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Venomous Bite",
      chance=0.35,
      damage_mult=1.3,
      on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
          name="Heavy Venom",
          kind=EffectKind.DOT,
          amount=4,
          duration=8,
          chance=1.0,
      ),
  )
  SpecialAttack(
      name="Silk Burst",
      chance=0.20,
      damage_mult=0.0,    -- no physical damage
      on_hit_effect=OnHitEffect(
          name="Mummified",
          kind=EffectKind.STUN,
          amount=0,
          duration=3,
      ),
  )

Venomous Bite (35%) applies 4 DOT for 8 turns = 32 total damage from one
proc. That is a serious threat over time. The player needs to finish her
before multiple venom procs stack.

Silk Burst (20%) is a 0-damage hit that stuns for 3 turns. This is her
"you're not going anywhere" move. When the children are still alive and she
lands this, the player gets 3 free turns of getting hit by pipe spiders plus
whatever she does on her next action.

--- On-Hit Effects ---
  OnHitEffect(
      name="Venom",
      kind=EffectKind.DOT,
      amount=2,
      duration=5,
      chance=0.50,
  )

Even basic attacks have a 50% chance to apply 2-DOT venom for 5 turns. The
venom stack mechanic (VenomEffect in effects.py) means her basic hits stack
with Venomous Bite procs. Multiple venom sources accumulate.

--- Death Split ---
death_split_type:  "pipe_spider"
death_split_count: 3

On death, 3 Pipe Spiders spawn from her corpse. This is the moment.
You killed the Brood Mother — congratulations, now there are 3 more spiders
where she was standing, plus the ones that arrived with her escort that might
still be alive. The room does not get quieter when she dies.

The death split is the most important piece of her design. Players who try to
kite her until she dies while dealing with the escorts discover that killing
her spawns a new wave. Players who burst her down fast minimize the escort
problem. The correct play is to kill her LAST after clearing escorts, so the
split spiders don't stack with existing ones. Or burst her so fast that the
split children spawn before the escorts are dead and the player has room to
handle them.

--- Behavioral Description ---
The Brood Mother wanders slowly. She is not aggressive until you enter her
sight — WANDER_AMBUSH means she's not actively hunting until she sees you.
When she locks on, she advances steadily. Her low speed (90) means she's
always slightly behind her children, who have already reached you. This
creates a natural combat flow: children hit first, you're managing venom
stacks, then she arrives and starts applying her own heavier venom.

Her 2 defense means the player's first hits deal reduced damage. She is meant
to feel tanky and threatening — not a health-sponge, but genuinely hard to
take down quickly. Combined with the steady venom application and the stun
threat, she creates a sustained combat scenario that tests resource management.

--- Tactical Identity ---
The boss encounter of the event. Each floor should have at most one or two
Brood Mothers (the spawn weight of 8 vs 25 for pipe spiders reflects this).
She is the named experience of the event floor — the reason the floor feels
different. Killing her is an achievement. Seeing her for the first time at a
bad moment is a potential run-ender.

Her role in the event's ecosystem: she is the target. The event floor forces
the player to navigate to a threat they can see coming (slow, large 'M' with
an escort cluster). The question is whether they're in good enough shape when
they reach her.

--- Interesting to Fight Because ---
The death split creates a genuine "oh come ON" moment that is actually fair
once the player understands it. The children on death are smaller than the
escort, and the player is usually better positioned after winning the fight.
But the first time it happens, the surprise is real. The second time, the
player manages it intentionally. This learning loop is good roguelike design.

The Venomous Bite stacking with on-hit venom means the player is always
racing against a DOT clock. The Stun means she can PAUSE that clock on
your side while adding to hers. High stakes, meaningful counterplay.

--- Arachnigga Interaction ---
Killing the Brood Mother gives a substantial Arachnigga XP bonus from:
  - escort kills (4x Pipe Spider kills en route)
  - venom applications (multiple procs from her attacks)
  - death split kills (3 more pipe spiders after)
A single Brood Mother encounter can be worth 5-8 Arachnigga skill point
events. This is the event's dedicated Arachnigga farming opportunity.

--- cash_drop: (3, 10) ---
--- death_drop_chance: 0.35 ---
--- death_drop_table: ["kush", "benadryl"] ---
(flavor note: benadryl is a stretch but fits — you definitely need it after this)

===========================================================================
FLOOR EVENT IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
===========================================================================

These notes are for the developer implementing the spider_infestation event.
No code changes required from the game designer — these are handoff notes.

--- How the event should modify the floor ---

1. SPAWN TABLE OVERRIDE:
   When floor_event == "spider_infestation", replace the normal crack_den
   spawn table for that floor with the spider event spawn table defined at
   the top of this document. Approximately 88% spiders, 12% normal enemies.
   The simplest implementation: in dungeon.py or zone_generators.py, check
   engine.get_floor_event(global_floor) before selecting the spawn table,
   and substitute the spider table if "spider_infestation" is active.

2. WEB HAZARD SEEDING:
   Place 8-14 cobweb entities (hazards.create_web) during spawn_entities
   for the event floor. Preferred placement:
   - Room corners (2-4 webs spread across 3+ rooms)
   - Doorway tiles (1-2 webs in narrow passages between rooms)
   - Within 3-tile radius of Orb Weaver spawn points (3-5 per orb weaver)
   Avoid placing webs on the player start tile or directly on stairs.

3. ORB WEAVER WEB IMMUNITY:
   In engine.py, where web_stuck is applied to entities stepping on webs
   (around line 1003-1010), add a check:
       if getattr(entity, 'enemy_type', '') == 'orb_weaver': break
   This mirrors the existing spider_hatchling immunity check.

4. ENTRY MESSAGE:
   The FLOOR_EVENT_REGISTRY entry already defines:
       "message": "The floor appears to be infested by spiders."
   This should appear when the player enters the floor, ideally with a
   distinct color (pale yellow/brown to evoke spider silk).

5. VENOM EFFECT AND ARACHNIGGA XP:
   The venom on-hit effects from Pipe Spider and Brood Mother should use

===========================================================================
DESIGN NOTES — HOW THESE 5 WORK TOGETHER AS AN EVENT
===========================================================================

The five spiders cover five distinct gameplay archetypes that don't overlap:

  PIPE SPIDER:   Swarm unit. Threat through numbers and venom accumulation.
  SAC SPIDER:    Territorial controller. Threat through room ownership and web CC.
  WOLF SPIDER:   Speed predator. Threat through raw speed and pressure.
  ORB WEAVER:    Ambush trap. Threat through combo CC and patience.
  BROOD MOTHER:  Boss encounter. Threat through tankiness, DOT, and death spawn.

A player clearing the event floor will encounter all five. Each encounter
demands a different response:
  - Pipe Spider: burst them fast, manage venom uptime
  - Sac Spider: approach carefully, deal with its web before engaging
  - Wolf Spider: stay healthy, don't let it catch you hurt
  - Orb Weaver: engage from range when possible, don't step in the webs
  - Brood Mother: kill escorts first, then burst her, manage the death split

No two rooms feel the same. The event floor has real texture.

Cross-event interactions:
  A Wolf Spider chasing the player through a web-dense area (near an Orb
  Weaver's room) can accidentally get web-stuck, giving the player breathing
  room. This emergent interaction isn't designed-in but will happen naturally
  and should feel fun when it does.

  If a Brood Mother's escort pipe spiders walk through an Orb Weaver's room
  on their way to the player, some of them may get web-stuck in the Orb
  Weaver's territory — forcing the player to re-evaluate whether to clean up
  that room on their way back or risk the stuck spiders breaking free later.

  All spiders are potential Arachnigga XP sources. The event is balanced to
  provide roughly 1.5-2x normal Arachnigga skill progression on a cleared
  floor compared to a normal floor. This makes it a skill investment moment
  rather than just a punishment.
