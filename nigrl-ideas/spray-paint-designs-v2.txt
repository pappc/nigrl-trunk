SPRAY PAINT DESIGNS V2 — 10 NEW COLORS
=======================================
NIGRL roguelike | Design document

Purpose: Expand the spray paint consumable roster from 5 to 15.
Each new paint follows the established pattern in ITEM_DEFS (items.py)
and requires corresponding entries in: render.py (bg colors for visible
and explored states), combat.py (_apply_tile_amp), engine.py
(_update_tile_stat_bonuses and any per-tick loops), and the examine
popup (_spray_descs in items.py). The Graffiti unlock perk table
(_SPRAY_TABLE in engine.py) should include all 15 paints.

Existing paints for reference (never overlap mechanically):
  Red    — enemy on tile: +25% damage taken
  Blue   — player on tile: +5 Street-Smarts; 25% preserve ability charges
  Green  — player on tile: +4 Defense
  Orange — all entities on tile: 5 + 2*Graffiti_Lvl damage per turn
  Silver — entity entering tile: slipped (skip move, +50% melee dmg taken)

PAINT 6 — YELLOW
============================
Item ID:    yellow_spray_paint
Name:       Yellow Spray Paint
RGB:        (230, 220, 40)
Render bg (visible):   (120, 115, 10)
Render bg (explored):  (60, 58, 5)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Player on tile — +3 Street-Smarts AND +3 Book-Smarts simultaneously.
  No enemy interaction.

Design rationale:
  Blue grants +5 STS alone. Yellow splits the bonus across STS and BKS,
  making it valuable for hybrid builds (Smartsness, Gatting+spell combos)
  without simply out-scaling blue for pure STS builds. "Dual-channel"
  stat tile — the first of its kind.

Scale with Graffiti: No direct scaling. Synergy: Living Canvas (L3) can
  auto-paint a tile yellow when the player uses an ability, rewarding
  Smartsness + Graffiti hybrid builds.

Examine popup lines (max 46 chars each):
  "Spray a tile yellow."
  "Player on tile: +3 Street-Smarts"
  "and +3 Book-Smarts."

Implementation notes:
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses: add branch
    elif spray == "yellow":
        bonuses["street_smarts"] = 3
        bonuses["book_smarts"] = 3
  No new effects.py entries needed.

PAINT 7 — PURPLE
============================
Item ID:    purple_spray_paint
Name:       Purple Spray Paint
RGB:        (160, 40, 210)
Render bg (visible):   (80, 15, 105)
Render bg (explored):  (40, 8, 52)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Enemy on tile — 30% chance per turn to become "confused" (1 turn).
  Confused enemies move in a random direction instead of toward the player.
  Does NOT stack: if enemy is already confused, no new roll.
  Player on tile — no direct bonus, but confusion rolls still trigger.

New effect required: confused_paint
  id = "confused_paint"
  category = "debuff"
  duration = 1
  before_turn: pick random walkable adjacent tile, move entity there
    (if that tile is blocked or wall, entity stays; turn still consumed).
  No stacking: on_reapply refreshes duration.

Design rationale:
  Offensive control tile. Pairs with orange (chase enemy onto orange,
  confusion delays escape). Purple discourages enemies from standing still
  on high-traffic corridors.

Scale with Graffiti:
  L1 Taggin': 50% chance spray doesn't consume charge — standard.
  Living Canvas (L3) auto-red proc is separate; purple doesn't auto-proc.
  No direct level scaling; the 30% per-turn rate is intentionally moderate
  since confused enemies still survive and may stumble toward the player.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile purple."
  "Enemy on tile: 30% each turn"
  "to become confused (1 turn)."
  "Confused enemies move randomly."

Implementation notes:
  Per-tick loop in engine.py (alongside orange damage loop):
    for entity in monsters:
        if spray_paint.get((entity.x, entity.y)) == "purple":
            if not has_effect(entity, "confused_paint"):
                if random.random() < 0.30:
                    apply_effect(entity, engine, "confused_paint")
  effects.py: new ConfusedPaintEffect class.

PAINT 8 — PINK
============================
Item ID:    pink_spray_paint
Name:       Pink Spray Paint
RGB:        (255, 100, 160)
Render bg (visible):   (130, 30, 75)
Render bg (explored):  (65, 15, 38)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Player on tile — +15 max HP (temporary, lasts while standing on tile).
  Implemented as a tile_max_hp_bonus field on PlayerStats, updated in
  _update_tile_stat_bonuses alongside tile_defense_bonus.
  HP does NOT change when entering: only max rises (player isn't
  healed by stepping on). HP clamped to new max on step off.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  A defensive tile that rewards holding ground. Unlike green (+4 Defense),
  pink rewards tanky CON builds by inflating their HP ceiling while
  positioned. Combined with green, creates a strong "fortified tile" combo
  — though on different tiles, so the player can only benefit from one at
  a time.

Scale with Graffiti:
  L2 Street Art: when enemy dies on painted tile, heal 5 HP. Pink tile
  qualifies for this trigger even though its own effect is player-facing.
  Encourages luring enemies onto your pink tile to secure both the max HP
  buffer AND the kill heal.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile pink."
  "Player on tile: +15 max HP"
  "while standing here."
  "HP capped on leaving."

Implementation notes:
  PlayerStats needs new field: tile_max_hp_bonus: int = 0
  engine.py: in _update_tile_stat_bonuses:
    elif spray == "pink":
        self.player_stats.tile_max_hp_bonus = 15
  In effective_max_hp property (or wherever HP cap is enforced):
    add tile_max_hp_bonus to the cap.
  On step-off: tile_max_hp_bonus resets to 0, then clamp
    player.hp = min(player.hp, effective_max_hp).

  normal (gold qualifies as "painted tile"). So gold + L2 provides:

  Hook into the "entity_died" EventBus event (already wired for other

PAINT 10 — TEAL / CYAN
============================
Item ID:    teal_spray_paint
Name:       Teal Spray Paint
RGB:        (40, 200, 185)
Render bg (visible):   (10, 100, 90)
Render bg (explored):  (5, 50, 45)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 35

Effect:
  Player on tile — +20% XP from all skill XP gains while standing here.
  Multiplier applied as a flat 1.20x on all skill XP ticks that occur
  while player.xy == spray tile.
  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Utility tile for grind-focused play. Incentivizes setting up a "learning
  corner" and fighting from a fixed position. Synergizes with any skill
  that awards XP per hit (Stabbing, Beating, Graffiti, Smoking).
  Distinctly different from blue (STS + ability charges) and yellow
  (dual stat); teal is about long-term character progression.

Scale with Graffiti:
  This tile directly accelerates Graffiti XP when the player earns
  graffiti XP events (kills on painted tiles, hits on painted tiles),
  creating a recursive loop: more Graffiti XP → higher level → stronger
  tile effects → more kills on tile → more Graffiti XP.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile teal."
  "Player on tile: +20% skill XP"
  "from all sources."

Implementation notes:
  PlayerStats needs new field: tile_xp_bonus: float = 0.0
  In _update_tile_stat_bonuses:
    elif spray == "teal":
        self.player_stats.tile_xp_bonus = 0.20
  In xp_progression.py or wherever skills.gain_potential_exp is called,
  apply multiplier:
    xp = round(xp * (1.0 + self.player_stats.tile_xp_bonus))
  Note: xp_multiplier (from Lain ring) is already separate; these stack
  multiplicatively in sequence: xp * xp_multiplier * (1 + tile_xp_bonus).

PAINT 11 — BROWN / SLUDGE
============================
Item ID:    brown_spray_paint
Name:       Brown Spray Paint
RGB:        (120, 75, 20)
Render bg (visible):   (70, 40, 10)
Render bg (explored):  (35, 20, 5)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: meth_lab (primarily), crack_den
Value: 30

Effect:
  Enemy on tile — movement slowed: enemy's move_cost increased by +50
  energy (so a normal enemy needs 150 energy to move instead of 100,
  effectively 33% slower movement from the tile).
  This is a MOVEMENT penalty only, not a turn skip (unlike silver).
  Does not affect attack actions or ability costs.
  Player on tile — no effect.

Design rationale:
  Silver = hard CC (skip turn entirely, +50% melee vulnerability).
  Brown = soft CC (movement penalty only, no extra damage bonus).
  Brown is safer for area denial and doesn't punish the player if they
  walk into it accidentally. Ideal for corridor choke points in the
  Meth Lab. Pairs well with orange (slowed enemies take more ticks on
  the DoT tile before escaping).

Scale with Graffiti:
  Graffiti XP: +5 XP when an enemy dies on a brown tile (slow enough
  to die in it). This rule can share the existing "dies on any painted
  tile" XP logic — simply extend the check to include brown.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile brown."
  "Enemies on tile: +50 move cost"
  "(move takes 50% more energy)."
  "Does not affect attacks."

Implementation notes:
  Evaluated in engine.py during monster action resolution (_run_energy_loop
  or equivalent). When selecting action_type == "move", check if current
  tile is brown: if so, add 50 to the energy cost for the move (after
  normal cost is set).
  No new Effect class needed — pure energy-cost modifier applied inline.
  In the monster move-cost block in engine.py:
    if action_type == "move":
        cost = ENERGY_THRESHOLD  # base 100
        if spray_paint.get((monster.x, monster.y)) == "brown":
            cost += 50
        # Silver slip check here (existing)

PAINT 12 — LIME GREEN / TOXIC
============================
Item ID:    lime_spray_paint
Name:       Lime Spray Paint
RGB:        (100, 240, 50)
Render bg (visible):   (40, 120, 15)
Render bg (explored):  (20, 60, 8)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: meth_lab (primary), crack_den
Value: 40

Effect (Meth Lab themed — toxicity):
  Enemy entering or standing on tile: gains +8 toxicity per turn.
  Player on tile: gains +3 toxicity per turn (lesser exposure, still
  dangerous — don't linger).

Tox reminder: tox multiplies incoming damage by 1.0 + (tox/100)^0.6.
  At tox 50 that is 1.38x; at tox 100 it is 1.62x.
  Enemies generally have tox resistance 0, so lime rapidly poisons them.

Scale with Graffiti:
  At Graffiti L1+, the standard 50% no-consume chance applies.
  Suggested direct scaling: enemy tox rate = 6 + 2*Graffiti_Lvl per turn.
  At L0: +6 tox; L1: +8 tox; L2: +10 tox; L3: +12 tox.
  Player rate stays flat at +3 (self-damage doesn't scale with level).

Design rationale:
  Meth Lab exclusive pain tile. Unlike orange (flat damage), lime
  inflates the enemy's tox so that ALL future damage — including from
  weapons — is amplified. Lime + red = devastating: enemy takes +25%
  from all sources AND has tox multiplier stacking on top.
  The self-tox risk means the player can't camp it indefinitely.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile lime."
  "Entities on tile gain toxicity:"
  " Enemy: +(6+2*Graffiti) per turn"
  " Player: +3 per turn."

Implementation notes:
  In the per-tick entity loop (alongside orange damage loop):
    if spray_paint.get((entity.x, entity.y)) == "lime":
        if entity == player:
            entity.toxicity = min(entity.toxicity + 3, 200)
        else:  # monster
            rate = 6 + 2 * graffiti_lvl
            entity.toxicity = min(entity.toxicity + rate, 200)
  Uses existing entity.toxicity field (from Meth Lab zone).
  Note: tox on monsters in Crack Den zone may be a no-op unless the
  damage formula checks tox there too. Safe to spawn in both zones;
  just less impactful in Crack Den.

PAINT 13 — GREY / STATIC
============================
Item ID:    grey_spray_paint
Name:       Grey Spray Paint
RGB:        (160, 160, 160)
Render bg (visible):   (75, 75, 80)
Render bg (explored):  (38, 38, 40)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 40

Effect:
  Enemy on tile — -2 to all their effective attack damage (minimum 1).
  Applied as a flat reduction in combat.py _apply_tile_amp: if attacker
  (the monster) is on grey, reduce computed damage by 2 before
  dealing it to the player.
  Player on tile — no direct bonus.

Design rationale:
  Red amplifies damage dealt TO enemies. Grey reduces damage dealt BY
  enemies. Both are "stand on enemy's tile" paints, but grey is
  defensive: place it where enemies congregate in approach corridors.
  The -2 flat is significant at early floors (enemy dmg 4-8; -2 = 25-50%
  reduction), less so later (floor 4+ enemies hit for 12-20+). Scales
  inversely with danger rather than directly — strongest when most needed.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Suggested: reduction = 1 + Graffiti_Lvl. At L0: -1; L1: -2; L2: -3;
  L3: -4. Capped at reducing attacker's final damage to minimum 1.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile grey."
  "Enemies on tile: -(1+Graffiti)"
  "to their melee damage."
  "Strongest on low-level floors."

Implementation notes:
  In combat.py _apply_tile_amp (or a new function _apply_tile_attacker_mod
  called on monster-to-player attacks):
    attacker_spray = engine.dungeon.spray_paint.get((attacker.x, attacker.y))
    if attacker_spray == "grey":
        graffiti_lvl = engine.skills.get("Graffiti").level
        damage = max(1, damage - (1 + graffiti_lvl))
  Called inside the monster-attacks-player branch of perform_attack.

PAINT 15 — WHITE
============================
Item ID:    white_spray_paint
Name:       White Spray Paint
RGB:        (245, 245, 245)
Render bg (visible):   (120, 120, 120)
Render bg (explored):  (60, 60, 60)
Tool charges: 10
Zones: crack_den, meth_lab
Value: 45

Effect:
  Player on tile — ALL enemies within 3 tiles that have line-of-sight
  to the player are revealed with a one-tile positional ping per turn
  (shown as a brief message: "You sense movement to the NE").
  This is an INFORMATION tile: no damage, no stat change. The player
  learns enemy positions before they enter FOV.

  The "sense" is directional only — the player is told the general
  direction (8-way compass) but not the exact tile. Works for enemies
  outside normal sight range (e.g., in explored but not currently visible
  tiles). Does NOT reveal enemies through walls.

  Enemy on tile — no effect.

Scale with Graffiti:
  Detection radius = 2 + Graffiti_Lvl.
  L0: 2 tiles; L1: 3 tiles; L2: 4 tiles; L3: 5 tiles.

Design rationale:
  A unique utility tile with no combat numbers. White paint rewards
  careful, position-conscious play. Especially powerful in the Meth Lab
  where enemy factions move unpredictably. Does not directly improve
  combat math — a deliberate choice to keep it a tactical support tool.
  Synergy: use white to detect then silver to set up CC in their approach.

Examine popup lines:
  "Spray a tile white."
  "While standing here, sense nearby"
  "enemies within (2+Graffiti) tiles."
  "Directional only; not exact pos."

Implementation notes:
  Per-turn check in _update_tile_stat_bonuses or a new
  _update_tile_sense_bonuses pass:
    if spray == "white":
        radius = 2 + graffiti_lvl
        for monster in living monsters:
            dx = monster.x - player.x
            dy = monster.y - player.y
            dist = max(abs(dx), abs(dy))  # Chebyshev
            if dist <= radius and has_los(player, monster):
                direction = _compass_dir(dx, dy)
                messages.append(f"You sense movement to the {direction}.")
  Helper _compass_dir(dx, dy) returns string: N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW.
  Deduplicate messages: only report once per enemy per turn (use a set).
  This runs each turn after player acts.

COMBO SYNERGIES
============================

Lime + Red (Meth Lab):
  Enemy on red tile: +25% damage from all sources.
  Enemy on lime tile: rapidly accumulates tox, pushing damage multiplier
  up via 1.0 + (tox/100)^0.6.
  Can't be on both simultaneously (single tile), but sequence: place red
  at an ambush point, lime in the approach corridor. Enemy walks through
  lime, arrives at red with high tox. Result: massively amplified burst
  damage window.

Grey + Orange:
  Grey reduces enemy melee damage. Orange deals per-tick damage to all
  entities. Place grey in corridor, lure enemy onto grey to reduce
  counterattack, then chase them toward orange for cleanup. Works because
  grey discourages standing still (they take no extra damage there) while
  orange punishes staying (DoT).

White + Silver:
  White reveals approach direction. Silver trips enemies entering a
  specific tile. Setup: paint white on your position, silver on the
  choke point between you and the sensed enemy. You know they're coming
  from the NE, you've pre-slipped the NE corridor entrance. Enemy walks
  in blind — slipped immediately.

Brown + Lime (Meth Lab):
  Brown slows enemy movement. Lime toxifies them per turn. The longer
  they're slowed, the more tox they accumulate. Forces enemies to
  stand in lime longer than they would otherwise, dramatically increasing
  total tox delivered.

Gold + Street Art (L2):
  Kill an enemy on a gold tile with Graffiti L2 equipped:
  +$5 cash, +5 HP (enemy on painted tile), +5 HP if player also on
  painted tile. Potential triple payoff per kill. Encourages "boss
  killing field" setup — designate one tile as the execution spot.

BALANCE NOTES
============================

Power ranking (approximate, relative to existing 5):
  Tier 1 (strongest): Lime, Rad, Gold+L2, White+L3
  Tier 2 (strong):    Purple, Grey, Pink+tanky build
  Tier 3 (utility):   Yellow, Teal, Brown, White (base)

- Lime and Rad carry high self-damage risk; justify their power.
- Gold has zero combat benefit — pure economy; value scales with kills.
- White is the only pure-information tile; no direct combat math.
- Purple's 30% confusion rate means ~1-in-3 turns lost for enemy standing
  still; less brutal than silver (guaranteed skip).
- Brown's +50 move cost is soft: enemy still acts eventually, just slower.
- Pink's +15 max HP is large but non-regenerating; the player must keep
  HP topped up by other means to actually benefit from the buffer.
- Teal's XP multiplier stacks with xp_multiplier from items; could
  accelerate late-game skill spikes on a prepared build. Monitor.
- Grey scales inverted with floor depth (most useful early) — intentional.

Graffiti unlock perk awards one random paint from all 15:
  Adjust distribution if some paints are too strong as random grants.
  Consider: Rad and Gold paints should have lower weight in the unlock
  table (Rad is meth_lab themed; Gold is rare/economy), e.g.:
    _SPRAY_TABLE = [
        "red_spray_paint", "blue_spray_paint", "green_spray_paint",
        "orange_spray_paint", "silver_spray_paint",
        "yellow_spray_paint", "purple_spray_paint", "pink_spray_paint",
        "teal_spray_paint", "brown_spray_paint", "lime_spray_paint",
        "grey_spray_paint", "white_spray_paint",
        # Rarer grants:
        "gold_spray_paint", "rad_spray_paint",
    ]
  Equal weights in the table, but gold and rad listed once while others
  could be listed twice for double probability. Decide per balance needs.
