DECONTAMINATION SKILL — LEVEL 5 CONSECRATE UPGRADE OPTIONS
NIGRL Design Document
Generated: 2026-04-02
======================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
Design 6 perk options for Decontamination Level 5. This perk is a
DIRECT UPGRADE to Consecrate (L3). The passive component (+30 Max Armor)
is already decided. The creative work here is: what does Consecrate's
zone DO that is new, offensively powerful, and distinct from what the
tree already provides?

TREE STATE AT L5
----------------
  L1 — Radiant:       +30% rad resist, +2 TOL, +2 STR, +10 max armor
  L2 — Gamma Aura:    +3 STR. Toggle: enemies w/in 2 tiles gain +5 rad/turn.
                       2x Decon XP from resisted rad. Nearby irradiated
                       kills remove 25 player rad.
  L3 — Consecrate:    2/floor, 30t CD. Lay a 5x3 rad zone in a chosen
                       cardinal direction (20t). Enemies: +10 rad/turn +
                       true damage (2 + decon_level//2). Player standing
                       in zone: +1 Swagger/turn (cap 5). Low rad = chance
                       to preserve charge. +15% rad resist, +20 max armor.
  L4 — Ironsoul Aura: +2 SWG. Toggle: +1 DR per visible enemy (cap 5),
                       +2 FOV. Hits from visible enemies grant +10 rad.
                       25% on melee hit: deal damage as rad gain to self,
                       receive as armor.
  L5 — THIS DOCUMENT  (+30 max armor — passive decided)

WHAT CONSECRATE DOES NOW (BASELINE)
-------------------------------------
  - Zone shape: 5 tiles deep x 3 tiles wide (15 tiles max), player at
    near-edge center, chosen cardinal direction
  - Duration: 20 turns
  - Charges: 2/floor, 30t cooldown
  - Enemy on tile: +10 rad/turn + true damage (2 + decon_level//2)
  - Player on tile: +1 Swagger/turn (capped at 5 above base)
  - Charge preservation: if player rad < 30 when cast, 50% chance to
    preserve charge (the "holy ground" grace condition)
  - Passive line: +15% rad resist, +20 max armor

THINGS DECONTAMINATION DOES NOT CURRENTLY DO
---------------------------------------------
The following status effects exist in the game but have NEVER been applied
by ANY Decontamination perk. These are the design space for L5:
  - Shocked (damage amp: +50% damage taken from all sources)
  - Stunned (skip turn)
  - Feared (flee behavior)
  - Bleeding (DOT)
  - Ignite (fire DOT, stacking)
  - Greasy (slip chance, fire interaction)
  - Dazed (wander aimlessly)
  - Hamstrung (flat damage reduction per stack)

The L5 upgrade should introduce at least one of these. Radiation has so
far been the tree's only offensive vector. That changes here.

ZONE SIZE UPGRADE (AVAILABLE TO ALL OPTIONS)
-------------------------------------------
The user noted interest in growing 5x3 -> 7x5. That's 35 tiles vs 15 —
a 2.3x footprint increase. Any option may include this as part of the
upgrade. If an option makes the zone bigger, it is the PRIMARY offensive
buff and the new zone effect should be appropriately smaller. If the zone
stays 5x3, the new zone effect should be stronger.

Size change implementation:
  The consecrate_zone_size field can be stored in engine.consecrate_zone_size
  = (5, 3) by default; L5 upgrade sets it to (7, 5). OR: simply check
  skills.get("Decontamination").level >= 5 in _execute_consecrate() to
  choose which footprint to compute.

NOTE ON IMPLEMENTATION PATTERN
-------------------------------
Each option modifies _execute_consecrate() and the per-tick rad_tile
processing in engine._run_energy_loop. The rad_tiles dict stores:
  dungeon.rad_tiles: dict[tuple, tuple] = {(x,y): (turns_remaining, mode)}
where mode is a string key. The L5 upgrade changes which mode string
_execute_consecrate writes. The tick handler already dispatches on mode.

======================================================================
OPTION 1 — "NUCLEAR VERDICT"
Concept: Zone becomes a death sentence. Enemies inside build a delayed
         detonation — they carry the rad charge OUT of the zone if they
         flee, and it explodes when they hit full saturation.
======================================================================

NAME: Nuclear Verdict
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 7x5 (upgrade from 5x3)
NEW MODE STRING: "verdict"

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "verdict":
    - +10 rad/turn (unchanged from base)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW: When an enemy exits a Verdict zone tile (moves from a rad tile
      to a non-rad tile), they gain the "verdict_marked" status effect
      (8 turns). While marked:
        - Their radiation cannot decay naturally (rad_decay blocked;
          modify in the enemy's per-tick rad processing).
        - If they reach 200+ rad while marked, they DETONATE: deal
          (rad // 8) true damage to all entities within 2 tiles including
          themselves; their rad is then zeroed.
        - Flavor: "The radiation doesn't let them go."

  NEW STATUS EFFECT: VerdictMarkedEffect
    id="verdict_marked", category="debuff", duration=8
    apply(): message "[enemy] is marked for nuclear verdict!"
    tick(): if entity.radiation >= 200: trigger detonation (deal damage,
            zero radiation, expire effect).
    modify_energy_gain(): no modifier (enemy still moves normally; they
            just can't shake the charge).
    Implementation note: block rad decay by checking for this effect in
    the enemy's rad-decay step (currently in engine._run_energy_loop
    where enemy.radiation -= 5 or similar decay value). If the effect is
    active on that enemy, skip the decay for that tick.

PLAYER STANDING BONUS (unchanged from L3):
  +1 Swagger/turn while in zone (cap 5). No change here. The size
  increase from 7x5 means this bonus is easier to maintain during kiting.

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura pushes enemies deeper into rad buildup (5 rad/turn from
  the aura). Verdict zones then lock that rad in place when the enemy
  escapes. An enemy irradiated by Gamma Aura + Verdict zone cannot
  dump their charge — they're carrying a bomb.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul rewards standing in the thick of it (+1 DR per visible enemy).
  Verdict punishes enemies for leaving — so THEY come to YOU. Ironsoul
  + Verdict creates a zone where enemies keep walking into the consecrated
  ground because fleeing is almost worse.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  engine._run_energy_loop (rad_tile tick):
    - In the "verdict" mode branch: add_radiation(engine, enemy, 10),
      deal true damage (2 + decon_level//2). No new flag needed on the tile.
  engine._run_energy_loop (monster movement):
    - After each monster move: if monster's OLD position was in
      dungeon.rad_tiles and new position is NOT: apply VerdictMarkedEffect.
    - Efficient check: store monster's prior position in ai.py's do_ai_turn()
      return value, or in entity.last_move_pos (entity.py already has
      entity.last_move_dir per MEMORY.md). Use that to check old pos.
  effects.py:
    - VerdictMarkedEffect(Effect): id="verdict_marked". tick() handles
      detonation check. Also: block rad decay on entity by checking in
      the same loop where enemy rad decay is processed.
  Zone size in _execute_consecrate(): if decon_level >= 5, use 7x5.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: zone grows to 7x5. Enemies that exit the zone
   are Verdict Marked (8t) — their rad cannot decay and detonates
   (rad//8 AOE true dmg) if they hit 200 rad. +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  The 7x5 zone is the primary buff — more coverage. Verdict is a
  conditional bonus that rewards the full tree investment: you need
  enemies to build 200 rad (requires Emission + time in zone). It's
  more of a "panic button" for enemies that try to flee than a guaranteed
  proc. Against low-rad enemies it does nothing. Against Emission-primed
  enemies it creates terrifying chase scenarios.

OPTION 2 — "IRRADIATED GROUND, SHOCKED SOUL"
Concept: Consecrate now deals with electricity's side of exposure.
         Radiation + electrical discharge = the Shocked debuff. Enemies
         in the zone are taking 50% more damage from everything.
======================================================================

NAME: Sanctified Discharge
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 5x3 (NO size increase — the debuff is strong enough)
NEW MODE STRING: "discharge"

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "discharge":
    - +10 rad/turn (unchanged)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW: Every turn an enemy is on a discharge tile, they have a 30%
      chance to gain Shocked (3 turns). Shocked: +50% incoming damage
      from all sources for its duration.
    - The chance accumulates: an enemy standing still in the zone for
      3 turns expects to be Shocked ~66% of those turns on average.
    - Enemies that are ALREADY shocked and step on a discharge tile reset
      their Shocked duration to 3 (no stacking to 6+, cap at 3).
    - Flavor: "The rad zone crackles with divine electricity. The sinners
      standing in it conduct it perfectly."

PLAYER STANDING BONUS:
  +1 Swagger/turn (unchanged). ADDITIONAL new bonus: the player is immune
  to the Shocked proc from their own Discharge tiles (you are the conduit,
  not the target).

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura + Discharge: enemy in Gamma Aura range AND on a discharge
  tile: +5 rad/turn from aura + +10 rad/turn from tile + 30% chance/turn
  to be Shocked. When Shocked, the rad DOT (rad//50 dmg/tick) is also
  amplified 50% by the Shocked modifier. The zone becomes an amplified
  execution chamber.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul is defensive. This option is offensively minded. The natural
  play: Ironsoul ON while closing distance (getting DR), then lay
  Discharge Consecrate and swap to Gamma Aura for the kill phase.
  Or: keep Ironsoul on and let Discharge do the offensive lifting.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  engine._run_energy_loop (rad_tile tick):
    - In "discharge" mode: add_radiation(engine, enemy, 10), deal true
      damage. Then: roll random.random() < 0.30; if True and enemy not
      already shocked (check active effects): apply_effect(enemy, engine,
      "shocked", duration=3). If already shocked: find the effect and
      reset its duration to 3.
  Shocked effect already exists in effects.py (it modifies incoming
  damage). No new effect needed. Just call apply_effect with the
  existing "shocked" id.
  Immunity: in the discharge mode branch, check if entity is engine.player;
  if so, skip the Shocked roll.
  Zone size: unchanged at 5x3.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: mode changes to Discharge. Enemies on Discharge
   tiles have 30% chance/turn to gain Shocked (3t, +50% damage taken).
   You are immune to your own Discharge tiles. +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  At 30%/turn, an enemy standing in the zone for 3 turns expects 0-1
  Shocked procs. But with 10 rad/turn AND the rad DOT from reaching 50+
  rad, Shocked doesn't need to proc constantly — it's a massive spike when
  it does. Against high-DR enemies (which are the late-floor threat), the
  Shocked damage amplification is more valuable than raw damage. This is
  the "anti-armor" Consecrate variant.

  Risk of being too strong: if enemy AI tends to stand still in the zone
  (room_guard, stunned enemies, etc.), Shocked procs consistently. This
  is acceptable — you chose the zone placement, that's the reward.

OPTION 3 — "SCOURGING FLAME"
Concept: Holy fire. Consecrate now IGNITES enemies in the zone.
         Radiation + fire = the most aesthetically violent option.
         Burning sanctified ground. Enemies are both irradiated and on fire.
======================================================================

NAME: Scourging Flame
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 7x5 (upgrade from 5x3)
NEW MODE STRING: "scourge"

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "scourge":
    - +8 rad/turn (REDUCED from +10 — fire handles the rest)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW: Every 3 turns an enemy spends on a scourge tile, they gain
      Ignite (3 stacks, 8 turns). Ignite is an existing stacking fire DOT.
      Threshold: tracked per-enemy via a counter stored in engine or the
      enemy entity. Use entity.scourge_tick_count (int, default 0): ++
      each tick the entity is on a scourge tile; reset on leaving the tile;
      at multiples of 3: apply ignite 3 stacks.
    - The zone itself does NOT visually become fire (no fire hazard
      entities spawned). This is radiant combustion — divine heat that
      doesn't need visible flames.
    - At Decontamination level 7+ (future): scourge tile ignite threshold
      drops to every 2 turns (future-proofing hook, not needed now).

PLAYER STANDING BONUS:
  +1 Swagger/turn (unchanged). ADDITIONAL: player is immune to Ignite
  procs from their own Scourge tiles (you are the source of the fire,
  not a recipient). Player can still be on greasy tiles and have fire
  interact with grease — but the scourge tiles themselves don't apply
  Ignite to the player.

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura irradiates enemies within 2 tiles. Scourge tiles ignite
  them if they spend time in the zone. A kiting strategy: chase enemies
  through the zone while Gamma Aura chips their rad up, and every 3 turns
  they get an Ignite stack. With 3+ stacks of Ignite + rad DOT + Gamma
  Aura's field: multi-vector DOT on a single enemy.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul's 25% on-melee-hit proc (deal damage as rad to self, receive
  as armor) means you're building both rad and armor. Scourging Flame
  rewards standing in the zone (you get Swagger regen + fire the enemies
  below you). Using both: lay the zone, stand in it with Ironsoul on,
  melee enemies as they burn.

SYNERGY WITH GREASE TILES (terrain interaction):
  This is a unique cross-system interaction available ONLY to this option.
  If a scourge tile overlaps with a grease tile (entity type="hazard",
  hazard_type="greasy"), the grease tile should explode: deal 8 fire
  damage to all entities in a 2-tile radius and remove the grease tile.
  Implementation: in the scourge tile tick, check if dungeon.grease_tiles
  contains this position. If so: fire explosion and remove grease.
  This is a very cool emergent interaction with an existing system.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  entity.py: add scourge_tick_count: int = 0 (new field for scourge
    ignite tracking). Reset in _do_rad_tile_processing for entities
    leaving tiles (set to 0 when entity not on scourge tile).
  engine._run_energy_loop (rad_tile tick):
    - In "scourge" mode: add_radiation(engine, enemy, 8), deal true damage.
    - Increment entity.scourge_tick_count. If entity.scourge_tick_count % 3 == 0:
      apply_effect(enemy, engine, "ignite", stacks=3, duration=8).
    - Check grease overlap for fire explosion.
  effects.py: Ignite effect already exists. apply_effect with stacks=3.
  Zone size: 7x5. The bigger zone matters more for Scourge because Ignite
    takes 3 turns to first proc — enemies need to stay in zone longer.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: zone grows to 7x5. Zone becomes Scourge mode:
   every 3 turns on the zone, enemies gain Ignite (3 stacks, 8t). Scourge
   tiles detonate overlapping grease. +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  The rad gain drops from +10 to +8/turn — slightly slower rad buildup.
  But Ignite at 3 stacks is a significant DOT on top of that (Ignite
  typically does 3-5 damage/tick/stack in early definitions). The grease
  interaction is a bonus with no downside. Zone size increase means more
  tactical flexibility.

  Potentially high combo: Ignite + rad DOT (from Emission + rad buildup)
  + true damage from zone + Scourge damage = 4 damage vectors on one enemy.
  This is the most burst-oriented option. Against low-HP enemies on late
  floors it might feel like overkill, but Ignite's stacking means it scales
  into harder content.

OPTION 4 — "DIVINE TERROR"
Concept: The zone makes enemies afraid. Feared enemies flee into your
         next attack. A crowd-control Consecrate — enemies who enter the
         zone don't fight back, they run. You choose where they run TO.
======================================================================

NAME: Divine Terror
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 5x3 (NO size increase — control scales with zone width)
NEW MODE STRING: "terror"

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "terror":
    - +10 rad/turn (unchanged)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW: When an enemy FIRST enters a terror tile (transitions from
      non-terror tile to terror tile), they must pass a morale check:
        if enemy.hp <= (enemy.max_hp * 0.6):  # below 60% HP
          apply_effect(enemy, engine, "feared", duration=4)
      If above 60% HP: no fear. The zone is a finishing tool — enemies
      that are already wounded break and run.
    - The 60% threshold is intentional. Full-HP enemies do NOT fear the
      zone. You have to soften them first (with Gamma Aura rad, melee,
      or Emission). Then they step in and panic.
    - Feared enemies flee away from the player. The zone placement
      geometry then matters: if the zone is in front of you (toward the
      enemy), a feared enemy turns and runs THROUGH the zone to escape,
      taking more ticks of damage as they flee through the zone tiles.
    - This creates a chase geometry: you place the zone between you and
      the enemy corridor, enemies run into it, fear triggers at 60%, they
      flee through the remaining tiles, radiating and taking true damage
      as they go.

PLAYER STANDING BONUS:
  +1 Swagger/turn (unchanged). ADDITIONAL: when a Feared enemy takes
  melee damage from YOU (and only you), the fear duration is extended
  by 2 turns. This rewards chasing feared enemies rather than letting
  them flee to a wall and re-engage.

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura + Terror: gamma softens enemies (5 rad/turn), getting them
  to 60% HP faster via the rad DOT (rad//50). Then Terror zone finishes
  the psychology — they step in and break. The Gamma Aura handles the
  setup damage; Terror handles the execution behavior.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul rewards visible enemies (DR per visible). Feared enemies are
  still visible — they count toward Ironsoul's DR stacks while they run.
  Bonus: feared enemies run away, which means they're not attacking you
  (0 attacks on the player), while you still benefit from the DR bonus
  of their presence. Very high defensive upside during a fear proc.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  engine._run_energy_loop (rad_tile tick):
    - In "terror" mode: add_radiation(engine, enemy, 10), deal true dmg.
    - Track first entry: entity.on_terror_tile (bool). Set to True when
      entity steps onto a terror tile; set to False when not on terror tile.
      OR: only trigger on entry — check entity's last position. If last
      turn entity was NOT on a terror tile and this turn it IS: fear check.
      Use entity.last_pos_was_terror: bool = False (entity field).
    - Fear proc: if entity.last_pos_was_terror == False and current tile
      is terror tile: set entity.last_pos_was_terror = True; if entity.hp
      <= entity.max_hp * 0.6: apply_effect(entity, engine, "feared", duration=4).
    - Reset entity.last_pos_was_terror = False when entity leaves all terror tiles.
  combat.py (player melee):
    - After melee hit: if target has "feared" effect and attacker is player:
      find feared effect, extend duration by 2.
  entity.py: add last_pos_was_terror: bool = False.
  Note: Feared effect already exists in effects.py. No new effect class.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: Terror mode. Enemies below 60% HP that enter the
   zone are Feared (4t). Melee hits on Feared enemies extend fear by 2t.
   +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  The fear threshold at 60% means this only triggers after meaningful
  damage has been dealt. Against fresh full-HP enemies it's dead weight —
  that's the explicit design tradeoff. Against softened enemies it's
  devastating: a 4-turn fear on a late-floor enemy with 30% HP remaining
  means you chase them through their own zone and finish them safely.
  The "chase through zone" interaction is particularly strong against
  corridor maps: a 5x3 zone placed across a hallway becomes an inescapable
  gauntlet for fleeing enemies.

  Risk: might be frustrating if the player is trying to kill an enemy
  and it keeps running away. The Feared extension on melee hit partially
  addresses this — the faster you chase, the longer the fear extends,
  so you can "steer" feared enemies rather than losing them.

OPTION 5 — "CURSED EARTH"
Concept: The zone now also inflicts Bleeding on enemies. Radiation burns
         them from the inside; the bleeding drains them from the outside.
         Enemies exiting the zone leave a trail of blood tiles.
         Two DOTs, one zone. The ground is permanently hostile.
======================================================================

NAME: Cursed Earth
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 7x5 (upgrade from 5x3)
NEW MODE STRING: "cursed_earth"

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "cursed_earth":
    - +10 rad/turn (unchanged)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW: Every 4 turns an enemy spends continuously on cursed_earth tiles,
      they gain Bleeding (5 turns, 4 damage/turn). Tracked via a per-enemy
      counter (same pattern as scourge_tick_count above; use
      entity.cursed_earth_ticks: int = 0, reset on leaving tiles, proc
      at multiples of 4).
    - NEW: When an enemy EXITS a cursed_earth tile (moves off it), they
      leave a "blood_patch" hazard tile at their old position. Blood
      patch: existing hazard_type in the system (per MEMORY.md, this type
      was designed in ability-granting-batch2-single.txt). Applies bleeding
      2t to enemies standing on it. Duration 10 turns.
      This means: enemies fleeing the zone SMEAR BLOOD across the floor
      behind them. Other enemies following them step into blood patches.

  BLOOD TRAIL GEOMETRY:
    The 7x5 zone combined with exit bleeding means: enemies that walk
    into the zone and then flee leave up to 8-10 blood patch tiles behind
    them (one per tile they moved through). A corridor becomes a slaughterhouse.
    Blood patches are entity-based (hazard entities) — they stack with rad
    tiles (both affect the same tile simultaneously).

PLAYER STANDING BONUS:
  +1 Swagger/turn (unchanged). ADDITIONAL: player is immune to their
  own blood patch tiles (you are consecrated ground, not a target of
  your own earth). Player gains +2 Decontamination XP per turn they
  stand in a cursed_earth tile — the zone rewards staying inside it.

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura irradiates enemies. Cursed Earth makes them bleed when they
  enter and leave blood everywhere when they flee. Enemies under Gamma
  Aura taking the rad DOT hit 60% HP faster, which means they start
  fleeing sooner, which means more blood patches scattered across the floor.
  More patches = more bleeding applied to more enemies following behind.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul's triggered armor gain (+10 rad to self on being hit, 25%
  convert damage to rad/armor) means the player benefits from staying
  put in the Cursed Earth zone (Swagger regen from zone + armor building
  from Ironsoul). The blood patches from fleeing enemies slow pursuit
  of the player (other enemies step in blood and take DOT), giving the
  player more time to continue healing through Ironsoul's mechanics.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  entity.py: add cursed_earth_ticks: int = 0.
  engine._run_energy_loop (rad_tile tick):
    - In "cursed_earth" mode: add_radiation(engine, enemy, 10), deal true
      damage. Increment entity.cursed_earth_ticks. If ticks % 4 == 0:
      apply_effect(enemy, engine, "bleeding", duration=5, amount=4).
  engine._run_energy_loop (monster movement) OR ai.py (post-move hook):
    - After entity moves: if old position was a cursed_earth tile and new
      position is NOT: create_blood_patch(old_x, old_y, engine.dungeon).
      Blood patch creation: instantiate a hazard entity with
      hazard_type="blood_patch", place in dungeon.entities at that position.
      Use existing blood_patch hazard from hazards.py (already designed).
  effects.py: Bleeding effect already exists. No new effect needed.
  Blood patch: already exists as a hazard type per MEMORY.md / the
    ability-granting-batch2-single.txt design. If not yet implemented,
    implement as a minimal hazard: entity_type="hazard",
    hazard_type="blood_patch", char='.', color=(180, 0, 0). Process in
    engine._run_energy_loop alongside grease tiles: if enemy stands on it,
    apply_effect(enemy, engine, "bleeding", duration=2, amount=2).

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: zone grows to 7x5, mode becomes Cursed Earth.
   Enemies bleed (4t) every 4 turns on tiles. Exiting enemies leave
   blood patches at their old position. +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  Bleeding (4 damage/4 turns) stacks with the existing rad DOT from
  the zone's +10 rad/turn (enemies at 50 rad take rad//50=1 dmg/tick,
  which compounds). Bleed on exit is tactically interesting because
  it punishes both staying (Bleeding buildup) and leaving (blood tile).
  The choice for enemies: stand still and bleed more; or leave and
  seed the floor with hazards. Either way the player wins.

  The 7x5 zone means blood patches can cover a lot of floor. If a room
  has multiple enemies all entering and exiting the zone, the floor
  outside the zone gradually fills with blood patches. This self-extends
  the zone's effective hazard range beyond its borders.

OPTION 6 — "JUDGMENT STRIKE"
Concept: Consecrate becomes a trigger. Enemies who take enough rad damage
         while in the zone are EXECUTED by a bolt of radiant energy — an
         instant-kill at a HP threshold. No DOT needed; just threshold.
         This makes Consecrate feel like holy judgment rather than slow death.
======================================================================

NAME: Judgment Strike
ZONE SIZE CHANGE: 5x3 (NO size increase — execution doesn't need big zones)
NEW MODE STRING: "judgment"
NOTE: This shares a name with the Consecrate ability variant in
  consecrate-ability-designs.txt (Design 5 "Judgment"). That document
  described a concept that became unused; this L5 perk is an upgrade to
  the IMPLEMENTED Consecrate, not a variant. The mechanic below is
  distinct from that design doc's version.

OFFENSIVE EFFECT (ENEMIES IN ZONE):
  Per-tick behavior in mode "judgment":
    - +10 rad/turn (unchanged)
    - True damage (2 + decon_level//2) per turn (unchanged)
    - NEW "JUDGMENT THRESHOLD": Each turn an enemy is in the zone, check:
        if enemy.radiation >= 150 and enemy.hp <= (enemy.max_hp * 0.35):
          EXECUTE: deal 9999 damage (guaranteed kill). Display:
          "JUDGMENT — [enemy_name] is struck down!"
          The execution emits a radiant flash: a bright yellow "!" is
          briefly displayed at the enemy's position via the floating text
          system (same as floating damage numbers). Enemy dies.
    - The double condition (150+ rad AND below 35% HP) means Judgment
      doesn't fire on fresh enemies or enemies with high HP remaining.
      You must have been running the zone (rad buildup) AND dealt
      meaningful prior damage (melee, abilities, other zone effects).
    - The threshold of 35% HP is intentional — this is not a one-tap
      ability. It's a finishing blow that eliminates the "grinding down
      a near-dead enemy while they chip away at you" problem.
    - "Execution immunity": bosses (entity.is_boss == True) are immune
      to the execute. They instead take (enemy.radiation // 12) true
      damage as a pseudo-execute proc that does NOT kill but still hurts.

PLAYER STANDING BONUS:
  +1 Swagger/turn (unchanged). ADDITIONAL: each Judgment execution
  restores 15 player HP (the "judge takes power from the condemned").
  This is NOT a regen mechanic — it's a burst heal on kill, like a
  vampiric proc. Only procs on executions, not on natural zone deaths.

SYNERGY WITH GAMMA AURA:
  Gamma Aura is the fastest way to push enemy rad above 150. At 5 rad/turn
  from the aura + 10 rad/turn from the zone = 15 rad/turn for an enemy
  standing in both the zone AND within 2 tiles. That's 150 rad in 10 turns.
  Meanwhile you're dealing rad DOT + true damage. The target hits 35% HP
  and Judgment fires. Gamma Aura is the natural partner for this option.

SYNERGY WITH IRONSOUL AURA:
  Ironsoul's hit-from-visible-enemy -> +10 rad to self means you're
  building your OWN rad while enemies build in the zone. With Ironsoul
  active, your rad can fuel Consecrate's next cast (Consecrate's "low
  rad = preserve charge" mechanic). In a weird way, Ironsoul makes
  you fight harder and more aggressively inside your own zone, which is
  exactly where Judgment is triggered.
  Also: +HP on Judgment proc means Ironsoul's damage-to-armor cycle
  and Judgment's kill-to-heal cycle combine for sustained fighting in
  the zone.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Consecrate upgrade: Judgment mode. Enemies at 150+ rad and below 35%
   HP in the zone are instantly executed. Each Judgment: +15 HP to you.
   Bosses take rad//12 true damage instead. +30 max armor."

BALANCE:
  The double condition is the limiter. This option is hardest to trigger
  but most satisfying when it fires. Against early-floor enemies with
  lower HP pools, the 35% threshold is reached faster (35% of 40 HP =
  14 HP — standard enemies reach this quickly). Against late-floor enemies
  with 120+ HP, you need to have dealt 80+ HP before Judgment fires.
  The 150 rad requirement means you need the zone up for 15 turns OR
  Gamma Aura + zone for 10 turns. It's a setup-intensive option.

  Reward is highest when it works: instant kills, no grinding, +15 HP
  per execution on a floor with 8 enemies = 120 potential HP restored.
  That's substantial sustain. This is the "big plays" option — unreliable
  against a single strong enemy, devastating against a room full of
  weakened clustered enemies.

  The boss interaction prevents trivializing boss fights with instant kills
  while still making Judgment feel impactful in boss encounters.


======================================================================
CROSS-OPTION COMPARISON
======================================================================

Option 1 — Nuclear Verdict (7x5, exit detonation)
  - Offensive style: Delayed bomb; enemies can't escape their rad charge
  - Complexity: Medium (new effect class, enemy exit detection)
  - Payoff: High variance — rare but explosive
  - Best with: Emission (pre-load enemy rad before entering zone)
  - Flavor fit: "The irradiation follows them"

Option 2 — Sanctified Discharge (5x3, Shocked debuff)
  - Offensive style: Damage amplification; everything hits harder
  - Complexity: Low (Shocked already exists, 30% roll per tick)
  - Payoff: Reliable but not flashy; every source of damage benefits
  - Best with: Gamma Aura (aura rad + shock = amplified rad DOT)
  - Flavor fit: "The zone makes enemies vulnerable to all punishment"

Option 3 — Scourging Flame (7x5, Ignite stacks + grease interaction)
  - Offensive style: Multi-DOT; fire + rad + true damage
  - Complexity: Medium (tick counter, grease cross-interaction)
  - Payoff: Best sustained DPS; 3 damage vectors
  - Best with: Grease tiles (emergent interaction) + melee combat
  - Flavor fit: "Divine fire consumes the wicked"

Option 4 — Divine Terror (5x3, Fear on wounded enemies)
  - Offensive style: Crowd control into follow-up hits
  - Complexity: Medium (entry detection, fear extension on melee)
  - Payoff: Safest to use; Feared enemies stop attacking
  - Best with: Ironsoul Aura (DR while feared enemies count toward cap)
  - Flavor fit: "Broken spirit, then broken body"

Option 5 — Cursed Earth (7x5, Bleeding + blood patches on exit)
  - Offensive style: Hazard seeding; zone extends beyond its borders
  - Complexity: High (exit blood patches, cursed_earth_ticks counter)
  - Payoff: Best at room control; floor fills with hazards over time
  - Best with: Gamma Aura (force enemies to flee and seed blood patches)
  - Flavor fit: "The earth remembers every step of the condemned"

Option 6 — Judgment Strike (5x3, execution threshold + HP restore)
  - Offensive style: Instant kill + sustain; finisher mechanic
  - Complexity: Low-Medium (threshold check, floating text, HP restore)
  - Payoff: Highest highs, lowest lows; requires setup
  - Best with: Gamma Aura (fastest to 150 rad) + anything that damages
  - Flavor fit: "The tribunal renders its verdict"

======================================================================
RECOMMENDED CHOICE BASED ON DESIGN BRIEF
======================================================================

User asked for OFFENSIVE focus, variety, unused status effects, and a
"feels powerful" upgrade. Ranking by how well each meets those criteria:

  BEST: Option 6 (Judgment Strike) — nothing in NIGRL currently instant-
    kills based on a threshold condition. The JUDGMENT! floating text
    moment is the highest-impact visual and mechanical fantasy.

  SECOND: Option 3 (Scourging Flame) — Ignite is unused by Decon; the
    grease interaction is genuinely emergent; the 7x5 zone is visually
    satisfying; multi-DOT is reliable and rewarding.

  THIRD: Option 2 (Sanctified Discharge) — Simplest to implement,
    Shocked is unused by Decon, the damage amplification is immediately
    felt. Lacks visual drama but mechanically tight.

  The other three (Verdict, Terror, Cursed Earth) are strong but either
  more niche (Terror requires wounded enemies), more complex (Cursed Earth
  blood trails), or high-variance in payoff (Verdict detonation).
