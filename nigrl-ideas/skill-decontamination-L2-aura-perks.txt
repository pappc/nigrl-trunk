DECONTAMINATION L2 — AURA PERK OPTIONS (replaces Emission)
=============================================================
NIGRL Design Document
Generated: 2026-04-02

PURPOSE
-------
Replace the current L2 perk "Emission" (set visible enemy rad to player rad, 1/floor) with a
toggleable persistent aura perk. The aura must fit the Decontamination "paladin of purification"
fantasy, interact with the radiation system in a meaningful way, and create interesting tactical
decisions around when to keep it active. Each option includes a small always-on passive bonus
that introduces a genuinely new mechanical interaction.

REFERENCE POINTS
----------------
- L1 Radiant: +30% rad resist, +2 TOL, +2 STR (strong stat foundation)
- L3 Consecrate: 2/floor zone ability, 30t CD, 5x3 rad tiles, +15% rad resist
- L2 must sit between these in power. Not as passive-dominant as L1, not as situational as L3.
- Bastion (WP L2) is the only existing toggle. Do NOT replicate its cost model (charge + tox cost
  to toggle ON) or its effect type (flat damage taken/dealt multipliers).
- Unstable (Mutation L2) already does on-hit irradiation. Do not replicate.
- No passive HP regen (banned per design rules).
- Enemy rad tick damage formula: rad // 50 damage/tick, decay 5 rad/tick.

TARGET DESIGN FEEL
------------------
Aura: an ambient field around the player with a per-tick presence. Enemies feel the paladin
approaching. Toggle decisions should involve reading the room — is the cost worth it right now?
Passive bonus: a small mechanical "always-on" that rewards Decontamination playstyle even
without the aura active.


===========================================================================
OPTION 1: "HALF-LIFE FIELD"
Concept: Aura that accelerates enemy radiation decay — but channels that decay as bonus damage.
===========================================================================

NAME: Half-Life Field
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura that doubles irradiated enemy rad decay, converting half the stripped rad
into true damage.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Radius: 4 tiles (Chebyshev / square radius to match existing FOV patterns)
  - Per-tick: Each living enemy within 4 tiles that has radiation > 0 loses an additional 5 rad
    beyond normal decay (total 10 rad decay instead of 5). Half the additional 5 (i.e., 2-3 dmg
    via round(additional_stripped / 2)) is applied as true damage (bypasses defense).
  - Note: enemies at 0 rad are completely unaffected. Aura has zero cost against non-irradiated
    rooms.
  - True damage is NOT blocked by armor or swagger defence. It DOES respect enemy death checks.
  - The player does NOT gain Decontamination XP from this forced decay (only from their own rad
    removed/resisted). No XP gaming.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: free to activate, but drains 3 rad/tick from the player (pierce_resistance=True,
    so no rad resist applies to your own cost). If player rad reaches 0 while the aura is ON, the
    aura auto-shuts off with a message.
  - Toggle OFF: free. Drain stops immediately.
  - No floor charges. Can be toggled freely at any time.
  - Decision logic: want the aura? You must carry rad on your body as fuel. The Skywalker OG
    strain cycle (smoke → high rad → Force Sensitive → remove rad for STR) gets a natural third
    axis: use the aura during the "high rad" phase before purging.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on, regardless of toggle state):
  - "Rad Reading": When an enemy in your FOV hits 50, 100, 200 rad thresholds, a brief
    message notification appears: "[Enemy] is critically irradiated!" This is a UI/information
    benefit. It lets the Decontamination player track when enemies are about to die from rad ticks
    without needing to examine each one. No damage component, purely informational. Implemented as
    a flag check in the tick loop comparing before/after entity.radiation thresholds.
  - Additionally: +5% to Decontamination XP gain rate (implemented via briskness-style multiplier
    on gain_potential_exp calls for this skill only — a new engine.decon_xp_mult float field, or
    inline the check in combat.py's gain_potential_exp calls).

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - Introduces "rad decay acceleration" as a new damage vector — a per-tick AoE that scales with
    how irradiated enemies already are, not with the player's stats. No current effect does this.
  - Creates a "ride the rad wave" decision: let enemies absorb radiation (from any source), then
    toggle on the field to accelerate their death while it costs you rad fuel.
  - Passive threshold notifications are a pure QoL mechanic with zero equivalent in the game.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist means the 3 rad/tick self-cost is 2.1 after resistance. So L1
    makes the aura cheaper to run — rewards investment.
  - Consecrate (L3): Place a rad zone, enemies soak 10 rad/turn, then Half-Life Field strips it
    faster. Strong combo — enemies die to rad faster while you stand in the zone's swagger bonus.
  - Force Sensitive (Skywalker OG): Cycle rad up for STR, use aura during the "burning it down"
    phase by letting enemies stand in rad while you have rad to spend as fuel.
  - Emission (displaced): The displaced ability set all visible enemy rad to yours. Half-Life Field
    no longer needs that because the aura itself feeds off accumulated enemy rad.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: HalfLifeFieldEffect (id="half_life_field", floor_duration=True)
  - tick() method: iterate engine.dungeon.get_monsters(), check distance <= 4, check
    entity.radiation > 0, call remove_radiation(engine, entity, 5) manually (NOT via the public
    function since we don't want XP), then deal true damage via a direct entity.hp -= damage
    check + death check. Then drain 3 from engine.player.radiation directly (not via
    add/remove_radiation to avoid XP noise). Check if player.radiation <= 0: auto-expire.
  - Toggle in ability execute: check for existing half_life_field effect → remove it (toggle OFF),
    else apply_effect(player, engine, "half_life_field").
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE (no charge limit), target_type=TargetType.SELF,
    tags={"radiation", "aura", "toggle", "active"}.
  - Passive bonus XP boost: add a check in combat.py remove_radiation() for the Decontamination
    XP gain line: multiply by 1.05 if player has Decon L2+.
  - Threshold notification: in the monster tick loop in engine.py (after tick_all_effects for
    monsters), compare monster.radiation before/after and fire a message if it crossed 50/100/200.

PERK TEXT (for skills.py):
  "Toggle aura (free ON/OFF, costs 3 rad/tick while active): enemies within 4 tiles lose double
  the normal radiation each turn; each extra rad stripped deals 1 true damage. Auto-shuts off
  if you hit 0 rad. +5% Decontamination XP gain."

BALANCE:
  - Against 1 enemy at 100 rad: 2-3 true dmg/tick extra. Modest.
  - Against 3 enemies at 150 rad: 6-9 true dmg/tick. Noticeable but not dominant (they're
    already taking rad//50 = 3 dmg/tick each from base rad).
  - Player cost: 3 rad/tick. At 30% resist (Radiant), effective 2.1/tick. A player at 150 rad
    can sustain ~50 turns of aura. That's roughly one combat encounter.
  - The aura does NOTHING in rooms with zero rad enemies, keeping it zero-risk to toggle off.


===========================================================================
OPTION 2: "PURIFICATION CORONA"
Concept: Aura that heals the player for rad resisted — but only while actively taking rad hits.
===========================================================================

NAME: Purification Corona
ONE-LINE: Toggleable aura that converts radiation you resist into temporary dodge charges, plus
a passive that lets you "bank" a small amount of rad resistance overhead as a damage shield.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Radius: self (affects only the player, not enemies)
  - Mechanic: A new hook point is added: whenever the player would gain radiation and resists any
    amount (i.e., resisted > 0 in add_radiation), the resisted amount is compared. Every 10 rad
    resisted while the aura is ON generates 1 "purification charge" (stored on the effect, capped
    at 5 stacks). On the next attack that would hit the player, 1 stack is consumed to auto-dodge
    that hit entirely (dodge, message "[Corona] Purified!", charge removed).
  - This is a purely defensive aura: it converts rad resistance into emergency dodge charges.
  - Unlike flat dodge chance (which applies to all attacks), Corona dodge charges are spent
    sequentially — you can bank up to 5 attacks to auto-dodge, but only if you've been soaking
    rad hits to earn them.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: costs 15 tox (pierce_resistance=True). No per-tick cost.
  - Toggle OFF: free.
  - No floor charges. Toggle freely, but each activation costs 15 tox.
  - Decision logic: in tox-heavy zones this is a real sacrifice. In Meth Lab where tox builds
    naturally (Chemical Warfare, Swamp Gas strain), the cost is almost built-in. The player must
    weigh: "am I in a rad-heavy area where I'll earn charges quickly? Or is the 15 tox cost too
    steep for zero rad gain?"

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Radiation Bloom": When the player is hit by a melee attack while their radiation is >= 100,
    there is a 25% chance the attacking enemy gains 10 rad. This is a passive contact irradiation
    effect that triggers off being hit, not off the player attacking. It creates a minor "don't
    touch me" aura even without the toggle active.
  - Implementation: a hook in combat.py's player_take_damage or the existing
    on_player_melee_hit framework — check player.radiation >= 100, roll 25%, call
    add_radiation(engine, attacker, 10).
  - NOTE: this is thematically distinct from Unstable (which procs on the player's own attack).
    Bloom procs when the player IS attacked. Different trigger, different gameplay.

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - "Banked dodge charges earned by absorbing radiation" is a completely new mechanic. Dodge
    exists (flat % chance), but earning it through a resource loop does not.
  - The Radiation Bloom passive is the inverse of Unstable: reactive contamination (you touch me,
    you get rad), not proactive (I hit you, you get rad).
  - Toggle cost (tox) creates cross-system tension with White Power / Chemical Warfare builds.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist means MORE rad is resisted per hit, meaning more charges
    generated per rad attack. High rad resistance amplifies the aura's charge generation.
  - Force Sensitive: keeping rad up to 100+ for Bloom to proc also fuels Force Sensitive STR
    stacks. Twin incentives for rad-positive builds.
  - White Power Bastion: Bastion already costs tox to toggle. Running both means tox expenditure
    is significant — but both skills are on different characters by design. Low overlap.
  - Consecrate (L3): Stand in your own rad zone → earn Corona charges from self-irradiation.
    Each 10 rad you absorb from the zone = 1 dodge charge. Excellent L2/L3 synergy.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: PurificationCoronaEffect (id="purification_corona", floor_duration=True)
    Fields: charges: int = 0 (capped at 5)
  - Hook: modify_incoming_damage() can be used to intercept attacks. If charges > 0, consume 1,
    return 0 (full dodge), append dodge message. This intercepts melee AND gun hits.
  - Charge generation: add a check in combat.py add_radiation() — after computing resisted —
    for the "purification_corona" effect on the player. If found and resisted > 0:
    effect.charges = min(5, effect.charges + resisted // 10)
  - Toggle ability execute: check existing corona effect → remove (OFF) vs apply + add 15 tox.
  - Radiation Bloom: add check in the player-is-hit path in combat.py. Not in effect system —
    it's a passive that's always-on when unlocked (check Decon level >= 2 inline).
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE, target_type=TargetType.SELF.
  - Display name: "Purification Corona (X charges)" where X is current stack count.

PERK TEXT:
  "Toggle aura (costs 15 tox to activate, free to deactivate): every 10 rad you resist earns
  1 dodge charge (max 5); charges are consumed to auto-dodge incoming hits. Passive: when hit
  at 100+ rad, 25% chance to inflict 10 rad on the attacker."

BALANCE:
  - Dodge charge rate: at +30% rad resist (Radiant), a 30-rad attack gives 9 resisted = 0
    charges; a 50-rad attack gives 15 resisted = 1 charge. So against moderate rad sources,
    it takes 2-3 rad attacks to earn 1 dodge charge. Not trivially spammable.
  - 5-charge cap prevents infinite banking. Each charge is one free hit avoidance.
  - 15 tox cost: at Meth Lab tox pressures this is meaningful but manageable. Keeps toggle
    non-trivial.

OPTION 3: "SANCTIFIED GROUND"
Concept: Aura that imprints low-level radiation onto tiles the player walks over, creating
a persistent trail of consecrated earth. Standing enemies on these tiles take minor rad damage
per turn and can't naturally decay rad while on them.
===========================================================================

NAME: Sanctified Ground
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura that leaves consecrated rad tiles as you walk — enemies on your trail can't
decay radiation and take extra rad damage per turn.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Each tile the player steps off of (i.e., each tile the player previously occupied) becomes a
    "sanctified tile" for 8 turns. This uses the existing dungeon.rad_tiles dict
    (dungeon.rad_tiles[(x,y)] = (8, "sanctified")).
  - Sanctified tiles are rendered as '.' in a faint green-yellow (existing rad tile color), with
    a distinct mode string separating them from Consecrate zones.
  - Effect on enemies standing on a sanctified tile each turn: +3 rad (bypasses enemy rad
    resistance — minor amount, consecrated punishment), and their rad CANNOT naturally decay
    that turn (their 5 rad/tick normal decay is suppressed while on the tile).
  - The trail extends naturally as the player moves, creating a geometric pattern based on the
    player's path. Standing still doesn't create new tiles. Moving through a corridor creates a
    glowing trail.
  - Tiles expire after 8 turns, so the player can "kite" enemies through fresh trail.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: 5 rad/turn player drain (pierce_resistance=True). If player rad hits 0, aura
    auto-shuts off.
  - Toggle OFF: free. Existing tiles remain until they naturally expire (8t), but no new tiles
    are placed.
  - Decision logic: toggling ON only matters when you're moving. Standing still pays the 5 rad/t
    cost for zero tile generation. Reward is tied to active movement — paladin on the march.
    Also: you might want to toggle OFF when retreating so the enemy can't follow your trail and
    de-rad themselves by stepping off.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Trail Sense": The player always knows how many sanctified tiles are currently active in the
    dungeon (shown in a UI element or message on toggle). Additionally, the player takes 10%
    less damage from any enemy currently standing on a rad tile (sanctified or Consecrate-made).
    Mechanically: in combat.py player_take_damage, check if attacker is standing on a rad_tile —
    if yes, reduce incoming by 10%. Thematically: irradiated enemies are weaker, their attacks
    less focused.

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - Trail-placement from movement is a completely new mechanic in NIGRL. No other skill creates
    persistent environmental tiles based on player movement.
  - The "can't decay" suppression on sanctified tiles is a new rad-manipulation mechanic — the
    enemy's natural rad burn is paused, locking them into rad damage longer.
  - Synergizes with kiting, corridors, and room chokepoints in a way no other effect does.
  - Creates visible tactical maps: the player leaves a glowing trail that enemies hesitate to
    pursue into (mechanically they still pursue, but they suffer for it).

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Consecrate (L3): Consecrate lays large 5x3 zones. Sanctified Ground lays narrow trail tiles.
    Combined, the player has both area denial (Consecrate) and path-based harassment (trail).
    Crucially the tile types are DIFFERENT ("consecrate" vs "sanctified") so their rules are
    independent. L2 trail doesn't overlap mechanically with L3 zone.
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist means the 5 rad/tick self-cost costs effectively 3.5/tick.
    Sustained movement through corridors becomes viable with high resist.
  - Force Sensitive: being at high rad (to fuel the aura) while also purging rad for STR creates
    the classic Decon cycle. Trail tiles slow enemy rad decay, letting you purge your own rad
    for STR while enemies are stuck in their rad.
  - Skywalker OG: enemies irradiated by trail take longer to de-rad, giving more windows for the
    player to smoke Skywalker and trigger Force Sensitive before enemies self-clean.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - Toggle execute: apply/remove a SanctifiedAuraEffect (id="sanctified_aura", floor_duration=True)
  - In SanctifiedAuraEffect.tick(entity, engine): check player.radiation <= 0 → auto-expire.
    Drain 5 from player.radiation directly (not via remove_radiation to avoid XP/STR stacking).
    No tile placement in tick — placement happens on movement.
  - Tile placement: in engine.py handle_move() (or equivalent), AFTER the player moves but BEFORE
    the energy loop, check if the sanctified_aura effect is active. If yes, add the OLD position
    (x_prev, y_prev) to dungeon.rad_tiles[(x_prev, y_prev)] = (8, "sanctified") only if that
    tile is a floor tile and not already in rad_tiles.
  - Per-turn sanctified tile effect: in engine.py _run_energy_loop monster processing, for each
    monster acting, check if its position is in dungeon.rad_tiles with mode "sanctified":
      - add_radiation(engine, monster, 3, pierce_resistance=True)
      - Suppress natural rad decay for that monster this tick (flag or inline skip).
    Natural rad decay for monsters: currently happens in how? Check effects.py — it may be
    in engine.py monster tick. The suppression would be: if (monster.x, monster.y) in
    dungeon.rad_tiles, skip the 5 rad decay step for that monster. Needs a boolean check around
    the existing decay.
  - Passive "10% less dmg from rad-tile enemies": in combat.py, player_take_damage or
    melee_attack function, check if engine.dungeon.rad_tiles.get((attacker.x, attacker.y))
    and Decon level >= 2 → apply 10% reduction (max(1, int(damage * 0.90))).
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE, target_type=TargetType.SELF, tags={"aura","toggle","radiation","active"}.

PERK TEXT:
  "Toggle (free ON/OFF, costs 5 rad/tick): each tile you step off becomes sanctified (8 turns)
  — enemies on sanctified tiles can't decay radiation and gain 3 rad/turn. Passive: take 10%
  less damage from enemies standing on any rad tile."

BALANCE:
  - 3 rad/turn per tile is minor per enemy, but the no-decay rider is meaningful: an enemy at
    100 rad normally burns off 5/tick (takes ~20 ticks to clear). Sanctified traps them at 100+
    for those 8 turns, guaranteeing the rad//50 = 2 damage/tick fires consistently.
  - Trail density depends on player movement patterns. In open rooms: sparse. In corridors: dense.
    Rewards tactical routing.
  - Passive 10% reduction is small but always relevant in Meth Lab rad-heavy encounters.


===========================================================================
OPTION 4: "RADIANT JUDGMENT"
Concept: Aura that converts the player's excess rad resistance (beyond what they're actually
using) into bonus outgoing melee damage. Decontamination as a strength-multiplier via purity.
===========================================================================

NAME: Radiant Judgment
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura: your unused rad resistance converts to a damage bonus; enemies that die
while you have the aura active cannot receive natural radiation decay on their corpse turn.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Each tick while active: bonus melee damage = floor(effective_rad_resistance / 10).
    At 30% resist (Radiant): +3 melee damage.
    At 45% resist (Radiant + items): +4-5 melee damage.
    At 60% resist: +6 melee damage.
    Implemented by setting player_stats.melee_damage_bonus += value each tick in apply() and
    tracking the delta for clean removal in expire().
  - Additionally: when an enemy dies while the aura is active, that enemy's corpse is "judged"
    — it emits a one-time pulse of 15 rad to all enemies within 2 tiles of the death location.
    This is a death-triggered aoe irradiation. No damage to the player.
  - The death pulse is thematic: the judged fall and their corruption spreads.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: each tick the aura is active, the player gains 5 rad (pierce_resistance=True —
    purity comes at a cost, you absorb the corruption you're redirecting). If player rad hits 500
    (cap), the aura auto-shuts off with a message: "Overwhelmed! Radiant Judgment extinguished."
  - Toggle OFF: free. The rad gain stops immediately. The melee bonus is removed.
  - Decision logic: aura generates rad as a COST. This creates a ceiling: in long fights, the
    player will be building toward the Skywalker OG purge window or approaching mutation territory.
    Toggle it on in a fight, toggle it off before mutations trigger. Or don't and embrace chaos.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Stainless": Once per floor, when the player would take enough damage to bring them below
    25% HP in a single hit, that hit is reduced to exactly leave them at 25% HP instead (minimum
    1 HP above 25% floor). This is a death-prevention mercy mechanic that fires at most once.
    Thematically: the paladin's purity shields them from a killing blow.
    Implementation: engine.player_stats.judgment_stainless_used: bool (reset on floor change).
    In combat.py player_take_damage: check if not stainless_used, if damage would drop HP below
    25%, clamp damage to (player.hp - max(1, player.max_hp//4) - 1), set stainless_used = True.
    Message: "Stainless! A blow that would have felled you was turned aside."

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - "Rad resistance as a stat that converts to offense" is a new design axis. Currently rad
    resistance is purely defensive. This perk makes stacking rad resistance actively good for
    melee damage, creating an incentive to pursue rad-resistant gear/food that normally has no
    offensive value.
  - Death-pulse irradiation is distinct from Emission (player-initiated, all visible enemies)
    and from Unstable (on-hit irradiation). Death-pulse is passive and spreads from the fallen.
  - "Stainless" one-time death mitigation is new — no current perk or effect does this. It's
    intentionally once-per-floor to avoid abusability (no permanent death avoidance loop).

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist = +3 melee damage base from the aura. Investing in rad resist
    items and food directly amplifies this perk's offense.
  - Consecrate (L3): Enemies in Consecrate zones take 10 rad/turn. When they die (faster),
    the death pulse fires to nearby enemies. Chain-irradiation through densely packed rooms.
  - Force Sensitive / Skywalker OG: Aura charges your own rad (5/tick). Smoke Skywalker right
    after a fight to cash in the accumulated rad for STR stacks. The timing window is clear.
  - White Power Bastion: Bastion reduces damage dealt — Radiant Judgment increases melee damage.
    They're thematically opposed and shouldn't be run simultaneously (WP and Decon share the
    Meth Lab fantasy but are different build directions).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: RadiantJudgmentEffect (id="radiant_judgment", floor_duration=True)
    Fields: active_melee_bonus: int = 0
  - apply(): compute bonus = player_stats.total_rad_resistance // 10, set active_melee_bonus,
    player_stats.melee_damage_bonus += active_melee_bonus
  - tick(): re-compute bonus each turn (in case resist changes), adjust melee_damage_bonus by
    the delta. Then add_radiation(engine, entity, 5, pierce_resistance=True). Check if
    entity.radiation >= 500: set self.expired = True (auto-shutoff).
  - expire(): player_stats.melee_damage_bonus -= active_melee_bonus. Clamp to 0 if needed.
  - Death pulse: hook into the entity_died event OR check inside combat.py death handling.
    When an enemy dies and player has radiant_judgment active: for each monster within 2 tiles
    of dead entity's (x, y), add_radiation(engine, neighbor, 15).
  - Stainless passive: new field engine.player_stats.judgment_stainless_used (bool). Reset in
    engine floor transition. Check in combat.py melee/gun damage path.
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE, target_type=TargetType.SELF.

PERK TEXT:
  "Toggle (free ON/OFF, gains 5 rad/tick while active): your rad resistance becomes melee power
  (+1 damage per 10% resist). Enemies that die while active pulse 15 rad to nearby foes.
  Passive: once per floor, a hit that would drop you below 25% HP is reduced to spare you."

BALANCE:
  - At Radiant L1 (30% resist): +3 melee dmg. Solid for L2 but not overwhelming.
  - At high resist (60%): +6 melee dmg. Meaningful in Meth Lab but still capped by the
    rad accumulation cost — can't run this forever.
  - 5 rad/tick cost: from 0 to 500 = 100 turns of aura max (without resist to reduce). At 30%
    resist the player's own rad gain from combat is offset somewhat. Long fights are risky.
  - Death pulse 15 rad to 2-tile radius: against small clusters, good chain irradiation.
    Against scattered enemies, negligible. Situational power spike.


===========================================================================
OPTION 5: "IRONSOUL AURA"
Concept: Aura that makes the player temporarily immune to SMALL rad hits by venting them as
an outward pulse — but deliberately allows large rad hits through. Paladin as a filter.
===========================================================================

NAME: Ironsoul Aura
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura that absorbs and vents small radiation hits (under 20 rad) as outward
pulses to nearby enemies; large rad hits still pass through to the player normally.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - Radius: 3 tiles from player
  - When the player would receive 1-19 rad from any source: instead, absorb it (player gains 0
    rad) and immediately vent it as a 3-tile radius pulse — each enemy in range gains half the
    absorbed amount as rad (round down, minimum 1). This proc fires through the modify_incoming
    RAD hook (a new "modify_incoming_rad" hook, or inline check in add_radiation()).
  - When the player would receive 20+ rad: the full amount passes through normally. The aura
    does NOT vent large hits. Paladin of purity, not of invulnerability.
  - This creates a threshold split: small rad ticks (from fire, lingering zones, weak enemies)
    get vented outward. Big hits (strains, special attacks, Consecrate) pass through.
  - No per-tick cost. No rad drain. The cost is the absence of a stat bonus while active.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: no charge cost. No per-tick resource cost.
  - BUT: while active, the player's Decontamination XP gain from "radiation resisted" is halved.
    Mechanically: the aura "resists" small rad hits fully, but since it vents them, the game
    doesn't count this as normal resistance for XP purposes. This is enforced by a flag check
    in add_radiation() — if the vent fires, don't grant Decon XP for that resist.
  - Toggle OFF: free.
  - Decision logic: the cost is opportunity cost. Running Ironsoul sacrifices Decon XP farming
    efficiency (small rad resist XP) in exchange for converting enemy actions into self-harm.
    In XP-rich rad environments (Meth Lab), the trade matters. In low-rad areas, Ironsoul is
    free value.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Contamination Sense": The player can see the radiation value of any enemy within FOV
    as an added display element (e.g., when examining or when rad > 50, a glyph appears above
    the enemy similar to the existing rad glyph in sdl_overlay.py). Mechanically: in render.py,
    for each visible monster with radiation > 50, display the existing rad status glyph.
    Currently the rad glyph only appears on the player. This extends it to enemies.
    Additionally: enemies with radiation >= 100 have their name displayed in green in the
    bestiary/look-at UI rather than their default color.
  - This is a visibility/information perk. Zero mechanical power. Pure tactical awareness.

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - "Absorb small rad hits and vent them outward" is a reflection/redirect mechanic. NIGRL has
    no existing reflect or redirect on damage or debuffs.
  - The threshold split (small vented, large passes) forces players to understand the rad values
    of incoming sources — you want to be in positions where small ticks dominate (fire, zones)
    rather than large spikes (strain bad rolls, special attacks).
  - Contamination Sense extending the rad glyph to enemies is a QoL/tactical info improvement
    that fits the Decon "scan and purify" fantasy perfectly.
  - No resource cost for the toggle: the trade-off is opportunity cost (XP) rather than a
    resource bar, a design pattern not used by any other toggle.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Consecrate (L3): Player places a rad zone, stands in it, absorbs 10 rad/turn. But 10 rad
    exceeds the 20-rad threshold, so Ironsoul does NOT vent Consecrate zone ticks. This
    prevents the aura from making L3 trivial. Intentional non-synergy.
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist already reduces how much rad gets through. Ironsoul handles
    the "small residuals" that slip past resistance. Together they form a layered filter.
  - Force Sensitive: Large rad hits (20+) still pass through, so smoking Skywalker for STR
    stacking still works. The vent only absorbs the small chip-rad that wouldn't fuel Force
    Sensitive meaningfully anyway.
  - XP opportunity cost: players farming Decon XP intentionally may toggle Ironsoul OFF in
    rad-rich areas to preserve XP from resistance. Interesting meta-decision.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: IronsoulEffect (id="ironsoul_aura", floor_duration=True)
  - The core hook needs to intercept add_radiation() BEFORE the player gains rad. This requires
    either: (a) a new "modify_incoming_rad" hook called inside add_radiation() before applying,
    or (b) a check at the top of add_radiation() for the ironsoul_aura effect on the player.
    Option (b) is simpler and already done for other special cases in add_radiation().
    Implementation: at the top of add_radiation(), after computing `gain`, check if entity is
    engine.player and has ironsoul_aura effect active and gain < 20:
      - Vent: for each monster within 3 tiles, add_radiation(engine, monster, gain//2)
      - Set gain = 0 (player gets nothing)
      - Do NOT grant Decon resist XP for this amount
      - Return early without modifying player.radiation
  - Toggle execute: check for existing ironsoul_aura → remove vs apply_effect.
  - Contamination Sense: in render.py render_map_entities() or wherever monster glyphs are drawn,
    check if monster.radiation > 50 and player Decon level >= 2 → draw the rad status glyph.
    The existing sdl_overlay rad glyph code (for player) can be extended with a loop over
    visible monsters. Color shift for >= 100 rad enemies is in look_at/examine render.
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE, target_type=TargetType.SELF.

PERK TEXT:
  "Toggle (free ON/OFF, no cost): radiation hits under 20 are vented — you absorb 0, and
  enemies within 3 tiles each gain half as rad. Hits of 20+ pass through normally. Passive:
  enemies with 50+ rad display their radiation status visually in your FOV."

BALANCE:
  - Vent value: small rad ticks (3-10 rad/tick from zones, fire, weak enemies) become 1-5 rad
    to each enemy in range. Low per-instance, but consistent over a fight.
  - Free toggle cost means no resource management — the trade is XP rate vs. vent value.
  - Against heavy rad sources (strains, special attacks, Consecrate): Ironsoul does nothing.
    The perk is designed for sustained rad-drip environments, not spike resistance.
  - Contamination Sense: zero power impact. Pure information.


===========================================================================
OPTION 6: "ABSCESS WARD"
Concept: The darkest option — aura that accepts radiation into the body willingly to power a
repulsion field. More rad the player carries, stronger the push. Pure offensive-defensive hybrid.
===========================================================================

NAME: Abscess Ward
ONE-LINE: Toggle aura: your current radiation level creates a repulsion field — enemies that
start their turn adjacent to you are pushed back 1 tile and take rad//25 damage. Costs tox/tick
to sustain, representing the physical toll of weaponizing your own contamination.

AURA EFFECT (while ON):
  - At the START of each enemy's turn (before_turn hook — but since this is player-side, it fires
    in the energy loop AFTER player acts but before monster actions): any monster within 1 tile
    (Chebyshev, i.e., adjacent) is:
      1. Dealt damage equal to player.radiation // 25 as true damage (no defense).
         At 100 rad: 4 dmg. At 200 rad: 8 dmg. At 300 rad: 12 dmg. At 500: 20 dmg.
      2. Pushed back 1 tile away from the player (using existing knockback logic — direction
         vector from player to monster, push monster 1 tile that direction if tile is walkable).
         If blocked (wall, other monster), they take the damage but aren't moved.
  - This fires each tick of the energy loop, not just when an enemy acts. The aura "breathes"
    every tick.

TOGGLE COST / CONDITION:
  - Toggle ON: costs 3 tox/tick (pierce_resistance=True) while active. Tox builds slowly but
    steadily. If player tox hits 500 (cap), the aura auto-shuts off.
  - Toggle OFF: free. Tox drain stops.
  - Decision logic: the aura deals no damage when the player has low rad. High-rad players
    deal meaningful chip damage. But sustaining costs tox, and tox increases incoming damage
    (1 + (tox/100)^0.6 multiplier). Running this at high tox is risky — the incoming damage
    amplifier grows, and if a big hit lands, you're punished doubly. Requires tox management
    (TOL stat, tox removal items) to run sustainably.
  - Thematically: you are sweating contamination, physically dangerous to be near, but the
    toxins running through you to power it are killing you slowly.

SMALL PASSIVE BONUS (always-on):
  - "Biohazard Presence": While the player's radiation is >= 150, adjacent enemies (within 1
    tile) have their dodge chance reduced to 0. No dodging through the field. Mechanically:
    in combat.py before computing dodge: check if player.radiation >= 150 and Decon level >= 2
    and distance to player == 1 → skip dodge roll entirely for that attack.
  - At 150 rad the player is genuinely dangerous to approach (Force Sensitive should give good
    STR by this point, Abscess Ward deals ~6 dmg/tick, and no adjacent dodge means melee hits
    land clean). Rewards high-rad melee builds.

WHY THIS IS NOVEL:
  - Knockback on an aura is new — no other effect in the game pushes enemies based on proximity
    each tick. Crowd control as ambient presence.
  - The "aura damage scales with player's rad level" creates a direct tie between the
    Decontamination cycling mechanic and moment-to-moment combat pressure. Peak-rad is now a
    combat power window, not just a transition state before purging.
  - Tox cost for a rad-based ability is a cross-system tension — punishes Chemical Warfare
    characters who already carry high tox, rewards Decon characters who manage tox low.
  - "Dodge suppression when adjacent at high rad" passive is mechanically unique. No existing
    perk or effect does this.

SYNERGY NOTES:
  - Radiant (L1): +30% rad resist means you can carry higher rad longer without mutating,
    sustaining the aura's damage longer. L1's STR bonus also amplifies the melee crits that
    land after the dodge suppression removes evasion.
  - Force Sensitive / Skywalker OG: Peak rad window = peak Abscess Ward damage. Smoke Skywalker
    to purge rad FOR STR stacks, but also to exit Abscess Ward before tox overwhelms you.
    The timing is: build rad → toggle aura → fight at high rad → smoke strain → purge rad for
    STR → toggle aura off. A complete combat rotation loop.
  - Consecrate (L3): Standing in your own Consecrate zone gives +1 Swagger/turn and deals rad
    damage to enemies. Abscess Ward pushes enemies out of melee. Together: push enemies into
    your zone, Consecrate irradiates them, you stand safe inside.
  - White Power Bastion: Both cost tox to sustain if running simultaneously. Very high tox
    generation. Different build directions — Abscess Ward is high-rad offense, Bastion is
    defensive. Not intended to combo.
  - Chemical Warfare (3 tox/tick from this + CW's own tox generation): massive tox liability
    if running both. A cross-tree penalty that discourages mindless stacking.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - New effect class: AbscessWardEffect (id="abscess_ward", floor_duration=True)
  - tick(entity, engine): add_toxicity(engine, entity, 3, pierce_resistance=True). Check if
    entity.toxicity >= 500: set self.expired = True (auto-shutoff + message).
    Then: for each monster in engine.dungeon.get_monsters() where Chebyshev distance to player
    == 1 and monster.alive:
      - Compute dmg = engine.player.radiation // 25
      - If dmg > 0: monster.hp -= dmg (true dmg, no defense). Check death.
      - Attempt knockback: compute dx = monster.x - player.x, dy = monster.y - player.y
        (normalize to -1/0/1). New pos = (monster.x + dx, monster.y + dy). If not blocked,
        move monster. If monster dies from the dmg, skip knockback.
  - Death check after tick damage: reuse engine's standard death-check path (or inline the
    gold drop / event bus emit for entity_died). Be careful not to double-process.
  - Knockback: exists in engine for on_hit_knockback items. Extract or replicate the logic for
    monster repositioning. Can call engine._apply_knockback(monster, dx, dy) if that method
    exists; otherwise inline with dungeon.is_blocked check.
  - Dodge suppression passive: inline check in combat.py melee attack path. Check Decon >= 2,
    player.radiation >= 150, Chebyshev distance from player to defender == 1 → skip dodge.
  - Toggle ability execute: check existing abscess_ward → remove vs apply_effect + messages.
  - AbilityDef: charge_type=ChargeType.INFINITE, target_type=TargetType.SELF.

PERK TEXT:
  "Toggle (free ON/OFF, costs 3 tox/tick): your radiation repels adjacent enemies — each takes
  rad//25 true damage and is pushed back every turn. Passive: while at 150+ rad, adjacent
  enemies cannot dodge your attacks."

BALANCE:
  - At 100 rad: 4 dmg/tick to each adjacent enemy. In a fight, this adds up but isn't dominant.
  - At 300 rad: 12 dmg/tick to each adjacent enemy. Against melee swarms, this is powerful.
  - At 500 rad (mutation territory): 20 dmg/tick. High risk (mutation chance), high reward.
  - 3 tox/tick: ~333 turns to hit tox cap (500). Practical fights are 10-30 turns. Manageable.
  - Tox incoming damage multiplier at 100 tox: 1 + (100/100)^0.6 = 1 + 1 = 2.0x incoming.
    So running at moderate-high tox is punishing. The player must track both bars.
  - Dodge suppression at 150+ rad: requires investment in the rad cycle. Not trivially active
    at L2 — the player needs to deliberately build rad (from enemy attacks, strain misses, etc.).
