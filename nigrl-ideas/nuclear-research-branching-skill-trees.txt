NUCLEAR RESEARCH — 10 Branching Skill Tree Variations
=======================================================
Design doc for the NIGRL roguelike (Meth Lab zone skill).
These are BRANCHING trees with player-choice topology — NOT linear L1-L4.
The player selects one branch at each split and cannot unlock the other.

Nuclear Research theme reference:
  - Rad Bomb is the SIGNATURE ability — appears in every tree.
  - Book-Smarts scales spell damage. Reference formula: base + BKS/2 (or BKS)
  - Rad cost for Rad Bomb: 25 rad (refunded at 100+ rad)
  - Mutation safety passive ("block mutations below 150 rad") must appear in every tree.
  - Rad gain formula: amount * (1 - rad_resistance/100) * (1 + rad_gain_multiplier_bonus)
  - Nuclear Research L1 extra rad: +round(rad_gained * effective_BKS / 100)
  - Mutation thresholds: Weak 75+ rad (50 rad cost), Strong 125+ rad (125 rad cost),
    Huge 250+ rad (200 rad cost). Passive trigger: (rad//50)*0.001 per energy tick.
  - Player HP: 30 + CON*10. Swagger defence: int((eff_swg-8)/2).
  - Crit chance: street_smarts * 3%. Energy: 100/tick base. +30 = ~23% faster.
  - MEGA_CRIT_MULTIPLIER = 4.
  - Stat names: constitution, strength, street_smarts, book_smarts, tolerance, swagger
  - ChargeType: INFINITE+cooldown | PER_FLOOR | TOTAL | ONCE | FLOOR_ONLY
  - TargetType: SELF | SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS | LINE_FROM_PLAYER | AOE_CIRCLE | ADJACENT | ADJACENT_TILE

XP source for Nuclear Research: casting Rad Bomb (50 XP/cast), dealing rad-based
damage to enemies, converting consumables via Nutrient Producer, surviving at high rad levels.

Flavor reference — names drawn from nuclear physics:
  fission, fusion, meltdown, critical mass, enrichment, isotope, half-life,
  fallout, reactor, chain reaction, containment, breeder, neutron, prompt criticality,
  decay, yield, detonation, ground zero, flash point, mushroom, fallout, shielding,
  moderator, criticality, quench, scram, dead hand, dead zone.

Shape glossary:
  Y-split (L3)        — L1, L2 trunk; branch at L3: L3a/L4a vs L3b/L4b. 6 perks, 2 capstones.
  Diamond             — L1 shared; L2a/L2b split; L3a/L3b branches; L4 shared convergence;
                        L5a/L5b final split. 8 perks, 2 capstones.
  Triple Fork (L4)    — L1, L2, L3 shared trunk; split at L4 into L4a/L4b/L4c. 6 perks, 3 capstones.
  W-shape             — L1/L2 shared; L3a/L4a and L3b/L4b branches; L5 shared convergence;
                        L6a/L6b final split. 8 perks, 2 capstones.
  Long Trunk Y        — L1, L2, L3, L4 shared trunk; branch at L5 into L5a/L5b. 6 perks, 2 capstones.
  Inverted Y          — TWO starting branches (L1a/L1b); each with L2; converge at shared L3-L4.
  Ladder/Zigzag       — L1 shared; L2 choose A/B; L3 shared; L4 choose A/B; L5 shared capstone.
  Trident             — L1-L2 shared; L3a/L3b/L3c split; L3a+L3b reconverge at L5; L3c stays solo.
  Double Diamond      — L1 shared; L2 split; L3 convergence; L4 split; L5 capstone.
  Asymmetric Y        — L1-L2 shared; split at L3: Branch A = L3a only (short); Branch B = L3b+L4b+L5b (long).

New engine state needed (all 10 variations combined):
  rad_bomb_detonations (int, run-wide; count of Rad Bomb detonations)
  fission_splinter_count (int, per-cast; how many Fission sub-bombs are placed)
  critical_mass_ready (bool; set by Critical Mass buff, cleared on next ability fire)
  chain_reaction_kills (int, per-floor; rad-damage kills that triggered chain reactions)
  half_life_marked_id (str|None; entity id bearing Half-Life mark; cleared on death/floor)
  isotope_marked_id (str|None; entity id bearing Isotope mark; cleared on death/floor)
  fusion_charge (int, 0-200; built from rad drain, used by Fusion Detonation)
  fusion_draining (bool; True while Fusion is accumulating)
  fallout_zone_tiles (list[tuple[int,int]]; tile coords currently radioactive terrain)
  fallout_zone_duration (int; turns remaining on Fallout Zone hazard)
  reactor_core_active (bool; Reactor Core passive aura is running)
  reactor_core_turns (int; turns remaining on Reactor Core aura)
  enrichment_stacks (int, 0-5; bonus damage stacks on next Rad Bomb from Enrichment)
  prompt_criticality_hits (int; consecutive turns at 200+ rad; resets on drop below 200)
  dead_hand_cooldown (int; turns until Dead Hand can trigger again)
  rad_nova_used_this_floor (bool; for FLOOR_ONLY variants)
  contaminate_targets (set[str]; entity ids currently contaminated via Contaminate ability)
  ground_zero_tile (tuple|None; tile where Ground Zero hazard is placed)
  scram_cooldown (int; turns until SCRAM can fire again)
  meltdown_fuse (int; turns until Meltdown detonates; -1 = not active)
  meltdown_tile (tuple|None; tile where Meltdown crystal is placed)
  decay_chain_bonus (float; accumulated damage multiplier from Decay Chain passive)

New PlayerStats fields needed (all 10 variations combined):
  rad_bomb_damage_bonus (int; flat additive to Rad Bomb base damage beyond the BKS formula)
  rad_bomb_area_bonus (int; bonus tiles added to Rad Bomb blast radius, default 0)
  rad_bomb_charges_bonus (int; extra per-floor charges on Rad Bomb, default 0)
  rad_bomb_fuse_reduction (int; turns removed from Rad Bomb fuse, default 0; min fuse = 1)
  rad_spell_damage_mult (float; multiplicative bonus to all Nuclear Research spell damage)
  rad_cost_reduction (int; flat rad cost reduction on all spells, min 0)
  nuke_crit_bonus (float; bonus crit multiplier for rad spells only, stacks with crit_multiplier_bonus)
  contaminate_dot_bonus (int; flat bonus to Contaminate DOT per tick)
  fallout_zone_radius_bonus (int; extra radius on Fallout Zone, default 0)
  reactor_aura_radius (int; tiles radius of Reactor Core passive irradiation, default 1)
  mutation_block_threshold (int; default 150; rad level below which mutations are blocked)
    — Note: every tree variation that includes the mutation safety passive sets this.
      Some variations raise it to 200, some keep it at 150.

New effects needed (all 10 variations combined):
  CriticalMassEffect — buff: next spell is guaranteed crit + 2x damage; consumed on fire; dur 20t
  ContaminateEffect  — DOT: target gains 3 rad/turn for 8t; stacks independently per application;
                       on death: irradiate all adjacent enemies for 20 rad
  HalfLifeEffect     — mark: next time enemy HP drops to <=50%, they take instant burst damage
                       equal to (current HP / 2 rounded up); the mark is then consumed
  IsotopeMarkEffect  — mark: on kill, the entity's tile becomes a Fallout Zone hazard (6t dur,
                       3 rad/turn to anything passing through); chains 1 time only
  FalloutZoneEffect  — hazard: persistent radioactive terrain (4 rad/turn to entity on tile);
                       interacts with Fallout Zone Hazard entity type (see below)
  ReactorAuraEffect  — passive ambient aura: at turn start, any enemy within reactor_aura_radius
                       tiles gains 3 rad (unresisted by rad_resistance); dur = reactor_core_turns
  DecayChainEffect   — passive: each consecutive floor-tick dealing rad damage to enemies
                       increases decay_chain_bonus by 0.05 (max 0.5); resets on floor change
  FusionChargeEffect — ongoing: while active, player gains +10 rad/turn but cannot cast spells;
                       clears when fusion_detonation is fired or player reaches 0 rad
  PromptCritEffect   — triggered: at 200+ rad for 5 consecutive turns, next Rad Bomb guaranteed
                       to deal MEGA_CRIT (×4) damage; consumed on detonation
  ScramEffect        — defensive: reduces incoming damage by 30% for 3 turns; 1/floor
  EnrichmentEffect   — stacking buff: each stack adds +5 damage to next Rad Bomb; max 5 stacks;
                       consumed fully on detonation; stacks persist across turns

New ability IDs (all 10 variations combined):
  rad_bomb_b          — existing (Rad Bomb, upgraded versions share same ID with stat bonuses)
  rad_nova_b          — instant AOE around player; no delay; costs 50 rad; 1/floor
  meltdown_b          — massive delayed nuke; 5-turn fuse; 9×9 area; costs 75 rad; 1/floor
  contaminate_b       — ranged DOT; single target; applies ContaminateEffect; 3/floor; costs 15 rad
  fallout_zone_b      — place radioactive terrain; 3×3 area; 12t duration; costs 30 rad; 2/floor
  critical_mass_b     — self-buff; costs 40 rad; next spell CRIT; dur 20t; 1/floor
  fission_b           — splits next Rad Bomb into 3 mini-bombs (each 40% damage, separate tiles);
                        replaces normal Rad Bomb for that cast; costs 25 rad extra; 1/floor
  fusion_detonation_b — drains all current rad into one hit; target single enemy; dmg = rad/2;
                        no charges limit but costs all rad; FLOOR_ONLY
  half_life_b         — mark single enemy; triggers on <=50% HP burst; costs 20 rad; 2/floor
  isotope_b           — mark single enemy; on kill → Fallout Zone; costs 20 rad; 3/floor
  chain_reaction_b    — passive trigger: rad-damage kills cause adjacent enemies to take
                        (10 + BKS/4) rad damage; no cost (passive)
  reactor_core_b      — toggle aura; costs 5 rad/turn while active; irrdiates all in radius;
                        turn on/off as free action (SELF target)
  enrichment_b        — spend 15 rad → +1 Enrichment stack (max 5); activated; unlimited uses
  ground_zero_b       — mark a tile (ADJACENT_TILE); next Rad Bomb detonation teleports epicenter
                        to that tile (overrides placement); costs 0 extra; 1/floor
  dead_hand_b         — passive trigger: if player reaches 0 HP, detonate all active Rad Bomb
                        crystals instantly; if no crystals active, place one and detonate same
                        turn; 1/run (ONCE); grants 50 Nuclear Research XP on trigger
  scram_b             — defensive ability; costs 20 rad; apply ScramEffect; 1/floor; SELF
  prompt_criticality_b — passive: watching engine for 5 consecutive turns at 200+ rad;
                          AbilityDef.is_passive = True; no targeting

New hazard types (all 10 variations combined):
  hazard_type = "fallout_zone" — char='~', color=(100,220,50), blocks_movement=False;
    applies 4 rad/turn to any entity on the tile; duration tracked by fallout_zone_duration;
    spawned by fallout_zone_b ability and Isotope mark kills
  hazard_type = "rad_crystal"  — char='*', color=(80,255,180); the Rad Bomb placed crystal;
    existing in concept, formalize as entity type with fuse_turns field

Balance reference:
  - Rad Bomb base: 15+BKS/2 damage in 5×5 area. At BKS 10 = 20 dmg; BKS 12 = 21 dmg.
  - Upgraded Rad Bomb (L4 original): 20+BKS damage. At BKS 10 = 30 dmg; BKS 12 = 32 dmg.
  - Enemy HP ranges: floor 1-2 enemies ~20-40 HP; floor 3-4 ~40-80 HP; bosses 150-250 HP.
  - Rad Nova: instant 5×5 hits ~3-5 enemies for (10+BKS) damage each = decent floor clear.
  - Meltdown: 9×9 area at (30+BKS*2) damage is approximately 2× a regular Rad Bomb in area
    and ~60-70% more damage — once-per-floor so it's the "nuke button."
  - Fusion Detonation at full 200 rad = 100 damage to one target. At BKS bonus scaling it's
    a one-shot for most non-boss enemies. High opportunity cost (full rad drain).
  - Contaminate DOT: 3 rad/turn × 8 turns = 24 total rad applied to enemy. Pairs with
    Reactor Core and Chain Reaction for passive irradiation builds.
  - Fission: 3 × 40% of Rad Bomb = 120% of single Rad Bomb across three points, 1 extra rad cost.
    Best vs grouped clusters; worse than normal vs isolated single targets.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 1: "GROUND ZERO" — Bomb Specialist vs. Fallout Controller
==========================================================================
Shape: Y-split (L3)
Fantasy: Master the Rad Bomb, then choose: become the bomb (bigger, faster detonation)
         or become the fallout (persistent radioactive terrain + contamination DOT).

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Irradiated Intellect
              |
    L2: Rad Bomb
              |
       ______/ \_______
      /                 \
[BRANCH A]           [BRANCH B]
L3a: Short Fuse       L3b: Half-Life
      |                     |
L4a: Fission             L4b: Fallout Zone
  (capstone)               (capstone)


TRUNK:

L1 — IRRADIATED INTELLECT (stat)
  Perk type: stat
  Bonuses: book_smarts +3
  Passive: Radiation gained is increased by BKS% of the amount gained.
    (e.g., at BKS 12, gaining 10 rad becomes 10 + round(10*12/100) = 11 rad)
  Additional passive: block mutations below 150 rad.
  Desc: "The smarter you are, the hotter you run. Background radiation is just
         a free education."

L2 — RAD BOMB (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: rad_bomb_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (3/floor)
  Target type: ADJACENT_TILE (places crystal within 2 tiles; fuse = 3 turns)
  Effect: Place a glowing crystal (*) on an adjacent tile. After 3 turns, it detonates:
          deals 15 + BKS/2 damage (rounded down) to all entities in a 5×5 area centered
          on the crystal. Costs 25 rad per cast; refunded if player has 100+ rad at
          cast time. Grants 50 Nuclear Research XP per detonation.
  Desc: "Three seconds to stand somewhere else."

BRANCH A — Bomb Specialist

L3a — SHORT FUSE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Rad Bomb fuse reduced from 3 turns to 2 turns (rad_bomb_fuse_reduction = 1).
          Additionally, each Rad Bomb detonation generates +1 Enrichment stack automatically
          (enrichment_stacks += 1 on each detonation, up to the 5-stack cap).
          The enrichment stacks carry to the next bomb, creating runaway momentum.
  Desc: "Patience is for people who aren't on fire."

L4a — FISSION (activated, capstone)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: fission_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (1/floor)
  Target type: SELF (consumed on next Rad Bomb cast, not a separate target)
  Effect: Activate Fission before placing a Rad Bomb. The next Rad Bomb you place
          becomes THREE mini-crystals placed simultaneously, spread across
          adjacent tiles (player picks the first; the other two are auto-placed
          at the closest walkable tiles). Each mini-bomb deals 40% of normal
          Rad Bomb damage (with full BKS scaling applied). Total potential
          damage = 120% of a normal bomb if all three hit different targets,
          but each target can only be caught in one blast. Costs 25 extra rad
          (total 50 rad for the cast). Grants 50 XP per mini-bomb detonation.
  Desc: "Why blow up one room when you can blow up three?"

BRANCH B — Fallout Controller

L3b — HALF-LIFE (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: half_life_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (2/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Mark a visible enemy with Half-Life (costs 20 rad). The mark persists until
          triggered or the enemy dies. The next time that enemy's HP drops to 50% or below
          (from any source), the mark triggers: the enemy instantly takes burst rad damage
          equal to half their current HP (rounded up). The mark is then consumed.
          Grants 25 Nuclear Research XP on trigger.
  Desc: "Every unstable nucleus has a breaking point. You just find it faster."

L4b — FALLOUT ZONE (activated, capstone)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: fallout_zone_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (2/floor)
  Target type: ADJACENT_TILE (places center tile; 3×3 affected area)
  Effect: Create a persistent radioactive terrain hazard (fallout_zone hazard type) centered
          on the target tile. The zone persists for 12 turns. Any entity (enemy or player)
          standing on a Fallout Zone tile receives 4 rad per turn (unresisted by rad_resistance
          for enemies; player's own rad_resistance applies for player). Enemies accumulate
          rad and can trigger irradiated damage penalties per the tox/rad multiplier system.
          Costs 30 rad per cast. Pairs with Half-Life: enemies marched into zones hit
          the 50% HP trigger faster. Grants 20 XP per enemy-turn spent in the zone.
  Desc: "The battle is over. The ground isn't."

Implementation notes:
  - Short Fuse: rad_bomb_fuse_reduction = 1; add detonation hook in engine that fires
    enrichment_stacks += 1 after each Rad Bomb detonation if this perk is active.
  - Fission: replace normal rad_bomb entity spawn with three entities; auto-place algo:
    find 2 nearest walkable tiles to player that aren't the chosen tile.
  - Half-Life: apply HalfLifeEffect to entity; in combat.py damage hook check
    entity.hp <= entity.max_hp//2 and strip mark → deal burst.
  - Fallout Zone: spawns fallout_zone hazard at 3×3 tiles; track via engine.fallout_zone_tiles.

Balance notes:
  - Short Fuse + Fission: 2-turn fuse + triple bomb = very aggressive placement window.
    The extra rad cost (50 vs 25) and 1/floor limit keep it from being spammable.
  - Fallout Zone synergy with Half-Life: strong but enemies must actually walk into zones;
    active monsters chase player, so placement near chokepoints is skill-based.

VARIATION 2: "THE REACTOR" — Aura Builder vs. Single-Target Nuke
==========================================================================
Shape: Diamond
Fantasy: Early game you build toward sustainable rad output via passive aura and DOTs;
         late game you pick between a persistent reactor god-mode or a Fusion one-shot cannon.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Irradiated Intellect
           /      \
          /        \
  L2a: Reactor Core  L2b: Contaminate
          \          /
           \        /
    L3: Critical Mass (convergence)
           /      \
          /        \
  L4a: Meltdown    L4b: Fusion Detonation
   (capstone)         (capstone)


TRUNK:

L1 — IRRADIATED INTELLECT (stat)
  (Same as V1 trunk L1 — BKS +3, rad gain bonus, mutation block at 150 rad.)
  Also grants Rad Bomb: 3/floor, 5×5, 15+BKS/2 damage, 25 rad cost.
  All Diamond paths require this before branching.

BRANCH SPLIT at L2 (pick one):

L2a — REACTOR CORE (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: reactor_core_b
  Charge type: INFINITE (toggle; costs 5 rad per player turn while active)
  Target type: SELF
  Effect: Toggle an ambient radiation aura (radius = reactor_aura_radius, default 1 tile).
          While active, every enemy within range gains 3 rad per player turn (unresisted).
          Cost: 5 rad per turn (player's own rad_resistance does NOT reduce this drain —
          you're the reactor, not the target). Toggle off to stop the drain. Cannot be
          active at 0 rad. Grants +2 Nuclear Research XP per enemy irradiated per turn.
          While Reactor Core is active, Rad Bomb detonations gain +3 damage per enemy
          in the aura at moment of detonation (up to +12 for 4 enemies).
  Desc: "You are the source."

L2b — CONTAMINATE (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: contaminate_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (3/floor)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Apply a ContaminateEffect to a visible enemy (costs 15 rad). The target gains
          3 rad per turn for 8 turns. On expiry or death, 20 rad splashes to all
          adjacent enemies. Multiple Contaminate stacks on the same target are
          tracked independently (each has its own 8-turn timer). Grants 10 XP per
          application; 15 XP on contamination expiry splash.
  Desc: "One carrier is worth a hundred bullets."

CONVERGENCE at L3 (both paths unlock this):

L3 — CRITICAL MASS (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: critical_mass_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (1/floor)
  Target type: SELF
  Effect: Spend 40 rad to enter Critical Mass state (CriticalMassEffect, dur 20t).
          Your next Nuclear Research ability (Rad Bomb, Meltdown, Fission, Rad Nova, etc.)
          automatically deals a guaranteed CRITICAL HIT with nuke_crit_bonus applied
          (bonus crit multiplier: +1.0 above base, so MEGA_CRIT × 2 = ×8 total).
          Mutation block threshold raised to 200 rad while this buff is active.
          Grants 50 XP when the buffed ability fires.
  Desc: "There is no safe amount. There is only the yield."

FINAL SPLIT at L4 (pick one):

L4a — MELTDOWN (activated, capstone)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: meltdown_b
  Charge type: FLOOR_ONLY (1/floor, resets on floor change)
  Target type: ADJACENT_TILE (places crystal within 2 tiles; fuse = 5 turns)
  Effect: Place a Meltdown crystal (*). After 5 turns it detonates: 9×9 blast area,
          deals 30 + BKS*2 damage. At BKS 12 = 54 damage across a 9×9 area.
          All surviving enemies hit are irradiated: gain 30 rad regardless of
          rad_resistance. Costs 75 rad. If fired while Critical Mass is active,
          the guaranteed crit applies (up to ×8 damage = up to 432 dmg at BKS 12 —
          screen-clearing nuke). Grants 150 XP on detonation.
  Desc: "You were not supposed to have this many isotopes."

L4b — FUSION DETONATION (activated, capstone)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: fusion_detonation_b
  Charge type: FLOOR_ONLY (can use whenever, but drains ALL current rad)
  Target type: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
  Effect: Drain every point of current rad into a single beam at a visible target.
          Deals damage equal to current rad / 2 (before drain). At 200 rad = 100 damage;
          at 150 rad = 75 damage. Enemy killed by Fusion Detonation explodes in a 3×3
          rad burst: nearby enemies gain 40 rad regardless of resistance.
          After firing, player rad drops to 0. Cannot fire at 0 rad. Costs no flat rad
          (the drain IS the cost). If fired while Critical Mass is active: damage = rad × 1.5
          (before drain), so 200 rad = 300 damage (one-shots any non-boss in the game).
          Grants 100 XP per cast.
  Desc: "Every electron you've ever absorbed. All at once. Goodbye."

Implementation notes:
  - Reactor Core: engine._run_energy_loop() checks reactor_core_active each player-turn;
    iterates entities within reactor_aura_radius; calls irradiate(entity, 3, bypass_resist=True).
    Rad drain: subtract 5 from player.radiation each player turn. Auto-disable at 0.
  - Critical Mass: CriticalMassEffect.on_player_spell_fire() sets force_crit=True,
    applies nuke_crit_bonus; then expiry removes effect. Check in spells.py execute path.
  - Fusion Detonation: calculate damage = player.radiation // 2, then set player.radiation = 0.
    On kill: spawn fallout burst at 3×3 tiles irradiating survivors.

VARIATION 3: "CHAIN REACTION" — Bomb Mastery vs. Cascade Killer
==========================================================================
Shape: Triple Fork (L4)
Fantasy: A long trunk of escalating bomb investment splits into three specializations:
         go deeper on bombs (Fission), pivot to killing chains (Chain Reaction passive),
         or create the ultimate terrain denial (Ground Zero repositioner).

TREE DIAGRAM:

         L1: Irradiated Intellect
                    |
         L2: Rad Bomb
                    |
         L3: Enrichment
                    |
         ___________|____________
        /            |            \
   [A]             [B]           [C]
L4a: Fission   L4b: Chain     L4c: Ground Zero
 (capstone)     Reaction       (capstone)
                (capstone)


TRUNK:

L1 — IRRADIATED INTELLECT (stat)
  (BKS +3, rad gain bonus, mutation block at 150 rad — same core as V1.)

L2 — RAD BOMB (activated)
  (3/floor, 5×5, 15+BKS/2 damage, 25 rad cost — same core as V1.)

L3 — ENRICHMENT (activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: enrichment_b
  Charge type: INFINITE (unlimited uses)
  Target type: SELF
  Effect: Spend 15 rad to gain 1 Enrichment stack (max 5). Each Enrichment stack adds
          +5 flat damage to your next Rad Bomb detonation. Stacks do NOT carry across floors.
          When the next Rad Bomb detonates, all stacks are consumed and the bonus is applied
          (5 stacks = +25 damage on top of the 15+BKS/2 formula). This can be pre-loaded
          before entering a dangerous room. Grants 10 XP per stack applied to a detonation.
  Desc: "The more you put in, the bigger the yield. Basic physics."

BRANCH A — Maximum Yield

L4a — FISSION (activated, capstone)
  (Same as V1 Branch A L4a — three mini-bombs, 40% each, 1/floor, 50 rad total cost.)
  Added synergy here: if Enrichment stacks are active when Fission fires, each mini-bomb
  individually inherits (stacks × +3 damage) — slightly reduced per-bomb but total output
  can exceed the single-bomb enriched version.
  Desc: "More fronts. More coverage. More chaos."

BRANCH B — The Cascade

L4b — CHAIN REACTION (passive, capstone)
  Perk type: passive
  Ability: chain_reaction_b (AbilityDef.is_passive = True)
  Effect: Whenever an enemy dies from rad damage (including Rad Bomb, Contaminate DOT,
          Reactor Core aura, or any rad source), every enemy adjacent to that corpse
          instantly takes (10 + BKS/4) rad damage (unresisted). This can trigger
          subsequent chain reactions if the adjacent enemies also die, but each chain
          is capped at 1 propagation hop per detonation event (so: original kill →
          adjacent damage → if those die, no further chain from THOSE deaths that same
          event). Grants 15 Nuclear Research XP per chain reaction enemy hit.
  Desc: "Proximity is destiny."

BRANCH C — Precision Targeting

L4c — GROUND ZERO (activated, capstone)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: ground_zero_b
  Charge type: PER_FLOOR (1/floor)
  Target type: ADJACENT_TILE
  Effect: Mark a tile as Ground Zero (visible marker). The next Rad Bomb you place
          will teleport its explosion epicenter to the Ground Zero tile at detonation
          (the crystal is still physically placed by the player normally, but at the
          moment of detonation, the blast center shifts to the marked tile). This
          allows indirect firing around corners: place the crystal somewhere safe,
          redirect the blast into a room you cannot enter. Ground Zero tile remains
          marked until the next Rad Bomb detonates or you use this ability again.
          Stacks with Enrichment: a redirected enriched Rad Bomb delivers the full
          enriched damage at the target tile, making it a true sniper nuke.
          Grants 30 XP on a successful redirected detonation.
  Desc: "The bomb goes here. The explosion goes there."

Implementation notes:
  - Enrichment: each enrichment_stacks += 1 on ability fire; deducted at Rad Bomb detonation.
    enrichment_stacks resets on floor change. Stacks persist on entity across turns.
  - Chain Reaction: hook in combat.py or engine death handler: on_entity_death check
    entity.damage_source == "rad"; if True, iterate adjacent tiles for living enemies,
    apply chain_reaction_damage = 10 + BKS//4; mark event as chain_reaction=True to
    prevent further propagation that same death event.
  - Ground Zero: set engine.ground_zero_tile = target_tile on ability fire; in Rad Bomb
    detonation logic, if ground_zero_tile is not None, use it as center; clear after use.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Irradiated Intellect
              |
    L2: Rad Bomb
      /               \
L3a: SCRAM          L3b: Prompt
      |              Criticality
L4a: Isotope              |

  (Same as V5 Branch A L5a — streak-based +50% max multiplicative damage ramp.)
  At this point the Branch B player has: Rad Bomb + Fissile Material (rad from combat) +

L6b — PROMPT CRITICALITY + FUSION DETONATION (dual capstone)
  Perk type: mixed (passive + activated in a single perk unlock)
  On unlock, grants BOTH:

  Prompt Criticality (passive):
    (Same as V4 Branch B L3b — 5 consecutive turns at 200+ rad → next Rad Bomb gets

  Fusion Detonation (activated):
    (Same as V2 L4b — drain all rad, single target, damage = rad/2; 3×3 burst on kill.)
    Upgrade here: if Decay Chain bonus is at max (0.50) when Fusion fires, the damage
    formula becomes (rad/2) × 1.5 — reflecting that the chain is at full power.
    At 200 rad with full Decay Chain: 150 damage to one target. Single highest-damage
    ability in the game.

  Combined fantasy: the experimentalist maintains 200+ rad through combat hits (Fissile
  Material) and enriched bombing (Enrichment + Decay Chain). After 5 turns at 200+ rad,
  Prompt Criticality fires the next Rad Bomb at ×4 for a massive area nuke. If that's
  not enough, Fusion Detonation is always available as a backup single-target delete
  (or the closer if the nuke leaves one survivor).

  Desc: "You are no longer doing science. Science is doing you."

Implementation notes:
  - Asymmetric Y: Branch A has 1 perk (L3a capstone), Branch B has 4 perks (L3b-L6b).
    Implementation: at L3 choice, player picks A or B. Branch A immediately shows the
    capstone as purchasable. Branch B begins a 4-perk chain. Both choices permanently
    lock out the other branch.
  - Dual capstone perk: in skills.py, implement as a single perk that calls grant_ability()

  - Balance note: Branch A is deliberately lower skill cap. The academic build is for players

==========================================================================
CROSS-TREE SYNERGIES (Nuclear Research with other Meth Lab skills)
==========================================================================

With Mutation:
  - Nuclear Research L1 "mutation block below 150 rad" is essential if combining both trees.
    Players who invest in both are accumulating heavy rad; the block prevents junk mutations
    while allowing intentional mutation at high rad. The V4 variant raises block to 200 rad,
    which is especially valuable for Mutation/Nuclear Research dual builds.
  - Prompt Criticality requires 200+ rad for 5 turns. Mutation's Huge tier triggers at 250+ rad.
    If the player is sitting at 200+ rad for Prompt Criticality, a mutation could fire.
    The block prevents weak/strong mutations; only Huge tier is possible at 250+.

With Chemical Warfare:
  - Contaminate DOT (rad application) pairs with Chemical Warfare's tox stacking. An enemy
    simultaneously contaminated and poisoned takes increasing damage from both multipliers.
  - Fallout Zone terrain + Chemical Warfare's acid pool terrain = overlapping hazard fields
    that deny room movement. Enemy is forced to take both rad and tox while standing still.

With Glow Up:
  - Glow Up's Emission passive (spread rad to enemies on player rad gain) doubles well with
    Reactor Core: the aura generates rad for the player (from nearby enemies dying at high rad)
    which triggers Emission, which further irradiates adjacent enemies.

With Meth-Head:
  - Meth-Head speed buffs allow faster bomb placement and detonation management.
    Short Fuse + high speed = 2-turn fuse is very manageable at fast move speed.

With Rolling:
  - Rolling's smoke cloud ability can mask bomb placement (enemies may move into the bomb's
    area without seeing the crystal). Tactical synergy.

==========================================================================
BALANCE SUMMARY
==========================================================================

Power curve targets:
  L1-L2 trunk (shared): Available from Meth Lab floor 1. Should feel useful immediately.
    Rad Bomb base damage 15+BKS/2 at BKS 8 = 19 damage. Floor 1-2 enemies: 20-40 HP.
    Three charges means roughly one bomb per major encounter per floor.

  L3-L4 (branch entry): Mid-game Meth Lab. Abilities should feel like significant upgrades.
    Upgraded Rad Bomb (20+BKS) at BKS 10 = 30 damage. Starting to one-shot floor 3 enemies.

  L5-L6 capstones: Late Meth Lab / endgame. Should be floor-defining moments.
    Meltdown at BKS 12 = 54 damage across 9×9 — clears most rooms entirely.
    Fusion Detonation at 200 rad = 100 damage — one-shots all non-boss enemies.

Rad costs and sustainability:
  - 25 rad per Rad Bomb (refunded at 100+) = sustainable for a player maintaining 100+ rad.
  - Contaminate costs 15 rad × 3/floor = 45 rad/floor minimum. Manageable.
  - Reactor Core: 5 rad/turn × 10 turns active per floor = 50 rad/floor drain. Expensive.
    Players must manage when to toggle or rad will collapse before Meltdown is castable.
  - Meltdown at 75 rad cost is a near-full-drain if player is at ~100-125 rad.
    The cost forces a decision: Meltdown now or wait to rebuild rad for better timing.

Mutation risk management:
  - All 10 trees include the mutation block at 150 rad (base or upgraded to 200).
    Without this, Rad Bomb players sitting at 100+ rad would be constantly mutating.
  - Players who go for Prompt Criticality (200+ rad builds) need the 200-rad block
    (V4 L6a, V10 Branch B) to safely operate in the 200+ zone.
  - Fusion Detonation's drain to 0 rad clears the mutation risk entirely — useful if the
    player is in dangerous mutation territory.

BKS scaling targets:
  - At BKS 8 (minimum investment): Rad Bomb 15+4 = 19. Meltdown 30+16 = 46.
  - At BKS 10 (moderate): Rad Bomb 15+5 = 20. Meltdown 30+20 = 50.
  - At BKS 12 (maximum): Rad Bomb 15+6 = 21 (base). Upgraded: 20+12 = 32. Meltdown 30+24 = 54.
  - The BKS scaling rewards stat investment but is not crippling at low BKS —
    the spell still functions as a crowd-clear at BKS 8.

Recommended implementation priority:
  1. V1 (Y-split) — simplest; establishes the two core Nuclear Research branch identities.
  2. V5 (Long Trunk Y) — closest to the existing linear tree; easiest transition.
  3. V3 (Triple Fork) — adds the Chain Reaction passive which opens many cross-tree synergies.
  4. V7 (Ladder) — tests the "choice node at L2 and L4" mechanic.
  5. Remaining shapes after those foundations are proven.
