================================================================================
TYRONE'S PENTHOUSE — 7 NEW SERVICE NPC DESIGNS
================================================================================

Context: These 7 NPCs add to the existing three (Gun Dealer, Chef, Plug). All are
one-time free services — no cash required, no repeat visits. Each NPC occupies a
small room or alcove off the main penthouse floor, accessible by bumping into them.
Interaction model: walk-into-NPC opens a menu overlay; player makes one choice; NPC
flags as used for the rest of the run; second approach gives a short dismissal line.

Design constraints:
- Each NPC must parallel the existing trio's interaction model (bump = menu).
- One free choice per run.
- No cash cost (that separates these from paid NPCs like Big Mama or Daquan).
- Every NPC must serve a different system to avoid overlap.
- ASCII rendering. Chars chosen to evoke their role at a glance.

================================================================================
NPC 1 — THE BARTENDER: DELROY
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
The penthouse parallel to the Chef (food) and the Plug (smoking). Delroy is a stocky
man behind a bar counter on the north wall. He is the drinks NPC: he offers the player
a free round from a curated selection of the game's alcohol consumables. Notably he
does not offer random garbage — he curates a short list of drinks that are genuinely
useful at the transition point between zones.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'B'  (Bartender)
color = (180, 130, 60)   — warm amber, like cheap whiskey

POSITION
--------
North wall, west quadrant. A counter entity (hazard_type="counter", char='=') runs
in front of him, blocking direct approach. Player bumps the counter tile to interact.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu overlay titled "DELROY'S BAR — FREE ROUND (ONE PER VISIT)"

  Delroy: "On the house. Just the one."

  Menu lists 5 drinks, always the same 5 (not randomized):

    a) [Malt Liquor]          — Drinking XP, STR buff, +tox
    b) [Homemade Hennessy]    — Drinking/Alcoholism XP, strong STR buff
    c) [Steel Reserve]        — Strong CON buff, bleeds slowly
    d) [Platinum Reserve]     — High Drinking/Alcoholism XP, big power spike
    e) [Virulent Vodka]       — Activates on-hit tox transfer (Meth Lab synergy)

  Player presses a-e to receive that drink in inventory. No immediate consumption —
  the drink is added as a normal inventory item. They can save it, use it immediately,
  or pass it through the Stash Box (if implemented). Menu closes.

  On second bump: "Delroy: Had your free one. You're buying next time."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine flag: engine.delroy_used = False; set True after pick; never resets mid-run.
  - On pick: call create_item_entity(chosen_drink_id) and append to player inventory.
  - The 5 curated drinks are hardcoded in the implementation (not random) — Delroy's
    "curation" is the design flavor.
  - If the player has a full inventory (unlikely given unlimited inventory), the item
    is placed on the floor beneath the player.
  - Drinking skill XP fires on actual consumption, not on receipt — same as any drink.

WHY THESE 5 DRINKS
------------------
  - Malt Liquor / Homemade Hennessy / Steel Reserve: three calibers of STR/CON buff;
    straightforward power for melee builds.
  - Platinum Reserve: the highest Drinking/Alcoholism XP drink in the game; for players
    trying to push past a skill threshold before entering the Meth Lab.
  - Virulent Vodka: explicitly a Meth Lab prep item — activates on-hit tox transfer,
    which combos with enemy tox pools on the Meth Lab floors.

BALANCE NOTES
-------------
Free single drink cannot be too powerful. None of these drinks are game-breaking in
isolation. Virulent Vodka is arguably the highest-value pick for savvy players going
into the Meth Lab, creating an interesting skill-expression moment. Melee builds will
likely take Platinum Reserve or Hennessy.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Alcoholism L3 Stash Finder perk (random alcohol added to inventory): stacks with
    Delroy's free pick — both can be ready for the Meth Lab entry.
  - Chef's Sandwich: if the player has a food item in inventory and uses Delroy's
    drink + Chef's sandwich, they can set up a powerful two-item combo burst.
  - Virulent Vodka + Chemical Warfare Spillover Aura: tox on hit transfers to kills
    which then transfer tox to nearby enemies — compounding poison chain in Meth Lab.

================================================================================
NPC 2 — THE SKILL TRADER: LA'QUISHA
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
The most mechanically complex of the seven NPCs. La'Quisha is a woman sitting at a
small table in the northeast corner, surrounded by piles of books and scribbled notes.
She offers a single "skill trade": drain 1 level from a skill the player has invested
in, and invest that energy into a skill the player has touched (has potential_exp in)
but never formally leveled. The result is a cross-skill power transfer that lets players
course-correct build paths that aren't working.

This is built around the user's specific vision: pick a skilled skill, pick an unleveled
skill that has latent potential, and trade levels. The transaction is irreversible.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'L'  (La'quisha)
color = (160, 200, 255)  — cool blue, intellectual

POSITION
--------
Northeast alcove, around ox+32, oy+3. A table entity (hazard_type="table", char=0xE002)
in front of her. She barely moves.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu overlay in two steps.

STEP 1 — SELECT THE SKILL TO DRAIN
Title: "LA'QUISHA'S SKILL TRADE — STEP 1: WHICH SKILL TO PULL FROM?"
Subtitle: "She'll drain 1 full level from this skill."

  Lists only skills where: skill.level >= 1
  Format for each row:
    [Smoking L3]     Real: 600 / 600 XP    (full level will be removed)
    [Stabbing L2]    Real: 400 / 400 XP

  Player presses a letter to select. Level must be >= 1. Skills at level 0 (even with
  real_exp toward level 1) are excluded — there must be a full level to take.

STEP 2 — SELECT THE SKILL TO BOOST
Title: "LA'QUISHA'S SKILL TRADE — STEP 2: WHERE TO PUT IT?"
Subtitle: "She'll inject enough XP to bring this skill to level 1."

  Lists only skills where: skill.level == 0 AND skill.potential_exp > 0
    (has potential but never been leveled — the player "touched" the skill)
  Format:
    [Meth-Head]      Potential: 840 XP   (needs 200 to reach L1)
    [Gunplay]        Potential: 320 XP   (needs 200 to reach L1)

  Player presses a letter to select.

CONFIRMATION PROMPT
  "Are you sure? This cannot be undone."
  Y = confirm, N / Esc = cancel without penalty.

ON CONFIRM
  - SOURCE skill: skill.level -= 1; skill.real_exp is set to 0 (the level's XP is gone,
    same as if the level was never earned). Corresponding perk is LOST.
  - TARGET skill: skill.real_exp += DEFAULT_EXP_CURVE[0] (200) — enough to hit level 1.
    Call skill.add_real_exp() to trigger level threshold check and unlock perk.
  - Skill point pool (engine.skills.skill_points) is unchanged — this is a direct XP
    injection that bypasses the normal conversion economy.
  - engine flag: engine.laquisha_used = True.
  - Post-confirmation message:
    "La'Quisha: Knowledge ain't destroyed. Just relocated."

EDGE CASES
----------
  - If player has no skills at level >= 1: La'Quisha says "You ain't learned enough
    to trade anything yet." No menu opens.
  - If player has no skills with level == 0 AND potential_exp > 0: La'Quisha says
    "You haven't touched anything new. Come back when you've been exploring."
  - If both pools exist but player has only 1 skill and it's the same one: impossible
    by definition (source must be leveled, target must be unleveled), so it can't
    be the same skill.
  - Perks from the SOURCE level are lost immediately. If the perk granted an ability
    (e.g., via engine.grant_ability), the ability should be removed from the player's
    ability list (engine.abilities dict — remove the entry).
  - The TARGET skill's perk at L1 fires immediately on level-up trigger.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.laquisha_used: bool = False
  - Step 1 pool: [s for s in engine.skills.all() if s.level >= 1]
  - Step 2 pool: [s for s in engine.skills.all() if s.level == 0 and s.potential_exp > 0]
  - Source mutation: s.level -= 1; s.real_exp = 0.0 (do NOT touch potential_exp)
  - Target mutation: target_skill.add_real_exp(200) — fires the normal level-up chain
    including perk unlock notification
  - Perk removal for source: call engine._remove_perk_at_level(skill_name, old_level)
    — this function does not yet exist; it must: (1) look up SKILL_PERKS[skill_name][old_level-1],
    (2) if perk grants an ability, remove it from engine.abilities, (3) if perk grants
    a stat bonus, subtract it from player_stats permanently. This is the most
    technically demanding part of the implementation.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
The key constraint is that you must LOSE a level to GAIN a level. This is never a free
lunch. Common use cases:
  - Player has Alcoholism L2 from drinking but never invested in Meth-Head, even though
    they've been hit by tox. La'Quisha lets them turn one of those Alcoholism levels into
    a Meth-Head L1 — better Meth Lab prep.
  - Player accidentally leveled Dismantling (from breaking items) but is going into
    the Meth Lab where Decontamination matters more. Trade 1 Dismantling level for
    Decontamination L1.
  - The tradeoff is genuine: high-level skills have the best perks at upper levels.
    Draining L3 to L2 loses the L3 perk. Players must weigh perk value carefully.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Professor Cracksworth (existing NPC): use Cracksworth to unlock a skill to L1,
    then use La'Quisha to trade a different skill — double skill manipulation in one
    penthouse visit.
  - Meth Lab entry: the Meth-Head, Chemical Warfare, and Decontamination skills are
    all best accessed through La'Quisha if the player has any potential_exp in them
    but zero levels (very common for Crack Den runs that barely triggered tox).

================================================================================
NPC 3 — THE PAWN MAN: RICO
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Rico runs a pawn operation out of a back room. He offers the player a deal: hand over
one equipped item (from any equipment slot), and he gives a replacement from his stock
— a randomly drawn item from the zone's loot tables, guaranteed to be from a slot the
player doesn't currently have filled. The tradeoff is that the player gives up
something known for a mystery item. High-risk, high-upside lateral equipment trade.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'R'  (Rico, pawn)
color = (200, 160, 80)  — dirty gold, pawn shop aesthetic

POSITION
--------
West wall, south quadrant, small alcove. A counter hazard in front.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "RICO'S PAWN — TRADE ONE PIECE (ONE-TIME)"

  Rico: "Bring me something good, I got something better. Maybe."

  Menu lists all currently equipped items across all slots:
    [Weapon]   Rusty Pipe (ATK +4)
    [Feet]     Sneakers (+1 STS)
    [Ring 1]   Ring of the Scholar (+2 BKS)
    etc.

  Player selects one item to trade in.

  After selection, Rico immediately produces 1 item from a CURATED PAWN POOL:
    - Item must be equippable (category="equipment")
    - Item must be drawn from the Meth Lab zone's normal loot tables (zone="meth_lab")
      to ensure the reward is Meth Lab-relevant
    - Item is drawn randomly with weights, same as normal loot generation
    - The traded-in item is removed from the player and unequipped
    - The new item is placed directly in inventory (not auto-equipped)
    - Message: "Rico: That'll do. Take this."

  On second bump: "Rico: We're square. Don't come back."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.rico_used: bool = False
  - On trade: unequip the selected item (set the equipment slot to None in engine),
    remove the item entity from inventory, then generate one random equippable item
    using loot.generate_floor_loot(zone="meth_lab", floor_num=1, ...) filtered to
    equippable items. Retry up to 10 times if result is non-equippable.
  - Do not restrict which slot the new item fills — the player gets what they get.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
The player gives up a known, quantified item for a random Meth Lab item. Meth Lab items
tend to be stronger overall (higher floor, harder enemies), so on average this is a
slight upgrade — but the variance is enormous. You might trade your best weapon for
boots. The risk is real.

Best use: trade a weak item you don't care about (a basic ring, starter weapon) for a
chance at a strong Meth Lab equippable. Players who intentionally enter the penthouse
with a low-value item to sacrifice will feel smart if the trade lands well.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Tyrone's shop: if the player buys a new gun from the Gun Dealer NPC, they free up
    their old weapon slot — then trade the old weapon to Rico for a Meth Lab random.
  - Stat Mirror (existing design): reroll stat spread before trading, then get a
    Meth Lab item that synergizes with the new spread.

================================================================================
NPC 4 — THE TATTOO ARTIST: PRECIOUS
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Precious runs a back-alley tattoo parlor. She offers the player a single permanent
body modification: choose one stat to boost by +1 permanently, but a different stat
(chosen randomly) takes a -1 penalty. The net stat total is unchanged, but the
distribution is forcibly shifted. Unlike the Stat Mirror (which lets you redistribute
at will), Precious forces a random penalty — you pick your gain, fate picks your loss.

This creates a tense gamble: players with a key stat already at the cap (12) cannot
raise it further, and getting the penalty on CON (affecting HP) or a combat stat is
genuinely painful.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'P'  (Precious, tattoo)
color = (180, 80, 160)  — purple/magenta, alternative aesthetic

POSITION
--------
Southwest alcove, small room. A chair hazard (hazard_type="chair", char='"') beside her.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "PRECIOUS TATTOO — ONE INK (FREE)"

  Precious: "I can put something on you that sticks. Your choice which. Not which hurts."

  Menu lists the 6 stats with current values:
    a) Constitution  [10]  — +1 to this
    b) Strength      [8]   — +1 to this
    c) Book-Smarts   [6]   — +1 to this
    d) Street-Smarts [12]  — +1 to this  (grayed out: ALREADY AT CAP)
    e) Tolerance     [9]   — +1 to this
    f) Swagger       [8]   — +1 to this

  Stats already at max (12 for the main 5; cap TBD for Swagger, suggest 12 also) are
  grayed out and unselectable. If all stats are at cap (theoretical edge case), Precious
  says "You're maxed out. Even I can't improve perfection." and does nothing.

  Player selects one stat to boost.

  Immediately after selection:
    - The chosen stat increases by +1 permanently (player_stats._base[stat] += 1).
    - The game randomly selects a DIFFERENT stat from the remaining 5 that is NOT
      already at its minimum (6 for main stats, or 1 for Swagger).
    - That stat decreases by -1 permanently.
    - Both changes are displayed simultaneously:
      "Constitution +1 | Tolerance -1"
      "Precious: The pain is part of it."

  engine flag: engine.precious_used = True.
  On second bump: "Precious: You're already inked. Come back next life."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.precious_used: bool = False
  - Stat caps for selection: cannot boost a stat already at 12.
  - Penalty selection: random.choice([stats except chosen]) filtered to those > minimum.
    Minimum for CON/STR/BKS/STS/TOL: 6. Minimum for Swagger: 1 (or 6 if unified).
  - Apply both changes via player_stats directly (not temporary bonuses) —
    same layer as character creation base stats.
  - HP recalculation: if CON changes, engine.player.max_hp should be recalculated
    (player.max_hp = 30 + player_stats.effective_constitution * 10).
  - If CON was chosen as the boost but current hp > new max somehow (edge case from
    penalty landing on CON of another path — impossible since they're different stats),
    handle gracefully.

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - The most extreme outcome: you boost STR by +1 and lose CON -1. This costs 10 max HP.
    For a player at 100 HP entering the Meth Lab, that is a 10% HP loss. Potentially
    very bad. Players must weigh this risk honestly.
  - The safest picks are stats you don't care about losing from: boosting BKS at the cost
    of risking TOL is relatively painless. Boosting CON at the risk of losing STR is a
    pure HP gain at the cost of slightly less melee damage.
  - Expected value is neutral (net 0 stat points) but variance is high. Skill-expressive.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Stat Mirror (existing design): use Stat Mirror FIRST to arrange your stats optimally,
    then use Precious to pick the safest possible boost. The Stat Mirror means you know
    exactly which stat you can afford to lose 1 from.
  - Repentance Pew (existing design): if the Pew gives +1 random stat, use Precious
    afterward — the total pool is now higher, making the Precious trade less painful.

================================================================================
NPC 5 — THE PHARMACIST: DR. PILL
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Dr. Pill is a shady unlicensed pharmacist who offers a free ability injection — but
the ability you receive is randomly drawn from the game's full ability registry (or a
curated "street pharmacy" subset of thematically appropriate abilities). The player
cannot choose which ability they get. They either take the mystery injection or decline.
No cash required. Pure risk: the ability could be incredibly powerful or fairly useless.

This parallels the Plug (random strain from a pick-5 list) but for abilities rather
than consumables, and with full randomness — you don't even get a menu of choices,
just a random result.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = '$'  (pill capsule silhouette, money sign as stand-in)
color = (80, 220, 120)   — hospital green

POSITION
--------
Southeast, small alcove. A shelf hazard (hazard_type="shelf", char=']') beside him.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: a yes/no confirmation only. No preview of what the ability is.

  Dr. Pill: "I got something that might help. Or might not. Fifty-fifty. You in?"
  [Y] Take the injection   [N] Decline

  If Y: a random ability is drawn from the PHARMACY_POOL (see below) and granted to the
  player via engine.grant_ability(ability_id) with ChargeType.PER_FLOOR or as designed.
  Message: "Dr. Pill: *injects* That's the stuff."
  The ability name and description are immediately displayed in a popup (same as the
  perk unlock popup pattern) so the player knows what they got.

  If N: "Dr. Pill: Your loss." No negative consequence.

  engine flag: engine.drpill_used = True.
  On second bump (whether they took it or not): "Dr. Pill: One dose per customer."

PHARMACY POOL — CURATED STREET ABILITY LIST
--------------------------------------------
A hardcoded list of ~15 abilities from the ABILITY_REGISTRY that make thematic and
mechanical sense as "street pharmacology injections." They should all be useful enough
that the player is tempted, and varied enough that the randomness matters.

Suggested pool (subject to what exists in ABILITY_REGISTRY by implementation time):
  1. warp               — instant teleport; escapability in Meth Lab
  2. smoke_screen       — visibility-blocking cloud; stealth utility
  3. rad_nova           — radiation burst on self + heal; Meth Lab synergy
  4. stim_pack          — speed boost; movement utility
  5. pandemic           — mass tox on visible enemies; Chemical Warfare synergy
  6. berserk            — damage boost at cost of defense; melee aggression
  7. force_push         — knockback ability; crowd control
  8. adrenaline_shot    — full HP heal or near-death recovery
  9. toxic_cloud        — places AOE tox tile; terrain control
  10. dimension_door    — short-range teleport variant
  11. armor_up          — temporary defense spike
  12. nerve_gas_grenade — heavy AOE damage; risky but powerful
  13. blood_frenzy      — leech on hit; sustain tool
  14. static_burst      — Electrodynamics synergy ability
  15. ice_nova          — Cryomancy synergy; Chebyshev AOE freeze

  Implementation note: the pool should be filtered at runtime to only include abilities
  that exist in ABILITY_REGISTRY — if some haven't been implemented yet, they are simply
  absent from the pool. The pool should have at least 5 entries to function.

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.drpill_used: bool = False
  - PHARMACY_POOL: list[str] = ["warp", "rad_nova", "pandemic", ...] — hardcoded list
    of ability IDs, filtered to keys present in ABILITY_REGISTRY at runtime.
  - random.choice(filtered_pool) selects the ability.
  - engine.grant_ability(chosen_id) — same call as any ability grant.
  - Show ability popup: same pattern as PERK_POPUP (MenuState.PERK_POPUP) with the
    ability's name and description. Player presses any key to dismiss.
  - If filtered pool is empty (all abilities missing): Pill says "I'm out of stock."
    engine flag still sets to True (he's "used up" even if nothing was given).

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - Full randomness is the whole design. Some injections (warp, pandemic) are high-value;
    others (armor_up) are situational. The player can decline, so this is never a trap.
  - Unlike the Plug, there is no browsing — no information before committing. The
    decision is purely "do I want a random ability?" which is a personality test as much
    as a strategy decision. Gambling builds and players who like variance will take it.
  - The ability arrives with its standard charge count (PER_FLOOR = refreshes, TOTAL =
    single-run uses, etc.) — no special scaling.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Meth-Head L2 (extra ability charges): if the player has Meth-Head perk that adds
    charges to abilities, Dr. Pill's random injection benefits from that too.
  - Ability-granting unique items: a player stacking multiple ability-grant sources can
    enter the Meth Lab with 3-4 abilities active, making Dr. Pill a key stop.

================================================================================
NPC 6 — THE FENCE: MARCUS
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Marcus is a information broker who deals in run intelligence. His service is uniquely
non-mechanical: he tells the player one of three true facts about the current run's
Meth Lab configuration — which faction has heavier presence, which enemy type spawns
most on floor 1, or what the first floor's loot table is biased toward. The player
picks which "intel package" they want. This is purely informational but in a roguelike,
information is power.

This parallels the Bulletin Board (existing design) but is interactive and run-specific
(the Bulletin Board is static educational text; Marcus gives run-specific intelligence).

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'M'  (Marcus, fence/broker)
color = (140, 140, 140)  — grey, nondescript, shady

POSITION
--------
Center-south area of the penthouse, standing alone — accessible from multiple angles.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "MARCUS — INTEL (PICK ONE)"

  Marcus: "I know what's down there. Worth more than anything Tyrone's selling."

  Menu offers exactly 3 intel options to pick from:

    a) FACTION REPORT
       Reveals: "Aldor presence is heavy on floor 1." or "Scryers outnumber Aldor
       this run." or "Faction activity is mixed." — derived from the Meth Lab's
       actual spawn tables by sampling which faction enemies were seeded into the
       zone's loot/spawn configuration.

    b) THREAT BRIEF
       Reveals: "The Purger patrols the first floor of the Meth Lab." or "Covid-26
       cases are dense on floor 1." — identifies which of the unique/dangerous enemy
       types is heaviest in the first Meth Lab floor spawn table.

    c) SUPPLY LINE
       Reveals: "Strain supply is heavy this run — expect lots of joints in the Meth
       Lab." or "Gunplay build — ammo drops are above average on floor 1." or "Cash
       is tight. Stock up here." — derived from loot table bias (which item categories
       have high aggregate weight in this run's meth_lab floor 1 table).

  Player presses a/b/c to receive that report.
  Report text is displayed in the message log and as a one-time popup.

  engine flag: engine.marcus_used = True.
  On second bump: "Marcus: I told you what I know. Move along."

MECHANICAL SPECIFICATION
------------------------
  - engine.marcus_used: bool = False
  - Each intel type derives its text from actual engine state at Penthouse time:
    a) Faction report: count Aldor vs. Scryer enemy types in ZONE_SPAWN_TABLES["meth_lab"]
       (or sample from engine's seeded randomness if the table is dynamic) and report
       whichever has higher aggregate spawn weight.
    b) Threat brief: find which of the "dangerous" enemy types (purger, covid_26,
       toxic_slug, sludge_amalgam, chemist) has the highest spawn_weight in
       ZONE_SPAWN_TABLES["meth_lab"][0] (floor 1 table).
    c) Supply line: sum weights by item category in loot.generate_floor_loot's internal
       table for meth_lab floor 1. Report the top category.
  - The intel is always TRUE — Marcus never lies. He is a fence, not a conman.
  - Implementation simplification if dynamic analysis is complex: pre-seed Marcus's
    three possible reports at zone generation time (generate_penthouse or
    generate_meth_lab entry point) and store as engine.marcus_reports: list[str].

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - Pure information; no mechanical effect. Cannot be imbalanced in the damage/HP sense.
  - Value varies by player knowledge. New players may not know what a "Purger" is, so
    the threat brief is confusing. The Bulletin Board (educational) and Marcus (run-
    specific) complement each other — Bulletin Board teaches the systems, Marcus applies
    them to THIS run.
  - The faction report directly informs whether the player should visit Smooth Carl
    (existing design) to buy reputation before descending.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Smooth Carl (existing design): Marcus's faction report tells you which faction to
    buy rep with. Perfect combo — Marcus gives intel, Carl sells the response.
  - Stat Mirror + Faction Report: if Aldor is dominant, maybe rebalance toward STS
    (for their stealth-sensitive enemies) before going down.
  - Bulletin Board: the Bulletin Board teaches what the Purger does; Marcus tells you
    if the Purger is actually down there this run. Use both.

================================================================================
NPC 7 — THE COACH: BIG JEROME
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
Big Jerome (not to be confused with floor-1's chicken-eating Jerome the enemy) is a
retired street brawler turned motivational coach. He offers the player a one-time
combat coaching session: a permanent flat boost to one of three combat-specific
mechanics — max HP, melee damage bonus, or dodge chance. The boost is small but
permanent. Jerome's coaching is the most straightforward of all seven NPCs: no
randomness, no tradeoffs, just a pure stat improvement where the player picks the
dimension that helps their build most.

Jerome is the "consolation NPC" for players who feel underprepared for the Meth Lab
but want a direct, non-gambling solution. He requires no items, no cash, no sacrifice.

CHAR / COLOR
------------
char = 'J'  (Jerome, coach)
color = (255, 200, 100)  — warm gold, encouragement

POSITION
--------
Center of the penthouse, south area — the most accessible NPC, easy to find.

INTERACTION FLOW
----------------
On first bump: menu titled "BIG JEROME — ONE SESSION (FREE)"

  Jerome: "You're not ready for what's down there. Let me fix that. Pick one."

  Menu offers 3 options, always the same:

    a) IRON CHIN
       "Increase your max HP by +15. Permanently."
       Effect: engine.player.max_hp += 15; engine.player.hp = min(engine.player.hp + 15,
       engine.player.max_hp) — heal up to the new max too.
       Also: player_stats.iron_chin_bonus = 15 (serialized field; added to max_hp calc
       at load time so save/load preserves it).

    b) MEAN HANDS
       "Your melee attacks deal +2 damage permanently."
       Effect: player_stats.melee_damage_bonus += 2.
       (melee_damage_bonus already proposed in engine state notes; if not yet implemented,
       this is its creation point. It is added to melee damage in combat.py's resolution.)

    c) GHOST STEP
       "Increase your dodge chance by +5%."
       Effect: player_stats.dodge_chance += 5.
       (dodge_chance already exists on PlayerStats — this simply boosts it permanently.)

BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------
  - Iron Chin is the most broadly useful (every build needs HP) — expect most players
    to take it. +15 HP = roughly 10% at CON 10. Meaningful, not game-breaking.
  - Mean Hands is best for melee builds (Stabbing/Beating/Slashing). +2 damage is small
    but permanent and doesn't require any resource.
  - Ghost Step is best for STS-heavy builds that already invest in dodge. +5% dodge at
    a base of 0% is minor; at 15% base it starts compounding nicely.
  - The lack of tradeoff is intentional. Jerome is the "safety valve" NPC — for players
    who need a guaranteed improvement, not a gamble. All other NPCs involve some variance
    or sacrifice. Jerome does not.

SYNERGIES
---------
  - Precious (NPC 4 above): Precious might randomly penalize CON (-1 = -10 HP).
    Jerome's +15 HP from Iron Chin is a direct hedge against Precious's worst outcome.
    Players can visit Precious first, and if CON gets penalized, take Jerome's Iron Chin
    to compensate partially.
  - Mutation system: Meth Lab mutations can permanently reduce stats. Ghost Step's
    dodge bonus is unaffected by mutations (it's a flat dodge %, not a stat). This makes
    it a mutation-proof defense investment.
  - Big Mama's Penthouse Plate (existing): Plate gives +HoT. Iron Chin raises the max HP
    that HoT tops off toward. Pair for maximum effective HP recovery before descent.
