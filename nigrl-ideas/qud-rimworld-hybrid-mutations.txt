QUD x RIMWORLD HYBRID MUTATIONS — ADDITIONAL CANDIDATES (10)
=============================================================
NIGRL Roguelike — gritty urban setting (Crack Den / Meth Lab)
Design goal: 10 additional mutations that combine Caves of Qud's gameplay
verb philosophy (what you CAN DO) with Rimworld Biotech's cost structure
philosophy (how you PAY for it). The result should feel like something
neither game has alone.

Author note: These mutations supplement mutation-overhaul-plan.txt and
rimworld-inspired-mutations.txt (total candidate pool: 100 before these).
Each entry is a candidate for its named category. Do not add any entry
that duplicates the existing 100. Format and upgrade principles match
the main overhaul plan.

THE HYBRID DESIGN PRINCIPLE:
  Qud gives us verbs — movement as weapon, terrain creation, possession,
  discharge arcs, corrosive auras, mass-mind scaling, glimmer accumulation.
  Rimworld gives us cost structures — spontaneous chaos, cognitive decay,
  escalating punishments, environmental inversion, the thing that hunts you.
  Neither game alone would produce these. The hybrid is the point.

Upgrade principles:
  - Good mutations get STRONGER on upgrade.
  - Bad mutations get WORSE on upgrade.
  - Typical cap: L3.
  - All mutations permanent; Shed removes entirely and resets level to 0.
  - Scarred Tissue fires on bad mutation upgrades (consolation +1 stat).

Distribution: 3 Weak Good, 2 Weak Bad, 3 Strong Good, 2 Strong Bad.

I. WEAK GOOD MUTATIONS (3)
================================================================

Design principle: These grant a new gameplay verb tied to an existing
mechanic (movement, electricity, enemy clustering) that creates new
positioning incentives without being build-defining on their own.

--

1. HORN GROWTH — "A bony ridge has erupted from your forehead. You can
   feel it wanting to hit things."
   Qud inspiration: Horns — charge attack (move 3+ tiles straight,
   bonus impact damage on the enemy you collide with).
   Rimworld inspiration: Unstoppable gene — cannot be slowed during
   movement; motion is commitment.
   Hybrid thesis: Movement becomes a weapon, creating a reason to
   stand far from enemies before engaging rather than closing directly.
   Effect: When the player moves 3 or more tiles in a single straight
   line (cardinal or diagonal, same direction each step) and ends that
   movement adjacent to an enemy, the final step triggers a Horn Charge
   instead of a normal move. The charge deals bonus damage equal to
   (distance_traveled + mutation_count // 4), added on top of normal
   melee damage. The charge requires no extra action — it resolves as
   part of the movement turn. If a wall or entity breaks the line before
   3 tiles, no charge fires.
   Tracking: engine.horn_charge_dir (tuple|None, reset each turn) and
   engine.horn_charge_distance (int, reset each turn). After each player
   move step, check whether the step continued in the same direction as
   the previous step. If 3+ steps in one direction end adjacent to an
   enemy, fire the charge bonus.
   Additionally, while a Horn Charge is available (i.e., during any
   turn where the player has already moved 3+ straight tiles and is now
   adjacent to an enemy), the player cannot be slowed by grease tiles
   or SlowEffect on that turn — momentum cannot be interrupted mid-charge.
   Upgrade:
     L1: 3+ straight-line tiles to charge; bonus dmg = distance +
         mutation_count//4; slow-immunity only on the charging turn.
     L2: Threshold drops to 2 straight-line tiles to trigger a charge.
         Bonus dmg = distance*2 + mutation_count//4. If the charge kills
         the enemy, the player may immediately take 1 free movement step
         (the momentum carries them through).
     L3: Threshold stays at 2 tiles. Bonus dmg = distance*2 +
         mutation_count//3. The free-step-on-kill is retained. Additionally,
         a successful charge stuns the target for 2 turns (the impact is
         bone-cracking). Cap.
   Reversal: Remove horn_charge_dir/horn_charge_distance tracking;
   remove the charge damage bonus; remove slow-immunity on charge turns.
   Shed only removes the charge bonus — movement itself is unaffected.
   System hooks: engine.py player movement path (per-step direction
   tracking), combat.py melee hit (charge bonus damage application),
   effects.py SlowEffect modify_movement check (skip if horn_charging
   flag is set this turn), engine.horn_charge_dir: tuple|None,
   engine.horn_charge_distance: int, player_stats.horn_growth_active: bool.
   --

2. CHAIN DISCHARGE — "Static builds up in your fingertips. You point,
   and it goes looking for the next closest thing to fry."
   Qud inspiration: Electrical Generation — arc that chains through
   conductive targets in clusters; more enemies = more chains.
   Rimworld inspiration: Environmental interaction design — crowd
   dynamics as a resource, rewarding the player for not isolating enemies.
   Hybrid thesis: Grants a crowd-punisher ability that rewards fighting
   groups instead of picking enemies off one at a time — the opposite of
   normal roguelike instinct.
   Effect: Grants the Chain Discharge ability (INFINITE charges,
   SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS target). Fire an electrical bolt at one visible
   enemy within 6 tiles. Deals 7 + (mutation_count // 3) shock damage
   and applies 2 stacks of "shocked" to the primary target. If a second
   enemy is within 2 tiles of the primary target, the bolt chains to
   them for 4 + (mutation_count // 4) shock damage and 1 shocked stack.
   Each chain can itself spawn another chain under the same rules (max
   chains: 1 at L1). Cooldown: 5 turns. Enemies already shocked take
   +2 bonus damage from the discharge (the current amplifies in a
   wet/shocked body).
   Upgrade:
     L1: Primary bolt 7 + count//3 dmg, 2 shocked; chain to 1 enemy
         within 2 tiles for 4 + count//4, 1 shocked; max 1 chain;
         5t cooldown.
     L2: Primary bolt 9 + count//3 dmg; chain range extends to 3 tiles;
         max chains increases to 2 (the bolt can bounce twice). Cooldown
         drops to 4 turns. Each chain now also applies 1 stack of "wet"
         to the chained target (electricity burns through their tissues,
         leaving them damp — the smell is awful).
     L3: Primary bolt 12 + count//3 dmg; chain range 3 tiles; max chains
         3. Cooldown 3 turns. Any enemy that dies while shocked immediately
         discharges an additional 1-chain burst centered on their corpse
         (death discharge: same chain rules but 3 tiles range and 1 chain
         max; fires once per death). Cap.
   Reversal: Revoke "chain_discharge" ability entirely.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "chain_discharge", ChargeType.INFINITE
   with cooldown, spells.py execute (LOS shot, chain resolver that BFS
   outward from hit target checking for other entities within range,
   apply shocked/wet effects), effects.py ShockedEffect (already exists),
   WetEffect (already exists), engine.chain_discharge_targets_hit: set
   (per-cast; prevents re-hitting same entity in one chain sequence).
   --

3. MASS RESONANCE — "The more of them there are, the more of you there
   is. You can feel them feeding it."
   Qud inspiration: Mass Mind — psychic power scales with number of
   nearby intelligent creatures; proximity becomes amplification.
   Rimworld inspiration: Environmental crowd dynamics and Kill Thirst's
   "I need enemies near me" reversal of standard safety instinct.
   Hybrid thesis: Rewards being surrounded — the exact opposite of normal
   roguelike positioning instinct — by turning enemy density into a stat
   multiplier that recalculates each turn.
   Effect: For each enemy within 4 tiles of the player, gain +1 temporary
   melee power bonus (recalculated every turn, stored as
   player_stats.mass_resonance_power_bonus: int). At 3 or more enemies
   in range, also gain +10 speed. At 5 or more enemies in range, gain
   +1 DR. These bonuses vanish the moment enemies leave the radius or die
   — there is no lag or grace period at L1. The bonuses are calculated at
   the start of the player's turn before their action resolves.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Radius 4 tiles; +1 power per enemy; +10 speed at 3+ enemies;
         +1 DR at 5+ enemies. Bonuses reset immediately when count drops.
     L2: Radius 5 tiles; +2 power per enemy (bonus doubles); +20 speed
         at 3+ enemies; +2 DR at 5+ enemies. Bonuses still reset immediately.
         Additionally, at 4+ enemies in range, your melee attacks briefly
         stagger all adjacent enemies after hitting your primary target:
         stagger applies a 1-turn debuff (MassResonanceStaggerEffect) that
         reduces those adjacent enemies' next attack damage by 2 (the
         psychic pressure disrupts their motor control for a moment).
     L3: Radius 6 tiles; +2 power per enemy; +30 speed at 3+ enemies;
         +3 DR at 5+ enemies. At 4+ enemies in range, your melee attacks
         cleave: damage the primary target AND all enemies immediately
         adjacent to you (standard melee damage, no bonus to cleave
         targets). The L2 stagger is replaced by cleave at L3 (stagger
         no longer applies). Additionally, when an enemy dies and exits
         the radius, the lost power bonus doesn't vanish for 3 turns
         (resonance echo: a ghost of the mass lingers, tracked as
         engine.mass_resonance_echo_turns: int, per-turn decrement). Cap.
   Reversal: Remove mass_resonance_active flag; zero power bonus, speed
   bonus, DR bonus immediately.
   System hooks: player_stats.mass_resonance_power_bonus: int,
   player_stats.mass_resonance_speed_bonus: int,
   player_stats.mass_resonance_dr_bonus: int,
   player_stats.mass_resonance_active: bool, engine.py player-turn-start
   (count enemies in radius, set bonuses), combat.py melee hit (cleave
   at L3 — hit all adjacent entities in one pass),
   engine.mass_resonance_echo_turns: int (L3; per-turn decrement; if > 0
   keep power bonus from last count frozen).


================================================================
II. WEAK BAD MUTATIONS (2)
================================================================

Design principle: These impose information and timing penalties that
alter your play pattern without simply reducing stats. The best ones
create a specific thing you fear that other builds would ignore entirely.

--

4. AMNESIAC PULSES — "The radiation ate something in your temporal lobe.
   You keep re-discovering rooms you've been in."
   Qud inspiration: Amnesia defect — periodically forgets map exploration
   in an area around the player, forcing re-scouting.
   Rimworld inspiration: Slow Learning and cognitive degradation genes —
   losing knowledge you already earned is more punishing than never
   having it, because it violates your model of safety.
   Hybrid thesis: An information-decay mutation that removes knowledge
   rather than stats, punishing backtracking and forcing the player to
   treat explored areas as unpredictably dangerous again.
   Effect: Every 30 turns (tracked as engine.amnesiac_pulse_counter: int,
   per-floor, resets each floor), a pulse fires. The pulse un-explores a
   6-tile radius around the player's current position — all tiles in that
   radius are reset from "explored" to "unexplored" in dungeon.explored.
   They return to fog of war. Enemies in those tiles become hidden again
   (the player loses the last-seen tile memory for those entities). Items
   on tiles in the un-explored radius also become invisible until re-stepped.
   The pulse fires BEFORE the player's action resolves. Message displayed:
   "Your memory blurs. The map recedes."
   Note: The FOV tiles the player can CURRENTLY SEE are not un-explored —
   only tiles outside current FOV within the 6-tile pulse radius are
   affected. Tiles in current FOV were re-confirmed this turn; the amnesia
   only erases what you remembered, not what you can see right now.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Every 30 turns; 6-tile un-explore radius; tiles currently in FOV
         are NOT un-explored. Items on affected tiles go hidden.
     L2: Timer drops to every 22 turns. Radius extends to 8 tiles. Also
         un-explores any item-location markers (items on the fog tiles
         disappear from memory as before). Additionally, the pulse also
         clears the player's entity memory for enemies that were last-seen
         in the pulse radius (engine.last_seen_positions dict cleared for
         those entities), meaning enemies can surprise you from areas you
         thought you'd cleared.
     L3: Timer drops to every 15 turns. Radius 10 tiles. The pulse now also
         has a 30% chance to apply 1-turn confusion to the player (the
         amnesia scrambles spatial orientation momentarily). The confusion
         applies after the un-explore, so the player may walk the wrong way
         during the first confused turn. Cap.
   Reversal: Remove amnesiac_pulse_counter, remove amnesiac_pulses_active
   flag. The already-cleared fog does not return — only future pulses are
   stopped. The player keeps whatever state the dungeon.explored dict is in
   at the moment of Shed.
   System hooks: player_stats.amnesiac_pulses_active: bool,
   engine.amnesiac_pulse_counter: int (per-floor),
   engine.py player-turn-start (increment counter, check threshold, fire
   un-explore pass over dungeon.explored), dungeon.py (provide a
   radius-clear helper for explored tiles), effects.py ConfuseEffect
   (already exists; apply at L3 30% chance), engine.last_seen_positions:
   dict[str, tuple] (entity_id -> last seen (x,y); already exists or add
   for L2 enemy-memory clearing).
   --

5. SPONTANEOUS COMBUSTION — "Your body temperature has opinions. Strong
   ones. Unpredictable ones."
   Qud inspiration: Spontaneous Combustion defect — the player randomly
   catches fire with no ability to prevent or predict it.
   Rimworld inspiration: Fire Spew gene's "dangerous to everyone including
   yourself" identity + Impid's environmental chaos that makes the player
   a liability as much as an asset.
   Hybrid thesis: Chaos timer that damages you but ALSO punishes nearby
   enemies — the bad effect has real upside in melee range, creating a
   genuine decision about how close you want to be when the timer fires.
   Effect: Every 20 turns (tracked as engine.combustion_counter: int,
   per-floor, resets each floor), roll 30%. On success, the player
   spontaneously ignites: apply IgniteEffect (3 turns, 2 fire damage/turn)
   to the player, AND deal 3 splash fire damage to all entities immediately
   adjacent to the player at the moment of ignition (no save, no roll —
   proximity is punishment and reward alike). Message: "You burst into
   flame without warning." The timer resets to 0 after each check whether
   or not combustion fires.
   You cannot control when this happens. The splash damage is the ONLY
   upside and requires you to be in melee range when it fires, which is
   precisely when fire damage to yourself is most dangerous. The tension
   is the design.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Every 20 turns, 30% combustion; 2 fire dmg/turn for 3t; 3 dmg
         splash to all adjacent entities; timer resets after check.
     L2: Timer drops to every 15 turns, 40% chance. Player fire damage
         increases to 3/turn for 4 turns. Splash damage increases to 4.
         Additionally, if combustion fires while the player is below 50%
         HP, the ignite duration extends to 6 turns (the weakened body
         burns longer). The HP check fires at the moment of combustion.
     L3: Timer drops to every 10 turns, 50% chance. Player fire dmg 4/turn
         for 5 turns. Splash increases to 5. The low-HP extended burn from
         L2 is retained (6 turns if below 50% HP). Additionally, at L3 the
         combustion can no longer be extinguished by the WetEffect — the
         mutation burns hot enough to steam off moisture. If IgniteEffect
         would be removed by a Wet application while Spontaneous Combustion
         is active, the Wet is consumed but the Ignite remains. Cap.
   Note: The existing fire hazard (entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="fire")
   already deals fire damage per turn to entities standing on it. This
   mutation adds a second, player-internal ignite source that uses the same
   IgniteEffect. The L3 wet-immunity is a flag on the ignite effect applied
   by this mutation only — combustion_ignite: bool on the IgniteEffect
   instance signals the wet-purge logic to skip removal.
   Reversal: Remove spontaneous_combustion_active flag; zero out the counter.
   If currently ignited by combustion, the ignite is NOT removed on Shed —
   the existing effect runs its duration.
   System hooks: player_stats.spontaneous_combustion_active: bool,
   engine.combustion_counter: int (per-floor), engine.py player-turn-start
   (counter increment, roll check, apply ignite to player + splash to
   adjacents), effects.py IgniteEffect (add combustion_ignite: bool field;
   L3 wet-immunity check), combat.py (splash fire damage helper — same
   pattern as existing AoE damage passes).


================================================================
III. STRONG GOOD MUTATIONS (3)
================================================================

Design principle: Strong good mutations should define or enable a build
identity. Each one creates a completely new gameplay verb that no other
system in NIGRL currently provides.

--

6. FORCE BARRICADE — "You think hard enough and the air solidifies.
   It's not pretty, but it holds."
   Qud inspiration: Force Wall — create a temporary wall of energy that
   blocks movement and projectiles; terrain creation as a tactical verb.
   Rimworld inspiration: Foam Spray's tactical utility + charge-gated
   ability design; resource-constrained terrain alteration.
   Hybrid thesis: Terrain creation is completely new to NIGRL. No other
   mutation, item, or ability creates obstacles. This introduces one of
   the most tactically rich verbs in the genre — controlling space.
   Effect: Grants the Force Barricade ability (1 charge/floor,
   ADJACENT_TILE target). The player targets an adjacent tile. A 3-tile
   line of force-wall entities spawns at that tile, oriented perpendicular
   to the direction from the player to the target tile (i.e., if you
   target the tile to your north, the wall runs east-west through that
   tile). Wall tiles are entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="force_wall",
   char='#', color=(180, 180, 255). They block movement for all entities
   including the player. They block gun shots (bullets cannot pass through
   them). They persist for 12 turns then expire and are removed. Enemies
   that pathfind into a force wall tile are blocked and must route around.
   Lasts 12 turns. If a force wall tile would spawn in a wall tile, that
   wall segment is skipped (the barricade has gaps in corners).
   Upgrade:
     L1: 1 charge/floor; 3-tile wall; 12 turns duration; blocks movement
         and gun shots; perpendicular orientation only.
     L2: 2 charges/floor; wall extends to 5 tiles; duration increases to
         18 turns. Additionally, enemies that attempt to move into a force
         wall tile take 3 psychic damage (the barrier pushes back — not
         a stun, just damage). This makes the wall an active deterrent
         rather than just an obstacle.
     L3: 2 charges/floor (unchanged). Wall extends to 5 tiles. Duration
         24 turns. The barrier damage increases to 6. Additionally, if
         the player is inside the closed area formed by the barricade
         plus existing walls (i.e., fully behind cover), they gain +2 DR
         for as long as the barricade remains active (cover bonus, tracked
         as engine.force_barricade_cover: bool, recalculated each turn by
         checking whether all 8 player-adjacent directions are blocked by
         wall, force wall, or map edge). Cap.
   Reversal: Revoke "force_barricade" ability. Existing force wall entities
   on the map expire naturally — they are not removed on Shed, since they
   are dungeon entities at that point. The ability simply cannot be used again.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "force_barricade", ChargeType.PER_FLOOR,
   spells.py execute (spawn 3 or 5 force_wall entities in a perpendicular
   line relative to player→target direction vector, check dungeon.is_wall()
   before each spawn), hazards.py (create_force_wall(x, y, duration)
   factory function), dungeon.py is_blocked() must recognize force_wall
   entities as blocking, gun_system.py LOS check must treat force_wall
   tiles as blocking, engine.py tick (decrement force wall durations, remove
   expired walls, recalculate cover bonus at L3),
   engine.force_barricade_cover: bool (L3 per-turn).
   --

7. BODY JACK — "You reach into their skull with something that isn't
   your hand. For a few seconds, you ARE them."
   Qud inspiration: Domination — possess an enemy's body and control it
   directly; the player's consciousness transfers, leaving their real
   body vulnerable.
   Rimworld inspiration: Psychic Bonding risk/reward — your original body
   is inert and exposed while you're absent; power requires vulnerability.
   Hybrid thesis: The most complex new verb in the document. Use an enemy
   as a vehicle — their stats, their position, their HP as a shield. But
   your body sits still while you're gone, creating genuine tension and
   the need for careful positioning before activation.
   Effect: Grants the Body Jack ability (1 charge/floor, SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS,
   range 5 tiles). Target a visible enemy within 5 tiles. You possess their
   body for 8 turns. While possessing:
     - The player's active position becomes the possessed enemy's position.
       All player movement, melee attacks, and ability use fires from the
       possessed entity's location using THEIR stats (their power, their
       defense, their speed).
     - Your real body goes dormant at your original position. It cannot act.
       It can be attacked by enemies. If your dormant body takes any damage,
       possession ends immediately and the damage is applied to your real HP.
     - If the possessed enemy takes lethal damage while you control them,
       possession ends immediately: that entity dies, and the player returns
       to their dormant body position with no damage. The enemy kill counts
       (XP, loot, gold) for the player.
     - On natural possession end (8 turns), the possessed entity is stunned
       for 3 turns and takes 5 psychic damage.
   Implementation note: This requires a possession_active: bool flag on
   engine and engine.possessed_entity_id: str|None. During a possessed turn,
   player input is routed to move/attack the possessed entity instead of the
   player entity. The player entity has its ai_type temporarily set to
   "dormant" (new AI mode: do nothing, just sit; enemy attacks are resolved
   against it normally). This is architecturally non-trivial — see Section V.
   Upgrade:
     L1: 1 charge/floor; 5-tile range; 8-turn possession; exit stun 3t,
         5 psychic dmg; dormant body vulnerable.
     L2: 1 charge/floor; 6-tile range; 12-turn possession; exit stun 5t,
         8 psychic dmg. Additionally, if you kill any enemy while possessing
         the host, those kills grant you the XP and gold as normal. This is
         clarified in L2 because L1 may be ambiguous — kills credit the player.
     L3: 2 charges/floor; 7-tile range; 15-turn possession; exit stun 5t,
         10 psychic dmg. Additionally, if the possessed target DIES while
         you control them (lethal damage taken), you absorb their remaining
         psychic energy: gain temp HP equal to 30% of their max HP (the death
         shock transfers to you). This makes intentional "sacrifice the host"
         a viable tactic. Cap.
   Reversal: Revoke "body_jack" ability. If possession is currently active
   when Shed fires, possession ends immediately and all normal exit conditions
   apply (stun, psychic damage to host). The player returns to their real body.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "body_jack", ChargeType.PER_FLOOR,
   spells.py execute (target valid, set possession_active flag, set dormant
   marker on player entity), engine.py input routing (check possession_active,
   redirect player commands to possessed entity), engine.py turn tick
   (decrement possession turns, handle exit condition), ai.py "dormant"
   AI mode (skip turn, allow incoming attacks), combat.py death handler
   (check if dying entity is possessed; if so, end possession, optionally
   apply L3 temp HP), entity.py possessed_by_player: bool field (used to
   skip the entity from normal AI processing while possessed).
   engine.possession_active: bool, engine.possessed_entity_id: str|None,
   engine.possession_turns_remaining: int.
   --

8. CORROSIVE AURA — "Something seeps from your pores. It eats metal.
   It eats skin. Not yours, though."
   Qud inspiration: Corrosive Gas Generation — persistent area that
   strips enemy defense over time; the generator is immune to their own
   product.
   Rimworld inspiration: Pollution Rush environmental inversion — thrive
   in your own hazard while everything else withers; the zone around you
   becomes hostile territory for everyone but you.
   Hybrid thesis: A passive debuff aura that stacks defense reduction on
   enemies who stay close — rewarding melee builds that want enemies at
   range 1, and punishing enemies that cluster around the player.
   Effect: Passive. Emit a corrosive zone in a 3-tile radius around the
   player at all times (no activation required). Each turn, ALL enemies
   within the radius take 1 stack of CorrosiveAuraEffect (new: -1 defense
   per stack, cap -5 stacks). You are fully immune to your own aura.
   The stacks persist on the enemy for 4 turns after they leave the radius
   (the corrosion lingers on their body). If an enemy reaches -5 defense
   stacks, they take 2 acid damage per turn FOR AS LONG AS THEY REMAIN
   WITHIN THE RADIUS (the corrosion has eaten through enough to hurt). The
   damage and defense reduction are tracked via CorrosiveAuraEffect on each
   enemy individually.
   Note: The "-1 defense per stack" means an enemy with 3 defense takes
   only 3 hits before reaching effective 0 defense; at -5 stacks their
   "effective defense" is their base defense minus 5 (can go negative,
   making them take more damage than normal). This is aggressive against
   high-DR enemies specifically — the aura punishes tanks.
   Upgrade:
     L1: 3-tile radius; -1 DR/stack per turn in radius; cap -5; 4-turn
         stack persistence after leaving radius; 2 acid dmg/turn at -5 stacks.
     L2: Radius extends to 4 tiles. Stack cap increases to -7 DR. Acid
         damage at cap increases to 3/turn. Additionally, the corrosive
         residue you leave behind persists on the floor: tiles the player
         stood on for 2+ consecutive turns become CorrosiveTile hazards for
         4 turns (dungeon.corrosive_tiles: dict[tuple, int] = {(x,y): turns_remaining};
         any enemy stepping on a corrosive tile takes 1 stack of CorrosiveAuraEffect
         at the start of their turn on that tile). This makes your movement
         path dangerous for enemies.
     L3: Radius 5 tiles. Stack cap -8 DR. Acid damage at cap 4/turn.
         Corrosive tiles persist (L2). Additionally, enemies at the -5+
         stack threshold (not just cap) that die within the radius explode
         in a corrosive burst: 3-tile radius of their corpse, deal 4 acid
         damage to all entities in range (enemies only — not the player,
         who is immune). This creates chain-reaction potential in clustered
         fights. Cap.
   Reversal: Remove corrosive_aura_active flag. Existing CorrosiveAuraEffect
   stacks on enemies expire naturally — they are not purged on Shed. Corrosive
   tiles on the dungeon floor also expire naturally.
   System hooks: player_stats.corrosive_aura_active: bool,
   engine.py turn tick (for each enemy within radius, apply/refresh
   CorrosiveAuraEffect or increment stack count), effects.py
   CorrosiveAuraEffect (new: tracks stacks as self.stacks: int; each
   tick decrements duration; on tick apply acid damage if stacks >= 5;
   modify_incoming_damage subtracts stacks from defender.defense),
   dungeon.py corrosive_tiles: dict[tuple, int] (L2; cleared on floor change),
   engine.py player move (L2: check if player stood on tile 2+ turns;
   create corrosive tile entry), combat.py (L3: on enemy death, check if
   in radius and stacks >= 5, apply corrosive burst AOE).


================================================================
IV. STRONG BAD MUTATIONS (2)
================================================================

Design principle: Strong bad mutations should create escalating, inescapable
pressure that permanently changes how you play the run. The best ones are
things you can work around but never ignore.

--

9. EVIL TWIN — "There's another you. It's angrier. It knows where you sleep."
   Qud inspiration: Evil Twin defect — a hostile copy of you spawns on the
   map and hunts you exclusively; no enemy AI, just pursuit.
   Rimworld inspiration: Aggressive/Hyper-Aggressive gene escalation + Kill
   Thirst's "something is hunting you" constant background tension; the threat
   that scales with how dangerous you've become.
   Hybrid thesis: An uncounterable procedural threat that scales with your
   power (because the Twin has your stats). The more dangerous you become,
   the more dangerous your shadow becomes. Equipment slot constraint makes
   it a physical manifestation, not just a mechanic tax.
   Effect: At the start of each floor, one Shadow Twin spawns at a random
   map location. The Twin is a new enemy entity (enemy_type="shadow_twin")
   with the player's current melee power, defense, and 70% of the player's
   max HP. It is hostile to the player only — it ignores all other enemies
   and cannot be provoked by them. AI mode: CHASING (always; ignores FearEffect,
   ConfuseEffect, StunEffect has full duration but does not prevent subsequent
   chase). The Twin always spawns — there is no way to prevent it.
   On kill: gain temp HP equal to 5 + (mutation_levels["evil_twin"] * 3)
   and +1 melee power for the rest of the floor (absorbing the shadow's energy).
   Equipment constraint: Cannot equip the hat slot (the Shadow Twin maintains a
   spiritual link through your exposed head; covering it severs the link but
   costs you your shadow's kill reward potential — actually: no hat means the
   player suffers a passive -2 SWG while this mutation is active because the
   exposed head is a vulnerability, NOT because wearing a hat is forbidden.
   The mechanical constraint is: player_stats.evil_twin_swagger_penalty = -2,
   permanently active; hats still work but this penalty does not go away).
   Note: The flavor of "can't wear hat" was compelling but mechanically
   hard to enforce without UI complications. Instead: the -2 SWG reflects
   the exposed, vulnerable head. The player can wear a hat if they want
   but the penalty remains.
   If the Twin lands 3 successful hits on the player during a floor, it
   steals 1 permanent point from a random stat (engine randomly picks a
   stat from the six and decrements its _base value by 1; min 1 for any
   stat; displayed in message log: "The twin takes something from you.").
   The Twin ALWAYS spawns. You must kill it or outlast it.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Twin has 70% player max HP; 3 hits needed for stat theft; on-kill
         reward: +5 temp HP, +1 melee power (floor); -2 SWG passive penalty.
     L2: Twin has 100% player max HP. Hits required for stat theft drops to 2.
         On-kill reward unchanged (+5 temp HP, +1 melee power). Additionally,
         the Twin now mimics the player's highest stat at a 50% bonus (+2 to
         whatever the player's highest stat is — this is a dynamic calculation
         each time the Twin is spawned). The Twin becomes competent at what
         you're competent at.
     L3: Twin has 130% player max HP, +2 melee power bonus over the player's
         current power. Stat theft on 1 hit. On-kill reward increases: +10 temp
         HP, +2 melee power (floor). The Twin is now your mirror at its worst.
         Additionally, if the Twin is alive when the player reaches the stairs,
         it BLOCKS the exit — the player cannot descend until the Twin is killed.
         The Twin moves to the stair tile and occupies it. This enforces a final
         confrontation. Cap.
   Reversal (Shed): Remove evil_twin_swagger_penalty, remove spawn logic.
   The Twin currently on the floor is not removed — it persists until killed
   or the floor is exited. Future floors spawn no Twin. The existing Twin
   can still steal stats if it lands hits before dying.
   System hooks: enemies.py MONSTER_REGISTRY "shadow_twin" (special spawn
   entry; power/defense/hp derived from player at spawn time via
   engine.player_stats context; spawn_weight=0; only spawned by mutation
   system), engine.py floor-start (check evil_twin_active, spawn Shadow Twin),
   ai.py (CHASING always, immunity to confuse/fear direction change; stun still
   applies duration but target resumes chase after), combat.py player death /
   stat-theft handler (engine.evil_twin_hits_this_floor: int; on 3/2/1 hits,
   decrement random stat _base), engine.py stair-exit check (L3: block if
   shadow_twin entity alive on this floor), player_stats.evil_twin_swagger_penalty: int.

10. GLIMMER BUILDUP — "They can feel you thinking. Every time you flex
    that gift, something in the dark takes notice."
    Qud inspiration: Esper Glimmer system — every use of a mental mutation
    or ability accumulates Glimmer, which attracts increasingly dangerous
    psychic-hunter enemies; the more power you use, the more you advertise
    yourself.
    Rimworld inspiration: Freak Magnet's "mutations attract enemies" +
    Kill Thirst's escalating consequence structure; punishment that compounds
    rather than resets.
    Hybrid thesis: Directly punishes ability-heavy builds in a game where
    abilities are otherwise always good. Creating synergistic conflict with
    Psychic Amplifier (Strong Good, which rewards ability use) — having both
    creates a volatile push-pull that is the design goal. The Glimmer counter
    NEVER resets and NEVER decreases.
    Effect: Each time the player fires ANY ability from ABILITY_REGISTRY,
    engine.glimmer_stacks (run-persistent int, never resets, never decreases)
    increments by 1. At specific thresholds, a special Glimmer Hunter enemy
    spawns on the CURRENT floor immediately:
      Threshold 5:  1 Glimmer Scout spawns (fast, low HP, AI mode ALERTING —
                    it chases you AND alerts every other enemy on the floor
                    to your position the turn it spots you).
      Threshold 10: 1 Glimmer Stalker spawns (medium, CHASING, has a psychic
                    bolt ability: SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS, 6 range, 5 dmg bypassing
                    armor, on hit applies "silenced" debuff for 3 turns —
                    new SilencedEffect: prevents ability use for duration).
      Threshold 15: 1 Glimmer Reaper spawns (strong, CHASING, AoE psychic
                    scream ability: 3-tile radius AOE around itself, 4 dmg to
                    all entities in range, applies ConfuseEffect for 4 turns
                    to all affected).
      Each 5 beyond 20: 1 additional Glimmer Reaper spawns.
    All Glimmer Hunters are hostile to the player only (ignore other enemies).
    They spawn at a random unblocked tile more than 5 tiles from the player.
    Glimmer Scouts can spot the player at 8 tiles range (larger than normal
    sight radius) — the psychic sensitivity makes them acute.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Thresholds: 5/10/15/+5; standard hunter stats above.
      L2: Thresholds drop to 3/7/12/+4. Glimmer Hunters gain +1 defense and
          +1 power. Glimmer Scouts additionally have a 20% chance on alerting
          to permanently reduce the player's next-ability charge by 1 (the
          alert disrupts your concentration — charge loss is permanent for
          that ability's current charge count).
      L3: Thresholds drop to 2/5/9/+3. Hunters gain +2 defense and +2 power.
          Glimmer Reapers now have a 20% chance on death to split: spawn a
          second Reaper at the death location (the psychic rupture of death
          echoes). This means killing a Reaper can beget another Reaper with
          20% probability. Additionally, at L3 the SilencedEffect from Stalkers
          now lasts 5 turns instead of 3. Cap.
    Note: This mutation has direct synergistic conflict with Psychic Amplifier
    (Strong Good — amplifies ability damage/range). A player with both gets
    stronger abilities AND more frequent Glimmer Hunters. The tension between
    these two mutations is intentional and creates the most interesting build
    decision in the mutation system. It is not a trap — it is a risk/reward
    dial. The player who manages their ability use carefully can leverage both.
    Reversal (Shed): Remove glimmer_buildup_active flag. glimmer_stacks value
    is NOT reset — the psychic signature is already out there. Future ability
    uses no longer increment the counter. Hunter spawning logic is disabled.
    Any Glimmer Hunters currently on the floor remain until killed.
    System hooks: player_stats.glimmer_buildup_active: bool,
    engine.glimmer_stacks: int (run-persistent; serialized in save),
    abilities.py or engine.py ability fire path (_fire_ability or equivalent:
    after successful ability execution, check glimmer_buildup_active and
    increment glimmer_stacks, then check thresholds and spawn accordingly),
    enemies.py MONSTER_REGISTRY "glimmer_scout", "glimmer_stalker",
    "glimmer_reaper" (spawn_weight=0; stats defined; glimmer_scout AI
    mode: alert_on_sight_then_chase; glimmer_stalker: CHASING with psychic
    bolt special attack; glimmer_reaper: CHASING with AOE scream special
    attack), effects.py SilencedEffect (new: dur=3 or 5; before_turn hook
    returns "silenced" to block ability input), engine.py ability dispatch
    (check silenced effect before allowing ability use),
    engine.glimmer_thresholds_triggered: set[int] (track which thresholds
    have already spawned their hunter — each threshold spawns exactly once;
    re-entrant protection needed since glimmer is run-persistent and you
    only want to spawn on first crossing, not every check).

VI. NEW EFFECTS REQUIRED
================================================================

CorrosiveAuraEffect (id="corrosive_aura_stacks")
  Category: debuff. Applied per-turn to enemies in Corrosive Aura radius.
  Fields: stacks: int (increments each turn in range).
  tick(): decrement duration normally. If stacks >= 5, deal 2/3/4 acid
          damage (scales with mutation level, pass as effect param).
  modify_incoming_damage(): subtract self.stacks from defender's defense
                             total before damage calculation.
  expire(): stacks lost.
  Note: Stacks don't accumulate in the traditional sense — each turn in
  radius adds 1 stack up to cap. Leaving radius does NOT reset stacks;
  they persist for 4 turns (the duration of the effect at point of leaving).

MassResonanceStaggerEffect (id="mass_resonance_stagger")
  Category: debuff. Applied to enemies adjacent to player after melee hit
  at L2 Mass Resonance.
  Duration: 1 turn.
  modify_incoming_damage() / before_turn: reduce next attack damage by 2.
  Note: L2 only; replaced by cleave at L3.

SilencedEffect (id="silenced")
  Category: debuff. Prevents ability use.
  Duration: 3 turns (5 at Glimmer Buildup L3).
  before_turn hook: if silenced, set a flag that blocks ability menu
                    access in input_handler.py or engine.py ability
                    dispatch. Message: "You are silenced — abilities
                    unavailable."
  Note: Does NOT prevent item use or physical attacks. Only blocks the
  ABILITY_REGISTRY activation pathway.

================================================================
VIII. BALANCE NOTES
================================================================

WEAK GOOD tier calibration:
  Horn Growth: charge bonus of distance*2 at L2 means a 4-step charge
  deals 8 bonus damage. Against Crack Den floor 2 enemies (30-50 HP),
  this is meaningful but not decisive — you still need to maneuver. The
  setup cost (needing 2-3 open tiles of run-up) prevents it from being
  reliable in cramped corridors. Strong in the larger room types
  (Oct, Circle, Cross); weaker in Hall and L-shaped rooms.

  Chain Discharge: L1 deals at most 7+count//3 primary + 4+count//4 chain.
  At mutation_count 6 (late run), that's 9+5 = 14 total potential damage.
  For comparison, Acid Spit L1 deals 8+2 = 10 to one target. Chain Discharge
  trades range for multi-target and requires clustering. Balanced: Acid Spit
  is better single-target, Chain is better in groups. At L3 death discharges
  can chain — that's the payoff for the weak-tier investment.

  Mass Resonance: +1 power per enemy within 4 tiles. At 3 enemies, +3 power
  and +10 speed. Against floor 3-4 content, player with 12 STR hits for
  roughly 12+weapon_bonus power. +3 power in a 3-enemy surround is a 25%
  power increase — strong but temporary. The downside is you are surrounded.
  At L2 (+2/enemy, +20 speed), being surrounded by 4 is +8 power — this
  approaches broken territory but is gated behind actively being mobbed,
  which is dangerous. Recommend monitoring in playtesting and capping L2
  power bonus at +6 total if needed.

WEAK BAD tier calibration:
  Amnesiac Pulses: A 6-tile radius un-explore every 30 turns is annoying
  but not dangerous — the current-FOV protection means you can't be
  immediately ambushed. The real pain is re-scouting rooms you thought
  were clear. At L3 (15 turns, 10 tiles, 30% confuse), this becomes a
  severe information tax. Comparable to Myopia in severity at base level,
  significantly worse at L3.

  Spontaneous Combustion: 30% every 20 turns means expected combustion
  roughly every 66 turns. Across a 4-floor run (~300 player turns/floor
  estimated), that's about 18 combustion events total at L1. Each costs
  6 HP (2/turn x 3 turns) = ~108 HP total. Player max HP ~130 at CON 10.
  This is a meaningful but survivable drain. The upside (3 splash to
  adjacent enemies) is worth ~9 total damage if consistently surrounded.
  At L3 (50% every 10 turns), expected combustion every 20 turns, far more
  dangerous — but at 4 turns or 8 if below 50% HP. This is brutal late
  run. Intended: L3 makes this mutation genuinely threatening.

STRONG GOOD tier calibration:
  Force Barricade: 1/floor terrain creation is an extraordinary effect.
  Comparable power level to Frog Legs Leap or Body Jack in tactical
  impact. Balance concern: can the player cheese certain rooms by
  barricading the only entrance? Answer: yes, but 1 charge/floor limits
  this to one encounter. The 12-turn duration means enemies can simply wait
  for the wall to drop. The tactical depth is the tradeoff: creating the
  wall takes resources and positioning. At L2/L3 with 2 charges, becomes
  a serious tactical tool. Appropriately rare (Strong Good).

  Body Jack: 1/floor possession is powerful but extremely risky. The
  dormant body being vulnerable creates genuine counterplay. An aggressive
  enemy pack can kill your real body mid-possession, which ends with a
  now-hostile possessed enemy (who may attack your just-returned, low-HP
  self). This is the defining tension of the mutation. L3's 2 charges per
  floor makes it a core build identity for a "puppetmaster" run.

  Corrosive Aura: The -1 DR/stack per turn in radius creates a ramp-up
  time before the aura is effective. Against a 3-DR enemy, the aura needs
  3 turns in range to neutralize defense, 5 more to cap and deal acid.
  This is too slow against fast enemies but devastating against slow,
  high-DR targets. The corrosive tiles at L2 reward careful path planning.
  L3 death explosions can chain if enemies have pre-stacked corrosion.
  Appropriately complex for Strong Good tier.

STRONG BAD tier calibration:
  Evil Twin: A monster with the player's stats appears every floor. At
  floor 1 (player ~12 power, ~15 defense typical), the Twin is a real
  threat with 70% player HP. It cannot be ignored — it always chases.
  The stat theft (3 hits → -1 permanent stat) is the run-ending consequence
  players fear. Kill reward (+5 temp HP, +1 melee power) is enough to
  make the fight worth having. L3 stair-blocking is the signature moment —
  the final floor always forces a confrontation with your shadow before the
  boss. A deeply roguelike design: escalating, inescapable, fair.

  Glimmer Buildup: Ability-heavy builds (Blackkk Magic, Mutation synergies,
  Deep Frying) can easily hit 15+ Glimmer over a run. Glimmer Scout at 5
  stacks is annoying but manageable. Stalker at 10 is a genuine threat
  (silence duration cuts into combat tempo). Reaper at 15 is a boss-tier
  encounter. The never-decreasing counter means ability-heavy builds feel
  this mutation compound over the run — it does not reset between floors.
  A player who carefully limits ability use can keep below 10 Glimmer
  indefinitely. The tension is in that discipline against powerful
  ability synergies. Balanced for its tier: the upside of abilities
  doesn't change; the downside is measured in hunters, not stat nerfs.
  Psychic Amplifier conflict: having both mutations is a build archetype,
  not a trap. It rewards skillful management of Glimmer vs. ability
  investment.

INTERACTIONS OF NOTE:
  - Horn Growth + Extra Joints: Extra Joints grants free movement step on
    stunned-enemy hit. Horn Growth gives +2 stun on charge at L3. These
    synergize: charge → stun → free step → charge again if enough distance.
    Strong synergy; monitor for looping.
  - Mass Resonance + Hemorrhagic Aura (Strong Good): Hemorrhagic Aura
    procs bleed on enemies near you. Mass Resonance rewards being surrounded.
    Both want enemies close. Combined, this is a high-risk melee identity.
  - Corrosive Aura + Venom Nodes: Both require enemies to stay in melee range
    to stack their effects. The aura strips DR; venom adds DoT. Very synergistic
    melee combination.
  - Glimmer Buildup + Psychic Amplifier: The intended tension pairing (see
    above). Having both is a build option, not a mistake.
  - Evil Twin + Blood Memory: Every floor, you are guaranteed a fight with
    the Twin. Blood Memory Surge rewards kills with 2x XP. Killing the Twin
    gives a surge. The Twin fight becomes a scheduled XP event.
  - Spontaneous Combustion + Toxic Metabolism (Weak Good): The player takes
    fire damage, which is NOT tox — these don't synergize mechanically, but
    the flavor of "burning and poisoned" is thematic. Combustion does NOT
    add toxicity, so Toxic Metabolism speed bonus is unaffected by combustion.
  - Body Jack + Neural Static: While possessing an enemy, the Neural Static
    aura is centered on the POSSESSED ENTITY (your active position), not
    your dormant body. Enemies surrounding your possessed host get the crit
    reduction aura. This is an interesting edge case — rule: aura effects
    apply at the player's active position (possessed or real).
