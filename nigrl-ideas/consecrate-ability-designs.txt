CONSECRATE — 5 ABILITY DESIGNS (DECONTAMINATION L3)
NIGRL Design Document
======================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE
----------------
This document designs five mechanically distinct versions of the
"Consecrate" ability for the Decontamination skill tree, Level 3 perk.
All five use the same directional 5x3 zone shape — the player picks a
cardinal direction and the zone extends forward. The versions differ in
what the zone DOES to enemies, what the player gains from standing in
it, and what special mechanic makes each variant unique.

SHARED SHAPE SPECIFICATION (ALL VERSIONS)
------------------------------------------
Zone footprint: 5 tiles deep x 3 tiles wide = 15 tiles maximum.
The player occupies the near-edge center tile; the zone extends in
the chosen direction.

Player at (px, py), direction EAST:
  Row N-1:  (px,   py-1) (px+1, py-1) (px+2, py-1) (px+3, py-1) (px+4, py-1)
  Row 0:    (px,   py  ) (px+1, py  ) (px+2, py  ) (px+3, py  ) (px+4, py  )
  Row N+1:  (px,   py+1) (px+1, py+1) (px+2, py+1) (px+3, py+1) (px+4, py+1)
  (player is at (px, py), near-edge center)

For WEST: offsets x by -1..-4.
For NORTH: offsets y by -1..-4 (up in dungeon coordinates).
For SOUTH: offsets y by +1..+4.
All tiles clipped to walkable dungeon floor tiles before writing to
dungeon.rad_tiles.

TARGETING UI:
  New ability target_type: TargetType.DIRECTIONAL (or handled as a
  special case in spells.py/input_handler.py). On ability activation:
  display message "Choose direction (arrow keys + Enter or tap arrow)."
  Arrow key press immediately fires if quick-tap; otherwise Enter
  confirms. No cursor movement required — four choices only.
  Similar pattern to how the gun targeting simplifies for direction-only
  abilities. Can be implemented as ADJACENT_TILE_TARGETING repurposed
  for direction selection, or as a dedicated four-key capture in the
  ability execute function that enters a sub-loop (same pattern as
  EntityTargeting for ADJACENT abilities).

  Implementation note: the execute callable receives (engine) and can
  query input state via a helper function
  _prompt_cardinal_direction(engine) -> Optional[str] that returns
  "N"/"S"/"E"/"W" or None on cancel. This helper processes the next
  keypress in the event loop and returns.

TILE SYSTEM:
  All five versions write to dungeon.rad_tiles: dict[tuple, int]
  (introduced alongside the original Consecrate design in
  skill-decontamination-perks-L3-L6.txt). This is the only new dungeon
  data structure needed — the five designs are differentiated purely by
  what happens to entities on those tiles each engine tick, which is
  controlled by flags or ability-id stored alongside the tile or by
  reading which ability granted the tiles.

  Implementation option A (simpler): dungeon.rad_tiles stores a tuple
  (turns_remaining, intensity_mode) where intensity_mode is a string
  key. The engine tick handler reads the mode to determine per-tick
  behavior. This requires one dict instead of five.

  Implementation option B (cleanest per-design): separate tile dicts per
  variant (dungeon.consecrate_tiles_v1, etc.). Heavier but allows fully
  independent behavior. NOT recommended — use option A.

  Recommended: dungeon.rad_tiles: dict[tuple, tuple] =
    { (x,y): (turns_remaining: int, mode: str) }
  Default behavior (mode="standard") matches the original Consecrate
  design. New modes documented per design below.

RENDERING (all versions):
  "+" character in yellow-green when visible, dimmed when explored.
  Colors by mode (foreground/background):
    "standard":    fg=(160, 255,  80), bg=( 25,  40,  10)
    "ironseal":    fg=(180, 255, 120), bg=( 20,  50,   5)
    "flensing":    fg=(200, 255,  60), bg=( 30,  50,   0)
    "cyclone":     fg=(140, 230, 255), bg=(  5,  20,  35)
    "vigil":       fg=(255, 230,  80), bg=( 40,  35,   0)
    "judgment":    fg=(255, 180,  60), bg=( 45,  25,   0)
  Explored-not-visible: all colors halved in value.


======================================================================
DESIGN 1 — "IRON SEAL"
======================================================================

NAME: Iron Seal
FLAVOR: You plant your feet and declare this ground off-limits.
        The zone glows with the cold green light of irradiated authority.
        Enemies that push forward into it find their legs going heavy and
        their radiation climbing — the closer they get to you, the worse.

COST: 60 radiation
DURATION: 18 turns
CHARGES PER FLOOR: 2
PASSIVE STAT BONUS (bundled at L3): +15% rad resistance, +1 STR

---- ZONE BEHAVIOR (ENEMIES) ----
Tiered radiation gain based on depth in zone:
  - Back row (far 3 tiles, col 3-4 from player): +10 rad/turn
  - Middle row (col 1-2 from player): +18 rad/turn
  - Front tile (col 0, adjacent to player): +30 rad/turn

The intensity gradient rewards enemies who charge at you — they suffer
the most radiation. The gradient is computed as (depth_rank * 10 + 10)
where depth_rank is the column index counted from player (0 = adjacent,
4 = far). Wait, reversed: deeper into the zone from the enemy's
perspective is closer to the player. So:
  col 0 (player-adjacent):  +30 rad/turn
  col 1:                    +22 rad/turn
  col 2:                    +14 rad/turn
  col 3-4 (zone far end):   +8 rad/turn
Values scaled by ironseal_scale = 1 + (decon_level - 3) * 0.05 at
higher Decontamination levels (future-proofing).

---- PLAYER STANDING BONUS ----
While standing on any Iron Seal tile (player's own tile counts as col 0):
  - Incoming melee damage reduced by the player's current rad // 40
    (capped at 6 damage reduction). At 200 rad this gives -5 defense.
  - This is a temporary defense bonus applied via a modify_incoming_damage
    hook on a "consecrated_ground" effect that is applied to the player
    as long as they are on a rad tile. The effect tracks its own
    "on_tile" flag refreshed each tick.

---- UNIQUE MECHANIC: "HOLD THE LINE" ----
If the player ends 3 consecutive turns standing on Iron Seal tiles
without retreating (tracked via engine.ironseal_hold_streak: int),
all remaining Iron Seal tiles refresh to full duration (18t). This
rewards positional commitment — plant yourself, deny the corridor,
and the zone stays live as long as you hold. Moving off resets the
streak to zero.

---- L2 EMISSION SYNERGY ----
Emission sets visible enemies to your current radiation level. If you
then cast Iron Seal, enemies already at high rad will be taking their
existing irradiated DOT (rad//50 damage/tick) PLUS the tiered rad gain
from walking into the zone. An enemy at 150 rad from Emission entering
col 0 of Iron Seal gains +30 rad/turn -> 180 rad -> DOT climbs to 3-4
damage/tick on top of the zone's radiation buildup.

---- SKYWALKER OG / FORCE SENSITIVE SYNERGY ----
Casting costs 60 rad, which triggers remove_radiation in the execute
function (deducting 60 rad). This fires ForceSensitiveEffect.on_rad_lost
if active, crossing 2 thresholds for +4 temp STR. The "Hold the Line"
bonus lasts long enough for you to stand in your own zone post-cast with
the STR active — the consecration act itself powers up your offensive output.

---- IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ----
  - dungeon.rad_tiles values for Iron Seal tiles: (18, "ironseal")
  - In engine.py rad tile tick handler, for mode "ironseal":
    col_distance = manhattan_x_distance(entity, player_near_edge)
    clamp 0..4. rad_gain = max(8, 30 - col_distance * 8)
    add_radiation(engine, entity, rad_gain, from_player=True)
  - Player defense bonus: apply Effect id="consecrated_ground" (new
    simple Effect, duration=1, re-applied each tick the player is on
    a rad tile, using on_reapply to refresh; modify_incoming_damage
    reduces by player.radiation // 40, capped at 6).
  - engine.ironseal_hold_streak (int, 0): increment each turn player
    is on a rad tile; reset on move; check at streak==3 in engine tick.
  - Direction must be stored per tile so col_distance can be computed:
    store as (turns_remaining, "ironseal", near_edge_x, near_edge_y,
    direction_char) — use the near_edge tile to determine depth offsets.
    Alternatively compute once at cast time and store a lookup dict:
    engine.ironseal_depth_map: dict[tuple, int] (pos -> col_index).
    This is cleaner. Clear on floor change or new cast.

---- BALANCE ----
  Tiered rad gain means the zone is mildly deterrent at range and
  strongly punishing at melee range — but melee range is also where the
  player's "Hold the Line" defense bonus kicks in. It's a calculated
  risk: stand your ground and benefit, or retreat and lose the zone.
  60 rad cost vs the original 50 rad is slightly higher to account for
  the defense bonus and the gradient's superiority. 2 charges/floor.
  At 18 turns duration in a 5x3 area, one cast can control a corridor
  for the majority of a floor fight.

---- PERK DESCRIPTION (skills.py) ----
  "Iron Seal — 2/floor. 60 rad: irradiate a 5x3 zone (18t) in chosen
   direction. Near tiles: +30 rad/t; far tiles: +8 rad/t. While in zone:
   DEF +rad//40 (max 6). 3 turns held: zone refreshes. +15% rad res, +1 STR."
  (120 chars)

DESIGN 2 — "FLENSING GROUND"
======================================================================

NAME: Flensing Ground
FLAVOR: The floor itself becomes a radiation flayer. Every step enemy
        boots take on your consecrated tiles strips them bare — not just
        irradiating, but bleeding the radiation off them as pure kinetic
        damage. The zone doesn't build rad. It converts it. Enemies that
        enter highly irradiated come out damaged, not irradiated. Yours
        to wield as execution ground.

COST: 45 radiation
DURATION: 14 turns
CHARGES PER FLOOR: 3
PASSIVE STAT BONUS (bundled at L3): +1 TOL, +1 STR

---- ZONE BEHAVIOR (ENEMIES) ----
Flensing Ground does NOT add radiation to enemies. Instead, for every
enemy standing on a Flensing tile at the end of an engine tick:
  - Deal direct damage equal to the enemy's current radiation // 15
    (minimum 0, no rad // floor division result if rad < 15 = 0 dmg).
  - Then reduce that enemy's radiation by 20 (clamped at 0).

This is a "radiation conversion" zone: the tile does not irradiate, it
FLENSES — stripping radiation off enemies and dealing it as damage.
High-rad enemies (set up via Emission) take heavy damage; low-rad
enemies take nothing. The zone punishes enemies that already carry rad.

Examples:
  Enemy at 200 rad: takes 200//15 = 13 damage, loses 20 rad per tick.
  Enemy at 50 rad: takes 50//15 = 3 damage, loses 20 rad per tick.
  Enemy at 10 rad: takes 0 damage, loses 10 rad.

Damage is dealt via deal_damage(engine, entity, amount, from_player=True)
so it triggers on-kill XP and death events normally.

---- PLAYER STANDING BONUS ----
While standing on any Flensing tile:
  - Player's own radiation naturally decays at +10 rad/turn faster
    (remove_radiation(engine, player, 10) each tick the player is on a
    Flensing tile). This is the "the ground is consuming YOUR rad too"
    drawback that is also a benefit: it fires ForceSensitiveEffect
    .on_rad_lost, generating Force Sensitive STR stacks passively.
  - Net: standing in your own Flensing zone passively fuels Force
    Sensitive without spending Skywalker OG. A slow trickle (10 rad/t)
    that crosses the 25-rad threshold after 2-3 turns.

---- UNIQUE MECHANIC: "EXECUTION THRESHOLD" ----
If Flensing damage would reduce an enemy to 25 HP or below, the tile
triggers an "execution" on that enemy: deal 25 additional true damage
(bypasses defense). This fires once per enemy per Consecrate cast (not
per tick) — tracked via a set of entity ids "already executed" stored
in engine.flensing_executed_ids: set.

This creates a satisfying execution zone: Emission irradiates a room,
enemies walk through Flensing Ground, their radiation is converted to
damage, and the weakest among them get executed automatically when they
drop low. It rewards high-rad enemies entering the zone.

---- L2 EMISSION SYNERGY ----
Emission is mandatory for Flensing Ground's ceiling. Without it, enemies
at 15-30 rad from incidental Meth Lab exposure deal 1-2 damage/tick —
interesting but not strong. WITH Emission: setting 4 enemies to 200 rad
then positioning Flensing Ground deals 13 damage/tick to each for as
long as they stay in the zone. That's effectively an AOE DOT of
comparable strength to fire (which deals 3-6 damage/tick from 2 ignite
stacks). The difference: fire fades, Flensing persists as long as they
stand there and the tiles last.

---- SKYWALKER OG / FORCE SENSITIVE SYNERGY ----
Two interaction layers:
  1. Cast costs 45 rad (remove_radiation fires on_rad_lost): one Force
     Sensitive threshold crossing at +2 temp STR.
  2. Standing on Flensing tiles drains 10 rad/turn from player, firing
     on_rad_lost repeatedly. Over 14 turns standing in the zone: 140 rad
     removed = 5 threshold crossings = +10 temp STR (if Force Sensitive
     buff is active the whole time). This is the highest Force Sensitive
     ceiling of the five designs — the zone is literally a STR engine if
     you stand in it.
  3. Skywalker OG removes rad fast — but here the zone removes it for
     free and converts enemies' rad to damage simultaneously. The OG
     strain becomes a top-off tool rather than the primary cycling tool.

---- IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ----
  - dungeon.rad_tiles mode: "flensing"
  - Per-tick for "flensing" tiles:
    if entity != engine.player:
        dmg = entity.radiation // 15
        if dmg > 0: deal_damage(engine, entity, dmg, from_player=True)
        # Execution check
        if entity.hp <= 25 and entity not in engine.flensing_executed_ids:
            engine.flensing_executed_ids.add(entity)
            deal_damage(engine, entity, 25, armor_piercing=True)
        remove_radiation(engine, entity, 20) (not via engine.remove_radiation
        since enemies don't gain Decon XP; just raw entity.radiation -= min(20, entity.radiation))
    else:
        remove_radiation(engine, player, 10) (fires Force Sensitive, awards Decon XP)
  - engine.flensing_executed_ids: set[Entity] = set() — clear on floor change and on new cast.
  - Note: armor_piercing on execution requires deal_damage to support a flag or
    be replicated inline as direct entity.hp reduction. Check combat.py
    deal_damage signature; if it doesn't support armor_piercing, implement
    execution as raw hp reduction with a message: entity.hp -= 25 clamped at 0.
  - Save/load: flensing_executed_ids is ephemeral (rebuild as empty set on load;
    items in it would be dead anyway).

---- BALANCE ----
  3 charges/floor at lower cost (45 rad) reflects the dependency on
  Emission — without that setup, the zone is weak. With Emission it
  becomes a true killing field. The execution threshold prevents enemies
  from tanking through a zone at low HP indefinitely. The player's own
  10 rad/turn drain is the tradeoff: you're slowly losing your radiation
  resource standing in your own zone, which means you need to top it off
  (Skywalker OG, Meth Lab exposure) to keep Force Sensitive cycling.
  This is the most "combo-dependent" of the five designs.

---- PERK DESCRIPTION (skills.py) ----
  "Flensing Ground — 3/floor. 45 rad: 5x3 zone (14t). Enemies lose 20 rad/t,
   take rad//15 dmg/t. Below 25 HP: execute (+25 true dmg). Standing drains
   10 rad/t from you (fuels Force Sensitive). +1 TOL, +1 STR."
  (118 chars)

DESIGN 3 — "CYCLONE GROUND"
======================================================================

NAME: Cyclone Ground
FLAVOR: The rad tiles hum with an invisible kinetic force that drags
        enemies toward you. Step into the zone and your feet stick to
        the floor as something vast and irradiating tries to pull you
        to its center — you. The player is the eye. The zone is the
        storm. Enemies can't outrun a consecrated corridor when it's
        pulling them in.

COST: 55 radiation
DURATION: 12 turns
CHARGES PER FLOOR: 2
PASSIVE STAT BONUS (bundled at L3): +15% rad resistance, +1 STR

---- ZONE BEHAVIOR (ENEMIES) ----
Every enemy on a Cyclone tile has their movement SLOWED: their energy
gain is reduced by 40% on turns when they are on a Cyclone tile.
This is applied via a transient effect (id="cyclone_slowed", duration=1,
re-applied each tick while on tile, same pattern as greasy stacks).
CycloneSlowedEffect.modify_energy_gain() returns energy_gain * 0.6.

In addition: +15 rad/turn to enemies on Cyclone tiles, flat (no gradient).
The slow is the primary mechanic; the rad gain is secondary pressure.

---- PLAYER STANDING BONUS ----
While standing on a Cyclone tile:
  - Ability cooldowns recover 1 extra charge this floor for every 4
    turns the player has been continuously on a Cyclone tile. Tracked
    via engine.cyclone_stand_turns (int). At each 4-turn threshold,
    restore 1 charge to the player's non-consecrate ability with the
    fewest remaining charges (prioritizing Emission, then any other
    Decontamination ability).
  - Message on proc: "Cyclone Focus! Emission charge restored (+1)."
  - This creates a "standing-in" reward that directly refuels your kit
    rather than giving a stat bonus.
  - Damage bonus while in zone: none — the design positions Cyclone as
    the zone-control and momentum-trading variant, not the damage variant.

---- UNIQUE MECHANIC: "MAGNETISM PULL" ----
When an enemy attempts to move OUT of a Cyclone tile, there is a
30% chance the move is negated (the enemy's action is "wasted" trying
to leave). This chance is checked in ai.py's movement resolution: after
pathfinding selects a tile that exits the rad zone, roll; if fails,
entity stays in place for that turn (action consumed).

Implementation: in do_ai_turn(), after computing the intended move:
  target_pos = (entity.x + dx, entity.y + dy)
  if (entity.x, entity.y) in engine.dungeon.rad_tiles and
     target_pos not in engine.dungeon.rad_tiles:
     if engine.skills.get("Decontamination").level >= 3:
         if random.random() < 0.30:
             # Action wasted — entity stays, expend action
             entity.energy -= ENERGY_THRESHOLD
             return

This is the most tactically interesting mechanic: enemies CAN leave the
zone (70% success rate per attempt), but they often can't, making the
zone a soft stun field for fast enemies and an effective cage for slow
ones. Combined with the 40% slow, enemies in the zone take their turns
much less frequently and can't reliably escape.

---- L2 EMISSION SYNERGY ----
Emission sets all visible enemies' rad to yours before you cast Cyclone.
Now they enter the zone already irradiated, and the zone both adds more
rad and keeps them inside. An enemy stuck in Cyclone for 4 turns while
already at 100 rad (from Emission) accumulates another 60 rad (+15/t x4)
= 160 total. At 160 rad the irradiated DOT is 3 damage/tick. Combined
with the slow (they act 40% less often), they're spending their few
turns in a zone they can't reliably leave.

---- SKYWALKER OG / FORCE SENSITIVE SYNERGY ----
Casting costs 55 rad: 2 Force Sensitive threshold crossings = +4 temp STR.
The player standing bonus (charge recovery) doesn't interact with Force
Sensitive directly. However: Cyclone's slow means you have more turns of
combat where you can smoke Skywalker OG without being attacked as much.
The zone is defensive — it buys you turns to use your strain safely.

---- IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ----
  - dungeon.rad_tiles mode: "cyclone"
  - Per-tick "cyclone" behavior:
    if entity != engine.player:
        add_radiation(engine, entity, 15, from_player=True)
        apply_effect(entity, engine, "cyclone_slowed", duration=1)
    else:
        engine.cyclone_stand_turns += 1
        if engine.cyclone_stand_turns % 4 == 0:
            _restore_decon_ability_charge(engine)
  - CycloneSlowedEffect: id="cyclone_slowed", category="debuff",
    duration=1. modify_energy_gain(amount, entity, engine) -> amount * 0.6.
    on_reapply: refresh duration. display_name="Cyclone Slowed".
  - Magnetism Pull: in ai.py do_ai_turn(), movement resolution section.
    Check rad_tiles membership of current vs destination position.
    Mode check: dungeon.rad_tiles[(entity.x, entity.y)][1] == "cyclone".
  - engine.cyclone_stand_turns: int = 0 (reset on new cast or floor change)
  - _restore_decon_ability_charge(engine): find "emission" or any
    Decontamination-granted ability with charges > 0 and charges <
    max_charges; add 1. If all at max, do nothing (no waste).
  - Save/load: cyclone_stand_turns is ephemeral — fine to reset.

---- BALANCE ----
  2 charges/floor. The 30% escape-deny is strong but not reliable — it
  does not guarantee an enemy stays, so it cannot be exploited to
  permanently trap enemies. Over a 12-turn zone duration a fast enemy
  attempting 12 escapes fails at ~96% probability... wait, inverse: each
  turn 30% deny means 70% success. After 12 turns: 0.7^12 ≈ 1.4% still
  inside. But the slow means they're only ATTEMPTING escape periodically
  — if they act every other turn (with slow), they try 6 times: 0.7^6
  ≈ 12% still inside. Effective. Not permanent. The charge restoration
  bonus is situationally strong — getting an Emission charge back mid-
  combat could let you re-emit onto fresh enemies.

---- PERK DESCRIPTION (skills.py) ----
  "Cyclone Ground — 2/floor. 55 rad: 5x3 zone (12t). Enemies slowed 40%,
   gain +15 rad/t; 30% chance to deny exit each turn. Standing: every 4
   turns, restore 1 Decon ability charge. +15% rad res, +1 STR."
  (119 chars)


======================================================================
DESIGN 4 — "VIGIL GROUND"
======================================================================

NAME: Vigil Ground
FLAVOR: Kneeling in irradiated earth, you mark this place as sacred.
        Not to protect yourself from radiation — you've made your peace
        with it. To make it HOLY. Enemies that enter the zone feel the
        consecration as physical force: their attacks weaken, their
        resolve cracks, their armor flakes. You stand at the center and
        the zone amplifies you.

COST: 50 radiation
DURATION: 20 turns
CHARGES PER FLOOR: 2
PASSIVE STAT BONUS (bundled at L3): +2 TOL, +10% rad resistance

---- ZONE BEHAVIOR (ENEMIES) ----
Enemies on Vigil tiles gain +12 rad/turn (moderate irradiation, lower
than other designs). The primary enemy debuff is NOT radiation-based —
it is a defense reduction:
  - Each turn an enemy stands on a Vigil tile, they lose 1 point of
    defense (entity.defense -= 1, clamped at a minimum of entity.defense
    - 5 total reduction). This loss persists for the DURATION of the
    combat encounter (it represents their radiation-corroded armor).
  - Track minimum defense floor per entity:
    engine.vigil_debuffed_entities: dict[entity_id, int] mapping to
    the original defense value. On enemy death, clean up. On floor
    change, clear.
  - Display as a "corroded" status message the first time an enemy
    is debuffed: "Slime Dealer's armor corrodes! (-1 DEF)"

---- PLAYER STANDING BONUS ----
While standing on a Vigil tile, the "while standing in Consecrate"
trigger is active. All player melee damage is increased by
1 + (player_decon_level - 3) * 1, so: +1 damage at L3, +2 at L4, etc.
This bonus is active ONLY on the player's tile (they must be standing
in the zone they placed). It is implemented as an inline check in
combat.py's player melee attack resolution:

  if engine.skills.get("Decontamination").level >= 3:
      player_pos = (engine.player.x, engine.player.y)
      if player_pos in engine.dungeon.rad_tiles and \
         engine.dungeon.rad_tiles[player_pos][1] == "vigil":
          bonus = 1 + (engine.skills.get("Decontamination").level - 3)
          damage += bonus

Additionally: while standing in Vigil, all Decontamination ability costs
(radiation costs to cast abilities) are reduced by 10. So Emission's
implicit cost (having radiation to share) is unaffected, but future
ability rad costs (at L4-L5) are slightly discounted in-zone.
Tracked as engine.player_stats.vigil_active: bool, set True when player
is on a Vigil tile each tick, False otherwise.

---- UNIQUE MECHANIC: "SENTINEL ECHO" ----
When a Vigil tile expires (reaches 0 turns remaining), it triggers a
Sentinel Echo: deal (original_vigil_duration - turns_remaining_at_death)
... wait, at expiry turns_remaining is 0, so this is always duration.
Instead: on Vigil tile expiry, deal (number_of_turns_enemies_spent_on_
this_tile) * 3 damage to any enemy currently standing on that tile at
expiry. Track per-tile enemy-turn accumulator:
  engine.vigil_tile_occupancy: dict[tuple, int] — (x,y) -> total
  enemy-turns spent on that tile.
  On each tick: for enemies on vigil tiles, increment
  vigil_tile_occupancy[(entity.x, entity.y)] by 1.
  On tile expiry: for each enemy at that position, deal
  vigil_tile_occupancy.get(pos, 0) * 3 damage.

This creates a "the zone remembers" effect — an enemy that has been
standing in your Vigil for 5 turns takes a 15-damage echo burst when
the tile expires under them. Enemies that move through briefly take
very little. This discourages enemies from statically tanking your zone
indefinitely.

---- L2 EMISSION SYNERGY ----
Emission's irradiated DOT is enhanced by the defense reduction:
irradiated enemies lose 1 DEF/turn in Vigil Ground, so by turn 4 they're
at -4 defense, making the player's melee hits deal 4 more damage in
addition to the Vigil standing bonus and the irradiated DOT. This is a
slow-building synergy — best realized against tanky enemies (Chemists,
Sludge Amalgams) who have high base defense.

---- SKYWALKER OG / FORCE SENSITIVE SYNERGY ----
50 rad cast cost: 2 Force Sensitive threshold crossings = +4 temp STR.
The vigil_active melee bonus stacks ADDITIVELY with the temp STR from
Force Sensitive (STR applies as a flat power bonus; Vigil bonus is a
separate damage adder). At the same time in the zone after casting
Skywalker OG: +4 temp STR from Force Sensitive + 1-2 raw melee bonus
from Vigil = meaningful melee upside from a single combined action.
The 20-turn duration (longest of the five designs) means Force Sensitive's
66-turn buff is active for most of the Vigil's life.

---- IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ----
  - dungeon.rad_tiles mode: "vigil"
  - Per-tick "vigil" behavior:
    if entity != engine.player:
        add_radiation(engine, entity, 12, from_player=True)
        original_def = engine.vigil_debuffed_entities.get(entity, entity.defense)
        if entity not in engine.vigil_debuffed_entities:
            engine.vigil_debuffed_entities[entity] = entity.defense
        if entity.defense > original_def - 5:
            entity.defense -= 1
            engine.messages.append(f"{entity.name} armor corrodes! (-1 DEF)")
        engine.vigil_tile_occupancy[(entity.x, entity.y)] = \
            engine.vigil_tile_occupancy.get((entity.x, entity.y), 0) + 1
    else:
        engine.player_stats.vigil_active = True  # refreshed each tick
  - On tile expiry (in rad tile expiry loop):
    for entity at expiry position:
        echo_dmg = engine.vigil_tile_occupancy.get(pos, 0) * 3
        if echo_dmg > 0: deal_damage(engine, entity, echo_dmg)
    del engine.vigil_tile_occupancy[pos] (if present)
  - engine.vigil_debuffed_entities: dict (entity -> original_defense) — clear on floor change.
    On entity death, restore entity.defense = original value (in entity death handler).
    Actually: since entity is dead, restoring defense is cosmetic. Skip. Just clear dict.
  - engine.vigil_tile_occupancy: dict[tuple, int] — clear on floor change, on new vigil cast.
  - engine.player_stats.vigil_active = False each tick before checking tiles,
    True if player on a vigil tile. Read in combat.py.
  - Melee bonus check in combat.py: after damage computed, add 1 + (level-3) if vigil_active.
  - Ability cost reduction: in _execute_* functions for Decon abilities, check
    vigil_active and subtract 10 from rad_cost before deducting.

---- BALANCE ----
  Vigil is the longest-duration design (20 turns) and the most supportive:
  moderate rad gain, slow armor erosion, and a delayed burst on tile expiry.
  The melee damage bonus from standing in it rewards staying in range,
  which is dangerous — you're in melee range of corroding enemies who are
  also irradiating. The 20-turn duration is the risk/reward tradeoff:
  very long zone, but you're incentivized to stay in it.
  The Sentinel Echo max damage: an enemy standing for 20 turns (unlikely)
  takes 20*3 = 60 echo damage at expiry. More realistic (8 turns) = 24
  echo damage — comparable to a good melee hit.

======================================================================
DESIGN 5 — "JUDGMENT FIELD"
======================================================================

NAME: Judgment Field
FLAVOR: The zone is not passive. It is a sentence. The moment an enemy
        enters, it starts counting their radiation — and when the count
        is met, it detonates that enemy in a local burst that irradiates
        their neighbors. You are not just a paladin cleaning the ground.
        You are handing down verdicts. Judgment is passed when the
        radiation total in the zone tips past the threshold.

COST: 70 radiation
DURATION: 16 turns
CHARGES PER FLOOR: 2
PASSIVE STAT BONUS (bundled at L3): +2 STR, +10% rad resistance

---- ZONE BEHAVIOR (ENEMIES) ----
Enemies on Judgment Field tiles gain +20 rad/turn (highest flat rate of
the five designs). No slow, no armor erosion, no gradient — just
relentless irradiation.

When any enemy on a Judgment tile reaches a Judgment Threshold of
200 radiation WHILE on a Judgment tile, they trigger a Judgment Burst:
  - The triggering enemy is dealt 40 true damage (bypasses defense).
  - All other enemies within radius 2 of the triggering enemy receive
    +60 radiation instantly (the enemy's "detonation" irradiates
    surrounding units — not the player).
  - The player receives +15 radiation from the burst (proximity
    irradiation — small cost for large payoff).
  - Message: "[Enemy] is judged! 40 true damage. Rad burst in radius 2."

An enemy can only trigger Judgment Burst ONCE per Consecrate cast —
tracked via engine.judgment_triggered_ids: set. After triggering, the
enemy no longer has this special interaction with the tiles. They still
take rad gain from tiles normally.

This creates a delayed detonation loop: Emission sets enemies to high
rad, Judgment Field adds +20/turn, and once the first enemy crosses 200
total rad they explode in a chain-irradiation event that potentially
bumps nearby enemies to their own Judgment Threshold.

---- PLAYER STANDING BONUS ----
While standing on a Judgment Field tile:
  - Player gains +10 radiation per turn they stand in the field. This
    is the price: the zone irradiates YOU too (at half the enemy rate).
    This fires add_radiation(engine, player, 10) each tick — not via
    remove_radiation, so it does NOT fuel Force Sensitive directly.
    Instead, it builds up your radiation for a later Force Sensitive
    activation or Emission cast.
  - The standing reward: each Judgment Burst that fires while the player
    is on a Judgment tile adds +25 bonus XP directly to Decontamination
    skill (bypassing potential XP and converting directly to real XP).
    This represents "witnessing Judgment" — you learn more about radiation
    purging by watching it execute.
    Implementation: engine.judgment_xp_pending (int, 0). When burst fires
    and player is on a Judgment tile: engine.judgment_xp_pending += 25.
    At start of player turn, grant this XP: engine.skills.gain_real_exp
    ("Decontamination", engine.judgment_xp_pending); reset to 0.

---- UNIQUE MECHANIC: "CHAIN JUDGMENT" ----
If two or more enemies trigger Judgment Burst within the same tile region
on the same floor (tracked via engine.judgment_burst_count: int), each
subsequent burst deals +20 additional true damage (cumulative: 2nd burst
= 60, 3rd = 80, etc.). The "Chain Judgment" escalation resets on floor
change. This rewards Judgment Field as a farm tool on floors with many
grouped irradiated enemies — each burst makes the next one more lethal.

To prevent the "just walk through the zone repeatedly" degenerate
strategy: the judgment_triggered_ids set ensures each enemy triggers
only once, so the chain escalation only matters if you have MANY enemies
cycling through the zone.

---- L2 EMISSION SYNERGY ----
Judgment Field is the most Emission-dependent of the five designs.
Without Emission, enemies need 10 turns to reach 200 rad in the zone
(starting from 0, gaining 20/tick). WITH Emission at 150+ rad:
enemies only need 2-3 turns to hit the 200-rad Judgment Threshold.
This makes Emission -> Judgment Field a reliable 2-action combo:
Emit, then Consecrate in the direction enemies are clustered, and the
first arrivals detonate within 2-3 turns, chain-irradiating the rest.

---- SKYWALKER OG / FORCE SENSITIVE SYNERGY ----
70 rad cast cost: 2-3 Force Sensitive threshold crossings depending on
exact rad amount (70 rad / 25 = 2.8 → 2 crossings if started at 0 rad
lost, potentially 3 if prior loss was partway through a threshold).
The player's +10 rad/turn from standing in the zone BUILDS rad for a
later Force Sensitive pop. Unlike Flensing Ground (which drains player
rad), Judgment Field CHARGES your rad while dealing enormous burst damage.
The tradeoff: you're gaining rad while standing in it, so Skywalker OG
consumption to trigger Force Sensitive happens AFTER the combat encounter
rather than during.

---- IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ----
  - dungeon.rad_tiles mode: "judgment"
  - Per-tick "judgment" behavior:
    if entity != engine.player:
        add_radiation(engine, entity, 20, from_player=True)
        # Judgment threshold check
        if entity.radiation >= 200 and entity not in engine.judgment_triggered_ids:
            engine.judgment_triggered_ids.add(entity)
            # True damage to triggering entity
            entity.hp -= min(40, entity.hp)  # True damage (bypass defense)
            if not entity.alive or entity.hp <= 0:
                entity.alive = False
                engine.event_bus.emit("entity_died", entity=entity, killer=engine.player)
            # Rad burst to neighbors
            for neighbor in engine.dungeon.entities:
                if neighbor != entity and neighbor.entity_type == "monster" \
                   and neighbor.alive:
                    dist = chebyshev_distance(entity, neighbor)
                    if dist <= 2:
                        add_radiation(engine, neighbor, 60, from_player=True)
            # Player rad gain from burst
            add_radiation(engine, engine.player, 15, pierce_resistance=True)
            # Player XP if on Judgment tile
            if (engine.player.x, engine.player.y) in engine.dungeon.rad_tiles \
               and engine.dungeon.rad_tiles[(engine.player.x, engine.player.y)][1] == "judgment":
                engine.judgment_xp_pending += 25
            # Chain escalation
            engine.judgment_burst_count += 1
            # Future burst damage bonus is applied at burst time:
            # true_dmg = 40 + (engine.judgment_burst_count - 1) * 20
            engine.messages.append([
                (f"{entity.name} is Judged! ", (255, 180, 60)),
                ("40 true dmg, rad burst in radius 2", (200, 255, 80)),
            ])
    else:
        add_radiation(engine, player, 10, pierce_resistance=True)
        # Not via remove_radiation — does not fire Force Sensitive on_rad_lost
        if engine.judgment_xp_pending > 0:
            engine.skills.gain_real_exp("Decontamination", engine.judgment_xp_pending)
            engine.judgment_xp_pending = 0
  - engine.judgment_triggered_ids: set[Entity] = set() — clear on floor change, new cast.
  - engine.judgment_burst_count: int = 0 — reset on floor change only (not per cast).
  - engine.judgment_xp_pending: int = 0 — processed each player turn.
  - Note on true damage: entity.hp -= 40 directly (or deal_damage with
    some armor_piercing mechanism). If deal_damage doesn't support this, do inline
    hp reduction followed by death check.
  - Chain escalation: apply to the true_dmg calculation in the burst handler:
    true_dmg = 40 + (engine.judgment_burst_count - 1) * 20 (applied BEFORE
    incrementing judgment_burst_count). The first burst is always 40.

---- BALANCE ----
  70 rad cost is the highest of the five designs; this reflects the burst
  ceiling. Judgment Field can one-shot or severely injure low-HP enemies
  the moment they cross 200 rad (which Emission sets up in 1 action).
  The player taking +10 rad/turn from standing in the zone is a real cost
  — after 5 turns standing, +50 rad to self, which at 200+ total starts
  generating mutation checks. The zone is aggressive and punishing; it
  wants you to burst, not linger.
  Chain escalation: in practice, getting 3+ bursts in a single floor
  encounter requires a large group of irradiated enemies. Cap chain_count
  at 5 for a maximum of 40 + 4*20 = 120 true damage per burst. Very high
  ceiling, but requires 5 separate enemies to individually trigger.

======================================================================
COMPARATIVE SUMMARY
======================================================================

Design         | Cost | Dur | Chr | Zone Dmg Style        | Standing Reward
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Iron Seal      |  60  |  18 |  2  | Gradient rad gain      | Defense = rad//40
Flensing Ground|  45  |  14 |  3  | Convert enemy rad->dmg | Drains player rad (fuels FS)
Cyclone Ground |  55  |  12 |  2  | Slow + mild rad gain   | Charge recovery
Vigil Ground   |  50  |  20 |  2  | Rad + armor erosion    | +1 melee dmg (scales level)
Judgment Field |  70  |  16 |  2  | Burst at threshold     | +XP per burst; +rad to player

Complexity:
  Iron Seal      — medium (gradient logic, streak counter, defense effect)
  Flensing Ground— medium-high (conversion dmg, execution, player drain)
  Cyclone Ground — high (slow effect, escape deny in AI, charge recovery)
  Vigil Ground   — high (armor erosion, tile occupancy tracking, echo)
  Judgment Field — medium-high (threshold burst, chain, XP grant, player rad)

Emission Dependency (how weak without Emission):
  Iron Seal      — moderate (gradient still punishes; not Emission-reliant)
  Flensing Ground— HIGH (no enemy rad = no damage; requires Emission setup)
  Cyclone Ground — low (slow+rad are independent of enemy starting rad)
  Vigil Ground   — low-moderate (armor erosion works on fresh enemies)
  Judgment Field — HIGH (200-rad threshold hard to reach without Emission)

Force Sensitive Fuel:
  Iron Seal      — cast cost fires FS
  Flensing Ground— HIGHEST (standing drains 10/t; 14 turns = 140 rad removed)
  Cyclone Ground — cast cost fires FS
  Vigil Ground   — cast cost fires FS; zone doesn't drain player
  Judgment Field — cast cost fires FS; zone ADDS rad to player (opposite)

DESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS:
  - Simplest to implement + most elegant: Iron Seal (gradient + hold streak).
  - Strongest build identity: Flensing Ground (converts Emission into a
    killing floor; Force Sensitive highway).
  - Best zone control: Cyclone Ground (slow + escape deny; defensive flavor).
  - Best for long fights vs tanky enemies: Vigil Ground (armor erosion).
  - Most explosive ceiling / most Emission-dependent: Judgment Field.

If only one is to be chosen for L3, Iron Seal or Flensing Ground are the
strongest design picks. Iron Seal introduces a new "tiered zone" pattern
with a natural positional reward loop. Flensing Ground creates the most
distinct verb: "converting enemy radiation into direct damage," which is
unique to this ability and makes Emission feel like mandatory setup.
