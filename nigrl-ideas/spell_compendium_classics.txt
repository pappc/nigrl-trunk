================================================================================
NIGRL SPELL / ABILITY COMPENDIUM — SUPPLEMENT I
Classic D&D & Roguelike Spells Re-Skinned for the Urban Setting
================================================================================

This supplement adds 35 spell and ability designs derived from the absolute
classics of Dungeons & Dragons and traditional roguelikes (NetHack, Dungeon
Crawl Stone Soup, Brogue, ADOM, ToME, Angband). Each entry is re-skinned
through NIGRL's urban/street lens with an appropriate NIGRL name, followed by
the classic inspiration in parentheses.

None of these duplicate abilities already in the registry or in Sections A-H:
  Registry: warp, dimension_door, chain_lightning, ray_of_frost, firebolt,
  arcane_missile, breath_fire, zap, corn_dog, lesser_cloudkill, pry,
  throw_bottle, bash, black_eye_slap, gouge, pickpocket, force_push,
  fry_shot, place_fire, dash, ignite_spell, quick_eat, double_tap, spray,
  burst, spray_and_pray, slow_metabolism, pandemic, radiation_nova

  Sections A-H ideas also avoided: liquid_courage, hair_of_dog, spiked_punch,
  corkscrew, blackout, last_call, gas_attack, deep_fried_fireball, iron_gut,
  corn_dog_judgment, caloric_surge, enzyme_bomb, phat_cloud_active,
  contact_high, smoke_screen, spiritual_transcendence, dutch_courage_cloud,
  slow_burn, roach_resurrection, mind_bullets, curse_of_bad_luck,

I-A-01  DIP OUT
  Classic:  Blink / Misty Step (D&D 5e) / Phase Door (NetHack/ADOM)
  NIGRL Name: "Dip Out" — you step sideways out of reality for one moment
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L2 activated  OR  item "Burner Phone" (one-time use)
            (replaces or precedes dimension_door as a shorter-range version)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 6 turns)
  Stat:     Street Smarts
  Mechanic: Instantly teleport to a random open tile within Chebyshev(4) of
            the player. Unlike Warp (full-map random), Dip Out is SHORT range:
            roll all open non-monster tiles within 4, pick one randomly. If
            no valid tile exists within 4, extend to 6. If still nothing:
            ability fails silently (refund the charge).
            The short range is the key distinction from Warp — it's an
            escape tool for "I need to not be HERE" rather than "I need to
            be somewhere entirely different." Very useful in corridors.
  Why:      Phase Door / Blink is a classic roguelike escape valve. The
            short range means enemies may stay in LOS; you haven't fully
            escaped, just repositioned. Rewards spatial awareness: do you
            know what's within 4 tiles that's safe to land on?
  Flavor:   "You blink. You're not there. You're over there now. That's the
             difference between you and most people."
  Notes:    Distinct from warp: warp is "escape anywhere on floor," Dip Out
            is "panic reposition." Candidates list: all open tiles (not wall,
            not blocked by living monster) with max(|dx|,|dy|) <= 4.
            Pick random. If player lands on item, pick it up (same as warp).
            ability_id: "dip_out"

I-A-02  SMOKE AND MIRRORS (Teleport Other)
  Classic:  Teleport Other (NetHack, DCSS Blink Other)
  NIGRL Name: "Smoke and Mirrors"
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L6 activated  OR  Stealing L7 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Book Smarts + Street Smarts
  Mechanic: Force an enemy to teleport to a random location on the current
            floor. The destination is selected the same way as Warp (any open
            non-monster tile). The target keeps their current AI state —
            if they were CHASING, they resume CHASING from the new position
            and may not be visible from it. This is a pure positional tool:
            send a dangerous enemy to the other side of the floor.
            Cannot target enemies that are ADJACENT (range minimum 2).
  Why:      Teleport Other is a beloved classic (NetHack's wand of
            teleportation, DCSS's Dispersal) because removing a specific
            threat from the immediate area feels powerful. The AI-state
            preservation means you've delayed the enemy, not eliminated them.
            They will return. High-intelligence play: teleport a heavy hitter
            before they reach melee range.
  Flavor:   "You reach out, grab them by the concept of their location, and
             move it somewhere else. They'll figure out where they are
             eventually."
  Notes:    Use same candidate-selection logic as _execute_warp but applied to
            the target entity (dungeon.move_entity(target, tx, ty)).
            Update target's position; AI pathfinding will recalculate next turn.
            Minimum range 2 (validate: Chebyshev(target) >= 2).
            ability_id: "smoke_and_mirrors"

I-A-03  BOUNCE (Word of Recall / Recall)
  Classic:  Word of Recall (ADOM) / Recall Town Portal (ToME)
  NIGRL Name: "Bounce" — street slang for leaving fast
  Source:   Item "Bus Pass" (single-use consumable, rare) OR Abandoning L8
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   ONCE (per item) or /FL 1 (via Abandoning perk)
  Stat:     None (flat utility)
  Mechanic: Instantly transport the player to the ENTRANCE STAIRS of the
            current floor (the tile the player arrived from). Does not change
            the floor — this is NOT stair descent. It's a "get back to the
            start of this floor" tool.
            Use cases: you've gone too deep into a dangerous room and need out
            fast; you want to lure enemies to the entrance from a known safe
            position; you dropped something at the start.
            In NIGRL context: this is crossing back to the crack in the
            doorframe you came in from.
  Why:      Word of Recall is a staple utility (ADOM especially) — the ability
            to bail to the entrance of the current area when things go wrong
            is powerful risk management without being overpowered (you're still
            on the same floor with the same enemies, just repositioned).
  Flavor:   "Back to the door. Fast. You can figure out the rest from there."
  Notes:    Engine needs to track entrance_pos (the stair entity the player
            descended from). Teleport player to that tile. FOV recompute.
            If entrance tile is blocked by a monster: find nearest open tile
            within 2 of entrance. ability_id: "bounce"

I-A-04  GHOST WALK (Etherealness / Gaseous Form short)
  Classic:  Gaseous Form (D&D) / Etherealness (D&D) / Passwall short (NetHack)
  NIGRL Name: "Ghost Walk"
  Source:   Smoking L7 activated  OR  item "Wet Backwood" (unusual food/smoke hybrid)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: For TOL/3 turns (minimum 3, maximum 10): player becomes intangible.
            While intangible:
            - Player can move through wall tiles (as if they were floor)
            - Enemies cannot melee-target the player (they swing at air)
            - Player cannot attack or use targeted abilities (SELF abilities only)
            - Player is invisible to enemies beyond 1 tile (they lose chase)
            On expiry: player materializes. If they are inside a wall tile when
            Ghost Walk expires: they take CON damage equal to tiles-they're-
            embedded-in * 5 and are pushed to the nearest open tile.
            The wall-damage-on-expiry is the risk management challenge.
  Why:      Passwall / Etherealness is a beloved utility in almost every classic
            roguelike. The NIGRL version trades full safety for TOL-limited
            duration and the wall-expiry risk. Smart players use it to cross
            a wall, not stay IN a wall. Tolerance-scaling means tanky builds
            get longer windows.
  Flavor:   "You stop being where you are. You move through the wall.
             You have limited time to be somewhere else before you're solid again."
  Notes:    IntangibleEffect: override dungeon.is_terrain_blocked check for player
            (or add an engine flag engine.player_intangible). In do_ai_turn:
            enemies beyond 1 tile set to WANDERING (lose chase). On expire:
            check player tile; if is_wall: find nearest open tile via BFS (1-step
            flood), move player there, apply (wall_depth * 5) damage.
            ability_id: "ghost_walk"

I-B-01  THROW THE NETS (Web)
  Classic:  Web (D&D 5e) / Entangle / Spider Webs (NetHack)
  NIGRL Name: "Throw the Nets" — casting fishing nets, chicken wire, whatever
             you can get your hands on
  Source:   Beating L5 activated  OR  item "Chicken Wire Roll" (2 uses)
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 2, centered on target tile, range 6)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Strength
  Mechanic: Hurl a tangle of wire/net/rope at a target area. All enemies within
            radius 2 of the impact point gain "Netted" debuff for STR/3 turns
            (minimum 3 turns):
            - Movement cost doubled (they struggle through the net)
            - 30% chance each turn their action is wasted as they try to free
              themselves (before_turn returns True on 30% roll)
            - On taking melee damage while netted: net is destroyed (debuff
              expires instantly — cut free by the impact)
            The net does not affect the player if they enter the area.
  Why:      Web is a D&D classic and an excellent control spell because it
            creates a persistent zone AND hits multiple targets. The "cut free
            on melee" tradeoff gives players a choice: kite the netted enemies
            from range (keep them tangled) or press in melee (cut them free but
            deal damage). Classic emergent decision.
  Flavor:   "You don't have a spell. You have thirty feet of chicken wire and
             a strong arm. Works out about the same."
  Notes:    NettedEffect: modify_energy_gain * 0.5 (halved speed). before_turn:
            30% return True. modify_incoming_damage override: check if source is
            melee (player melee), then expire self immediately. Radius preview
            via get_affected_tiles. ability_id: "throw_the_nets"

I-B-02  WAKE UP CALL (Thunderwave)
  Classic:  Thunderwave (D&D 5e) / Force Pulse (various)
  NIGRL Name: "Wake Up Call" — a concussive blast that sends people flying
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L4 activated  OR  Beating L3 activated
  Target:   SELF (affects all adjacent — Chebyshev 1)
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 12 turns)
  Stat:     Strength + Book Smarts
  Mechanic: An explosion of force/sound/psychic energy bursts outward from the
            player. ALL entities adjacent (Chebyshev 1, all 8 directions) are
            hit simultaneously:
            - Damage: max(1, STR + BKS/3 - defense)
            - Knockback: each target is pushed 2 tiles directly away from the
              player (cardinal/diagonal based on relative position). If they
              hit a wall during knockback, they take 1 bonus damage and stop.
            - The player takes no damage but uses their turn.
            Unlike force_push (single target), Wake Up Call affects ALL adjacent
            simultaneously. Very strong for escaping being surrounded.
  Why:      Thunderwave is a quintessential D&D spell for escape from melee.
            In NIGRL's tight corridors this would be extremely powerful —
            balanced by the cooldown. Distinct from force_push (which is single-
            target ranged); this is melee-range panic AOE.
  Flavor:   "You don't push one person. You push the whole crowd. Same energy,
             eight directions."
  Notes:    Execute iterates all 8 adjacent tiles, collects monsters, applies
            damage + knockback simultaneously. Knockback logic: same as force_push
            direction calculation but applied to each target's relative direction
            from player. Wall-stop: dungeon.is_terrain_blocked check per step.
            ability_id: "wake_up_call"

I-B-03  LIGHTS OUT (Darkness / Silence)
  Classic:  Darkness (D&D 5e) / Silence (D&D 5e) / Dark (NetHach Blindness scroll)
  NIGRL Name: "Lights Out" — kill the lights in a whole area
  Source:   Stealing L5 activated  OR  item "Busted Fuse Box" (interact)
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 4, centered on target tile, range 6)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Street Smarts
  Mechanic: Plunge an area into darkness. For 8 turns, any enemy whose tile is
            inside the radius 4 zone treats their sight_radius as 1 (they can
            only "see" 1 tile regardless of their actual sight_radius).
            The player is NOT affected by Lights Out — they navigate in darkness
            by touch, street instinct, or phone flashlight. The player's FOV
            remains normal.
            Enemies already chasing the player WITHIN the zone immediately lose
            chase (sight lost). Enemies outside the zone are unaffected.
  Why:      Darkness is a classic ambush/control spell. In a LOS-based AI game
            like NIGRL, reducing enemy sight to 1 is extremely powerful because
            they can functionally only react to the player if adjacent. Perfect
            setup for Stealing or ambush-style play. The asymmetric effect
            (player immune) makes it feel powerful without being unfair to the
            player.
  Flavor:   "You kill the lights. They're not built for the dark the way you
             are. They panic. You don't."
  Notes:    DarknessEffect on enemy: override sight_radius check in ai.py —
            if entity has darknessed flag, cap effective sight_radius at 1.
            Apply to all monsters currently inside the radius at cast time AND
            to enemies who enter the zone later (check each turn).
            Simplest: apply "blinded" or a new DarknessEffect to each entity
            in range on cast, duration 8. Recalculate for entities entering
            mid-effect by checking per-tick in ai prepare step.
            ability_id: "lights_out"

I-B-04  SPEED BUMP (Grease / Slow Zone)
  Classic:  Grease (D&D 5e) / Entangle terrain / Slow terrain (various)
  NIGRL Name: "Speed Bump" — spill something slippery across the floor
  Source:   Deep-Frying L3 activated  (early tree access to grease mechanic)
            OR item "Bucket of Fryer Oil"
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 2, centered on target tile, range 5)
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     None (flat utility; Constitution increases duration by 1 turn per
            4 CON above 8)
  Mechanic: Spill a massive quantity of oil/grease/slick substance on a tile
            cluster. For 10 turns, all tiles within radius 2 of the target
            are "slicked." Any enemy entering or standing on a slicked tile:
            - Movement energy cost increases by 50% (slower to walk through)
            - 25% chance to "slip" and waste their turn (stumble, fall, recover)
            The player can walk through slicked tiles without penalty (you
            know where you put it). Enemies that FALL (25% slip) take 1 point
            of damage from the fall.
  Why:      Grease is one of D&D's most beloved low-level utility spells because
            it's cheap, flexible, and creates emergent zone-control situations.
            In NIGRL's corridor-heavy dungeon: place it in a doorway, stand on
            the far side, and watch enemies struggle through it. Pairs with
            ranged abilities to create a kill zone.
  Flavor:   "You dump the fryer grease in the hallway. They come in fast.
             They don't go anywhere fast."
  Notes:    SlickedEffect on tile: use dungeon hazard tile tracking (like
            scorched tiles from G-06 in main compendium). Enemy step onto
            slicked: apply SlippedEffect (1% turn waste check) OR handle
            inline in ai.py move step. Player immune flag: engine._player_
            immune_to_slick while ability is active (or just exclude player
            entity from the check). ability_id: "speed_bump"

I-B-05  HOLD IT RIGHT THERE (Hold Person/Monster)
  Classic:  Hold Person (D&D) / Hold Monster (D&D) / Paralyze (ADOM/NetHach)
  NIGRL Name: "Hold It Right There" — freeze an enemy on the spot
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L3 activated (alternative slot, or additional ability)
            OR item "Taser" (single-use, non-magic flavor)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 6)
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Lock one enemy in place. The target is Paralyzed for BKS/4 turns
            (minimum 2, maximum 6): they cannot move OR attack. They stand
            completely still. The paralysis breaks immediately on taking damage
            (first hit frees them). Every turn they're paralyzed, they lose
            1 HP from strain (very low — this is not a damage spell).
            Large/boss enemies (those with base_hp > 40) get half duration.
  Why:      Hold Person is one of D&D's most strategically powerful spells
            because paralysis completely negates a target. The "breaks on
            damage" rule prevents cheesy kill-while-held exploitation while
            still allowing exactly ONE hit before they wake up. Classic
            roguelike design tension: you can paralyze, but you must choose
            whether to attack and lose the hold OR keep them frozen while you
            handle other threats.
  Flavor:   "They stop. You didn't use words. You used something older than words.
             They'll move again when you let them."
  Notes:    ParalyzeEffect: before_turn returns True (skip action). Blocks
            movement (modify_energy_gain returns 0 — can't even move into action).
            modify_incoming_damage: if damage > 0, expire self immediately (first
            hit breaks hold). The 1 HP/turn drain: simple tick damage.
            Boss check: entity.base_hp > 40 (tunable).
            ability_id: "hold_it"

I-B-06  SHUT UP (Silence)
  Classic:  Silence (D&D 5e) / Deafen / Bell of Oloiëa (ToME)
  NIGRL Name: "Shut Up" — suppress all alarm calls in an area
  Source:   Stealing L4 activated  (thief silence is thematically classic)
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 3, centered on player)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Street Smarts
  Mechanic: Apply a zone of enforced silence around the player for 6 turns.
            While Silenced Zone is active:
            - No enemy within radius 3 can "call for help" — the alarm_chaser
              AI type cannot ALERT, female_alarm AI cannot broadcast, and
              enemies cannot chain-alert to off-screen allies.
            - ALERTING enemies within the radius have their alert cancelled.
            The player can still make noise (run, fight). This specifically
            suppresses the communication/alarm AI behaviors, not the enemies
            themselves. They can still attack normally.
  Why:      Silence is a beloved utility in games with group enemies and alarm
            mechanics. In NIGRL with alarm_chaser and female_alarm AI types,
            preventing alert-cascades is enormously valuable for managing large
            encounters. Thematically perfect for a Stealing tree — the ultimate
            pickpocket creates absolute quiet.
  Flavor:   "You shut them all up. Whatever they were about to say, it doesn't
             happen. The room stays quiet. You prefer it that way."
  Notes:    SilenceZoneEffect on player: check during ai.py alert/alarm transitions
            — if player has silence_zone effect active AND enemy is within radius 3
            of player, block the alert action. Simple flag check in prepare_ai_tick.
            ability_id: "shut_up"

I-C-01  POP POP POP (Magic Missile)
  Classic:  Magic Missile (D&D) — the iconic never-misses force bolt
  NIGRL Name: "Pop Pop Pop" — three quick shots of raw street magic
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L1 activated  (entry-level spell, basic damage)
            OR item "Wizard Weed" use effect variant
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 10)
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 5 turns)
  Stat:     Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Fire 3 bolts of arcane energy at one target. Each bolt deals
            BKS/4 damage (rounded up, minimum 1 per bolt), IGNORING defense.
            Total guaranteed damage: 3 * ceil(BKS/4). No targeting variance,
            no defense, no miss — always hits.
            At BKS 8 (moderate): 3 * 2 = 6 damage, bypassing defense.
            At BKS 12 (high): 3 * 3 = 9 damage.
            The spell's value is reliability against high-defense enemies.
  Why:      Magic Missile's "always hits, ignores armor" is its identity.
            In NIGRL, where defense can negate a lot of low-power attacks,
            having guaranteed minimum damage is valuable. Low scaling keeps
            it competitive at all levels but never dominant. Classic entry-
            level spell that stays useful across the whole run.
  Flavor:   "Three. Fast. You don't aim. You send. There's a difference."
  Notes:    Execute: deal ceil(BKS/4) damage three times to target, no defense
            check each time (unlike normal max(1, power-defense) formula, this
            calls damage directly without subtraction). Mark is_spell=True.
            ability_id: "pop_pop_pop"

I-C-02  WHAT YOU SAID (Dissonant Whispers / Word of Pain)
  Classic:  Dissonant Whispers (D&D 5e) / Word of Pain
  NIGRL Name: "What You Said" — psychic shock from confronting someone with
             the worst thing they've ever heard
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L5 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     Book Smarts + Street Smarts
  Mechanic: A targeted psychic assault that replays the target's own darkest
            memories at them. Three effects simultaneously:
            - Deals BKS + STS/2 psychic damage (ignores defense)
            - Forces the target into FLEEING AI state for 4 turns
              (they must move away from the player each turn)
            - While fleeing from this ability: target provokes an opportunity
              attack from the player (the player immediately gets one free melee
              attack against the retreating enemy right now, before they move)
            The opportunity attack uses normal melee damage formula.
  Why:      Dissonant Whispers' D&D mechanic (run away + opportunity attack)
            is brilliant because the opportunity attack turns the enemy's retreat
            into extra damage. In NIGRL's turn structure this is implemented as
            an immediate free attack before the flee begins. Strong against
            single powerful enemies.
  Flavor:   "You say the one thing. They react the way people react to that one
             thing. You take advantage."
  Notes:    Execute: apply FLEEING to target, then immediately execute one
            player melee attack against them via engine._player_attack(target).
            The free attack fires in the same turn as the spell, before the
            enemy acts. If the attack kills them: they die, no fleeing needed.
            ability_id: "what_you_said"

I-C-03  TOLL THE BLOCK (Toll the Dead)
  Classic:  Toll the Dead (D&D 5e) — scales based on target's wounds
  NIGRL Name: "Toll the Block" — ring the bell on whoever's already hurt
  Source:   Negromancy L4 activated  (thematically about death closing in)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 8 turns)
  Stat:     Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Wound-scaling damage spell. Damage depends on target's HP percentage:
            - Target at 100% HP: deals BKS/3 damage (weak — they're fine)
            - Target at 50-99% HP: deals BKS/2 damage (moderate)
            - Target below 50% HP: deals BKS damage (full — they're close)
            - Target below 25% HP: deals BKS * 1.5 damage (brutal — the bell
              tolls loud when the end is near)
            All damage bypasses defense (necrotic in nature, seeping in).
            No soul shard interaction (purely damage, not shard-gated).
  Why:      Toll the Dead's wound-scaling mechanic is elegant because it
            transforms the decision of when to cast into a tension: do you open
            with it (weak) or save it for the kill (stronger)? In NIGRL's
            Negromancy tree this occupies a different niche from Reap (which
            costs soul shards) — Toll is the reliable damage-at-range option.
  Flavor:   "You hear it before they do. The bell. It gets louder the closer
             they get to the edge. Right now it's almost deafening."
  Notes:    Execute: calculate target.hp / target.max_hp ratio. Apply the
            appropriate multiplier. Bypass defense (apply to HP directly,
            or use a defense=0 override in the damage call). Mark is_spell=True.
            ability_id: "toll_the_block"

I-C-04  IN YOUR WALLS (Inflict Wounds / Close-Range Necrotic)
  Classic:  Inflict Wounds (D&D 5e) — massive close-range necrotic damage
  NIGRL Name: "In Your Walls" — reach inside them and hurt them from within
  Source:   Negromancy L3 activated (adjacent-range alternative to Reap)
            (Reap is ranged and shard-gated; this is adjacent but shard-free)
  Target:   ADJACENT
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 10 turns)
  Stat:     Book Smarts + Constitution
  Mechanic: A brutal close-range necrotic touch attack. Reach out and touch
            an adjacent enemy. Deals BKS + CON damage, IGNORING defense

            early Negromancy builds, but requires being adjacent (melee range).
            Additionally: gain 1 soul shard on hit (regardless of whether it
            kills — the necrotic contact harvests energy). Does NOT require
            existing soul shards to use.
  Why:      Inflict Wounds in D&D is the high-risk high-reward close-range
            alternative to safer ranged spells. In NIGRL's Negromancy tree,
            In Your Walls fills the "close damage without shard cost" role
            while feeding the shard economy. Creates a pull toward melee for
            caster builds that's uncomfortable but rewarding.
  Flavor:   "You put your hand on them. Just your hand. What happens next
             isn't something they can explain later."
  Notes:    Execute ADJACENT: after target selection, deal BKS + CON damage
            (no defense reduction — call engine._deal_damage_direct or similar
            with defense=0 override). Then engine.soul_shards += 1.
            Mark is_spell=True. ability_id: "in_your_walls"

  Source:   Blackkk Magic L6 activated  OR  Beating L6 activated
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 2, centered on target tile, range 6)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Strength + Book Smarts
