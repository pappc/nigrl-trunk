================================================================================
TYRONE'S PENTHOUSE — FEATURE BRAINSTORM
Zone transition hub between Crack Den (floors 1-4) and Meth Lab
================================================================================

CONTEXT
-------
The Penthouse is a single-floor "pseudozone" (type: pseudozone in ZONE_ORDER) with a
fixed 39x14 layout. Currently it has: Tyrone (gold T, npc_wander AI behind counter),
5 shop items from UNIQUE_TABLE_A displayed on the counter at 2x item value in cash,
and an exit staircase tucked in a bottom-right alcove. The large open floor area
(roughly 30x10 tiles) is almost entirely empty.

The Meth Lab introduces: toxicity damage multiplier (1 + (tox/100)^0.6), radiation +
mutation system, faction reputation (Aldor/Scryer), and harder enemies (higher base HP,
~5-12 cash_drop, more special attacks). Preparing the player for these systems is the
Penthouse's job.

Design constraints:
- No code edits. This is a pure design document.
- All features must be feasible within existing engine patterns:
  entity_type="npc", entity_type="hazard" (hazard_type=...), AbilityDef, Effect
  subclass, MonsterTemplate, FOOD_DEFS extension, PlayerStats fields.
- Meaningful choices, not free power. Each feature should have a cost or tradeoff.
- Fits Tyrone's kingpin/luxury persona and the game's gritty urban tone.
- ASCII rendering for NPCs and props. Graphical tiles (0xE010+) only if visually critical.

================================================================================
FEATURE 1: TYRONE'S PERSONAL COOK — MAMA'S KITCHEN
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A second NPC, "Big Mama" (char: 'M', color warm tan ~(220, 180, 130)), stands at a
cooking station on the west side of the room. She sells a fixed menu of three
high-value prepared foods that are unique to the Penthouse — you cannot find them in
the Crack Den or the Meth Lab. Each item has a cash price and a real tradeoff built in.

POSITION IN LAYOUT
------------------
West wall area, roughly ox+2 to ox+6, rows oy+5 to oy+10 — on the player's side of
the counter, away from Tyrone's shop. The cooking station itself is a couple of hazard
entities (hazard_type="stove") using char='=' in orange-red, blocking movement.

MENU (3 items, fixed per run)

  1. Penthouse Plate — $40
     A full hot meal. FOOD_DEF style: eat_length=8, heal=[25,35], then HoT=CON/4 for
     12 turns. Best pure HP recovery in the game at this point. No downside — just
     expensive. Fills the "safe big heal" slot.

  2. Rat Poison Gumbo — $25
     Looks delicious. eat_length=6. On finish: removes all current toxicity, then
     permanently raises tox_resistance by +10 (additive to PlayerStats.tox_resistance).
     Tox resistance is the most important Meth Lab mechanic — this is a direct counter.
     Tradeoff: spending $25 now vs. buying a shop item. Only useful if the player has
     or anticipates tox buildup.

  3. Nuclear Nachos — $30
     Spicy, glowing, questionable. eat_length=5. On finish: immediately grants +50 rad,
     then permanently raises good_mutation_base_bonus by +0.10 (on PlayerStats) and
     good_mutation_multiplier by +0.15. The player is paying $30 and accepting 50 rad
     (bringing them closer to a weak mutation threshold of 75) in exchange for a
     significantly better mutation polarity going into the Meth Lab. High-risk,
     high-reward for Mutation/Nuclear Research builds.

MECHANICAL IMPLEMENTATION
--------------------------
- Big Mama entity: entity_type="npc", ai_type="npc_wander" (same as Tyrone), speed=20,
  wander_idle_chance=0.95 — she barely moves.
- Interaction: same walk-into-NPC pattern as Tyrone opens the shop. Opens a separate
  "MAMA'S KITCHEN" menu (new MenuState.KITCHEN or handled inline as a variant of the
  shop item popup).
- Foods are not placed as ground entities. They are virtual menu options — on purchase,
  the item is directly added to inventory (same as buying from Tyrone).
- Food effect for Rat Poison Gumbo: add a new FOOD_DEFS entry "penthouse_gumbo" with a
  "remove_toxicity" effect (amount=999 to clear all) plus a new effect type
  "permanent_tox_resistance" {amount: 10} that calls
  engine.player_stats.tox_resistance += 10 on eating completion.
- Food effect for Nuclear Nachos: existing "radiation" effect type (amount=50) plus a
  new "permanent_mutation_bonus" effect type that adjusts good_mutation_base_bonus and
  good_mutation_multiplier on PlayerStats.

BALANCE NOTES
-------------
- +10 tox resistance is meaningful (at 100 tox, damage mult goes from ~1.63x to ~1.49x)
  but not game-breaking. It's one purchase.
- +50 rad pushing you toward mutation threshold is a real cost — you might mutate badly.
- Prices ($25-$40) compete directly with Tyrone's shop items ($60-$200+ for uniques).

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Tox resistance is the single most valuable stat entering the Meth Lab. Nuclear Nachos
give Mutation builds an early advantage. Penthouse Plate lets a low-HP player top off
before descending. All three options are optional but genuinely useful.

================================================================================
FEATURE 2: THE SKILL SAGE — PROFESSOR CRACKSWORTH
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
An eccentric academic NPC (char: 'P', color light cyan ~(160, 220, 220)) in the corner
of the penthouse, sitting at a table. He offers a one-time skill consultation:
spend cash to convert your accumulated potential_exp in a skill into real_exp, bypassing
the normal book_smarts conversion tax. Essentially he "tutors" you on one skill for a fee.

POSITION IN LAYOUT
------------------
Far east side of the room, southeast quadrant, around ox+30, oy+7 — away from both
Tyrone's counter and the exit alcove. A small table hazard entity (hazard_type="table",
char=0xE002 which is the existing table tile) sits in front of him.

MECHANIC
---------
On interact, a menu shows all skills that have potential_exp > 0 (i.e., the player has
earned but not yet cashed in). For each skill it shows:
  [Smoking]   - Potential: 1240 XP — Cost: $18
  [Stabbing]  - Potential: 3800 XP — Cost: $55
  etc.

Pricing formula: ceil(potential_exp / 100) * 1.5 dollars, rounded to nearest $5.
This means a skill with 1000 potential XP costs ~$15, one with 5000 costs ~$75.

On purchase: engine.skills.get(skill_name).real_exp += potential_exp for that skill;
potential_exp for that skill is set to 0. The player may level up if the real_exp
threshold is crossed. Only one skill can be tutored per visit (one-time per penthouse
floor — tracked by a flag on engine).

TRADEOFF
--------
The player could have gotten all this XP "for free" by just spending skill_points
normally. What Cracksworth sells is the conversion rate — bypassing the book_smarts
penalty. For a player with low book_smarts (6-7), the normal SP conversion is very slow
(10% rate). Cracksworth gives the full potential_exp without the tax. Cash cost is the
equalizer.

Low book_smarts players value this enormously (they're normally throttled). High
book_smarts players have less incentive (they already convert efficiently). Natural
self-balancing.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- New MenuState.TUTOR or handled as a specialized inline menu using existing menu
  infrastructure (similar to ITEM_MENU pattern).
- engine flag: engine.cracksworth_tutored = False; set True after purchase; resets on
  new run. (Does not reset on floor change since the Penthouse is only one floor.)
- Dialogue on second approach after tutoring:
  "Professor Cracksworth: Already taught you everything I can. For now."

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Lets low-BKS players unlock skill perks (tox resistance from Meth-Head, mutation perks
from Mutation skill, etc.) that they'd otherwise not reach until mid-Meth Lab. Forces a
cash vs. power decision that's genuinely interesting.

FEATURE 3: THE GUN BENCH — DAQUAN'S CUSTOMS
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A gun modification NPC (char: 'D', color steel blue ~(100, 150, 200)), standing at a
workbench on the east side of the room. He offers two permanent gun upgrade services
(one per visit) and one consumable purchase. Relevant only if the player has a gun
equipped but interesting enough that it's worth gun-running into the Penthouse for.

POSITION IN LAYOUT
------------------
East wall, middle rows — roughly ox+32, oy+5. A workbench hazard entity in front of him
(hazard_type="counter", reuse existing char/color).

SERVICES

  A. Magazine Extension — $35
     Permanently increases the mag_size of the player's currently equipped gun by +3.
     On purchase: engine.player.mag_size += 3; engine.player.current_ammo is adjusted
     proportionally (capped at new max). One service per visit.

  B. Suppressor — $45
     Applies a permanent flag "suppressor" to the player's equipped gun entity
     (entity.suppressor = True). Effect: enemies that are not the direct target of a
     gunshot do not enter ALERTING state when the player fires near them. Implemented
     in ai.py — the existing ALERTING logic already propagates sound; suppress it when
     the flag is present.

  C. Buy: Speed Loader (consumable) — $20 each, up to 3 in stock
     An inventory item (item_id="speed_loader"). Use effect: immediately reloads the
     equipped gun to full mag_size (equivalent to a free instant reload). Useful in
     the Meth Lab's faster-paced combat.

TRADEOFF
--------
Mag extension and suppressor are both genuinely strong for gun builds. The cash cost is
real — $35-$45 competes with Tyrone's shop. Suppressor is more strategically valuable
than mag extension in most situations but less flashy. Player must choose one.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Meth Lab enemies have more HP and hit harder. More ammo before reloading and the ability
to snipe without pulling the whole room are both high-value upgrades. Gun builds go from
"decent" to "actually powerful" with these services.

================================================================================
FEATURE 4: THE FACTION BROKER — SMOOTH CARL
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A smooth-talking fixer NPC (char: 'C', color olive green ~(140, 180, 100)) who can
improve your Aldor or Scryer faction reputation before you enter the Meth Lab. The
Meth Lab's faction system currently starts both factions at -1000 (Archenemy). Smooth
Carl lets you buy goodwill.

POSITION IN LAYOUT
------------------
Near the center of the open floor, roughly ox+18, oy+8. He's just standing there.
Approachable from any direction.

SERVICES (one per visit, choose faction)

  A. Aldor Introduction — $50
     Adds +500 to reputation["aldor"] on PlayerStats.
     Takes you from Archenemy (-1000) to Hated (-500). Enemies will still fight you
     but you won't trigger the Aldor faction's most aggressive escalation behaviors.

  B. Scryer Introduction — $50
     Same as above for reputation["scryer"].

  C. Double Introduction — $90
     Both factions simultaneously. Takes both from -1000 to -500.

TYRONE DIALOGUE TIE-IN
-----------------------
If the player has bought from Smooth Carl, Tyrone's greeting message changes to:
  "Tyrone: Oh, you paid Carl? Smart. Don't waste it down there."

TRADEOFF
--------
$50-$90 is expensive. Faction reputation only matters if you interact with faction
enemies strategically (some have passive_until_hit AI at Friendly+ tier). Going in at
-500 vs -1000 is a mild advantage, not a game-changer. It's prep for players who
understand the Meth Lab's faction system. Ignorant players will not feel punished for
skipping it.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- engine.smooth_carl_used = False; set True after any purchase (disables further deals).
- Reputation system already exists on PlayerStats.reputation dict.
- The reputation tiers and what Friendly/Hombre unlocks are defined per faction in
  existing Meth Lab enemy AI logic.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Directly addresses the faction system — the most opaque new mechanic in the Meth Lab.
Rewards players who've learned how it works on a previous run. Creates a meaningful
investment path for builds that want to leverage faction passives.

================================================================================
FEATURE 5: THE STAT MIRROR — TYRONE'S VANITY
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
An interactable object: a large ornate mirror on the south wall of the penthouse
(hazard_type="mirror", char='|', color silver (200, 210, 220), blocks_movement=True).
On interact, opens a one-time "Stat Reroll" service with no cash cost, but with a
meaningful constraint: you can redistribute your current base stats (CON, STR, BKS, STS,
TOL, SWG), but the total points stay exactly the same. It's a chance to fix a terrible
stat roll before entering the hardest zone.

MECHANIC
---------
Opens a new MenuState.STAT_MIRROR menu that shows all 6 stats as adjustable dials.
Player can increment/decrement each stat within [6, 12] (for the 5 main stats) and [6, 16]
for Swagger (which has a different range since it's independent). The total pool of
(constitution + strength + book_smarts + street_smarts + tolerance) must equal the
current total — no points gained or lost. Swagger is adjusted separately but capped at
the current swagger value + 4 (can't boost it indefinitely).

On confirm: all _base values on PlayerStats are set to the new distribution.
Engine recalculates max HP immediately (since CON * 10 + 30).
One-time use; engine flag engine.mirror_used = False → True.

FLAVOR TEXT ON INTERACT
------------------------
"You stare into the mirror. For a moment you see exactly who you could be.
 Tyrone: Redistribute yourself. Once."

TRADEOFF
--------
No cash cost, but it's one-time and irreversible. The constraint is maintaining the same
total — you cannot improve your build overall, only rebalance a lopsided roll. A player
who already has a good stat spread will ignore it. A player who rolled 6 CON and 12 STR
might desperately want to fix that before the Meth Lab's higher damage threats.

BALANCE NOTES
-------------
- Cannot exceed [6,12] per stat, so you cannot create a degenerate build.
- HP impact: shifting CON from 6 to 10 increases max HP by 40, but costs 4 points
  from other stats. Real tradeoff.
- This is not free power — it's redistributed power with strategic depth.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Toxicity damage scales with incoming damage. Low CON (low HP) combined with high tox is
a death spiral. The mirror lets players correct a CON deficiency without abandoning
their build archetype entirely.

FEATURE 6: THE SHOOTING RANGE — "JUST IN CASE"
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A small practice range in the southeast area of the room (before the alcove) — three
paper target hazard entities (hazard_type="target", char='O', color=(180, 80, 80),
blocks_movement=False) on the east wall. Interacting with any target does nothing
combat-wise, but it triggers a one-time XP bonus event:

  "You put a few rounds through Tyrone's practice targets.
   [Gatting XP: +300] [Sniping XP: +150]"

These bonuses apply whether or not the player has a gun equipped. Flavor: even dry-
firing and thinking about shooting counts as practice.

A secondary use: if the player has any gun equipped and fires at a target (using the
normal F gunfire command targeting a target entity), the target takes no damage but
the shot grants double normal Gatting/Sniping/Drive-By XP for that one shot. This
reuses the existing gun XP system — targets are just entities flagged with
target_type="practice" that the gun system checks for.

MECHANICAL IMPLEMENTATION
--------------------------
- Three Entity objects, entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="target".
- engine.range_bonus_claimed = False. On first player move onto or adjacent to a target
  tile: grant XP, set flag. No interaction key required.
- Alternatively: walk-up auto-trigger — player enters the east area and a message fires.
- Targets do not block movement.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Meth Lab has two gun-focused skills (Gatting, Sniping) with meaningful perks at L2-L3.
Getting a free 300 XP in Gatting (out of 600 needed for L2) meaningfully nudges gun
builds closer to their first real perk. Low cost to implement; no cash required.

================================================================================
FEATURE 7: THE STASH BOX — TYRONE'S SAFE
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A large floor safe (hazard_type="safe", char='S', color=(120, 130, 100),
blocks_movement=True) in the far west corner of the room. Interacting with it opens a
special "STASH" interface: the player can deposit items and cash to be retrieved at the
start of Meth Lab floor 1. Think of it as a carry-forward mechanic — you can commit
resources to survival by "sending them ahead."

But the stash is not free. Tyrone takes a 20% cut of all cash stashed.
Items stash for free (he likes holding onto your stuff).

MECHANIC
---------
On interact: opens MenuState.STASH (new). Shows current inventory. Player selects items
to stash and an amount of cash. On closing the menu:
- Cash amount is multiplied by 0.8 (Tyrone's cut) and stored in engine.stash_cash.
- Selected items are removed from inventory and stored in engine.stash_items (list of
  item_ids).
- On Meth Lab floor 1 spawn: if stash_cash > 0 or stash_items is non-empty, player
  receives them immediately with a message:
  "Tyrone's guy drops off your stash. [$X cash, Y items retrieved]"

Items can be stashed: consumables, weapons (even equipped — unequips first), rings, etc.
You cannot stash the item you'll need immediately — so stashing a weapon means going
into floor 1 of the Meth Lab unarmed until the delivery.

Actually: the delivery happens at floor 1 spawn before the player takes their first turn.
So weapon stashing IS viable (just a bit chaotic).

TRADEOFF
--------
Stashing cash costs 20% but protects it from permadeath on the Penthouse (there's no
combat, but the stash mechanic creates an interesting meta-choice: "do I spend now
or bank for later?"). Stashing items trades now-availability for guaranteed future
possession.

The most compelling use: buy cheap consumables (tox removers, foods) in the Penthouse
and stash them so you start Meth Lab floor 1 already stocked.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Players can create their own custom starting kit for the Meth Lab. A well-stocked
tox-removal consumable supply at the start of floor 1 changes the threat profile of
the entire zone. Forces deliberate planning rather than hoping for good loot.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- engine.stash_cash: int = 0 (persists through floor transition)
- engine.stash_items: list[str] = [] (item_ids)
- engine.stash_delivered: bool = False (set True after Meth Lab floor 1 delivery)
- The 20% cut is applied at stash time, not retrieval time.
- Stashing does not require cash (you can stash 0 cash and just items).

FEATURE 8: THE MUTATION CLINIC — DR. IRRAD
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A shady back-room NPC (char: '+', color sickly green ~(80, 200, 80), dressed as a
doctor) operating out of a makeshift medical area on the west side, separated from
Big Mama's kitchen by a couple of wall-adjacent hazard entities acting as partitions.
Dr. Irrad offers controlled low-dose radiation exposure as a service.

SERVICES

  A. Micro-Dose ($20): grants exactly +40 rad to the player.
     If player is below 75 rad (weak mutation threshold), this might push them into
     range for a mutation. If they're already near a threshold, this tips them over.
     The player sees their current rad before buying (shown in the menu).

  B. Stable Isotope ($35): grants +40 rad AND applies a one-time "mutation insurance"
     flag: engine.mutation_insurance = True. When this flag is set, the next mutation
     that fires will be forced-good (overrides the 67% bad polarity for one roll).
     The flag is consumed on the next mutation trigger.

  C. Rad Flush ($15): removes exactly -60 rad (capped at 0). Pure defensive prep.

TRADEOFF
--------
Micro-Dose: you want a mutation NOW, before the harder Meth Lab enemies, but you can't
control polarity. It might be bad.
Stable Isotope: costs $35 but guarantees your next mutation is good. Expensive insurance.
Rad Flush: defensive, especially if you're sitting at 70 rad and don't want an
uncontrolled mutation during the upcoming harder encounters.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- engine.mutation_insurance: bool = False (checked in mutations.py trigger logic;
  consumed and set to False when a mutation fires while it is True).
- engine.dr_irrad_visited: bool = False; set True after any service to prevent
  repeated purchases (one visit per penthouse, but can buy all three on that visit).
  Actually — allow multiple service purchases on a single visit; just don't reset the
  NPC between visits. Cap: can only buy each service once per Penthouse visit.
- Dr. Irrad dialogue: "You don't ask what's in the needle. That's smart."

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
The Meth Lab is a radiation-heavy zone. Entering with a controlled mutation (especially a
good one obtained via Stable Isotope) gives a substantial power spike. Alternatively,
entering rad-clean (Flush) prevents early uncontrolled mutations during difficult first
encounters. This feature directly targets Meth Lab's defining mechanic.

FEATURE 9: THE FLOOR BAR — TYRONE'S CABINET
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A small bar setup on the east side of the penthouse — a few barstools (non-blocking
decorative entities, char=',') and a bar surface (hazard_type="bar", char='=', brown).
The bar is stocked with a rotation of Penthouse-exclusive drinks not available in either
zone. 3 drinks in stock, randomized per run from a fixed pool. Walk up to the bar
(adjacent to any bar-surface entity) to see the menu and purchase.

No NPC attendant — the drinks are self-serve behind the bar. A small sign entity
(char='"', color gold) labeled "Tyrone's Reserve" floats above it.

PENTHOUSE-EXCLUSIVE DRINK POOL (6 options, 3 appear per run)

  1. 40-Ounce of Resilience — $18
     Drink: grants +20 temporary tox_resistance (via temporary_tox_resistance on
     PlayerStats) for the duration of Meth Lab floor 1 only.
     Implementation: a long-duration effect (floor_duration=True) that hooks into
     total_tox_resistance. Expires at floor 2 of Meth Lab.

  2. The Purple Promise — $22
     Drink: immediately heals 15 HP and grants +1 permanent swagger.
     Implementation: immediate heal + engine.player_stats.modify_base_stat("swagger", 1).
     One of the only sources of permanent swagger outside of mutations.

  3. Static Shock Soda — $15
     Drink: grants +15% dodge_chance for 20 turns.
     Implementation: apply_effect with a dodge_bonus effect for 20 turns.
     Dodge_chance already exists on PlayerStats with set_dodge_chance().

  4. Fermented Lean — $25
     Drink: removes 30 toxicity (existing remove_toxicity effect type), then grants
     +8 temporary tolerance boost for 15 turns. Tolerance is weird — lower is better
     (stronger drug effects). This boosts tolerance (makes drugs weaker) but clears tox.
     Good for a tox emergency, bad for the Nigle Fart/Iron Lung strain builds.

  5. Paint Thinner Special — $10
     Cheap. Drink: 50% chance to heal 20 HP, 50% chance to take 10 damage and gain
     +20 toxicity. Classic gamble drink. Flavor: "Smells like turpentine. Tastes worse."

  6. Gold Bottle ($50)
     Premium. Drink: heals 30 HP, grants +1 to ALL base stats permanently, +1 temporary
     swagger for 30 turns. Most expensive item in the Penthouse besides some shop uniques.
     Luxury item — exists to be aspirational and reward cash hoarding.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
--------------------
- Bar entities: a row of 4-5 hazard entities (hazard_type="bar") forming a surface.
  Interacting with any of them opens the drink menu.
- Drinks are not ground items — virtual menu options, purchased directly into inventory
  or consumed immediately (player chooses, like a ITEM_MENU choice: "Buy / Buy & Drink").
- engine.bar_stock: list[str] = [] (set at spawn time, 3 random draws from pool of 6).
- Each drink tracks a "sold" flag so stock depletes.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
The 40-Ounce of Resilience directly addresses tox damage. Gold Bottle is aspirational
permanent power. Static Shock Soda helps agile builds dodge the harder Meth Lab hits.
The choice of 3 from 6 creates variety between runs.

it quickly. The procedural card selection adds light replay value.

================================================================================
FEATURE 11: TYRONE'S BLACKJACK TABLE — HIGH STAKES
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A gambling mini-game. A felt-covered table (hazard_type="gambling_table", char='T',
dark green, blocks_movement) in the center of the room. Tyrone occasionally drifts to
it (his npc_wander AI). Interacting with the table when Tyrone is adjacent (or within 2
tiles) triggers the game. If Tyrone is too far away, a message fires:
  "Tyrone: (busy right now)"

GAME MECHANIC — SIMPLIFIED BLACKJACK
--------------------------------------
Not a full blackjack implementation. A simplified abstraction:

On enter: player selects a bet from $10 to $50 (in $10 increments).
The engine rolls: player_roll = random.randint(1,10) + int(street_smarts/2)
               house_roll = random.randint(1,15)

If player_roll > house_roll: player wins bet amount (net +bet).
If player_roll < house_roll: player loses bet amount (net -bet).
If player_roll == house_roll: push — no gain or loss, but player gets a consolation
  prize: one random low-value consumable placed in inventory.

Street-Smarts stat gives a real edge (higher STS = higher player roll average).
This rewards the STS builds that won't be as well-served by the standard shop.

Three rounds maximum per visit. Engine tracks engine.blackjack_rounds_played (0-3).

TRADEOFF
--------
Gambling on cash you might need for better upgrades. Winning $50 is meaningful but
losing $50 before the Meth Lab is painful. STS builds get a legitimate edge but it's
not deterministic — there's real risk.

FLAVOR
------
Tyrone's dialogue on win: "Tyrone: Next time I'll take it back."
Tyrone's dialogue on loss: "Tyrone: That's what I thought."
Tyrone's dialogue on push: "Tyrone: Take the snack. We square."

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
Doesn't directly prepare — it's a risk/reward mechanism. A winning session can fund
three extra services from other NPCs. A losing session is a meaningful cost. The STS
synergy rewards a stat that doesn't have many direct Penthouse interactions otherwise.

================================================================================
FEATURE 12: THE REPENTANCE PEW — ONE LAST PRAYER
================================================================================

CONCEPT
-------
A pew bench (char='_', dark wood color (100, 75, 40), blocks_movement=False) against
the south wall with a small candle hazard entity (char='i', orange-yellow) beside it.
On interact: the player kneels. A message fires:
  "You take a moment. The city hums beneath you. Something shifts."

Then: one of three things happens, weighted randomly:

  (A) 60% — Nothing. Just the message. Flavor beat only.

  (B) 30% — The player receives a "floor_duration=True" status effect called "Blessed"
      which grants: +5% crit chance (STS bonus equivalent +1 or so), reduces all
      incoming damage by 1 flat for the floor. Minor but real.
      Effect category: "buff". Display name: "Blessed".

  (C) 10% — A permanent +1 to one randomly selected stat (CON, STR, BKS, STS, or TOL).
      Message: "Something in you hardened. [+1 Constitution]" etc.
      Implemented via engine.player_stats.modify_base_stat().

Interact is one-time per visit (engine.pew_used flag).

TRADEOFF
--------
No cost. The tradeoff is purely probabilistic: you interact and either get nothing or
something. The 10% permanent stat boost is genuinely exciting. The 60% empty result
means players cannot rely on this. It creates a small ritual — players will always try
it, know it might give nothing, and accept the outcome. Classic roguelike probability
tension.

FLAVOR NOTE
-----------
The pew has no religious denomination specified — it could be anything. The tone fits
the game's irreverence: a moment of stillness in a brutal world that might mean nothing,
or might change everything. Tyrone's penthouse having a pew is absurdist but plausible
for a kingpin of his caliber.

WHY IT PREPARES FOR METH LAB
-----------------------------
A 10% chance at a free permanent stat point is the strongest possible Penthouse reward
with no cost attached. It rewards players for exploring every interactable. The Blessed
buff is a small guaranteed-floor buffer. Neither is overpowered — they're small joys
in a hard game.

  to redistribute the adjusted total. Sequence matters.

  one for Meth Lab floor 1 delivery. Guaranteed strong heal at zone entry.
