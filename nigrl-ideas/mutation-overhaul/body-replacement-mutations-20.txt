BODY REPLACEMENT MUTATIONS — 20 NEW MUTATIONS
================================================
NIGRL Roguelike — Mutation Overhaul Appendix #3
Inspired by: Caves of Qud body-slot mutations, CDDA mutation chains
Purpose: 20 mutations that GROW biological replacements for body parts.
         Your body becomes the equipment. You don't wear gear — you ARE gear.

Design philosophy (from Qud and CDDA):
  In Qud, mutations like Carapace, Stinger, Horns, and Burrowing Claws physically
  alter your body. They block equipment slots but grant permanent natural weapons
  or defenses. In CDDA, mutation chains evolve: Claws → Talons → Retractable Talons.
  Your body progressively transforms until equipment is irrelevant.

  These 20 mutations follow that pattern. Every single one grows something on your
  body that replaces or transforms an equipment slot. The mutation IS the item.
  No stat buffs. No percentage tweaks. Actual new body parts with their own rules.

Numbering: Categories get entries 31-35 (extending from previous 26-30 slot mutations).

31. BARBED KNUCKLES — "Bone hooks punched through your skin. Brass knuckles, but forever."
    Body part: HANDS (replaces weapon slot)
    Your fists have grown hooked bone protrusions. You cannot equip weapons — your
    hands ARE the weapon. Base 5 power, weapon_type "beating." Every melee hit has a
    25% chance to hook the enemy in place — the target cannot move away from you for
    1 turn (they can still attack, they just can't flee). This is a "snare" effect,
    not a root — the enemy chooses its action, but movement options that increase
    distance from you are blocked.
    Upgrade:
      L1: 5 power (beating), weapon locked, 25% snare on hit (1 turn).
      L2: 7 power. 35% snare chance. Snare duration extends to 2 turns. Additionally,
          snared enemies take +2 damage from your hits (the hooks dig in deeper when
          they try to pull away). This bonus only applies while the snare is active.
      L3: 9 power. 45% snare. 2-turn snare. +3 bonus damage to snared. Additionally,
          the hooks can grab items off the ground as a free action when you walk
          over them (instant pickup, no turn cost) — your barbs catch everything.
          This includes gold piles. Cap.
    Note: Snare is implemented as a SnaredEffect on the enemy: movement actions that
    would increase distance from the player are blocked (checked via pathfinding
    heuristic — if the target tile is farther from player than current tile, blocked).
    The enemy can still move sideways or toward you.
    Inspired by: Qud's Stinger (extra natural weapon that occupies a body slot) and
    CDDA's Talons (natural weapon that replaces gloves slot).
    Reversal: Remove barbed_knuckles_active; restore weapon slot to empty.
    System hooks: combat.py melee hit (snare proc), effects.py SnaredEffect (new:
    blocks distance-increasing movement), engine.py equip validation (lock weapon),
    inventory_mgr.py (L3 free pickup).

32. CALCIFIED SHINS — "Your lower legs are solid bone now. Steel-toed nothing."
    Body part: LOWER LEGS (replaces feet slot)
    Your shins and feet have calcified into solid bone columns. Cannot equip boots.
    Benefits: immune to leg-targeting status effects (future content — for now,
    immune to the "slow" component of grease tiles). Your kicks deal 3 damage
    as a free bonus hit on every melee attack (the calcified leg swings through
    on the follow-through of every punch/weapon swing). This is a guaranteed
    extra hit, similar to Qud's Stinger extra attack.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Feet locked; grease-slow immune; +3 bonus kick on every melee (armor-piercing).
      L2: Kick increases to +5 damage. Additionally, enemies you kill with a kick
          (the kick is the hit that drops them to 0 HP) are launched 2 tiles backward
          — if they collide with another enemy, the second enemy takes 4 impact damage.
          Corpse-as-projectile flavor.
      L3: Kick +7 damage. Corpse launch 3 tiles, 6 impact damage on collision.
          Additionally, your movement through enemies that are stunned, rooted, or
          snared is free — you can walk through them (trampling them for 2 damage as
          you pass). You end up on the other side. This only works on crowd-controlled
          enemies, not alert ones. Cap.
    Note: The kick damage is a separate damage instance resolved after the main melee
    attack. It uses the player's STR scaling but ignores weapon type. The kick always
    fires, even if the main attack misses (you swing and miss but your leg connects).
    Inspired by: Qud's Horns (body-part attack that fires on movement/charge) and
    CDDA's Hooves (natural weapon replacing feet slot with trample ability).
    Reversal: Remove calcified_shins_active; restore feet slot; remove kick logic.
    System hooks: combat.py melee (bonus kick damage instance), engine.py movement
    (L3 trample through CC'd enemies), combat.py entity_died (L2 corpse launch),
    engine.py equip validation (lock feet).

33. MEMBRANE COWL — "A translucent membrane grew over your head. You can see through it. Barely."
    Body part: HEAD (replaces hat slot)
    A thin, tough membrane has grown over your skull and face like a biological
    hood. Cannot equip hats. The membrane filters airborne contaminants: you gain
    +15% toxicity resistance (the membrane captures tox particles before they reach
    your lungs). Additionally, the membrane is slightly adhesive — small projectiles
    (darts, splinters from future content) stick to it instead of penetrating.
    For now: incoming gun damage is reduced by 2 (flat reduction, the membrane
    catches fragments).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Hat locked; +15% tox resistance; incoming gun damage -2.
      L2: +25% tox resistance; gun damage -3. Additionally, the membrane can
          inflate briefly — grants the Membrane Puff ability (INFINITE charges,
          SELF target, 8t cooldown): inflate the membrane around your head for
          2 turns, during which ALL incoming ranged damage is reduced by 50%
          (the membrane acts as a deflection bubble).
      L3: +35% tox resistance; gun damage -4. Membrane Puff cooldown drops to 5t,
          deflection 60%. Additionally, enemies that hit you in melee while Puff
          is active are splashed with membrane secretion — they gain 5 tox
          (the membrane oozes when compressed). Cap.
    Note: The tox resistance is a permanent passive. The gun damage reduction is flat
    and applied after normal armor/DR calculations. Membrane Puff is a short defensive
    window specifically against ranged attacks — useless in pure melee encounters.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Thick Skin / Bark Skin (natural armor that blocks equipment)
    and Qud's Carapace (activated defensive ability on a body-replacing mutation).
    Reversal: Remove membrane_cowl_active; revoke "membrane_puff" ability; restore hat slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.membrane_cowl_active, gun_system.py incoming damage
    (flat reduction), combat.py tox resistance, ABILITY_REGISTRY "membrane_puff",
    effects.py MembraneDeflectionEffect (ranged damage reduction).

34. GRASPING TENDRILS — "Finger-like growths sprouted from your palms. Rings slide right off."
    Body part: HANDS/FINGERS (replaces ring slots — lose 3 of 5 ring slots)
    Tendrils have grown from your palms, replacing three of your fingers with
    elongated prehensile appendages. You lose 3 ring slots (down to 2). In exchange,
    the tendrils grant +1 melee reach — you can attack enemies 2 tiles away (not just
    adjacent). The tendril strike hits the target tile AND the tile between you and
    the target (line attack, like a short whip). Both tiles take your full melee damage.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Lose 3 ring slots (2 remaining); +1 melee reach (hit 2 tiles in a line).
      L2: Reach stays +1. Additionally, when you hit an enemy at 2-tile range, the
          tendrils grab and pull the enemy 1 tile toward you (bringing them to melee
          range for your next turn). This pull is automatic and free.
      L3: Reach stays +1. Pull on hit. Additionally, the tendrils can disarm enemies —
          when you hit an enemy at 2-tile range, there is a 20% chance to knock their
          weapon to the ground (the weapon entity is placed at the enemy's feet as a
          droppable item). Disarmed enemies deal unarmed damage until they pick it up
          on their next move action. Cap.
    Note: The 2-tile reach attack is resolved as a LINE_FROM_PLAYER targeting with
    length 2. It hits all valid targets in the line. This makes corridor combat very
    strong — you can hit 2 enemies in a line from safety.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Tentacles (extra appendage that grapples at range) and
    Qud's Multiple Arms (extra attack appendages that cost body slots).
    Reversal: Remove grasping_tendrils_active; restore 3 ring slots (empty).
    System hooks: combat.py melee attack (reach extension, line hit), engine.py
    ring slot count (reduce by 3), ai.py (L2 pull on hit), combat.py (L3 disarm).

35. SPORE SAC — "A fleshy pouch on your shoulder. It puffs when you're stressed."
    Body part: SHOULDER (replaces outfit slot — the growth is too large for clothing)
    A biological sac has grown on your shoulder/upper back. It produces fungal spores
    autonomously. When you take damage that reduces your HP below 50%, the sac
    ruptures and releases a spore cloud in a 2-tile radius around you. The cloud
    lasts 3 turns and applies 2 stacks of "confused" to any enemy that enters or
    starts their turn in it (they inhale spores). You are immune to your own cloud.
    The sac regenerates after 10 turns (auto-refills).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Outfit locked; spore burst at <50% HP, 2-tile radius, 3 turns, 2 confuse
          stacks; 10t recharge.
      L2: Threshold <60% HP; 3-tile radius; 4 turns; 3 confuse stacks; 8t recharge.
          Additionally, enemies confused by your spores deal 25% less damage for the
          duration of the confusion (the spores also impair muscle coordination).
      L3: Threshold <70% HP; 3-tile radius; 5 turns; 4 confuse stacks; 6t recharge.
          Confused enemies -35% damage. Additionally, if a confused enemy dies inside
          your spore cloud, a Spore Sprout spawns at their location — a temporary
          friendly entity (10 HP, 4 power, 80 speed, 5-turn lifespan) that attacks
          the nearest hostile. Maximum 2 Spore Sprouts active at once. Cap.
    Note: The spore cloud is rendered as a set of tiles with a pale green '.' overlay.
    dungeon.spore_cloud_tiles: dict[(x,y), int] (turn placed, for expiry tracking).
    L3 Spore Sprouts are summoned entities with is_summon=True, ally AI (attack enemies),
    that despawn after 5 turns.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Spore Cloud (fungal mutation that releases spores on damage)
    and Qud's conceptual Budding mutation (growth that produces offspring).
    Reversal: Remove spore_sac_active; clear cloud; despawn sprouts; restore outfit slot.
    System hooks: combat.py incoming damage (HP threshold check, burst trigger),
    dungeon.spore_cloud_tiles (new dict), effects.py ConfusedEffect (apply in cloud),
    engine.py per-turn (cloud expiry, sprout timer), entity.py (sprout summon creation).

31. FUSED FINGERS — "Your ring finger and middle finger grew together. Then the others joined."
    Body part: HANDS (cripples ring slots AND weapon grip)
    Your fingers have partially fused into mitten-like stumps. You lose 2 ring slots
    (down from 5 to 3). Your weapon grip is compromised — melee damage dealt is
    reduced by 1 (flat penalty, you can't grip properly). Two-handed items
    (large guns, mauls, battle axes) now require a 1-turn "grip adjustment" each
    time you switch targets (first melee attack against a NEW enemy costs +50 energy).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Lose 2 ring slots (3 remaining); -1 melee damage; +50 energy on first
          attack vs new target (two-handed items only).
      L2: Lose 3 ring slots (2 remaining); -2 melee damage. Target-switch penalty
          applies to ALL weapons (not just two-handed). Additionally, your clumsy
          hands make item use take +1 turn (eating, using drugs — you fumble
          with packaging).
      L3: Lose 4 ring slots (1 remaining); -3 melee damage. Target-switch +100
          energy. Item use +2 turns. Additionally, dropping items is now involuntary —
          every 25 turns, you drop a random equipped ring (if any) because your
          fused fingers can't maintain the grip. Cap.
    Note: The target-switch penalty tracks engine.fused_fingers_last_target: entity_id.
    When you melee an entity with a different ID than last_target, the extra energy
    cost is applied. Switching to the same target (continuation of same fight) is free.
    Inspired by: CDDA's mutation chains where hand mutations (Paws, Hooves) progressively
    reduce manipulator effectiveness.
    Reversal: Remove fused_fingers_active; restore ring slots; remove all penalties.
    System hooks: engine.py ring slot count, combat.py melee (damage penalty, target-switch
    cost), item_effects.py (use time penalty), engine.py per-turn (L3 ring drop).

32. DORSAL FIN — "A bony ridge along your spine. It rips through everything you wear."
    Body part: SPINE/BACK (blocks outfit slot)
    A cartilaginous fin has grown along your spine from neck to tailbone. It protrudes
    6 inches from your back. No outfit fits over it — outfit slot permanently blocked.
    The fin serves no useful purpose (it's not sharp enough to hurt enemies, not large
    enough to intimidate). It's purely a penalty. Additionally, enemies attacking you
    from behind gain +1 damage (the fin gives them something to grab and lever their
    strike into). The fin makes you a larger target from behind.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Outfit slot blocked; behind-attacks deal +1 bonus damage.
      L2: Behind-attacks +3 bonus. Additionally, the fin catches on doorways — passing
          through any 1-tile-wide passage costs +50 energy (the fin scrapes the sides).
          This stacks with Spinal Spur's corridor penalty if both are active.
      L3: Behind-attacks +5. Passage penalty +100 energy. Additionally, the fin has
          grown nerve endings — when an enemy hits you from behind, you flinch, losing
          your next turn 20% of the time (pain reflex). The flinch also triggers
          from environmental damage taken from behind (fire, hazards). Cap.
    Note: "Behind attack" check: attacker is not in player's FOV at time of attack.
    The passage check uses the same corridor definition as Spinal Spur: both
    perpendicular tiles are walls.
    Inspired by: CDDA's body mutations that block clothing slots with no upside (Pure
    cosmetic mutations that just lock slots are common in CDDA's negative mutation pool).
    Reversal: Remove dorsal_fin_active; restore outfit slot; remove behind-attack bonus.
    System hooks: engine.py equip validation (block outfit), combat.py incoming melee
    (behind-attack check, bonus damage), engine.py movement (passage energy cost),
    combat.py (L3 flinch chance).

33. CALCIFIED JOINTS — "Your knees don't bend like they used to. Or at all."
    Body part: LEGS (cripples feet slot effectiveness AND movement)
    Your knee and ankle joints are calcifying into rigid bone. Boots can still be
    equipped (the slot isn't blocked), but your movement speed is permanently reduced
    by 15. Additionally, you cannot enter diagonal movement when moving AWAY from
    an enemy that is chasing you — you can only flee in cardinal directions (your
    rigid legs can't handle the lateral movement under pressure). Moving toward
    enemies or with no enemies chasing is unaffected.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: -15 speed; no diagonal flee from chasing enemies.
      L2: -25 speed. Diagonal restriction extends to ALL movement when enemies are
          in LOS (not just when fleeing). Additionally, the calcification makes
          crouching impossible — you cannot interact with items on the ground while
          an enemy is adjacent (can't bend down while being threatened).
      L3: -35 speed. No diagonal movement at all, ever (full calcification — your
          legs only move in 4 directions). Can't pick up items with adjacent enemies.
          Additionally, being pushed/knocked back has a 30% chance to crack your joints
          — you take 5 damage and are slowed by an additional 10 for 5 turns. Cap.
    Note: L3 permanent no-diagonal is devastating for pathfinding. Moving between
    rooms in a dungeon takes significantly more steps when limited to 4-directional.
    Kiting is nearly impossible. This pushes toward "stand and fight" playstyles.
    Inspired by: CDDA's joint stiffness mutations that reduce movement options
    progressively, and Qud's design where some mutations restrict how you navigate.
    Reversal: Remove calcified_joints_active; restore speed; restore diagonal movement.
    System hooks: player_stats.calcified_joints_active, engine.py movement (diagonal
    block based on enemy state / L3 always), engine.py speed penalty, combat.py
    knockback (L3 crack chance), inventory_mgr.py (L2 adjacent-enemy item pickup block).

34. WEEPING SORES — "Open wounds that never close. They're on your hands."
    Body part: HANDS (cripples weapon AND ring functionality)
    Your hands are covered in permanent open sores that seep fluid. Equipped weapons
    become slippery — 10% chance per melee attack to drop your weapon (it falls to
    your feet, costs 1 turn to re-equip on your next action). Ring stat bonuses are
    reduced by 1 each (the sores interfere with the rings' contact with your skin —
    hand-worn magical items need skin contact, apparently). Additionally, every melee
    attack deals 1 self-damage (the impact reopens the sores).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 10% weapon drop per swing; ring bonuses -1; 1 self-damage per melee.
      L2: 15% weapon drop; ring bonuses -2; 2 self-damage per melee. Additionally,
          the seeping fluid makes your hands toxic — items you pick up have a 10%
          chance to become "tainted" (a new flag: tainted=True). Tainted food heals
          50% less. Tainted drugs have 20% chance to do nothing. Tainted ammo is
          unaffected (bullets don't care about your hand juice).
      L3: 20% weapon drop; ring bonuses -3; 3 self-damage per melee. Taint chance 20%.
          Additionally, enemies you hit in melee take 3 tox (your wound seepage is
          genuinely toxic at this point — the only upside to this mutation). This
          makes your disease into a weapon, but you're still bleeding and dropping
          things constantly. Cap.
    Note: L3's enemy tox on melee hit is a small silver lining in an otherwise purely
    bad mutation. Synergy: Chemical Warfare skill benefits from the tox application.
    This creates a tragic-but-functional "my disease is my weapon" identity.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Sores/Scabs mutations that penalize equipment use while the body
    is actively deteriorating.
    Reversal: Remove weeping_sores_active; remove all penalties; clear tainted flags.
    System hooks: combat.py melee (weapon drop roll, self-damage, L3 enemy tox),
    stats.py ring_bonuses (reduce per level), inventory_mgr.py (L2 taint roll on pickup),
    item_effects.py (tainted item check).

35. BLISTERED SCALP — "Your head is a minefield of fluid-filled bumps."
    Body part: HEAD (cripples hat slot)
    Your scalp is covered in large, pressurized blisters. Hats can still be equipped
    but any hat you equip has its stat bonuses reduced by 50% (the blisters prevent
    proper fit). Additionally, when you take a melee hit, there is a 15% chance a
    blister pops — you take 2 self-damage (the burst blister is painful) and any
    enemy within 1 tile takes 3 tox (the blister fluid is contaminated).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Hat bonuses -50%; 15% blister pop on melee hit (2 self-dmg, 3 tox splash).
      L2: Hat bonuses -75%; 25% blister pop; 3 self-damage, 5 tox splash. Additionally,
          popping a blister has a 20% chance to also apply "blinded" to you for 1 turn
          (fluid gets in your eyes).
      L3: Hat bonuses -100% (hat provides nothing). 35% blister pop; 4 self-damage,
          8 tox splash. Blind chance 30%. Additionally, when 3 blisters have popped on
          the same floor, a large abscess forms — you lose 1 CON permanently (tracked
          as blistered_scalp_con_loss: int, resets... wait, no. This should be per-floor
          to avoid permanent spiral). Correction: you gain the "Infected Scalp" debuff
          for the rest of the floor (-1 CON, -10 speed) after 3 pops on the same
          floor. Resets on floor change. Cap.
    Note: Blister pop counter: engine.blister_pop_count: int (per-floor, resets).
    The tox splash on pop creates an interesting dynamic where getting hit in melee
    while surrounded by enemies actually punishes THEM more than you (8 tox at L3).
    Inspired by: CDDA's medical/pathological mutations that create volatile body
    reactions on damage.
    Reversal: Remove blistered_scalp_active; restore hat effectiveness; clear pop counter.
    System hooks: combat.py incoming melee (blister pop roll, self-damage, tox splash),
    stats.py hat_bonuses (reduce effectiveness), effects.py BlindedEffect (L2),
    effects.py InfectedScalpEffect (L3 temp debuff).

31. CHITIN GAUNTLETS — "Your forearms are encased in armor. The armor has teeth."
    Body part: ARMS/HANDS (replaces weapon slot AND loses 1 ring slot per hand = -2 rings)
    Both forearms have grown a chitinous shell that terminates in mandible-like
    protrusions where your hands were. Cannot equip weapons (the gauntlets ARE your
    weapons). Cannot wear rings on those hands (lose 2 ring slots, down to 3). The
    gauntlets have 10 base power (weapon_type "beating") and +3 permanent DR to the
    player (the chitinous forearms can block incoming strikes).
    Every melee attack has a dual-strike: you hit with BOTH gauntlets for a single
    attack action. The first hit deals full damage. The second hit deals 50% damage.
    Total effective damage per attack: 150% of base power.
    Upgrade:
      L1: 10 power (beating), dual-strike (150% per attack), weapon locked, -2 ring slots,
          +3 DR.
      L2: 12 power. Dual-strike second hit increases to 75% (total 175%). DR +5 total.
          Additionally, the mandibles can crush — enemies at 15% HP or less are
          instantly killed by your melee attack (execution threshold). The message
          reads "[Chitin Gauntlets] Crushed!" and the kill counts for any on-kill effects.
      L3: 14 power. Dual-strike second hit 100% (total 200% — double damage per attack).
          DR +7 total. Execution at 20% HP. Additionally, the gauntlets secrete a
          hardening resin — after each melee kill, you gain 2 temp HP (the resin coats
          your arms and hardens briefly). This temp HP doesn't stack, it refreshes. Cap.
    Note: At L3 with 14 power and 200% damage, each attack effectively deals 28 base
    damage before stat scaling. This is extremely competitive with endgame weapons.
    The -2 ring slots and weapon lock are the cost. Melee builds using this never
    need another weapon.
    Inspired by: Qud's Burrowing Claws (natural weapon that replaces hands, dual-purpose
    combat + utility) and CDDA's Chitin/Insect mutation tree (chitinous growths that
    provide both armor and natural weapons).
    Reversal: Remove chitin_gauntlets_active; remove DR; restore weapon slot; restore 2 rings.
    System hooks: combat.py melee (dual-strike resolution), stats.py permanent_dr,
    engine.py equip validation (lock weapon, reduce rings by 2), combat.py (L2 execution
    threshold), engine.py (L3 temp HP on kill).

32. TENDRIL SPINE — "Your spine grew... extensions. They grab things on their own."
    Body part: SPINE/BACK (replaces outfit slot)
    Six prehensile tendrils have erupted from your spine. Cannot wear outfits (the
    tendrils shred any clothing). In exchange, the tendrils provide:
    - Passive: Any enemy that ends their turn adjacent to you is GRAPPLED by a tendril.
      Grappled enemies have -20 speed and cannot move away from you for 1 turn.
      You can grapple up to 3 enemies simultaneously.
    - Passive: Enemies grappled by tendrils take 2 damage per turn (constriction).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Outfit locked; grapple up to 3 adjacent enemies (-20 speed, no flee, 2 dmg/turn).
      L2: Grapple up to 4 enemies; grapple damage 3/turn; speed penalty -30.
          Additionally, grappled enemies have -2 to their melee damage (the tendrils
          interfere with their swing). Your melee attacks against grappled enemies
          deal +2 bonus damage (the tendrils hold them still for you).
      L3: Grapple up to 5 enemies (nearly every adjacent tile); grapple damage 4/turn;
          speed -40; enemy melee -3; your bonus +3 vs grappled. Additionally, once per
          floor, when 3+ enemies are grappled simultaneously, you can activate
          Tendril Crush (free action): deal 8 damage to all grappled enemies and
          stun them for 2 turns. Uses are tracked: 1/floor. Cap.
    Note: Grapple is checked at the END of each enemy's turn — if they end adjacent,
    a tendril grabs them. It's passive and automatic. The tendrils don't distinguish
    between enemies — all adjacent hostiles are grabbed.
    This mutation excels in tight corridors where enemies queue up adjacent to you.
    In open spaces, enemies can choose not to be adjacent (ranged enemies, fleeing).
    Inspired by: CDDA's Tentacle mutations (extra appendages that grapple) and Qud's
    Multiple Arms (extra body parts that act independently in combat).
    Reversal: Remove tendril_spine_active; release all grapples; restore outfit slot.
    System hooks: engine.py per-turn (check adjacency, apply grapple), effects.py
    GrappledEffect (new: -speed, no-flee, dmg/turn), combat.py melee (bonus vs grappled),
    ABILITY_REGISTRY "tendril_crush" (L3), engine.py equip validation (lock outfit).

33. COMPOUND LEGS — "Your legs split. You have four now. Sort of."
    Body part: LEGS (replaces feet slot)
    Your legs have bifurcated below the knee — you now have four lower leg segments
    ending in wide, splayed foot-pads. Cannot equip boots (nothing fits). Benefits:
    +25 permanent speed (four contact points = faster movement). You can move through
    enemies that are 1 size smaller than you (currently: all normal enemies — this
    creates a "trample" movement option where you walk through an enemy's tile,
    dealing 3 damage in passing, and end up on the other side). Trampling costs
    normal movement energy.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Feet locked; +25 speed; trample through enemies (3 damage in passing).
      L2: +35 speed total. Trample damage increases to 5. Additionally, you can
          change direction mid-run without speed penalty — your four legs provide
          stability for sharp turns. In game terms: consecutive movements in different
          directions do not lose the "momentum" bonus (if any future mechanic tracks
          consecutive same-direction movement for bonus effects, your legs exempt you
          from the direction requirement).
      L3: +45 speed total. Trample 7 damage. Additionally, your quad-legs allow
          a powerful leap: grants the Bounding Leap ability (INFINITE charges, SELF
          target, 6t cooldown) — leap to any visible tile within 4 tiles, landing on
          the destination. If an enemy occupies the destination, you land ON them for
          8 impact damage and push them 1 tile in a random direction. Cap.
    Note: Trample movement: when player moves into a tile occupied by a living enemy,
    instead of attacking, the player passes through to the opposite side of the enemy
    (if that tile is free). The enemy takes trample damage. If the opposite tile is
    blocked, the trample fails and a normal melee attack occurs instead.
    Inspired by: Qud's Multiple Legs (extra leg pairs that grant speed and stability,
    block boots slot) and CDDA's mutation chains where leg mutations progressively
    replace human movement with animal locomotion.
    Reversal: Remove compound_legs_active; revoke "bounding_leap"; remove speed; restore feet.
    System hooks: engine.py movement (trample logic — move through enemies), combat.py
    (trample damage), stats.py speed bonus, ABILITY_REGISTRY "bounding_leap" (L3),
    engine.py equip validation (lock feet).

34. FLESH HOLSTER — "A cavity opened in your ribcage. It's... functional."
    Body part: TORSO (replaces outfit slot, creates biological sidearm slot)
    A hollow cavity has formed in your side, lined with muscle fibers that can grip
    and manipulate a gun. It's a biological sidearm — your body literally grew a
    holster INTO itself. Cannot wear outfits (the cavity is on your torso). Benefits:
    you can fire your sidearm weapon as a FREE ACTION once per turn (the cavity aims
    and fires independently using muscle memory). The free shot fires at the nearest
    visible enemy within the gun's range, with -20% accuracy (it's autonomous, not
    precise). This is in ADDITION to your normal action — you attack, and your
    body-holster shoots.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Outfit locked; 1 free gun shot/turn at nearest enemy, -20% accuracy.
      L2: Free shot accuracy penalty reduces to -10%. Additionally, the cavity
          reloads the gun automatically — reload costs are eliminated (the muscle
          fibers strip ammo from your inventory and feed it into the gun). No reload
          turn cost ever.
      L3: Free shot at full accuracy (no penalty). Auto-reload. Additionally, the
          cavity can hold a SECOND gun — you can equip 2 guns, one in the normal
          sidearm slot and one in the body cavity. The cavity gun fires the free
          shot; the sidearm gun fires on your normal gun-fire action. Two guns
          active simultaneously. Cap.
    Note: The free shot is resolved in engine.py player turn processing, after
    the player's chosen action resolves. It targets the nearest visible enemy.
    If no valid target exists, no shot is fired (no ammo wasted).
    L3's second gun is stored in engine.flesh_holster_gun: Entity (separate from sidearm).
    Inspired by: CDDA's mutation chains where biological weapons supplement or replace
    manufactured ones, and Qud's body-slot stacking (Multiple Arms carrying extra weapons).
    Reversal: Remove flesh_holster_active; eject cavity gun (drop to floor); restore outfit.
    System hooks: engine.py player turn (free shot logic), gun_system.py (auto-reload at L2),
    engine.flesh_holster_gun (L3 second gun), engine.py equip validation (lock outfit).

35. CROWN OF THORNS — "Bone spikes erupted from your skull. They're still growing."
    Body part: HEAD (replaces hat slot)
    A crown of sharp bone spikes has grown from your skull. Cannot equip hats. The
    crown provides: +2 DR (the spikes deflect glancing blows). Any enemy that hits
    you in melee takes 3 puncture damage (the spikes cut their attacking limb —
    this is a thorns effect specific to the crown, separate from and stacking with
    Second Skin of Thorns or Spiked Extrusion). Additionally, once per floor, when
    an enemy's melee attack against you would be a crit, the crown's spikes intercept —
    the crit is negated (becomes a normal hit) and the attacker takes 5 damage.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Hat locked; +2 DR; 3 thorns damage on melee hit; crit negation 1/floor (5 dmg).
      L2: +4 DR; 4 thorns. Crit negation 2/floor; 7 damage to attacker. Additionally,
          the spikes extend your headbutt range — grants the Skull Spike ability
          (INFINITE charges, ADJACENT target, 4t cooldown): slam your spiked head into
          an adjacent enemy for 6 + (mutation_count // 3) damage. This attack bypasses
          2 points of enemy defense.
      L3: +6 DR; 5 thorns. Crit negation 3/floor; 10 damage. Skull Spike: 8 + count//3
          damage, bypasses 3 defense, cooldown 3t. Additionally, enemies killed by Skull
          Spike have their corpse impaled on your spikes — the next enemy that attacks
          you in melee takes 3 extra damage from the corpse remnants (one-time per corpse,
          cleared after it fires or on floor change). Cap.
    Note: Crown thorns + Second Skin of Thorns + Spiked Extrusion can all stack on the
    same incoming melee hit. A player with all three could reflect 5 + 7 + 4 = 16 damage
    per hit they take. That's the "thorns build" fantasy — enemies kill themselves hitting you.
    Inspired by: Qud's Quills (passive thorns + activated burst) and Horns (charge attack
    that uses a body part as a weapon).
    Reversal: Remove crown_of_thorns_active; revoke "skull_spike"; remove DR/thorns; restore hat.
    System hooks: combat.py incoming melee (thorns + crit negation), stats.py permanent_dr,
    ABILITY_REGISTRY "skull_spike", engine.py equip validation (lock hat).

STRONG BAD MUTATIONS (31-35)

31. TUMOROUS MASS — "It's growing out of your neck. It has its own pulse."
    Body part: NECK (blocks neck slot, actively harmful)
    A large tumor has grown on the side of your neck, blocking the neck equipment
    slot entirely. The tumor is alive and metabolically active — it consumes your
    resources. Every 10 turns, the tumor absorbs nutrients: you lose 3 HP (it feeds
    on your blood supply). When the tumor feeds, it grows slightly — tracked as
    engine.tumor_size: int (starts at 1, increases by 1 every 5th feed, cap at 5).
    As tumor_size increases, the penalties worsen: each size adds -1 to your maximum
    HP permanently (the tumor displaces healthy tissue).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Neck blocked; -3 HP every 10 turns; tumor grows (size 1-5); -1 max HP per size.
      L2: Feed: -4 HP every 8 turns; tumor grows every 4th feed; max size 7; -2 max HP
          per size (up to -14 max HP). Additionally, the tumor's mass throws off your
          balance — -10 speed permanently.
      L3: Feed: -5 HP every 6 turns; tumor grows every 3rd feed; max size 10; -2 max HP
          per size (up to -20 max HP). -15 speed. Additionally, when tumor_size reaches
          5+, it becomes visible to enemies — all enemies on the floor become aware of
          you (same as Bioluminescence) because the pulsing tumor is conspicuous.
          At size 8+, the tumor occasionally ruptures: 20% per turn to spray toxic
          fluid (5 tox to you and 5 tox to all adjacent enemies). Cap.
    Note: The tumor growth is tracked separately from mutation_levels. Even at L1,
    the tumor keeps growing across the entire run. A long run with this mutation
    could see the tumor at size 5 by floor 6-7. The max HP loss is permanent and
    cumulative. Combined with Accelerated Aging (also permanent max HP loss),
    this can create terrifyingly low max HP caps.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Medical/Pathological mutations where growths progressively
    worsen, and the concept of mutations that are themselves living organisms.
    Reversal: Remove tumorous_mass_active; restore neck slot; tumor_size resets to 0.
    Max HP lost from tumor is NOT restored (tissue already destroyed).
    System hooks: engine.py per-turn (feed counter, HP drain, size increment),
    player_stats.tumor_size, entity.py max_hp calculation (subtract tumor penalty),
    ai.py (L3 size 5+ detection), combat.py (L3 size 8+ rupture tox).

32. PARASITIC ARM — "A third arm grew. It doesn't listen to you."
    Body part: TORSO/SHOULDER (blocks outfit slot, introduces hostile autonomous limb)
    A third arm has grown from your shoulder. It has its own will and its own agenda.
    Cannot wear outfits (the arm tears through everything). The parasitic arm acts
    independently every turn — and it acts AGAINST your interests:
    - 20% per turn: the arm grabs a random item from your inventory and throws it
      2 tiles in a random direction (item lands on the floor, retrievable).
    - 15% per turn: the arm punches YOU for 2 damage (self-harm).
    - 10% per turn: the arm grabs your equipped weapon and un-equips it (weapon goes
      to inventory, not dropped — but you lose it mid-combat).
    Only one of these events fires per turn (checked in priority order).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Outfit blocked; 20% item throw, 15% self-punch (2 dmg), 10% weapon un-equip.
      L2: 25% item throw, 20% self-punch (3 dmg), 15% weapon un-equip. Additionally,
          the arm occasionally grapples enemies and pulls them TOWARD you — 10% per
          turn to pull a random visible enemy 1 tile closer. This sounds helpful but
          is dangerous because it pulls enemies into melee range when you might not
          want them there.
      L3: 30% throw, 25% self-punch (4 dmg), 20% un-equip. Pull 15%. Additionally,
          the parasitic arm has grown strong enough to block your attacks — 10% chance
          per melee attack that the arm intercepts your swing (deals 0 damage, your
          turn is wasted). The arm is literally fighting you for control. Cap.
    Note: This is the "worst body part" mutation. Every turn is a gamble — will your
    third arm sabotage you this turn? The item-throwing is the most common and least
    dangerous (items are retrievable). The self-punch adds up over time. The weapon
    un-equip mid-combat is devastating. The L3 swing interception is pure suffering.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Chimera instability (body parts that act independently and
    unpredictably) and horror tropes of possessed/autonomous limbs.
    Reversal: Remove parasitic_arm_active; restore outfit slot.
    System hooks: engine.py per-turn (priority roll for arm action), inventory_mgr.py
    (random item throw), combat.py (self-punch, L3 swing intercept), engine.py
    (weapon un-equip), ai.py (L2 enemy pull logic).

33. CRYSTALLINE BONES — "Your skeleton is turning to glass. Beautiful. Fragile."
    Body part: SKELETON (cripples ALL equipment slots indirectly)
    Your bones are crystallizing — becoming harder but catastrophically brittle.
    All equipment bonuses are reduced by 25% (the crystalline structure interferes
    with how your body transmits the benefits of worn equipment — armor doesn't flex,
    weapons feel wrong, rings don't settle). This is a universal equipment penalty.
    Additionally, when you take 12+ damage in a single hit, there is a 30% chance of
    a "shatter event": one random piece of equipped gear is destroyed instantly
    (weapon, hat, neck, ring, boots, or sidearm — whatever you have equipped, one is
    chosen and removed from the game permanently).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: All equipment bonuses -25%; 30% shatter at 12+ damage (destroy 1 equipped item).
      L2: Equipment bonuses -40%; 25% shatter at 10+ damage. Additionally, your base
          melee damage (unarmed or weapon) has a 15% chance per swing to shatter
          YOUR hand bone — dealing 3 self-damage (you hurt yourself hitting things).
      L3: Equipment bonuses -50%; 20% shatter at 8+ damage. Hand shatter 25%,
          4 self-damage. Additionally, your crystalline bones emit audible chimes
          when you move — all enemies within 5 tiles are always aware of your
          position (the bones ring with each step). Cap.
    Note: Equipment bonus reduction is multiplicative: a +4 STR weapon gives +3 at
    L1, +2 at L2, +2 at L3 (round down). This affects ALL stat bonuses from ALL
    equipped items. The shatter event destroys the item permanently — it's gone.
    This is the most equipment-hostile mutation in the game.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Fragile mutations that make the player's body structurally
    unsound, and the concept of crystalline/glass bone diseases.
    Reversal: Remove crystalline_bones_active; restore equipment effectiveness.
    Destroyed items are NOT returned.
    System hooks: stats.py all equipment bonus calculations (multiplicative reduction),
    combat.py incoming damage (shatter threshold check, random gear destruction),
    combat.py melee (L2 hand shatter), ai.py (L3 bone chime detection).

34. SYMBIOTIC SKIN — "Your skin is a colony now. It has opinions about what you wear."
    Body part: SKIN (blocks outfit AND hat slots, intermittent ring disruption)
    Your entire skin surface has been colonized by a symbiotic organism — a living
    membrane that covers you head to toe. The symbiote rejects foreign materials:
    cannot equip outfits or hats (the symbiote dissolves fabric on contact). Rings
    work intermittently — every 15 turns, the symbiote shifts and disrupts ring
    contact, disabling all ring bonuses for 3 turns (rings are still equipped, just
    temporarily non-functional). The symbiote provides +2 DR as compensation
    (it's tough, just hostile to your stuff).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Outfit + hat blocked; ring disruption 3t every 15t; +2 DR.
      L2: Ring disruption 5t every 12t. DR stays +2 (doesn't improve). Additionally,
          the symbiote occasionally extends over your hands — 10% chance per turn to
          block item use for that turn (can't grip items through the membrane). Food,
          drugs, abilities still work — only physical item USE (applying salves, reading
          scrolls, future content) is blocked.
      L3: Ring disruption 7t every 10t (rings are non-functional more than half the time).
          DR still +2. Hand block 15%. Additionally, the symbiote has metabolic needs —
          it absorbs 10% of all healing you receive (before it reaches your HP). A 20 HP
          heal becomes 18 HP. The symbiote uses this to maintain itself, offering you
          nothing in return. Cap.
    Note: The ring disruption timer is tracked as engine.symbiotic_skin_ring_timer: int.
    When it fires, all ring bonuses are suppressed for the disruption duration. The
    rings don't need to be re-equipped — they just temporarily do nothing.
    Two slots blocked for +2 DR is a terrible deal. This mutation is designed to be
    very punishing for equipment-dependent builds.
    Inspired by: CDDA's Slime Body mutation (living slime replaces skin, dissolves
    worn equipment, provides minor natural armor as compensation).
    Reversal: Remove symbiotic_skin_active; restore hat + outfit slots; remove DR.
    System hooks: engine.py equip validation (block hat + outfit), engine.py per-turn
    (ring disruption timer), stats.py ring_bonuses (check disruption active),
    item_effects.py (L2 hand block), engine.py healing path (L3 absorption).

35. BONE CAGE RIBS — "Your ribs grew outward. They're a cage. You're inside it."
    Body part: TORSO/RIBS (replaces outfit slot AND restricts sidearm)
    Your ribs have expanded and grown outward, forming an external bone cage around
    your torso. Cannot wear outfits (the cage is your torso covering). The cage also
    restricts arm movement — gun accuracy is permanently reduced by 20% (your arms
    can't fully extend through the rib gaps). The cage provides +4 DR (it's solid
    bone). However, the cage restricts breathing — your maximum energy gain per tick
    is reduced by 10% (you tire faster because your lungs can't fully expand).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Outfit locked; +4 DR; -20% gun accuracy; -10% energy gain per tick.
      L2: +4 DR (no increase). Gun accuracy -30%. Energy gain -15%. Additionally,
          consumable items (food, drugs) take +1 turn to use (hard to bring items
          to your mouth through the rib cage gaps). The cage is increasingly
          restrictive.
      L3: +4 DR still. Gun accuracy -40%. Energy gain -20%. Consumable use +2 turns.
          Additionally, the cage makes you LARGER — enemies can target you with
          ranged attacks at +15% accuracy (bigger target). The ribs also occasionally
          snap under stress: when you take 15+ damage, 20% chance a rib cracks,
          dealing 3 self-damage and reducing DR by 1 for the rest of the floor
          (bone_cage_cracks: int tracks lost DR per floor). Cap.
    Note: The energy gain reduction is applied in engine.py's _run_energy_loop():
    the player's energy_per_tick is multiplied by 0.9/0.85/0.8. This means the
    player acts slightly less often than normal — fewer turns per floor. This is
    subtle but compounds over a long floor.
    The DR is mediocre compensation for all the penalties. This mutation is "armor
    that imprisons you."
    Inspired by: Qud's Carapace (bone/shell armor that provides DR but restricts),
    taken to the extreme where the armor actively hinders every action.
    Reversal: Remove bone_cage_ribs_active; restore outfit slot; remove DR; remove
    all accuracy/energy/consumable penalties.
    System hooks: stats.py permanent_dr, engine.py _run_energy_loop (energy gain
    modifier), gun_system.py accuracy (penalty), gun_system.py enemy accuracy vs
    player (L3 bonus), item_effects.py consumable use time, combat.py incoming
    damage (L3 rib crack), engine.py equip validation (lock outfit).

================================================================
"FULL MUTANT" BUILD FANTASY
================================================================

A player who rolls perfectly could replace their entire body with biological gear:

  HEAD:    Crown of Thorns (SG35) — +6 DR, thorns, crit negation, Skull Spike ability
  NECK:    Neural Jack (SG30) — neck item bonuses doubled (can still wear one amulet!)
  OUTFIT:  Tendril Spine (SG32) — grapple 5 enemies, constriction damage, Tendril Crush
  WEAPON:  Chitin Gauntlets (SG31) — 14 power, dual-strike 200%, execution threshold
  SIDEARM: Flesh Holster (SG34) — free gun shot every turn, 2 guns at L3
  FEET:    Compound Legs (SG33) — +45 speed, trample through enemies, Bounding Leap
  RINGS:   Suction Cups (WG27) — remaining 1-3 rings get +3 bonus each

This character:
  - Has 13+ DR from mutations alone (Crown 6 + Gauntlets 7)
  - Deals 28+ melee damage per attack (dual-strike) plus phantom arm bonus
  - Grapples everything adjacent (tendril spine)
  - Gets a free gun shot every turn (flesh holster)
  - Moves at +45 speed with trample and leap
  - Reflects 5 thorns damage per hit taken (crown)
  - Never needs to find a single piece of equipment

This is the ultimate mutation build. It's also nearly impossible to achieve
(requires rolling 7 specific good mutations, several of which are Strong tier).
But the FANTASY of it existing is what makes the mutation system exciting.
