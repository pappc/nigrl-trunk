EQUIPMENT SLOT MUTATIONS — 20 NEW MUTATIONS
==============================================
NIGRL Roguelike — Mutation Overhaul Appendix #2
Purpose: 20 mutation candidates that REPLACE, BLOCK, or TRANSFORM equipment slots.
Every mutation in this doc interacts with at least one equipment slot.

Slot reference:
  weapon   — melee weapon (main hand)
  sidearm  — gun slot
  hat      — head slot
  neck     — necklace/amulet slot
  feet     — boots slot
  rings    — 5 ring slots (Right Pinky/Ring/Middle/Index/Thumb)
  outfit   — NEW SLOT (shirt + pants + gloves combo, e.g. "McDonald's Uniform")

Design distinction from existing slot-blocking mutations:
  Existing mutations simply BLOCK a slot ("Cannot equip hat"). These new mutations
  REPLACE the slot with a biological alternative that grows, evolves, and has its
  own mechanics. The mutation IS your equipment for that slot. You don't lose a
  slot — you gain a mutant organ that fills it permanently.

Numbering: These extend from the previous expansion. Categories get entries 26-30.

WEAK GOOD MUTATIONS (26-30)
================================================================

26. BONE BLADE — "A sharpened ridge of bone erupted from your forearm."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES weapon slot. You cannot equip any weapon — a bone
    blade permanently occupies the slot. The blade has base 6 power (comparable
    to early-game weapons). It cannot be removed, dropped, or degraded.
    The bone blade's weapon_type is "slashing" and it benefits from the Slashing
    skill tree. Unlike normal weapons, it scales with your mutation count:
    +1 power per 3 total mutations you have (any tier, any polarity).
    Upgrade:
      L1: 6 base power, slashing type, scales +1 per 3 mutations. Weapon slot locked.
      L2: 8 base power. Scale improves to +1 per 2 mutations. Additionally, the
          blade has a 15% on-hit chance to apply "lacerated" (2 stacks bleed, 4 turns)
          — the bone edge is ragged and tears flesh.
      L3: 10 base power. +1 per 2 mutations. Bleed chance 25%, 3 stacks. Additionally,
          the blade ignores 2 points of enemy defense (bone tip penetrates armor).
          This defense ignore stacks with any other armor penetration effects. Cap.
    Note: The bone blade is implemented as a virtual weapon entity that exists only
    in the engine state — it has power, weapon_type, and on_hit fields but no
    physical item representation. It cannot be enchanted, upgraded by items, or
    interacted with via inventory. It IS you.
    At 10 mutations: L3 gives 10 + 5 = 15 power (very competitive mid-game weapon).
    At 20 mutations: 10 + 10 = 20 power (endgame competitive). High-mutation builds
    never need to find a weapon.
    Reversal: Remove bone_blade; restore weapon slot to empty.
    System hooks: combat.py player power calculation (check bone_blade_active, apply
    scaling from mutation_count), engine.py equip validation (block weapon slot).

27. SUCTION CUPS — "Your fingertips are different now. Things don't slip."
    Slot interaction: ENHANCES ring slots. All equipped rings gain +1 to their
    primary stat bonus (a ring that gives +2 STR now gives +3 STR). Additionally,
    you cannot accidentally drop rings (immune to ring-removal effects from
    enemies or bad mutation procs like Vestigial Limb weapon dropping — if that
    ever extended to rings).
    Upgrade:
      L1: All rings: +1 to primary stat bonus; rings cannot be forcibly removed.
      L2: +2 to primary stat bonus per ring. Additionally, you can equip a 6th ring
          (extra finger growth — RING_SLOTS effectively becomes 6 for you).
          The 6th slot is labeled "Left Thumb."
      L3: +3 to primary stat bonus per ring. 6th ring slot. Additionally, once per
          floor, when a ring's passive effect would normally expire or be suppressed
          (e.g., by Permeable Skull or future debuffs that disable gear), the suction
          cups keep the ring's effect active for 3 extra turns. Cap.
    Note: Ring bonus enhancement is applied at the stats.effective_* calculation
    layer — add suction_cup_ring_bonus per equipped ring. The 6th slot requires
    extending RING_SLOTS conditionally in engine.py.
    Reversal: Remove suction_cups_active; remove 6th ring slot (unequip 6th ring
    if one is equipped — drops to floor); remove ring bonus enhancement.
    System hooks: player_stats.suction_cups_active, stats.py ring bonus calculation
    (add per-ring bonus), engine.py ring slot count (conditionally +1).

28. PADDED SOLES — "Your feet grew their own shoes. They're surprisingly comfortable."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES feet slot. Your feet have mutated into thick, padded
    hooves/pads. You cannot equip boots — the mutation IS your footwear. Provides:
    grease tiles deal 0 damage to you (padded grip). Movement through corridors
    makes no sound (enemies in corridors are not alerted by your approach if they
    are in IDLE state — you must enter their LOS before they react).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Grease immunity; silent corridor movement; feet slot locked.
      L2: Fire tiles deal 50% less damage (heat-resistant pads). Silent movement
          extends to ALL tiles, not just corridors — you never alert IDLE enemies
          by proximity, only by entering their direct LOS or attacking.
      L3: Fire 75% less damage. Full silent movement. Additionally, your movement
          speed on grease tiles is INCREASED by 20% instead of penalized (you grip
          and push off — the pads have traction). Also, kicking attacks (if future
          content adds them) deal +3 damage. For now: your melee attacks against
          enemies standing on grease tiles deal +2 bonus damage (stable footing
          advantage). Cap.
    Note: Silent movement modifies ai.py detection — normally IDLE enemies within
    a certain range switch to WANDERING when the player moves nearby. With padded
    soles, this proximity-detection is suppressed. LOS detection still works.
    Reversal: Remove padded_soles_active; restore feet slot to empty.
    System hooks: player_stats.padded_soles_active, engine.py tile damage (grease/fire
    immunity/reduction), ai.py proximity detection suppression, combat.py (L3
    grease-tile melee bonus).

29. PREHENSILE TAIL — "It's got a mind of its own. And a grip."
    Slot interaction: ADDS a virtual sidearm "slot extension." The tail can hold
    a small item — specifically, it can hold ONE consumable item (food, drug, drink)
    in reserve. When your HP drops below 30%, the tail automatically uses the held
    consumable on you (instant use, no turn cost). The tail slot is separate from
    your inventory and sidearm. Think of it as a biological auto-injector.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Hold 1 consumable in tail; auto-use at <30% HP; no turn cost.
      L2: Auto-use threshold increases to <40% HP. Additionally, the tail can hold
          any small item (not just consumables — ammo clips, throwables, materials).
          Non-consumables in the tail slot instead drop at your feet when triggered
          (emergency jettison to lighten your load).
      L3: Threshold <50% HP. Hold any small item. Additionally, the tail can perform
          a free melee attack once per turn on an enemy directly behind you (the
          opposite direction from where you're facing). The tail attack deals 3
          damage and applies 1 stack of the "disoriented" debuff (1 turn stun).
          This rear attack fires automatically — no input needed. Cap.
    Note: "Held in tail" is a new single-item slot: engine.tail_slot: Entity | None.
    The auto-use logic fires in the combat.py incoming damage path, after HP
    reduction, checking threshold. Items must be manually placed in the tail slot
    via inventory menu (new action: "Hold in Tail").
    Reversal: Remove prehensile_tail_active; drop tail_slot item to floor; remove slot.
    System hooks: engine.tail_slot (new field), inventory_mgr.py (tail slot
    management), combat.py (auto-use on HP threshold), engine.py (L3 rear attack
    in per-turn processing).

30. IRON CROWN — "A ridge of hardened bone encircles your skull."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES hat slot. A bony crown has grown around your head.
    You cannot equip hats — the crown IS your hat. Provides: +2 permanent DR
    (head protection), and immunity to the "confused" status effect (the crown
    stabilizes your brain's electromagnetic field).
    Upgrade:
      L1: +2 DR; confused immunity; hat slot locked.
      L2: +4 DR. Confused immunity stays. Additionally, when an enemy lands a
          critical hit on you, the crown absorbs 3 of the bonus crit damage
          (flat reduction applied specifically to the crit damage portion).
      L3: +6 DR. Crit absorption increases to 5. Additionally, the crown emits
          a low-frequency hum — enemies within 2 tiles have a 10% chance per turn
          to become briefly confused for 1 turn (the hum disrupts their cognition).
          You are obviously still immune to your own hum. Cap.
    Note: This is a "trade your hat for a mutation-hat" design. It's strictly better
    than no hat and competitive with mid-tier hats, but worse than endgame hats.
    The confused immunity is the main draw — it's a reliable defensive passive.
    Reversal: Remove iron_crown_active; remove DR; restore hat slot to empty.
    System hooks: player_stats.iron_crown_active, stats.py permanent_dr,
    effects.py apply() confuse immunity check, combat.py crit resolution
    (L2 absorption), engine.py per-turn (L3 enemy confuse aura).

26. FUSED TRIGGER FINGER — "Your index finger locked up. Permanently."
    Slot interaction: CRIPPLES sidearm slot. Your mutated hand can still hold a gun,
    but your trigger finger is stiff and unresponsive. All gun fire actions cost
    +1 turn of delay (you squeeze the trigger and nothing happens for a beat, then
    it fires). Additionally, reloading takes +1 turn. You can still USE the sidearm
    slot — it's not blocked — but guns are significantly worse.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Gun fire: +1 turn delay; reload: +1 turn extra.
      L2: Fire delay +2 turns; reload +2 turns. Additionally, your gun's base
          accuracy is reduced by 15% (the stiff finger pulls your aim off-center).
      L3: Fire delay +3 turns; reload +3 turns; accuracy -25%. Additionally, there
          is a 10% chance per shot that the gun "jams" — the shot doesn't fire, the
          ammo is consumed, and you waste the turn. Unjamming costs 1 additional
          turn. Cap.
    Note: Fire delay means: you press fire, nothing happens for N turns, THEN the
    shot goes off. During the delay you're locked in the firing animation and
    cannot take other actions. This is distinct from Twitchy Nerves (miss chance)
    — Fused Trigger Finger doesn't make you miss, it makes you SLOW.
    Reversal: Remove fused_trigger_finger_active flag; remove all gun penalties.
    System hooks: player_stats.fused_trigger_finger_active, gun_system.py fire
    (add turn delay), gun_system.py reload (add turn cost), gun_system.py
    accuracy (L2 penalty), gun_system.py (L3 jam chance).

27. SWOLLEN KNUCKLES — "Your rings don't fit right anymore."
    Slot interaction: DEGRADES ring slots. Your fingers have swollen — rings are
    painful to wear. Each equipped ring now inflicts 1 damage per 10 turns (the
    ring cuts into your swollen flesh). The more rings you wear, the more damage
    you take. At 5 rings: 5 damage per 10 turns (0.5 DPS). Not lethal (minimum
    1 HP), but a constant tax on ring-heavy builds.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 1 damage per ring per 10 turns.
      L2: 1 damage per ring per 7 turns. Additionally, the stat bonuses from rings
          are reduced by 1 each (a +3 STR ring gives +2 STR). Rings at +1 bonus
          give +0 (no benefit but still hurt you).
      L3: 2 damage per ring per 7 turns. Ring stat bonuses -2 each. Additionally,
          equipping a new ring takes 3 turns (forcing it onto swollen fingers).
          Removing a ring also takes 2 turns (yanking it off painfully, dealing
          3 damage). Cap.
    Note: This creates real decisions about ring management. Do you keep wearing
    5 rings for reduced bonuses and constant pain, or strip down to 1-2 rings?
    Every ring slot becomes a risk/reward calculation.
    Reversal: Remove swollen_knuckles_active; remove all ring penalties.
    System hooks: player_stats.swollen_knuckles_active, engine.py per-turn
    (ring damage counter), stats.py ring_bonuses (reduce by level), inventory_mgr.py
    equip/unequip (L3 turn cost and damage).

28. EXPOSED VERTEBRAE — "Your spine is showing. Through the shirt."
    Slot interaction: CRIPPLES outfit slot. Your spine has mutated outward, jutting
    through any clothing on your back. Outfit items equipped in the outfit slot
    provide 0 defense bonus (the holes in the back negate any protective value).
    Outfits still provide their other effects (if any) but armor/DR from the
    outfit slot is zeroed. Additionally, attacks from behind (enemy not in your
    FOV at time of hit) deal +2 bonus damage (exposed spine is a weak point).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Outfit DR = 0; behind-attacks deal +2 damage.
      L2: Outfit provides 0 DR and 0 stat bonuses (all outfit benefits negated
          except cosmetic). Behind-attack bonus increases to +4.
      L3: Outfit completely unusable (slot locked — cannot equip outfits at all,
          your spine shreds anything you put on). Behind-attack +6. Additionally,
          enemies have a 15% chance to specifically target your back when attacking
          (they aim for the visible spine), which counts as a "behind attack" even
          if they're in your FOV. Cap.
    Note: At L1-L2 you can still WEAR outfits for non-stat effects (if outfits
    have special abilities or tags). At L3 the slot is completely locked. This
    creates a progression from "outfits are weaker" to "outfits are impossible."
    Reversal: Remove exposed_vertebrae_active; restore outfit slot functionality.
    System hooks: player_stats.exposed_vertebrae_active, stats.py outfit bonus
    calculation (zero DR, L2 zero all bonuses), combat.py behind-attack resolution
    (bonus damage), engine.py equip validation (L3 block outfit slot).

29. CORRODED CROWN — "Your skull is dissolving from the outside in."
    Slot interaction: BLOCKS hat slot AND damages you when hit on the head. Your
    skull's outer layer is porous and brittle. Cannot equip hats (nothing sits
    right on the uneven surface). Any melee hit has a 20% chance to be a "head hit"
    — these deal +3 bonus damage (the corroded skull provides no protection).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Hat slot blocked; 20% head hit chance (+3 bonus damage per head hit).
      L2: 30% head hit chance; +5 bonus damage. Additionally, head hits have a
          15% chance to also apply 1 turn of stun (your brain rattles inside the
          weakened skull).
      L3: 40% head hit chance; +7 bonus damage; 25% stun chance on head hit.
          Additionally, the corroded skull leaks fluid — you lose 1 book_smarts
          permanently on acquisition at L3 (cerebrospinal fluid loss, permanent
          cognitive decline). Cap.
    Note: "Head hit" is a sub-roll after a successful melee hit lands. The 20-40%
    chance is checked after damage is dealt, and the bonus is ADDED to the
    already-dealt damage (it's extra, not replacement).
    Distinct from Permeable Skull (CC duration extension). Corroded Crown is about
    PHYSICAL head vulnerability, not mental/CC vulnerability.
    Reversal: Remove corroded_crown_active; restore hat slot. L3 book_smarts loss
    is NOT reversed (permanent brain damage).
    System hooks: player_stats.corroded_crown_active, combat.py incoming melee
    (head hit sub-roll, bonus damage, L2 stun), engine.py equip validation (block hat).

30. BRITTLE SOLES — "Every step is a negotiation with pain."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES feet slot with a WORSE biological alternative. Your
    feet have mutated into hard but brittle bone plates. Cannot equip boots. The
    bone plates provide 0 defense (they crack, they don't cushion). Moving more
    than 15 tiles in a single floor without stopping (consecutive moves without
    attacking or using items) causes a "stress fracture" — you take 3 damage and
    are slowed by 15 for 5 turns. The fracture counter resets when you stop moving
    (attack, use item, wait).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Feet slot locked; 0 defense; stress fracture at 15 consecutive moves
          (3 damage, -15 speed, 5 turns).
      L2: Fracture threshold drops to 10 consecutive moves; 5 damage; -20 speed.
          Additionally, stepping on any hazard tile (fire, grease) deals +3 extra
          damage to you (the brittle plates shatter on impact with hazards).
      L3: Fracture threshold 7 moves; 7 damage; -25 speed, 7 turns. Hazard +5 extra.
          Additionally, each stress fracture also reduces your dodge chance by 5%
          for the rest of the floor (cumulative — the fractures don't heal). A player
          who keeps running could lose 20-30% dodge over a floor. Cap.
    Note: The consecutive move counter is tracked as engine.brittle_soles_move_streak: int.
    Resets to 0 on any non-move action. This punishes long sprints through corridors
    and encourages deliberate, stop-and-go movement patterns.
    Reversal: Remove brittle_soles_active; restore feet slot. Dodge penalties from
    fractures on the current floor remain until next floor.
    System hooks: player_stats.brittle_soles_active, engine.py movement (streak
    counter, fracture check), combat.py (hazard damage bonus), stats.py dodge
    (cumulative fracture penalty).

26. ORGANIC HOLSTER — "Your hip grew a socket. It loads itself."
    Slot interaction: ENHANCES sidearm slot. A biological holster has grown into
    your hip — it interfaces directly with whatever gun is equipped. Benefits:
    reloading is instant (0 turn cost instead of 1). Swapping between weapon and
    sidearm is instant (0 turn cost). The holster also stores 1 extra magazine
    worth of ammo internally (organic_ammo_reserve: int = mag_size of equipped gun).
    When your gun empties, it auto-reloads from the reserve before you need to
    manually reload. After the reserve is used, you reload normally.
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip outfit slot (the holster growth covers your
    entire hip/torso left side — clothing doesn't fit over it).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Instant reload, instant swap, 1 reserve magazine, auto-reload from
          reserve when empty. Outfit slot blocked.
      L2: Reserve increases to 2 magazines. Additionally, the holster's bio-interface
          gives you +10% gun accuracy (the gun is literally part of your body now —
          it moves with your muscle memory).
      L3: Reserve 3 magazines. +15% accuracy. Additionally, the first shot after
          switching to your sidearm deals +50% damage (quick-draw bonus — the
          holster ejects the gun at speed, and the first shot carries that
          momentum). Quick-draw bonus refreshes each time you switch away from
          and back to the sidearm. Cap.
    Note: Reserve ammo is tracked as engine.organic_reserve: int. It fills from
    inventory ammo when you enter a new floor (auto-load). The auto-reload from
    reserve happens transparently — when current_ammo hits 0, set current_ammo =
    min(mag_size, organic_reserve) and subtract from reserve.
    Reversal: Remove organic_holster_active; outfit slot restored; reserve cleared.
    System hooks: player_stats.organic_holster_active, gun_system.py reload
    (instant), gun_system.py swap (instant), gun_system.py fire (auto-reload
    from reserve, L3 quick-draw), engine.py equip validation (block outfit).

27. LIVING ARMOR — "Your skin hardened into plates. It's still alive. It still grows."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES outfit slot. Your torso is encased in a living
    chitinous shell that functions as permanent body armor. Cannot equip outfits.
    Provides: +3 DR that GROWS over the course of a floor. Every 5 enemies killed
    on the current floor, the Living Armor gains +1 DR (capped at +5 bonus DR per
    floor, resets each floor). Flavor: the armor feeds on combat and thickens.
    Equipment constraint: Outfit slot locked.
    Upgrade:
      L1: +3 base DR; +1 DR per 5 kills (max +5 bonus/floor); outfit locked.
      L2: +5 base DR; +1 DR per 4 kills (max +7 bonus/floor). Additionally, the
          armor has a passive thorns effect: enemies that hit you in melee take 1
          damage (the plates have small ridges). This is separate from and stacks
          with Spiked Extrusion or Second Skin of Thorns.
      L3: +7 base DR; +1 DR per 3 kills (max +10 bonus/floor). Thorns 2 damage.
          Additionally, at the start of each floor, the Living Armor sheds and
          regrows — you gain 5 temp HP (the fresh growth is extra padding). This
          temp HP does not stack with other temp HP sources (take the higher). Cap.
    Note: At full L3 stacks on a combat-heavy floor: 7 + 10 = 17 DR. This is
    extremely high and competes with endgame equipment. Balanced by outfit slot
    loss (no outfit stat bonuses or special effects) and requiring kills to ramp.
    On a low-combat floor, you only have 7 DR.
    Reversal: Remove living_armor_active; remove base DR; restore outfit slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.living_armor_active, engine.py kill counter per floor
    (armor growth), combat.py (thorns at L2+), engine.py floor_start (L3 temp HP,
    reset kill counter).

28. PHANTOM LIMB — "You lost the arm. It came back. But not... physically."
    Slot interaction: ADDS a ghost weapon slot. The Phantom Limb is an invisible
    spectral arm that attacks independently. It functions as a second weapon that
    you don't need to equip — it swings at whatever you're attacking. Every melee
    attack you make is accompanied by a phantom strike that deals 4 damage (flat,
    armor-piercing — the phantom fist passes through armor). The phantom strike
    cannot miss. It's a guaranteed bonus hit on every melee action.
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip rings on ONE hand (lose 2 ring slots — your
    phantom arm's living hand is gone, so those finger slots vanish). RING_SLOTS
    effectively drops from 5 to 3 for you.
    Upgrade:
      L1: +4 bonus armor-piercing melee damage per attack; lose 2 ring slots.
      L2: +6 bonus damage. Additionally, the phantom limb attacks enemies ADJACENT
          to your target as well — if you melee an enemy and another enemy is
          adjacent to your target (not you), the phantom limb strikes the second
          enemy for half damage (3). One bonus target max.
      L3: +8 bonus damage. Adjacent bonus target takes full damage (8). Additionally,
          once per floor, the phantom limb can perform a special "Phantom Reach"
          attack — target any enemy within 3 tiles (not just adjacent). This is an
          automatic activation: the first time you would melee an enemy but can't
          because they're 2-3 tiles away, the phantom arm extends and strikes. Cap.
    Note: The phantom damage is added as a separate damage instance after your
    normal melee attack resolves. It bypasses defense because it's spectral. This
    makes it especially good against high-armor enemies.
    Ring slot loss: engine.py rings list shrinks from 5 to 3. If rings are already
    equipped in slots 4-5, they drop to the floor.
    Reversal: Remove phantom_limb_active; restore 2 ring slots (empty).
    System hooks: player_stats.phantom_limb_active, combat.py melee (add phantom
    strike after normal hit), engine.py ring slot count (reduce by 2),
    combat.py (L2 adjacent splash), engine.py (L3 phantom reach trigger).

29. GRAFTED MASK — "It grew over your face. You can't remove it. It has expressions."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES hat slot with a living face-mask. The mask is a
    biological growth that covers your face entirely. It has its own musculature
    and can make expressions independently of your will. Benefits: the mask's
    expressions intimidate enemies. All enemies that enter your LOS for the first
    time on each floor must make a "fear check" — 30% chance they are feared for
    3 turns (flee from you). This only triggers once per enemy per floor.
    Equipment constraint: Hat slot locked.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Hat locked; 30% fear on first LOS contact per enemy per floor (3 turns).
      L2: 40% fear chance; 4 turns. Additionally, the mask can "roar" — grants
          the Graft Roar ability (2 charges/floor, SELF target): all enemies within
          4 tiles are feared for 3 turns. No cooldown between charges.
      L3: 50% fear chance; 5 turns. Graft Roar: 3 charges/floor, 5-tile radius,
          4-turn fear. Additionally, enemies that are feared by the mask and recover
          from fear have their attack power permanently reduced by 1 for the rest
          of the floor (traumatized — they've seen the face and it shook them). Cap.
    Note: Fear check is tracked per-enemy per floor: engine.grafted_mask_feared: set
    of entity IDs (reset per floor). The fear effect uses the standard FearEffect.
    Synergy anti: Redundant Brain (enemy CC immunity blocks YOUR fear on THEM? No —
    Redundant Brain blocks CC on YOU. This fears ENEMIES. No conflict.)
    Reversal: Revoke "graft_roar" ability; remove fear-on-LOS; restore hat slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.grafted_mask_active, ai.py (fear check on player
    entering enemy LOS), ABILITY_REGISTRY "graft_roar", effects.py FearEffect,
    combat.py (L3 permanent power reduction on fear recovery).

30. NEURAL JACK — "A bony port grew behind your ear. It interfaces with... everything."
    Slot interaction: ENHANCES neck slot. Whatever neck item you have equipped
    gains +50% to its stat bonuses (a +2 CON amulet becomes +3 CON). Additionally,
    the neural jack grants you perfect information about the enemy you last
    attacked — after your first melee hit on any enemy, you can see their exact
    HP, power, defense, speed, and active status effects in the message log
    (as if you had an "examine" ability that fires automatically post-hit).
    Equipment constraint: None — enhances rather than blocks.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Neck item bonuses +50%; auto-examine enemies on melee hit.
      L2: Neck bonuses +75%. Auto-examine extends to gun hits as well. Additionally,
          examined enemies take +1 damage from all your subsequent attacks (you
          know where to hit — exploiting weaknesses).
      L3: Neck bonuses +100% (doubled). Auto-examine on all hits. Vulnerability
          bonus increases to +2. Additionally, once per floor, when you examine
          an enemy via the neural jack and that enemy has a status effect, you can
          "download" that effect — it is removed from the enemy and NOT applied to
          you (stolen debuff removal). Fires automatically on first examine of an
          enemy with a debuff. Cap.
    Note: The +50/75/100% bonus is applied multiplicatively to the neck item's
    stat fields. A neck item with power_bonus=3 becomes 4/5/6 at L1/L2/L3.
    The auto-examine outputs to the message log like:
    "[Neural Jack] Crackhead: 45/60 HP, 8 PWR, 3 DEF, 100 SPD [Venom x2, Bleed x1]"
    Reversal: Remove neural_jack_active; remove neck bonus enhancement; remove
    auto-examine and vulnerability bonus.
    System hooks: player_stats.neural_jack_active, stats.py neck_bonuses (multiply
    by level factor), combat.py melee/gun hit (auto-examine, L2 vulnerability bonus),
    engine.py (L3 debuff download on first examine).

26. ATROPHIED ARMS — "Your arms are withering. Your body found other priorities."
    Slot interaction: CRIPPLES weapon AND sidearm slots progressively. Your arms
    are shrinking and weakening. Weapon power is reduced by 3 (applied as a flat
    penalty to whatever weapon is equipped). Sidearm accuracy is reduced by 20%.
    Additionally, two-handed weapons (large guns, battle axes, mauls) cannot be
    equipped at all — your arms can't support the weight.
    Equipment constraint: Weapon power -3; sidearm accuracy -20%; large items blocked.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Weapon power -3; gun accuracy -20%; large items blocked.
      L2: Weapon power -5; gun accuracy -35%. Additionally, equipping or swapping
          ANY weapon takes +2 turns (fumbling with weak arms). This applies to
          both weapon and sidearm swaps.
      L3: Weapon power -7; gun accuracy -50%. Equip time +3 turns. Additionally,
          there is a 10% chance per melee attack that you cannot complete the swing —
          the attack deals 0 damage (your arms gave out mid-swing). The swing still
          costs energy. Cap.
    Note: This is the "your arms are vestigial" mutation taken to its logical
    extreme. It devastates melee AND ranged builds equally. The only combat style
    not affected is ability-based combat (no arm involvement for psychic/mutation
    abilities). This pushes players toward ability-heavy builds.
    Anti-synergy: Bone Blade (mutation-generated weapon) is still penalized by the
    power reduction — the blade is attached to the atrophied arm.
    Reversal: Remove atrophied_arms_active; remove all weapon/gun penalties.
    System hooks: player_stats.atrophied_arms_active, combat.py player power
    (flat penalty), gun_system.py accuracy (penalty), engine.py equip validation
    (block large items, L2 equip time), combat.py (L3 failed swing chance).

27. PARASITIC CAUL — "Something is wearing you as a hat."
    Slot interaction: BLOCKS hat slot with a parasitic organism. A fleshy caul
    has grown over your head — it's alive, it feeds on you, and you can't remove
    it. Every 15 turns, the caul feeds: you lose 2 HP (non-preventable, bypasses
    armor). In exchange, the caul occasionally rewards you with random garbage —
    every 3rd feeding, a random low-value item materializes at your feet (the caul
    regurgitates something it absorbed from the environment). The items are always
    low-tier: a single unit of ammo, 1-5 gold, a common food item.
    Equipment constraint: Hat slot permanently locked.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Hat locked; -2 HP every 15 turns; random trash item every 3rd feed.
      L2: -3 HP every 12 turns; trash item every 4th feed (less reward). Additionally,
          the caul obscures your vision periodically — every 20 turns, your FOV is
          reduced by 2 for 3 turns (the caul shifts over your eyes).
      L3: -4 HP every 10 turns; trash item every 5th feed. FOV reduction every 15
          turns for 4 turns. Additionally, the caul has developed its own hunger —
          when you eat food, 30% of the HP healed goes to the caul instead of you
          (the caul intercepts the nutrition). Cap.
    Note: The trash items are generated from a simplified loot table:
    [(ammo_light, 1), (ammo_medium, 1), (gold, 1d5), (food_random, 1)]. Weighted
    randomly. The items are real and usable, just individually low-value.
    This is distinct from Extra Mouth (which devours YOUR items). Parasitic Caul
    drains your HP but sometimes gives you garbage in return.
    Reversal: Remove parasitic_caul_active; restore hat slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.parasitic_caul_active, engine.py per-turn (feed counter,
    HP drain, trash item spawn, L2 FOV timer), item_effects.py (L3 food heal
    interception).

28. OVERGROWN DIGITS — "Your fingers are thick as sausages. Nothing fits."
    Slot interaction: CRIPPLES all ring slots AND sidearm. Your fingers have
    swollen to twice their normal size. Ring slots are all blocked — you cannot
    equip any rings (0 of 5 ring slots usable). Additionally, your fat fingers
    make gun use clumsy — gun reload time +2 turns, and you cannot toggle fire
    modes (stuck in whatever mode the gun defaults to — usually "accurate").
    Equipment constraint: All ring slots blocked; gun reload +2; no fire mode toggle.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 0 ring slots; gun reload +2 turns; no fire mode toggle.
      L2: Same slot locks. Additionally, your bloated hands reduce melee attack
          speed — every melee attack costs +25 energy (you swing slower). However,
          each melee hit deals +2 bonus damage (the sheer mass of your fists adds
          weight). This is the one "silver lining" — slow but heavy hits.
      L3: Same locks. Melee +50 energy cost; +4 bonus damage. Additionally, using
          items from inventory (eat food, use drugs) takes +1 turn per use (fumbling
          with thick fingers to open packaging). Cap.
    Note: L2-L3 melee energy cost increase means you attack less frequently but
    harder. A player at 100 speed normally attacks every energy cycle; with +50
    energy cost, each melee attack effectively takes 1.5 cycles. Combined with
    the damage bonus, DPS is roughly similar but burst patterns are worse.
    The ring slot loss is the real pain — losing 5 rings worth of stat bonuses.
    Reversal: Remove overgrown_digits_active; restore all 5 ring slots (empty);
    remove gun and melee penalties.
    System hooks: player_stats.overgrown_digits_active, engine.py ring validation
    (block all slots, unequip existing rings on acquisition), gun_system.py (reload
    penalty, block toggle), combat.py (L2/L3 melee energy cost + damage bonus),
    item_effects.py (L3 use time penalty).

29. EXOSKELETAL LOCK — "Your joints fused. Movement is a suggestion now."
    Slot interaction: REPLACES outfit AND feet slots with a rigid exoskeletal
    growth. Your torso and legs are encased in a bone-like exoskeleton that
    provides substantial DR (+5) but at the cost of nearly all flexibility.
    Cannot equip outfits or boots. Your movement is limited to 4 cardinal
    directions only — diagonal movement is impossible (your joints don't bend
    that way). Additionally, your base speed is permanently reduced by 20.
    Equipment constraint: Outfit slot locked; feet slot locked.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: +5 DR; outfit + feet locked; no diagonal movement; -20 speed.
      L2: +5 DR (no DR increase — the exoskeleton doesn't grow stronger, just
          more restrictive). Speed penalty increases to -30. Additionally, you
          cannot move and attack in the same turn — if you moved this turn, you
          cannot melee attack, and vice versa. Abilities are still usable after
          movement.
      L3: +5 DR still. Speed -40. No move+attack same turn. Additionally, changing
          direction costs double energy — if you were moving north and want to move
          east, the first eastward step costs 200 energy instead of 100 (turning
          your rigid body). Subsequent moves in the same direction are normal cost.
          Cap.
    Note: The DR is a consolation prize. The movement restrictions are brutal:
    no diagonals means you take longer paths around obstacles and can't kite
    effectively. No move+attack means you must close distance one turn, then
    attack the next. L3 direction-change cost makes you deeply predictable.
    This pushes builds toward gun-focused (stand still and shoot) or ability-
    focused (don't need to close distance) playstyles. Melee is nearly unplayable
    at L3.
    Reversal: Remove exoskeletal_lock_active; remove DR; restore outfit + feet
    slots; restore diagonal movement and speed.
    System hooks: player_stats.exoskeletal_lock_active, engine.py movement
    (block diagonals, L2 block attack-after-move, L3 direction-change cost),
    stats.py permanent_dr, engine.py equip validation (block outfit + feet).

30. NECROTIC HOLSTER — "Your hip socket is rotting. Whatever you put there rots too."
    Slot interaction: CORRUPTS sidearm slot. Your sidearm slot works, but any gun
    equipped in it slowly degrades. Every 15 turns with a gun equipped in the
    sidearm, the gun permanently loses 1 damage (tracked as gun.necrotic_damage_lost).
    When a gun reaches 0 damage, it crumbles to nothing (removed from inventory).
    The necrosis also spreads to ammo — every reload, 1 round of ammo is "spoiled"
    (consumed but not loaded into the gun).
    Equipment constraint: Sidearm slot functional but items degrade.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Gun loses 1 damage per 15 turns equipped; 1 ammo spoiled per reload.
      L2: Gun loses 1 damage per 10 turns; 2 ammo spoiled per reload. Additionally,
          the necrotic field extends to your weapon slot — melee weapons lose 1
          power per 20 turns equipped.
      L3: Gun -1 damage per 7 turns; 3 ammo spoiled per reload. Melee weapon -1
          power per 15 turns. Additionally, any item you pick up while the necrosis
          is active has a 5% chance to immediately lose 1 point of its primary
          stat (the necrotic field corrupts on contact). Cap.
    Note: Very similar in concept to Entropic Aura (strong bad from the CDDA
    expansion) but specifically targets gun/sidearm rather than all equipment.
    If BOTH are in the game, make Entropic Aura affect weapon/rings/neck and
    Necrotic Holster affect sidearm/ammo specifically. They should not overlap.
    If only one is used, Necrotic Holster is the gun-focused version.
    Reversal: Remove necrotic_holster_active. Damage already lost on items is NOT
    reversed (the rot has happened).
    System hooks: player_stats.necrotic_holster_active, engine.py per-turn (gun
    degradation counter), gun_system.py reload (ammo spoilage), combat.py (L2
    melee weapon degradation), inventory_mgr.py (L3 pickup corruption).

================================================================
DESIGN NOTES
================================================================

REPLACEMENT vs BLOCKING vs CRIPPLING:
  These mutations use three distinct patterns:

  REPLACEMENT: The mutation IS the equipment. It provides a biological version
  of the slot's function, often with unique scaling or mechanics. Examples:
  Bone Blade (weapon), Padded Soles (feet), Living Armor (outfit), Iron Crown (hat).
  Players lose the slot but gain something permanent that doesn't need looting.

  BLOCKING: The mutation simply locks the slot. Nothing can go there. The mutation
  provides some other benefit or penalty unrelated to the slot's usual function.
  Examples: Corroded Crown (hat), Parasitic Caul (hat). The slot loss is a cost.

  CRIPPLING: The slot still works, but poorly. Items can be equipped but they're
  penalized, degraded, or dangerous. Examples: Fused Trigger Finger (sidearm works
  but slowly), Swollen Knuckles (rings work but hurt), Necrotic Holster (sidearm
  degrades). Players face a choice: use the crippled slot or leave it empty.

SLOT ECONOMY:
  With 7 equipment slots and these 20 mutations, a maximally mutated player could
  lose access to most of their equipment. This is intentional — high-mutation builds
  trade gear for biological alternatives. The mutation system IS the endgame gear
  for mutation-focused characters.

  A player with Bone Blade + Living Armor + Iron Crown + Padded Soles has replaced
  4 slots with biological versions. They never need to find a weapon, outfit, hat,
  or boots. Their "gear" grows with their mutation count and floor kills. This is
  the "mutant build" fantasy: you don't loot, you BECOME the loot.
