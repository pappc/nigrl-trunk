MUTATION — 10 Branching Skill Tree Variations
=============================================
Design doc for the NIGRL roguelike (Crack Den / Meth Lab skill).
These are BRANCHING trees with player-choice topology — NOT linear L1-L3.
The player selects one branch at each split and cannot unlock the other.

Mutation system reference:
  - Passive trigger: (current_rad // 50) * 0.001 per energy tick. Min 75 rad.
  - Tier thresholds: Weak 75+ rad (costs 50 rad, 100 XP), Strong 125+ rad (125 rad, 250 XP),
    Huge 250+ rad (200 rad, 500 XP)
  - Good mutation chance: min(1.0, (0.33 + base_bonus) * (1 + multiplier))
    Base = 33%. Mutation L3 (Favorable Odds) adds +0.50 multiplier → ~50% good.
    Five Loco drink: +0.25/stack multiplier. Yellowcake food: 10x trigger rate, blocks Weak.
  - Bad weak: -1 stat, -10% resist, -100 SP. Good weak: +1 stat, +10% resist, +100 SP.
  - Bad strong: -3 stat, -1 skill level, -200 SP.
    Good strong: +3 stat, +1 skill level, +200 SP, +5% briskness, +3 DR.
  - Bad huge: -5 stat, -2 all stats, lose equipment (neck/feet/hat), -1 to 5 random skill levels.
    Good huge: +5 stat, +2 all stats, +5 skill levels, +5% briskness, +5 DR.
  - Rad gain: amount * (1 - rad_resistance/100) * (1 + rad_gain_multiplier_bonus)
  - Player HP: 30 + CON*10. At CON 10 = 130 HP.
  - Swagger defence: int((eff_swg-8)/2).
  - Crit chance: street_smarts * 3%. Energy: 100/tick base. +30 = ~23% faster.
  - Stat names: constitution, strength, street_smarts, book_smarts, tolerance, swagger

Current linear trunk (L1-L3, for reference):
  L1: Mutagen     — +1 all stats, immediately mutate for free
  L2: Unstable    — 20% on melee hit: Unstable buff (20t, +5 rad, +2 melee dmg,
                    hits irradiate enemies 10 rad)
  L3: Favorable Odds — +50% good mutation multiplier (raises good% from 33% to ~50%)

XP source for Mutation: gaining radiation, triggering mutations, surviving bad mutations.

Related trees to synergize with:
  - Nuclear Research: BKS-scaling, Rad Bombs, blocks mutations below 150 rad, Nutrient Producer
  - Glow Up: Rad resistance, Emission (spread rad to enemies), Fallout (20% to irradiate on damage)
  - Chemical Warfare: tox/rad dual-damage builds; status stacking
  - White Power: rad/tox dual resistance options; SWG stacking

Shape glossary:
  Y-split (L3)        — L1, L2 trunk; branch at L3: L3a/L4a vs L3b/L4b. 6 perks, 2 capstones.
  Diamond             — L1 shared; L2a/L2b split; L3a/L3b; L4 convergence; L5a/L5b final split.
  Triple Fork (L4)    — L1, L2, L3 trunk; split at L4 into L4a/L4b/L4c. 6 perks, 3 capstones.
  W-shape             — L1/L2 shared; L3a/L4a and L3b/L4b branches; L5 convergence; L6a/L6b.
  Long Trunk Y        — L1-L4 shared trunk; late branch at L5 into L5a/L5b.
  Inverted Y          — Two starting branches (L1a/L1b pick one); converge at shared L3-L4.
  Ladder/Zigzag       — L1 shared; L2 choose A/B; L3 shared; L4 choose A/B; L5 capstone.
  Trident             — L1-L2 shared; L3a/L3b/L3c split; L3a+L3b reconverge L5; L3c solo.
  Double Diamond      — L1 shared; L2 split; L3 converge; L4 split; L5 capstone.
  Asymmetric Y        — L1-L2 shared; split at L3: A=short (L3a cap); B=long (L3b-L5b cap).

New engine state needed (all variations combined):
  mutation_count (run-wide int, increments each mutation regardless of tier or polarity)
  mutation_reroll_available (bool, per-floor, from Splice ability)
  genome_locked_stat (str, name of stat locked from bad mutation this floor)
  rad_berserker_stacks (int, per-floor; cleared on floor exit)
  adaptive_dr_bonus (int, run-wide; accumulates from Scar Tissue)
  freak_speed_stacks (int, resets on floor; from Volatile Evolution)
  last_mutation_was_good (bool, for Streak mechanic)
  mutation_streak (int, consecutive same-polarity mutations)
  irradiate_splash_radius (int, default 0; set by Rad Spreader)
  contamination_cloud_active (bool; from Fallout Aura capstone)
  forced_strong_next (bool; forces next passive mutation to be Strong tier)
  forced_huge_next (bool; forces next passive mutation to be Huge tier)
  mutation_xp_multiplier (float; additive bonus to mutation XP gain)
  scar_tissue_hits (int, run-wide; tracks hits absorbed after bad mutations)

New PlayerStats fields needed (all variations combined):
  rad_gain_multiplier_bonus (float; multiplicative bonus to rad gain, stacks additively)
  mutation_good_base_bonus (float; additive to the 0.33 base in good chance formula)
  mutation_trigger_multiplier (float; multiplies passive trigger chance per tick)
  mutation_tier_skip_weak (bool; if True, minimum mutation tier is Strong)
  adaptive_dr (int; permanent DR gained from Scar Tissue perk)
  mutation_melee_bonus (int; flat melee damage bonus per mutation count stack)
  rad_to_power_ratio (float; converts current rad to melee damage bonus)
  mutation_count (int; run-wide total mutations, also on engine but mirrored here)

New effects needed (all variations combined):
  UnstableEffect        — existing (L2 trunk)
  SplicedEffect         — after Splice reroll: +2 to chosen stat for 20t, visual feedback
  BerserkerSurgeEffect  — after mutation mid-combat: +6 melee dmg, +20 speed for 8t
  GlowingEffect         — rad tank: constant low-level radiation aura; irradiates adjacent foes 5 rad/turn
  ContaminationCloud    — capstone aura: 3-tile radius, irradiates all in range 3 rad/tick
  AdaptiveShieldEffect  — temp HP equal to (mutation_count * 3) on each floor entry
  OverripeEffect        — 10% on melee hit: trigger forced mutation check immediately
  PrimordialRageEffect  — capstone berserk: +12 melee, -50% defense, lasts 15t; triggered by Huge mutation
  EvolutionaryPressure  — passive: each good mutation adds 1 permanent stack of +1 STR (no UI effect)

New ability IDs needed (all variations combined):
  splice_reroll          — reroll outcome of most recent mutation (1/floor, FLOOR_ONLY)
  rad_burst              — consume 75 rad → deal 8-12 rad damage to all adjacent (ADJACENT, INFINITE cd 8t)
  rad_pulse              — consume 50 rad → irradiate all in sight 15 rad (AOE_CIRCLE r=8, 2/floor)
  inoculate_self         — consume 100 rad → guarantee next mutation is Good polarity (ONCE/floor)
  genome_lock            — protect one stat from mutation degradation this floor (FLOOR_ONLY)
  primal_scream          — consume all current rad → deal (rad/10) damage to all visible enemies (PER_FLOOR 1)
  contaminate_aura       — toggle aura: irradiate all adjacent 3 rad/tick, costs 1 rad/tick to self (INFINITE)
  irradiate_surge        — consume 60 rad → gain BerserkerSurgeEffect + 3 rad to all in LOS (INFINITE cd 12t)
  forced_evolution       — spend 150 rad → immediately trigger one Strong mutation (PER_FLOOR 1)
  absorb_rad             — consume 100 rad → heal 15 HP and gain +3 DR for 20t (INFINITE cd 15t)
  genome_splice          — costs 0 rad → choose between two mutation stat options shown in menu (ONCE/run)


==========================================================================
VARIATION 1: "FREAKSHOW" — Mutation Farming
==========================================================================
Shape: Y-split (L3)
Fantasy: Stack as many mutations as possible, good or bad. Each mutation,
  regardless of polarity, makes you permanently stronger in combat. You are
  gambling with your genome but the house always wins something.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Willing Subject
          |
    L2: Rapid Metabolism
         / \
        /   \
  [BRANCH A]  [BRANCH B]
  A: More Mutations  B: Better Mutations
  L3a: Overexposure  L3b: Selective Pressure
       |                  |
  L4a: Full Freak     L4b: Apex Specimen
   (capstone)          (capstone)

TRUNK:

L1 — WILLING SUBJECT (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +0.50 to rad_gain_multiplier_bonus. You gain 50% more radiation from
    all sources (enemies, hazards, items, abilities). Immediately upon taking
    this perk, gain 75 rad and trigger a free mutation (same as existing L1 Mutagen).
    Also gain +1 CON as a permanent stat bonus.
  Design note: Replaces the existing L1 entirely in this variation. The free mutation
    on unlock is the hook — you see immediately what you're signing up for.

L2 — RAPID METABOLISM (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Passive mutation trigger chance doubled (mutation_trigger_multiplier = 2.0).
    Additionally: good mutation base bonus increased by +0.10 (from 0.33 to 0.43
    before multipliers). You're mutating more often and the odds are slightly better.
  Design note: Sets up both branches. Branch A accelerates even further. Branch B
    focuses on tilting the odds.

BRANCH A: More Mutations (Quantity over Quality)

L3a — OVEREXPOSURE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: mutation_trigger_multiplier increases by another 2.0 (total 4.0x base rate).
    Each mutation (good or bad) permanently adds +1 to a random stat with a floor of
    the current stat value (i.e., bad mutations can no longer reduce stats BELOW their
    pre-mutation value; the bonus from this perk is separate from the normal mutation result).
    In practice: every mutation = at minimum a net +0 stat change, but now also +1 random stat on top.
  Design note: The mutation rate is now very high. Bad mutations still apply their full -stat penalty,
    but Overexposure immediately gives +1 elsewhere. Net effect per bad mutation: -1 in one stat,
    +1 in another. Net per good mutation: +1 stat, +1 elsewhere = +2 spread across stats. Over time
    the player's stat total only goes up regardless of polarity.

L4a — FULL FREAK (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: For every 5 mutations accumulated (mutation_count), gain +1 permanent melee damage bonus
    (mutation_melee_bonus). This bonus is displayed in the character sheet.
    Additionally: mutation_trigger_multiplier increases to 6.0x base rate. At 30 total mutations,
    melee damage bonus is +6. At 50 mutations: +10. This scales indefinitely.
    Visual: When mutation_count crosses a multiple of 5, log message: "Your body grows stronger."
  Synergies: Pairs with Nuclear Research (blocks mutations below 150 rad forces them all to be
    Strong/Huge, each worth more XP and stats). Pairs with Glow Up Fallout passive (20% irradiate on
    damage = more ambient rad = faster trigger rate). Five Loco drink becomes a farming tool.
  Balance: Diminishing returns from mutation costs vs. stat gains. Bad huge mutations can still
    destroy equipment (neck/feet/hat). At high mutation counts, even bad outcomes rarely matter
    because the bonus scaling outpaces the stat loss.

BRANCH B: Better Mutations (Quality over Quantity)

L3b — SELECTIVE PRESSURE (passive + activated)
  Perk type: passive + activated
  Ability: splice_reroll (FLOOR_ONLY, 1/floor)
    When a mutation resolves, you may spend your floor charge to reroll it once. The second result
    is final. Shows a log message: "Splice reroll: [original] → [new result]."
  Passive: good mutation base bonus increases by +0.20 (cumulative with L2: now +0.30 base bonus,
    which at the 0.50 Favorable Odds multiplier = min(1.0, 0.63 * 1.5) = ~94.5% good chance if
    Favorable Odds also taken; or ~78% good chance at just the L2+L3b multiplier of 1.0).
  Design note: This branch is about manipulating the RNG rather than raw frequency. The reroll
    ability is most valuable when a bad Huge mutation is incoming.

L4b — APEX SPECIMEN (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: All future Good mutations have their stat bonuses doubled (Good weak = +2 stat instead of +1,
    Good strong = +6 stat instead of +3, Good huge = +10 stat instead of +5, and +4 all stats instead
    of +2). Bad mutation stat penalties are reduced by 1 (minimum 0 for weak, minimum 1 for strong,
    minimum 3 for huge; equipment loss from bad huge still applies).
    Additionally: +2 STR, +2 CON as permanent stat bonuses.
  Synergies: Devastating with Favorable Odds (L3 existing tree). Stack every good-mutation amplifier:
    Five Loco, Selective Pressure, Favorable Odds, Apex Specimen = near-certain doubled good mutations.
    Nuclear Research's Nutrient Producer pairs well (stat gains from food now augment what are already
    very large mutation stat bonuses).
  Balance: Requires significant investment (L1+L2+L3b+L4b = 4 skill levels). Bad huges still
    cripple you. The equipment loss on bad huge is untouched. High-investment, high-payoff.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 2: "CHERNOBYL TANK" — Rad as Armor
==========================================================================
Shape: Diamond
Fantasy: You don't avoid radiation — you wear it. High radiation means high
  defense, high threat presence, and eventually the ability to absorb rad
  damage like a sponge and redirect it as healing or power.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Rad Hide
       /       \
  L2a: Thick Skin   L2b: Rad Soak
       \       /
    L3: Glow Harder
       /       \
  L4a: Meltdown  L4b: Rad Absorption
   (capstone)     (capstone)

TRUNK / CONVERGENCE:

L1 — RAD HIDE (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 CON, +20% rad_resistance. Your skin has started to calcify.
    Additionally: for every 50 rad you currently carry, gain +1 defense (capped at +6 defense
    from this source, i.e., at 300 rad = max bonus). This is a dynamic bonus checked each
    combat calculation. Stored as a computed property, not a flat stat.
  Design note: L1 teaches the core mechanic — holding high rad is now a defense buff, not just
    a liability. Two stat-scaling paths diverge here.

BRANCH A (early): Thick Skin

L2a — THICK SKIN (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +20% rad_resistance (cumulative: now 40% total from tree). +1 CON.
    Damage from enemy radiation attacks is reduced by a flat 3 (minimum 1). Enemies with
    irradiate on-hit effects now irradiate you for 50% less (e.g., an enemy that inflicts 20 rad
    on hit now inflicts 10 rad). This slows your rad accumulation but keeps you safer.
  Design note: The "safer" path to the convergence. Lower damage risk but also lower passive
    mutation rate because you accumulate rad more slowly.

BRANCH B (early): Rad Soak

L2b — RAD SOAK (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +0.30 rad_gain_multiplier_bonus (gain 30% more rad from all sources). +1 STR.
    Each time you gain radiation (any source), 10% of the rad gained also heals you for
    (rad_gained * 0.10) HP, rounded up (minimum 1 HP per irradiation event, max 8 HP per event).
    You are converting incoming radiation into life force.
  Design note: The "dangerous" path. You're getting irradiated faster and mutating more
    frequently, but every irradiation heals you. Pairs with Glow Up's Fallout passive beautifully:
    20% irradiate on damage means enemy hits heal you back a little.

CONVERGENCE:

L3 — GLOW HARDER (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: The defense bonus from Rad Hide's rad-scaling now caps at +10 instead of +6 (requires
    500 rad for full bonus). Additionally, your visible glow (flavor) causes enemies within 3 tiles
    to gain 5 rad per turn they remain adjacent (irradiate_splash_radius = 1 tile, 5 rad/turn).
    This is a passive aura — always on while you have 75+ rad.
  Design note: Convergence point rewards staying at high rad. The passive irradiation of nearby
    enemies sets up both capstones differently.

CAPSTONE SPLIT:

L4a — MELTDOWN (activated, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: primal_scream (PER_FLOOR 1)
    Consume ALL current rad (minimum 100 to activate). Deal (rad_consumed / 10) damage to every
    visible enemy — raw rad damage, ignores defense, ignores DR. At 200 rad: 20 damage to all
    visible enemies. At 500 rad: 50 damage to all visible enemies.
    After the burst: your rad drops to 0. You gain no defense bonus until you rebuild rad.
    Gain PrimordialRageEffect: +12 melee damage, -50% defense for 15t (post-meltdown frenzy).
  Design note: Nuclear option. You've been building rad all floor as a defense stat, then you
    cash it all in for massive AOE damage. Requires careful timing — you lose your defense buffer
    immediately after. Best used when multiple enemies are nearby.
  Synergies: Glow Up's Emission (spread rad to enemies before detonating = enemies are also irradiated
    when hit). Nuclear Research's Rad Bomb + Meltdown on same floor = massive rad burst sequence.

L4b — RAD ABSORPTION (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: You no longer take HP damage from radiation-based sources (enemy rad attacks, rad hazards,
    rad food, rad effects). Instead, all incoming rad is treated as a direct HP regen trigger:
    for every 10 rad you would have gained, heal 1 HP. Bad mutation stat losses are reduced by 2
    (minimum 0 for weak). Your rad cap effectively becomes a second HP pool.
    Additionally: +3 CON, +10% rad_resistance (cumulative).
  Design note: The ultimate defensive capstone. Radiation is now net-positive. You gain rad freely,
    it translates to healing, you hold high rad for defense bonus, and bad mutations are softened.
    Weakness: no damage output from this branch. Pure survival.
  Synergies: Pairs with Rad Soak (L2b path) for maximum healing-from-rad. Pairs with Glow Up Emission
    for irradiating enemies while you sit in the middle absorbing everything.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 3: "SPLICE AND DICE" — Mutation Control
==========================================================================
Shape: Triple Fork (L4)
Fantasy: You study the science of mutation. You learn to choose your mutations,
  lock stats from degradation, and eventually unlock one of three mastery paths:
  combat supremacy, total stat control, or one perfect mutation per run.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Research Subject
          |
    L2: Genome Mapping
          |
    L3: Directed Evolution
       /    |    \
      /     |     \
  L4a:   L4b:   L4c:
  Combat  Perfect  One True
  Genome  Balance  Splice
 (cap)    (cap)    (cap)

TRUNK:

L1 — RESEARCH SUBJECT (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 BKS, +1 STR. When any mutation (passive or triggered) occurs, you see the outcome
    in a detailed log: "Mutation resolved: [polarity] [tier] → [changes]." This is purely informational
    in vanilla play but the key is the bonus: after seeing any mutation resolve, gain +50 XP toward
    Mutation skill (in addition to normal XP). Effectively doubles mutation XP gain.
  Design note: Flavor perk. Low-power but establishes the "scientist studying themselves" identity.
    The +50 mutation XP per event means you level this tree faster.

L2 — GENOME MAPPING (passive + activated)
  Perk type: passive + activated
  Ability: genome_lock (FLOOR_ONLY, 1/floor)
    Before any mutation this floor, you may declare one stat to be "locked."
    If the upcoming mutation would reduce that stat, the reduction is blocked (the mutation still
    fires and costs rad, but the stat penalty is negated for the locked stat). Effectively
    insurance against losing your most important stat.
  Passive: +0.15 to mutation_good_base_bonus. Good mutation chance now ~48% base (before multipliers).
  Design note: Genome Lock is the defining perk of this variation. Managing your most critical stats
    against the randomness of mutations is the core skill expression.

L3 — DIRECTED EVOLUTION (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: You gain the ability to choose mutation TIER (but not polarity or outcome):
    Once per floor, when a passive mutation triggers, you may spend an additional 75 rad to force
    it up to the next tier (Weak→Strong costs +75 rad; Strong→Huge costs +75 rad; Huge stays Huge).
    If you don't have the additional rad, the mutation resolves normally.
    Also: +0.20 to mutation_good_base_bonus (cumulative: now ~68% good base before multipliers).
  Design note: Significant power. Spending 125+75=200 rad to force a Strong mutation that's also
    68% likely to be good is an attractive proposition. Synergy with Nuclear Research which can
    block Weak mutations (let NucResearch handle the floor requirement, Directed Evolution
    upgrades whatever tier you're at to Huge if you have the rad).

BRANCH A: Combat Genome

L4a — COMBAT GENOME (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: Every mutation (any polarity, any tier) permanently grants +1 melee damage (mutation_melee_bonus).
    This bonus is multiplicative with weapon damage, not additive (applied after str_scaling).
    At 10 total mutations: +10 raw melee bonus. At 20: +20. Uncapped.
    Additionally: +2 STR, +1 STS. Crits gained from mutations that award STS upgrades now
    benefit from double crit chance for the remainder of the run (crit_multiplier_bonus).
  Synergies: Pairs devastatingly with any mutation-frequency increase. FREAKSHOW (Variation 1)
    combined with Combat Genome would create extremely high-damage late runs. Also pairs with
    Glow Up's Fallout (damage triggers irradiation which speeds up mutation triggers).

BRANCH B: Perfect Balance

L4b — PERFECT BALANCE (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: Bad mutations of Weak or Strong tier no longer reduce stats. Instead, bad weak mutations
    grant +100 SP (same as good weak) and bad strong mutations grant +200 SP + +1 skill level
    (same as good strong). You've learned to absorb the chaos constructively.
    Bad Huge mutations still lose equipment — this is the remaining risk that justifies the power.
    Additionally: Good mutation stat bonuses are increased by +1 (Good weak = +2, Good strong = +4).
    +2 BKS permanent.
  Design note: Potentially the strongest capstone in the entire document if the run goes long enough.
    Almost every mutation outcome is now net-positive. The only real failure state is bad Huge,
    which still punishes. Works best with low rad (staying near 75-125 range to trigger only Weak/Strong).
  Synergies: Pairs with Nuclear Research "Inoculation" (blocks mutations below 150 rad) to force
    only Strong+ — but then Perfect Balance doesn't neutralize Strong bad mutations' stat loss
    (it does reduce it). The real combo is Perfect Balance + moderate rad so you hit Weak/Strong
    with high frequency and near-zero downside.

BRANCH C: One True Splice

L4c — ONE TRUE SPLICE (activated, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: genome_splice (ONCE per run)
    Opens a selection menu: choose between two mutation options. Each option shows:
    "[stat name]: [current value] → [new value]" and "[polarity] [tier] result."
    You choose one, and it applies immediately consuming the normal rad cost for its tier.
    The chosen mutation is guaranteed good, guaranteed of the tier you select (Weak/Strong/Huge),
    and you pick which stat is affected. This is the only ability in the game that lets you
    fully control a mutation outcome.
    On activation: gain forced_strong_next = False, forced_huge_next = False (this supersedes them).
    Gain +5 skill levels distributed randomly across your non-maxed skills (same as good Huge).
    Gain +2 all stats (same as good Huge). No rad cost on activation; the genome_splice ability
    bypasses the rad cost entirely.
  Design note: ONCE per run. This is essentially a guaranteed perfect Huge mutation whenever you
    want it. The value is in timing: use it when you need a specific stat boosted, or when all
    your stats are low from bad mutations. The +5 skill levels make it attractive mid-run.
  Balance: The inability to repeat this ability is the limiting factor. Perfect Balance (L4b) will
    outscale this over a long run; One True Splice is better for runs that don't last long enough
    to accumulate many mutations.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 4: "VOLATILE EVOLUTION" — Rad Berserker
==========================================================================
Shape: W-shape
Fantasy: Each mutation makes you faster and more dangerous in combat, but
  the clock is always ticking. You live in the space between mutations —
  burning hot, burning fast, burning out.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Adrenaline Splice   L2: Freak Speed
           |   \           /   |
           |    \         /    |
           |  [BRANCH A]  [BRANCH B]
           |   L3a: Hot  L3b: Burnout
           |   L4a: Mach L4b: Death
           |    \         /    |
           |     \       /     |
    L5: Apex Predator (convergence)
           / \
          /   \
    L6a: Speed  L6b: Explosion
    God          Hazard
    (capstone)   (capstone)

TRUNK (shared):

L1 — ADRENALINE SPLICE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Each mutation (any polarity, any tier) grants a 12-turn speed buff:
    +30 energy gain per tick (same value as a full speed boost item). This buff does not stack
    but refreshes on each new mutation. You are rewarded for mutating frequently.
    Also: first mutation of each floor always triggers this buff regardless of passive trigger timing.
  Design note: Immediately makes "having mutations happen" feel good. Every mutation event is now
    also a combat burst. Sets up the W-shape's two branches which explore how to exploit the speed.

L2 — FREAK SPEED (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 STS, +1 STR. Speed buff from Adrenaline Splice extended to 20 turns (up from 12).
    Additionally: freak_speed_stacks tracks how many speed buffs have occurred this floor.
    Each stack of freak_speed_stacks adds +2 melee damage while at least one stack is active
    (i.e., while you have the Adrenaline Splice speed buff running). At 3 stacks = +6 melee.
    Resets on floor exit.
  Design note: Now mutations mid-combat are explicitly powerful. The more mutations you trigger
    mid-fight, the more damage you deal.

BRANCH A: Hot (mid-run)

L3a — SCORCHED GENOME (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: While the Adrenaline Splice speed buff is active, all your melee attacks have a 20%
    chance to inflict BerserkerSurgeEffect on yourself (+6 melee dmg, +20 speed for 8t) when
    you score a critical hit. This is a crit-on-crit effect. Also: +0.30 rad_gain_multiplier_bonus.
    You're building rad faster which means more mutations which means more speed buffs.

L4a — MACH FREAK (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: While freak_speed_stacks >= 2 this floor, your attack cost is reduced by 25%
    (attacks cost 75 energy instead of 100). Effectively 33% faster attack rate when mutating
    frequently. +1 STS.

BRANCH B: Burnout (mid-run)

L3b — UNSTABLE CORE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: The Adrenaline Splice speed buff now also grants +4 melee damage (on top of the speed).
    BUT: when the buff expires, take 8 HP damage (the crash). This is unavoidable — mutation speed
    buffs now have a painful ending. In exchange, while the buff is active, all incoming damage is
    reduced by 20%.

L4b — DEATH OR GLORY (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Each time you take the Unstable Core crash damage (8 HP on buff expiry), permanently
    gain +1 CON (min 1 CON gained). You are literally getting tougher from the punishment.
    Also: the crash damage is now 12 HP but triggers a mutation check immediately (consume
    the crash as if it were a mutation trigger — doesn't cost rad, doesn't award XP, just
    fires a weak mutation check at current rad thresholds). You crash, you mutate, you surge again.

CONVERGENCE:

L5 — APEX PREDATOR (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 STR, +1 STS. freak_speed_stacks cap raised to 8 (was 4 implicitly).
    While freak_speed_stacks >= 3, gain +15% crit chance (multiplicative with existing crit chance).
    At STS 10: base crit = 30%. With this perk at 3+ stacks: 30% * 1.15 = 34.5% crit chance.
    Each crit scored while speed-buffed adds 5 rad. Crits now fuel more mutations which fuel more speed.
  Design note: The convergence is where both mid-tree paths become equally valuable. Branch A
    (more attacks, cheaper attacks) feeds crits which feed rad which feed mutations. Branch B
    (harder crash, tougher body) now has enough CON to survive the crashes.

CAPSTONE SPLIT:

L6a — SPEED GOD (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: The Adrenaline Splice speed buff duration is now permanent until the end of the floor
    (it no longer has a 20-turn timer; instead it lasts until floor exit or until 5 minutes of
    real-time inactivity as an anti-softlock). While this buff is active, movement costs 50 energy
    instead of 100 (double movement speed). +2 STS.
    You essentially move and attack at double speed any floor where you've mutated at least once.
  Balance: Permadeath tension — if the floor is short you may not mutate before combat starts.
    Running Nuclear Research to block low-tier mutations means you might not trigger at all on a
    floor where you never hit 150 rad.

L6b — EXPLOSION HAZARD (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: When you would take crash damage (from Unstable Core / Death Or Glory), instead of
    just taking HP damage, you also explode: 5 rad damage to all adjacent enemies and
    irradiate them for 20 rad each. The crash is now a weapon.
    Additionally: freak_speed_stacks never decrement — once earned this floor, stacks persist
    until floor exit even after speed buffs end. Melee damage bonus from freak_speed_stacks is now
    permanent (doesn't require buff to be active): each stack = +2 melee bonus all floor.
    This path pairs with Death Or Glory: each crash = explosion = more kills = more exp = more
    rad from combat = more mutations = more speed buffs.
  Design note: The wild path. You become an exploding mutation engine. Weaker than Speed God
    for pure speed but more dynamic in actual combat.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 5: "BODY HORROR" — Mutation Stacking
==========================================================================
Shape: Long Trunk Y
Fantasy: You obsessively track every single mutation that has ever happened
  to you. The mutations compound each other. By the late run, your body
  is a catalogue of modifications and each new one resonates with all the others.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Catalogue
          |
    L2: Resonance
          |
    L3: Compound Effect
          |
    L4: Living Record
         / \
        /   \
  L5a: Mass  L5b: Deep
  Accumulate  Memory
  (capstone)  (capstone)

TRUNK:

L1 — CATALOGUE (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 BKS. Gain the "Catalogue" passive: mutation_count is now displayed in the character
    sheet under a new line "Mutations: [count]". Each mutation grants +10 HP (temporary for the
    floor, stored as temp HP so it follows Temp HP → Armor → HP absorption order). The first
    5 mutations of the run grant +5 permanent HP each (i.e., effectively +1 CON equivalent split
    across 5 mutations). Catalog flavor text appears on each mutation: "Body adapts. [count] modifications."

L2 — RESONANCE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: For every 3 mutations accumulated (checked via mutation_count % 3 == 0), gain a permanent
    +1 to a random stat. This is additional to normal mutation outcomes — it fires after the mutation
    resolves. At 3 mutations: +1 random stat. At 6: another. At 9: another. Etc.
    The resonance bonus stat is fully random — this is the "mutations synergizing" flavor.

L3 — COMPOUND EFFECT (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Bad mutation penalties are reduced based on your total mutation count:
    - For every 5 mutations in mutation_count, bad weak stat loss reduced by 0.1 (floor to 0 min).
      At 5 mutations: bad weak = -0.9 → -1 still (it's an integer, so rounds). At 10: -0.8.
      At 15: -0.7. Actually implement as: bad weak stat penalty blocked with probability
      (mutation_count * 0.05), max 75%. So at 15 mutations: 75% chance the bad weak does nothing to stats.
    - For Strong bad: blocked with probability (mutation_count * 0.02), max 40%.
    - For Huge bad: equipment loss blocked with probability (mutation_count * 0.01), max 20%.
  Design note: The longer the run and the more mutations, the more you're protected. This is
    "experience protects you" expressed mechanically.

L4 — LIVING RECORD (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 to all stats (flat, permanent). Mutation XP is doubled from this point forward
    (mutation_xp_multiplier = 2.0 on top of any existing multipliers). Also: the Resonance
    perk (L2) now fires for every 2 mutations instead of every 3. The compound is accelerating.
  Design note: Long trunk rewards commitment. By the time you take L4, you should have 10-20
    mutations already logged, meaning the L2 Resonance has already given several free stat points
    and Compound Effect is already providing meaningful protection.

CAPSTONE SPLIT:

L5a — MASS ACCUMULATE (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: Every mutation now grants the following based on tier:
    - Weak (any polarity): +3 melee damage permanently (mutation_melee_bonus +3).
    - Strong (any polarity): +5 melee damage + +5 max HP permanently.
    - Huge (any polarity): +10 melee damage + +10 max HP + +1 to all stats permanently.
  The "any polarity" is critical — bad mutations now give power too. Every outcome is
  win/win. You take the stat hit from bad mutations, but you gain the combat power regardless.
  At 10 mutations (average 3-4 Weak, 4-5 Strong, 1-2 Huge): approximately +20-40 melee damage bonus.
  Balance: Very strong in long runs. In short runs (few mutations), weaker than single-mutation-control
    options. Permadeath risk is higher because you need volume of mutations to unlock the full power.

L5b — DEEP MEMORY (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: All past mutations are "remembered." The Deep Memory passive applies a retroactive bonus:
    for every mutation in mutation_count, gain +2% of the positive stat changes that mutation
    would have given if it had been Good. This is computed as: for each mutation in count,
    add (mutation_count * 0.02) to a permanent "memory bonus" float applied to melee damage and
    defense calculations. Concretely: at 20 mutations, +40% bonus applied to your base STR for
    melee damage and to your base SWG for defense purposes. Effective multiplier on combat stats.
    Additionally: once per run, the Deep Memory ability allows you to "recall" a mutation from
    memory — recovering 200 HP and clearing all active debuffs (this is the memory of being whole).
  Design note: More thematic than Mass Accumulate. The retroactive compensation makes bad
    mutations feel "rewound" over time. The heal ability is a safety net.
  Synergies: Pairs strongly with Perfect Balance (Var 3 capstone) concept but as a standalone:
    Deep Memory essentially gives you ghost-gains from every bad mutation as a damage multiplier,
    making bad mutations almost welcome.

VARIATION 6: "FALLOUT PROPHET" — Rad Spreader
==========================================================================
Shape: Inverted Y
Fantasy: You are not a victim of radiation. You are its source. Two origin
  stories — passive contaminator or aggressive irradiator — both lead to
  the same nightmare: you irradiate everything around you as an ambient fact
  of your existence.

TREE DIAGRAM:

  [BRANCH A: Carrier]   [BRANCH B: Vector]
  L1a: Passive Emission  L1b: Active Spread
  L2a: Contamination Zone L2b: Targeted Fallout
             \               /
              \             /
          L3: Fallout Prophet (convergence)
                    |
          L4: Living Reactor (capstone)

BRANCH A: Carrier (passive contaminator)

L1a — PASSIVE EMISSION (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: You emit a constant low-level radiation field. GlowingEffect is permanently active:
    all entities within 1 tile gain 3 rad per energy tick (enemy-tick, not player-tick).
    This is roughly 3 rad/turn for adjacent enemies. +1 TOL (your body tolerates its own output).
    You also gain 2 rad per tick yourself (you irradiate yourself slightly; this fuels mutation triggers).
  Note: This is Branch A start — player chooses L1a OR L1b, not both.

L2a — CONTAMINATION ZONE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Emission range increases from 1 to 2 tiles (irradiate_splash_radius = 2).
    Irradiation rate from proximity increases from 3 to 6 rad/tick for adjacent (1 tile),
    and 3 rad/tick for 2-tile radius. You also leave "hot spots" — when you move off a tile,
    that tile radiates 3 rad to any entity that steps on it for the next 5 turns. This creates
    a trailing contamination path as you move.
  Design note: Enemies following you get irradiated on their approach path. Kiting behavior is now
    a contamination delivery tool.

BRANCH B: Vector (active irradiator)

L1b — ACTIVE SPREAD (passive + activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: rad_burst (INFINITE, cooldown 8t)
    Consume 75 rad → deal 8 rad damage to all adjacent entities AND irradiate them 20 rad.
    This is the active version of what Branch A does passively — more burst, less ambient.
    +1 STS (aiming the burst requires focus).
  Note: This is Branch B start — player chooses L1a OR L1b, not both.

L2b — TARGETED FALLOUT (passive + activated)
  Perk type: activated
  Ability: rad_pulse (PER_FLOOR 2)
    Consume 50 rad → irradiate all visible enemies for 15 rad each. No damage, just irradiation.
    Multiple enemies hit simultaneously. Used to set up enemy mutations (see below).
  Passive: Enemies with 75+ rad have a 5% chance per energy tick to mutate — this is the enemy
    mutation mechanic. When an enemy mutates: 60% bad mutation (they take -1-5 HP as a one-time event
    and gain a debuff "Mutated: confused" for 10t, -30% to hit), 40% good mutation (they gain +2
    melee damage for 10t — but at 40% rate and only under active irradiation management, this is
    rare and controllable). You are manipulating the enemies' own genomes as a weapon.

CONVERGENCE:

L3 — FALLOUT PROPHET (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 TOL, +1 CON. Your irradiation aura (from either path) now also applies a "contaminated"
    debuff to affected enemies: -20% movement speed (they become sluggish) and -10% to-hit chance
    per 50 rad they carry (stacks up to -40% to-hit at 200+ rad). Enemies drowning in your fallout
    fight with increasing inefficiency. The player themselves gains rad_resistance +20% from this perk.
  Design note: Convergence power. Both paths have different delivery (ambient vs. burst), but
    both now benefit from enemies being heavily irradiated as a debuff system.

L4 — LIVING REACTOR (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + activated
  Ability: contaminate_aura (INFINITE toggle)
    Toggle a full contamination aura: 3-tile radius, irradiates all enemies 3 rad/tick.
    While active, you also gain 1 rad/tick (the output irradiates you slightly).
    This ability has no charge cost — it's simply on or off.
  Passive: contamination_cloud_active flag is now permanent. Whether the toggle ability is on or off,
    a residual 1-tile, 2 rad/tick baseline remains. The minimum emission never turns off.
    Additionally: when any enemy dies with 150+ rad, they "detonate" — dealing (enemy_rad / 20) damage
    to all entities within 2 tiles, and irradiating them for 30 rad. Enemies become radiation bombs
    when they die at high irradiation. This synergizes catastrophically with Targeted Fallout (L2b)
    — pulse an entire room, enemies detonate when killed, their explosions irradiate the next group.
  Synergies: Glow Up's Emission and Fallout both accelerate enemy rad accumulation. Nuclear Research's
    Rad Bomb becomes lethal when aimed at a group of 150+ rad enemies (it detonates them).

VARIATION 7: "SCAR TISSUE" — Adaptive Defense
==========================================================================
Shape: Ladder / Zigzag
Fantasy: Every wound makes you stronger. Every bad mutation leaves scar tissue.
  You play a choose-your-own-pain game where the two choices at each fork
  determine whether you're building a body that absorbs everything,
  or one that adapts and improves from any harm.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Scar Tissue (shared)
       / \
  L2a: Hard  L2b: Flex
  Callus     Tissue
       \ /
    L3: Regenerative Adaptation (shared)
       / \
  L4a: Ablative  L4b: Reactive
  Shell          Response
       \ /
    L5: Immortal Body (capstone, shared)

TRUNK L1:

L1 — SCAR TISSUE (passive)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 CON. Gain the scar_tissue_hits tracker. Whenever you receive any HP damage, gain
    1 scar_tissue_hit. For every 10 scar_tissue_hits accumulated over the run, gain +1 permanent DR
    (stored in adaptive_dr_bonus). This is entirely run-wide. At 50 total hits taken: +5 DR.
    At 100 total hits taken: +10 DR. This is a "tank XP" system — getting hit makes you harder.

FORK 1 (choose one):

L2a — HARD CALLUS (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 CON, +1 SWG. Bad mutations now grant +1 DR immediately upon resolving (in addition
    to their normal stat penalty — you take the hit but your skin gets harder). Bad weak: -1 stat, +1 DR.
    Bad strong: -3 stat, +2 DR. Bad huge: full normal penalties, +3 DR.
    Hard Callus DR does not have a cap (unlike scar_tissue_hits which caps at +10; this adds on top).
  Design note: Leans into taking damage as a resource. Pairs with high-mutation builds.

L2b — FLEX TISSUE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Whenever you receive any damage, 15% of that damage is converted to temporary HP on
    your NEXT turn (not this turn, preventing abuse). Max 20 temp HP regenerated this way per floor.
    Also: incoming rad damage is reduced by 30%. You're adapting to what hurts you.
  Design note: More defensive survivability path. Lower raw DR gain but consistent damage mitigation.

SHARED L3:

L3 — REGENERATIVE ADAPTATION (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Between floors (on floor transition), gain HP equal to (adaptive_dr_bonus * 2 + mutation_count * 1).
    This is a meaningful heal for Scar Tissue builds. At 5 DR + 10 mutations = 20 HP healed on
    each floor transition. Also: +10% rad_resistance.
  Design note: The ladder convergence rewards both paths. Hard Callus players have more DR going in
    (better heal). Flex Tissue players have more mutations going in (also good heal, different source).

FORK 2 (choose one):

L4a — ABLATIVE SHELL (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 SWG, +1 CON. At the start of each floor, gain temp HP equal to (mutation_count * 3 + adaptive_dr_bonus * 5).
    This is AdaptiveShieldEffect. At 10 mutations + 5 DR = 55 temp HP at floor start. Scales hard.
    Also: this temp HP absorbs rad damage (treat as HP for rad damage calculations).
  Design note: The "turtle" path. You start each floor with a huge buffer. Pairs with Scar Tissue
    (more hits = more DR = more temp HP) and with Fallout Prophet (rad detonation damage hits temp HP).

L4b — REACTIVE RESPONSE (passive + activated)
  Perk type: passive + activated
  Ability: absorb_rad (INFINITE, cooldown 15t)
    Consume 100 rad → heal 15 HP and gain +3 DR for 20t (temporary DR on top of permanent).
    Effectively: convert rad surplus into healing and defense.
  Passive: When your HP drops below 30%, gain BerserkerSurgeEffect automatically (once per floor).
    You go berserk when bloodied. +1 STS.
  Design note: The "reactive" path. You respond to threats dynamically rather than pre-loading
    defense. The absorb ability is flexible and recharging.

CAPSTONE (shared):

L5 — IMMORTAL BODY (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +3 CON, +2 SWG. Your body has become a monument to survival:
    1. scar_tissue_hits now grant +1 permanent max HP every 5 hits (on top of the DR from L1).
       At 50 hits: +5 DR + +10 max HP permanent. At 100 hits: +10 DR + +20 max HP.
    2. Bad mutations no longer reduce stats below their initial (Level 1) values. You cannot
       have a stat permanently reduced below what it was when you first entered the game.
       Equipment loss from bad Huge still applies.
    3. Once per run, automatically avoid death: if you would reach 0 HP, survive at 1 HP instead.
       This is a traditional roguelike death save. Only works once. Log message: "Scar tissue holds."
  Balance: The death save makes this one of the most powerful capstones in the game. Justified by
    the extremely long investment chain (5 perks, two fork choices, no shortcuts).
  Synergies: Pairs with literally every defensive system in the game. Meth Lab zone's toxicity
    multiplier becomes less threatening with +10+ DR and +20+ max HP from scar tissue.

VARIATION 8: "YELLOWCAKE PROTOCOL" — Forced Tier Manipulation
==========================================================================
Shape: Trident
Fantasy: You've cracked the mutation code. Weak mutations are beneath you.
  You force every mutation up to Strong or Huge tier, then specialize in
  what to do with the resulting chaos — control the odds, weaponize the
  process, or squeeze the system for maximum XP.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Skip Ahead
          |
    L2: Yellowcake Protocol
       / |  \
      /  |   \
  L3a: L3b:  L3c:
  Sure  Raw   XP
  Thing Power  Harvest
   |         (solo cap)
  L4a:  L5: Convergence
  Huge  (L3a+L3b merge)
  Only   |
  (cap) L5: Twin Towers (cap)

Note: Trident shape — L3a and L3b reconverge at L5 (Twin Towers), L3c stays solo (XP Harvest).

TRUNK:

L1 — SKIP AHEAD (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: mutation_tier_skip_weak = True. The minimum mutation tier is now Strong (125+ rad required,
    costs 125 rad). Passive mutation triggers at 75-124 rad simply do nothing. At 125+ rad,
    mutations can occur but will be Strong tier at minimum.
    Also: When a Strong tier mutation occurs, gain +50 bonus mutation XP.
  Design note: This fundamentally changes your radiation management. You now need to reach 125 rad
    before anything happens. Pairs with Nuclear Research's "Inoculation" (which also sets 150 rad
    minimum) but Skip Ahead sets 125 as the floor. The combination would be 150 minimum.
    You are explicitly trading mutation frequency for mutation quality.

L2 — YELLOWCAKE PROTOCOL (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 BKS, +1 STS. Whenever a Strong tier mutation fires (now the minimum due to L1),
    it has a 30% chance to automatically upgrade to Huge tier for free (no additional rad cost,
    the player just pays the Strong rad cost of 125 and gets a Huge outcome).
    This stacks with Directed Evolution (Var 3 L3) — if you have both, you can spend rad to force
    the upgrade AND there's a 30% free upgrade chance on top.
    Good mutation base bonus +0.15 (cumulative, now ~48%).

BRANCH A: Sure Thing (reliability)

L3a — SURE THING (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: +0.30 to mutation_good_base_bonus (cumulative: now ~78% good base before multipliers).
    Additionally: once per floor, inoculate_self ability:
  Ability: inoculate_self (FLOOR_ONLY 1)
    Consume 100 rad → guarantee the next mutation is Good polarity. The tier is still random/determined
    by rad level, but the polarity is locked to Good. This ability's floor charge refreshes each floor.
  Design note: Very high good% combined with tier control (Strong minimum) means virtually every
    mutation outcome is Good Strong (at minimum), which permanently adds +3 stat + skill level + SP.

L4a (capstone only if NOT taking Branch B) — HUGE ONLY (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: forced_huge_next is now permanent (every mutation triggers at Huge tier — pays Huge's 200 rad
    cost from your pool). The minimum rad threshold remains 125 (from L1) but mutations will spend
    200 rad when they fire (requiring you to stay above 125 rad when the trigger fires, and have
    200+ rad for the Huge cost). If you have less than 200 rad, the mutation fires as Strong instead.
    Also: Good Huge mutation stat bonuses are tripled (+15 to single stat, +6 all stats, 15 skill levels).
    +3 CON.
  Balance: Extreme. Each Good Huge mutation (with ~78% good chance + Sure Thing) gives +15 to one stat,
    +6 all stats, +15 skill levels. At even 3-4 of these per run, the stats become ridiculous. The
    limitation is that you need to maintain 200 rad, which requires ignoring most good-rad-reducing
    items and actively seeking rad sources.
  Design note: This is the Branch A solo capstone. You can't take this AND Twin Towers.

BRANCH B: Raw Power (per-mutation damage scaling)

L3b — RAW POWER (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 STR. Each Strong or Huge mutation (any polarity) permanently increases melee damage
    by 3 (mutation_melee_bonus +3 per Strong, +6 per Huge). The stat loss from bad mutations is
    accepted as a cost of doing business. At 5 Strong mutations: +15 melee bonus. At 5 Huge: +30.
    Also: +10% rad_gain_multiplier_bonus (get more rad = more Strong/Huge triggers).

RECONVERGENCE (L3a + L3b merge to L5):

L5 — TWIN TOWERS (passive, CAPSTONE — requires L3a AND L3b)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +3 STR, +3 BKS. Combines the effects:
    - Good mutation% from Sure Thing path is applied to the Raw Power damage scaling, meaning
      each Good Strong mutation gives +3 stat AND +3 melee bonus.
    - Each Huge mutation (any polarity) now grants ALL of: +6 melee bonus, +1 all stats,
      the BKS-scaling ability to deal (BKS * 2) bonus rad damage on next hit (lasts 20t).
    - The 30% free Huge upgrade chance from Yellowcake Protocol now applies to every mutation
      without exception.
    Requires that you've taken BOTH L3a and L3b (this is a converging trident path,
    not a Y-split — you pick Branch A, then Branch B, or vice versa, spending 2 perk levels
    to converge here).
  Note to implementer: Trident convergence requires player to have unlocked both L3a AND L3b
    before the L5 convergence perk becomes available. This means 5 perks: L1+L2+L3a+L3b+L5.
    It is the most expensive path in this document.

BRANCH C: XP Harvest (solo, no reconvergence)

L3c — XP HARVEST (passive, CAPSTONE — solo)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: Mutation XP is now triple the base rate (mutation_xp_multiplier = 3.0). Each Strong
    mutation grants 750 XP toward Mutation skill (3x 250). Each Huge grants 1500 XP (3x 500).
    Additionally: mutation XP is spread to all skill trees you currently have XP in, at a rate
    of 10% overflow. So a 750 XP mutation event gives 750 to Mutation AND 75 XP each to up to
    3 other skilled trees you've been investing in.
    +2 BKS, +1 STS (you are a student of your own evolution).
  Design note: This is the "I am leveling via mutations" build. Combined with Skip Ahead (Strong minimum)
    and Yellowcake Protocol (30% free Huge), each mutation is worth enormous XP. The overflow mechanic
    means you're cross-leveling all your other skill trees too. A Mutation-primary build with 5-8 Strong
    Huge mutations per run could be 60-70% of the way through multiple other skill trees.
  Balance: XP doesn't help you survive. This is a late-game-accelerator, not a combat build. Works
    best in tandem with active combat skill trees (Stabbing, Gatting) that benefit from the bonus XP.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 9: "THE EXPERIMENT" — Double Diamond (Risk/Reward RNG)
==========================================================================
Shape: Double Diamond
Fantasy: Every choice you make about your mutations feeds into the next choice.
  A double-diamond of calculated risks where good paths open more options and
  bad outcomes close them. One capstone at the top, but four very different
  roads to get there.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Guinea Pig
       /         \
  L2a: Positive   L2b: Negative
  Feedback        Feedback
       \         /
        L3: Mutagenic Cascade (convergence)
       /         \
  L4a: Amplify    L4b: Negate
  Good            Bad
       \         /
        L5: Genome Complete (capstone)

DIAMOND 1:

L1 — GUINEA PIG (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 to all stats. Take a free mutation immediately on unlock (same as existing Mutagen perk).
    Additionally: last_mutation_was_good and mutation_streak are now tracked. Mutation XP +50 flat bonus
    per event. The streak tracking is purely mechanical setup for the two L2 branches.

L2a — POSITIVE FEEDBACK (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: When last_mutation_was_good is True (your most recent mutation was good):
    - Next passive mutation trigger chance is tripled (one-time bonus; resets after next mutation).
    - Good mutations add an extra +1 to their stat bonus (Good weak = +2, Good strong = +4, etc.).
    Momentum builds on good luck. After a good mutation, you're more likely to get another one
    and the benefits are larger.

L2b — NEGATIVE FEEDBACK (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: When last_mutation_was_good is False (your most recent mutation was bad):
    - Next good mutation chance is increased by +0.40 base bonus (one-time, resets after).
    - Bad mutations grant +50 rad instead of costing you rad (wait — bad mutations normally cost
      rad; instead they give 50 rad back, partially refunding the cost). Bad weak still costs
      50 rad but you get 50 back = net 0 rad cost. Bad strong costs 125 but you get 50 back = 75 net.
    The universe corrects itself. Bad luck begets good luck.

CONVERGENCE L3:

L3 — MUTAGENIC CASCADE (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: mutation_streak now grants bonuses:
    - After 2 consecutive same-polarity mutations: next mutation of opposite polarity cannot occur
      (forced to same polarity as streak). E.g., two goods in a row → third mutation is also good.
    - After 3 consecutive: the streak bonus (extra stat for positive; rad refund for negative)
      from L2 persists for 3 turns instead of just the next mutation.
    Also: +20% to rad_gain_multiplier_bonus.
  Design note: The convergence creates a momentum system. Either path (Positive Feedback or Negative
    Feedback) can now build a streak, and the streak locks in favorable conditions for multiple mutations.

DIAMOND 2:

L4a — AMPLIFY GOOD (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 STR, +1 BKS. Good mutations have all effects doubled:
    - Good weak: +2 stat, +20% resist, +200 SP.
    - Good strong: +6 stat, +2 skill levels, +400 SP, +10% briskness, +6 DR.
    - Good huge: +10 stat, +4 all stats, +10 skill levels, +10% briskness, +10 DR.
    Mutation streak of 3+ good mutations: the 4th good mutation is treated as the next tier up
    (Weak→Strong→Huge) with no additional rad cost. The cascade amplifies and upgrades.

L4b — NEGATE BAD (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +2 CON, +1 STS. Bad mutations are partially negated:
    - Bad weak: stat reduced by 0 (fully negated), still costs rad, still awards 100 XP.
    - Bad strong: stat reduced by 1 (instead of 3), skill level loss negated.
    - Bad huge: equipment loss negated; stat reduced by 2 (instead of 5), all-stats reduction by 1.
    This does not make bad mutations good — they still cost rad and have reduced (but present) penalties.
    Mutation streak of 3+ bad mutations: the streak is broken forcibly (next mutation is always Good).

CAPSTONE (shared):

L5 — GENOME COMPLETE (passive, CAPSTONE)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +3 to all stats (permanent). The double-diamond paths combine:
    The Amplify Good bonuses are now also applied when Negate Bad is active, and vice versa.
    In other words: good mutations give doubled stats AND bad mutations are negated.
    Additionally: mutations can no longer be bad Huge (the worst tier of bad mutation is capped
    at bad Strong). You may still get bad outcomes, but you will never lose your equipment
    from mutations again for the rest of the run.
    One final bonus: gain genome_splice ability (ONCE per run) — see ability definition above.
    The genome is complete. You choose one final perfect mutation.
  Balance: The combined effect of Amplify Good + Negate Bad + no bad Huge + genome_splice is
    genuinely powerful but requires 5 perks in this tree (L1+L2+L3+L4+L5). The investment is real.
  Synergies: The path you took through Diamond 1 (L2a vs L2b) still matters: Positive Feedback
    players have slightly better good-mutation amplification (the streak mechanic still works).
    Negative Feedback players have better bad-mutation mitigation (the rad refund still works).
    The capstone merges both; the mid-tree perks make the journey different.


==========================================================================
VARIATION 10: "DARWIN AWARD" — Asymmetric Y (High-Speed Obliteration)
==========================================================================
Shape: Asymmetric Y
Fantasy: One path ends fast and hard — a single devastating capstone for
  players who want immediate power and are willing to pay with their genome.
  The other path is a long slow-burn commitment to the ultimate mutation
  supremacy build in the game. Two very different levels of ambition.

TREE DIAGRAM:

    L1: Primordial Soup
          |
    L2: Darwin's Law
         / \
        /   \
  [BRANCH A: Short]  [BRANCH B: Long]
  L3a: Award         L3b: Patient
  (CAPSTONE, 1 perk)       |
                      L4b: Recombination
                           |
                      L5b: Perfect Organism
                       (CAPSTONE, 3 perks)

TRUNK:

L1 — PRIMORDIAL SOUP (passive + stat)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +1 to all six stats (CON, STR, STS, BKS, TOL, SWG). Immediately trigger a forced
    Strong mutation (not Huge, not Weak — exactly Strong). This is different from normal Mutagen
    (which is any tier at current rad level). The Strong mutation fires at current polarity odds.
    This perk establishes that you are committed to QUALITY mutations.
  Design note: The +1 all stats is deliberately generous as an L1 perk because this tree
    has a short branch (L3a) that ends immediately and a long branch (L5b) that requires maximum
    commitment. Both branches need a strong foundation.

L2 — DARWIN'S LAW (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Good mutation base bonus +0.30 (cumulative: ~63% good chance base).
    mutation_trigger_multiplier = 1.5x (50% more frequent passive triggers).
    Also: the free Strong mutation from Primordial Soup now fires again (second free Strong mutation).
    You get two free Strong mutations just from taking L1 + L2.
  Design note: Very front-loaded power. L1+L2 alone give +1 all stats, +2 free Strong mutations (likely
    good at 63% chance each), and a 50% faster mutation rate. This is intentional — both branches
    of the Asymmetric Y get to build from this powerful trunk.

BRANCH A: Short Path

L3a — DARWIN AWARD (passive, CAPSTONE — short branch)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +3 STR, +3 CON. This is explicitly the "wrong evolution" choice in terms of raw power
    ceiling, but it's immediately available after just L1+L2:
    For every current mutation in mutation_count, gain +2 melee damage permanently (retroactive).
    If you've already had 5 mutations from Primordial Soup + Darwin's Law + earlier play: +10 melee bonus immediately.
    Additionally: mutation_trigger_multiplier increases to 3x (from the 1.5x at L2). Mutations happen
    very frequently. For every 10 mutations total, gain +1 to all stats (permanent).
    At 30 total mutations: +3 all stats bonus from this perk alone.
    You pay no more perk points. This branch is done at L3.
  Design note: The "fast food" build. You spent 3 perk levels, you got massive melee scaling and
    mutation frequency, and you stop there. Excellent for players who don't want to commit to a long
    investment chain. The short branch is ALSO a valid strategy — not a trap.
  Balance: The Branch B capstone (Perfect Organism) is arguably more powerful ceiling, but Darwin
    Award activates 2 levels earlier, leaving those 2 perk levels for OTHER skill trees.

BRANCH B: Long Path

L3b — PATIENT EVOLUTION (passive)
  Perk type: passive
  Effect: Minimum mutation tier raised to Strong (mutation_tier_skip_weak = True). Strong minimum.
    Each Strong mutation grants +150 bonus mutation XP (on top of base 250 = 400 total). Each Huge
    grants +300 bonus (on top of base 500 = 800 total). You are accumulating mutation XP at
    extraordinary rates.
    Also: Yellowcake Protocol synergy: if Yellowcake food is consumed, the 10x mutation trigger rate
    now also has a 50% chance to upgrade each Strong trigger to Huge automatically (additional to the
    food's normal effects).

L4b — RECOMBINATION (passive + activated)
  Perk type: passive + activated
  Ability: forced_evolution (PER_FLOOR 1)
    Spend 150 rad → trigger one Strong mutation immediately (does not consume additional rad for
    the mutation cost — the 150 rad IS the cost). This is a manually triggered Strong mutation,
    once per floor, at your current good-mutation odds. At the ~63% good chance from Darwin's Law:
    63% chance of +3 stat, +1 skill level, +5% briskness, +3 DR for free.
  Passive: each forced_evolution use reduces the next natural mutation's rad cost by 25 rad.
    Also: +2 BKS, +1 STS.

L5b — PERFECT ORGANISM (passive, CAPSTONE — long branch)
  Perk type: passive + stat
  Effect: +3 to all stats. The culmination of the long path:
    1. All Strong mutations are treated as Huge (for effects only; rad cost stays at 125).
       You pay 125 rad but get Huge mutation outcomes: +5 single stat, +2 all stats, +5 skill levels,
       +5% briskness, +5 DR. At 63%+ good chance from trunk perks.
    2. Bad mutations of any tier no longer reduce stats. They still fire and cost rad, but all
       penalties to stats, skill levels, SP, and equipment are negated. Mutations are either a blessing
       (good polarity) or a void (bad polarity — costs rad but does nothing harmful).
    3. Gain genome_splice ability (ONCE per run) — choose a perfect mutation at any time.
    4. forced_evolution (from L4b) now fires TWICE per floor (2 charges/floor) instead of once.
  Balance: This is the highest-ceiling single-tree capstone in the entire document. But it costs:
    L1+L2+L3b+L4b+L5b = 5 perk levels, AND requires the long path setup. The player will
    also have spent turns/floors accumulating Strong+ mutations (minimum 125 rad threshold means
    slower mutation rate than mutation-farming builds). Perfect Organism is a late-run payoff
    for a very focused, patient investment.
  Synergies: Pairs with Nuclear Research (Inoculation at 150 rad min + forced_evolution = every
    floor you get 2 guaranteed Strong mutations, upgraded to Huge by this capstone, at 63%+ good
    chance). Glow Up (Emission + Fallout for rad accumulation). Five Loco drink (multiplier
    stacks push good chance toward 90%+). Yellowcake food (10x trigger rate on top of the already
    frequent floor-based mutations). This is the mutation tree that synergizes with literally
    everything in the game.

Mutation resolution hook points (in mutations.py):
  - After tier determination: check mutation_tier_skip_weak, forced_strong_next, forced_huge_next
  - After polarity determination: check genome_locked_stat, mutation_reroll_available, inoculate flags
  - After full resolution: increment mutation_count, update last_mutation_was_good, mutation_streak,
