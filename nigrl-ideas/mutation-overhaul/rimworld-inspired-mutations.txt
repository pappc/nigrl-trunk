RIMWORLD-INSPIRED MUTATIONS — ADDITIONAL CANDIDATES (20)
==========================================================
NIGRL Roguelike — gritty urban setting (Crack Den / Meth Lab)
Design goal: 20 additional mutations for cherry-picking into the 80-mutation
overhaul plan. Inspired by Rimworld Biotech's gene design philosophy, adapted
to NIGRL's urban body-horror aesthetic. Every mutation changes how you play.

Author note: These mutations supplement mutation-overhaul-plan.txt.
Do not add any entry that duplicates the existing 80. Each mutation below
is a candidate for its named category only. Select freely and append to
the master document.

Rimworld design lessons applied:
  - Behavioral dependencies (Kill Thirst, drug dependencies)
  - Environmental inversion (turning negatives into personal positives)
  - Hard constraints ("cannot do X") over stat nerfs
  - Biological ranged weapons (new gameplay verbs)
  - Vulnerability stories (immortal vs. fire; strong vs. corridor)
  - One unique ability beats five stat modifiers

Upgrade principles carried from main doc:
  - Good mutations get STRONGER on upgrade.
  - Bad mutations get WORSE on upgrade.
  - Typical cap: L3. Self-escalating mutations have no re-roll upgrade.
  - All mutations permanent; Shed removes entirely and resets level to 0.
  - Scarred Tissue fires on bad mutation upgrades (consolation +1 stat).

I. WEAK GOOD MUTATIONS (5)
================================================================

Design principle: Weak good mutations grant a new tool or a small niche
identity. They should be immediately useful without being build-defining.

--

A. FROG LEGS — "Your hamstrings snapped into something else. Longer."
   Effect: Grants the Leap ability (INFINITE charges, self-targeting).
   When activated, select a destination tile within 4 tiles in any
   direction (including diagonal). The player moves directly to that tile,
   bypassing all entities and terrain in between (walls excluded —
   destination tile must be open floor). Cooldown: 6 turns. The leap
   ignores grease tile penalties on landing. If an enemy occupies a tile
   adjacent to the landing spot, that enemy is knocked back 1 tile
   (stumbled by the impact) and stunned for 1 turn.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Leap range 4 tiles; landing knockback 1 tile; stun 1t; 6t cooldown.
     L2: Leap range 5 tiles; landing knockback extends to 1-tile radius
         (all adjacent enemies knocked and stunned, not just one). Cooldown
         reduces to 5 turns.
     L3: Leap range 6 tiles. Landing in the same tile as a standing enemy
         (i.e., directly on their space) is now valid — you kick through them
         for 6 + (mutation_count // 2) damage and land in the next open tile
         in the same direction (like a bowling ball). Standard knockback/stun
         still applies to surrounding enemies. Cooldown 4 turns. Cap.
   Implementation notes:
     - Ability id: "frog_legs_leap". TargetType: ADJACENT_TILE (reuse for
       short-range movement targeting — player selects floor tile). If the
       current ADJACENT_TILE targeting doesn't support range > 1, may need
       a new TargetType: RANGED_TILE or allow ADJACENT_TILE with a range
       parameter. Alternatively implement as SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS-adjacent
       logic but for floor tiles.
     - Knockback logic: reuse existing knockback helper in combat.py.
     - StunEffect (already exists) applied to adjacent entities.
     - L3 "land-on-enemy" requires collision check before movement resolves.
     - "Destination tile must be open floor" — check dungeon.is_blocked() at
       destination, plus not a wall tile.
     - Grease immunity on landing: skip dungeon.grease_tiles check for the
       landing step only.
   Reversal: Revoke "frog_legs_leap" ability.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "frog_legs_leap", spells.py movement
   execution, combat.py knockback/stun helpers, dungeon.py tile/entity
   checks.
   Rimworld inspiration: Longjump Legs — a hard terrain-traversal ability
   that grants a new movement verb (not just +speed).

--

B. SPINE QUIVER — "There's something along your back. It's sharp. It aims."
   Effect: Grants the Spine Shot ability (3 charges/floor, SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS).
   Fire a biological bone-spur projectile at a visible enemy within 8 tiles.
   Deals 5 + (mutation_count // 3) piercing damage, ignoring 2 points of
   the target's DR. Applies 1 stack of bleed (3 turns). No ammo required —
   this is your body. Cannot be disarmed.
   Upgrade:
     L1: 3/floor; 5 + count//3 dmg; DR bypass 2; 1 bleed stack.
     L2: 4/floor; 7 + count//3 dmg; DR bypass 3; 2 bleed stacks.
         Additionally, if the target is already bleeding when hit by Spine
         Shot, the projectile lodges in the wound — target is slowed
         (SlowEffect, 3 turns) in addition to bleed.
     L3: 5/floor; 9 + count//3 dmg; DR bypass 4; 3 bleed stacks.
         The slow on-bleed-target upgrade is retained. Additionally, Spine
         Shot now bounces once to a second visible enemy within 3 tiles of
         the primary target for 50% of the damage (round down). The bounce
         does NOT apply bleed or slow — just raw damage. Cap.
   Note: "Cannot be disarmed" is flavor only — there is no disarm system.
   The important point is it does not require any item in inventory.
   Reversal: Revoke "spine_shot" ability.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "spine_shot", spells.py execute (LOS
   targeting, DR bypass logic, bleed application), effects.py SlowEffect
   (already exists or create), spells.py L3 bounce resolver (find nearest
   second visible target within 3 tiles of primary).
   Rimworld inspiration: Piercing Spine — a biological ranged weapon that
   creates a new "gun without ammo" gameplay verb.

--

C. TOXIC METABOLISM — "Your body eats the poison. Then it runs on it."
   Effect: While you have 10 or more current toxicity, gain a speed bonus
   equal to (current_tox // 20) * 10, capped at +30. This bonus is
   recalculated every turn. It is NOT a separate effect — it is a direct
   speed modification applied during the energy tick, similar to how
   existing status effect speed mods work. The bonus only applies if tox
   >= 10; drops to 0 instantly when tox falls below 10.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Speed bonus = (tox // 20) * 10, cap +30, threshold tox >= 10.
     L2: Speed formula improves: (tox // 15) * 10, cap +40.
         Additionally, at 50+ tox you shrug off 1 point of venom damage
         per venom tick (the metabolism processes some of it — this is NOT
         tox removal, just venom damage reduction by 1 per tick while tox >= 50).
     L3: Speed formula: (tox // 10) * 10, cap +50. Venom dmg reduction
         stays at 1/tick (no further increase). Additionally, when you
         naturally lose toxicity (via effects, foods, zone transitions that
         decay tox), you gain a brief Catharsis burst: +2 melee power for
         5 turns (triggers once per floor per tox-loss event, max 1 stack).
         Cap.
   Note: This creates an interesting tension with Chemical Sensitivity
   (Weak Bad — amplifies tox gain), Porous Membrane (Weak Bad), and Meth
   Lab zone toxicity. A player who willingly stacks tox for speed is
   making a real tradeoff. It does NOT turn tox into a resource you drain;
   tox still damages you normally. The speed benefit comes from riding the
   tox line dangerously.
   Reversal: Remove toxic_metabolism_active flag; speed bonus immediately
   drops to 0.
   System hooks: player_stats.toxic_metabolism_active,
   engine.py energy distribution (or combat.py speed-calc path) to apply
   the dynamic bonus each tick, effects.py VenomEffect.tick() for L2
   damage reduction, item_effects.py/engine.py tox-loss events for L3
   catharsis trigger.
   Rimworld inspiration: Pollution Rush — turning a universal negative
   (toxicity buildup) into a personal performance boost.

--

D. BLOOD MEMORY — "Every body you drop teaches your hands something new."
   Effect: Killing an enemy grants Blood Memory Surge for 12 turns.
   During the surge, all skill XP gained is doubled (multiplier applies to
   all skill XP sources: melee hits, gun kills, item use, etc.).
   Stacks: multiple kills within the 12-turn window do NOT extend duration
   — they reset it to 12 turns remaining.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Kill → Blood Memory Surge: skill XP ×2 for 12 turns (reset on kill,
         not stack).
     L2: Surge now lasts 18 turns. Additionally, killing an enemy WHILE the
         surge is already active grants 1 bonus Skill Point (directly added,
         bypassing the potential/real conversion system). This can happen at
         most once per floor to prevent SP farming.
     L3: Surge lasts 24 turns. The L2 bonus SP on-kill-during-surge now
         applies up to 2 times per floor instead of once. Additionally, each
         kill during a surge permanently grants +0.5 bonus to the XP multiplier
         for whichever skill is currently closest to its next unlock
         threshold — capped at a maximum of +2 accumulated across the run.
         (This represents your body memorizing specific technique pathways.)
         Cap.
   Note: Blood Memory Surge is a named effect (BloodMemorySurgeEffect) with
   modify_xp_gain() hook — or alternatively implement as a flag on engine
   with duration tracked. The "closest to threshold" bonus at L3 is computed
   at kill-time by scanning player skills for max(potential_exp / threshold).
   Reversal: Remove blood_memory_active flag; if L2/L3 bonus SP was granted,
   it is NOT reclaimed (already spent into the progression system).
   System hooks: player_stats.blood_memory_active, combat.py entity_died
   handler (grant/refresh surge), xp_progression.py XP gain (check flag,
   apply multiplier), skills.py SP grant (L2 direct SP bonus), engine.py
   blood_memory_turns_remaining: int (per-floor reset), blood_memory_bonus_sp_given
   (int, per-floor counter, max 1 then 2 at L3).
   Rimworld inspiration: Fast Learner / Learning rate genes — accelerated
   skill growth tied to active performance rather than passive time.

--

E. COAGULATION GLANDS — "You bleed less than you should. Noticeably less."
   Effect: Grants the Clot ability (2 charges/floor, SELF target).
   On activation: instantly heal 8 HP. Cannot overheal past max HP. This
   ability is a biological emergency response — it is immediate, requires
   no food or item, and costs no resource other than the charge. The heal
   is NOT a HoT — it fires instantly. Note: this is explicitly charge-gated
   (not per-turn regen) to comply with no-passive-regen design rule.
   Upgrade:
     L1: 2/floor; instant heal 8 HP.
     L2: 2/floor; instant heal 12 HP. Additionally, if the Clot is used
         while you have an active bleed effect, the bleed is immediately
         removed (the coagulation gland purges it). If no bleed is present,
         the heal fires normally.
     L3: 3/floor; instant heal 16 HP. Bleed purge is retained (L2).
         Additionally, using Clot while below 30% HP doubles its heal
         (32 HP at the 30% threshold) — an emergency overdrive.
         The double-heal also triggers if any bleed was just purged
         regardless of HP threshold. Cap.
   Note: 3/floor at L3 with 32 HP potential (on low HP) is significant
   but gated behind both the threshold AND the floor charge limit. In the
   Meth Lab zone, where you're taking consistent tox damage, this becomes
   a genuine survival tool vs. luxury.
   Reversal: Revoke "coagulation_clot" ability.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "coagulation_clot", ChargeType.PER_FLOOR,
   spells.py execute (instant heal, bleed purge check, L3 threshold double).
   Rimworld inspiration: Coagulate — a biological self-heal ability that
   makes the player feel resilient rather than reliant on external items.


================================================================
II. WEAK BAD MUTATIONS (5)
================================================================

Design principle: Weak bad mutations should create behavioral constraints
that alter your play pattern. Ideally they produce interesting decisions,
not just punishment. The best ones make you avoid things that other builds
would do freely.

--

F. NARCOLEPTIC FITS — "It comes on without warning. You just go down."
   Effect: Every 25 turns, there is a 30% chance of a Narcoleptic Fit.
   When a fit triggers: the player is stunned for 3 turns (cannot act,
   cannot use abilities or items). The stun is identical to an enemy stun
   but applied by the mutation system. A message is displayed: "A wave of
   crushing fatigue hits you." The timer resets to 25 turns after each
   check (whether or not the fit fires).
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Every 25 turns, 30% fit chance, 3-turn stun.
     L2: Every 20 turns, 40% fit chance, 4-turn stun. Additionally, if a
         fit triggers while you are in melee range of an enemy, that enemy
         immediately gets a free extra attack against you (they capitalize
         on your moment of unconsciousness).
     L3: Every 15 turns, 50% fit chance, 5-turn stun. The free enemy attack
         on fit-in-melee is retained. Additionally, if you are below 50% HP
         when a fit triggers, the stun duration is doubled (10 turns). Cap.
   Note: The 25-turn timer is tracked as engine.narcoleptic_counter: int
   (per-floor; resets on each floor). The check fires during the player's
   turn, before their action resolves, meaning they see the stun hit before
   they can act. This is brutal but predictable — the player learns to
   manage the timer.
   Reversal: Remove narcoleptic_fits_active flag, remove stun logic, clear
   narcoleptic_counter.
   System hooks: player_stats.narcoleptic_fits_active, engine.py
   player-turn-start (narcoleptic_counter increment, fit check, apply stun),
   effects.py StunEffect (standard; already exists), ai.py free-attack path
   (reuse existing "enemy gets a free attack" hook from Slow Blink L3).
   Rimworld inspiration: Very Sleepy / Narcoleptic gene — random loss of
   player agency that creates genuine fear during combat, especially when
   outnumbered.

--

G. PYROPHOBIC NERVES — "Fire is wrong. Your body understands this on a
   primal level it refuses to negotiate."
   Effect: When there is any fire entity (hazard_type="fire") within 5 tiles
   of the player, a FearEffect is applied (identical to enemy-induced fear —
   the player is compelled to move away on their turn). Unlike enemy fear
   which has a direction target, Pyrophobic fear movement always picks the
   direction maximizing distance from the nearest fire tile. The FearEffect
   lasts as long as fire remains within 5 tiles + 1 extra turn after moving
   out of range (the nerves need a moment to calm). Additionally, the player
   takes 1 extra fire damage from any fire-based source (ignite ticks, fire
   hazard damage, etc.).
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Fire within 5 tiles → forced-flee movement; +1 fire damage from all
         sources.
     L2: Fire detection radius increases to 7 tiles. +2 fire damage from
         sources. Additionally, when fear triggers (entering fire radius for
         the first time per encounter), the player also has a 30% chance to
         drop their currently equipped weapon item (it clatters to the floor
         from the spasm — reachable and re-equippable).
     L3: Fire detection radius 9 tiles. +3 fire damage. Weapon drop chance
         increases to 50%. Additionally, any ignite status effect applied to
         YOU has its tick count doubled (the nerves amplify the sensation —
         your own body's inflammation is experienced as catastrophic). Cap.
   Note: The forced movement on pyrophobic nerves is the existing FearEffect
   repurposed. The fire proximity check runs in engine.py on the player turn.
   The weapon drop (L2/L3) drops the currently equipped weapon to the floor
   under the player as if manually unequipped and dropped — uses existing
   drop-item logic. Player can walk back and pick it up. The ignite duration
   doubling at L3 means an ignite that would last 4 ticks lasts 8 ticks.
   Reversal: Remove pyrophobic_nerves_active flag, remove fire damage bonus.
   System hooks: player_stats.pyrophobic_nerves_active, engine.py player
   turn-start (fire proximity scan, apply FearEffect if fire in radius),
   effects.py FearEffect direction logic (maximize distance from fire tile
   instead of enemy tile), effects.py IgniteEffect.apply() for L3 duration
   doubling, combat.py fire damage (add bonus), inventory_mgr.py unequip/drop
   path (L2/L3 weapon drop).
   Rimworld inspiration: Fire Terror (Sanguophage) — a fundamental
   vulnerability that turns a common environmental hazard into a personal
   horror story, even for a powerful character.

--

H. PIG HANDS — "The fingers fused. You can still hold things. Sort of."
   Effect: You cannot equip rings. All 5 ring slots are blocked.
   Additionally, using an item (strains, food, drinks) from inventory has a
   20% chance to fumble — the item is used and consumed normally, but its
   primary effect is cut to 50% intensity (fumbled dose, sloppy application).
   Fumbling does NOT cause the item to drop or be wasted — just degraded.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Ring slots all blocked; 20% fumble on item use (50% effect).
     L2: Ring slots blocked. Fumble chance increases to 30%. Additionally,
         equipping or unequipping weapons and armor now costs 2 extra turns
         of energy (the hands struggle with clasps and grips — simulated
         by the equip action costing extra energy).
     L3: Ring slots blocked. Fumble chance 40%. Equip/unequip energy cost
         remains at 2 extra turns. Additionally, the fumble now also applies
         to ability items — activated abilities that cost charges have a 15%
         chance to fire but consume the charge without dealing their effect
         (the pig hands fail to execute the gesture properly). Cap.
   Note: Ring slot blocking is the most impactful part for most builds.
   The fumble interacts interestingly with Second Stomach (Strong Good —
   which amplifies item intensity) and Iron Gut (Weak Good — which
   negates strain bad outcomes). At L2, equip/unequip energy cost can be
   simulated by adding extra energy cost to the equip action in
   inventory_mgr.py.
   Reversal: Unblock all 5 ring slots, remove fumble logic, remove equip cost.
   System hooks: player_stats.pig_hands_active, engine.py equip check (block
   ring slots), item_effects.py item use dispatch (fumble roll, cut intensity
   if fumbled), abilities.py execute path (L3 gesture-fail roll), inventory_mgr.py
   equip/unequip energy cost.
   Rimworld inspiration: Pig Hands / Reduced Manipulation — blocking a key
   equipment slot is a stronger design statement than "-30% item effectiveness."
   A player can build around no rings in one run; they can't build around
   diminishing everything slightly.

--

I. NOISE BLEED — "Everything you do tears something. The sound is the proof."
   Effect: Every melee attack you make generates a loud crack of bone and
   cartilage. All enemies within 6 tiles who are in IDLE or WANDERING state
   immediately transition to WANDERING (alerted), and enemies in WANDERING
   state within 6 tiles immediately transition to CHASING (they heard it).
   Enemies already in CHASING are unaffected. This alert fires on every
   melee attack, not just kills.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Melee attack → 6-tile alert (IDLE→WANDERING, WANDERING→CHASING).
     L2: Alert radius increases to 8 tiles. Additionally, the noise now
         also triggers on ability use (any ability activation, not just melee),
         so using Touch of Frost or Clot also alerts enemies in range.
     L3: Alert radius 10 tiles. Ability-alert is retained. Additionally, the
         cracking noise is so extreme that enemies with CHASING state within
         4 tiles gain a brief Enraged buff for 2 turns (+2 melee power,
         from EnragedEffect already defined in main doc — they hear you
         struggling and focus). Cap.
   Note: This mutation transforms "stay quiet" into a genuine strategic
   constraint. In the Crack Den (4 floors, dense enemy packs), pulling
   multiple WANDERING enemies every attack fundamentally changes engagement
   style. The alert uses existing ai.py state transition logic — just
   trigger the same transitions the player's FOV entry would.
   Reversal: Remove noise_bleed_active flag.
   System hooks: player_stats.noise_bleed_active, combat.py player melee
   attack path (post-attack: scan radius, trigger state transitions),
   spells.py ability execute (L2: post-ability scan), ai.py state transition
   (EnragedEffect application for L3), effects.py EnragedEffect (already in main doc).
   Rimworld inspiration: Roaring Voice — actions that should be quiet are
   constantly broadcasting your location, fundamentally altering stealth
   and engagement planning.

--

J. GENOME DRIFT — "Something in you is un-writing itself. One gene at a time."
   Effect: Each time the player descends to a new floor (zone transitions and
   floor-within-zone descents), one random stat among the six (CON, STR,
   BKS, STS, TOL, SWG) is permanently decreased by 1. This uses the same
   permanent stat modification system as other mutations (modifies _base
   stat). Stats cannot drift below 4 (hard floor — prevents numerical
   impossibilities).
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: -1 to 1 random stat per floor descent. Hard floor at 4.
     L2: -1 to 1 random stat per floor descent (same). Additionally, stats
         at 5 or below from drift also suffer a functional penalty for that
         specific stat (e.g., CON at 4 means you lose 10 max HP beyond the
         normal formula, STR at 4 means -1 additional melee power, etc.).
         These functional penalties are tracked separately and accumulate.
     L3: -1 to 1 random stat per floor descent. Additionally, once per
         run (not per floor), if any stat reaches 4 (the hard floor), a
         one-time Cascade triggers: EVERY stat drops by 1 simultaneously
         (the genome collapses further). After the Cascade, the hard floor
         for all stats lowers to 3 instead of 4. The Cascade can only
         happen once per run. Cap.
   Note: At L1, this is 1 stat per floor × 8 floors = -8 stat points
   spread across 6 stats over a full run. Average each stat loses ~1.3
   points. Meaningful but not catastrophic. At L3, the Cascade is a
   dramatic climax event that the player both fears and is fascinated by.
   Track drift penalties with genome_drift_penalties: dict[str, int] in
   engine (per-run, maps stat name to accumulated extra penalty).
   The functional penalties at L2 are implemented as permanent additional
   modifiers in stats.py effective_* properties (read from the penalty dict).
   Reversal: Remove genome_drift_active flag. The stat reductions are NOT
   reversed (they are permanent _base changes). The functional penalties
   dict is cleared (L2). The Cascade trigger flag is cleared (L3).
   Note on reversal fairness: Shedding Genome Drift is extremely valuable
   precisely because it stops future degradation, but past losses are kept.
   This is intentional — the mutation rots you while you have it.
   System hooks: player_stats.genome_drift_active, engine.py floor descent
   handler (stat decrement via same path as permanent stat mutations), stats.py
   effective_* properties (L2 functional penalties from genome_drift_penalties
   dict), engine.genome_drift_penalties: dict (run-wide), engine.genome_drift_cascade_used:
   bool (run-wide).
   Rimworld inspiration: Aging degradation / Cell instability genes — a
   slow-burn permanent decay that makes every floor descent slightly dreadful.


================================================================
III. STRONG GOOD MUTATIONS (5)
================================================================

Design principle: Strong good mutations are build-defining. They should
create a distinct playstyle identity and come with a meaningful cost
(equipment constraint, HP cost, per-use charge, or behavioral limitation).

--

K. UNSTOPPABLE MASS — "When you move, you keep moving. Physics catches up later."
   Effect: Complete immunity to knockback. You cannot be pushed, displaced, or
   stumbled by any source (knockback abilities, Lopsided Growth, etc.).
   Additionally, you are immune to the SlowEffect (speed reduction status).
   Passive: once per floor, if you move through the same tile as an enemy
   (i.e., you attempt to walk into an occupied enemy tile and the standard
   move-attack would trigger), you instead Charge Through — the enemy is
   displaced to the nearest open tile behind them (1 tile) and takes
   4 + (STR // 2) impact damage, and you continue your movement into their
   vacated tile. This is NOT an attack action — it is part of your move.
   The move still costs normal movement energy. You do NOT stop moving on
   a Charge Through.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Knockback immune; slow immune; 1/floor Charge Through (4 + STR//2
         impact, displace to nearest open tile).
     L2: 2/floor Charge Through. Impact damage increases to 6 + STR//2.
         Additionally, enemies you Charge Through are stunned for 2 turns
         (not just displaced). Knockback/slow immunity retained.
     L3: Charge Through uses per floor becomes 3. Impact damage 8 + STR//2.
         Stun retained. Additionally, enemies ADJACENT to the Charge Through
         path (the tiles on either side of your movement line) take 3 splash
         impact damage from the pressure wave. Cap.
   Note: Charge Through must check that the tile behind the enemy is open
   floor. If no open tile is available behind them, the Charge Through
   instead pushes the enemy into the nearest open adjacent tile and deals
   double damage (2 × normal impact) — they hit a wall.
   Reversal: Remove unstoppable_mass_active flag, remove all immunities,
   remove Charge Through behavior.
   System hooks: player_stats.unstoppable_mass_active, combat.py knockback
   path (block if flag set), effects.py SlowEffect.apply() (block if flag
   set), engine.py movement handler (Charge Through check per move — consume
   charge, displace enemy, deal damage), engine.unstoppable_charge_uses: int
   (per-floor counter), combat.py splash damage resolver (L3 adjacency check).
   Rimworld inspiration: Robust + Unstoppable combo — a body built to ignore
   consequences, creating a "just walk through them" fantasy that rewards
   aggressive positioning.

--

L. HEMOGENIC GLAND — "Something opened up in your neck. It's hungry."
   Equipment constraint: Cannot equip neck slot.
   Effect: Grants the Blood Drain ability (ADJACENT target, INFINITE charges,
   cooldown 8 turns). On activation: deal 0 damage but drain from the
   adjacent target, healing yourself for 10 HP + (mutation_count // 3).
   The target takes no combat damage but is drained of vitality — they
   receive the WetEffect (representing blood-loss, flavor: they're
   weakened and clammy) for 5 turns. The drain cannot be used on enemies
   at full HP — only enemies below 80% HP are valid targets (you need an
   opening in their defenses to feed). Bosses are immune to the drain heal
   but still take the Wet status.
   Upgrade:
     L1: Neck blocked; Blood Drain: heals 10 + count//3 HP; Wet 5t; 80% HP
         threshold; 8t cooldown.
     L2: Heal increases to 15 + count//3. Wet 6t. Threshold raises to 90%
         HP (easier to use — they only need to be slightly hurt). Cooldown
         reduces to 6 turns. Additionally, if the target has 3 or more active
         negative status effects, the Blood Drain also heals 5 HP bonus
         (the corruption in their blood is extra nourishing).
     L3: Heal 20 + count//3. Wet 8t. Threshold 90%. Cooldown 5t.
         Bonus heal for debuffed targets is retained. Additionally, killing
         an enemy within 2 turns of draining them triggers Feeding Frenzy:
         grant 15 temp HP (not capped by max HP — this is temp HP on top of
         max HP, absorbed first as per NIGRL damage rules). Feeding Frenzy
         can trigger at most once per floor. Cap.
   Note: No neck slot + a drain that requires the enemy to be damaged first
   means this mutation rewards sustained combat management, not opening burst.
   It explicitly does NOT grant per-turn regen. The heal fires once, on use,
   gated by a cooldown and an HP threshold.
   Reversal: Revoke "blood_drain" ability; restore neck slot.
   System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "blood_drain", engine.py equip check (block
   neck slot), spells.py execute (target HP% check, instant heal, Wet apply,
   boss immune logic), effects.py WetEffect (already exists), engine.hemogenic_frenzy_used:
   bool (per-floor), combat.py entity_died (check if blood_drain was used on
   target recently — hemogenic_drain_turns_remaining: dict[entity_id, int]).
   Rimworld inspiration: Sanguophage Bloodfeed — a thematic drain ability that
   heals the player from enemies, with natural constraints that prevent it from
   being free infinite sustain.

--

M. PSYCHIC AMPLIFIER — "Your thoughts hit harder. So does everything else."
   Effect: All ability damage is increased by 50% (multiplied after all other
   modifiers). All ability cooldowns are reduced by 1 turn (minimum 1). Each
   time an ability is activated, you take 3 HP damage (the neural strain of
   amplified psionic output). This HP cost bypasses all DR and cannot be
   prevented by any effect — it is metabolic self-harm, not incoming damage.
   Upgrade:
     L1: All ability damage ×1.5; all cooldowns -1; 3 HP per ability use
         (bypasses DR, unblockable).
     L2: Ability damage ×1.75; cooldowns -2 (minimum 1). HP cost per
         ability use increases to 5 HP. Additionally, abilities that already
         have a secondary effect (status effects applied, knockback, etc.)
         have the secondary effect's potency increased by 1 tier
         (e.g., if the ability applies 2 stacks of shocked, it now applies
         3 stacks).
     L3: Ability damage ×2.0; cooldowns -2 (same as L2 — cap at -2 reduction).
         HP cost per use 8 HP. Secondary effects +1 tier is retained.
         Additionally, if you use two different abilities in the same turn
         (possible with speed bonuses giving extra actions), the second
         ability costs 0 HP but its damage is reduced by 25%. Meant to
         reward ability combos while capping the cascade cost somewhat. Cap.
   Note: The HP cost is implemented as a direct self_damage call after the
   ability execute() fires, using a dedicated "psionic_strain" damage type
   that bypasses DR. This is NOT an Effect — it's a synchronous fire-and-
   forget damage event. The cooldown reduction stacks with all other sources.
   If a cooldown would be reduced below 1, it is clamped at 1.
   Reversal: Remove psychic_amplifier_active flag; remove the ability damage
   multiplier, cooldown reduction, and HP cost hooks. Existing cooldowns are
   NOT retroactively extended.
   System hooks: player_stats.psychic_amplifier_active, spells.py execute()
   (apply ×1.5/1.75/2.0 damage multiplier, trigger HP cost after execute,
   secondary effect tier bonus at L2/L3), abilities.py cooldown calculation
   (subtract 1 or 2 based on level), engine.py turn (track second-ability-
   same-turn flag for L3 discount: engine.psychic_amp_second_this_turn: bool).
   Rimworld inspiration: Psychically Sensitive / Super Psychically Sensitive
   — amplified mental output at physical cost. The HP cost per use makes
   reckless ability spam genuinely dangerous.

--

N. DEATHLESS CORE — "That's not how dying works anymore. Not for you."
   Effect: ONCE PER RUN: When the player would die (HP reaches 0 or below),
   instead of triggering permadeath, enter Regenerative Coma for 3 turns.
   During Regenerative Coma:
   - Player cannot act, cannot use abilities, cannot use items.
   - All incoming damage is reduced to 0 (the body is physically shut down
     — it has nothing left to take damage from).
   - After 3 turns, revive at 25% max HP.
   - At revival, one random skill loses 1 level (the coma scrambles the
     neural pathways — the player feels the loss as a dull wrongness).
     If the player has no skill at level > 1, no level is lost (grace clause
     for new characters).
   Tracked by: engine.deathless_core_used: bool (run-wide; set True when
   triggered, never resets — once per run, no exceptions).
   Upgrade:
     L1: 1/run death save; Regenerative Coma 3 turns; revive at 25% HP;
         lose 1 random skill level.
     L2: 1/run death save; Coma 2 turns; revive at 35% HP; lose 1 skill level.
         Additionally, at L2, the revival triggers a brief Surge: +3 to all
         stats for 10 turns (the body overcorrects coming out of coma).
     L3: 1/run death save; Coma 1 turn; revive at 50% HP; lose 1 skill level.
         The Surge is retained (+3 all stats, 10t). Additionally, at L3,
         the random skill level loss is replaced by a choice: the player sees
         a prompt asking them to pick which skill to lose the level in (3
         options shown). This transforms the cost from pure bad luck into a
         difficult decision. Cap.
   Note: Regenerative Coma MUST be implemented as a high-priority intercept
   in the death pathway (in engine.py's entity_died or combat.py's damage
   application), before any save deletion logic runs. The coma state is tracked
   as engine.deathless_coma_active: bool and engine.deathless_coma_turns: int
   (per-run, but functionally per-event — resets to 0 after revival). Incoming
   damage of 0 means the damage calculation still fires, the DR-after-armor
   reduction still fires, but the final HP subtraction is overridden to 0.
   Reversal: Remove deathless_core_level; remove the death intercept hook.
   If deathless_core_used is already True, removing the mutation has no
   practical effect (the save is already spent). The Surge effect, if active,
   expires naturally.
   System hooks: combat.py damage path (intercept at hp <= 0 check),
   engine.py entity_died (override for player death), engine.deathless_core_used:
   bool (run-wide), engine.deathless_coma_active: bool, engine.deathless_coma_turns: int,
   effects.py new temporary all-stat buff (AllStatSurgeEffect, 10t, +3 all stats),
   xp_progression.py or skills.py skill-level-down function, menu_state.py
   new prompt for L3 choice (or inline message system).
   Rimworld inspiration: Deathless (Archite gene) — a fundamental bypass of
   permadeath as a mechanic. In NIGRL, a permadeath game, this is the most
   powerful possible mutation, appropriately wrapped in costs (skill loss) and
   constraints (once per run, coma window).

--

O. ADAPTIVE IMMUNITY — "Your body takes notes. Bad experiences become policy."
   Effect: Each unique status effect type that is successfully applied to you
   (venom, bleed, ignite, chill, shocked, corroded, wet — each counts as one
   unique type) grants a permanent stacking resistance to that effect type.
   Each application of a new-to-you effect type grants 10% resistance to that
   type. Each subsequent application of the SAME type (after the first grants
   resistance) adds 5% more resistance, capped per type at 30%.
   Resistances granted: reduced tick damage for damage-over-time effects;
   reduced duration for behavioral effects (chill slow, stun).
   Tracking: engine.adaptive_immunity_resistances: dict[str, int] — key is
   effect type name, value is current % resistance (0-30 per type).
   Upgrade:
     L1: New effect type → 10% resistance. Repeat exposure → +5% (cap 30%/type).
         Duration/damage reduction as described.
     L2: New effect type → 15% resistance. Repeat exposure → +5% (cap 40%/type).
         Additionally, when a resistance reaches its cap (40% at L2), you gain
         a 1-time charge of immunity to that specific effect type that you can
         "spend" — the next application of that capped effect is fully blocked
         and negated (immune charge auto-fires). This charge regenerates once
         per floor. Tracked: engine.adaptive_immunity_charges: dict[str, bool].
     L3: New effect type → 20% resistance. Repeat exposure → +5% (cap 50%/type).
         Immunity charges retained (regenerate 1/floor per capped type).
         Additionally, at L3, once you have any 4 different effect types at cap
         resistance, you gain a passive aura: enemies within 2 tiles of you
         take 1 less damage from ALL their own damage-over-time effects
         (your immune field interferes with their biology). This aura is
         passive and perpetual (not per-turn energy, just a damage modifier).
         Cap.
   Note: Resistances for DoT effects = damage reduction per tick. For
   behavioral effects = duration multiplier (e.g., 30% resistance means
   chill lasts 70% of its normal duration, rounded up). The adaptive_immunity_resistances
   dict is run-wide (persists across floors). This mutation rewards playing
   through bad status application rather than avoiding it, which is a meaningful
   behavioral shift.
   Reversal: Remove adaptive_immunity_active flag, clear adaptive_immunity_resistances
   dict, clear adaptive_immunity_charges dict.
   System hooks: player_stats.adaptive_immunity_active, effects.py apply()
   (for any effect applied to player: check flag, update resistances dict,
   check for charge grant at L2/L3 cap), effects.py tick() (reduce damage/duration
   per resistance value), engine.adaptive_immunity_resistances: dict (run-wide),
   engine.adaptive_immunity_charges: dict (per-floor reset).
   Rimworld inspiration: Immunity spectrum / Robust immune system — the body
   adapting to adversity over time. The asymmetry between good runners (who
   avoid status effects) and bad runs (who get hit constantly but build up
   immunity) creates interesting run-to-run variation.


================================================================
IV. STRONG BAD MUTATIONS (5)
================================================================

Design principle: Strong bad mutations are run-defining constraints. Each
one should feel like it imposes a new game rule that fundamentally changes
how you approach every encounter. They are NOT just penalties — they are
alternative game modes.

--

P. KILL THIRST — "There's a thing behind your eyes that wants to watch
   something stop moving."
   Equipment constraint: Cannot equip feet slot (claws have replaced your
   feet — shoes no longer fit the anatomy).
   Effect: A Kill Counter is introduced (engine.kill_thirst_counter: int,
   counts UP from 0 each turn, resets to 0 on enemy kill). If the counter
   reaches 20 without a kill, escalating penalties begin:
     - Counter 20-29: Frustrated (−1 effective STR, −10 speed). Message: "The
       thirst is building."
     - Counter 30-39: Starved (−2 STR, −20 speed, −1 CON equivalent HP loss
       at the 30-turn mark — one-time HP deduction, not per-turn).
     - Counter 40+: Feral Depletion (−3 STR, −30 speed, lose 5 HP per 10
       additional turns of no kill). Message: "You're barely functional."
   On kill: counter resets to 0. All penalties immediately clear.
   Kill Satiety buff fires: +3 STR, +30 speed, +2 CON (temp HP: 10 HP),
   for 15 turns. Message: "The thirst is satisfied. For now."
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Counter threshold 20 turns. Satiety: +3 STR, +30 speed, 10 temp HP, 15t.
         Feet slot blocked.
     L2: Counter threshold drops to 15 turns (the thirst grows more urgent).
         Satiety buff is unchanged (same reward). The Frustration penalty now
         also applies confuse (ConsumedEffect, 2 turns) when crossing the 15-turn
         threshold (the mind starts slipping). Feet slot blocked.
     L3: Counter threshold drops to 12 turns. Satiety unchanged.
         The Frustration penalty now also forces a random melee attack against
         the nearest entity (including other enemies, i.e., friendly fire is
         possible) when crossing the threshold — the player lashes out
         involuntarily. Feral Depletion (40+) still applies. Cap.
   Note: Kill Satiety is a named buff implemented as KillSatietyEffect
   (duration 15t, +3 STR / +30 speed / +10 temp HP via apply(), clear all
   on expire()). The involuntary attack at L3 uses the standard melee attack
   path pointed at the nearest entity (measured by Chebyshev distance),
   and yes, it can hit another enemy (which counts as a kill and resets the
   counter — perverse but satisfying).
   Reversal: Remove kill_thirst_active flag, restore feet slot, clear counter,
   clear any active penalty effects, clear Satiety buff if active.
   System hooks: player_stats.kill_thirst_active, engine.py player turn-end
   (counter increment, threshold checks, penalty application), combat.py
   entity_died (counter reset, Satiety buff grant), engine.kill_thirst_counter:
   int (per-floor reset would be too lenient — run-wide), inventory_mgr.py
   equip check (block feet slot), effects.py KillSatietyEffect (new),
   effects.py ConsumedEffect (confuse at L2 threshold), combat.py random melee
   path (L3 involuntary attack).
   Rimworld inspiration: Kill Thirst + Kill Satiety — the dependency mechanic
   that makes the satisfaction of killing feel earned and dangerous at once.
   The feet slot constraint is visual and mechanical flavor: you literally walk
   on claws now.

--

Q. SUBSTANCE DEPENDENCY — "You thought it was a choice. It stopped being a
   choice around floor two."
   Effect: A Craving Counter is introduced (engine.substance_dep_counter: int,
   counts UP from 0 per turn, resets on any strain use). If the counter reaches
   30 without strain use, withdrawal begins:
     - Counter 30-44: Shaking (−1 to ALL stats). Message: "Your hands won't
       stop shaking."
     - Counter 45-59: Craving (−2 to ALL stats, -10 speed). Message: "You
       need something. Anything."
     - Counter 60+: Collapse (−3 to ALL stats, -20 speed, 1 HP per 10 extra
       turns — same escalation as Kill Thirst). Message: "You're falling apart."
   On ANY strain use: counter resets to 0. All penalties immediately clear.
   Satiated buff fires: +2 to ALL stats, +10 speed for 10 turns. The dependency
   feeds the power — using strains feels rewarding because it IS rewarding.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Counter threshold 30 turns. Satiated: +2 all stats, +10 speed, 10t.
     L2: Counter threshold drops to 22 turns. Satiated buff unchanged.
         The Shaking penalty now also drains 1 Skill Point from the player's
         pool (not a permanent loss — removes 1 unspent SP if available, then
         has no extra effect if SP pool is empty). The desperation makes it
         hard to focus on growth.
     L3: Counter threshold drops to 15 turns (you need a hit every 15 turns).
         Satiated buff unchanged. The SP drain is retained at L2.
         Additionally, at Collapse level, the player has a 20% chance per
         turn to involuntarily use and consume a random strain from inventory
         (if one exists) — the body self-medicates. If no strains in inventory
         at Collapse, this does not trigger. Cap.
   Note: The Satiated buff is a positive reinforcement loop — using strains
   is NOW mechanically good for you because of this mutation. This is
   Rimworld's drug dependency design directly adapted: the dependency amplifies
   the drug rather than just punishing its absence.
   At L3 threshold of 15 turns, this mutation demands a consistent strain
   supply. Combined with Rolling skill tree (which generates strain resources)
   or the Plug NPC in the Penthouse, this can be managed. Without supply
   management, Collapse is frequent.
   Reversal: Remove substance_dep_active flag, clear counter, clear all active
   penalty effects, clear Satiated buff if active.
   System hooks: player_stats.substance_dep_active, engine.py player turn-end
   (counter increment, threshold checks, penalties), item_effects.py strain
   dispatch (counter reset, Satiated buff), effects.py SatiatedEffect (new:
   +2 all stats, +10 speed, 10t), engine.substance_dep_counter: int (run-wide),
   skills.py SP drain (L2: check unspent SP, decrement by 1), engine.py
   inventory scan for L3 auto-use (random strain pickup + apply effects).
   Rimworld inspiration: Drug dependency (Go-juice Hussars) — the drug becomes
   both your power source and your leash. Supply management becomes a core
   run strategy.

--

R. PSYCHIC DEAFNESS — "The frequencies that make thought possible went quiet.
   You're alone in here. It's peaceful. It's also terrifying."
   Effect: You are immune to ALL crowd control effects regardless of their
   source. No enemy can stun, confuse, fear, slow, or disorient you. You
   are completely CC-immune.
   However: You cannot use ANY ability from ABILITY_REGISTRY. All abilities
   are disabled — not just ones with mind/psionic tags, ALL of them. The
   psychic deafness that shields you from CC also cuts off your access to
   the neural architecture that executes abilities. You can still attack,
   use items, use guns, equip gear. Abilities are simply gone.
   No equipment constraint.
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Full CC immunity. All abilities disabled.
     L2: CC immunity retained. All abilities disabled. Additionally, the
         deafness now also prevents strains that have ability-granting effects
         (e.g., strains that grant Rad Nova charges, etc.) from granting their
         ability component — the strain's stat effects still apply, the ability
         charges are simply not granted (they vanish into the silence).
     L3: CC immunity retained. All abilities disabled. Strain-ability block
         retained. Additionally, you cannot equip ability-granting unique items
         (items with grants_ability field) — they do nothing in your hands
         because the receiver is offline. If you have such items equipped when
         L3 is acquired (via upgrade), their ability effect is immediately
         suspended until the mutation is shed. Cap.
   Note: This is the "hard constraint" design from Rimworld in its purest form.
   CC immunity is incredibly powerful in NIGRL (enemies have stuns, fear,
   confuse, slowed — now all useless against you). The ability lockout is
   the brutal price. The player must decide: am I an ability-dependent build?
   If yes, Psychic Deafness is a run-ender. If not, it's a legitimate power.
   This mutation explicitly rewards builds that focus on weapons, items, and
   movement over ability use.
   Reversal: Remove psychic_deafness_active flag; re-enable all abilities.
   The strain-ability block (L2) and equipment suspension (L3) are also lifted.
   System hooks: player_stats.psychic_deafness_active, effects.py apply()
   (block any effect with a CC category if flag set), abilities.py execute()
   or spells.py dispatch (block ability use if flag set), item_effects.py
   strain dispatch (block ability-grant portion at L2), inventory_mgr.py equip
   (block grants_ability items at L3).
   Rimworld inspiration: Psychically Deaf — not "-20% psi ability power" but
   "no psi" entirely. A hard binary that creates a distinct identity and forces
   a real tradeoff.

--

S. PYROKINETIC LEAK — "You're not setting things on fire. Things near you just
   get hot. You can't turn it off."
   Effect: Every 5 turns, one random floor tile within 2 tiles of the player
   becomes a fire hazard (entity_type="hazard", hazard_type="fire") at that
   tile's location. This fire spawns automatically — no player action, no
   control. The player cannot choose which tile.
   Additionally: While the player is standing on or adjacent to a fire hazard,
   they heal 1 HP per fire tile in that radius (Chebyshev distance 1) per turn
   they remain adjacent. This healing is NOT per-turn regen — it only fires if
   fire is actively adjacent. (The mutation converts environmental fire to
   sustain, creating a feedback loop: you're making fire, fire heals you.)
   Fire heals a maximum of 3 HP per turn regardless of how many fire tiles
   are adjacent (aggregate cap).
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: 1 fire tile spawns every 5 turns within 2 tiles. Heal 1 HP/adjacent
         fire tile per turn (max 3 HP/turn while adjacent to fire).
     L2: 1 fire tile every 4 turns. Spawn radius stays at 2 tiles. The heal
         is retained. Additionally, the fires you spawn now persist 5 turns
         longer than normal fire hazards (normal fire expires in X turns; yours
         expire in X+5). More fire, longer lasting, harder to avoid.
     L3: 2 fire tiles every 4 turns. Spawn radius increases to 3 tiles.
         The heal is retained. The extended duration is retained.
         Additionally, enemies that die while standing on a fire tile you
         spawned (tracked by fire ownership — engine.player_owned_fires: set
         of entity ids) have a 30% chance to explode in a 1-tile-radius burst
         of 4 fire damage. Cap.
   Note: The fire you spawn is identical to crate/torch fire — enemies take
   normal fire damage standing on it, corridors can be blocked, items on the
   floor can be destroyed (same rules as existing fire hazard). This means
   the player is constantly creating environmental chaos they must also manage.
   The healing creates the "I'm burning everything around me, including myself,
   but I'm sustained by the flames" fantasy.
   No "you are immune to fire damage" — this mutation does not protect you from
   fire. It HEALS you near fire. There is a subtle difference when taking ignite
   damage vs. gaining heal from fire tiles.
   Reversal: Remove pyrokinetic_leak_active flag. Existing fires continue to
   burn normally until they expire naturally (they are real hazard entities in
   the dungeon, not removed on shed).
   System hooks: player_stats.pyrokinetic_leak_active, engine.py player turn-end
   (fire spawn counter: engine.pyrokinetic_counter: int per floor; spawn fire
   via hazards.create_fire() on random eligible tile near player),
   engine.py player turn-start (fire-adjacency heal: scan Chebyshev 1 for fire
   hazards, apply heal up to cap), hazards.py fire entity (ownership tracking:
   add player_owned: bool field to fire entity, or track in engine.player_owned_fires
   set), combat.py entity_died (L3 explosion check for owned fires).
   Rimworld inspiration: Fire Spew + Cremator environmental interaction — a
   power you cannot turn off that reshapes every environment you enter. The
   corridors of the Crack Den become a fire hazard maze of your own making.

--

T. HULKING FRAME — "You grew. Not in the good way. In the way where doorframes
   are now your enemy."
   Equipment constraint: Cannot equip hat slot (head too large for any headgear).
   Effect: Permanent +4 melee power. Permanent +20 max HP (added to HP formula;
   does NOT heal you when acquired — you get the max HP, not the HP itself).
   However: Movement through corridor tiles (tiles where the dungeon generator
   determined width = 1, tracked as dungeon.corridor_tiles: set of (x,y) tuples)
   costs triple the normal movement energy (300 instead of 100). You fill the
   corridor completely. Enemies attacking you in a corridor tile deal +3 bonus
   damage (you can't dodge in tight spaces — flat bypass, not a DR reduction).
   Upgrade (gets worse):
     L1: Hat slot blocked; +4 melee power; +20 max HP; corridor moves ×3 energy;
         +3 enemy damage bonus when you're in corridor.
     L2: Penalties intensify: corridor moves ×4 energy. Enemy damage bonus in
         corridor increases to +5. The +4 power and +20 HP are unchanged.
         Additionally, you cannot voluntarily move through a corridor tile if
         there is an enemy directly on the other side of it (the frame blocks
         you — you literally cannot squeeze past them). You must clear the
         path. (This means the player cannot retreat through corridors past
         enemies.)
     L3: Corridor moves ×5 energy. Enemy damage bonus in corridor +7.
         The blocking behavior is retained. Additionally, in rooms (non-corridor
         tiles), your hulking frame generates a Presence aura: enemies that
         start their turn within 2 tiles of you in a room suffer -2 morale
         (flavor: they are physically intimidated). Mechanically this applies
         a 1-turn FearEffect (immediate flee impulse) on a 35% chance per enemy
         per turn while within range. Cap.
   Note: corridor_tiles set must be populated during zone generation. Room-type
   tiles already tracked (Rect, L, U, T, Hall, Oct, Cross, Diamond, Cavern,
   Pillar, Circle are all room types — Hall type tiles may need reclassification
   as corridor vs. room depending on width). An alternative approach: define
   corridor tiles as any floor tile that has exactly 2 or fewer floor neighbors
   (cardinal), which is a simpler check without requiring a pre-built set.
   The energy cost for corridor movement is best added as a check in engine.py's
   handle_move() — if player is moving to a corridor tile (or is currently on
   a corridor tile), multiply movement energy cost by 3/4/5.
   The +20 max HP is permanent — add to player_stats.max_hp_bonus: int field
   (already proposed in main doc for other mutations).
   Reversal: Restore hat slot; remove +4 melee power; remove +20 max HP from
   max_hp_bonus (DOES reduce max HP, possibly killing the player if currently
   above the new max HP); remove corridor energy penalty; remove corridor damage
   bonus; remove Presence aura.
   System hooks: player_stats.hulking_frame_active, player_stats.max_hp_bonus
   (already in main doc), engine.py handle_move() (corridor tile check, energy
   multiply, enemy-blocking check at L2), combat.py incoming damage from
   enemies (add corridor flat bonus if player on corridor tile), ai.py enemy
   turn (L3 presence fear: check range to player in room tile, apply FearEffect),
   dungeon.py or zone_generators.py (corridor_tiles set population), inventory_mgr.py
   equip check (block hat slot).
   Rimworld inspiration: Body type genes + Dirtmole corridor limitations —
   turning size into a liability in specific terrain while remaining dominant in
   open space. The fantasy: you're a monster in rooms, a liability in hallways.

================================================================
VIII. BALANCE NOTES
================================================================

WEAK GOOD BALANCE:
  Frog Legs — Range-4 leap is strong; 6-turn cooldown is the constraint.
    At L3, landing-on-enemy bounce is the most complex — ensure destination
    tile check is strict (no OOB, must be open floor in direction of travel).
    Consider: bosses immune to landing-on-enemy bounce (they can't be
    bowled through).
  Spine Quiver — 3/floor is relatively conservative. At L3, 5/floor with
    bounce + 3 bleed is meaningful ranged output. Watch interaction with
    Bone Spurs (both apply bleed — stacking is fine, potentially very
    high bleed counts on single targets). Bleed count cap may be needed.
  Toxic Metabolism — Speed bonus caps at +30/40/50 which is considerable.
    At full tox (200-300 range), L3 formula gives high values. The cap
    prevents abuse. Primary check: does this make Chemical Sensitivity (Weak
    Bad) into a good mutation if combined? Answer: yes, and that is
    intentional. The synergy creates a risky build identity.
  Blood Memory — Surge reset on kill means it's essentially always active
    in active combat. It is mostly a combat-XP accelerator; value is that
    it accelerates progression, not that it deals more damage. L2 bonus SP
    once per floor is powerful but floor-gated. Watch: Blood Memory + Feral
    Instincts (Strong Good, kill → FeralRush) creates a very aggressive
    kill-chain build.
  Coagulation Glands — 8 HP / 2 per floor is equivalent to a small food
    item twice per floor. L3 double-heal at low HP (32 HP) is a near-
    death bailout tool. This is not overpowered given Strong Bad mutations
    that can bring max HP down (Accelerated Aging, Genome Drift).

WEAK BAD BALANCE:
  Narcoleptic Fits — 30% chance every 25 turns is roughly one fit per 3
    floors on average. L3 (50% every 15t, doubled at low HP) is severe.
    The primary check: does this make certain floor types unplayable? In
    the Crack Den, the density means a 5-turn stun near multiple enemies
    is often lethal. Intentionally brutal.
  Pyrophobic Nerves — Fire hazards currently spawn in room index 1 on every
    floor. The Crack Den has regular fire. This mutation is moderately
    impactful in Crack Den, increasingly painful in Meth Lab (where fire
    abilities exist). L2 weapon drop (50%) is the most dangerous upgrade:
    losing your weapon in active combat is a genuine emergency.
  Pig Hands — Ring slot blocking is 5 slots of 0 benefit. This is the
    correct severity for Weak Bad. The 40% fumble at L3 is noticeable but
    not run-ending since items still trigger (just at 50% effect). Key
    check: interaction with Substance Dependency (Strong Bad) — fumbled
    strain use at low-effect could fail to reset the craving counter. The
    fumble does NOT affect the timing or reset logic of Substance Dependency
    (the strain is still "used" — counter resets normally). Just the effect is halved.
  Noise Bleed — The alert radius of 10 tiles at L3 means every melee attack
    is a floor-wide announcement. This fundamentally changes engagement from
    "clear room by room" to "always fighting everything." In a Crack Den run
    this is consistently severe. Main concern: does this stack with
    Territorial Marking (Weak Bad, which already alerts enemies passively)?
    Answer: yes, they stack, and having both is genuinely run-ending.
    That's an edge case, not a balance problem — extremely unlucky polarity.
  Genome Drift — At 8 floors (4 Crack Den + 4 Meth Lab), L1 results in
    8 stat decrements across 6 stats. Roughly -1.3 per stat on average.
    Manageable but meaningful. At L3 Cascade, if any stat bottoms out,
    -1 to all stats simultaneously is dramatic. Hard floor at 3 (L3) means
    no stat can reach 0 or negative. Key synergy check: Genome Drift +
    Deathless Core — the drift erodes your combat potential over the run
    while the core gives you a safety net. Thematically appropriate.

STRONG GOOD BALANCE:
  Unstoppable Mass — CC immunity to knockback + slow is powerful; the
    Charge Through is a fun once-or-twice-per-floor bonus. The key check:
    does knockback immunity break any existing enemy design? Review
    enemies that use knockback as primary offense (if any have knockback as
    part of their special_attacks — the immunity just blocks the displacement,
    full damage still applies).
  Hemogenic Gland — Neck blocked, requires damaged target, 8-turn cooldown.
    At L3, 20 + count//3 HP with 5/floor Feeding Frenzy temp HP is
    substantial healing gated behind both the neck slot loss and the target
    requirement. Main concern: does the 90% HP threshold (L2+) allow use
    too freely? The cooldown prevents spam. Acceptable for Strong Good tier.
  Psychic Amplifier — Ability damage ×2.0 with 8 HP unblockable per use at
    L3 is the most dangerous combination. The HP cost means heavy ability
    users can kill themselves with their own abilities in large pull situations.
    This should be specifically called out: if the player has < 8 HP, using
    any ability will be lethal to themselves at L3. The game should warn but
    not prevent. The warning is part of the design.
  Deathless Core — The most powerful mutation in NIGRL (bypasses permadeath,
    once). Correctly balanced: skill level loss, 3-turn coma window (can be
    killed during coma if... actually, the coma reduces ALL incoming damage
    to 0, so they CANNOT be killed during coma). This is intentional. The
    coma is a guaranteed escape from any near-death situation, paid for by
    skill regression and a 1-turn to 3-turn pause. The main playtesting
    concern is L3's choice UI — the menu during coma must not allow enemies
    to continue attacking mid-choice. The 0-damage-during-coma mechanic
    handles this by protecting the player while the choice resolves.
  Adaptive Immunity — The resistance caps are reasonable (30/40/50% per
    type). The immunity charges at L2+ are the most powerful element —
    one full-block per type per floor is strong against venom/bleed builds.
    At L3, the 2-tile aura requiring 4 capped resistances is a high bar
    (requires taking 4 different effect types enough times to cap). Likely
    only achievable in Meth Lab zone where tox/rad/corroded/wet are common.

STRONG BAD BALANCE:
  Kill Thirst — The 20-turn threshold is generous in Crack Den (enemies are
    everywhere); it is dangerous in Meth Lab corridor runs where enemies
    are more spread out. The Satiety buff (+3 STR, +30 speed) is deliberately
    good — the mutation should feel like a reward when the thirst is satisfied
    and a nightmare when it isn't. Feet slot blocking means no foot-slot
    boots, which in current NIGRL means no boots at all (the only foot
    equippable). This is meaningful for builds relying on foot items.
  Substance Dependency — At L3 (15-turn threshold), this requires a strain
    every 15 turns. The Crack Den has 4 floors; with ~60-100 turns per floor
    (estimate), that's 4-7 strains needed per floor. This is tighter than the
    normal strain availability. The design intent is that Rolling skill tree
    (which generates strain resources) or Plug NPC strain purchases become
    near-mandatory. This is the "go-juice Hussar" design: the dependency
    defines your resource management strategy.
  Psychic Deafness — The most binary Strong Bad mutation: full CC immunity
    against all-abilities-disabled. This is deliberately polarizing. For an
    ability-light build (melee weapon + gun + items only), this is a free
    CC immunity. For a spell/ability build, it is a run-redefining catastrophe.
    There is no middle ground. That's the point.
  Pyrokinetic Leak — Fire spawning every 4 turns (L3) with 5+5 persistence
    means a long run will have the floor increasingly covered in fire you
    spawned. This fire damages the player too (via existing hazard damage
    logic). The healing offset (max 3 HP/turn near fire) may not keep pace
    with fire damage if several fires overlap in a corridor. This is
    intentional — the mutation is a controlled chaos engine. Main concern:
    fire blocking pathways could strand the player in Crack Den rooms. The
    fire spawns randomly within 2-3 tiles, so corridors will eventually be
    blocked. This is intended but means the player must keep moving.
  Hulking Frame — +4 power and +20 max HP is legitimately strong for Strong
    Bad tier. The corridor punishment is severe: at L3, ×5 energy per
    corridor step means corridor traversal costs 5 turns of energy. In
    dense Crack Den maps (predominantly corridor-based generation), this
    mutation can halve your effective action rate. That is the correct level
    of severity for a mutation that gives +4 power and +20 max HP.
    Presence aura at L3 in rooms is a strong crowd control buff (35% fear
    per enemy per turn in rooms). This rewards positioning in open areas,
    making the corridor penalty feel like a positional tradeoff rather than
    a pure penalty.
