CAVES OF QUD MUTATION SYSTEM - COMPREHENSIVE REFERENCE
=======================================================

Source: Caves of Qud (Freehold Games), full release (1.0).
Purpose: Inspiration for overhauling NIGRL's radiation mutation system from
stat buff/debuff rolls toward unique, mechanically interesting mutations.

Note: The wiki was unreachable via automated fetch (403). This reference is
compiled from deep knowledge of the game through its 1.0 release. Mutation
point costs, exact scaling formulas, and cooldown numbers are included where
known with confidence. Some exact per-level numbers may have shifted in
late patches -- the mechanical *design* of each mutation is accurate.

========================================================================
I. SYSTEM OVERVIEW
========================================================================

Character Creation:
- Mutants start with mutation points (MP) to spend on mutations.
- Each mutation has an MP cost (1-5). Defects give bonus MP.
- Chimera morphotype: extra MP, can get 2 extra physical mutations, but
  locked out of some mental ones. Esper: extra mental, locked out of some
  physical.
- Mutations have levels (1-10+). They level up via "rapid advancement"
  (random chance each level-up) or by spending mutation points at certain
  XP thresholds.

Gaining Mutations Mid-Game:
- Gamma moths, radioactive zones, certain items can trigger mutation gain.
- "Unstable Genome" mutation: gain a random mutation every ~50 levels.
- Cooking with certain ingredients can grant temporary mutation effects.
- Some items grant mutation-like abilities without being true mutations.

Mutation Levels:
- Every mutation scales with level. Physical mutations often scale
  linearly (e.g., +1 AV per level). Mental mutations often scale more
  dramatically (cooldown reduction, radius increase, damage increase).
- At character level-up, one random mutation gains a level. You also earn
  mutation points periodically to spend on advancing chosen mutations.

Key Design Principle:
  EVERY mutation in Qud does something *mechanically unique*. There are
  NO mutations that are just "+2 Strength" or "-1 Agility." Even the
  simplest mutations create new gameplay verbs, alter terrain interaction,
  or change fundamental rules about how your character operates.


========================================================================
II. PHYSICAL MUTATIONS (24 mutations)
========================================================================

--- OFFENSIVE ---

1. FLAMING HANDS
   Type: Activated ability
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Fire a cone of flame in a direction. Deals fire damage and
   sets tiles/creatures on fire. The cone has a fixed shape.
   Scaling: Damage increases per level (~Level*d4). Range/cone size
   increases at certain thresholds.
   Cooldown: ~15 turns base, decreases with level.
   Design Note: One of the most iconic Qud mutations. Creates fire on the
   map that persists and spreads. Synergizes with "Flaming Ray" mental
   mutation conceptually but they're separate abilities. Can destroy items
   on the ground, burn down wooden walls, cook food.

2. FREEZING HANDS
   Type: Activated ability
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Fire a cone of cold in a direction. Deals cold damage and
   can freeze creatures/liquids solid. Frozen creatures are stunned and
   brittle (take extra damage from physical attacks).
   Scaling: Damage increases per level. Freeze duration increases.
   Cooldown: ~15 turns base, decreases with level.
   Design Note: Frozen water becomes walkable ice. Frozen enemies that
   take enough damage shatter (instant kill). Environmental interaction
   is key -- freeze a pool of acid to walk across it.

3. CORROSIVE GAS GENERATION
   Type: Activated ability
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Release a cloud of corrosive gas centered on self. Gas
   spreads, deals acid damage per turn, and corrodes equipment (reduces
   AV permanently). Player is immune to own gas.
   Scaling: Damage per turn increases. Gas volume/density increases.
   Cooldown: ~40 turns base, decreases with level.
   Design Note: Equipment corrosion is devastating -- both to enemies
   (strips their armor) and accidentally to allies. Gas is physics-based,
   drifts with wind, fills rooms. The "you're immune to your own" rule
   is critical for usability.

4. ELECTRICAL GENERATION
   Type: Activated ability
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Discharge electricity in an arc. Hits a primary target and
   can chain to nearby targets in conductive contact (standing in water,
   wearing metal armor). Deals electrical damage.
   Scaling: Damage increases per level. Chain range/count increases.
   Cooldown: ~20 turns base.
   Design Note: Incredibly powerful in water. Standing in a pool and
   discharging can hit everything in the water. Metal equipment on enemies
   makes them more vulnerable. Has natural synergy with water-based
   environments.

5. QUILLS
   Type: Passive + Activated
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: PASSIVE: When hit in melee, attacker takes quill damage
   (reflection damage). ACTIVATED: Fire quills as a ranged attack in a
   burst pattern.
   Scaling: Passive reflection damage increases per level. Activated
   burst damage and range increase.
   Cooldown: Activated ~20 turns. Passive is always-on.
   Design Note: Dual-mode mutation. The passive thorns effect is one of
   the only ways in Qud to damage enemies just by being hit. The active
   burst gives ranged option to what's otherwise a melee-focused mutation.

6. STINGER (multiple variants)
   Type: Passive (extra attack)
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Grants a stinger natural weapon on a specific body part.
   Stinger attacks happen as an extra attack each melee round. Injects
   venom (poison damage over time). Different variants: tail stinger,
   arm stinger, etc.
   Scaling: Venom damage increases per level. Penetration value increases.
   Design Note: This is Qud's "extra natural weapon" template. The
   stinger occupies a body slot, preventing equipment there. This
   equipment-slot tradeoff is a core Qud mutation pattern.

7. HORNS
   Type: Passive (charge attack modifier)
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Grants a charge attack. When you move 3+ tiles in a straight
   line and hit an enemy, deal bonus horn damage that bypasses some armor.
   Can also be used to break walls/doors.
   Scaling: Charge damage increases per level. Can eventually break
   harder materials.
   Design Note: Movement-as-damage mutation. Rewards open-space fighting
   and corridor play. The wall-breaking utility is sometimes more valuable
   than the combat damage.

8. BURROWING CLAWS
   Type: Passive (extra attack) + Activated (dig)
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: PASSIVE: Extra claw attack each melee round. ACTIVATED: Dig
   through walls and terrain. Higher levels dig through harder materials
   (dirt -> rock -> metal).
   Scaling: Claw damage increases. Digging speed increases. Material
   hardness threshold increases per level.
   Cooldown: Digging is continuous (costs turns per tile dug).
   Design Note: Fundamentally changes dungeon navigation. You can create
   shortcuts, escape fights, access sealed areas. One of the strongest
   utility mutations in the game.

9. SPIT ACID / SLIME
   Type: Activated ability
   Cost: 2-3 MP
   Mechanic: Spit a glob of acid/slime at range. Acid damages and
   corrodes. Slime variant creates slippery terrain.
   Scaling: Damage/range increases per level.
   Cooldown: ~10 turns.

--- DEFENSIVE ---

10. CARAPACE (Turtle Shell)
    Type: Passive + Activated
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: PASSIVE: Grants significant bonus AV (armor value), but
    BLOCKS THE BODY EQUIPMENT SLOT. You cannot wear body armor.
    ACTIVATED: "Hunker down" -- crouch into shell for massive additional
    AV, but you cannot move or attack for the duration.
    Scaling: Base AV bonus increases per level (+1 AV per ~2 levels).
    Hunker AV bonus also scales.
    Design Note: THE iconic Qud mutation tradeoff. Huge defensive power,
    but permanently locks you out of the body armor slot (the single most
    important equipment slot). Hunker is a panic button that makes you
    nearly invincible but totally immobile. Teaches players about
    meaningful tradeoffs.

11. THICK FUR
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: Grants cold resistance and a small AV bonus. Does NOT
    block equipment slots.
    Scaling: Cold resistance and AV increase per level.
    Design Note: Simpler than Carapace but less powerful. No tradeoff
    beyond the MP cost. Good example of a "minor passive" mutation.

12. TWO-HEARTED
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Gain a second heart. +1 Toughness (permanent stat bonus).
    Bleed effects have reduced duration/severity. Increased natural
    healing rate.
    Scaling: Toughness bonus increases at higher levels. Bleed resistance
    improves.
    Design Note: One of the few mutations that gives a direct stat bonus,
    but it's wrapped in a thematic package (redundant organ) with specific
    mechanical riders (bleed resistance, healing).

13. ADRENAL CONTROL
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Activate to gain a burst of Quickness (extra actions per
    turn) for several rounds. Essentially a speed boost / haste.
    Scaling: Duration increases per level. Quickness bonus may increase.
    Cooldown: ~100 turns base, decreases with level.
    Design Note: Qud's version of "berserk speed." Very powerful for
    either bursting down a tough enemy or fleeing. Long cooldown makes
    it a strategic resource.

14. REGENERATION
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Regenerate HP every turn. Also regenerate severed/damaged
    limbs over time (Qud has a limb system). Higher levels regenerate
    faster and can regrow limbs more quickly.
    Scaling: HP/turn increases per level. Limb regrowth time decreases.
    Design Note: In Qud this is incredibly powerful because limb loss is
    permanent without it. The HP regen alone is strong, but the limb
    regrowth is the real draw. For NIGRL: note that passive regen is
    flagged as a design anti-pattern (players spam wait). Qud handles
    this because enemies are also acting on those turns.

15. DOUBLE-MUSCLED
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: +2 Strength permanently. Increased carry capacity. Natural
    melee attacks deal more damage.
    Scaling: Strength bonus increases per level.
    Design Note: The "simple but effective" physical mutation. Almost
    pure stat bonus but framed as a body modification.

16. TRIPLE-JOINTED
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: +2 Agility permanently. Increased dodge (DV). Can fit
    through smaller spaces.
    Scaling: Agility/DV bonus increases per level.

17. WINGS
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Fly. While flying, you can move over any terrain (pits,
    lava, water) without penalty. Ground-based melee attacks against you
    have a miss chance. You cannot fly while wearing heavy body armor.
    Scaling: Flight duration increases per level. At high levels, flight
    becomes nearly permanent.
    Cooldown: Flight duration limited, then cooldown.
    Design Note: One of the most mobility-altering mutations. Equipment
    restriction (no heavy armor while flying) creates build tension.
    Flying over hazards is massive utility.

18. MULTIPLE LEGS
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Extra pairs of legs. Increased movement speed. Harder to
    knock prone. Can equip extra boots (each pair of legs has a feet slot).
    Scaling: More legs at higher levels = more speed + more boot slots.
    Design Note: More equipment slots is unique to Qud because of its
    body-part-based equipment system. The speed bonus alone is strong.

19. MULTIPLE ARMS
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Extra pairs of arms. Each arm can hold a weapon or shield.
    Extra attacks per round. Can equip more arm-slot equipment.
    Scaling: More arms at higher levels.
    Design Note: Outrageously powerful at high levels. Four arms with
    four weapons is a damage multiplier. Balanced by being 4 MP and by
    requiring good weapons to fill all slots.

20. PHOTOSYNTHETIC SKIN
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: Regenerate HP while in sunlight (outdoors, daytime). Gain
    nutrition from sunlight (reduced food consumption). Skin turns green.
    Scaling: Regen rate and nutrition gain increase per level.
    Design Note: Zone-dependent mutation. Useless underground, strong
    outdoors. Creates interesting decisions about when to explore where.

21. HEIGHTENED HEARING
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: Detect creatures through walls within a radius. Detected
    creatures show as blips on the map even outside FOV. Increased
    awareness prevents ambushes.
    Scaling: Detection radius increases per level.
    Design Note: Information mutation. You can "see" enemies before they
    see you. Incredibly useful for planning. Works through walls, unlike
    normal sight.

22. HEIGHTENED QUICKNESS
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 5 MP
    Mechanic: Permanent Quickness bonus (extra speed). More actions per
    turn than normal creatures.
    Scaling: +2-3 Quickness per level. At high levels, you act
    significantly more often than enemies.
    Design Note: Most expensive physical mutation at 5 MP. Pure power --
    speed is king in roguelikes. In Qud's energy system (similar to
    NIGRL's), this is a permanent +X% actions.

23. NIGHT VISION
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: See in the dark. Radius of night vision increases per level.
    At high levels, darkness is essentially eliminated.
    Scaling: Vision radius in dark increases per level.

24. ELECTROMAGNETIC PULSE
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Release an EMP burst that disables robots and electronic
    equipment in a radius. Disabled robots can't act for several turns.
    Electronic equipment (force fields, turrets) shuts down.
    Scaling: Radius and disable duration increase per level.
    Cooldown: ~50 turns base.
    Design Note: Niche but devastating against the right enemies. Qud
    has a significant robot/tech faction, making this a faction-counter
    mutation.


========================================================================
III. MENTAL MUTATIONS (24 mutations)
========================================================================

--- PSYCHIC OFFENSE ---

1. CONFUSION
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Target a creature in LOS. That creature becomes Confused
   for several turns -- it moves and attacks randomly, potentially hitting
   allies or walking into hazards.
   Scaling: Duration increases per level. At high levels, can confuse
   multiple targets or confuse for very long durations.
   Cooldown: ~30 turns base.
   Design Note: Crowd control mutation. Confused enemies are dangerous
   to their allies, creating emergent chaos.

2. FORCE WALL
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 4 MP
   Mechanic: Create a wall of force across tiles in a line. The wall
   blocks movement and projectiles. Lasts for several turns then fades.
   Scaling: Wall length and duration increase per level. Wall HP (can
   be broken) increases.
   Cooldown: ~50 turns base.
   Design Note: Terrain creation mutation. Block corridors, split enemy
   groups, create cover from ranged attacks. Extremely tactical in a
   turn-based game. One of the strongest defensive mental mutations.

3. FORCE BUBBLE
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 5 MP
   Mechanic: Create a bubble of force around yourself. Nothing can enter
   or leave the bubble (no attacks in or out, no movement). You're
   invulnerable but also can't act offensively. Lasts several turns.
   Scaling: Duration increases per level.
   Cooldown: ~100 turns base.
   Design Note: Panic button / time-out mutation. Use it when surrounded,
   wait for allies, let enemies get bored and wander, or wait for another
   cooldown to come up. High cost (5 MP) reflects its power.

4. LIGHT MANIPULATION
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 3 MP
   Mechanic: Fire a beam of focused light (laser) in a direction. Deals
   damage to everything in the line. Can set flammable things on fire.
   Passes through multiple enemies.
   Scaling: Damage increases significantly per level. At high levels it's
   one of the highest single-target damage abilities.
   Cooldown: ~20 turns base.
   Design Note: Line AOE -- hits everything in a line. High damage but
   requires careful aiming to avoid allies. Unlike physical fire/cold,
   this is a precision weapon.

5. PYROKINESIS
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 4 MP
   Mechanic: Set a creature on fire with your mind. Targeted at range,
   no projectile (can't miss, can't be blocked). Deals fire damage over
   time as they burn. Can spread to adjacent flammable things.
   Scaling: Fire damage per turn and duration increase per level.
   Cooldown: ~40 turns base.
   Design Note: Reliable targeted DOT. Unlike Flaming Hands (cone, can
   miss), this always hits. But it's single-target and DOT rather than
   burst. The "no projectile" aspect makes it great against high-DV
   enemies.

6. CRYOKINESIS
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 4 MP
   Mechanic: Freeze a creature with your mind. Targeted at range, deals
   cold damage and applies frozen status (like Freezing Hands but
   single-target and ranged with no projectile).
   Scaling: Freeze duration and damage increase per level.
   Cooldown: ~40 turns base.

7. SUNDER MIND
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 4 MP
   Mechanic: Psychic attack that deals damage directly to a creature's
   mind, bypassing armor entirely. Target must be intelligent. Deals
   damage and can apply Dazed (reduced accuracy/actions).
   Scaling: Damage increases significantly per level. Daze duration
   increases.
   Cooldown: ~10 turns base.
   Design Note: Armor-piercing psychic damage is incredibly valuable.
   Short cooldown makes this a bread-and-butter attack for Esper builds.
   Only works on creatures with minds (useless vs robots/plants).

8. BURGEONING
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 5 MP
   Mechanic: Cause plants to rapidly grow in an area around you. The
   plants are hostile to all non-plant creatures and attack enemies that
   enter. Creates difficult terrain. Plants persist until killed.
   Scaling: Number and strength of plants increase per level. At high
   levels, creates a small army of plant allies.
   Cooldown: ~100 turns base.
   Design Note: Summoning mutation. The plants are permanent (until
   killed) and hostile to everyone. You need to be careful not to get
   trapped in your own jungle. Powerful zone denial.

9. DISINTEGRATION
   Type: Activated
   Cost: 5 MP
   Mechanic: Begin disintegrating a target. Takes several turns of
   concentration (channeled). If completed, deals massive damage that
   ignores all armor and resistance. Can destroy walls and objects.
   Scaling: Damage increases dramatically per level. Channel time may
   decrease.
   Cooldown: ~100 turns base.
   Design Note: The "nuclear option." Channel time makes it risky --
   getting hit interrupts the channel. But the damage is astronomical.
   Creates intense tactical moments: can I safely channel for 3 turns?

10. SPACE-TIME VORTEX
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 5 MP
    Mechanic: Create a vortex at a location. The vortex sucks in nearby
    creatures and teleports them to a random location on a random map
    zone (essentially removing them from the fight). Can affect the player
    too if standing nearby.
    Scaling: Vortex size and suction strength increase per level.
    Cooldown: ~200 turns base.
    Design Note: The most chaotic mutation. Removes enemies from existence
    (for the current fight) but doesn't kill them -- they're just
    somewhere else in the world now. Can accidentally teleport yourself
    or important NPCs. High risk, high reward.

11. TEMPORAL FUGUE
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 5 MP
    Mechanic: Create 2-3 temporal copies of yourself. Copies have all
    your equipment and abilities. They act independently (AI-controlled)
    and fight alongside you. When they die or the duration expires, all
    items they picked up or used are lost.
    Scaling: Number of copies and duration increase per level.
    Cooldown: ~200 turns base.
    Design Note: One of the most powerful mutations in the game. Multiple
    copies of a fully-equipped character is devastating. But copies can
    use your consumables, waste your ammo, and pick up loot that vanishes.
    Creates hilarious emergent situations.

--- PSYCHIC UTILITY / DEFENSE ---

12. TELEPATHY
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Sense the locations of all creatures with minds within a
    large radius. See through their eyes (reveals map around each
    detected creature). At higher levels, can identify creature types
    and see their inventory.
    Scaling: Detection radius increases per level.
    Design Note: The premier information-gathering mutation. You always
    know where intelligent enemies are, even through walls. Combined
    with "Sense Psychic" (detects other psychics specifically), gives
    massive tactical advantage. Does NOT work on robots, plants, or
    mindless creatures.

13. DOMINATION
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 5 MP
    Mechanic: Take direct control of a creature's body. You control the
    target as if it were your character (full inventory, ability, and
    movement access). Your real body goes dormant and vulnerable. If the
    dominated creature dies, you snap back to your body. If your body
    dies while you're dominating, YOU die.
    Scaling: Range and duration increase per level.
    Cooldown: ~200 turns base.
    Design Note: One of the most unique abilities in any roguelike. You
    literally play as another creature. Use their abilities, equipment,
    everything. Can dominate a powerful enemy and use them against their
    allies. Risk: your body is defenseless.

14. PRECOGNITION
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 5 MP
    Mechanic: See into the future. When activated, the game takes a
    SNAPSHOT of the current state. You then play forward normally. At
    any point, you can REVERT to the snapshot, undoing everything that
    happened. Essentially a controlled save-scum built into a mutation.
    Scaling: Duration of the "future sight" window increases per level.
    Cooldown: ~200 turns base.
    Design Note: Probably the single most powerful mutation in Qud. It
    lets you scout dangerous situations risk-free, try risky actions and
    revert if they fail, and essentially eliminates permadeath for one
    sequence of actions. Astronomically powerful. 5 MP and huge cooldown
    are necessary.

15. CLAIRVOYANCE
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Reveal the full map of your current zone for a limited
    duration. See all creatures, items, and terrain as if the entire
    level were in FOV.
    Scaling: Duration increases per level.
    Cooldown: ~100 turns base.
    Design Note: Map reveal -- the classic scrying ability. Less combat
    power than other mentals but incredible utility for navigation and
    avoiding danger.

16. TELEPORTATION
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Teleport to a random location within a radius. The
    destination is random within your chosen direction/area. Higher
    levels give more control over destination.
    Scaling: Range increases per level. Scatter radius decreases
    (more precise targeting at high levels).
    Cooldown: ~100 turns base.
    Design Note: Escape mutation. Not as controllable as you'd like at
    low levels (random destination can put you in worse danger). At high
    levels with small scatter, becomes a reliable tactical repositioning
    tool.

17. TELEPORT OTHER
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Teleport a targeted creature to a random location within
    the current zone. Can be used offensively (teleport enemy away) or
    supportively (rescue an ally).
    Scaling: Range to target increases per level.
    Cooldown: ~50 turns base.
    Design Note: Unlike Space-Time Vortex, this keeps the creature on
    the same map, just repositioned. Safer and more controllable but
    less powerful.

18. MASS MIND
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Link your mind with all intelligent creatures in a radius.
    Gain a bonus to mental mutations' strength proportional to the number
    of linked minds nearby. More creatures = stronger psychic powers.
    Scaling: Link radius and bonus per linked mind increase per level.
    Design Note: Scaling mutation that gets stronger in crowds. Normally
    being surrounded is bad, but Mass Mind turns it into a power source.
    Interesting risk-reward: you want enemies nearby to be strong enough
    to kill them.

19. EGO PROJECTION
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Project your force of will at a target. Target is
    intimidated (may flee) or awed (may become friendly temporarily).
    Effect depends on your Ego vs their MA (Mental Armor).
    Scaling: Effectiveness increases per level.
    Cooldown: ~30 turns.

20. SENSE PSYCHIC
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 1 MP
    Mechanic: Detect the presence and location of psychic creatures
    (those with mental mutations) in a radius. Highlighted on map.
    Scaling: Range increases per level.
    Design Note: Cheap, niche detection. Very useful in specific
    situations (avoiding Esper hunters).

21. MENTAL MIRROR
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 4 MP
    Mechanic: Chance to reflect mental attacks back at the attacker.
    When an enemy uses a mental mutation against you, it can bounce back
    and affect them instead.
    Scaling: Reflection chance increases per level. At high levels, ~50%+
    reflection rate.
    Design Note: The "thorns" of mental defense. Punishes enemies for
    using psychic powers against you. Very meta -- only useful against
    psychic enemies, but devastating against them.

22. PHOTOSYNTHESIS (Mental variant if distinct from Physical)
    Note: In some versions this is physical-only. Including for
    completeness -- see Physical #20 for details.

23. KINETIC DAMPENER / FORCE FIELD
    Type: Passive
    Cost: 3 MP
    Mechanic: Passively reduce incoming physical damage by a flat amount.
    Functions as a mental-powered damage shield.
    Scaling: Damage reduction amount increases per level.

24. PSYCHOMETRY
    Type: Activated
    Cost: 2 MP
    Mechanic: Touch an item to learn its full properties (identify it).
    At higher levels, can learn the history of items, identify artifacts,
    and gain bonus XP from identifying rare items.
    Scaling: Information revealed increases per level. XP bonus for rare
    IDs.
    Cooldown: No cooldown (but requires touching the item).
    Design Note: Utility mutation that replaces scrolls of identification.
    In Qud's complex item system with many unknown artifacts, this is
    very valuable.


========================================================================
IV. MORPHOTYPES, PHYSICAL DEFECTS, AND MENTAL DEFECTS
========================================================================

MORPHOTYPES (modify mutation access and body plan):

- CHIMERA: +1 MP per physical mutation chosen. Gain random physical
  mutations more often. Extra body parts possible. Locked out of some
  mental mutations. Body can become increasingly monstrous.

- ESPER: +1 MP per mental mutation chosen. Mental mutations cost less.
  Gain "Glimmer" -- a danger meter that attracts Esper hunters (psychic
  assassins) as your mental power grows. More mental mutations = more
  Glimmer = more danger.

  Design Note: Glimmer is a BRILLIANT design. Your mental mutations
  make you stronger but also paint a target on your back. It's a
  scaling difficulty modifier tied to your own power choices.

PHYSICAL DEFECTS (grant bonus MP at character creation):

- Albino: Vulnerable to light-based damage. -1 MP (gives you 1 extra MP).
- Brittle Bones: Reduced HP. Falls deal extra damage.
- Myopic: Reduced sight radius.
- Hemophilia: Bleed effects are more severe and last longer.
- Hooks for Feet: Movement speed penalty on certain terrain.
- Beak: Replaces face slot, cannot wear face equipment, but grants a
  peck attack.
- Spontaneous Combustion: Randomly catch fire. Very dangerous but gives
  significant bonus MP.

MENTAL DEFECTS (grant bonus MP):

- Amnesia: Periodically forget map exploration (fog of war returns).
- Evil Twin: A hostile copy of you spawns somewhere in the world and
  hunts you. Has all your stats and equipment.
- Narcolepsy: Randomly fall asleep during combat. Waking deals are
  possible but risky.
- Blunted Senses: Reduced sight/hearing/detection radius.
- Social Anxiety: Penalties when near multiple NPCs. Trading is harder.
- Unstable Genome: Gain random mutations over time -- sounds good but
  they're random (could be bad).


========================================================================
V. KEY DESIGN PATTERNS FOR NIGRL ADAPTATION
========================================================================

PATTERN 1: "EQUIPMENT SLOT TRADEOFFS"
  Qud mutations: Carapace (blocks body armor), Stinger (occupies limb
  slot), Beak (replaces face), Multiple Arms (adds arm slots).

  NIGRL parallel: NIGRL has weapon, sidearm, rings (5), neck, feet, hat.
  Mutations could block or replace these slots. Example: "Chitinous
  Shell" blocks neck slot but grants +3 DR. "Mutant Claw" replaces weapon
  slot but grows stronger with radiation level.

PATTERN 2: "NEW GAMEPLAY VERBS"
  Every Qud mutation creates a NEW thing you can DO, not just a number
  that goes up. Burrowing Claws = dig through walls. Wings = fly over
  terrain. Domination = play as another creature. Force Wall = create
  terrain.

  NIGRL parallel: Mutations should grant abilities (like the existing
  ability system). "Acid Spit" = new ranged attack. "Phase Shift" = walk
  through walls for X turns. "Pheromone Aura" = pacify adjacent enemies.

PATTERN 3: "ENVIRONMENT INTERACTION"
  Qud mutations interact with the physical world: Flaming Hands sets
  tiles on fire, Freezing Hands freezes water, Burrowing Claws digs
  walls, Corrosive Gas corrodes gear, Electrical Generation chains
  through water.

  NIGRL parallel: Meth Lab has toxicity/radiation as environmental
  features. Mutations could interact with these. "Rad Absorption" =
  clear radiation from tiles you walk on, healing you. "Toxic Cloud" =
  leave poison gas when hit. "Grease Blood" = spill grease tiles when
  wounded.

PATTERN 4: "SCALING BEYOND NUMBERS"
  Qud mutation scaling often adds new capabilities at thresholds, not
  just bigger numbers. Burrowing Claws at level 5 can dig metal walls
  (previously only dirt). Telepathy at level 8 sees inventory. Teleport
  at high level reduces scatter to near-zero.

  NIGRL parallel: Mutations could gain new riders at radiation thresholds.
  "Thick Hide" at base = +2 DR. At 150+ rad = also reflects 25% melee
  damage. At 250+ rad = also grants immunity to bleed.

PATTERN 5: "GLIMMER / POWER-HAS-A-COST"
  Esper Glimmer mechanic: more mental mutations = stronger psychic
  hunters come after you. Power attracts danger.

  NIGRL parallel: Could tie to existing faction system. More mutations =
  higher "Mutant Signature" that attracts special enemies or reduces
  faction reputation with groups that hate mutants.

PATTERN 6: "DEFECTS AS MEANINGFUL CHOICES"
  Qud defects aren't just bad -- they're interesting constraints that
  change how you play. Narcolepsy means you might fall asleep mid-fight
  (terrifying), but the bonus MP lets you take stronger mutations. Evil
  Twin creates a persistent nemesis.

  NIGRL parallel: Bad mutations shouldn't just be "-2 Strength." They
  should be: "Bioluminescent" (enemies can see you from further away),
  "Glass Bones" (falls/knockback deal 3x damage), "Paranoid" (random
  hallucination enemies appear on screen).


========================================================================
VI. CONCRETE NIGRL RECOMMENDATIONS
========================================================================

Based on the Qud analysis, here are specific mutation categories for
NIGRL's radiation mutation system overhaul:

CATEGORY A: COMBAT MUTATIONS (grant activated abilities)
- Acid Spit: Ranged attack, 3-tile line, deals damage + corrosion debuff
- Toxic Nova: AOE burst centered on self, applies toxicity to all
  adjacent enemies
- Bone Spurs: Passive thorns (reflect melee damage) + activated burst
- Mutant Strength: +power but -speed (trade one stat axis for another)
- Adrenal Surge: Gain double energy for 5 turns, 30-turn cooldown

CATEGORY B: MOBILITY MUTATIONS (change how you move)
- Phase Shift: Walk through walls for 3 turns, 40-turn cooldown
- Long Legs: +15% movement speed permanently, but kick attack replaces
  normal melee
- Gecko Grip: Move through enemy-occupied tiles without attacking (slip
  past enemies)
- Tunnel Digger: Dig through destructible walls (new terrain interaction)

CATEGORY C: DEFENSE MUTATIONS (passive body changes)
- Chitinous Shell: +3 DR but blocks hat slot (equipment tradeoff)
- Rubber Skin: Immune to electrical damage, but -2 to all melee damage
  dealt
- Regeneration: Heal 1 HP when you kill an enemy (NOT passive regen --
  respects the no-passive-regen rule)
- Adrenaline Glands: When below 30% HP, gain +50% damage for 10 turns
  (once per floor)

CATEGORY D: UTILITY MUTATIONS (information/environment)
- Echolocation: Detect enemies through walls within 5-tile radius
- Pheromone Cloud: 30% chance enemies that enter melee range are Confused
  for 2 turns
- Radiation Sense: See radiation levels on all visible tiles, immune to
  rad ambushes
- Toxic Metabolism: Convert toxicity to HP (spend 20 tox to heal 10 HP,
  activated)

CATEGORY E: DEFECT MUTATIONS (bad but interesting)
- Bioluminescent: Your sight radius +2, but enemies can also detect you
  from +3 tiles away
- Brittle Bones: Knockback effects deal 2x damage, you can't wear boots
- Hemophilia: All bleed durations doubled, but your blood creates grease
  tiles
- Paranoid: Phantom enemies occasionally appear in FOV (not real, waste
  your attacks)
- Unstable Form: Every 50 turns, one random stat shifts by +/-1

IMPLEMENTATION NOTE:
Each mutation should be implemented as an Effect subclass (using NIGRL's
existing @register decorator system) with the appropriate lifecycle hooks.
Combat mutations can also grant AbilityDef entries via engine.grant_ability().
Equipment-blocking mutations use a new "blocked_slots" field checked
during equip validation.

The existing tier system (weak/strong/huge) maps well to mutation power:
- Weak: Simple single-effect mutations (Thick Fur, Night Vision)
- Strong: Dual-mode or scaling mutations (Quills, Adrenal Control)
- Huge: Game-changing mutations (Phase Shift, Burrowing Claws, Temporal)

Bad mutations (67% chance) should draw from DEFECT category.
Good mutations (33% chance) should draw from COMBAT/MOBILITY/DEFENSE/UTILITY.
