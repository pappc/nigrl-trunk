CDDA-INSPIRED MUTATION EXPANSION — 20 NEW MUTATIONS
=====================================================
NIGRL Roguelike — Mutation Overhaul Appendix
Inspired by: Cataclysm: Dark Days Ahead mutation system
Purpose: 20 candidate mutations for selection and inclusion in the master plan

Numbering: These extend the existing 80-mutation plan. Each category gets
5 new entries (numbered 21-25 within their category).

Design principles carried forward:
  - Every mutation changes HOW you play, not just tunes stats
  - No passive per-turn HP regen
  - Bad mutations create interesting decisions, not just punishment
  - Strong mutations are build-defining
  - Upgrade paths change behavior, not just scale numbers
  - All mutations must have valid reversal logic

Themes explored (gaps in the existing 80):
  - Terrain/environment creation (leaving trails, creating obstacles)
  - Summoning/splitting (spawn decoys or temporary allies)
  - Sound/noise mechanics (not explored in existing mutations)
  - Item/equipment fusion (mutations that change how gear works)
  - Temporary body morphing (grow extra reach, extend limbs)
  - Enemy manipulation (charm, redirect, puppeteer)
  - Conditional triggers (activate only under specific game states)
  - Resource transformation (convert one resource to another)
  - Probability manipulation (change how RNG treats you)
  - Map awareness/memory (know things about the dungeon itself)

WEAK GOOD MUTATIONS (21-25)
================================================================

21. SLIME TRAIL — "You leave something behind. It's useful, actually."
    Effect: Every 4th tile you move, you leave a slime tile at your previous
    position. Slime tiles function like grease tiles for ENEMIES only — they
    slip and lose 1 turn if they step on one. You are immune to your own
    slime. Slime tiles persist for 20 turns then evaporate.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Slime every 4 tiles; enemies slip (1 turn loss); 20t persist; you immune.
      L2: Slime every 3 tiles; 30t persist. Enemies that slip on your slime also
          take 2 tox (the slime is mildly corrosive). Your slime tiles are
          visually distinct from normal grease (rendered as '~' in green).
      L3: Slime every 2 tiles; 40t persist; slip tox increases to 5. Additionally,
          if 3+ slime tiles are adjacent to each other, they form a "slime patch"
          that also slows enemy movement through them by 50% even if they don't
          slip (wading through goo). Cap.
    Note: Slime tiles are stored in dungeon.slime_tiles: dict[(x,y), int] where
    the int is the turn placed. Check expiry each tick. Different from grease_tiles.
    Synergy: Pheromone Gland pulls enemies toward you — through your slime trail.
    Reversal: Remove slime_trail_active flag; clear all slime tiles on the floor.
    System hooks: player_stats.slime_trail_active, engine.py player movement
    (slime placement counter), dungeon.slime_tiles (new dict), ai.py enemy
    movement (slip check), combat.py (L2 tox on slip).

22. ECHOLOCATION PULSE — "You click. The room answers."
    Effect: Grants the Echolocation Pulse ability (INFINITE charges, SELF target,
    8-turn cooldown). When activated: reveal ALL entities (enemies, items, hazards)
    on the current floor for 1 turn, even through walls. They appear as '?' markers
    on the map (similar to Eyeless sonar but temporary and much wider). After the
    pulse, enemies that were revealed and are within 5 tiles become briefly
    "disoriented" — they skip their next action (the sonic ping stuns them).
    Upgrade:
      L1: Reveal all entities for 1 turn; disorient enemies within 5 tiles (skip 1 action);
          8t cooldown.
      L2: Reveal lasts 2 turns; disorient range extends to 7 tiles; cooldown drops to 6t.
          Disoriented enemies also have their AI state forced to WANDERING for 3 turns
          (they lose track of you after the ping).
      L3: Reveal lasts 3 turns; disorient range 9 tiles; cooldown 4t. Additionally,
          the pulse itself deals 2 damage to all enemies within 3 tiles (sonic
          concussion — the click is LOUD at close range). Enemies within 1 tile
          take 4 damage instead. Cap.
    Note: This is a sound-based mutation — distinct from FOV mutations because it
    doesn't change your persistent vision. It's a snapshot pulse. The disorient
    is a 1-action stun, lighter than a full stun effect.
    Reversal: Revoke "echolocation_pulse" ability.
    System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "echolocation_pulse", render.py (temporary
    entity reveal), ai.py (forced WANDERING on disorient), combat.py (L3 damage).

23. STICKY FINGERS — "Things stick to you now. Literally."
    Effect: When you kill an enemy with a melee attack, there is a 30% chance
    the enemy drops an extra item from the floor's loot table (bonus loot from
    the kill). This item materializes at the kill location. Additionally, picking
    up gold piles grants +10% bonus gold (rounded down, minimum +1).
    Upgrade:
      L1: 30% bonus loot on melee kill; +10% gold pickup bonus.
      L2: 40% bonus loot; +20% gold bonus. Additionally, when you pick up any item,
          there is a 15% chance to find a second copy of the same item stuck to it
          (duplication — only works on stackable items like ammo, food, materials).
      L3: 50% bonus loot; +25% gold bonus; 20% item duplication on pickup.
          Additionally, once per floor, a killed enemy drops a random ring or
          neck item instead of its normal loot (the mutation's adhesive quality
          pulls hidden treasures from corpses). Cap.
    Note: "Bonus loot" uses loot.py generate_floor_loot() with current zone/floor.
    The duplication only works on items where count > 0 (stackable). Unique items,
    equipment, and non-stackable items cannot be duplicated.
    Reversal: Remove sticky_fingers_active flag.
    System hooks: player_stats.sticky_fingers_active, combat.py entity_died
    (bonus loot roll), inventory_mgr.py pickup (gold bonus, L2 duplication),
    loot.py (L3 ring/neck drop).

24. CHAMELEON SKIN — "Hold still. They can't see you if you hold still."
    Effect: If you do not move for 2 consecutive turns (attacking counts as not
    moving — only tile changes break it), you become "camouflaged." While
    camouflaged, enemies outside 3 tiles cannot detect you regardless of LOS.
    Enemies within 3 tiles still see you normally. Camouflage breaks the instant
    you move to a different tile. Your first melee attack from camouflage deals
    +50% damage (ambush strike).
    Upgrade:
      L1: 2-turn stillness → camouflage; 3-tile detection radius; +50% ambush strike.
      L2: 1-turn stillness → camouflage (activates immediately after any non-move action).
          Detection radius shrinks to 2 tiles. Ambush strike deals +75% damage.
          Additionally, while camouflaged, enemy ranged attacks that target your tile
          have a 30% miss chance (they can't quite pin you down).
      L3: 1-turn activation; detection radius 1 tile (essentially melee range only).
          Ambush strike +100% damage. Ranged miss 50%. Additionally, the ambush strike
          also applies 2 turns of stun to the target (they didn't see it coming). Cap.
    Note: Camouflage is tracked as engine.chameleon_active: bool, with
    engine.chameleon_still_turns: int counting consecutive non-move turns.
    Attacking does NOT break camouflage (only movement does). This creates a
    "stand your ground" playstyle where staying put is rewarded.
    Anti-synergy: Lopsided Growth (stumble after attacks forces movement, breaking camo).
    Synergy: Extra Joints free step doesn't trigger since you need to stand still.
    Reversal: Remove chameleon_skin_active flag; clear camouflage state.
    System hooks: player_stats.chameleon_skin_active, engine.py movement/action
    (track stillness), ai.py detection (check camouflage), combat.py melee
    (ambush bonus), gun_system.py (L2 ranged miss chance).

25. PROBABILITY FLUX — "Luck bends around you. Not always favorably."
    Effect: All percentage-based rolls in your favor (crit chance, dodge chance,
    on-hit effect procs, loot drop rolls) gain +5% bonus. However, all percentage
    rolls AGAINST you (enemy crit, enemy on-hit procs, trap trigger chance) also
    gain +5% bonus. Everything is more likely to happen near you — good and bad.
    The universe is chaotic in your presence.
    Upgrade:
      L1: +5% to ALL percentage rolls (yours and enemy's).
      L2: +10% to all rolls. Additionally, critical hits you land deal +2 bonus
          damage (the probability flux amplifies lucky strikes).
      L3: +15% to all rolls. Crit bonus +4 damage. Additionally, once per floor,
          when you would die (HP reaches 0), there is a 25% chance the flux
          intervenes — you survive with 1 HP and the attacking enemy takes the
          killing blow's damage reflected back at them (probability reversal).
          This is NOT a death save — it's a 25% chance, and it can fail. Cap.
    Note: The +X% is additive to existing percentages, not multiplicative. A 10%
    crit chance becomes 15%/20%/25% at L1/L2/L3. Enemy procs are equally boosted.
    This is the only mutation that directly modifies probability across the board.
    Reversal: Remove probability_flux_active flag and level.
    System hooks: player_stats.probability_flux_level: int, combat.py (all roll
    modifiers — crit, dodge, on-hit procs), loot.py (drop rolls), engine.py
    (L3 death-prevention roll).

21. VOLATILE ANATOMY — "Your insides rearrange themselves. Mid-fight."
    Effect: At the start of each floor, one of your 6 stats (randomly chosen)
    is reduced by 2 and a DIFFERENT random stat is increased by 1. The swap
    persists for the entire floor. On next floor transition, the swap reverts
    and a new random swap occurs. You never know which stat will be drained.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: -2 to one stat, +1 to another (random each floor).
      L2: -3 to one stat, +1 to another. Additionally, the stat chosen is
          weighted — it has a 40% chance to hit your HIGHEST current stat
          (the mutation targets what you rely on most).
      L3: -4 to one stat, +1 to another. Highest-stat targeting at 60%.
          Additionally, mid-floor there is a 20% chance every 50 turns for a
          "re-shuffle" — the stat swap changes again within the same floor
          (your anatomy keeps shifting). Cap.
    Note: The swap is tracked in engine.volatile_anatomy_current: dict with
    keys "drained_stat" and "boosted_stat" and their respective amounts.
    Reverted on floor change, then re-rolled.
    Reversal: Remove volatile_anatomy_active flag; revert any current swap.
    System hooks: player_stats.volatile_anatomy_active, engine.py floor_transition
    (roll swap), engine.py per-turn (L3 re-shuffle timer),
    player_stats.modify_base_stat (apply/revert swap).

22. RESONANT BONES — "Every hit you take echoes. The whole floor hears it."
    Effect: When you take melee damage, a sound pulse emanates from your position.
    All enemies within 6 tiles that are NOT currently in CHASING state switch to
    CHASING. Flavor: your mutated bone structure vibrates audibly on impact,
    like a tuning fork. You're a walking alarm bell when hit.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Melee hit taken → alert enemies within 6 tiles (non-CHASING → CHASING).
      L2: Alert radius extends to 10 tiles. Additionally, alerted enemies gain
          +10 speed for 5 turns (the resonance agitates them into a frenzy).
      L3: Alert radius: entire floor. Speed bonus +20 for 8 turns. Additionally,
          enemies that are alerted by the resonance and reach you within 3 turns
          of being alerted deal +2 damage on their first attack (resonance-guided
          strike — they home in on the vibrations). Cap.
    Note: This is distinct from Territorial Marking (passive detection) and
    Bioluminescence (constant position knowledge). Resonant Bones only triggers
    ON BEING HIT — if you never get hit, it never fires.
    Reversal: Remove resonant_bones_active flag.
    System hooks: player_stats.resonant_bones_active, combat.py incoming melee
    damage path (trigger alert pulse), ai.py (force CHASING state on alerted),
    effects.py (L2 speed buff on alerted enemies).

23. MAGNETIC BLOOD — "Metal sticks to you. Bullets find you interesting."
    Effect: Incoming gun/ranged attacks against you gain +15% accuracy bonus
    (your blood has a magnetic pull that guides projectiles toward you).
    Additionally, metallic items in your inventory weigh more on your psyche —
    for every 3 metallic items (weapons, ammo, rings) in your inventory, your
    speed is reduced by 5 (maximum -25 speed from this effect).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: +15% enemy ranged accuracy vs you; -5 speed per 3 metal items (max -25).
      L2: +25% ranged accuracy. Speed penalty: -5 per 2 metal items (max -35).
          Additionally, when an enemy fires a gun at ANY target within 3 tiles of
          you, there is a 20% chance the bullet is magnetically deflected toward
          you instead (you intercept shots not even aimed at you).
      L3: +35% ranged accuracy. Speed penalty: -5 per 1 metal item (max -50).
          Bullet deflection chance 35%. Additionally, equipping or un-equipping
          metallic items takes 2 turns instead of being instant (magnetic
          resistance — the items don't want to let go). Cap.
    Note: "Metallic items" are defined by a new tag on items: is_metallic: bool
    (weapons, ammo, rings are metallic; food, drugs, organic items are not).
    This creates inventory management pressure unique to this mutation.
    Reversal: Remove magnetic_blood_active flag; clear speed penalty.
    System hooks: player_stats.magnetic_blood_active, gun_system.py (enemy
    accuracy bonus), engine.py (speed calculation from inventory metal count),
    gun_system.py (L2 bullet deflection), inventory_mgr.py (L3 equip delay).

24. NEURAL LEAKAGE — "Your thoughts broadcast. Enemies learn your patterns."
    Effect: Every time you use the same ability twice in a row (consecutive uses
    of the same ability_id without using a different ability in between), the
    second use deals 25% less damage/effect intensity. A third consecutive use
    of the same ability deals 50% less. Resets when you use a different ability
    or make a melee attack. Flavor: your neural patterns become predictable
    and enemies adapt to the broadcast.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 2nd consecutive same-ability: -25% intensity; 3rd+: -50%.
      L2: 2nd: -35%; 3rd+: -65%. Additionally, enemies within 3 tiles of you
          can "read" your next ability and gain +2 dodge against it (they
          anticipate the attack from your mental broadcast).
      L3: 2nd: -50%; 3rd+: -75% (nearly useless on repetition). Dodge bonus +4.
          Additionally, if you use the same ability 4 times in a row, it goes
          on a forced 5-turn cooldown regardless of its normal cooldown
          (neural overload — your brain can't broadcast the same signal). Cap.
    Note: Tracked as engine.neural_leakage_last_ability: str (last ability_id used),
    engine.neural_leakage_streak: int (consecutive same-ability count).
    This mutation rewards ability variety and punishes spam. Players with multiple
    touch spells (Frost/Fire/Shock) can rotate and avoid the penalty entirely.
    Reversal: Remove neural_leakage_active flag; clear streak tracker.
    System hooks: player_stats.neural_leakage_active, abilities.py execute()
    (check streak, apply intensity reduction), ai.py (L2 dodge bonus).

25. UNSTABLE MASS — "Your body's center of gravity has its own agenda."
    Effect: When you take a hit that deals 10+ damage (after armor/DR), there is
    a 30% chance your body involuntarily shifts — you are pushed 1 tile in a
    random direction (not toward the attacker, just random). If the push moves
    you onto a hazard tile (fire, grease, slime), you take the hazard damage.
    If pushed into a wall, you take 2 impact damage instead of moving.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 10+ damage hit → 30% chance push 1 tile randomly; wall impact 2 dmg.
      L2: 8+ damage threshold; 40% push chance; push 1-2 tiles (random). Wall
          impact 4 dmg. Additionally, being pushed breaks any channeled effects
          (eating food mid-eat, abilities with charge-up — if any exist).
      L3: 6+ damage threshold; 50% push chance; push 1-3 tiles. Wall impact 6 dmg.
          Additionally, every time you are pushed, all enemies within 3 tiles of
          your NEW position are alerted to CHASING (the violent displacement draws
          attention). Cap.
    Note: Push direction is fully random (8 directions). Push path checks each tile
    for walls — if a wall is in the path, stop at the wall and take impact damage.
    If pushed through open tiles, land at the destination.
    Distinct from knockback (which is directional from attacker). This is random.
    Reversal: Remove unstable_mass_active flag.
    System hooks: player_stats.unstable_mass_active, combat.py incoming damage
    path (threshold + push roll), engine.py (push resolution — tile checks,
    hazard damage, wall impact), ai.py (L3 alert on push).

21. FLESH PUPPETEER — "You reach into them and pull the strings."
    Effect: Grants the Flesh Puppeteer ability (2 charges/floor, SINGLE_ENEMY_LOS
    target, range 5). Target a visible enemy: for 4 turns, that enemy fights for
    you. The puppeteered enemy attacks its former allies, using its normal AI but
    treating you as friendly and all other enemies as hostile. After 4 turns, the
    puppet is released and becomes hostile again — but at 50% current HP (the
    puppeteering damages their body from the inside).
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip weapon slot (your hands are busy with
    invisible psychic threads).
    Upgrade:
      L1: 2/floor, range 5, 4-turn puppet duration, 50% HP on release.
      L2: 3/floor, range 7, 6-turn puppet, 40% HP on release. Additionally,
          while a puppet is active, you can see through the puppet's FOV (you
          gain vision of everything the puppet can see, even around corners).
      L3: 4/floor, range 10, 8-turn puppet, 30% HP on release. Puppet FOV active.
          Additionally, if the puppet kills an enemy while under your control,
          you gain the kill XP (the puppet is an extension of your will). The
          puppet also gains +3 power while controlled (your will surges through
          their muscles). Cap.
    Note: Puppeteered enemies use do_ai_turn() with their target overridden
    to attack other monsters. They move and attack normally but with flipped
    friend/foe. Does not work on bosses (immune to puppet).
    Reversal: Revoke "flesh_puppeteer" ability; restore weapon slot.
    System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "flesh_puppeteer", ai.py (override target
    selection for puppeted entity), engine.py (puppet timer, release logic,
    HP reduction), combat.py entity_died (L3 XP attribution).

22. MIMIC FLESH — "Your body remembers every hit. Then it becomes the hit."
    Effect: When an enemy with a special_attack hits you (any special attack type:
    venom, bleed, ignite, shock, chill, etc.), your body absorbs the attack pattern.
    Your next 3 melee attacks apply that same status effect to enemies you hit
    (at 1 stack per hit). You can only store ONE absorbed pattern at a time.
    Absorbing a new pattern overwrites the old one. Flavor: your flesh literally
    mimics the biological weapons used against you.
    Equipment constraint: None.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Absorb enemy special_attack on being hit; next 3 melee attacks apply
          1 stack of that effect. One stored pattern at a time.
      L2: Next 5 melee attacks apply the absorbed effect. 2 stacks per hit
          instead of 1. Additionally, you also absorb the effect from enemies
          that MISS you in melee (your skin reads their intent from proximity).
      L3: Next 8 melee attacks. 3 stacks per hit. Miss absorption active.
          Additionally, you can store TWO patterns simultaneously — both apply
          on every melee hit (double status application). The second absorption
          doesn't overwrite the first; instead the older one is replaced when
          a third is absorbed. Cap.
    Note: Stored patterns tracked in engine.mimic_flesh_patterns: list of
    {"effect_id": str, "stacks": int, "hits_remaining": int} dicts (max 1 at
    L1-L2, max 2 at L3). On melee hit, decrement hits_remaining and apply effect.
    Synergy with Leeching Strike: absorbing debuffs means you can create the
    conditions for Leeching Strike's HP leech on your own hits.
    Reversal: Remove mimic_flesh_active flag; clear stored patterns.
    System hooks: player_stats.mimic_flesh_active, combat.py incoming melee
    (absorb special_attack), combat.py outgoing melee (apply stored patterns),
    effects.py (generic effect application from pattern).

23. SPLIT CONSCIOUSNESS — "Two minds. One body. They take turns."
    Effect: Once per floor, you can activate Split Consciousness (SELF target,
    ONCE per floor). For 10 turns, you get TWO actions per turn instead of one.
    Each turn, you perform your chosen action AND then a second automated action:
    the second action is always a melee attack on the nearest adjacent enemy
    (if any), or a free 1-tile move toward the nearest visible enemy (if no
    adjacent enemy). You don't control the second action — the "other mind" does.
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip hat slot (the second consciousness needs
    room — your skull is visibly distended).
    Upgrade:
      L1: 1/floor, 10 turns, second action: auto-melee adjacent OR auto-step
          toward nearest enemy. Hat slot blocked.
      L2: 1/floor, 15 turns. Second action now also fires any on-hit effects
          from your weapon (venom, bleed, etc. — previously it was a "bare"
          attack). Additionally, the auto-step toward enemies is 2 tiles instead
          of 1 (the second mind is more aggressive).
      L3: 2/floor (can activate twice per floor). 15 turns. Second action fires
          on-hit effects. Additionally, if you use an ability as your primary
          action, the second mind can still melee attack (they're independent).
          The second mind's attacks also benefit from crit chance. Cap.
    Note: The second action is computed in engine.py after the player's primary
    action resolves. It does NOT cost energy — it's a free bonus action.
    Reversal: Revoke "split_consciousness" ability; restore hat slot.
    System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "split_consciousness", engine.py (dual-action
    processing loop), combat.py (auto-melee targeting), ai.py (auto-step pathfinding).

24. WEB SPINNER — "Silk from your wrists. Not as cool as it sounds."
    Effect: Grants the Web Spinner ability (INFINITE charges, ADJACENT_TILE target,
    2-turn cooldown). Place a web tile on an adjacent empty floor tile. Web tiles
    are invisible to enemies until they step on one. When an enemy steps on a web:
    they are rooted for 2 turns (cannot move, can still attack if adjacent to a
    target). You can have up to 6 web tiles active at once. Webs persist until
    triggered or until you leave the floor.
    Equipment constraint: None.
    Upgrade:
      L1: Place web (ADJACENT_TILE), 2t cooldown, max 6 webs, root 2 turns.
      L2: Max 10 webs; root 3 turns; cooldown 1 turn. Additionally, enemies
          rooted by your web take +2 damage from all sources while rooted
          (they're entangled and vulnerable).
      L3: Max 15 webs; root 4 turns; cooldown 0 (free action). Rooted enemies
          take +4 damage from all sources. Additionally, when an enemy triggers
          a web, all enemies within 2 tiles are slowed by 25% for 3 turns
          (sympathetic web vibration — they sense danger and hesitate). Cap.
    Note: Web tiles stored in dungeon.web_tiles: set[(x,y)]. Rendered as a
    nearly-invisible char (period '.' in dim white — hard to distinguish from
    floor). Use existing 0xE003 web tile graphic for triggered/visible webs.
    This mutation creates a "trapper" playstyle: set up webs, lure enemies into
    them, punish the entangled. Synergy with Pheromone Gland (attract enemies
    into webs) and Chameleon Skin (stand still near webs, ambush rooted enemies).
    Reversal: Revoke "web_spinner" ability; clear all web tiles.
    System hooks: ABILITY_REGISTRY "web_spinner", dungeon.web_tiles (new set),
    engine.py enemy movement (web trigger check), effects.py RootedEffect
    (new: blocks movement, allows attacks), render.py (web tile display).

25. ADAPTIVE METABOLISM — "Your body solves problems. Violent, chemical problems."
    Effect: The first time each floor you are affected by a status effect
    (any debuff: venom, bleed, ignite, chill, shock, confuse, fear, stun, slow),
    you develop an "adaptation" to that specific effect for the rest of the floor.
    Adaptation means: the SECOND time that same effect is applied to you on the
    same floor, its duration is halved and its damage is reduced by 30%. The third
    time: duration quartered, damage -60%. Fourth+: immune for that floor.
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip feet slot (your feet have mutated into
    something that processes chemicals faster — they can't fit shoes).
    Upgrade:
      L1: First application: normal. Second: -50% duration, -30% damage. Third:
          -75% duration, -60% damage. Fourth+: immune. Feet slot blocked. Resets
          each floor.
      L2: Adaptation kicks in faster — second application is already at -60% dmg,
          third: immune. Additionally, when you become immune to an effect via
          adaptation, you heal 3 HP (your body rejects the effect with vigor).
      L3: Second application: -75% dmg, third: immune. Heal 5 HP on immunity.
          Additionally, effects you've become immune to on the current floor also
          cannot be applied to allies within 2 tiles of you (future NPC/summon
          hook — for now, this means puppeted enemies via Flesh Puppeteer). Cap.
    Note: Adaptation tracking: engine.adaptive_metabolism_counts: dict[str, int]
    where key = effect_id, value = times applied this floor. Reset on floor change.
    This mutation makes extended fights on a single floor increasingly favorable.
    Early in a floor you're vulnerable; by mid-floor you've adapted to everything
    the local enemies throw at you.
    Reversal: Remove adaptive_metabolism_active; clear adaptation counts; restore feet slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.adaptive_metabolism_active, effects.py apply()
    (check adaptation count before applying, modify duration/damage),
    engine.py floor_transition (reset counts).

21. ECHO CHAMBER SKULL — "Every sound you make reverberates. Inside your head."
    Effect: Using any ability, firing any gun, or eating any item produces an
    internal echo that deals 3 damage to you (psychic reverberation — bypasses
    armor). This is a flat cost on every non-melee, non-movement action. Melee
    attacks are silent enough to avoid the echo. This fundamentally taxes
    ability-heavy and gun-heavy builds.
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip hat slot (your skull is hollow and resonant).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 3 self-damage on ability/gun/item use; hat blocked.
      L2: 5 self-damage. Additionally, the echo creates a noise pulse — enemies
          within 4 tiles are alerted (switch to CHASING) on every ability/gun/item use.
      L3: 7 self-damage. Alert radius 8 tiles. Additionally, if you use 3 or more
          abilities within 10 turns, the accumulated echoes cause a "resonance
          cascade" — you are stunned for 2 turns and take 10 damage (the echoes
          compound into a feedback loop). The cascade counter resets after firing
          or after 10 turns with fewer than 3 ability uses. Cap.
    Note: The self-damage is applied AFTER the ability/gun/item resolves (you get
    the benefit, then pay the cost). This is distinct from Hemorrhaging Constitution
    (melee-only) and Second Skin of Thorns (melee hit self-damage).
    Reversal: Remove echo_chamber_skull_active; restore hat slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.echo_chamber_skull_active, abilities.py execute()
    (self-damage post-fire), gun_system.py fire (self-damage), item_effects.py
    use_item (self-damage), ai.py (L2 alert), engine.py (L3 cascade counter).

22. SYMPATHETIC PAIN — "When they hurt, you hurt. When you hurt, they don't care."
    Effect: Whenever you deal damage to an enemy (any source: melee, gun, ability,
    effect), you take 15% of the damage dealt back as self-damage (rounded down,
    minimum 1). This sympathetic link means every point of damage you output costs
    you a fraction of your own HP. The self-damage bypasses armor (it's internal,
    sympathetic nervous response).
    Equipment constraint: Cannot equip neck slot (the sympathetic nerve cluster
    grew along your cervical spine).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 15% of dealt damage returns as self-damage (min 1, bypasses armor); neck blocked.
      L2: 20% return. Additionally, DoT effects you've applied to enemies (venom,
          bleed, ignite from your attacks) also trigger the sympathetic response —
          each tick of YOUR DoTs on enemies deals 1 damage to you.
      L3: 25% return. DoT sympathetic ticks increase to 2 damage. Additionally,
          if you kill an enemy, the sympathetic backlash is doubled on the killing
          blow (25% × 2 = 50% of the killing blow's damage returns to you). Cap.
    Note: This is the anti-glass-cannon mutation. High-damage builds pay
    proportionally more. Low-damage utility builds (debuff-focused) are less
    affected. Creates real tension around burst damage.
    Synergy (anti): Feral Instincts (high burst melee) + Sympathetic Pain = painful.
    Synergy (pro): Web Spinner (root enemies, chip away) minimizes per-hit self-cost.
    Reversal: Remove sympathetic_pain_active; restore neck slot.
    System hooks: player_stats.sympathetic_pain_active, combat.py (all damage
    dealt paths — melee, gun, ability, effect tick), effects.py (L2 DoT tick
    self-damage check).

23. ENTROPIC AURA — "Things fall apart around you. Faster than they should."
    Effect: Your equipped weapon and sidearm degrade. Every 20 melee attacks with
    a weapon, that weapon permanently loses 1 power (tracked per weapon as
    weapon.degradation_count: int). Guns lose 1 accuracy per 10 shots fired.
    These penalties are permanent on the item and persist even if unequipped and
    re-equipped. The aura corrupts whatever you touch.
    Equipment constraint: None (but effectively punishes all equipment).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Melee weapon: -1 power per 20 attacks. Gun: -1 accuracy per 10 shots.
      L2: Weapon: -1 power per 15 attacks. Gun: -1 accuracy per 7 shots.
          Additionally, rings and neck items you have equipped lose 1 of their
          stat bonus per floor (e.g., a +3 STR ring becomes +2 after 1 floor,
          then +1, then +0 and becomes useless). Tracked per item.
      L3: Weapon: -1 power per 10 attacks. Gun: -1 accuracy per 5 shots.
          Ring/neck decay continues. Additionally, armor/DR items (if DR comes
          from equipment) lose 1 DR per floor. Items at 0 in all bonuses
          crumble to dust (removed from inventory). Cap.
    Note: Degradation is stored on the Entity itself as degradation_power_lost: int,
    degradation_accuracy_lost: int. Save/load must serialize these.
    This mutation makes every weapon temporary and creates a constant search for
    replacements. Crate-heavy and loot-heavy playstyles benefit. Synergy with
    Subdermal Toolkit (more loot from crates to replace degraded gear).
    Reversal: Remove entropic_aura_active flag. Degradation already applied to
    items is NOT reversed (damage is done).
    System hooks: player_stats.entropic_aura_active, combat.py melee attack
    counter (check degradation threshold), gun_system.py fire counter,
    engine.py floor_transition (L2/L3 accessory/armor decay).

24. MIDAS CURSE — "Everything you touch turns to gold. Including things you need."
    Effect: When you pick up any non-gold item, there is a 20% chance the item
    is transmuted into a gold pile worth 50% of the item's "value" (a new field
    on items: sell_value: int, default 10). The item is destroyed and replaced
    with gold. This can hit ANY item: food, ammo, weapons, rings — anything
    you pick up. Equipped items are safe (only triggers on pickup).
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: 20% transmute chance on pickup; item → gold at 50% value.
      L2: 30% transmute chance; gold at 40% value (less gold too). Additionally,
          items picked up from crates have a 50% transmute chance instead of 30%
          (the mutation is drawn to loot-source items).
      L3: 40% transmute chance (50% from crates). Gold at 30% value. Additionally,
          once per floor, a random item already in your inventory is transmuted
          to gold spontaneously (the curse activates on stored items — checked at
          the start of each floor, excludes equipped items). Cap.
    Note: This creates constant tension around pickup priority — do you grab that
    food item and risk losing it? The gold is real and can be spent at vending
    machines, so it's not a total loss. But losing a critical ammo stack or a
    ring is devastating.
    Reversal: Remove midas_curse_active flag. Transmuted items are gone forever.
    System hooks: player_stats.midas_curse_active, inventory_mgr.py pickup
    (transmute roll), engine.py floor_start (L3 random inventory transmute).

25. SPLIT IDENTITY — "There are two of you in there. One of them hates the other."
    Effect: Every 30 turns, your "other self" takes over for 1 action. During that
    action, you perform a random harmful action (chosen from: attack a random
    adjacent entity including walls for 0 damage but triggering thorns/on-hit;
    throw a random inventory item in a random direction; use a random ability on
    a random target; move in a random direction). You cannot prevent this action.
    The other self acts with YOUR stats, YOUR equipment, YOUR abilities.
    Equipment constraint: None.
    Upgrade (gets worse):
      L1: Every 30 turns: 1 random harmful action.
      L2: Every 25 turns: 1 random action. Additionally, the other self's actions
          deal +50% damage if they happen to hit an enemy (the other self is
          aggressive — this is NOT a benefit, because you don't control the target
          and it might hit a faction-friendly NPC or waste an ability charge).
      L3: Every 20 turns: 1 random action. +75% damage. Additionally, the other
          self occasionally speaks — 25% chance per takeover to broadcast a
          hostile message to all enemies on the floor ("COME AND GET ME"), alerting
          every enemy to CHASING state. Cap.
    Note: The random action selection: 25% melee random adjacent, 25% throw
    random item, 25% use random ability (consuming a charge), 25% random move.
    The "throw item" picks any item from inventory and drops it 3 tiles in a
    random direction (item is retrievable from the floor but you have to go get it).
    This is distinct from Dissociative Episodes (random MOVEMENT for multiple turns).
    Split Identity is a single disruptive ACTION every N turns.
    Reversal: Remove split_identity_active flag.
    System hooks: player_stats.split_identity_active, engine.py per-turn
    (identity counter, random action dispatch), combat.py (random melee target),
    abilities.py (random ability fire), inventory_mgr.py (random item throw).


================================================================
DESIGN NOTES — SYNERGIES AND ANTI-SYNERGIES
================================================================

NEW SYNERGY HIGHLIGHTS:

  Slime Trail + Pheromone Gland:
    Pull enemies toward you → they walk through your slime → slip and get toxed.
    Classic lure-and-trap combo.

  Web Spinner + Chameleon Skin:
    Place webs, stand still to activate camo, wait for enemies to walk into webs,
    then ambush-strike the rooted target for +100% damage at L3. Patient assassin build.

  Flesh Puppeteer + Split Consciousness:
    Puppet an enemy while your second mind auto-attacks adjacent targets.
    Multi-front combat with puppeted ally + dual-action player.

  Mimic Flesh + Leeching Strike:
    Absorb enemy special attacks, then apply those debuffs to enemies you hit,
    which creates the debuff conditions for Leeching Strike's HP leech.

  Adaptive Metabolism + Reactive Skin:
    Reactive Skin makes hazards worse, but Adaptive Metabolism means repeated
    exposure to the same debuff builds immunity. Extended fights get safer.

  Echolocation Pulse + Eyeless:
    Already have sonar from Eyeless; pulse adds temporary full-floor awareness
    plus the disorient stun. Information overload in the best way.

NEW ANTI-SYNERGY WARNINGS:

  Chameleon Skin + Lopsided Growth:
    Lopsided Growth forces movement after melee attacks (stumble), which breaks
    camouflage. Ambush playstyle is constantly disrupted.

  Sympathetic Pain + Feral Instincts:
    Feral Rush grants massive melee damage, but every point dealt costs you 15-25%
    back. Going berserk might kill you faster than the enemies.

  Entropic Aura + any equipment-dependent build:
    All your gear degrades. You're constantly replacing weapons. Builds that rely
    on a single powerful weapon (like a high-power gun) are devastated.

  Echo Chamber Skull + ability-spam builds:
    Nuclear Research (rad-spell tree) or any multi-ability build takes 3-7
    self-damage per ability use. Combined with Degenerating Nervous System's
    cooldown penalty, ability builds face both higher cooldowns AND self-damage.

  Midas Curse + Iron Gut:
    Iron Gut benefits from consuming drugs. Midas Curse might transmute those
    drugs into gold before you can use them. Direct resource conflict.

BALANCE NOTES
================================================================

Weak Good power level check:
  - Slime Trail: minor area control, doesn't deal damage directly. Weak-appropriate.
  - Echolocation Pulse: information + light stun. Comparable to Neural Static in
    impact. Weak-appropriate.
  - Sticky Fingers: loot quality — no combat impact. Weak-appropriate.
  - Chameleon Skin: powerful ambush but requires standing still (vulnerability).
    The +50-100% damage is strong but conditional. At edge of weak/strong —
    kept weak because it requires specific playstyle commitment to activate.
  - Probability Flux: +5-15% to rolls is subtle. The L3 death prevention is
    unreliable (25%). Weak-appropriate.

Weak Bad pain level check:
  - Volatile Anatomy: -2 to a random stat per floor is moderate. Comparable to
    Vestigial Limb (-1 power) but random and temporary. Weak-appropriate.
  - Resonant Bones: conditional on being hit. If you avoid damage, it never fires.
    Weak-appropriate (only punishes mistakes).
  - Magnetic Blood: gun accuracy boost against you is niche (gun enemies aren't
    on every floor). Speed penalty from inventory is constant but manageable.
    Weak-appropriate.
  - Neural Leakage: only punishes ability spam. Players with one ability barely
    notice. Weak-appropriate.
  - Unstable Mass: conditional on 10+ damage hits. Early game, rare. Late game,
    more common. Weak-appropriate (scales naturally with game difficulty).

Strong Good power level check:
  - Flesh Puppeteer: build-defining (blocks weapon slot). Very powerful control.
    Comparable to Telekinetic Lash in impact. Strong-appropriate.
  - Mimic Flesh: versatile combat mutation. Comparable to Bone Spurs in complexity.
    Strong-appropriate.
  - Split Consciousness: extra action per turn is EXTREMELY powerful. 10-15 turns
    of dual-action per floor. Balanced by hat slot cost and uncontrollable second
    action. Strong-appropriate (possibly best strong good — monitor).
  - Web Spinner: battlefield control. 15 traps per floor at L3 is a lot. Balanced
    by requiring setup time. Strong-appropriate.
  - Adaptive Metabolism: defensive. Gets better over long fights. Blocks feet slot.
    Comparable to Turtle Neck. Strong-appropriate.

Strong Bad pain level check:
  - Echo Chamber Skull: 3-7 self-damage per ability/gun/item is harsh for
    ability-heavy builds. Pure melee builds barely care. Strong-appropriate
    (build-warping).
  - Sympathetic Pain: 15-25% damage return is severe. At 20 damage dealt, that's
    3-5 HP back per attack. Over a floor of 50 attacks: 150-250 self-damage.
    Strong-appropriate (run-altering).
  - Entropic Aura: slow equipment degradation. Over multiple floors, weapons become
    useless. Strong-appropriate (creates resource pressure).
  - Midas Curse: 20-40% item loss on pickup is devastating for item-dependent
    builds. Strong-appropriate.
  - Split Identity: 1 random harmful action per 20-30 turns. Disruptive but not
    lethal directly. Strong-appropriate (comparable to Dissociative Episodes).
