CRACK-HEAD SKILL TREE — THREE VARIANT TAKES
Meth Meter as Builder-Spender Resource
================================================================================

Document Status: Design Draft
Zone Context:    Zone 2 — The Meth Lab
Related Docs:    toxicity_mechanic.txt, meth_lab_skill_concepts.txt, skill_perks_L4_L5.txt
Systems Touched: engine.py (meter tracking + decay), skills.py, abilities.py,
                 effects.py, items.py (Blue Meth item), loot.py

================================================================================
SHARED SYSTEM FOUNDATION
================================================================================

METH METER
----------
engine.meth_meter: int = 0
Soft cap: 200. No hard cap — you can theoretically push higher, but decay
accelerates past 200 (see per-variant notes on exact decay behavior).

Displayed on the HUD as a simple integer or bar next to Toxicity.

BLUE METH (item)
----------------
item_id: "blue_meth"
entity_type: "item"  (NOT entity_type "cash" — no separate pile system needed)
char: '%' (distinct from food '!')
color: (0, 180, 255)  — bright cyan/blue
category: "consumable"
use_verb: "Smoke"
use_effect: {"meth_meter": 80}   — calls engine._add_meth_meter(80)

Zone placement: scattered directly on Meth Lab floor tiles via dungeon.py
spawn, similar to how cash piles appear. Guaranteed 2-4 per floor, weighted
toward open corridors (high-risk exposure areas).

Blue Meth does NOT add Toxicity by default. It interacts with Toxicity only
if the player has specific Crack-Head perks (see variants below). This keeps
the two resources cleanly orthogonal at baseline.

METER DECAY
-----------
Base decay runs in engine._run_energy_loop (same cadence as effect ticks).
Exact rate is variant-specific but the flavor contract is: meth is a sprint,
not a marathon. You feel it burning off between fights. If you sit still,
you lose it. The design intent is that Blue Meth is the generator and the
player's actions are the spender.

TOLERANCE INTERACTION (baseline, modified per variant)
------------------------------------------------------
Tolerance already governs drug effect strength in the Toxicity system
(rate = max(0.3, 1.5 - tolerance * 0.1)). The Meth Meter uses the inverse
framing:

Low Tolerance (< 5):  Blue Meth gives +80 to meter (full spike). Meter decays
                      fast — the high is intense but burns quick.
                      High-variance playstyle. Feels like a tweaker.

High Tolerance (> 8): Blue Meth gives diminished meter (+40-50 instead of 80).
                      Decay is slower, meter holds longer.
                      Lower ceiling, more stable floor.

Tolerance modifying formula (applied at consume time):
  spike = int(80 * max(0.5, 1.5 - (tolerance * 0.1)))
  At TOL 3:  spike = int(80 * 1.2) = 96
  At TOL 5:  spike = int(80 * 1.0) = 80
  At TOL 10: spike = int(80 * 0.5) = 40

This matches the Toxicity formula's framing and reuses the same stat without
new complexity. It also creates a natural tension with the Meth-Head existing
tree (which gives -2 Book Smarts but no Tolerance penalty) vs builds that
stack Tolerance for the Chemical Warfare tree.


================================================================================
VARIANT 1 — "SPEED FREAK"
Identity: Action economy vampire. Spend meter to take extra turns.
================================================================================

PITCH
-----
You move three times while everyone else moves once. Blue Meth doesn't make
you stronger — it makes you faster and more frantic. Every spike of the meter
is borrowed time. You rob turns from the future to spend them right now, and
when the meter's empty you're just standing there panting.

Distinct from Chemical Warfare: Chemical Warfare scales damage numbers.
Speed Freak scales action count. You don't hit harder — you hit more times
before anyone can respond.

TOLERANCE BEHAVIOR (Variant 1)
-------------------------------
Low Tolerance: Bigger spikes, faster decay. You're burning through it.
  - Decay: 4 meter/turn while > 0 (aggressive drain)
  - Spike multiplier as per baseline formula (TOL 3 = +96 per Blue Meth)

High Tolerance: Smaller spikes, slower decay.
  - Decay: 2 meter/turn while > 0
  - Lower spike (TOL 10 = +40 per Blue Meth)
  - Net effective duration is actually comparable — ~20 turns either way —
    but the high-TOL version is smoother and easier to manage.
  - Low-TOL variant gets bigger burst windows for the same total meter.

DECAY FEEL
----------
4/turn at low TOL feels urgent — a full spike (96 meter) lasts ~24 turns.
That's roughly 3-5 combat encounters before you're dry. Map pressure is real.
You need to find more Blue Meth or fight smarter.

PERKS
-----

L1 — "Another One" (Activated)
  Ability: another_one
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: INFINITE (no CD — you can fire this every turn if you have meter)
  Cost: 50 meth_meter
  Effect: Grants +100 energy this turn only (effectively takes an extra half-
          action immediately). Does NOT stack with itself in the same tick.
  Thematic: "You hit it again. The world slowed down. You slipped through a
            crack in time."
  Implementation note: engine._add_energy_bonus(player, 100) called immediately
  inside execute(); acts within the current tick resolution, letting the player
  take another move or attack before enemies respond.

L2 — "Tunnel Vision" (Passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  While meth_meter >= 100, all movement costs 0 energy (you teleport-step).
  You cover ground at inhuman speed when you're peaking.
  When meter drops below 100, normal movement energy costs resume instantly.
  Implementation note: modify_energy_gain hook on a persistent passive buff
  tracked by a "tunnel_vision_active" flag in engine; flag set/cleared as meter
  crosses 100 threshold.
  Balance note: This does NOT affect attack energy — you can't attack-spam,
  only move freely. Keeps the kit about positioning, not pure DPS.

L3 — "Cardiac Event" (Activated)
  Ability: cardiac_event
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: PER_FLOOR 1
  Cost: ALL remaining meth_meter (minimum 80 to activate)
  Effect: You dump everything. For a number of turns equal to meter_spent // 25,
          you gain +60 energy/tick (on top of base 100 → 160 total; roughly
          60% faster than baseline). After the duration, apply "crash_hard"
          debuff: -20 energy/tick for 5 turns.
  Example: 160 meter spent → 6 turns at 160 speed, then 5-turn slowdown.
  Thematic: "You blew the whole stash at once. The next few seconds were a
            blur. Then your heart reminded you it was still there."
  New effect needed: cardiac_rush — +60 energy/tick, expires after N turns
  crash_hard: -20 energy/tick, 5t (alias of or replacement for existing "crash")

SYNERGIES
---------
- Meth-Head L1 (Tweakin) also gives +30 energy/tick on kills. Stacks with
  Cardiac Event's buff for devastating momentum windows.
- Low Tolerance builds maximize spike size for bigger Cardiac Event durations.
- Anti-synergy with Munching (CON stacking) — you're moving too fast to eat.
- Gouge (Stabbing L1) combos hard: stun an enemy, take two actions while they
  stand there.

================================================================================
VARIANT 2 — "TWEAKER TANK"
Identity: Berserker. The meter is not fuel — it IS you. Taking hits while high
          converts pain into meter. Spend it all to become unkillable briefly.
================================================================================

PITCH
-----
You don't avoid hits. You absorb them. Blue Meth stacks the meter, but so does
getting punched. The more the enemies beat on you, the higher you get, until
you activate the big button and everything explodes outward. Where Speed Freak
is about graceful action economy, Tweaker Tank is about face-tanking until the
moment is right to go nuclear.

Distinct from Chemical Warfare: Chemical Warfare rewards building toxicity to
massive numbers for multiplicative crits. Tweaker Tank uses the meter as an
HP buffer, then converts it to a panic nuke. Toxicity is a creeping corrosion;
meter is a volatile pressure gauge.

TOLERANCE BEHAVIOR (Variant 2)
-------------------------------
Tolerance changes HOW MUCH meter you gain from taking hits.

Low Tolerance: Each hit of incoming damage grants meter = damage_taken * 0.5
  The pain hits harder emotionally. You feel everything.

High Tolerance: Each hit grants meter = damage_taken * 0.2
  You're numbed out. Takes more punishment to build a head of steam.

Low-TOL build is the natural fit here — you want to feel the hits.
High-TOL players are probably in Chemical Warfare anyway.

Blue Meth spike formula still applies; TOL also affects spike size.

DECAY FEEL
----------
Decay: 2/turn base (slower than Speed Freak — meter persists across rooms).
Post-combat, the meter drains slowly. You stay high for a while after a fight.
The tension is managing a meter that fills up in danger (good!) but that you
cannot easily spend without hitting the L3 button. You're always a little
twitchy, always ready.

PERKS
-----

L1 — "Tweaker Reflex" (Passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  Whenever you take 6+ damage in a single hit, gain meth_meter = damage_taken // 2.
  No cap per hit. Takes full damage first (this is NOT damage reduction), then
  meter fills.
  Thematic: "It hurt. Somehow that made things clearer."
  Implementation note: Hook in modify_incoming_damage or on_player_damaged
  event in engine.py. Meter gain triggers after damage is applied and HP is
  deducted.
  Balance note: At 6 damage minimum, most early Meth Lab enemies (damage 5-8)
  qualify. This creates steady passive income during normal play.

L2 — "Iron Skin" (Passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  While meth_meter >= 80, incoming physical damage is reduced by meter // 40.
  At 80 meter:  -2 defense equivalent
  At 160 meter: -4 defense equivalent
  At 200 meter: -5 defense equivalent
  Thematic: "You couldn't feel your skin anymore. Couldn't feel much of
            anything. That was fine."
  Implementation note: modify_incoming_damage hook; read engine.meth_meter at
  call time. Stacks additively with armor.
  Balance: this is a soft incentive to HOLD meter rather than spend it — which
  creates tension with the L3 ability.

L2 — "Iron Skin" TOLERANCE NOTE
  High Tolerance stacks BETTER with Iron Skin because the meter decays slower
  and Iron Skin's benefit requires holding the meter. So high-TOL is actually
  viable here as a defensive variant. Low-TOL hits harder and gains faster,
  but the meter drains before Iron Skin pays out. Different risk profiles.

L3 — "Superconductor" (Activated)
  Ability: superconductor
  TargetType: AOE_CIRCLE, radius = 3 (centered on player)
  ChargeType: PER_FLOOR 1
  Cost: ALL remaining meth_meter (minimum 100 to activate)
  Effect: Explosive discharge. All enemies within r=3 take damage equal to
          meter_spent // 8, ignoring defense. Apply "shook" debuff to all
          survivors (existing effect: -30 energy/tick + -2 damage, 15 turns).
          Player takes no self-damage.
  Example: 180 meter → 22 damage to everyone in radius, no defense.
  After firing: meter resets to 0, apply "drained_out" debuff (3t, -10 energy/tick).
  Thematic: "You felt it all at once — every hit they'd landed on you, every
            jolt, every moment of pain — discharge outward like a shockwave."
  New effect needed: drained_out — -10 energy/tick, 3t (lighter version of crash)

SYNERGIES
---------
- Plays extremely well with Munching (CON stacking) — more HP to absorb hits,
  more hits to fill meter.
- Iron Skin creates a genuine spend-vs-hold dilemma with Superconductor.
  Players who hold until they find a big group get a better nuke; players who
  blow it on a single enemy waste the radius.
- Anti-synergy with Speed Freak (you can't build meter from hits if you're
  never getting hit).
- Pairs with Paranoia (Meth-Head L3) — you see enemies coming, you let them
  get close, you Superconductor the cluster.
- Hits Meth Lab cooks (high HP, clustered spawn) especially hard.

================================================================================
VARIANT 3 — "MANIFEST"
Identity: Hallucination tactician. The meter warps your perception. Spend it
          to see things that aren't there — or make things that aren't there
          real.
================================================================================

PITCH
-----
You're not fast. You're not a tank. You're paranoid, brilliant, and barely
coherent. The Meth Meter in Manifest isn't fuel for physical feats — it's fuel
for visions. High meter means your hallucinations become combat assets.
Spend it to plant a decoy that enemies will chase, to "predict" an enemy's
next move and dodge it automatically, or to tear open a corridor by
convincing yourself there's a door there.

This tree is the weird one. It's for players who want map control, deception,
and information, not raw numbers. It requires understanding the AI and working
with it, not just through it.

Distinct from Chemical Warfare: Chemical Warfare is a math tree — damage
multipliers, resource costs, exact thresholds. Manifest is a behavior tree.
Its power is in manipulating enemy AIState transitions and exploiting the FOV
system. No toxicity interaction needed or desired.

TOLERANCE BEHAVIOR (Variant 3)
-------------------------------
Tolerance determines WHAT YOU SEE when the meter is high.

Low Tolerance: Your hallucinations are vivid and convincing — to you and to
  enemies. Decoys are MORE convincing (enemies will path to them and stay
  longer). Vision effects last longer.

High Tolerance: You've seen it all before. Your hallucinations are duller.
  Decoys expire faster, vision effects shorter. But you resist the downside
  of the tree better (see Paranoid Clarity below).

Mechanically:
  Low TOL (<= 5): Decoy duration = 12 turns. Vision radius granted = 5.
  High TOL (>= 9): Decoy duration = 6 turns. Vision radius granted = 3.
  Scale linearly between.

DECAY FEEL
----------
Decay: 3/turn base. Medium urgency.
Additionally: using any ability in this tree accelerates decay by a fixed burst
(ability-specific — spending meter on abilities drains an extra amount on top
of the spend cost). This gives the tree a "crash after the vision" feel.
You can binge abilities but you'll be dry fast.

PERKS
-----

L1 — "Ghost In The Room" (Activated)
  Ability: ghost_in_the_room
  TargetType: SELF (places decoy at player's current tile, player steps away)
  ChargeType: INFINITE (cooldown 10 turns)
  Cost: 60 meth_meter
  Effect: Spawn a "Decoy" entity on the player's current tile. The Decoy has
          char '@', player's color, entity_type="decoy", alive=True, blocks_movement=False.
          All enemies currently chasing the player (AIState=CHASING) switch their
          target to the Decoy's tile (use existing alarm_chaser redirect logic).
          Enemies that reach the Decoy attack it — it dies in 1 hit and gives
          the attacking enemy the "confused" debuff (3 turns; existing effect,
          40% wander chance).
          Decoy expires after TOL-scaled turns even if not hit.
          Player re-appears one tile in the direction they move next.
  Thematic: "You left a shadow of yourself behind. They went for it. They always
            go for it."
  Implementation: Create a temporary Entity with is_decoy=True. In ai.py
  pathfinding step, enemies whose target_pos matches player's last tile will
  continue to Decoy pos until Decoy dies or expires. On reach: attack Decoy
  (auto-hit, 1 damage kills it), apply confused to attacker.
  New entity flag needed: is_decoy: bool = False
  Extra decay on ability use: -20 meter after spend

L2 — "Paranoid Clarity" (Passive)
  perk_type: "passive"
  Two effects, toggling on meter thresholds:

  HIGH: While meth_meter >= 80, gain a temporary +3 bonus to sight_radius
        (you see further — your paranoia extends your perception before
        enemies can close on you). Reveal all enemies currently in sight_radius.

  LOW: While meth_meter < 30, you LOSE 3 from sight_radius. The crash
       narrows your world. Tunnel vision in the bad way.

  High-TOL players: The low-meter penalty is shorter (meter decays slower).
  Low-TOL players: The low-meter penalty is infrequent but hits hard if they
                   let the meter drain to empty.
  Thematic: "The meth made everything sharp. You could feel the walls. Then
            it wore off, and you couldn't see past your own hands."
  Implementation: modify_sight_radius hook (not currently in effects.py —
  would need adding). OR simpler: track sight_radius override in engine based
  on meth_meter thresholds, recalculate after each meter change. Recompute FOV.

L3 — "The Vision" (Activated)
  Ability: the_vision
  TargetType: SELF
  ChargeType: PER_FLOOR 1
  Cost: ALL remaining meth_meter (minimum 80 to activate)
  Effect: You see everything. For 8 turns, you have full map visibility (entire
          dungeon is visible as if explored AND currently lit — all enemies,
          items, and tiles revealed). Additionally, for the duration, your
          next 3 attacks each have 100% crit chance (you see exactly where to
          hit before they move). After the 8 turns, apply "come_down" debuff:
          lose 4 sight_radius for 10 turns and apply confused (40% wander) for
          3 turns. The harder you peak, the harder you crash.
  Thematic: "You saw the whole building. Every room. Every man. You knew
            exactly where they were, what they were going to do. It lasted
            eight seconds. When it stopped, you didn't know where you were."
  New effect needed:
    vision_high — full map reveal + guaranteed_crit_charges = 3, duration 8t;
                  on expire triggers come_down
    come_down   — -4 sight_radius, 10t; confused (3t) applied on expire hook
  Implementation: full_map_reveal flag on engine, set True for 8 turns.
  render.py: if engine.vision_high_active, mark all tiles as visible regardless
  of FOV. guaranteed_crit_charges: int on engine, each player melee hit
  consumes 1 charge and forces is_crit=True in damage calculation.

SYNERGIES
---------
- Paranoia (Meth-Head L3) stacks devastatingly with The Vision — you already
  had expanded vision, now you see everything.
- Ghost In The Room combos with natural chokepoints. Leave a decoy at a
  doorway, step through, enemies pile into a corridor, they all get confused.
- Anti-synergy with Tweaker Tank — you HATE taking hits (each hit is a miss
  you didn't see coming if your meter was low). Manifest wants to stay at
  arm's length.
- Snitchin L1 (Rat) + Ghost In The Room: stack both confusion sources. Two
  enemies fighting each other while you hit them from the dark.
- The 100% crit charges from The Vision interact with any crit-scaling. At
  the time of writing, player crits do 2.0x damage. High Street Smarts also
  means normal fights have natural crit pressure — The Vision locks in 3
  guaranteed power hits regardless.

================================================================================
CROSS-VARIANT DESIGN NOTES
================================================================================

WHY THREE VARIANTS?
-------------------
Each take answers a different question the player might have:

  Speed Freak: "What if I just had more turns?"
  Tweaker Tank: "What if getting hit made me dangerous?"
  Manifest:     "What if the meth made me smarter instead of faster?"

They all use the same Blue Meth resource and the same Tolerance stat, but the
TOL interaction pulls in completely different directions:
  - Speed Freak: low TOL = bigger spike windows (high-variance sprint)
  - Tweaker Tank: low TOL = more meter per hit (built for punishment)
  - Manifest:     low TOL = better hallucinations (quality, not quantity)

WHAT NOT TO STACK
-----------------
Crack-Head and Chemical Warfare occupy different zones of the same stat:
  Crack-Head uses the Meth Meter (instant, volatile, floor-scoped).
  Chemical Warfare uses Toxicity (accumulating, permanent, zone-spanning).

A player theoretically could pursue both, but Blue Meth does NOT raise Toxicity
by default. If a future design wanted to bridge the trees, a single perk could
make Blue Meth consumption also add +30 Toxicity as a double-edged unlock.
That is intentionally left out of these three base variants.

IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITY
-----------------------
1. Meth Meter engine.py field + HUD display (shared by all three)
2. Blue Meth item (items.py / loot.py)
3. engine._add_meth_meter() and decay tick in _run_energy_loop
4. Variant-specific effects and abilities
5. Tolerance formula applied at consume time

Suggested starting point: Speed Freak L1 (Another One) — simplest ability
(energy bonus), tests the full pipeline without new effects or entity flags.

NEW ENTITY FLAG SUMMARY

    Used by ai.py to prevent enemies from chasing past a dead decoy.
