================================================================================
NIGRL — TRADITIONAL ROGUELIKE-INSPIRED LEGENDARY EQUIPMENT
================================================================================
Items 41-50 of the legendary equipment series.
Design theme: each item has a SPECIFIC traditional roguelike artifact or mechanic
as its ancestor. The roguelike source, original mechanic, and NIGRL translation
are documented per item.

Design conventions (same as items 1-40):
- All items are unique (one per run), not generated from procedural tables.
- All stat names use canonical PlayerStats keys:
    constitution, strength, book_smarts, street_smarts, tolerance, swagger
- "armor" = point-absorb pool. "defense" = flat damage reduction from swagger formula.
- New effects follow @register / lifecycle hook architecture (effects.py).
- New abilities follow ABILITY_REGISTRY pattern (abilities.py).
- PER_FLOOR X = ChargeType.PER_FLOOR, max_charges=X.
- Negative stat_bonus values are valid (see Dead Weight, Rat King Shiv precedents).

ITEM 41 — WEAPON
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM ID:         "cold_snap"
NAME:            Cold Snap
SLOT:            weapon (equip_slot: "weapon")
CHAR:            /
COLOR:           (120, 200, 255)  -- pale cold blue, like winter light on metal
SUBCATEGORY:     weapon
WEAPON TYPE:     stabbing
VALUE:           0
ZONES:           crack_den floor 2-4. Appears in a "Cold Storage" room event — a
                 room with an ASCII freezer unit (graphical tile or 'U' character
                 drawn on the wall) and frost decorations. The knife is embedded
                 in a block of ice (flavor text entity that the player bumps to
                 claim the weapon). Room spawns at 10% on floors 2-4.
                 Alternate obtain: dropped by any "Cryo" enemy variant (if added)
                 OR found if the player currently has 5+ stacks of Chill effect
                 active when a new floor generates.
TAGS:            ["legendary", "unique"]

TRADITIONAL ROGUELIKE INSPIRATION:
  NetHack — Frost Brand (artifact long sword)
  In NetHack, Frost Brand is a +3 artifact sword aligned to none-neutral that
  deals extra cold damage to non-cold-resistant enemies, melts ice, freezes pools
  of water on the floor, and grants cold resistance while wielded. Its key
  mechanical identity is the COLD RESISTANCE INTRINSIC — the weapon teaches the
  wielder to resist cold simply by using it. Cold damage bypasses normal
  damage reduction. The weapon's "melting" behavior (interacting with ice pools
  and fire) represents ENVIRONMENTAL PHYSICS in the roguelike world.

  The secondary inspiration is DCSS Frostbite: an unrandart staff that creates
  freezing clouds on the tiles of enemies it kills, leaving hazardous terrain.

TRANSLATION TO NIGRL:
  Cold Snap deals bonus chill-branded damage (adds Chill stacks on every hit,
  not just on certain procs). The wielder passively builds cold resistance over
  the course of the run. On killing blow, the dead enemy's tile becomes a "Frost
  Patch" hazard that slows enemies who walk over it. This creates environmental
  battlefield control from kills — the fantasy of turning the dungeon cold as you
  fight through it.

STAT PROPERTIES:
  base_damage:   8
  str_req:       5
  reach:         1
  str_scaling:   {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}   -- +1 per 2 STR above 5
  defense_bonus: 0
  armor_bonus:   0

SPECIAL PROPERTY — "Frost Brand" (passive):
  New field: "cold_snap_weapon": True
  New engine state:
    engine.cold_snap_chill_immunity_stacks: int = 0  -- tracks kills for resist buildup

  COMPONENT A — Chill Brand (passive, always-on while equipped):
    Every successful melee hit applies 1 stack of the "chill" effect to the target.
    Duration: 8 turns (doubled from the normal 10 via Time Served if equipped, or
    standard application otherwise).
    This fires regardless of other on-hit effects and does NOT cost additional
    damage; the cold is inherent to the blade.
    Chill effect (existing): each stack reduces energy gain by 15% multiplicatively.
    At 3 chill stacks: enemy acts at ~61% of normal speed.
    The chill application also grants Cryomancy XP (10 per stack, as per the
    existing ChillEffect.apply hook — the weapon simply causes the apply hook to
    fire more often).
    Message on first chill of combat: [pale blue] "The blade bites cold."

  COMPONENT B — Cold Resistance Buildup (passive, cumulative per-run):
    Each time the player kills an enemy with Cold Snap as the equipped weapon:
      engine.cold_snap_chill_immunity_stacks += 1
    At each threshold (tracked via engine.cold_snap_chill_immunity_stacks):
      5 kills:  player gains "cold_adapted" passive — incoming Chill effects applied
                to the player have their duration halved (rounded down).
                Message: "The cold no longer bites like it used to."
      10 kills: player also gains -50% Chill stack application (enemies have only 50%
                chance to apply Chill to the player when they would normally do so).
                Message: "You are becoming the cold."
      20 kills: full Chill immunity — Chill effects cannot be applied to the player
                at all. Message: [bright blue] "You ARE the cold."
    These thresholds persist for the run (do NOT reset on new floor).
    Implement: engine.cold_snap_chill_immunity_stacks tracked in GameEngine.__init__.
    In the Chill effect apply/on_reapply hooks, add a check for the immunity level
    if the target is the player.

  COMPONENT C — Frost Patch On Kill (passive, environmental):
    When the player kills an enemy with Cold Snap equipped:
      Spawn a "frost_patch" hazard entity on the dead enemy's tile.
      frost_patch properties:
        entity_type: "hazard", hazard_type: "frost_patch"
        char: '.' (or use existing floor tile but tinted; simpler: a '~' char)
        color: (150, 200, 255) -- icy blue
        blocks_movement: False
        duration_turns: 15  (tracked internally, destroyed after 15 turns)
        effect: any entity (player or monster) that ENDS its turn on a frost_patch
                tile receives 1 stack of Chill (duration 5 turns). This fires in
                the _run_energy_loop hazard-check step, same as fire tile ignite.
                The player is immune once cold_snap_chill_immunity_stacks >= 20.
    Message on spawn: "The corpse freezes the ground solid."
    Note: frost patches do NOT stack on the same tile. If a frost patch already
    exists at a location, the new kill's patch is skipped.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - Chill on hit: in handle_attack() after hit confirmed, if weapon is
    "cold_snap_weapon": call apply_effect(target, engine, "chill", duration=8, stacks=1).
    This is UNCONDITIONAL (every hit, not a chance-based proc).
  - Frost patch spawn: in the "entity_died" event handler in engine.py, if
    equipped weapon has "cold_snap_weapon" and killer is player: create a new
    entity with hazard_type="frost_patch" at entity.x, entity.y. Add to dungeon.
  - Frost patch tick: in _run_energy_loop hazard-check step (same location as fire
    ignite check), iterate frost patch entities, check if any entity stands on that
    tile, apply Chill duration 5 stacks 1. Check player immunity level first.
  - Frost patch expiry: add a duration_turns counter to the frost_patch entity
    (entity.hazard_duration = 15). Decrement each full energy loop cycle. Remove
    when reaches 0 (dungeon.entities.remove(entity)).
  - cold_adapted / Chill immunity: in ChillEffect.apply() and ChillEffect.on_reapply():
    if entity == engine.player: check immunity level. At 5 kills: halve duration
    before appending. At 10 kills: 50% chance to skip apply entirely. At 20 kills:
    always skip.

FLAVOR TEXT:
  "You started bringing the cold with you. At some point it stopped
  being a problem and started being a tool."

SYNERGIES:
  - Cryomancy skill: Chill-on-hit every attack is the best Cryomancy XP source in
    the game. At 3 hits per enemy × N enemies per floor, potential XP is enormous.
    Cryomancy perks (Freeze threshold, enhanced slow) compound with this.
  - Two Down Ring (item 21): Chill is a debuff. Against a chilled enemy (1+ stack),
    adding a second debuff from any source (ignite, stun) immediately activates the
    x1.5 damage multiplier. Cold Snap naturally primes this.
  - Time Served neck (item 29): doubles Chill duration from 8 to 16 turns per hit.
    At 3 hits, enemy has 3 Chill stacks lasting 16 turns — nearly guaranteed
    extreme slow for the entire room encounter.
  - Electrodynamics / shocked: Chill + shocked stacked is a classic dual-debuff setup.
    Cold Snap applies the Chill; any electric source adds Shocked; Two Down fires.
  - Frost patches as map denial: in corridor-heavy rooms, frost patches narrow enemy
    paths; any enemy moving toward the player must pass through a chilling tile.
    Effective passive crowd control without using any ability charges.

BALANCE:
  - Chill-on-hit is more reliable than a proc-based chill (all existing chill sources
    are percentage-based). This makes Cold Snap the definitive Cryomancy weapon.
    Balanced by the relatively low base damage (8) compared to endgame weapons.
  - The Chill resistance buildup takes 20 kills — approximately 2 floors of combat
    before full immunity. Until then, the player is still vulnerable to enemy chill.
  - Frost patches persist 15 turns and affect 1-4 additional enemies in typical
    fights. This is moderate environmental control but not overpowering on a per-
    patch basis.
  - str_scaling divisor 2 is standard; the weapon scales normally, not exceptionally.

ITEM 42 — WEAPON
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM ID:         "one_for_one"
NAME:            One For One
SLOT:            weapon (equip_slot: "weapon")
CHAR:            /
COLOR:           (240, 60, 60)   -- arterial red, the color of a very bad deal
SUBCATEGORY:     weapon
WEAPON TYPE:     beating
VALUE:           0
ZONES:           Both zones. Appears in a "Pawn Shop Back Room" event (a hidden
                 room behind a locked door that requires either Stealing L2+ or
                 dealing 20 damage to the door entity). The bat rests on the back
                 counter. Alternatively: dropped by the final enemy killed on any
                 floor if the player has EXACTLY 1 HP at the time of the kill.
                 Message: "You held on. It noticed."
TAGS:            ["legendary", "unique"]

TRADITIONAL ROGUELIKE INSPIRATION:
  NetHack — Stormbringer (chaotic runesword with sentience)
  Stormbringer is the most dangerous artifact in NetHack: it drains levels from
  everything it hits, including tame (friendly) creatures. It is SENTIENT — it
  sometimes attacks on its own without the player's input. The famous warning is
  that Stormbringer will attack your pets if they are adjacent. The drain-life
  mechanic means every hit, regardless of whether you intended it, permanently
  weakens the target. The weapon does what it wants. You merely hold it.

  Secondary inspiration: Angband's "hunger" weapons, which consume the wielder's
  food satiation faster to power their enhanced abilities.

TRANSLATION TO NIGRL:
  "One For One" deals double damage to every target — and double damage to the
  player every time the player is hit while wielding it. The weapon is perfectly
  balanced in the most dangerous way: every hit doubled, every hit taken doubled.
  It also "awakens" periodically and takes an autonomous action (attacks an
  adjacent enemy on its own without costing a player turn), analogous to
  Stormbringer's sentient aggression. But when it awakens, it is equally likely
  to drain the player's health instead.

STAT PROPERTIES:
  base_damage:   10
  str_req:       8
  reach:         1
  str_scaling:   {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}
  defense_bonus: 0
  armor_bonus:   0

SPECIAL PROPERTY — "The Price" (passive, always-on):
  New field: "one_for_one_weapon": True
  New engine state:
    engine.one_for_one_awaken_timer: int = 0  -- counts down to next autonomous act

  COMPONENT A — Double Damage Mirror:
    OUTGOING: All player melee hits with this weapon deal double damage.
      In _compute_player_attack_power(), if weapon is "one_for_one_weapon":
      atk_power *= 2 (applied AFTER all additive bonuses, BEFORE defense reduction).
      This doubles crit multipliers' base (e.g., a 2x crit becomes 4x effective
      because the base is already doubled — 10 damage becomes 20, then 4x crit = 80).
      The weapon IS NOT compatible with Windfury's extra hit (the autonomous nature
      of the weapon means it does not participate in bonus proc chains — Windfury's
      extra hit does NOT double via One For One; only the initial attack doubles).
      Implement: in handle_attack() Windfury check, add a guard: if weapon is
      "one_for_one_weapon", skip the Windfury roll.

    INCOMING: Every time the player takes melee damage from any enemy while wielding
      this weapon, that damage is DOUBLED before being applied.
      In deal_damage() or the damage pipeline targeting the player: if engine
      equipment["weapon"] item_id is "one_for_one_weapon": damage_taken *= 2.
      This applies to MELEE hits only (not DoT, not gun fire, not self-damage, not
      hazard effects). Check by flagging the damage source in deal_damage().
      Specifically: add an optional `melee_source: bool = False` parameter to
      deal_damage(). Monster melee attacks pass melee_source=True. The double-
      incoming only fires when melee_source=True.
      At floor 1-2 enemy damage of 5-8: wielding this weapon means 10-16 incoming.
      At floor 3-4 enemy damage of 10-15: wielding means 20-30 incoming per hit.
      This is the most severe cost of any legendary weapon.

  COMPONENT B — Sentience (autonomous action, occurs every 12 turns):
    engine.one_for_one_awaken_timer decrements by 1 each full energy loop tick.
    (Not per player action — per energy tick. At 100 energy/tick base: 12 ticks ≈
    1200 energy units ≈ ~12 player turns before the first awakening.)
    When engine.one_for_one_awaken_timer reaches 0:
      Reset timer: engine.one_for_one_awaken_timer = 12.
      Roll: random.choice(["attack", "drain"])

      IF "attack":
        The weapon autonomously attacks a random adjacent enemy (Chebyshev 1).
        If no adjacent enemy: nothing happens.
        Damage = base_damage + str_scaling (same formula, but NOT doubled — the
        weapon's own violence is its natural output, not amplified).
        No player turn consumed. No crit. No Windfury.
        Message: [red pulse] "One For One acts on its own! ([enemy] takes X damage!)"

      IF "drain":
        The weapon turns inward. The player takes damage equal to base_damage //2
        (5 at base_damage 10). This is not melee_source damage (no doubling loop).
        Message: [dark red] "One For One drinks from you. (-{dmg} HP)"
        If this kills the player: emit entity_died.

    The timer persists across floors (it is a run-level internal clock, reset only
    if the weapon is unequipped and re-equipped, which resets to 12).

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - Double outgoing: in _compute_player_attack_power() AFTER all additive bonuses,
    check if weapon item_id is "one_for_one_weapon": multiply total atk_power by 2.
  - Double incoming: in deal_damage() — add melee_source flag. When True and player
    is the defender and one_for_one is equipped: damage_taken *= 2.
  - Monster melee calls to deal_damage must pass melee_source=True. Check existing
    attack paths in engine.py and combat.py to tag appropriately.
  - Sentience timer: increment/decrement in _run_energy_loop at a reliable point
    (once per full loop cycle, not per entity action). When timer hits 0: resolve
    and reset.
  - The autonomous attack is NOT a player action — it does not trigger handle_attack
    hooks, Windfury, blood stacks, etc. It calls entity.take_damage(dmg) directly
    with a death check.
  - Windfury guard: add check in handle_attack() Windfury section.

FLAVOR TEXT:
  "Everything you put in, it gives back. To whoever's closest.
  Sometimes that's them. Sometimes that's you."

SYNERGIES:
  - Dead Man's Ring (item 6): the ring's 25% survival proc becomes essential when
    taking doubled incoming melee. The Low HP threshold (+25% outgoing, -2 incoming)
    helps offset the doubling cost partially.
  - Blood Pact Ring (item 15): doubled incoming damage means blood stacks accumulate
    at double speed (each hit adds 2x the damage to blood stacks). This converts
    the weapon's downside into the ring's resource.
  - Constitution builds: higher max HP absorbs doubled hits. CON 12 = 150 max HP;
    30-60 damage per hit still leaves survival windows.
  - One For One + Dead Weight neck: the doubled-damage-out is multiplied again by
    Dead Weight's inverted swagger bonus. Stack glass cannon mechanics.
  - Anti-synergy: any healing or HoT build. If you take 30 incoming damage per hit,
    OG Kush healing (50-70 HP) covers 1-2 hits. The math is tight.
  - Anti-synergy: Windfury (explicitly disabled), multiple-hit builds.

BALANCE:
  - Double outgoing is the most powerful single damage multiplier in the game. At
    10 base + 5 STR scaling + any additive bonuses: 30 damage * 2 = 60 per hit.
    With crit: 60 * 2 = 120. This is a one-shot threshold for most enemies.
  - Double incoming is the most severe penalty in the game. At Meth Lab floor 2
    enemies hitting for 10-15: wielding means 20-30 per hit. Without armor/defense,
    a CON 8 player (110 max HP) dies in 4-5 hits.
  - The sentience drain (5 damage every 12 turns) adds ~1 HP per turn of passive
    drain on average. Over a 60-turn floor: ~25 damage from the weapon itself.
  - Net assessment: for skilled players, One For One converts every fight into a
    one-shot-or-die scenario. Against enemies that can be killed in 1-2 hits, it
    is overwhelmingly efficient. Against anything durable (bosses), it is a gamble.

SUBCATEGORY:     weapon
WEAPON TYPE:     stabbing
VALUE:           0
ZONES:           crack_den only. Appears in a "Church Hall" room event — a room
                 with pew-like ASCII furniture and a donation box entity that the
                 player can smash for 5-20 cash. The knife sits on the altar table.
                 Church Hall spawns at 8% on any floor. Alternate: the knife
                 appears guaranteed in any run where the player has smoked all 13
                 available strains at least once (a "seen all vice" condition —

TAGS:            ["legendary", "unique"]

TRADITIONAL ROGUELIKE INSPIRATION:
  NetHack — Grayswandir (lawful artifact silver saber)
  Grayswandir is a +5 silver saber that does DOUBLE DAMAGE versus most monsters
  (specifically because of its enchantment doubling property) and grants hallucination
  resistance. The most mechanically interesting aspect is its SILVER DAMAGE property:
  it does extra damage against beings sensitive to silver (demons, undead, were-
  creatures, shapeshifters). In ADOM, silver weapons similarly bypass certain
  monster natural DR. Silver = alignment-aware damage that hits some things harder
  based on their NATURE rather than their armor.

  Secondary inspiration: ADOM's precrowning mechanic — artifacts that appear based
  on the player's piety/alignment history, not random luck. The "Church Hall" and
  "smoked all strains" conditions are alignment-flavor unlock conditions.

TRANSLATION TO NIGRL:
  Silver Service deals bonus damage against enemies with HIGH SWAGGER — the urbane,
  image-conscious, swaggering enemy types. It "cuts through the performance."
  The knife tracks the player's swagger stat and grants a percentage of it as bonus
  damage, meaning LOW-swagger players wielding it are punished (anti-synergy with
  the blade's own silver purpose: you need to be someone of substance to make it
  work). It also grants halluicination resistance (Grayswandir's secondary effect),
  translated as immunity to the "confuse" debuff.

STAT PROPERTIES:
  base_damage:   9
  str_req:       6
  reach:         1
  str_scaling:   {"type": "tiered", "divisor": 2}  -- +1 per 2 STR above 6
  defense_bonus: 0
  armor_bonus:   0
  bonus_crit_mult: 1                                -- +1x crit mult

SPECIAL PROPERTY — "Silver Edge" (passive):
  New field: "silver_service_weapon": True

  COMPONENT A — Silver Damage vs. High-Swagger Enemies:
    On every successful hit, check if the target's effective swagger is >= 8:
      silver_bonus = int(engine.player_stats.effective_swagger / 2)
      -- At player swagger 8: +4 bonus damage.
      -- At player swagger 12: +6 bonus damage.
      -- At player swagger 16 (with buffs): +8 bonus damage.
    This bonus damage is TRUE DAMAGE (bypasses defense): target.hp -= silver_bonus
    directly (after deal_damage resolves the base hit).
    Message: [bright silver] "Silver cuts true! (+{silver_bonus})"
    vs. LOW-swagger targets (swagger < 8):
      Silver damage bonus is REVERSED: the weapon deals 2 LESS effective damage
      (subtract 2 from atk_power for targets with swagger < 8).
      The blade is disdainful of easy prey. Low-swagger enemies deserve a lesser cut.
      Message: nothing (the penalty is silent — the player must observe it).

  COMPONENT B — Confuse Immunity (passive, always-on):
    While Silver Service is equipped, the player CANNOT receive the "confuse" debuff.
    Any application of "confuse" to the player is blocked.
    Implement: in apply_effect() for "confuse" targeting the player, check if weapon
    is "silver_service_weapon"; if so, skip application.
    Message if confuse is blocked: "The Silver Service keeps your mind clear."
    Note: "confuse" currently exists in effects.py as ConfuseEffect.

  COMPONENT C — Strain Synergy (passive):
    If the player has smoked any strain this floor (tracked via a flag:
    engine.smoked_this_floor: bool, set True when any strain use_effect fires):
      The silver_bonus above is increased by +2 flat for the remainder of that floor.
      This represents the "vices clarify rather than cloud" Grayswandir theme.
      Flag resets on new floor in engine._on_new_floor().

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
  - Silver bonus check: in handle_attack() after deal_damage, if weapon is
    "silver_service_weapon": check target's swagger (entity.base_stats.get("swagger",
    8) for monsters). If >= 8: compute silver_bonus, add strain bonus if applicable,
    call target.take_damage(silver_bonus) with death check. If < 8: subtract 2 from
    atk_power BEFORE the deal_damage call (modify in the atk_power computation block).
  - Confuse immunity: in apply_effect() caller with the entity == engine.player check,
    before applying ConfuseEffect: check weapon item_id.
  - Swagger of enemies: MonsterTemplate base_stats may not always include swagger.
    Default to swagger 8 for any monster without an explicit swagger stat.
    High-swagger enemy types (faction leaders, Scryer/Aldor commanders, Ugly Stripper)
    will have base_stats swagger >= 10 and be prime targets.
  - engine.smoked_this_floor: bool added to GameEngine.__init__ and reset in
    _on_new_floor(). Set True in item_effects.py when any "smoke" use_verb item fires.

FLAVOR TEXT:
  "Silver doesn't lie about what it cuts. The flashy ones feel it most."

SYNERGIES:
  - High-swagger builds (swagger 12+, crown + rings): Silver Service deals 6-8
    true silver damage per hit. Combined with the +1 crit mult, this is extremely
    powerful against high-swagger boss-tier enemies.
  - Rolling L3+ (Spectral Paper, Connoisseur branching): smoking strains triggers
    the +2 strain bonus on silver damage. A smoking-heavy build stacks silver damage
    throughout the floor.
  - Stabbing L3 Windfury: extra hits = extra silver true damage procs.
  - Meth Lab high-swagger faction enemies (Aldor, Scryer officers): these enemies
    likely have high swagger as their faction identity. Silver Service is optimal
    against them.
  - Anti-synergy: Dead Weight neck (-4 swagger): deliberately lowering swagger
    reduces the silver bonus significantly. Cannot run both.
  - Anti-synergy: Any Cryomancy / Chill build against low-swagger enemies
    (Tweakers, etc.) — the -2 penalty on low-swagger targets makes Silver Service
    underperform against the most common floor-1 enemies.

BALANCE:
  - Silver true damage (4-8) is significant but requires high player swagger.
    A starting swagger-8 character gains +4 true per hit — roughly equivalent to
    having a permanently applied "shocked 1 stack" bonus on every hit.
  - At swagger 12 and with smoking bonus: +6 + 2 = 8 true damage per hit.
    Against a Tweaker (15 HP, no swagger): -2 penalty means the knife deals
    7 base instead of 9. Against high-swagger targets: 17+ per hit. The
    weapon has very clear intended targets and punishes unfocused use.
  - Confuse immunity is purely defensive and cannot be valued offensively.
    Its value grows with the number of confusion-inflicting enemies encountered.

ZONES:           Both zones. Only spawns if the player has the Blackkk Magic or

TRADITIONAL ROGUELIKE INSPIRATION:
  NetHack — Magicbane (neutral artifact athame)
  Magicbane is the most mechanically complex weapon in NetHack. On each hit it has
  a 2/5 chance to trigger a random magical effect: probing (reveals monster stats),
  stunning the target, scaring (fear), or cancellation (removes monster's magical
  properties entirely). The chances of each effect shift with the weapon's enchantment
  level. Magicbane also resists 95% of curses thrown at the player and grants magic
  resistance. The key design principle: RANDOM MAGICAL PROCS that are sometimes
  beneficial, sometimes disruptive, but always interesting. Importantly, Magicbane
  is a NEUTRAL alignment item — it doesn't care about your moral axis.

TRANSLATION TO NIGRL:
  Magicbane Chain is a NECK item (not a weapon) that grants random magical procs
  on the player's melee hits. It translates the "random proc table on hit" mechanic
  into NIGRL's status effect vocabulary. The neck slot fits because it's the chain
  that does the magic, not the weapon. Each hit has a roll against a 5-effect table
  based on the player's Book-Smarts (which governs magic affinity). The chain also
  gives strong curse resistance (translated as debuff immunity proc chance) and
  magic resistance (translated as spell damage reduction).

STAT PROPERTIES:
  armor_bonus:   15
  defense_bonus: 1
  power_bonus:   2
  stat_bonus:    {"book_smarts": 3, "tolerance": 1}
  tox_resistance: 0
  rad_resistance: 0

SPECIAL PROPERTY — "The Old Magic" (passive, proc on hit):
  New field: "magicbane_chain": True

  ON EVERY PLAYER MELEE HIT, roll once:
    Proc chance = 20% + (engine.player_stats.effective_book_smarts * 2)%
    -- At BkSmt 6: 32% proc per hit.
    -- At BkSmt 10: 40% proc per hit.
    -- At BkSmt 15 (with bonuses): 50% proc per hit (capped at 50%).

  IF PROC TRIGGERS — roll 1d6 for the effect (uniform distribution):

    1 — PROBE: Reveal the target's full stat block in the message log.
        "Magicbane Chain probes [enemy]: HP {hp}/{max_hp}, PWR {power}, DEF {defense},
         SPD {speed}." Plus any notable flags (vampiric, death_split, etc.).
        Implement: format entity's key stats into a message string.
        No combat effect. Purely informational.
