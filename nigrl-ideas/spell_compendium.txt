================================================================================
NIGRL SPELL / ABILITY COMPENDIUM
A design reference for future ability additions
================================================================================

This document catalogs 45 spell and ability ideas for NIGRL, organized by
source (drinking, eating/munching, smoking, Blackkk Magic, and miscellaneous
combat/utility trees). Each entry includes implementation notes tied to the
existing AbilityDef / TargetType / ChargeType / effects.py architecture.

Abilities already in the registry (as of compendium writing):
  warp, dimension_door, chain_lightning, ray_of_frost, firebolt, arcane_missile,
  breath_fire, zap, corn_dog, lesser_cloudkill, pry, throw_bottle, bash,
  black_eye_slap, gouge, pickpocket, force_push, fry_shot, place_fire, dash,
  ignite_spell, quick_eat, double_tap, spray, burst, spray_and_pray,
  slow_metabolism, pandemic, radiation_nova

These 45 ideas do NOT duplicate the above. Cross-synergies with them are noted.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NOTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  TARGET:  SELF | ADJACENT | ADJ_TILE | SINGLE_LOS | LINE | AOE_CIRCLE
  CHARGE:  INF (infinite, cooldown-gated) | /FL (per floor) | TOTAL (permanent
           uses) | ONCE | FL_ONLY (per floor, not saved on descent)
  SOURCE:  which skill tree, perk level, or item grants this ability
  STAT:    which player stat scales the damage/effect

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION A — DRINKING MAGIC
Skill trees: Drinking (L1-L10), Alcoholism (L1-L10)
Thematic core: liquid transformation, reality blurring, bravado, poison
XP source for Drinking tree: consuming drinks
XP source for Alcoholism tree: taking hits while drunk, throwing bottles
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A-01  LIQUID COURAGE
  Source:   Drinking L4 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   INF (25-turn cooldown)
  Stat:     Tolerance + Swagger
  Mechanic: Apply a buff (LiquidCourageEffect) for 20 turns. While active:
            - All melee damage dealt +TOL/3 (rounds down)
            - All incoming damage -SWG/4 (rounds down, minimum 0 reduction)
            - Player cannot voluntarily retreat (cannot move away from the
              nearest visible enemy — movement in that direction blocked)
  Why:      The restriction is the cost. Double-edged: incredible for offense-
            forward builds, dangerous for low-SWG fragile players who need to
            kite. Makes every Drinking investment a meaningful tradeoff.
  Flavor:   "You drink deep and feel yourself turn into a freight train. Can't
             back down now even if you wanted to."
  Notes:    LiquidCourageEffect overrides modify_movement to block retreat dirs.
            Check: is movement vector moving away from nearest visible enemy?
            If yes, block it. Pairs with Alcoholism perks that reward staying
            adjacent.

A-02  HAIR OF THE DOG
  Source:   Alcoholism L4 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: Consumes ALL currently active drink buffs on the player. For each
            buff consumed, heal max(1, TOL/3) HP and apply +1 temporary
            Tolerance for 5 turns per buff consumed (stacking). Net effect:
            burning your buffs for a burst heal + brief tankiness surge.
            Minimum 1 active drink buff required to use.
  Why:      Forces a choice — save the buffs for their primary effects or cash
            them in for emergency HP. Interacts directly with Slow Metabolism
            (Drinking L3) which doubles buff durations; those doubled buffs
            become doubled heal value here. Natural combo for high-Tolerance
            drinker builds.
  Flavor:   "Best hangover cure in the game. Drink through the pain."
  Notes:    Iterates player.status_effects, collects all with id in the drink
            buff set (_DRINK_BUFF_IDS in abilities.py). Extend that set as
            new drink items are added.

A-03  SPIKED PUNCH
  Source:   Drinking L5 activated perk  OR  item "Spiked Drink" (future item)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS  (range 3)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: Splash a drink at an enemy. Deals no direct damage. Applies a new
            effect "Spiked" (duration: 8 turns): target has -30% accuracy on
            all attacks (relevant when enemies with ranged AI are added) and
            takes double damage from any Ignite stacks they currently have or
            gain during the duration.
  Why:      Pure debuff with zero damage makes it interesting — it converts your
            other tools (fire, zap stacks, ignite_spell) into a combo finisher.
            Great against high-defense targets where damage would be mitigated
            anyway.
  Flavor:   "You don't throw it at them to hurt them. You throw it at them so
             the next thing you throw at them hurts them worse."
  Notes:    SpikedEffect.modify_incoming_damage checks if entity also has
            ignite effect; if yes, doubles the ignite's tick damage that turn.
            Future synergy: Agent Orange strain reduces TOL-roll for enemies;
            Spiked could extend this.

A-04  CORKSCREW
  Source:   Drinking L6 activated perk
  Target:   LINE (8-tile ray in aimed direction)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Tolerance + Strength
  Mechanic: Hurl a spinning bottle along a line. Hits every enemy in the path.
            Damage = max(1, STR + TOL/2 - target.defense). The bottle then
            BOUNCES off the last wall/enemy it hits and travels back 4 tiles.
            Enemies hit on the return trip take half damage. Cannot hit the
            same entity twice per cast.
  Why:      Line abilities in roguelikes are interesting because positioning
            matters — lining up multiple enemies in a corridor is rewarding but
            requires planning. The bounce adds geometry decision-making: do I
            fire at an angle so the bounce hits another cluster?
  Flavor:   "You spin the bottle. Spin the bottle. Let fate decide who catches
             it on the way back."
  Notes:    Bounce logic: after the forward pass resolves, reflect the direction
            vector (dx -> -dx or dy -> -dy depending on collision surface) and
            run a 4-step return pass, skipping already-hit entities.

A-05  BLACKOUT
  Source:   Alcoholism L5 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   ONCE (single use, permanent)
  Stat:     Tolerance + Constitution
  Mechanic: The ultimate alcoholism finisher. Player drinks everything they
            have at once in a dramatic moment. Effect:
            - Applies 15-turn "Blackout" buff: player cannot be killed (HP
              floors at 1 on any hit that would be lethal), cannot see more
              than 3 tiles (sight_radius reduced), moves randomly 25% of turns
            - On expiry: heals TOL * 3 HP. If the player survived the entire
              duration, also permanently grants +2 Tolerance.
            - On expiry if player did NOT survive (was below 20% HP when it
              expired): no bonus, but player vomits (future vomit effect).
  Why:      The ONCE restriction makes this a high-stakes finisher — you only
            get one blackout per run. The random movement and vision penalty
            mean you are genuinely out of control. The survival bonus is a
            tempting risk for high-investment Alcoholism builds.
  Flavor:   "You don't remember what happened next. But apparently you survived
             it."
  Notes:    BlackoutEffect.before_turn 25% chance returns True (skip turn, move
            randomly instead). modify_incoming_damage floors incoming lethal
            damage to leave target at 1 HP (track a "downed_this_blackout"
            flag to prevent double-proc). On expire, check player.hp against
            player.max_hp * 0.2 to determine bonus.

A-06  LAST CALL
  Source:   Alcoholism L5 (already in memory — variant design)
            Alternative: grant from a unique "Closing Time" item (tavern tier)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   ONCE
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: Activate with 0-3 HP. Forces a confrontation with consequences:
            - Immediately heal to 50% max HP (the adrenaline)
            - Apply Staggered debuff (from memory: -30 energy/tick, 15% skip
              chance, 10 turns) to all enemies within 5 tiles
            - After 10 turns: player takes TOL-scaled damage (less TOL = more
              damage taken as the crash hits)
  Why:      Designed specifically for desperation situations — a roguelike
            staple (NetHack's prayer, DCSS's divine intervention equivalents).
            Players who hit it too late lose the HP window; players who use it
            prophylactically waste the burst. Tight timing window.
  Notes:    This is a variant of the Last Call in memory's L5 Alcoholism design.
            The version here is item-granted rather than perk-granted, making
            it a one-run consumable ability.

SECTION B — EATING MAGIC
Skill trees: Munching (L1-L10), Deep-Frying (L1-L10)
Thematic core: bodily transformation, power through consumption, gas attacks,
               grease as a weapon, metabolic alchemy
XP source for Munching: eating food
XP source for Deep-Frying: using the deep fryer, eating fried foods
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

B-01  GAS ATTACK
  Source:   Munching L4 activated perk
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 2, centered on player)
  Charge:   INF (12-turn cooldown)
  Stat:     Constitution + Tolerance
  Mechanic: Violent gastrointestinal event. All enemies within radius 2 take
            damage = max(1, CON/2 - defense) and are "nauseated" for 5 turns
            (NauseaEffect: reduces their energy gain by 40%, -1 to all attack
            rolls). Player is briefly deafened but not harmed (flavor only).
            Damage scales up by +3 for each active food buff the player has.
  Why:      Short-range AOE pushes players into melee range, which is risky.
            Food-buff scaling rewards the Munching fantasy of being permanently
            buffed from multiple active food items. Works especially well after
            eating Lightskin Beans (10-turn eat gives time to set up a surround).
  Flavor:   "The lightskin beans were a mistake. Or maybe they weren't."
  Notes:    NauseaEffect: category="debuff", modify_energy_gain multiplies by
            0.6. Count active food buffs by scanning player.status_effects for
            any with category="buff" and id in food-effect id set.

B-02  DEEP-FRIED FIREBALL
  Source:   Deep-Frying L4 activated perk
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     Constitution + Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Toss a ball of blazing hot grease in an arc to a target tile.
            On impact: 3x3 AOE centered on target tile. All entities in the
            area take max(1, CON + BKS/2 - defense) damage and receive 2
            Ignite stacks. Leaves a fire hazard tile at the impact point that
            persists for 5 turns (using existing fire hazard system).
  Why:      This is the Deep-Frying tree's answer to the firebolt from item
            drops — but with meaningful spread and environmental setup rather
            than single-target damage. The grease fire tile created is a
            persistent threat, synergizing with Pyromania's Wildfire passive
            if both trees are active.
  Flavor:   "The Fry Daddy doesn't judge. It just burns."
  Notes:    Use get_affected_tiles lambda for the 3x3 preview. Spawn fire
            hazard at target tile via create_fire(tx, ty). Needs a duration
            on fire — add a turns_remaining field to fire hazard entity if
            not already present, or simply not clean them up (permanent fires
            are fine for balance since floors are temporary anyway).

B-03  IRON GUT
  Source:   Munching L5 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Constitution + Tolerance
  Mechanic: For 15 turns, apply IronGutEffect:
            - All incoming damage is reduced by CON/4 (minimum 0, does not
              stack with other DR effects — takes the higher value)
            - Player is immune to all food-related negative effects (tainted
              food, hot cheetos expire damage, NauseaEffect from enemies)
            - Any poison/toxicity applied to the player this turn is converted
              to healing at 50% efficiency (1 tox dealt -> 0.5 HP restored)
  Why:      High-end defensive cooldown that rewards stacking CON via Munching.
            The tox-to-heal conversion is the centerpiece — makes the player
            temporarily unkillable by chemical hazards, very useful in Meth Lab.
  Flavor:   "Your stomach lining is basically asbestos at this point."
  Notes:    The tox-to-heal hook needs a new lifecycle method or can be handled
            by checking IronGutEffect in combat.py's add_toxicity path. Model
            it like modify_incoming_damage but for toxicity sources.

B-04  CORN DOG JUDGMENT
  Source:   Deep-Frying L5 activated perk  OR  future food item
  Target:   LINE (6-tile ray)
  Charge:   TOTAL (stack from any food item proc, shared pool)
  Stat:     Strength + Constitution
  Mechanic: Hurl a deep-fried corn dog like a javelin. The corn dog flies in
            a straight line and hits the first enemy in its path. Damage =
            STR + CON (no defense reduction — grease penetrates everything).
            The target is then covered in hot grease: applies 3 Greasy stacks
            AND 2 Ignite stacks simultaneously. The corn dog itself drops at
            the target's feet as a consumable entity (Corn Dog item, 1 charge).
  Why:      Unusual because the projectile becomes loot — you might throw it
            into a room to create a food item you can collect later (if the
            enemy dies). The grease+ignite double-apply makes it a setup tool
            for fire builds. The "penetrating defense" mechanic is significant
            flavor: grease is greasy.
  Flavor:   "It was a perfectly good corn dog. And then it became a weapon.
             And then it became a perfectly good corn dog again."
  Notes:    "Creates item at landing spot" — spawn a corn_dog Entity at the
            first position AFTER where the enemy was hit (if enemy dies, drops
            there; if alive, drops behind target). Use create_item_entity().

B-05  CALORIC SURGE
  Source:   Munching L6 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Constitution
  Mechanic: Convert excess calories into raw power. Effect lasts 12 turns:
            - Player gains +CON/2 bonus power (melee damage bonus)
            - Player moves at +20 energy per tick
            - On expiry: player loses 1 temporary CON for 10 turns (the crash)
            Stacking two uses back-to-back is allowed — the crash timers
            compound and the bonuses overlap, which is risky.
  Why:      Short-window melee damage spike with a real downside. The stacking
            risk/crash mechanic invites players to gamble on double-stacking for
            big fights. Similar to Meth-Head's Wire/Crash loop but for Munching.
  Flavor:   "Everything they said about sugar highs was true. So are the lows."
  Notes:    CalorieSurgeEffect.expire: apply a temporary CON-1 effect for 10
            turns (use existing temporary_stat_bonuses system from stats.py).
            On double-stack: both crash effects apply independently.

B-06  ENZYME BOMB
  Source:   Deep-Frying L6 activated perk
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 5)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Constitution + Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Hurl a jar of hyper-concentrated frying oil that erupts on impact.
            Primary target: CON + BKS/3 damage, 5 Greasy stacks, defense
            permanently (for this floor) reduced by 2.
            Splash: all monsters adjacent to target take CON/2 damage and
            2 Greasy stacks.
            The defense reduction is a big deal — it stacks with Pry (which
            sets defense to 0). Use it on armored enemies before Pry.
  Why:      Creates a distinct role for the Deep-Frying tree in group combat:
            debuffing entire clusters for follow-up attacks. The permanent
            defense reduction (for the floor) makes it worth saving for tanky
            enemies.
  Flavor:   "The enzyme is actually a cooking term. Here it means 'acid.'"
  Notes:    "Permanent for this floor" defense reduction: add a floor_defense_
            penalty field to Entity or track via a long-duration effect that
            resets on floor change (use FLOOR_ONLY charge as a model).

SECTION C — SMOKING MAGIC
Skill trees: Smoking (L1-L10), Rolling (L1-L10), Pyromania (L4-L10)
Thematic core: clouds and confusion, contact highs, fire and smoke screens,
               time dilation, spiritual/altered perception
XP source: smoking joints, rolling, setting things on fire
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

C-01  PHAT CLOUD (ACTIVE VARIANT)
  Source:   Smoking L3 (currently "Phat Cloud" is listed as passive/TBD)
            Suggest: make L3 an activated ability instead of pure passive
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   INF (8-turn cooldown)
  Stat:     Tolerance + Constitution
  Mechanic: Exhale a massive cloud of smoke centered on the player, filling
            all tiles within radius 2. Any enemy entering or already in the
            cloud has a 60% chance per turn to waste their action (wander
            randomly instead of chasing). Cloud persists for 4 turns.
            Damage: none. The cloud is a zone-of-control tool.
            Higher Tolerance: cloud radius increases (+1 at TOL 12, +1 at TOL 16).
  Why:      True crowd control through area denial is rare in NIGRL's current
            ability set. The cloud doesn't deal damage, making it pure utility —
            interesting because good players use it proactively (walk through
            enemy approach corridor before being seen) rather than reactively.
  Flavor:   "You blow it all out in one go. The hallway fills up. Good luck
             thinking straight in that."
  Notes:    Implement as a hazard entity or track cloud tiles in engine with a
            timer. Simplest approach: place SmokecloudHazard at each tile in
            radius at time of cast; on entity_tick, check if monster is on
            smoke tile, roll 60% confusion chance. Clear after 4 turns.
            Or: apply Confused debuff (existing or new) to each monster that
            starts their turn inside the cloud radius.

C-02  CONTACT HIGH
  Source:   Smoking L5 activated perk (currently listed as passive/TBD)
            Suggest: make this an activated ability rather than auto-passive
  Target:   AOE_CIRCLE (radius 3, centered on player)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Tolerance + current active strain effect tier
  Mechanic: Release a supercharged exhale. Every enemy within radius 3 gains
            a copy of the CURRENT strain effect the player is experiencing
            (the active strain buff from the last joint smoked), but at half
            duration and inverted (buffs become debuffs, debuffs become buffs).
            An OG Kush healing buff would become a heal-over-time that drains
            enemy HP. A DOT from a bad strain roll would become an HP regen
            on the enemy — so the inversion matters and sometimes timing is
            everything.
  Why:      Mechanically ties the Smoking tree's strain system into the combat
            system in a direct and surprising way. Forces players to think about
            what strain they last smoked before activating this. No strain
            active = no effect (risk of wasting the charge).
  Flavor:   "You breathe out. They breathe in. It hits different when you don't
             see it coming."
  Notes:    This requires reading engine's active_strain_effect (or tracking
            last-smoked-strain on engine). The inversion logic is the tricky
            part — a clean approach is to map effect_type -> inverted_effect_type
            in a dict table. Consider: if the strain produced "none" effect, the
            contact high also does nothing.

C-03  SMOKE SCREEN
  Source:   Rolling L4 activated perk  (complement to the L5 Rollin' Cloud)
  Target:   ADJ_TILE
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     None (flat effect)
  Mechanic: Light up a blunt and toss it. The tile becomes a smoke source for
            8 turns. Any enemy with line-of-sight to the player THROUGH the
            smoke tile has their LOS broken (they lose track of player and
            return to IDLE/WANDERING state). Essentially creates a hard LOS
            blocker that persists without a wall.
  Why:      Tactical positioning tool. Roguelikes with LOS-dependent enemy AI
            (like NIGRL) make LOS manipulation extremely powerful — you can
            walk past enemies they were chasing you, reset their state, then
            re-engage on your terms. The ADJ_TILE placement means you must
            choose your position carefully.
  Flavor:   "You toss the blunt in the doorway. They can't see through smoke.
             You can. That's the difference."
  Notes:    Simplest implementation: add SmokeScreenHazard entity type. In
            ai.py's transition logic for CHASING -> WANDERING: check if
            line-of-sight path passes through a smoke screen tile. If yes,
            break chase even if player would normally be visible. The smoke
            hazard entity just has a turns_remaining timer.

C-04  SPIRITUAL TRANSCENDENCE
  Source:   Smoking L8 or special item "Holy Backwood" (very rare)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   ONCE
  Stat:     Tolerance + all stats combined
  Mechanic: Take a cosmic hit. Duration: 20 turns. Effect:
            - Player becomes "phased" — enemies cannot target player with melee
              (they walk through your tile instead of blocking on it), but the
              player also cannot deal melee damage
            - Ranged attacks still work both ways
            - Player regenerates TOL/2 HP per turn during transcendence
            - On expiry: player returns to normal. Permanent +1 to a random
              stat (the insight gained).
  Why:      The ONCE restriction and the melee-immunity/melee-disabled tradeoff
            create a very different style of play for 20 turns. Forces adaptation
            to a pure ranged/ability playstyle. The random stat bonus is the
            long-term benefit — replayability from not knowing which stat.
  Flavor:   "You leave your body for a while. Your body continues to exist. This
             is fine."
  Notes:    PhasedEffect: blocks_movement is now false for player (add special
            case in dungeon.is_blocked where player entity ignores monster
            blocking). Monster pathfinding still pathing to player but can
            "pass through" — implement by having monster step onto player tile
            momentarily (they swap or the monster just occupies the same tile).
            On_player_melee_hit hook: if player has phased, cancel damage.
            Simplest route: just prevent player from using melee attack action.

C-05  DUTCH COURAGE CLOUD
  Source:   Rolling L5 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Tolerance + Swagger
  Mechanic: Roll and smoke a particularly fat one. Apply DutchCourageEffect:
            for 10 turns, every time the player takes damage, they deal
            retaliatory damage to ALL visible enemies equal to the damage taken
            divided by number of visible enemies (minimum 1). This retaliation
            bypasses defense. The cloud of smoke visual signals to the player
            that they're "on one."
  Why:      Pure reactive mechanic — transforms taking damage from bad into
            slightly good. Very interesting in corridor fights where you're
            surrounded. Synergizes with Blackout (A-05) where you're forced
            to take hits anyway.
  Flavor:   "The more they hit you, the more it spreads. That's the thing about
             smoke."
  Notes:    DutchCourageEffect.modify_incoming_damage: calculate and deal
            retaliatory damage immediately in that hook. Retaliatory loop
            collects visible monsters, distributes damage/(num_visible) each,
            no defense. Use max(1, ...) per target.

C-06  SLOW BURN
  Source:   Smoking L6 activated perk
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 6)
  Charge:   INF (20-turn cooldown)
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: You breathe a long stream of slow-burning smoke at one enemy.
            The target gains "Slow Burn" debuff for 15 turns: takes TOL/5
            damage per turn (rounding up), and moves at -25 energy per tick.
            Unlike Ignite (which burns hot and fast), Slow Burn cannot be
            stacked but its damage grows each turn (+1 flat per turn until
            expiry, then resets on reapply). Reapplying refreshes duration
            but also resets the growing damage back to TOL/5.
  Why:      Sustained DoT with a patience mechanic — if you let it run its
            full 15 turns without re-applying, the final ticks deal TOL/5 + 14
            damage (substantial). Re-applying prematurely resets the ramp.
            Forces players to commit to the "let it burn" strategy vs. topping
            up. The slowing component also enables melee follow-up.
  Flavor:   "You don't rush a slow burn. You let it cook."
  Notes:    SlowBurnEffect: store a turns_elapsed int. tick() increments it.
            Actual tick damage = base_damage + turns_elapsed. on_reapply
            refreshes duration but sets turns_elapsed = 0 (resets ramp).

C-07  ROACH RESURRECTION
  Source:   Rolling L6 activated perk
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Tolerance
  Mechanic: You dig through your pockets, find every roach (joint stub), and
            smoke them all at once. Immediately re-trigger the effect of the
            last joint the player smoked at the SAME roll value they got before.
            If no joint has been smoked this floor, instead grants TOL/2 HP.
            Cannot re-trigger joint rolls that resulted in "none" effects — it
            tries the next-previous roll.
  Why:      Memory and bookkeeping mechanic. Smart players remember which strain
            gave them a great roll and save Roach Resurrection for after a high
            roll. Rewards paying attention to the strain result display.
  Flavor:   "Desperate times. But the roach never lies."
  Notes:    Engine needs to track last_strain_roll_value and last_strain_name
            on floor. Roach Resurrection replays the effect lookup with that
            roll value. Simpler fallback: just re-apply the last strain's
            effect at 50% duration.

SECTION D — BLACKKK MAGIC
Skill tree: Blackkk Magic (L1-L10)
Thematic core: forbidden knowledge, mind manipulation, spiritual/cosmic power,
               curses, reality bending, street voodoo
XP source: casting any spell/ability tagged "spell" or "is_spell=True"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D-01  MIND BULLETS (Telekinetic Burst)
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L3 activated  (after L2 Force Be With You)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   INF (cooldown 18 turns)
  Stat:     Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Direct telekinetic force projectile. Dmg: BKS + 4. No defense
            reduction on half the damage (the "psychic half" bypasses armor).
            Formula: (BKS + 4) where BKS/2 is armor-bypassing psychic dmg and
            the remaining BKS/2 + 4 is subject to normal defense. Applies
            "Psychic Daze" for 3 turns: enemy cannot dash/move diagonally
            (reduced movement options).
  Why:      The split psychic/physical damage is mechanically interesting
            against high-defense enemies — BKS scaling means it stays useful
            late, but defense still matters partially.
  Flavor:   "You think real hard at them. It works."
  Notes:    Psychic Daze effect: modify_movement hook limits to cardinal
            directions only (dx and dy cannot both be nonzero simultaneously).

D-02  CURSE OF BAD LUCK
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L4 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 10)
  Charge:   /FL 3
  Stat:     Book Smarts + Street Smarts
  Mechanic: Hex an enemy. CurseEffect (duration 20 turns): target's every
            attack has a 30% chance to miss entirely (they stumble, their
            weapon jams, they swing at air). Additionally, if the cursed
            target is killed by the player, they drop +50% extra loot (adds
            an extra item roll on death). The loot bonus only triggers if
            the kill happens while the curse is active.
  Why:      Curse-for-loot is a classic roguelike pattern (NetHack's magic
            marker, DCSS's various curse mechanics). Invites a "curse them all,
            kill for loot" playstyle. The miss chance is defensive utility.
  Flavor:   "You put a hex on that fool. Whatever they pick up falls. Whatever
             they swing misses. Whatever they reach for ain't there."
  Notes:    CurseEffect.before_turn: roll 30%, if hit return True (skip the
            attack portion, still move). Need a "cursed" flag on entity for
            the death handler in combat.py to check for the loot bonus.

D-03  SPIRIT STEAL
  Source:   Blackkk Magic L5 activated (capstone combo with Negromancy)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 6)
  Charge:   /FL 2
  Stat:     Book Smarts
  Mechanic: Drain the spiritual essence of a living enemy. Deals max(1,
            BKS - target.defense) psychic damage. Then, for 10 turns, the
            player gains HALF of every stat bonus the target has above their
            baseline (rounded down). Example: if Jerome has +3 STR above the
            minimum, Spirit Steal gives the player +1 temporary STR for 10
            turns. Affects all six stats. Does NOT kill the target.
  Why:      Unique mechanic that makes high-stat enemies worth targeting WITH
            a non-kill spell first. Creates a sequence: Spirit Steal -> buff

             it takes."
  Notes:    Cash cost = $25 * target enemy level (use enemy's experience_value
            as a proxy for floor_level if no explicit level field). Faction
            check: look for entity.faction field. Can_use validate: check
            target's ai_state != CHASING (must be non-hostile to bribe).

G-06  IMPROVISED EXPLOSIVE
  Source:   Deep-Frying L7 activated  OR  Dismantling L5 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 5)
  Charge:   TOTAL (1-3 charges from specific item finds)
  Stat:     Strength + Constitution
  Mechanic: Hurl an improvised pipe bomb (or pressurized grease canister).
            On target tile: 2-tile radius explosion. All entities in radius
            take STR + CON damage, ignoring defense. The explosion also
            destroys crate hazards in the blast radius (same as player bumping
            them, but remote). Any fire hazard in radius becomes a 3x3 fire
            for 3 turns (spread mechanic). Blast leaves scorched tiles that
            slow movement (half energy) for 2 turns.
  Why:      Multi-purpose ability: damage, environmental control, hazard
            interaction. The fire-spread interaction with existing fire hazards
            creates emergent chained explosions in Pyromania builds. Scorched
            tiles are a new mechanic (persistent terrain modification).
  Flavor:   "It's not complicated. It's pressure and heat and timing."
  Notes:    Scorched tiles: add to dungeon.hazard_tiles dict (similar to fire
            tracking). Entities on scorched tiles during their movement spend
            +50% energy. Clear after 2 global turns. The fire-spread from
            existing fires: check all fire hazard entities within blast radius
            and expand their coverage area by 1 tile in all directions.

G-07  CLOCK OUT (Turn Skip Gambit)
  Source:   Jaywalking L8 activated
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Mechanic: The player deliberately "clocks out" — skips their next 3 turns
            entirely. During those 3 skipped turns:
            - All enemies are stunned for 1 turn each (they flinch — the
              unexpected passivity confuses them)
            - Player gains +8 armor for the 3-turn period
            - After the 3 turns, the player's next action is FREE (costs 0
              energy — representing the burst of movement after resting)
  Why:      Counterintuitive "skip turns to gain advantage" mechanic. In a
            roguelike where every turn costs resources and enemies are threats,
            willingly losing turns feels bad until you understand the payoff.
            The free action at the end is powerful. Very NetHack "prayer" in
            feel — strange but rewarding if understood.
  Flavor:   "You stop. They don't know what to do with that. Neither do you,
             but you had a plan."
  Notes:    ClockOutEffect.before_turn returns True for 3 turns (skips player
            action). On first turn of each enemy with this active: apply stun
            for 1 turn. Track turns remaining on the effect. On expire: grant
            player a "free_action_pending" flag that makes the next action cost
            0 energy.

G-08  NOISE COMPLAINT
  Source:   Item "Air Horn" or Stealing L6 activated
  Target:   SELF (radius 6 area-of-effect on enemies)
  Charge:   TOTAL (1-2 uses from item)
  Mechanic: An earsplitting noise in a 6-tile radius. All enemies:
            - Currently IDLE: immediately flee (FLEEING) for 3 turns before
              investigating again
            - Currently CHASING: stunned for 2 turns, then resume
            - Currently ALERTING: interrupt their alert, reset to IDLE
            The player is also affected: slight energy loss next turn (-30
            energy from the disorientation).
  Why:      Situational panic button. Best for resetting an alerted crowd
            before they call for backup. Particularly useful against the
            "alarm_chaser" AI type and "female_alarm" type enemies that spread
            alerts. The player self-disorientation keeps it balanced.
  Flavor:   "Everything stops. For two seconds. That's enough."
  Notes:    ALERTING state reset: set ai_state back to IDLE, clear any alarm
            propagation queued. female_alarm AI calls for help mid-tick; if
            Noise Complaint interrupts her turn, the alarm should not go out.
            Requires checking if alarm has already been sent this turn.

G-09  MONEY SHOT
  Source:   Stealing L7 activated  (capstone)
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 8)
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Street Smarts + Swagger
  Mechanic: Throw a fistful of cash at an enemy. Costs $50 cash. Damage =
            $50 flat (not affected by defense — cash is dense and heavy).
            Additionally: EVERY enemy in a 3-tile radius of the target becomes
            IDLE for 3 turns as they scramble for the bills on the ground.
            The scattered cash remains as a pickup item on the floor (totaling
            $25 — you "lose" $25 permanently as the cost of the scatter).
  Why:      Economic spell. You spend $50 to neutralize a room and deal 50
            unavoidable damage. The $25 cash recovery on the floor means you
            can sometimes pick it up and recoup the loss — but that requires
            approaching the tile, which is risky. High-Swagger builds that
            accumulate cash more easily find this less costly.
  Flavor:   "You throw the money. Time stops. They all look. You walk away."
  Notes:    Spawn a cash pile entity at the target tile with value=25 after
            the ability fires. Enemies that become IDLE mid-chase: set
            ai_state = IDLE, clear their chasing target. Reactivates after 3
            turns via normal LOS checks.

G-10  LAST MAN STANDING
  Source:   Passive (no activation) — unlocked at CON 14 via Constitution tree
            OR granted by a specific item
  Target:   SELF (passive)
  Charge:   ONCE per run (triggers automatically)
  Mechanic: When the player's HP would drop below 5 for the first time:
            - Damage is blocked (HP stays at 5)
            - All enemies within 4 tiles are pushed away 2 tiles (knockback)
              and stunned for 1 turn (the "get off me" moment)
            - Player heals 15 HP immediately
            - Effect triggers ONLY ONCE PER RUN (not per floor)
            After triggering: a "Battered" debuff applies for 20 turns (-2 to
            all stats, representing the close call).
  Why:      Classic "cheat death" mechanic (DCSS has multiple versions of this,
            ADOM has similar divine favor). The ONCE PER RUN restriction makes
            it precious. Players who know they have it play slightly more
            aggressively. The Battered debuff after ensures the save isn't a
            free escape — you're in worse shape after.
  Flavor:   "That should have been it. It wasn't."
  Notes:    Engine: track a "last_man_used" flag in engine state. On damage
            resolution: if player.hp - damage < 5 and not last_man_used:
            block damage (set hp to 5), trigger knockback + stun on nearby
            enemies, heal 15, set last_man_used = True. Apply Battered debuff.
            Knockback: same logic as force_push but centered on player.

H-01  RECURSIVE BLUNT (Item ability)
  Source:   Rolling L9 activated (near-max skill)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   TOTAL (2 uses total, gained from Rolling perk unlock)
  Mechanic: You roll a blunt inside a blunt. When smoked, it counts as TWO
            simultaneous joint smokes: rolls TWICE on the effect table for the
            current strain, takes the BETTER of the two effects (not both),
            AND also triggers the second Tolerance roll independently. The
            Tolerance system (calc_tolerance_rolls) applies to both rolls.
  Why:      Deep interaction with the strain/tolerance system. Max Rolling
            investment reward: making your rolls dramatically more consistent
            by selecting the better of two outcomes. The "takes the better"
            rule prevents degenerate combos while still being powerful.
  Flavor:   "It's a joint inside a joint. It's philosophical."
  Notes:    Execute: call the normal joint-smoking logic twice (same strain).
            Compare effect results, apply the more favorable one. "More
            favorable" needs a comparison heuristic (e.g., higher heal%
            wins over lower heal%; player_effect "none" loses to any
            actual effect).

H-02  IDENTITY THEFT
  Source:   Stealing L9 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 5)
  Charge:   ONCE
  Mechanic: Copy one enemy's entire ability kit for 20 turns. During those
            20 turns: player gains the target's special_attacks as usable
            abilities (added to ability list), the target's ai_type becomes
            "passive_until_hit" (they don't know who to be anymore), and the
            player gains the target's base stat modifications (their bonus
            stats above the floor minimum).
  Why:      Transformative one-time ability — you become the enemy for a fight.
            The permadeath stakes make ONCE abilities that dramatically change
            the character feel meaningful. Extraordinary risk/reward.
  Flavor:   "You look them in the eye. They look back. You take everything."
  Notes:    This requires iterating the enemy's special_attacks list and
            converting them to AbilityDef entries temporarily. Complex to
            implement cleanly but architecturally consistent with data-driven
            enemy definitions in enemies.py. The ability_id could be a dynamic
            "stolen_{enemy_type}_{attack_name}" key.

H-03  MARKET CRASH
  Source:   Abandoning L5 activated  (letting go of material things)
  Target:   SELF
  Charge:   ONCE
  Mechanic: Sacrifice ALL your cash permanently ($0 remaining). For every $10
            sacrificed: permanently gain +0.5% critical hit chance (rounded to
            nearest 0.5%). Additionally: for the rest of the run, all items
            on every floor have a 15% chance to be "discarded" (free —
            the merchants and looters gave up too).
  Why:      Economy-to-power conversion at maximum commitment. Players who
            hoarded cash the whole run sacrifice it for permanent power.
            Very risky because cash is also defense (buying items). The 15%
            free item rate is the secondary benefit that compounds over time.
            Thematic: Abandoning means letting go of everything.
  Flavor:   "It was never real. None of it. You knew that."
  Notes:    Permanent crit change: modify player_stats.crit_modifier (add
            a permanent_crit_bonus float field). The "15% items free" would
            need a flag checked in loot presentation. Significant UI work
            but the flag is simple.

H-04  CHAIN REACTION
  Source:   Chemical Warfare L7 activated
  Target:   SINGLE_LOS (range 6) — initial target
  Charge:   /FL 1
  Stat:     Book Smarts + Tolerance
  Mechanic: Inject a catalytic compound into one enemy. That enemy becomes
            "volatile" for 5 turns. If the volatile enemy takes ANY damage
            during those 5 turns:
            - They immediately explode: 3-tile radius blast, BKS + TOL damage,
              applies 50 toxicity to all enemies in range
            - The explosion can trigger ANOTHER enemy's volatility if multiple
              were set simultaneously (chain reaction — 1 follow-on explosion)
            If the volatile enemy dies without taking damage (somehow), no
            explosion — they just die normally.
  Why:      Delayed trigger mechanic requires patience. Do you wait for enemies
            to cluster? Do you kite the volatile enemy into a group? The
            chain-reaction property rewards multi-target setup. Risk: you might
            accidentally trigger it yourself with an AoE ability.
  Flavor:   "You don't make the explosion. You create the conditions for the
             explosion. There's a difference in liability."
  Notes:    VolatileEffect: on any damage event (modify_incoming_damage hook —
            but this one needs to RESPOND to damage, not modify it), trigger
            the explosion. Track via engine.volatile_entities set. On damage
            to entity in volatile_entities: run the explosion function. The
            chain: check if any OTHER entity in blast radius is also volatile;
            if yes, trigger that explosion after a 1-turn delay (or immediately
            — simpler).
